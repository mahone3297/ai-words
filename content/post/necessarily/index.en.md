## Pronunciation
/ˈnɛsərəli/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: In a way that is required by the circumstances; indispensably or inevitably. It implies that something must happen because of the nature of the situation or due to logical necessity. For example: "To succeed, we necessarily need to work hard."
  
  - **Adjective**: (less commonly used) That must happen or be the case; essential. Example: "The necessary changes were made to improve the system."

## Phrases
- "Necessarily so": Expressing inevitability or absolute necessity. Example: "It will necessarily so be a challenging task."
- "Not necessarily": Indicating that something may not be required or is not always true. Example: "Just because it's raining doesn't mean we can't go out; it's not necessarily the case."
  
## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "In order to graduate, students necessarily have to pass all their exams."
- "Given the circumstances, delays are necessarily going to occur."
- **Adjective (less common)**: "These steps are the necessary ones for achieving your goal."
