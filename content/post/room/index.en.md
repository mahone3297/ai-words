## Pronunciation
/roʊm/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **A room** is a enclosed space within a building, typically having walls, a ceiling, and a floor, intended for various purposes such as sleeping, working, or socializing.
   - Example: "The living room is where we spend most of our evenings together."
2. **Space or quarters** given to a person or group, often in a hotel or dormitory.
   - Example: "Each student has their own room in the dormitory."

### Verb (transitive)
1. To assign or provide a room for someone.
   - Example: "The hotel reserved a room for the guests."
2. To occupy or use a room.
   - Example: "They moved into their new apartment and started rooming together."

### Adjective
1. Relating to or denoting a room or its contents.
   - Example: "The room was decorated with antique furniture."

## Phrases
- "Take a room" means to book or reserve a room.
- "Room for improvement" suggests there's space to enhance or do better.
- "The room was filled with books" describes a room packed with literature.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The children were playing in their bedroom room."
- **Verb**: "She spent the afternoon rooming with her friends at the library."
- **Adjective**: "The study has a spacious room for a comfortable desk."
