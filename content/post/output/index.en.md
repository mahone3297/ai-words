## Word: Output

## Pronunciation
/ˈaʊtˌpʊt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The information, data, or results produced by a process, especially from a computer program, machine, or system. For example: "The program's output was displayed on the screen."
  - _Economic sense_: The goods or services produced by a company or industry over a period of time. For instance: "The factory's monthly output increased significantly this quarter."
  
- **Verb**: To produce or supply something as a result of a process, operation, or function. For example: "This machine outputs high-quality images."

## Phrases
- "Output device" refers to any peripheral that conveys information from a computer to the user, such as a monitor or printer.
- "Input-output model" is an economic theory that analyzes the relationship between different sectors within an economy and their outputs.
- "Increase/decrease output" can refer to boosting or reducing the production of goods, data, or results.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After running the algorithm, we analyzed its output to identify patterns."
- **Verb**: "This software will output a report detailing your daily sales."
