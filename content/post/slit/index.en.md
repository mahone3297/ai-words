## Pronunciation
/slɪt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. A long, narrow cut made in something, typically along its length. "The hunter made a slit in the deer's throat." 2. A thin opening or gap, often in a fabric or material. "The curtains had a slit at the top for ventilation."
- **Verb**: 1. To make a long, narrow cut or opening in something. "He slit the package open with a knife." 2. (informal) To separate or tear something apart, usually along a weak point. "The paper easily slit when folded."

## Phrases
- "Cut a slit in" or "make a slit"
- "A vertical slit" or "a horizontal slit"
- "Slit the seam" (referring to a garment)

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The archaeologist carefully examined the ancient pottery, noting the small slits on the rim."
- **Verb**: "The tailor slit the fabric to let in more light."
- **Informal**: "The old book had pages that easily slit apart from age."
