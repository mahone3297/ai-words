## Word: New

## Pronunciation
/nuː/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Not existing before; made, introduced, or discovered recently or now for the first time. For example: "They bought a new car last week."
  - Also used to describe something that has not been previously used or worn by anyone else: "She wore a new dress to the party."
- **Adjective (comparative form newer, superlative form newest)**: Referring to the most recent in time or order, or replacing something older. For example: "This is the newest model of smartphone on the market."

## Phrases
- "New kid on the block": A person or thing that is new to a situation or environment and has yet to establish themselves.
- "A new lease on life": A fresh start or renewed vigor after a difficult period.
- "Out with the old, in with the new": A phrase suggesting that old things should be replaced by new ones.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "He started his new job today."
- "She decided to redecorate her house with all new furniture."
- "After a long illness, he felt like a new person once he recovered."
