## Pronunciation
/səˈpɛktɪk/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Specific** (noun): Referring to a particular person, thing, or instance, distinguished from others by its unique characteristics or features. Example: "Each student was assigned a specific task for the project."
- **Specificity** (noun): The quality or state of being specific; clarity or precision in defining or describing something. Example: "The experiment aimed for high specificity in detecting the target molecule."

### Adjective
- **Specific** (adjective): Designated for or referring to a particular person, thing, or situation. Example: "The instructions were very specific about the materials needed."
- **Specifically** (adverb): In a particular or exact manner, often emphasizing a point. Example: "She asked specifically about your experience in that field."

### Adverb
- **Specifically** (adverb): With particular emphasis or detail. Example: "The report addressed the issue specifically, outlining potential solutions."

## Phrases
- "Be specific" means to give clear and precise details.
- "A specific request" refers to a particular want or demand.
- "In a specific way" denotes a particular method or approach.

## Example Sentences

- **Adverb**: "He explained the process specifically to ensure everyone understood."
