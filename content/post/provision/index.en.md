## Pronunciation
/prəˈvɪʒn/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Provision** (noun): The act of providing something in advance or beforehand; a supply or allocation of goods, services, or money to meet future needs.
   - Example: "The government made provisions for emergency aid during the pandemic."
2. **Provisioning** (noun, verb): The process of preparing or supplying something for use, especially in a ship or aircraft.
   - Example: "The crew was busy provisioning the ship with food and water."

### Verb
1. To make provisions: To arrange or plan for something in advance.
   - Example: "The company made provisions to expand its operations next year."
2. To provide (something) in advance: Supplying something before it's needed.
   - Example: "The school provided textbooks for the students in advance."

## Phrases
- "Make provisions for" implies planning and anticipating needs.
- "Provisional arrangements" denote temporary or temporary solutions.
- "Understood, we'll provision accordingly" means agreeing to fulfill a request or requirement.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The new building has ample provisions for disabled access."
- **Verb**: "They made provisions for a backup generator in case of power outages."
- **Provisioning**: "The expedition team was provisioning their campsite for the long journey."
