## Pronunciation
/piˈleɪt/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A person who pilots a vehicle, such as an aircraft or spacecraft: *The pilot skillfully landed the plane on the runway.*
2. A professional in charge of navigating or operating a vehicle during transportation: *The bus pilot guided the passengers safely through the city.*

### Verb
1. To operate or control the navigation of a vehicle, especially an aircraft: *The pilot navigated the plane through the stormy weather.*
2. To serve as the driver or operator of a vehicle: *She asked her son to pilot the car on the long trip.*

### Adjective
1. Characterizing or relating to a pilot: *Pilot lessons are essential for aspiring aviators.*
2. Acting as a guide or leader: *The pilot program aimed to set a positive example for other schools.*

## Phrases
- "Pilot a plane" or "Pilot a ship"
- "Pilot program"
- "Pilot error"
- "Pilot light"
- "First officer/pilot"

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The experienced pilot managed to land the plane despite the fog."
- **Verb**: "The new trainee will shadow the pilot for a few flights before taking over solo."
- **Adjective**: "The pilot school curriculum emphasizes hands-on experience and critical thinking skills."
