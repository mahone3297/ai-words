## Word: Magnet

## Pronunciation
/ˈmæɡnɪt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A material or object that produces a magnetic field and attracts iron or other ferromagnetic materials. For example: "The child played with the magnets on the fridge."
  - _As a physical object_: "A horseshoe magnet is often used in science experiments."
  - _Metaphorical usage_: "The amusement park was a magnet for children during the summer holidays."

- **Verb**: (transitive) To attract or draw something using a magnetic force or figuratively by a strong appeal or influence. For example: "This new restaurant has been magnetizing food lovers from all over the city."

## Phrases
- "Magnetic field": The area around a magnet where its force can be detected.
- "Magnetic north": The direction towards which a compass needle points, corresponding approximately to the Earth's magnetic poles.
- "Love magnet": A person who naturally attracts others due to their charm or charisma.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The speaker held up a bar magnet to demonstrate polarity."
- **Verb**: "Her infectious personality magnetized people to her cause."
