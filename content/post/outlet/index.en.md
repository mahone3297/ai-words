## Pronunciation
/ˈaʊtˌlet/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A point where something, especially a river or stream, flows out from a larger body of water or from a pipe, drain, or channel. Example: "The river's outlet empties into the sea."
- **Noun**: A place where goods are sold, often directly from the manufacturer or at reduced prices; a retail store or shop. Example: "There's a new clothing outlet opening up in the mall."
- **Noun**: An electrical socket or receptacle used to connect appliances or devices to a power source. Example: "Make sure the appliance is plugged into a grounded outlet."
- **Noun** (figurative): A means of expression or release for emotions, creativity, or energy. Example: "Art became her outlet for dealing with stress."

## Phrases
- "Outlet store" refers to a retail store that sells end-of-line or surplus stock from a manufacturer at discounted prices.
- "Power outlet" specifically refers to a socket or receptacle for connecting electronic devices to a power supply.
- "Emotional outlet" suggests a means through which one can express or relieve their feelings.

## Example Sentences
- **Retail Outlet**: "I found a great bargain on designer clothes at the factory outlet."
- **Natural Outlet**: "The river’s natural outlet was blocked by a dam."
- **Electrical Outlet**: "Ensure that the vacuum cleaner is plugged into a functioning electrical outlet."
- **Figurative Usage**: "Writing poetry serves as her creative outlet."
