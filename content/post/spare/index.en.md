## Definitions
### Noun
- **Spare (noun)**: 1. An extra or additional item, often in a reserve or not currently needed. For example: "She kept a spare key under the doormat."
- 2. A person who is not fully occupied or engaged; a reserve player. For example: "The team had several spares on the bench for emergencies."
- 3. A room or space set aside for a specific purpose, such as a spare bedroom or a spare tire. For example: "The house had a small spare room for guests."

### Verb
- **To spare** (verb): 1. To save or reserve something for later use or to avoid using. For example: "The farmer spared some of his crops for the winter."
- 2. To refrain from harming or causing damage to someone or something. For example: "The judge decided to spare the defendant from a harsh sentence."
- 3. (informal) To leave or set aside time for something. For example: "I'll spare half an hour for your meeting tomorrow."

### Adjective
- **Spare** (adjective): 1. Not excessive or unnecessary; simple or minimal. For example: "The spare, no-frills apartment suited her lifestyle."
- 2. Unused or available for use when needed. For example: "The car had a spare tire in the trunk."

### Adverb
- **Sparingly** (adverb): In moderation or sparingly. For example: "She used the paint sparingly to achieve a subtle effect."

## Phrases

- "Use the spare parts" refers to utilizing backup components.
- "Spare no effort" implies going all out to achieve a goal.
