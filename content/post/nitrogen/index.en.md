## Nitrogen - Noun (n)

### Pronunciation
/ˈnaɪ.troʊ.ɡən/

### Definitions
- **Noun**: A chemical element with the symbol N and atomic number 7. It is a colorless, odorless, tasteless gas that constitutes about 78% of Earth's atmosphere by volume. Nitrogen is essential for all living organisms, being a key component of amino acids, nucleic acids, and chlorophyll. It can be found in many compounds such as proteins, DNA, and RNA.

#### Example Sentences
- _"Plants obtain nitrogen from the soil through their roots and convert it into organic compounds."_
- _"The air we breathe is primarily composed of nitrogen and oxygen gases."_
  
### Phrases
- _"Nitrogen fixation"_ refers to the process by which atmospheric nitrogen is converted into a form that plants can absorb and use.
- _"Dissolved nitrogen"_ refers to the amount of nitrogen present in a liquid solution, often referring to water bodies affected by pollution.

### Synonyms
- Azote
- Nitrigen
- Elemental nitrogen

### Chemical & Scientific Usage
- _Ammonia (NH₃)_, a compound formed from nitrogen and hydrogen, is widely used in fertilizers and industrial chemicals.
- _Nitrate (NO₃⁻)_, a nitrogen-containing ion, is crucial for plant nutrition and can also be found in some explosives and propellants.
- _Nitrogen gas_ is often denoted as _N₂_ in chemical equations and contexts. 

### Related Terms
- Nitrogen cycle: The natural process through which nitrogen is converted between its various chemical forms in the environment.
- Denitrification: A bacterial process that converts nitrates back into nitrogen gas, releasing it into the atmosphere.
- Nitrogen deficiency: A condition where plants do not have enough available nitrogen for growth and reproduction.
