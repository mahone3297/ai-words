## Pronunciation
/əˈmʌŋ/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Adjective**: One of several things or people chosen from a group, set, or placed in a particular order; intermediate between two or more. For example: "She picked a book among the many on the shelf."
2. **Preposition**: Indicating the relationship of one thing to another within a group, often with "one of" or "the." For example: "He is among the top students in his class."

### Adverb
1. **Describing position**: In the middle of, or situated with respect to others. For example: "The house was located among the trees."
2. **Describing degree**: To a certain extent or level; partly. For example: "She agreed somewhat among the suggestions."

## Phrases
- "Among friends" implies a relaxed or familiar setting.
- "Among the best" signifies being one of the highest quality or most exceptional.
- "Not among them" means not included in a specified group.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "I saw several paintings among the collection, each with its own unique style."
- **Adverb**: "He shared his thoughts, but opinions were divided among the team members."
- **Preposition**: "She was among the first to arrive at the event."

## Synonyms
- Amidst
- Between (when referring to position)
- In the midst of
- Amid
- Amongst (informal, mainly British English)
