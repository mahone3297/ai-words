## Pronunciation
/rɪˈpɔːrt/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **A report** is a written or oral summary of information, findings, or events, typically prepared for a particular purpose, such as to inform, analyze, or evaluate. Example: "The manager submitted a progress report on the project."
2. **Financial report** - A document detailing a company's financial performance. Example: "The quarterly earnings report showed a significant increase in profits."
3. **Crime report** - A record of a crime committed. Example: "The police issued a report on the recent theft."

### Verb
1. To compile or prepare a report. Example: "She was tasked with reporting on the meeting proceedings."
2. To give an account or description of something. Example: "He reported the incident to the authorities."

### Adjective
1. Relevant or pertinent to a given topic or situation. Example: "The report findings were highly relevant to our research."

## Phrases
- "Submit a report" means to send or present a written report to someone.
- "Read the report" is the act of reviewing a document.
- "Draft a report" refers to creating a preliminary version of a report.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The team submitted a comprehensive report on their findings."
- **Verb**: "After the experiment, he reported his observations to the lab supervisor."
- **Adjective**: "The report highlighted the importance of sustainable practices in business."
