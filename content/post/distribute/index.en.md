## Pronunciation
/dɪˈstrɪbjuːt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Distribution**: The act of giving out or spreading something evenly among a group. For example: "The book was distributed among the students."
- **Distribution channel**: The system through which goods or services reach consumers. For example: "The company uses multiple distribution channels to sell its products."

### Verb
- **To distribute**: To allocate, scatter, or pass out something in equal or separate portions. For example: "The teacher distributed the homework assignments."
- **To redistribute**: To change the allocation or distribution of something. For example: "The government is planning to redistribute wealth."

## Phrases
- "Distribute materials" means to distribute physical items like papers or supplies.
- "Distributed computing" refers to a network where tasks are divided among multiple computers.
- "Random distribution" describes a spread that is not concentrated in one area.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The company's revenue is distributed across different regions."
- **Verb**: "The librarian helped the children distribute the books among themselves."
- **Redistributive policy**: "The government implemented a redistributive tax system to reduce income inequality."
