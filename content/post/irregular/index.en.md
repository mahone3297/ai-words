## Word: Irregular

## Pronunciation
/ɪˈrɛɡjələr/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Not conforming to regularity, uniformity, or established rules; deviating from what is usual, normal, or expected. For example: "The irregular heartbeat concerned the doctor."
  - In grammar: Not adhering to the typical patterns or rules of a language, such as verbs that do not follow a standard conjugation pattern. Example: "In English, 'go' is an irregular verb because its past tense form is 'went', not 'goed'."
- **Noun**: A person or thing that is not regular or does not conform to a set pattern. Example: "The soldiers were on the lookout for any irregulars trying to cross the border."

## Phrases
- "Irregular verb": A verb with a non-standard conjugation pattern, e.g., "be," "have," "do," "go."
- "Irregular shape": Describing something that lacks symmetry or has no defined geometric form.
- "Irregular schedule": A timetable or frequency that doesn't follow a consistent pattern.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "Due to its irregular shape, the puzzle piece wouldn't fit in the gap."
- **Noun**: "The army was bolstered by a group of irregular fighters who knew the terrain well."
