## Pronunciation
/sheɪm/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. A feeling of guilt, remorse, or dishonor due to one's actions or conduct. 2. Public disgrace or disapproval. 3. A cause for embarrassment or shame.
- **Verb**: To feel ashamed or embarrassed about something; to experience a sense of shame.
- **Adjective**: Causing or expressing shame; disgraceful; humiliating.

## Phrases
- "Feel no shame" implies that there is nothing to be embarrassed about.
- "Save face from shame" means to recover from a shameful situation.
- "In one's darkest shame" refers to the most embarrassing or shameful moment.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: She couldn't bear the shame of her family knowing what she had done.
- **Verb**: After realizing his mistake, he felt a deep shame.
- **Adjective**: The act was so shameful that it was reported in the news.
