## Pronunciation
/paɪl/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **A collection of things placed or lying one above the other, often in a disordered heap**: *A pile of books on the table.*
2. **A large amount or quantity**: *There's a pile of work waiting for me to finish.*
3. **A small hill or mound**: *The children were building a sand pile at the beach.*
4. (informal) A gambling bet or stake: *He put a big pile on the winning team.*

### Verb
1. **To accumulate or gather (things) in a heap**: *She piled the dishes in the sink.*
2. **To form a pile or heap**: *The leaves piled up in the yard after the storm.*
3. (informal) To bet a significant amount: *He piled all his chips on black.*

## Phrases
- "Build a pile" means to create a stack or heap.
- "Clear off the pile" is an expression to remove items from a heap or stack.
- "Pile on the pressure" is a figurative way to say to intensify or add to the stress.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She carefully arranged her clothes into neat piles before packing for vacation."
- **Verb**: "He quickly piled the wood in preparation for the fire."
- **Informal Verb**: "I decided to pile all my savings into stocks hoping for a good return."
