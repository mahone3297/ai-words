## Pronunciation
/ˈrɒlər/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A device with wheels, used for transportation or for rolling something along, especially a roller coaster or a roller skate.
- A person who works on or operates such a device, as a roller mechanic.
- A thin, cylindrical strip of material, often used to apply paint or other substances, e.g., wall coverings or printing ink.

### Verb
- To move along or over an area using a roller, as in painting or tarmacking.
- To use a roller or rolling motion to apply or spread a substance, like paint or gravel.

## Phrases
- "Roller coasters are thrilling rides."
- "The painter rolled the paint onto the wall."
- "A team of workers rolled out the new carpet."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The construction crew used a roller to smooth the concrete."
- **Verb**: "She decided to hire a professional roller to paint her house."
- **Intransitive**: "The roller skater glided effortlessly across the rink."

## Synonyms
- Rollerblade (for the activity)
- Rolling machine
- Paint roller
- Road roller
- Steel drum (in the context of music)

## Antonyms
- Static (motionless)
- Unrolling (opposite of rolling)
