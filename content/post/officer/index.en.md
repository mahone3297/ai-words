## Pronunciation
/ˈɒfɪsər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A person who holds a position of authority or command in the armed forces, the police, or other organizations such as a company or club, typically one who is responsible for carrying out administrative, managerial, or supervisory duties. Example: "The police officer on duty asked to see my identification."
- **Noun**: In a broader sense, it can also refer to any person holding a position of authority or responsibility in a particular field, such as a government officer, customs officer, or bank officer. Example: "She's been working as a loan officer at the bank for several years now."

## Phrases
- "Officer on duty": Refers to an officer who is currently serving in their official capacity, e.g., "The officer on duty was patrolling the area."
- "Rank and file officer": This term refers to a regular, non-commissioned officer, not of high rank, within an organization.
- "Executive officer": A high-ranking officer in charge of the day-to-day management of an organization, often second-in-command.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (armed forces)**: "The captain ordered the officer to lead the platoon into battle."
- **Noun (police)**: "The traffic officer directed us to move our car from the no-parking zone."
- **Noun (organization)**: "The HR officer handled the new employee orientation."
