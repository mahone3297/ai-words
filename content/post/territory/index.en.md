## Definitions
### Noun
1. A region or area, especially one controlled or claimed by a particular nation or group, with its own distinct boundaries and characteristics.
   - Example: "The Amazon rainforest is a vast territory rich in biodiversity."
2. The extent or range of power or authority over something.
   - Example: "His diplomatic territory extended beyond the borders of his country."

### Verb
1. To control or have dominion over a particular area or region.
   - Example: "The explorer charted new territories for the empire."
2. To claim or establish a claim to a specific area or subject matter.
   - Example: "The company has been expanding its territorial reach in the tech market."

### Adjective
1. Characteristic of a particular region or place, often marked by cultural or historical significance.
   - Example: "The architecture of the region is distinctive and unique to its territory."
2. Pertaining to the rights or jurisdiction of a particular entity.
   - Example: "The territorial waters of a country extend up to 12 miles from its shore."

## Phrases
- "Secure the territorial integrity" means to maintain the undivided control of a region.
- "Expanding territorial claims" refers to the growing demand for land or territory.
- "Historical territorial disputes" denote disagreements over land ownership or control.
