## Pronunciation
/weed/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Plant**: A herbaceous plant, often considered unwanted due to its ability to grow rapidly and displace other plants, sometimes used for recreational or medicinal purposes (e.g., marijuana, cannabis).
   - Example: "The garden was overrun with weeds after neglecting it for weeks."

2. **Removal**: The act of removing unwanted plants, especially those that compete with desired crops.
   - Example: "We spent the afternoon weeding the vegetable garden."

3. **Drug**: A slang term for marijuana, particularly when used as a recreational drug.
   - Example: "Many people argue that marijuana has medicinal benefits and should be decriminalized for medical weed use."

### Verb
1. To remove weeds from (an area).
   - Example: "I need to weed the garden before the plants get choked out."

2. To clear or eliminate something undesirable, often metaphorically.
   - Example: "We need to weed out the irrelevant information from the report."

## Phrases
- "Get rid of weeds"
- "Prevent weed growth"
- "Control weed population"
- "Weed out" (to remove unsuitable individuals from a group)

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She carefully removed the weeds from around the rosebush."
- **Verb**: "It's important to weed out fake news from social media feeds."
- **Metaphorical**: "The company is trying to weed out office politics."

## Synonyms
- Grass
- Dandelion
- Untended growth
- Weed killer
- Herbicide (as a chemical for controlling weeds)
