## Word: Occupy

## Pronunciation
/ˈɑːkjəˌpaɪ/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To take up space or a position; to reside or stay in a place. It can also refer to taking control or possession of a building, land, or area, often as part of a protest or military action.
   - _Example Sentence:_ "The protesters decided to occupy the city square overnight."
- **Verb**: (v) To engage or fill up (time); to keep busy with an activity.
   - _Example Sentence:_ "She occupies her weekends with painting and hiking."

## Phrases
- "Occupy oneself": To keep oneself busy or engaged in an activity.
   - _Example Sentence:_ "After retirement, he occupied himself with gardening and reading."
- "Occupy a position": To hold a job or role within an organization.
   - _Example Sentence:_ "She has occupied the position of CEO for five years now."
- "Military occupation": The control and domination of a territory by foreign armed forces after its surrender.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb (possession)**: "The army occupied the strategic hilltop to gain an advantage over the enemy."
- **Verb (engagement)**: "Her passion for writing occupies most of her free time."

## Synonyms
- Inhabit
- Occupy
- Take up
- Hold
- Occupy positions such as live in, inhabit, reside
- Engage in, fill up, spend (time)
- Control (in the context of taking over a place)
