## Pronunciation
/ðeə/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: Used to indicate the place or position of something or someone. For example: "The book is there on the table."
- **Existential particle**: Indicates the presence or existence of something without specifying it. For example: "There's a car outside."
- **Relative pronoun**: In a relative clause, "there" introduces the subject, often referring to something previously mentioned. For example: "That's the house where we lived."

## Phrases
- "There it is" points out the location of something.
- "Over there" indicates a specific distance away.
- "Somewhere there" implies a vague but known location.

## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "I left my keys right there on the kitchen counter."
- **Existential particle**: "There seems to be a misunderstanding."
- **Relative pronoun**: "The dog that was barking belongs to the family over there."

## Synonyms
- **None specific to "there" as an adverb; it is unique in its usage.**

## Antonyms
- None (as "there" does not have a direct opposite in terms of meaning)

## Usage Notes
- "There" can also be used as an adverb of place, similar to "here," but with a focus on a specific point rather than proximity.
- It is not a standalone sentence unless functioning as a relative pronoun.
