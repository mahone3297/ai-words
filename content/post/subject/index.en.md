## Pronunciation
/subˈdʒekt/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **A person or thing that is being discussed, considered, or acted upon**: *The subject of our conversation was climate change.*
   - Example: "The main subject of the meeting was the budget proposal."
2. **A topic or theme under consideration**: *History is a fascinating subject for students.*
   - Example: "Art history is one of her favorite subjects."

### Verb
1. **To be the focus or matter of concern**: *The subject of the debate was healthcare reform.*
   - Example: "The subject changed from politics to sports after a short discussion."
2. **To be assigned or concerned with (a task or assignment)**: *Students are required to choose a subject for their research project.*
   - Example: "She is subject to her teacher's instructions."

### Adjective
1. **Characterizing or determining a person or thing**: *Her personality is quite different from his, which can make them a contrasting subject.*
   - Example: "The subject of the portrait is a beautiful young woman."

## Phrases
- "Focus on the subject" means to concentrate on a topic.
- "Switching subjects" indicates changing the topic of conversation.
- "Subject matter" refers to the content or topics covered in a lesson or discussion.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The teacher asked the class to discuss the subject of global warming."
- **Verb**: "The professor will cover various subjects in his lecture series."
- **Adjective**: "The subject's expertise lies in psychology and sociology."
