## Pronunciation
/subˈstɪtjuːt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A substitute** is a person or thing that takes the place of another when the original is not present, unavailable, or needed. For example: "The teacher asked for a volunteer to be a substitute for the absent student."
- **A substitute teacher** is a temporary educator who fills in for a regular teacher who is absent or on leave.

### Verb
- To **substitute** means to replace someone or something with another, usually temporarily. For example: "The coach decided to substitute the goalkeeper for a fresher player."
- To **serve as a substitute** means to function in place of another, often in a less preferred or official capacity. For example: "The spare key serves as a substitute in case we lose the main one."

## Phrases
- "Substitute for" indicates replacement or stand-in. For example: "This new software will serve as a substitute for the outdated one."
- "Use a substitute" means to utilize an alternative. For example: "Use low-fat milk as a substitute for whole milk in your recipe."
- "In lieu of" is a phrase meaning instead of, often used with a substitute. For example: "In lieu of a personal appearance, the CEO sent a video message."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The company hired a temporary substitute employee to cover the maternity leave."
- **Verb**: "The coach made several substitutions during the match to keep the team fresh."
- **Phrasal**: "I'll bring some extra pens in case someone forgets theirs; you can use a substitute if you need one."
