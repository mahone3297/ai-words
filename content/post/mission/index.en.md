## Word: Mission

## Pronunciation
/ˈmɪʃən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A specific task or assignment given to a person or group, often involving travel, especially one that involves military, religious, or charitable activities. E.g., "Their mission was to build a school in the remote village."
  - An important goal or purpose that is accompanied by strong conviction, e.g., "Her life's mission is to fight against climate change."
  - (Military) A deployment of a military force to accomplish a specific objective, e.g., "The mission was to secure the strategic location."
  - (Religious) A journey or endeavor undertaken by a religious organization or individual for proselytizing, teaching, or charitable work, e.g., "The missionaries went on a year-long mission to South America."

## Phrases
- "Mission statement": A formal summary of the aims and values of a company, organization, or individual.
- "On a mission": Describing someone who is very focused and determined to achieve a particular goal.
- "Mission impossible": Referring to a task that seems extremely difficult or virtually impossible to complete.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The space agency launched a new mission to explore Mars."
- "After completing his military mission, he returned home with honors."
- "Our mission today is to raise funds for the local food bank."
