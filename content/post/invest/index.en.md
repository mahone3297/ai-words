## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈvest/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To commit money or capital to purchase financial products, property, or a business with the expectation of obtaining income or profit. For example: "They decided to invest their savings in stocks and shares."
- **Verb**: (v) To spend time, effort, or resources to achieve a benefit or gain in the future. For example: "She invested years of hard work into her education."
- **Verb**: (v) To endow with a quality, character, or ability. For example: "The experience has invested him with a deep understanding of the market."
  
## Phrases
- "Invest in": To buy or put money into something with the hope of making a profit or gaining interest. Example: "We're planning to invest in some new technology startups."
- "Invest one's time/money/efforts": To use these resources with the aim of achieving a beneficial outcome. Example: "She invested her time wisely by volunteering for causes she believed in."
  
## Example Sentences
- **Verb (financial)**: "After researching the company thoroughly, he decided to invest a substantial amount in its shares."
- **Verb (time/effort)**: "By investing hours each day practicing, the musician significantly improved his skills."
- **Verb (endow)**: "Her extensive travels have invested her with a unique cultural perspective that enriches her writing."
