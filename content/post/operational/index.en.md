## Pronunciation
/ˌɑːpərˈteɪʃənəl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: (of a system, machine, or process) functioning or able to function; working correctly or ready for use. For example: "The new software update made the application fully operational."
- **Noun**: (in military contexts) a period during which a unit or force is actively engaged in combat or otherwise fulfilling its primary purpose. For example: "The aircraft carrier entered operational service in 2023."

## Phrases
- "Operational hours" refer to the times when a business, facility, or service is open and functioning.
- "Go operational" means to begin functioning or become active, often used for systems or projects that have just been launched.
- "Operational readiness" indicates that something, typically a piece of equipment or a team, is prepared and capable of performing its intended function immediately.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "After extensive testing, the satellite was deemed operational and ready to be launched into orbit."
- **Noun**: "The special operations team completed their final training exercise before going operational in the field."
