## Pronunciation
/ˌmɛkəˈnɪkl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Relating to machinery or tools; operated by machines or devices rather than by hand. Example: "The factory uses a lot of mechanical equipment."
  - Also refers to something done in a routine or automatic manner without showing original thought or feeling. Example: "His responses were purely mechanical."

  - In reference to living things, it can mean relating to the physical processes or functions of a body, rather than the mental or emotional aspects. Example: "A mechanical problem with his heart required surgery."

- **Noun**: A person skilled in mechanics or machinery; a mechanic. Example: "The mechanical was able to fix the engine within hours."

## Phrases
- "Mechanical failure": A breakdown or malfunction of a machine or system.
- "Mechanical engineering": The branch of engineering that deals with the design, construction, and use of machinery.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "They designed a mechanical clock that runs on solar power."
- "Her movements became increasingly mechanical after working on the assembly line for hours."
- **Noun**: "The aircraft had to make an emergency landing due to a suspected mechanical issue."

## Synonyms
- (adjective) Automated, mechanized, technical, methodical
- (noun, in the context of a person skilled in mechanics) Mechanic, technician, engineer
