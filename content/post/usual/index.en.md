## Pronunciation
/ˈjuːʒuəl/

## Definitions
### Adjective
- **1.** Characteristic or typical of a particular person, situation, or time; normal or ordinary. For example: "Her usual routine involves waking up early."
- **2.** Expected or customary; standard. For example: "The usual procedure is to sign your name on the document."

### Noun (colloquial)
- A regular or frequent occurrence; a norm. For example: "That's just his usual behavior during meetings."

## Phrases
- "In one's usual style" describes something done in a familiar manner.
- "Out of the ordinary" contrasts with "usual" to indicate something not typical.
- "Break from the usual" suggests doing something different from what is expected.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "She was late, which wasn't her usual promptness."
- **Noun**: "His usual coffee order was a large latte with extra foam."
- **Colloquial**: "After the long day, it felt good to break from our usual routine and have a picnic."
