## Pronunciation
/tæks/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A compulsory financial charge paid by individuals or businesses to the government for the provision of public services or goods, such as education, healthcare, or infrastructure.
   - "The government collects taxes on income, property, and sales."
   - "Tax revenues help fund social programs."

### Verb
1. To impose or levy a tax on (a person, company, or transaction).
   - "The government decided to raise taxes on imported goods."
   - "The corporation is required to file a tax return by the deadline."

### Adjective
1. Relating to a financial charge or levy.
   - "A tax-exempt organization doesn't pay taxes on its income."
   - "The tax-free zone attracts shoppers with lower prices."

## Phrases
- "File a tax return" means to submit the necessary paperwork to report income and pay taxes owed.
- "Tax deductions" are reductions in taxable income.
- "Tax season" refers to the time when people typically prepare and submit their tax returns.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The citizens must pay their income tax every year."
- **Verb**: "The accountant helped the client minimize their tax liability."
- **Adjective**: "Many small businesses operate in a tax-efficient manner."
