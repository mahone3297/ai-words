## Definitions
### Noun
- **Sour** (adj): Referring to a taste that is unpleasantly acidic or sharp, typically due to the presence of vinegar or rotting fruit. Example: "The lemonade tasted a bit sour."
- **Sourness** (noun): The quality or condition of being sour; a feeling of disappointment or unhappiness. Example: "She had a sour expression after hearing the news."

### Verb
- **To sour** (v): To become spoiled or unpleasant, often in terms of taste or mood. Example: "The milk has soured and needs to be thrown out."
- **To sour on** (phrase): To lose interest or enthusiasm in something. Example: "Her mood soured on the project after a series of setbacks."

### Adjective
- **Sour-faced** (adj): Having an angry or unhappy expression. Example: "The waiter looked sour-faced when we asked for more salt."

## Phrases
- "A sour note" can refer to a dissonant or disagreeable element in a situation or conversation.
- "Bitter-sweet memories" evoke a mixture of both happiness and sadness, like a slightly sour taste.
- " Sour grapes" is a saying used to describe someone who is envious and begrudgingly criticizes what they cannot have.
