## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈtaɪtl/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (transitive) To give someone the right or claim to something; to confer a title upon. For example: "The inheritance entitled him to a substantial fortune."
  - **Verb**: (transitive) To give a name to something, especially a book, film, or piece of music. For example: "The author entitled her latest novel 'Echoes of the Past'."
  - **Verb**: (intransitive) To have a right to something because of one's position, qualifications, or accomplishments. For example: "By virtue of his degree, he is entitled to practice law."

## Phrases
- "To be entitled to something" means that one has a legal or moral right to receive or do something.
- "Entitle someone to something" indicates granting someone a right or privilege.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb (granting rights)**: "Winning the competition entitled the team to represent their country at the international level."
- **Verb (naming a work)**: "The artist entitled her painting 'The Sunrise Symphony' to reflect its theme."
- **Intransitive use**: "As citizens, we are all entitled to certain basic human rights."

## Synonyms
- Grant
- Confer
- Endow
- Allocate
- Assign
- Bestow
- Authorize
- Qualify
- Entrench (in the sense of establishing a legal right)
