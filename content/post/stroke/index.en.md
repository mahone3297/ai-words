## Pronunciation
/stroʊk/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A sudden, forceful movement or blow:** *He executed a perfect stroke of the golf club.*
- **A mark or line made with a tool:** *The artist's brushstroke was delicate and expressive.*
- **An instance of action or effort:** *He finished his homework in one swift stroke.*
- **A unit of currency in some countries, particularly in Asia:** *The exchange rate was 100 strokes to the dollar.*

### Verb
- **To perform a movement or action with a specific technique:** *She stroked the cat gently.*
- **To make a mark or pass an object through a liquid or substance:** *The chef stroked the sauce across the pasta.*
- **To write or draw with a fluid motion:** *He stroked out a quick sketch on the paper.*
- **To indicate or suggest something with a gesture or expression:** *His nod was a silent stroke of approval.*

### Adjective
- **A single act or movement:** *The final stroke of the pen completed the signature.*
- **Severe or drastic:** *A sudden financial stroke of bad luck.*
- **Requiring great skill or effort:** *The gymnast's complex stroke was breathtaking to watch.*

### Adverb
- **In a single movement:** *He finished the task in one stroke.*
- **Effortlessly or with ease:** *She answered the question with a single stroke of her pen.*
