## Pronunciation
/prəˈnɑːspɔɪnt/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **A point of view or perspective**: A particular way of looking at something or considering an issue, often from a specific individual or group's standpoint.
   - Example: "From his unique viewpoint, the problem had a different solution."
2. **An opinion or judgment**: A personal assessment or evaluation of a situation or matter.
   - Example: "Her viewpoint on climate change was well-informed."

### Verb
1. To adopt or express a particular point of view: To consider or present one's thoughts or beliefs on a topic.
   - Example: "It's important to maintain an open viewpoint on different opinions."
2. To have a particular perspective on something: To perceive or understand it through one's own viewpoint.
   - Example: "After traveling, he gained a broader viewpoint on global issues."

### Adjective
1. Characterizing a person's perspective or attitude: Reflecting their way of thinking or seeing things.
   - Example: "The book offers multiple viewpoints on the same historical event."

## Phrases
- "Hold a different viewpoint" means to have a contrasting opinion.
- "From your viewpoint" indicates the speaker's intention to acknowledge or consider your perspective.
- "Objective viewpoint" refers to a neutral, unbiased perspective.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Each character has a distinct viewpoint in the novel, contributing to its complexity."
- **Verb**: "Let's hear each other's viewpoints before making a decision."
- **Adjective**: "The debate highlighted the diverse scientific viewpoints on the subject."
