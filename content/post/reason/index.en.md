## Pronunciation
/rɪˈz(n) ən/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A **cause or explanation** for something that has happened or exists: *The reason for his absence was a family emergency.*
2. **A logical or justifiable basis**: *There's no good reason to do that.*
3. **An individual or thing that provides a motive**: *She was the main reason he joined the team.*

### Verb
1. To give or offer a reason: *Can you please explain the reason for your delay?*
2. To attribute or explain something by a reason: *They couldn't find a reason why the machine wouldn't start.*

### Adjective
1. Giving or serving as a cause or explanation: *The reason behind his decision was clear.*
2. Justifiable or logical: *A sound reason for wanting change.*

### Adverb
1. In terms of reason or logic: *He acted rationally, without any emotional reason.*

## Phrases
- "Without a good reason" implies a lack of justification.
- "Reasonable request" denotes a justifiable or acceptable demand.
- "Reasons for believing" refers to the grounds supporting a belief.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The reason why he failed the exam was his lack of preparation."
- **Verb**: "She explained her reason for leaving early."
- **Adjective**: "His decision was based on sound reasons."
- **Adverb**: "He acted rationally, without any emotional reason for his anger."
