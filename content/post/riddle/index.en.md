## Pronunciation
/rɪˈdʌɪl/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A form of entertainment that consists of a question or statement with a hidden meaning, often requiring a clever answer to reveal the intended message. For example: "The children loved solving the riddle before bedtime."
- **Verb**: To pose a riddle or enigma, usually for amusement or intellectual challenge. For example: "The teacher posed a challenging riddle to her class."

## Phrases
- "To crack a riddle" means to solve a puzzle or figure out the answer to a riddle.
- "Riddle me this" is a common introductory phrase for presenting a riddle.
- "A riddle of ages" describes an ancient or long-standing riddle.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The children were delighted to unravel the teacher's riddle."
- **Verb**: "The bard entertained the crowd with a series of witty riddles."
- **Sentence**: "Can you guess the answer to this riddle? What has keys but can't open locks?"
