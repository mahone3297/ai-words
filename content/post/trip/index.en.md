## Definitions
### Noun
- **A trip**:
  - Refers to a journey, usually for leisure or travel, involving one or more stops: "We're planning a road trip to the coast."
  - In computing, it can mean a series of actions or steps: "The software went through a system update without any trip."

### Verb
- **To trip**:
  - To stumble or fall, often accidentally: "She tripped on a loose stone and sprained her ankle."
  - (Informal) To make a mistake or stumble while performing a task: "I accidentally tripped over my own words during the presentation."

### Adjective
- **Tripping**:
  - (Of a person) moving with a light, quick step: "She was dancing in a tripping manner."
  - (Of a signal or event) occurring frequently or irregularly: "The server is experiencing tripping errors."

### Adverb
- **Trippingly**:
  - With a light, graceful movement: "He danced trippingly around the room."

- "Long trip" refers to a lengthy journey.
- "A slip and trip hazard" indicates a danger of falling.
- "Tripped the wire" (informal) means to cause an alarm or warning.
