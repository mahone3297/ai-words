## Pronunciation
/thɪn/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Adjective**: Having a small amount of substance or body; not thick or robust. Example: "The model is very thin compared to last year's version."
2. **Noun**: A narrow or slender shape or form. Example: "The alleyway was so thin that only one person could pass at a time."

### Verb
1. To become or make something thinner in size, quantity, or intensity. Example: "The soup thinned out after being cooked for too long."
2. **Verb**: (figurative) To reduce in number, importance, or effectiveness. Example: "Their arguments thinned as the night went on."

### Adverb
1. In a thin manner; with little substance or intensity. Example: "She spoke softly and thinly through the phone."

## Phrases
- "Stay thin" refers to maintaining a slim or lightweight state.
- "A thin layer of frost" describes a minimal covering of ice.
- "Get thin-skinned" implies becoming easily offended.
