## Pronunciation
/ˈθɜrməmɪtər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A device used to measure temperature, typically containing a scale marked in degrees or other units of temperature. For example: "The doctor took my temperature with a digital thermometer."
- **Adjective**: Relating to or denoting a thermometer or measurement of temperature. For example: "A mercury thermometer is a classic example of an old-fashioned thermometer."

## Phrases
- "Take one's temperature" means to measure someone's body temperature.
- "Thermometer reading" refers to the displayed temperature measurement.
- "A high thermometer" signifies an unusually high temperature, often used metaphorically.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The thermometer showed a feverish 102°F."
- **Adjective**: "The new smart home system includes a wireless thermometer for precise temperature control."
- "The nurse took the patient's temperature before administering medication."

## Synonyms
- Thermometric
- Temperature gauge
- Hygrometer (for relative humidity)
- Thermograph
- Thermometric indicator

## Antonyms
- Non-thermometer (implying no temperature measurement)
- Isothermal (relating to a constant temperature)
- Cold (in terms of low temperature)
