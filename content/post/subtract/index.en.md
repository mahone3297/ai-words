## Pronunciation
/subˈstræk/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: n. & v. To take away or remove (a quantity or amount) from a total, leaving a smaller value. *Example: In mathematics, subtract 5 from 10.*
- **Adjective**: (informal) Decreased; reduced in amount or quality. *Example: The team's morale subtracted after the loss.*

## Phrases
- "Subtracting one number from another" describes the mathematical operation.
- "Subtract the cost of taxes" implies adjusting a total for tax payments.
- "Subtracting negative emotions" can mean overcoming or dismissing them.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The accountant subtracted the expenses to determine the company's profit."
- **Adjective**: "The gloomy weather subtracted from our enjoyment of the beach vacation."
- "After subtracting his savings, he had only $50 left in the bank."
