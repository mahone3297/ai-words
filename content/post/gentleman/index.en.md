## Word: Gentleman

## Pronunciation
/ˈdʒentlmən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A man who is polite, courteous, and well-mannered; typically one who comes from a higher social class or has a good education and upbringing. A gentleman is also characterized by his respect for others and adherence to a code of conduct that emphasizes fairness, integrity, and chivalry. 

  - Example: "The young gentleman offered his seat to the elderly lady on the bus."
  
- **Historical Definition**: In earlier times, this term was used specifically to denote a man of high social rank or a member of the gentry (lower nobility).

## Phrases
- "A real gentleman": This phrase is used to describe a man who consistently displays admirable qualities such as politeness, kindness, and respect towards others.
  
- "Gentleman's agreement": An informal, unwritten agreement based on mutual trust and honor between parties, often used when discussing business deals or ethical behavior.
  
- "Gentleman caller": A man who visits a woman with romantic intentions, especially in a formal or old-fashioned context.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The host greeted each guest like the perfect gentleman he was."
- "In historical novels, the young protagonist aspired to be a true gentleman of his time."

## Synonyms
- Gentlemanly person
- Polite man
- Courteous man
- Respectable man
- Civilized man
- Nobleman (in the historical sense)
- Knight (in a figurative sense)
