## Pronunciation
/prɪnˈsaɪər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A person who has been confined as a result of being accused, convicted, or held for trial of a crime, typically in a prison or jail. ["Oxford English Dictionary"]
- **Verb**: To hold captive or confine, especially as a punishment or security measure. ["Cambridge Dictionary"]

## Phrases
- "prisoner of war" (POW)
- "life sentence" for a prisoner
- "on parole" (released from prison under certain conditions)

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The prisoner was granted a temporary release to visit his family."
- **Verb**: "The authorities have been searching for the escaped prisoner for weeks."
- **Sentence with Noun**: "The former president served time as a political prisoner during his regime."
