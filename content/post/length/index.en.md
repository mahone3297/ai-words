## Pronunciation
/ˈlɛŋkθ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The measurement or extent of something from end to end; the greater dimension of an object. For example: "The length of the table was six feet."
  - In mathematics, it can also refer to the magnitude of a line segment or vector.
  - In clothing, it may describe the measurement from shoulder to hem of a garment (e.g., "the dress has a longer length").
- **Verb**: To make something longer or to extend in length. For example: "She lengthened her stride to catch up with him."
- **Adjective**: Relating to distance along an axis or in a direction. For example: "Lengthy discussions often lead to better decisions."

## Phrases
- "In length and breadth": Referring to the overall size or dimensions of something.
- "At length": After a long time or after much discussion; finally.
- "To go the full length": To pursue something to its complete or utmost extent.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "We need to measure the length of the cable to ensure it reaches both sockets."
- **Verb**: "She decided to lengthen the sleeves of her coat by adding extra fabric."
- **Adjective**: "The book's length intimidated him initially, but he found it engaging once he started reading."
