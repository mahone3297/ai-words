## Pronunciation
/dɪˈspaɪz/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To hold in strong contempt or disrespect; to regard with hatred or scorn. (n.): The act or feeling of despising something or someone.
- **Noun**: The act of despising, or the feeling of contempt or hatred towards someone or something.

## Phrases
- "Despise someone/something" expresses complete contempt or hatred.
- "To be despised by others" indicates being held in low regard.
- "Despising their actions" shows disapproval of someone's conduct.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "He despised his rival's dishonest tactics and vowed to expose them."
- **Noun**: "Her friends had completely despised her after she betrayed their trust."
- **Phrasal**: "Despite his success, he was despised by those who saw him as arrogant."

## Synonyms
- Loathe
- Hate
- Abhor
- Contemn
- Detest

## Antonyms
- Admire
- Respect
- Appreciate
- Like
- Cherish

## Usage
Despise is often used to describe a deep-rooted disdain or hatred towards someone or something, and can be used in both formal and informal contexts. It's commonly found in situations where there is a significant disagreement or moral aversion.
