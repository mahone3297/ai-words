## Pronunciation
/ˈfɪzɪks/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The branch of science concerned with the fundamental laws of nature, especially concerning matter, energy, and their interactions.
   - Definition: The study of matter, its properties, motion, and forces that govern the universe.
   - Example: Physics is a crucial foundation for many other scientific disciplines.

2. The state or quality of being physical; material existence.
   - Definition: The tangible and observable aspects of the world, distinct from the abstract or immaterial.
   - Example: The physics of a building determine its structural stability.

### Verb
1. To apply or study the principles of physics in a particular context or problem.
   - Example: Scientists are working on physics-based solutions to climate change.

2. To engage in discussions or explanations about physics concepts.
   - Example: She was discussing physics with her classmates during the break.

### Adjective
1. Relating to the principles or methods of physics.
   - Example: A physics-based simulation of the solar system.

2. Characteristic of or involving physical phenomena.
   - Example: The physics behind roller coasters.

## Phrases
- "Exploring the physics of light"
- "Physics tells us that energy cannot be created or destroyed"
- "The laws of physics remain constant"
