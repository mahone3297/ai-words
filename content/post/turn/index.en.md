## Pronunciation
/tɜːrn/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A change in direction or position**: A turn in the road, a turn of the page. Example: "The path took a sharp turn to the left."
- **An act or instance of rotating or moving around a center**: The turn of a wheel, a turn of the hourglass. Example: "He gave a full turn before sitting down."
- **A chance or opportunity**: A turn at bat, a turn to speak. Example: "Everyone had a turn to share their ideas."
- **A period of time or responsibility**: Shift rotation, taking a turn at work. Example: "It's your turn to watch the kids this weekend."

### Verb
- **To rotate or move around**: She turned the doorknob to open the door.
- **To change direction or course**: He turned away from the argument.
- **To present something to someone**: The host turned over the book to the guest.
- **To control or direct (attention, focus, or events)**: The game was turning in our favor.
- **To change one's opinion or attitude**: His views on the topic have taken a dramatic turn.

### Adjective
- **Characterizing a change or sequence**: A sudden turn of events, a gradual turn of opinion.
- **Requiring a change of direction**: A sharp turn, a U-turn.
- **Temporary or intermittent**: A turn-off, a turnabout.

### Adverb
- **In a particular manner**: He turned quickly to face her.
- **After a certain period**: He takes turns every two hours.
- **By degrees or stages**: The temperature is turning colder.

## Phrases
- "Take a turn" means to have a go or participate.
- "Make a turn" refers to changing direction.
- "On your turn" signals that it is someone's opportunity.
- "Turn-based" describes a game where players take turns.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The car took a tight turn around the corner."
- **Verb**: "She turned the key in the ignition to start the engine."
- **Adjective**: "The plot took a surprising turn halfway through the book."
- **Adverb**: "He turned around to face his friends."
