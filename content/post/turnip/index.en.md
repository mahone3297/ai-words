## Pronunciation
/tɜːrnɪp/

## Definitions
### Noun
A turnip is a root vegetable with a white or purple bulbous shape, often used in culinary dishes, especially in stews and soups. It has a slightly sweet and earthy flavor.

### Verb (informal)
To eat or consume a turnip, often in the context of using it as a food item.

## Phrases
- "Eat your turnips" as a request for someone to consume their share of turnips.
- "Store turnips for winter" implies saving vegetables for colder months.
- "A turnip's health benefits" refers to the nutritional value of the vegetable.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She planted turnips in her garden every spring."
- **Verb**: "He grated some turnips into the soup for extra flavor."
- **Informal**: "I tried to get him to eat his turnips, but he refused."

## Synonyms
- Radish (specifically when referring to a similar root vegetable)
- Rutabaga
- Swede
- Yarrow
- White cabbage

## Notes
Turnips can also be used metaphorically, such as "He was a tough nut to crack, like a stubborn turnip." In this case, "turnip" acts as a metaphor for someone who is difficult to persuade or change their mind.
