## Word: Middle

## Pronunciation
/ˈmɪd·l/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The central point or part of something that lies between two ends or extremes; the center. Example: "The house is located in the middle of the street."
  - Also used to describe a period of time that is neither the beginning nor the end: "In the middle of the night, he woke up with a brilliant idea."
- **Adjective**: Situated at or near the center of something; intermediate. Example: "The middle child often feels overlooked."
  - Also used to denote something that is average or mediocre: "His grades were consistently middle-of-the-road."

## Phrases
- "In the middle of": Being within a situation or surrounded by something. Example: "We found ourselves in the middle of a heated argument."
- "Middle ground": An intermediate position between two opposing viewpoints or extremes. Example: "Both sides need to find a middle ground on this issue."
- "Middle school": A period of education in some countries between elementary and high school, typically for children aged around 11 to 14 years old.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She sat in the middle seat of the car."
- **Adjective**: "The middle section of the book was the most captivating."
