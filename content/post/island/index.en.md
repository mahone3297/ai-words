## Word: Island

## Pronunciation
/ˈaɪlənd/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A piece of land completely surrounded by water, typically smaller than a continent and often located in a river, lake, or sea. For example: "They spent their vacation on a tropical island."
  - _Island can also refer to an area of land that's isolated within a city or larger body due to geographical or infrastructural reasons._ For example: "The kitchen island was the focal point of the house."
  
## Phrases
- "Island nation": A country consisting primarily of one or more islands. Example: "Japan is an island nation."
- "Desert island": An uninhabited island, often imagined as a place where someone is stranded alone. Example: "If I were stranded on a desert island, what book would I take?"
- "Every man for himself": A phrase suggesting that people should fend for themselves in difficult situations, often metaphorically referring to being stranded on an island with no help.
  
## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After crossing the bridge, we finally reached the peaceful island."
- "In the middle of the bustling city, Central Park serves as a green island amidst concrete buildings."
- "He dreamt of living on a secluded island far from civilization."

## Synonyms
- Isle
- Archipelago (when referring to a group of islands)
- Key (in some geographical contexts, especially Florida Keys)
- Peninsula (technically not an island but sometimes referred to as such due to its isolation)
- Landmass
