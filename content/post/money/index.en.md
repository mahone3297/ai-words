## Word: Money

## Pronunciation
/ˈmʌni/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A medium of exchange that can be used to buy goods and services or to pay debts; typically, coins or banknotes, but also includes digital currencies and checks. It serves as a store of value and a unit of account. Example: "She saved enough money for her college tuition."
- **Noun (informal)**: Wealth or riches, especially a large amount of it. Example: "He comes from a family with a lot of money."
- **Verb**: To supply with money or capital. Example: "The government moneyed the project to boost infrastructure development."

## Phrases
- "Money talks": Suggests that financial resources have significant influence or power in decision-making processes.
- "Make money": To earn or acquire wealth through various means such as employment, investments, or business ventures.
- "Put one's money where one's mouth is": To demonstrate commitment by investing resources in something one claims to support or believe in.
- "Money laundering": The process of disguising the proceeds of illegal activities to make them appear legal.

## Example Sentences

- **Verb**: "The venture capitalist decided to money several startup companies this year."
