## Pronunciation
/ˈmiːntaɪm/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A period of time that elapses during an interval or waiting period; a meanwhile. For example: "In the meantime, we can work on the preliminary tasks."
- **Adverb**: During a period of waiting or between two events; meanwhile. For example: "We'll have to wait; meantime, let's discuss the backup plan."

## Phrases
- "In the meantime": This phrase is used to indicate what will happen or should be done during a period of waiting or before something else happens. For example: "We're waiting for the test results; in the meantime, we'll continue with our regular activities."
- "Meanwhile back at...": This informal expression is often used humorously to shift focus from one scenario to another concurrent scene. For example: "Meanwhile, back at the ranch, the horses were getting restless."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The renovations will take several months; we'll stay at a hotel in the meantime."
- **Adverb**: "I'm saving up for a new car; meantime, I'm using my old one."
