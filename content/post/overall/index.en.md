## Pronunciation
/ˌoʊvərˈɔːl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Concerning or applying to all aspects or parts; total or comprehensive. For example: "Overall, it was a successful year for the company."
- **Adverb**: As a whole; taking everything into account. For example: "Overall, the project went smoothly."
- **Noun**: A protective garment that covers the whole body or most of it, often worn as part of a uniform or during messy tasks. For instance: "The painter put on his white overall before starting work."

## Phrases
- "In general/overall": This phrase is used when summarizing or giving a broad perspective based on all elements considered. Example: "In general/overall, the reviews for the new movie were positive."
- "Overall view/situation/plan": Refers to a broad or comprehensive understanding or design. Example: "The manager presented an overall plan for the company's restructuring."

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "There was an overall improvement in her grades this semester."
- **Adverb**: "Overall, the team played well despite some individual mistakes."
- **Noun**: "The mechanic changed his oil-stained overall before going home."
