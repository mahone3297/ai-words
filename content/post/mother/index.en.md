## Word: Mother

## Pronunciation
/ˈmʌðər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A woman in relation to her child or children; one's female parent. For example: "She was a loving and caring mother to her three children."
- **Noun (figurative)**: A person or thing regarded as the originator, source, or protector of something. Example: "Mother Earth"
- **Noun (derogatory)**: A term sometimes used to refer disrespectfully to an older woman. 
- **Noun (religious)**: In certain religious contexts, it can refer to the Virgin Mary, the mother of Jesus Christ.
  
## Phrases
- "Mother Nature": Refers to the natural environment and its processes.
- "Mother tongue": A person's first language learned from birth.
- "The mother of all [something]": Used to emphasize that something is the largest, most significant, or most extreme of its kind. Example: "This project will be the mother of all challenges."
- "Like mother, like daughter": A proverb suggesting similarities in behavior between a mother and her daughter.
  
## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "His mother taught him how to bake his first cake."
- **Figurative**: "The company considers innovation to be the mother of their success."
- **Religious**: "The faithful pray to the Virgin Mary, the mother of Christ."
