## Pronunciation
/pɪnt/

## Definitions
### Noun
A unit of volume commonly used in the United States and some Commonwealth countries, equivalent to 16 U.S. fluid ounces or approximately 0.568 liters.

### Verb
To measure or serve a quantity of liquid, typically in a pint glass.

### Adjective
Large or excessive; often used humorously.

## Phrases
- "Order a pint of beer"
- "Half a pint will do"
- "She filled a pint glass to the brim"

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "I bought a pint of milk from the corner store."
- **Verb**: "The bartender served me a pint of my favorite ale."
- **Adjective**: "He was so thirsty, he asked for a pint-sized lemonade."

## Synonyms
- Pint-sized (informal, referring to something small)
- Quart (a larger unit, equivalent to two pints)
- Pot (primarily British, a large glass of ale)
- Jug (a larger container for holding liquids)

## Usage Note
In the context of slang or informal conversation, "pint" can also be used to refer to a person's mood or temperament, as in "He's in a good pint today."
