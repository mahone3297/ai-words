## Pronunciation
/maʊθ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The opening in the face through which food is taken in and vocal sounds are emitted. For example: "She covered her mouth with her hand when she yawned."
- **Verb**: (mouth something) To form words silently with one's lips, often while reading or rehearsing lines. For example: "He was mouthing the lyrics to the song as he drove."
- **Noun**: (informal) A person's speech or expressed opinions; what someone says. For example: "His mouth got him into trouble again."
- **Verb**: (mouth off) To speak disrespectfully or impertinently. For example: "Don't you dare mouth off to your elders!"

## Phrases
- "Put words in someone's mouth": To attribute a statement to someone that they did not actually make.
- "Keep your mouth shut": To advise someone to be quiet or not reveal information.
- "Out of the mouths of babes": Expressing surprise at wisdom or insight coming from a child.
- "Hand to mouth": Living on very little, just enough to survive each day.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The baby's first teeth began to appear in his tiny mouth."
- **Verb**: "As an actor, she practiced mouth movements to perfect her delivery."
- **Informal Noun**: "Be careful what you say, because your mouth can get you into trouble."
- **Verb (mouth off)**: "After he mouthed off to the teacher, he was sent to the principal's office."
