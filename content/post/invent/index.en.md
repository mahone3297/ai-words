## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈvent/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To create or produce something new, often by using skill and imagination, especially a process or device that did not exist before. For example: "Alexander Graham Bell invented the telephone."
  - *Derivatives*: invention (noun), inventive (adjective)

### Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "Thomas Edison invented the light bulb, which revolutionized the way we live."
- "The Wright brothers invented the first successful airplane."
  
## Phrases
- "Invent a solution" means to come up with a new and original way to solve a problem.
- "To invent oneself" refers to creating or redefining one's own identity or persona.
- "Invent from scratch" implies creating something without any prior existing materials or foundations.

## Further Information
- The word "invent" can also be used metaphorically to describe devising new ideas, theories, or excuses, e.g., "She invented a new recipe for her cooking blog."
- When used as a transitive verb, it typically takes an object, such as "to invent a machine."
  
Please note that "invent" does not have noun or adjectival forms directly related to its primary meaning; however, as mentioned earlier, "invention" serves as a related noun form, referring to something created through the act of inventing.
