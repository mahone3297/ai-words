## Pronunciation
/rɪˌgjuːlˈeɪʃn/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **A rule or set of rules**: A system of guidelines or instructions that control or organize behavior, especially in a particular area or context. For example: "The new regulations on emissions will help reduce pollution."
2. **Management or administration**: The process of organizing and overseeing a system or activity. For example: "The company's regulations are strict to ensure productivity."
3. **Adaptation to a particular environment**: The adjustment of living organisms to their surroundings. For example: "Species have evolved different regulations to survive in different climates."

### Verb
1. To establish or enforce rules: To make sure something is done according to specific guidelines or requirements. For example: "The government is regulating the use of cryptocurrencies."
2. To control or manage: To oversee and maintain order in a process or situation. For example: "The teacher regulated the class's behavior during the exam."

## Phrases
- "Comply with regulations" means to follow the rules or guidelines.
- "Lack of regulation" refers to a situation where there are no clear rules or oversight.
- "Financial regulations" are the rules governing financial institutions and transactions.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The new health and safety regulations were implemented last month."
- **Verb**: "The government is working on stricter regulations for the tech industry."
- **Adjective**: "The garden thrives under the careful regulation of its owner."
