## Pronunciation
/ˈsemɪstər/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **semester** is a unit of time typically used in educational institutions, especially universities, to divide the academic year into two equal or nearly equal parts. It usually lasts for 15 weeks or approximately half a school year.

### Verb
To **semester** can be used metaphorically to refer to the division or allocation of time into semesters or to describe the process of following a semester system.

## Phrases
- "Complete a semester of study"
- "Take a course for a semester"
- "Switch to a different semester schedule"

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Students typically enroll in four courses per semester to maintain a full load."
- **Verb**: "After finishing her summer break, she began her new semester with enthusiasm."
- **Metaphorical usage**: "The company decided to semester their project into two phases for better management."
