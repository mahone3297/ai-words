## Pronunciation
/tɛˈdiəs/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Characterized by boredom, monotony, or dullness; tiresome or uninteresting. For example: "The lecture was tediously long."
- **Adverb**: In a tedious manner; slowly and monotonously. For example: "She worked tediously on the project all day."

## Phrases
- "A tedious task" refers to something that is very boring or time-consuming.
- "Endless tedium" describes a situation without any excitement or variety.
- "To endure tediously" means to tolerate something with great patience, but not necessarily willingly.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The book's plot was tediously predictable."
- **Adverb**: "He read the instructions tediously, line by line."
- "She had to tediously sort through piles of paperwork."
