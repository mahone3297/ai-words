## Word: Ladder

## Pronunciation
/ˈlædər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A long, rigid structure with two sides and multiple steps or rungs, designed for climbing up or down. For example: "He leaned the ladder against the wall to fix the roof."
    - _Also used metaphorically_ (e.g., career ladder): A series of steps or stages through which one progresses toward a goal.
- **Verb**: To climb up or down something using a ladder. For example: "She laddered up to reach the top shelf."
    - _Metaphorically_ (e.g., ladder one's way to success): To advance or progress steadily through a series of stages.

## Phrases
- "Climb the ladder of success": Advance in one's career or achieve higher social status.
- "Step up the ladder": To progress or move towards a higher position or level.
- "Ladder of opportunity": Refers to a series of opportunities that allow someone to advance in life.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The fireman carried a long ladder to rescue the cat from the tree."
- **Verb**: "He laddered up to paint the ceiling of his bedroom."

## Synonyms
- Steps
- Stairway
- Ascender
- Climbing frame (in some contexts)
- Rungs (referring specifically to the horizontal bars on a ladder)

- Pathway to success
