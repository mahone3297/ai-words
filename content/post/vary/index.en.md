## Pronunciation
/vəˈraɪ/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The act or quality of being different or varying from one thing to another; diversity.
   - "The weather can vary greatly depending on the season."
2. A range or spectrum of something, often values or quantities.
   - "The prices of houses vary widely in this neighborhood."

### Verb
1. To change or differ in nature, degree, or amount; have different forms or aspects.
   - "The color of the leaves varies from green to red during autumn."
2. To present different versions or alternatives.
   - "We'll need to vary our approach based on the audience."

### Adjective
1. Characterized by or showing variation or diversity.
   - "A diverse range of opinions were expressed."
2. Capable of changing or adapting.
   - "A flexible schedule that can vary easily."

### Adverb
1. In a different manner or to different degrees; variously.
   - "Their styles of cooking vary significantly."

## Phrases
- "Vary in" indicates differences within a group.
- "Vary from...to..." describes a range or progression.
- "Varying conditions" implies conditions that change.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The students' abilities vary greatly, so the teacher adjusts the lesson plans accordingly."
- **Verb**: "The artist's work varies in style from one painting to the next."
- **Adjective**: "Her moods can be quite variable, making it hard to predict her reaction."
- **Adverb**: "The temperature will vary throughout the day, so bring layers."
