## Word: Night

## Pronunciation
/naɪt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The period of darkness between sunset and sunrise, especially when this period is considered as the time for sleeping, resting, or nightlife. For example: "The stars shine bright in the night sky."
- **Noun (figurative)**: A long period of time characterized by a specific condition or situation. For instance: "After years of hard work, his troubles finally came to an end, and he saw the light at the end of the night."

### Phrases
- "At night": During the hours of darkness. Example: "The streets are quiet at night."
- "In the dead of night": Late at night, especially when most people are asleep. Example: "The thieves broke in during the dead of night."
- "A night out on the town": An evening spent socializing or enjoying entertainment away from home. Example: "We're planning a night out on the town to celebrate our anniversary."
- "Night owl": A person who tends to stay up late and is active during the nighttime. Example: "She's a real night owl; she does her best work after midnight."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The nocturnal animals come out to hunt their prey during the night."
- **Noun (figurative)**: "That difficult period felt like a never-ending night before we found a solution."
