## Pronunciation
/pɪɡ/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A domesticated mammal (Sus scrofa) of the pig family, often raised for its meat, which has a thick, hairless body, a snout, and a rooting mouth.
   - Example: "The farmer was feeding the pigs in the pen."
   - Synonyms: hog, sow, boar (male pig), piglet (young pig)

### Verb
- To eat or consume food in large quantities, often in an excessive or greedy manner.
   - Example: "She pigged out on the buffet, trying every dish."
   - Synonym: overindulge

### Adjective
- Describing someone who is very fat or overweight.
   - Example: "The piggy child begged for another slice of cake."
   - Synonym: chubby

### Adjective (informal)
- Used to refer to someone with a strong sense of loyalty or affection, especially towards a person or activity.
   - Example: "He's such a piggy fan of football, he attends every match."
   - Synonym: devoted

## Phrases
- "Pig out" is a colloquial expression for indulging in a lot of food.
- "He's a bit of a porker" is an informal way of describing someone who is quite heavy.
- "She's a right little piglet" is a term of endearment for a small child.
