## Pronunciation
/siːˈmən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A person who works on a ship, especially one who is not an officer. Also used more generally to refer to a sailor. ["A seasoned seaman with years of experience."]

- **Verb**: To navigate or work on a ship, often in a professional capacity. ["The young apprentice was learning to seaman under the watchful eye of the captain."]

## Phrases
- "Be a good seaman" means to be skilled and competent at sea.
- "To man the helm as a seaman" signifies taking control of a ship's steering.
- "Seamanlike qualities" describe traits of being reliable and capable at sea.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The crew consisted of experienced seamen and a few novice sailors."
- **Verb**: "After completing his training, he became a certified seaman and joined a merchant fleet."
- **Adjective**: "The seaman's life can be both adventurous and challenging."

## Synonyms
- Sailor
- Mariner
- Deckhand
- Seafarer
- Oceanic worker

## Antonyms
- Landlubber (a person unused to or unskilled at sea)
