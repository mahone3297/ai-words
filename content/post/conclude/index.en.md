## Pronunciation
/kənˈkluːd/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The act or process of reaching a conclusion; final determination or assessment. [Oxford](https://en.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/conclusion)
2. A summary or final statement, especially one drawn from evidence or arguments. [Merriam-Webster](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/conclusion)

### Verb
1. To come to a decision or form an opinion based on available information. [Cambridge](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/conclude)
2. To end or finish something, typically after a series of events or discussions. [Macmillan](https://www.macmillandictionary.com/dictionary/british/conclude)

### Adjective
1. Characterizing or denoting something that is reached or arrived at as a result of reasoning or investigation. [Collins](https://www.collinsdictionary.com/dictionary/english/conclusive)

## Phrases
- "Draw a conclusion" means to form an opinion or judgment based on the evidence.
- "In conclusion" is used to introduce a summary or final thoughts at the end of a speech or argument.
- "Without further conclusion" implies that a matter has been settled or no additional action will be taken.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After analyzing the data, we drew a clear conclusion about the cause of the problem."
- **Verb**: "The jury concluded that the defendant was guilty of murder."
- **Adjective**: "The study's findings were conclusive, supporting our hypothesis."
