## Word: Hurry

## Pronunciation
/ˈhʌri/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A state of rushed movement or action; haste. For example: "We're in a great hurry to catch the train."
- **Verb**: (1) To act or move quickly due to urgency or impatience. For example: "Hurry up, we're going to be late!"
   (2) To cause someone or something to move or act with speed. For example: "I had to hurry the kids along so they wouldn't miss the bus."

## Phrases
- "In a hurry": To be moving or acting quickly because one is pressed for time. Example: "She was in such a hurry that she forgot her keys."
- "Hurry along": To encourage someone to move faster or progress quicker. Example: "Let's hurry along, the movie starts in ten minutes."
- "Make haste"/"Hurry up": An expression urging someone to speed up their actions. Example: "Hurry up, the sale ends soon!"

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The chef worked in a hurry to get all the orders out on time."
- **Verb**: "I hurried to finish my homework before my parents came home."
