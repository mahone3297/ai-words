## Pronunciation
/rɪˈtɜːr/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A person who produces written works, especially books or articles, professionally or as a hobby. ["She is a talented writer who pens thrilling novels."]
2. An author; a composer of written works. ["John is a renowned writer of historical fiction."]
3. A creator or contributor of written content for various media, such as journalism or advertising. ["The freelance writer delivered the article on time.]

### Verb
1. To produce written works, especially as a profession or creative endeavor. ["She spends her days writing articles for the magazine."]
2. To compose or create written content, as in a story or essay. ["He was writing a new novel that year."]

### Adjective
1. Characteristic of or involving writing; literary. ["The writer's style was praised by critics."]
2. Engaged in writing or skilled in written expression. ["She is a writer with a unique voice."]

## Phrases
- "Write a book" or "write an article"
- "Be a writer" or "become a professional writer"
- "Writer's block" (a temporary inability to write)

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The writer's latest novel hit the bestseller list."
- **Verb**: "She decided to become a writer after graduating from college."
- **Adjective**: "The writer's descriptive prose brought the story to life."
