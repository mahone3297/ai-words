## Pronunciation
/ˈmɪnɪt/

## Definitions
- **Noun** (n): 
  - A unit of time equal to one sixtieth of an hour or 60 seconds. Example: "The meeting lasted for half an hour, give or take a few minutes."
  - A written or printed record of a meeting or proceeding, typically one made at a formal assembly. Example: "The secretary read out the minutes from the previous meeting."
  - A very short period of time; an instant. Example: "I'll be back in a minute."
  - (in sports) A point scored in certain games, such as football or basketball. Example: "They scored two goals in the final minute of the game."

- **Adjective** (adj): Extremely small; tiny; minuscule. Example: "Minute details can often make a significant difference."

## Phrases
- "To take minutes" means to write down or record the proceedings of a meeting.
- "In a minute" implies something will happen soon or very quickly.
- "Every minute counts" suggests that time is precious and should not be wasted.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (time)**: "Please wait for just a minute while I check my schedule."
- **Noun (record)**: "She was tasked with taking the minutes during the board meeting."
- **Adjective**: "Under the microscope, we could see minute organisms swimming in the water."

## Synonyms
- (noun - time unit) Second, instant, moment
- (adjective - extremely small) Tiny, minuscule, infinitesimal, microscopic
