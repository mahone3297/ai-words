## Pronunciation
/ˈlɒdʒɪkəl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Relating to or based on logic; having a clear, sound, and rational reasoning. For example: "A logical argument requires premises that lead inevitably to a valid conclusion."
  - _Example Sentence_: "Her decision to study computer science was a logical choice given her interest in math and problem-solving."

- **Noun**: (plural: logicians) A person skilled in or studying logic. For instance: "As a professional logician, he specializes in identifying fallacies in arguments."

## Phrases
- "Logical consequence": The relationship between statements where if one statement logically entails another, then the second must be true whenever the first is.
- "Logical thinking": The ability to reason, analyze facts, and draw conclusions based on evidence and sound judgment.
- "Logical fallacy": An error in reasoning that can make an argument invalid, even if it seems convincing.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "It's not logical to assume that just because it's raining today, it will rain tomorrow."
- **Noun**: "The logician presented a series of complex syllogisms to illustrate his point."
