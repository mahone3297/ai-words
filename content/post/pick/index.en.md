## Pronunciation
/pɪk/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A verb, often with a direct object, meaning to select or choose from a group. For example: "She picked a ripe apple from the tree."
- In baseball, the act of catching a thrown ball before it hits the ground. For example: "The shortstop made a great pick to save the run."
- A small piece or quantity taken or chosen. For example: "A single pick of tobacco leaves."

### Verb
- To lift or remove something by using a tool, especially a pointed instrument. For example: "He picked up the screwdriver from the floor."
- To choose or select someone or something. For example: "She picked the best candidate for the job."
- To pluck or gather (flowers, fruit, etc.). For example: "The children picked wildflowers along the path."

### Adjective
- Characterized by or involving choice or selection. For example: "The picky eater only ordered salad."

### Adverb
- Skillfully or carefully. For example: "He picked the lock with ease."

## Phrases
- "Pick out" means to choose or select something from a group.
- "Pick up" can mean to learn or acquire a skill, or to physically retrieve something.
- "Pick oneself up" is a phrase used to describe getting back on one's feet after a setback.
