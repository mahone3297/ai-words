## Pronunciation
/ˈɪmpɔːrt/

## Definitions
### Noun (n)
- The action or process of bringing goods or products into one country from another country for sale, use, or further manufacturing.
  - Example: "The company's main business involves the import of electronics from Asia."
- The thing that is brought into a country from abroad; an imported item.
  - Example: "Imports include luxury cars and fine wines."
- Importance or significance, especially in terms of ideas or information.
  - Example: "The import of his speech was clear: we must act now."

### Verb (v)
- To bring goods or products into a country from another country for trade or personal use.
  - Example: "We import raw materials to manufacture our products."
- To convey meaning or significance.
  - Example: "Her words imported a sense of urgency."

## Phrases
- "Import duty/tax" refers to taxes levied on imported goods.
- "Import restrictions" are limitations placed by a country on what can be brought in from other countries.
- "Importance of something" refers to its value, significance, or relevance.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The rise in imports has affected local production levels."
- **Verb**: "They decided to import high-quality fabrics from Italy for their fashion line."
