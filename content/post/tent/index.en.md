## Pronunciation
/tɛnt/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **tent** (noun) is a portable structure made of fabric or other material, typically with poles and guy ropes, designed to be used as temporary shelter, especially outdoors camping or for events.

### Verb
To **tent** (verb) can mean to set up a tent, but it can also imply to provide temporary shelter or protection for something, like "to tent a wounded animal."

### Adjective
In some cases, "tent-like" can be used as an adjective to describe something having the appearance or shape of a tent.

## Phrases
- "Pitch a tent" means to set up a tent.
- "Tent city" refers to a large temporary settlement of tents, often used to describe disaster relief areas or music festivals.
- "Under the tent" can signify a private or informal gathering.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The campers had set up their tents near the lake."
- **Verb**: "We'll need to tent the injured hiker until help arrives."
- **Adjective**: "The tent-like shelter provided some relief from the rain."

## Synonyms
- Camper's shelter
- Quonset hut
- Tarpaulin
- Pavilion
- marquees (plural, informal)

Note: The verb "tent" is less common than "set up" or "erect" when referring to physically putting up a tent, but it can still be used in certain contexts.
