## Pronunciation
/ˈpækɪdʒ/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A container or wrapper made of paper, cardboard, plastic, or other material in which goods are packaged for storage, transport, sale, or delivery. For example: "She ordered several packages from the online store."
- **Noun (n)**: A set of things wrapped or boxed together; a collection of related items or services offered as a single unit. For instance: "The holiday package includes flights, accommodation, and meals."
- **Noun (n)**: A bundle of software programs or features sold as one entity. E.g., "The operating system comes with a productivity package."
- **Verb (v)**: To put something into a package or wrap it up for storage, transportation, or sale. Example: "She carefully packaged the fragile vase in bubble wrap."
- **Verb (v)**: To offer or present something as a combined or organized set. Example: "The company packages its products to appeal to different market segments."

## Phrases
- "In the package" – referring to something being included within a group or set.
- "Under the package deal" – when multiple items or services are provided together at a discounted price.
- "Think outside the box/package" – a metaphorical expression meaning to consider new or unconventional ideas.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The courier delivered a large package this morning."
- **Verb**: "They had to package the food in vacuum-sealed bags to maintain freshness."
- **As a marketing concept**: "The telecommunications company offers a comprehensive package that combines TV, internet, and phone services."
