## Pronunciation
/ˈrəʊd/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. A strip of ground, paved or unpaved, designed for the movement of vehicles, pedestrians, or animals, typically connecting two places. 2. (informal) A way or method; a means: "This is one road to success." 3. (figurative) A path or course of action: "Life is a long road."
- **Verb**: To travel along a road or path; go by road: "We decided to road to the beach."
- **Noun** (countable): An individual route or segment of a road: "There are several roads leading to the mountain."

## Phrases
- "Take the road less traveled" implies choosing a unique or unconventional path.
- "Highway" or "freeway" can be used to refer to a major road with multiple lanes.
- "Road trip" denotes a journey made primarily by car or other vehicle on a series of roads.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The winding road through the countryside was breathtaking."
- **Verb**: "We road home after a long day at work."
- **Noun (countable)**: "The driver took a detour on a dirt road to avoid the traffic."
