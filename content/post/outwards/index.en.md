## Word: Outwards

## Pronunciation
/ˌaʊtˈwərdz/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: In a direction away from the center or inside; towards the outside. It can also indicate movement or extension to the exterior of something. For example: "The petals of the flower opened outwards."
- **Adjective**: Directed or facing towards the outside. For example: "The windows on the house open outwards."

### Adverbial Usage Examples
- The explosion propelled debris outwards.
- She turned her gaze outwards, looking towards the horizon.
- The force of the wind pushed the door outwards.

### Adjectival Usage Examples
- The balcony had a railing that extended outwards.
- Make sure the arrow points outwards when you place the sign.

## Phrases
- "Move outwards" - To go or extend further away from the center.
- "Radiate outwards" - To spread or extend in all directions from a central point.
- "Expand outwards" - To grow or increase in size by moving beyond current boundaries.

## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "As the balloon filled with air, it expanded outwards."
- **Adjective**: "The branches of the tree grew outwards, providing shade for the garden."

## Synonyms
- Outwardly (adv.)
- Away from the center
- Exteriorly
- Externally
- Towards the outside
