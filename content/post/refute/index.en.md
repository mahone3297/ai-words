## Pronunciation
/refjuːt/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To disprove or contradict; to show that something is false or incorrect. [TFD](https://www.thefreedictionary.com/refute)
- **Noun**: The act of refuting; the process of disproving or disproving a claim or argument. [Collins](https://www.collinsdictionary.com/us/dictionary/english/refutation)

## Phrases
- "Refute a claim" means to provide evidence against its truth.
- "Refute someone's argument" means to disprove their reasoning.
- "To refute popular beliefs" is to challenge commonly held opinions.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The scientist was able to refute the hypothesis with experimental data."
- **Noun**: "His arguments were thoroughly refuted during the debate."
- **Infinitive**: "It's important to carefully consider evidence before attempting to refute someone's point."

## Usage
In a sentence, "refute" is often followed by a claim, argument, or theory, while "disprove" and "debunk" are similar but may imply a more sensational or debunking nature. For example:

- "The historian successfully refuted the widely accepted theory."
- "Scientists worked tirelessly to disprove the existence of a link between vaccines and autism."
- "Many conspiracy theories have been debunked by researchers."
