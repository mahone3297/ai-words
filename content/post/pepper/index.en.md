## Pronunciation
/pəˈpɛr/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A plant** ( Piper nigrum): A flowering herb with long, slender stems and fruit that contains small, peppery seeds. Often used as a spice or condiment.
- **Ground pepper**: The dried, crushed seeds from the pepper plant, used to add flavor and heat to food.
- **Peppercorn**: One of the peppery seeds, usually black, white, or red.

### Verb
- To season with pepper: To add ground pepper to food while cooking or eating.
- To pepper someone (with questions): To ask someone a series of questions rapidly.

### Adjective
- Spicy: Referring to the pungent taste of pepper.
- Coarsely ground: Describing pepper that has not been finely crushed.

### Adverb
- Pepperily: In a manner similar to pepper; with a peppery flavor.

## Phrases
- "Add some pepper to your taste": Suggesting to increase the seasoning with pepper.
- "Peppered steak": A dish of cooked steak seasoned with pepper.
- "Black pepper tree": Referring to the pepper plant.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She ground some fresh pepper onto her salad dressing."
- **Verb**: "He peppered the meat with spices before grilling it."
- **Adjective**: "The dish had a spicy kick from the generous amount of red pepper flakes."
- **Adverb**: "She seasoned the soup pepperily to enhance its flavor."

## Synonyms
- Cayenne
- Chili powder (for ground pepper)
- Paprika (for a type of sweet pepper)
- Hot sauce (for a spicy pepper flavor)
- Ground cloves (a similar spice)
