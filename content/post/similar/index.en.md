## Pronunciation
/sɪmələr/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Adjective**: Having qualities or features that are alike or nearly the same; resembling. For example: "The two paintings are similar in style."
2. **Adjective**: Comparable or corresponding in some respect; corresponding. For example: "Their plans were similar in scope."

### Verb
1. To be or make something resemble another thing or person. For example: "The new design is meant to be similar to the original."
2. To have a resemblance or likeness. For example: "Their voices sounded similar on the phone."

### Adverb
1. In a similar manner or way. For example: "They approached the problem similarly."

## Phrases
- "Be similar to" indicates a comparison between things.
- "Not identical, but similar" describes a distinction with some similarities.
- "She's very similar to her sister" highlights a familial resemblance.
