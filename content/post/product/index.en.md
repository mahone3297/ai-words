## Pronunciation
/prəˈdʌkt/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Product**: A tangible or intangible item resulting from a manufacturing, creation, or process, often sold for commercial use or consumption. For example: "The company's latest product is a state-of-the-art smartphone."
2. **Outcome**: The result of a process or effort, especially in terms of quality or success. For example: "The project's success was measured by its end product."
3. **Artifact**: An object made or produced by human activity. For example: "Archaeologists study ancient artifacts to learn about past civilizations."

### Verb
1. To create or bring forth a finished item or outcome. For example: "The engineers worked tirelessly to product a new car model."
2. To generate or contribute to a result or effect. For example: "Her marketing strategy really products sales growth."

## Phrases
- "A product of their environment" refers to something shaped by the conditions in which it was created.
- "High-quality products" are those that meet or exceed expectations in terms of performance or durability.
- "The end product" is the final result or outcome of a process.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The factory produces a wide variety of consumer products."
- **Verb**: "The chef's culinary skills produce amazing dishes every night."
- **Interjection**: "Wow, the company's product line has really taken off!"
