## Pronunciation
/rəʊˈæktɪv/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Characterized by the emission of radiation, especially as a result of nuclear decay or certain types of atomic reactions. For example: "The sample was highly radioactive."
- **Noun**: The process or property of emitting radiation, often harmful to living organisms. For example: "The radioactive waste needed careful disposal."
- **Verb**: To become contaminated or exposed to radioactive substances. For example: "The workers were regularly monitored for radioactivity."

## Phrases
- "Radioactive decay" describes the breakdown of atomic nuclei.
- "Radioactive shielding" is used to protect against radiation exposure.
- "Highly radioactive" refers to a substance with a strong radiation emission rate.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The area around the nuclear reactor was marked as highly radioactive."
- **Noun**: "The scientists studied the long-term effects of radioactive fallout."
- **Verb**: "The miners had to take precautions after being exposed to radioactivity."
