## Pronunciation
/ˈtrænsmɪt/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (transmit) 
  - **Noun**: The act of conveying information, energy, or signals from one place or medium to another. For example: "Radio stations transmit music to listeners."
  - **Adjective**: Characterized by transmission; transmitting. For example: "The wireless signal has a high-transmit power."

## Phrases
- "Transmit a message" means to send a communication.
- "Transmit data" refers to sending digital information.
- "Transmit electricity" is the process of transmitting electrical energy.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The satellite transmits live television signals to homes across the country."
- **Noun**: "The radio tower transmits a strong signal throughout the city."
- **Adjective**: "The new router has a high-transmit speed, allowing for faster internet connection."

## Synonyms
- Transmit (no synonyms, as it is a technical term)
- Broadcast
- Send
- Convey
- Pass (information)
- Transmit (noun) can also be replaced with "signal" or "broadcast"

## Antonyms
- Receive
- Block
- Interrupt (in the context of preventing transmission)
