## Pronunciation
/net/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A meshed fabric made of threads, cords, or ropes, often used for catching fish or birds, or for sports like tennis or badminton. Example: "The fisherman cast his net into the sea."
- **Noun (n)**: In computing, a network of computers or devices connected together for communication and data exchange. Example: "The company's intranet is accessible only to employees."
- **Noun (n)**: The total amount after all deductions have been subtracted; profit or loss. Example: "Their net income rose by 10% last year."
- **Verb (v)**: To catch or capture with a net. Example: "They successfully netted several fish from the river."
- **Verb (v)**: To gain or achieve after accounting for all costs, deductions, or losses. Example: "After expenses, they netted a profit of $5000."
- **Adjective (adj)**: Remaining after deductions; final or total. Example: "The net weight of the product does not include packaging."

## Phrases
- "Cast the net wide" means to search or consider many possibilities.
- "Cut the net" is a metaphorical expression used when something disrupts or terminates a system or process, especially a computer network.
- "In the black/net" refers to being financially profitable.
