## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈsɜːrt/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To place or put something into a position within something else; often used when referring to text, images, or data in documents or digital media. For example: "I need to insert the missing paragraph into the report."
  - *Also*: To introduce or add a person or thing into a situation or group. "The new employee was smoothly inserted into the team."
  
- **Noun**: (n) Something that is placed or inserted, especially a piece or part added to complete or supplement something else. For example: "An insert for a CD case."

## Phrases
- "Insert something into something": This phrase is used when talking about physically placing one object inside another or adding content to a document. Example: "Please insert the disk into the drive."
- "Insert yourself into a conversation": This means to participate or join in a discussion that was already happening. Example: "Don't hesitate to insert yourself into the debate if you have something valuable to contribute."

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "She carefully inserted the key into the lock."
- "After editing, he inserted the revised data into the spreadsheet."
- **Noun**: "The advertisement was printed as a paper insert in the magazine."
