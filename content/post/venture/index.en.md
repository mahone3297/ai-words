## Pronunciation
/ˈvɛn.tʃə(r)/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **venture** can refer to:
1. An undertaking, especially one that involves risk and financial investment, with the aim of profit or success: "He embarked on a business venture to start his own tech company."
2. A bold or daring attempt: "Her artistic endeavors were a creative venture."
3. A journey or expedition: "Their hiking venture took them through rugged terrain."

### Verb
To **venture** (as in "venture out" or "venture into"):
1. To go or do something, often with some level of risk or uncertainty: "They ventured into unknown territory in search of adventure."
2. To embark on a new project or activity: "He ventured into writing after years of working in finance."

### Adjective
- Adventurous: "He's a person who loves to take risks and go on ventures."
- Risky: "The startup venture had many uncertain aspects."

## Phrases
- "Venture into" suggests starting a new activity or exploring something unfamiliar.
- "Risk-taking venture" describes a daring or potentially risky project.
- "A venture capital firm" invests in new or growing businesses.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After months of planning, he finally launched his entrepreneurial venture."
- **Verb**: "The team decided to venture out into the wilderness for their annual retreat."
- **Adjective**: "Starting a new business is a bold and potentially risky venture."
