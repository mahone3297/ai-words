## Pronunciation
/ˈpænəl/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A group of people gathered together to discuss, make decisions, or give their opinions on a particular subject, often in an official or public capacity. For example: "The expert panel discussed the latest findings on climate change."
- **Noun**: A flat, usually rectangular piece of wood, metal, or another material, often used as part of a wall, door, or fence, or forming the face of a control board or display screen. For example: "The artist painted a beautiful mural on the wooden panel."
- **Noun**: In a courtroom, it refers to the bench where the judge and sometimes jurors sit. For example: "The defendant approached the witness stand and swore to tell the truth before the panel."
- **Verb**: (transitive) To fit with panels or to form into panels. For example: "The architect designed the building to be panelled with oak."

## Phrases
- "Panel discussion": An organized conversation where a group of experts or individuals with diverse perspectives exchange views on a specific topic.
- "Panel interview": A type of job interview where the candidate is questioned by a group of people representing different departments or areas of expertise.
- "Control panel": A collection of switches, buttons, and indicators that allow a user to control a machine or system.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (group of people)**: "The government convened a panel of scientists to advise on vaccine policy."
- **Noun (flat piece of material)**: "The interior of the car was finished with leather panels."
- **Noun (courtroom)**: "The judge presided over the case from behind the raised panel."
- **Verb**: "They decided to panel the walls with dark wood to create a cozy atmosphere."

## Synonyms
- Board (for a group of people)
- Panelists (for members of a panel)
- Section (for a physical panel)
- Plank (for a single piece of a wooden panel)
- Jury (in the context of a legal panel)
- Fit with boards (as a verb)
