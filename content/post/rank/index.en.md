## Pronunciation
/ræŋk/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Rank** (noun): The position or order of importance, value, or hierarchy within a series, list, or group. For example: "In the army, he held the rank of captain."
2. **Ranking** (noun): The system or process of assigning positions based on a particular criterion. For example: "The universities are ranked according to their academic reputation."
3. **Social rank** (noun): The position or status in a social hierarchy. For example: "Her family's high social rank allowed her access to exclusive events."

### Verb
1. **To rank** (verb): To place or assign a position or value to something in comparison with others. For example: "The company ranks its employees based on their performance."
2. **To hold rank** (verb): To have a specific position or rank in an organization or group. For example: "He holds the rank of lieutenant in the navy."

## Phrases
- "Promote to a higher rank" means to give someone a better position or title.
- "Maintain rank" implies staying in the same position without any changes.
- "Ranks from bottom to top" refers to a hierarchical structure with various levels.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "He was proud of his new rank in the police force."
- **Verb**: "After several years of hard work, she finally ranked first in her class."
- **Interjection**: "The team's rank slipped after losing several games in a row."
