## Pronunciation
/rɪˈleɪʃnʃɪp/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **1.** A **connection or association** between people, things, or concepts, often based on shared characteristics, feelings, or experiences. Example: "Their relationship as siblings was strong."
- **2.** The **state or quality of being related** to someone or something else. Example: "The company's relationship with its customers is crucial for success."
- **3.** A **degree of intimacy or closeness** in a personal connection. Example: "They have a close relationship after years of working together."

### Verb
- **To establish, maintain, or understand the nature of a relationship between individuals or entities.** Example: "She is trying to improve her relationship with her coworkers."
- **To perceive or regard something as related or connected.** Example: "He related the story to the theme of the book."

### Adjective
- **Characterizing a connection or bond between people or things.** Example: "A strong familial relationship."
- **Showing a sense of connection or familiarity.** Example: "Her relationship with nature is evident in her art."

## Phrases
- "Maintain a good relationship" means to keep a positive connection with someone.
- "End a toxic relationship" refers to severing a harmful connection.
- "Relationship dynamics" describes the patterns and interactions within a relationship.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Their professional relationship blossomed over time."
- **Verb**: "She tried to reestablish a relationship with her estranged brother."
- **Adjective**: "The apartment had a cozy relationship with the sunlight."
