## Pronunciation
/səˈkreɪd/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Sacred** (adj): Holy or set apart for special religious or spiritual significance; devoted to a deity or treated with great reverence.
   - Example: The temple was filled with sacred relics from ancient times.
   - Phrase: A sacred place

2. **Sacredness** (n): The quality or state of being sacred; the quality of being holy or revered.
   - Example: The sacredness of nature is often emphasized in spiritual beliefs.

### Verb
1. To **sacralize** (v): To make something sacred or give it religious or spiritual importance.
   - Example: The community decided to sacralize the annual festival.

### Adjective
1. **Sacredly** (adv): In a sacred manner; with deep respect or reverence.
   - Example: She performed the ritual sacredly, following the ancient traditions.

## Phrases
- "Keep it sacred" means to maintain something as private or confidential.
- "Break a sacred trust" refers to violating a deeply held belief or confidence.
- "A sacred bond" symbolizes a strong emotional connection between people.

## Example Sentences
