## Pronunciation
/shed/

## Definitions
### Noun
A shed is a small, simple building, usually made of wood or other materials, typically used for storage, workshops, or as a shelter for animals, especially for livestock or garden tools.

### Verb (transitive)
To shed (something) means to get rid of or dispose of, often referring to the removal of an outer layer, like peeling paint or skin, or to lose or drop something unintentionally, as in "shed tears" or "shed old habits."

### Verb (intransitive)
To shed light on a situation means to reveal or explain something that was previously unclear.

## Phrases
- "Shed some light" implies providing clarification or insight.
- "Shedding a tear" signifies expressing sadness or emotion through tears.
- "Shedding old skin" is a metaphor for personal growth and change.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The farmer built a new shed for storing hay."
- **Verb (transitive)**: "She peeled off her old jacket, ready to shed her winter coat."
- **Verb (intransitive)**: "His explanation shed light on the mystery."
