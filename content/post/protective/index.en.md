## Pronunciation
/prəˈtɛktɪv/

## Definitions
### Noun
A protective noun refers to something that serves to guard, shield, or preserve. It can be a physical object, action, or attitude designed to prevent harm or damage. For example: "The protective barrier prevented the intruders from entering."

### Adjective
1. **Describing an action**: Intended to keep someone or something safe from harm or danger. Example: "The protective measures taken by parents for their children's safety."
2. **Characterizing a characteristic**: Marking a quality that guards against harm or injury. Example: "Her protective clothing saved her from burns."

### Adverb
In this context, "protectively" describes how something is done with the intention of guarding or preserving. Example: "He held the baby protectively in his arms."

## Phrases
- "Provide protective cover" means to give shelter or defense.
- "A protective instinct" refers to a natural tendency to safeguard oneself or others.
- "Protective gear" includes equipment worn to prevent injury.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The protective umbrella provided shade from the rain."
- **Adjective**: "She felt safe under his protective gaze."
- **Adverb**: "He drove protectively around the corners."
