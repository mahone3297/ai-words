## Word: Modern

## Pronunciation
/ˈmɑːdərn/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Relating to the present time or recent times as opposed to the remote past; contemporary. For example: "Modern technology has transformed the way we communicate."
  - *Architecture*: Characterized by simplicity and functionalism, often employing new materials and construction techniques that distinguish it from earlier styles. Example: "The house boasts sleek, modern design."
  - *Art*: Of or relating to recent artistic styles or movements that break with tradition. Example: "Modern art often challenges traditional aesthetics."

**Noun**
- (usu. 'the modern') A person who lives in the present time and has a contemporary outlook. Example: "She's very much a modern woman, embracing both career and family life."

## Phrases
- "Modern era": The period of history that is considered recent in comparison to what came before.
- "Modern society": The current social structure and norms of a community or nation.
- "Modernize": To bring something up to date, make it conform to contemporary standards.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "This city has undergone rapid modernization over the last decade."
- "Their furniture is a blend of traditional and modern styles."
- **Noun**: "The novel explores the struggles of the modern balancing work, relationships, and personal growth."
