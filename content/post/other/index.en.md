## Word: Other

## Pronunciation
/ˈʌðər/

## Definitions
- **Determiner**: Used before a noun to refer to additional people or things that are distinct from those already mentioned or known about. For example: "I have two dogs; the other is white."
- **Adjective**: Belonging to or associated with another person or thing. For example: "She has a different opinion; it's the other point of view."
- **Pronoun**: Used to refer to a person or thing that is different from one already mentioned or known about. For example: "This dress is too small; I'll try on the other one."

## Phrases
- "The other side": Refers to the opposing or alternative aspect of something. Example: "There are two sides to every story; let's hear the other side."
- "On the other hand": Introduces an alternative or contrasting viewpoint. Example: "He's very intelligent, but on the other hand, he can be quite forgetful."
- "Each other": Refers to mutual action between two people or things. Example: "They looked at each other in surprise."

## Example Sentences
- **Determiner**: "We have two options; the other requires more funding."
- **Adjective**: "That's not my car; mine is the other red one."
- **Pronoun**: "Jack and Jill dislike each other."
