## Word: Odour

## Pronunciation
/ˈoʊ.dər/ (British English) /ˈoʊ.dər/ (American English)

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A distinctive smell, especially one that is unpleasant or strong. For example: "The pungent odour of onions filled the kitchen."
  - *Formal synonym*: scent, fragrance, aroma
  - *Informal synonym*: stench, stink, whiff
- **Verb**: (rarer usage) To emit or give off an odour; to have a particular smell. For example: "The flowers odoured sweetly in the spring air."

## Phrases
- "A bad odour": Refers to something that has an unpleasant smell or metaphorically to a situation with negative implications.
- "An odour of sanctity": A figurative expression describing a person who seems to have a morally or spiritually pure aura about them.
  
## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The distinct odour of fresh coffee wafted through the house, waking everyone up."
- **Verb**: "The damp carpet began to odour after the flood, signaling the need for replacement."

## Synonyms
- Scent
- Fragrance
- Aroma
- Smell
- Stench (for unpleasant odours)
- Perfume (for pleasant odours)
- Bouquet (typically for pleasant fragrances from wine, flowers, etc.)
- Whiff (a brief indication of an odour)
