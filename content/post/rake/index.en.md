## Pronunciation
/rɪkeɪk/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **rake** is a gardening tool with a blade attached to a long handle, used for moving soil, leaves, and other debris. It can also refer to the act of scoring runs in cricket.

### Verb
To **rake** (v) means to sweep or gather dirt, leaves, or grass with a rake, or metaphorically to clear or organize something.

## Phrases
- "Rake through the leaves" means to carefully search or sift through something.
- "Rake up the lawn" refers to the action of cleaning the grass by removing debris.
- "Score a run with a rake" (cricket) is a specific sports term.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She was out in the garden raking up fallen leaves."
- **Verb**: "After the rain, he went outside to rake the puddles away."
- **Cricket**: "The batter managed to rake a six with a powerful swing."
