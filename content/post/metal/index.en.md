## Word: Metal

## Pronunciation
/ˈmɛtl/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A solid material that is typically hard, shiny, malleable, fusible, and ductile, with good electrical and thermal conductivity. It usually occurs as a crystalline structure and is made up of one or more metallic elements. Examples include gold, silver, iron, copper, aluminum, and steel. Example: "The necklace was made from pure gold metal."
- **Adj**: Relating to, composed of, or containing metal. Example: "A metal bucket."
- **Adj (Music)**: Referring to a genre of rock music characterized by amplified, distorted sounds and often featuring complex guitar work and aggressive rhythms. Example: "They played a heavy metal concert last night."

## Phrases
- "Metal detector": An electronic device used to detect the presence of metal nearby, often used for security or treasure hunting purposes.
- "Heavy metal": The aforementioned music genre, known for its powerful sound and sometimes dark or fantastical themes.
- "Metalanguage": In linguistics, it refers to a language used to describe another language or systems of language.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The spacecraft was built using lightweight yet strong metals."
- **Adj**: "The antique vase was adorned with intricate metalwork."
- **Adj (Music)**: "She's a big fan of 80s thrash metal bands like Metallica."
