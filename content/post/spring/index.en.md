## Pronunciation
/sprɪŋ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. The season between winter and summer, characterized by warmer weather, increased plant growth, and longer days. 2. A device or mechanism that stores energy and releases it suddenly, often causing movement or action. 3. An instance when something comes back into use or activity after a period of rest or dormancy. 4. (U.S.) A public water supply system, typically with an underground network of pipes.
- **Verb**: To come forth from a resting position, especially after a long period of dormancy. "The flowers start to spring up in the garden."
- **Adjective**: 1. Active, lively, or full of energy. "A spring in one's step." 2. Of a person, showing a sudden burst of enthusiasm or creativity. "Her ideas were filled with spring."

## Phrases
- "Spring cleaning" refers to the act of thoroughly cleaning one's home in preparation for the warmer season.
- "Spring forward" (verb) means adjusting clocks ahead when daylight saving time begins.
- "Spring into action" means to start being energetic or proactive.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After a long winter, people look forward to the first signs of spring."
- **Verb**: "The gardener carefully planted seeds, hoping they would spring to life."
- **Adjective**: "With a spring in her voice, she shared her exciting travel plans."
