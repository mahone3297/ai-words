## Pronunciation
/tɪˈmɪd/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Characterized by a lack of courage or confidence, easily frightened, or shy. For example: "The timid child was afraid to speak in front of the class."
- **Verb**: To act timidly; to be hesitant or fearful. For example: "She timidly raised her hand to ask a question."

## Phrases
- "Be timid about" means to be cautious or reserved in a particular situation.
- "Displaying timidity" shows a lack of courage or self-assurance.
- "Overcoming one's timidity" refers to gaining confidence in a challenging task.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The timid puppy cowered under the bed when it heard a loud noise."
- **Verb**: "He was too timid to share his ideas during the meeting."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "After many practice sessions, she managed to overcome her timidity on stage."

- Fearful
- Reticent
- Hesitant
- Timorous
- Cowardly (but carries a stronger negative connotation)
