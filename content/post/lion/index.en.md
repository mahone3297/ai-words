## Pronunciation
/ˈlaɪən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A large, typically tawny-yellow cat with a tufted tail and, in the male, a shaggy mane, native to Africa and southern Asia. For example: "The lion is known as the king of the jungle."

### Phrases
- "Lion's share": A substantial portion or the largest part of something. For example: "He took the lion's share of the profits."
- "Lie like a lion": To sleep soundly or for a long time. (This phrase is less commonly used.)
- "The lion's den": A situation that is difficult or dangerous, often metaphorically referring to facing one's fears or opponents directly. For example: "Appearing before the board of directors was like entering the lion's den."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The pride of lions roamed across the savannah in search of prey."
- "In many cultures, the lion symbolizes strength and courage."
- "She had a pet lion cub when she worked as a wildlife conservationist."

## Synonyms
- Leo (in astrology)
- Panthera leo (scientific name)
- King of beasts
- Big cat
- Royal Bengal tiger (a related species) 

Note: While synonyms might not be direct replacements for "lion" in all contexts due to differences in meaning and usage, they can connote similar characteristics or associations. For instance, "big cat" refers generically to large predatory cats, which includes lions, while "Royal Bengal tiger" is another large feline predator but a different species. The term "king of beasts" emphasizes the lion's symbolic status as a powerful and regal creature.
