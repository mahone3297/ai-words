## Pronunciation
/ˈlɪmɪt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The maximum amount, level, or extent allowed; a restriction on size, quantity, or time. For example: "There's a weight limit on checked luggage."
  - _Geographical sense_: The boundary or furthest point beyond which something does not extend or is not permitted. Example: "The city limits."
  - _Financial sense_: The maximum amount of money that can be borrowed or spent. Example: "We exceeded our credit card limit last month."
  
- **Verb**: To restrict or set a maximum boundary for (something), especially with regards to quantity, time, or activity. Example: "The government has limited the number of cars per household."

## Phrases
- "Push the limits" means to try to exceed what is usually possible or considered normal.
- "Within limits" suggests staying within acceptable boundaries or restrictions.
- "No limit" implies that there is no restriction or cap on something.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The company imposed strict time limits on meetings to increase efficiency."
- _As geographical noun_: "The small town was located just outside the county limits."
- _As verb_: "Her health problems have limited her ability to travel."
