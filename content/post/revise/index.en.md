## Pronunciation
/rɪˈvaɪz/

## Definitions
### Noun
A noun referring to the act of reviewing, checking, or improving something for accuracy, clarity, or correctness. For example: "The teacher assigned a revision task for the essay."

### Verb
1. **To revise** (v): The action of going over a written work, making corrections, or updating content. For example: "I need to revise my notes before the exam."
2. **To revise one's opinion** (v): Changing one's mind or viewpoint after new information or reflection. For example: "After reading the book again, she revised her initial review."
3. **To revise a schedule** (v): Altering plans or arrangements. For example: "They revised their travel plans due to the weather forecast."

### Adjective
Referring to something that has been reviewed or updated. For example: "The revised budget proposal."

## Phrases
- "Revise a document" means to edit or update a written piece.
- "Revision history" refers to the changes made to a file or text over time.
- "Self-revision" is the act of reviewing one's own work without external input.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The students spent hours revising their project for the final submission."
- **Verb**: "She revised her script multiple times before the movie's release."
- **Adjective**: "The revised tax laws have significant implications for businesses."
