# Mathematics

## Pronunciation
/ˌmæθəˈmætɪks/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The abstract study of numbers, quantity, space, structure, change, and related concepts, using a specialized system of symbols, rules, and logical reasoning. It constitutes a fundamental tool in understanding the natural world, engineering, economics, and many other fields. For example: "Mathematics is the language of science and technology."

Example sentence: _Her passion for mathematics led her to pursue a career in theoretical physics._

## Phrases
- "Pure mathematics" refers to the field that deals with abstract structures and theories without direct consideration of their applications.
- "Applied mathematics" involves mathematical methods used to solve real-world problems in science, engineering, business, and industry.
- "Mathematical proof" is a logically rigorous demonstration that some statement is necessarily true.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: _The professor's lecture on higher mathematics was challenging but ultimately enlightening._
- _In mathematics class, we're learning about complex numbers and their applications._
