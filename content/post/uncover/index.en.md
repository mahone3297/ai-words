## Pronunciation
/unˈkʌvər/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Uncover**: A verb meaning to reveal or expose something that was hidden, buried, or concealed. It can also refer to a discovery or revelation.
- **noun**: The act of revealing or exposing; discovery. For example: "The archaeologist uncovered an ancient artifact."
- **noun**: Information or knowledge that has been revealed. For example: "The truth slowly began to uncover."

### Verb
- **To uncover**: To reveal, expose, or discover something through effort or investigation. For example: "The detective uncovered new evidence in the case."
- **Uncovering**: The process of revealing or exposing something. For example: "The team is in the process of uncovering a secret underground tunnel."

### Adjective
- **Uncovered**: Not covered or protected; exposed. For example: "The garden had an uncovered area where plants grew freely."

## Phrases
- "Uncover a secret" implies revealing something that was previously unknown or hidden.
- "Uncover真相" (Discover the truth) is the Chinese equivalent.
- "Undercover work" refers to infiltrating a group or organization in secret.
