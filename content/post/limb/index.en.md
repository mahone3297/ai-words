## Pronunciation

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: One of the four main branches (an arm or a leg) extending from the human torso. For example: "She broke both her limbs in the accident."
  - Also used metaphorically to describe a branch or part of something larger: "The tree's upper limbs were heavy with fruit."
  - In a figurative sense, it can refer to a field or area of activity: "This project extends into many different limbs of technology."

  - As a part of a structure or mechanism: "The robot has several articulated limbs for movement."

- **Verb (v)**: To climb or move using one's limbs. For example: "He limbed up the tree to rescue the cat."

## Phrases
- "To be out on a limb": This phrase means to be in a precarious or unsupported position, often because of taking a risk or expressing an unpopular opinion.
- "Limb from limb": This phrase means to tear or dismember something into separate parts.
