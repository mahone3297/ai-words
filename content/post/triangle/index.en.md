## Definitions
### Noun
A triangle is a three-sided polygon with three vertices (corners) and three edges. It is one of the basic shapes in geometry and has various types, such as equilateral, isosceles, scalene, right-angled, acute-angled, and obtuse-angled.

### Verb
To describe or form a triangle is often used metaphorically, referring to creating a triangular relationship or structure.

### Adjective
A triangle can be used as an adjective to describe something having a three-part arrangement or shape.

## Phrases
- "Form a triangle" means to arrange objects or people in a triangular pattern.
- "Triangle formation" refers to a strategic position in sports or military formations.
- "A geometric triangle" specifies the mathematical shape.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The pyramid was built with large triangular stones."
- **Verb**: "They arranged the chairs in a triangle around the fire pit."
- **Adjective**: "Her laughter created a harmonious triangle with her friends' voices."
