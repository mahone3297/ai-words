## Pronunciation
/tɪl/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Till** (British) or **cash register** (American): A machine used for recording and handling cash transactions in a store or restaurant. Example: "The cashier scanned the items and entered them into the till."
2. **Until**: The point in time up to which something continues or is done. Example: "I'll work till 6 pm tonight."

### Verb
1. To operate or serve at a cash register. Example: "She was working behind the till all day."
2. To perform tasks until a specified time or condition. Example: "We'll continue the project till it's completed."

## Phrases
- "Check out the till" means to look at the cash register or inventory.
- "Till midnight" refers to staying open or working until midnight.
- "Don't forget to till the soil" is a gardening instruction, meaning to loosen the earth before planting.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The till showed a healthy balance after a busy weekend."
- **Verb**: "She finished ringing up customers at the till."
- **Prepositional phrase**: "The store will remain open till 10 pm."

## Antonyms
- After
- Beyond
