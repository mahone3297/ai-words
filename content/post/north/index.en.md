## Pronunciation
/nɔːθ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: One of the four main points of the compass, located at 0°, which lies directly opposite south and is the direction towards the North Pole. For example: "The house faces north."
- **Adjective**: Situated in, moving towards, or facing the direction of north. For example: "We're heading north on the highway."

### Noun Usage Examples
- "The sun rises in the east and sets in the west, while the North Star remains fixed in the northern sky."
- "She moved to the northern part of the country where winters are colder."

### Adjective Usage Examples
- "The north wind brought a chill to the air."
- "The north-facing windows receive less sunlight."

## Phrases
- "Go north" means to travel in the direction of the North Pole.
- "North of something" refers to a location that is further up along the same meridian than another place. Example: "Our new house is north of the city center."
- "To the north of" signifies a direction or location relative to something else. Example: "Canada is to the north of the United States."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "They embarked on a journey to explore the uncharted territories of the north."
- **Adjective**: "The north side of the mountain receives less snowfall due to its sheltered position."

## Synonyms

- Northern (adjective)
-北极 (noun, referring to Earth's North Pole)
