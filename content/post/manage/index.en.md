## Pronunciation
/ˈmænɪdʒ/

## Definitions
- **Verb (v)**: To control, direct, or be responsible for (a company, organization, or group of people), often dealing with various administrative tasks and decision-making processes. Example: "She manages the sales team effectively."
- **Verb (v)**: To organize, coordinate, and oversee the execution of (an activity, event, or process). Example: "They are managing the project from start to finish."
- **Verb (v)**: To handle or cope with (a situation or task) successfully. Example: "He's managed to keep his business afloat during tough times."
- **Noun (n)**: The act or skill of controlling and making decisions about a company, department, sports team, etc. Example: "Effective management is key to success in any organization."
- **Noun (n)**: A period of time during which someone controls or directs something. Example: "Under her management, the company doubled its profits."

## Phrases
- "To manage affairs" means to handle or conduct one's business or responsibilities.
- "To manage without" implies being able to function or survive without something.
- "To manage expectations" refers to setting realistic goals or outcomes for others to avoid disappointment.

## Example Sentences

- **Noun**: "His promotion put him in charge of the entire sales management."
- **Verb**: "The team manager is responsible for managing the training schedule."
