## Pronunciation
/rɪˈmɑːrk/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **noun** in this context is:
1. A statement or observation made, especially one that is brief or不经意.
   - Example: "Her remark about the weather was unexpected."
2. An expression of opinion or criticism.
   - Example: "He made a rude remark about her cooking."

### Verb
To **remark** as a verb means:
1. To make a comment or observation, typically about something that has just been said or done.
   - Example: "The professor remarked on the significance of the discovery."
2. To express an opinion or feeling.
   - Example: "She remarked on how well the children were behaving."

## Phrases
- "Make a remark" suggests offering a comment.
- "In passing, he remarked" implies a comment made casually.
- "An offhand remark" refers to a comment made without much thought.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "His witty remark had everyone laughing."
- **Verb**: "After finishing her meal, she remarked on the excellent service."
- **As a verb**: "Don't take his remarks too seriously; he tends to be sarcastic."
