## Pronunciation
/rʌn/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A continuous or repeated act of moving at a speed, often with the intention of reaching a destination. "The marathon runner pushed themselves to the finish line."
- The result or outcome of a competition or series of events. "In the election, he had a clear run to victory."
- A period without interference or interruption. "They had a quiet run of good weather."

### Verb
- To move swiftly or continuously along a course, path, or track. "The dog likes to run through the park in the morning."
- To operate or perform (a machine, equipment, etc.). "The car engine runs smoothly."
- To have control or be in charge of something. "She had a run of the company while her boss was away."

### Adjective
- Quick or fast-paced. "He completed the race in record run time."
- Regular or uninterrupted. "We had a run of successful business months."
- Unopposed or unchallenged. "He had a run for the presidency."

### Adverb
- In a running motion or continuously. "The children were playing tag, running around the playground."
- Without pause or interruption. "They ran the experiment multiple times to ensure accuracy."

## Phrases
- "Have a good run" is a way to wish someone well in a race or endeavor.
- "A run for office" refers to a campaign for political office.
- "Run out of" means to exhaust or deplete something.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "He had a long run of bad luck on the racetrack."
- **Verb**: "She started the car and let it run warm before hitting the road."
- **Adjective**: "The athlete had an impressive run in the 100-meter dash."
- **Adverb**: "He ran the errand as quickly as he could."
