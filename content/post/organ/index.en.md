## Pronunciation
/ˈɔːɡən/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A part of the body that has a specific function, usually consisting of multiple tissues working together. For example: "The heart is a vital organ in the circulatory system."
  - An instrument, typically with pipes, played by means of compressed air, such as in a church or theatre. For example: "The organist performed a moving piece on the grand organ."
  - A structure or part of a plant or animal that performs a particular function. For example: "The roots of the plant act as organs for absorbing nutrients from the soil."
  
- **Verb (v)**: To arrange systematically; to organize. Less commonly used. For example: "She was tasked with organing the files chronologically."

## Phrases
- "Organ transplant" refers to the surgical transfer of an organ from one person (the donor) to another (the recipient).
- "Organ music" refers to music written specifically for performance on pipe organs or similar instruments.
- "Organize" means to arrange elements into a structured whole, although it's derived from the same root but often not considered a direct usage of the word "organ".

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The human brain is the most complex organ in the body."
- **Verb**: "Before the concert, he spent hours organing his sheet music according to the order of performance."

## Synonyms
- Body part
- Tissue
- Organism (in the context of a functioning part of a larger whole)
- Instrument (when referring to the musical instrument)
- Coordinator (for the verb sense of organizing) 

Note: The verb form "organ" meaning 'to organize' is less common than its homophone "organize".
