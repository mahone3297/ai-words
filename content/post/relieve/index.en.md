## Pronunciation
/rɪˈliːv/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Relief**: The feeling of ease or comfort after experiencing stress, pain, or difficulty. For example: "The medication brought immediate relief to his headache."
- **Reliever**: A person or thing that provides relief, often in a temporary manner. For example: "The first aid kit contained various relievers for common injuries."

### Verb
- **To relieve**: 1) To make less severe or intense; alleviate. For example: "The rain relieved the drought conditions."
   - 2) To take over a duty or responsibility from someone else. For example: "She relieved her colleague at the end of their shift."
   - 3) To grant (a prisoner) temporary release from custody. For example: "The judge decided to grant bail, relieving him of jail time."

### Adjective
- **Relieving**: Providing relief or comfort. For example: "The cool breeze was very relieving after a hot day."

## Phrases
- "Find relief from" implies seeking or achieving a reduction in discomfort.
- "Provide relief to" means to alleviate someone's suffering or distress.
- "Temporary relief" signifies a short-term improvement.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The painkillers provided much-needed relief after the surgery."
- **Verb**: "The nurse relieved the patient's anxiety by explaining the procedure."
- **Adjective**: "The news of the promotion was a huge relief to him."
