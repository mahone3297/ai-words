## Pronunciation
/ˈmeɪnteɪn/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To keep or preserve something in its original state or condition; to uphold or support continuously or recurrently. For instance: "The gardener maintains the park's beauty by regular trimming and watering."
  - *Example Sentence*: "It's important to maintain good health through regular exercise and a balanced diet."
- **Verb**: To assert or affirm something as true or accurate. For instance: "The company maintains that their products are of the highest quality."
  - *Example Sentence*: "She maintained her innocence throughout the trial."
  
## Phrases
- "Maintain contact" means to continue communicating with someone.
- "Maintain order" refers to keeping control or discipline, often in a group or situation.
- "Maintain a relationship" means to work at keeping a relationship healthy and functioning.
- "To maintain a position" can mean to defend or stick to a point of view or physical location.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb (preserve condition)**: "They've managed to maintain the old mansion in excellent condition over the years."
- **Verb (assert truth)**: "Despite the evidence presented, he continues to maintain his version of events."
