## Pronunciation
/ˈmoʊstli/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: Mainly, for the most part, largely, predominantly, or almost completely. It is used to indicate that something is true or applies to a large extent but not entirely.

Example usage:
- **Adverb**: "She eats healthily **mostly** but occasionally indulges in sweets."
- "The sky was **mostly** clear, with only a few scattered clouds."
- "His work involves writing **mostly**, with some editing tasks thrown in."

## Phrases
- "Mostly likely" - indicating a high probability or strong likelihood: "It's **mostly likely** going to rain later today."
- "Mostly harmless" - a phrase popularized by Douglas Adams' book series "The Hitchhiker's Guide to the Galaxy" to describe Earth, meaning that it poses little to no threat.
  
## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "I **mostly** agree with your proposal; however, there are a few points I'd like to discuss further."
- "The house is **mostly** finished; we just need to add the final touches."
