## Pronunciation
/sɪɡ/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A visible symbol, mark, or gesture that communicates a particular meaning, often used for identification or communication.
- In a legal context, a document indicating agreement or acceptance.
- In mathematics or science, a shape or pattern that represents a concept or idea.

### Verb
- To show or indicate by a sign or gesture.
- To use a sign to communicate or convey information.
- To give notice or indication of something.

### Adjective
- Characteristic or indicative of a particular quality or feature.
- Designating something as having a particular meaning or function.

### Adverb
- In a manner that is symbolic or expressive.
- By means of a sign or gesture.

## Phrases
- "Hold up a sign" asks someone to display a message or signal.
- "A traffic sign warns drivers of upcoming hazards."
- "A smile is a universal sign of friendliness."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The museum had a special exhibit featuring historical signs from different eras."
- **Verb**: "She signaled for help by raising her arms."
- **Adjective**: "The shop displayed a 'closed' sign on the door."
- **Adverb**: "He silently signed his name on the contract."
