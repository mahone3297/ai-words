## Pronunciation
/sent/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A person widely regarded as having lived a holy and virtuous life, often revered as an object of veneration. [Oxford](https://en.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/saint)
- **Adjective**: Having the qualities of a saint; morally excellent. [Merriam-Webster](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/saintly)
- **Proper Noun**: A title given to certain individuals in certain religions, such as Catholicism (e.g., Saint Peter, Saint Francis).

## Phrases
- "Saintly virtues" refers to the moral excellence associated with saints.
- "Living like a saint" implies a life of selflessness and devotion.
- "A patron saint" is a saint venerated by a particular group or country.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She was a local saint, known for her charity work."
- **Adjective**: "His saintly demeanor inspired others to follow his example."
- **Proper Noun**: "Saint Paul played a crucial role in the early Christian church."

## Synonyms
- Holy person
- Sainted

- Righteous

## Etymology
The word "saint" comes from the Latin "sanctus," meaning "holy" or "set apart." It has religious connotations in various Christian traditions, but can also be used more broadly to describe someone of exceptional virtue or holiness.
