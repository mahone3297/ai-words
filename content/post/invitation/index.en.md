## Word: Invitation

## Pronunciation
/ˌɪnvɪˈteɪʃən/

## Definitions
### Noun (n)
- A written or verbal request inviting someone to go somewhere or do something, often suggesting a social event or activity. For example: "She received an invitation to her friend's wedding."
- The act of inviting or attracting; allurement or enticement. Example: "The smell of fresh coffee was an invitation to start the day."

### Phrases
- "Send an invitation" means to dispatch a written or electronic request asking someone to attend an event.
- "RSVP to an invitation" means to respond to the invitation indicating whether one will attend or not.
- "Accept/decline an invitation" refers to responding positively or negatively to an offer or request.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "We've sent out invitations for our housewarming party."
- "His winning smile was an unspoken invitation to dance."

## Synonyms
- Request
- Invite
- summons
- bidding
- call
- Exhortation
- Appeal
- Entreaty
- Proposition

## Phrase Examples
- "I have extended an invitation to my colleagues for dinner this weekend."
- "Don't forget to RSVP by the deadline mentioned in your birthday party invitation." 

## Derived Forms & Phrasal Verbs
- **Invite (v)**: To extend an invitation to someone. Example: "They invited us over for a barbecue on Saturday."
- "Issue an invitation": To formally present or distribute invitations. Example: "The hostess issued invitations to her annual charity gala."
