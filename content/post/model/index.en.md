## Pronunciation
/ˈmɑːdəl/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A representation, typically in miniature, of something, usually created to study or understand it better. For example: "The architect built a scale model of the new building design."
- **Noun (n)**: A person who serves as a subject for artwork, especially a photographer's fashion shoot or painter's portrait. Example: "She was a popular model for Vogue magazine."
- **Noun (n)**: A system or thing used as an example to follow or imitate. Example: "The Scandinavian welfare model is often cited by policymakers."
- **Noun (n)**: In mathematics, statistics, or computing, a mathematical construct that represents a real-life situation or process. Example: "A linear regression model predicts the relationship between two variables."
- **Verb (v)**: To make or construct a model of something. Example: "Scientists modeled the effects of climate change on sea levels."
- **Verb (v)**: To act or pose as a model for an artist or photographer. Example: "She modeled for several famous painters during her career."
- **Verb (v)**: To serve as an example or template for imitation. Example: "His successful business model has been emulated by many startups."

## Phrases
- "Role model": A person whose behavior or success can be emulated by others.
- "Fashion model": A person who models clothing or other fashion items for photographers and designers.
- "Statistical model": A mathematical model constructed using statistical principles to analyze data.
