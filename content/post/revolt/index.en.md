## Pronunciation
/rɪˈvəlt/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **revolt** is:
1. An act of open resistance or rebellion against an authority or established order, often seeking fundamental change or independence.
2. A sudden and strong uprising or protest, usually against an oppressive regime or injustice.

### Verb
To **revolt** can mean:
1. To rise up in opposition or rebellion against something or someone.
2. To experience a strong feeling of disgust or repulsion.

## Phrases
- "Join the revolt" implies supporting a cause or movement against authority.
- "A silent revolt" refers to a nonviolent resistance without overt public expression.
- "Revolt against tradition" signifies breaking away from established customs.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The people staged a peaceful revolt against the corrupt government."
- **Verb**: "His decision to quit the company was a personal revolt against his boss's unfair treatment."
- **Intransitive**: "The students felt a growing sense of revolt against the strict rules."
