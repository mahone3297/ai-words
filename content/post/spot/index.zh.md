## 读音
/spot/

## 释义
- **名词**:
  - 小块，点：A small, round mark or stain. 例如: "There's a red spot on your shirt."
  - 场所，地点：A particular place or position. 例如: "The restaurant is located in a prime spot downtown."
  - 节目，场地：A specific time or space for a television or radio program. 例如: "The ad agency booked a 30-second spot during the Super Bowl."

- **动词**:
  - 发现，认出：To notice or identify something or someone. 例如: "I spotted my friend in the crowd right away."
  - 指定，选中：To choose or assign a particular place or person. 例如: "The coach spotted her as a potential star athlete."

## 短语
- "Spot something" 意味着注意到或发现某物。
- "In the spotlight" 指处于公众关注的中心或受到重视。
- "Spot a mistake" 是指找出错误。

## 例句
- 名词: "She found a tiny spot on her otherwise perfect painting."
- 动词: "He quickly spotted the error in the calculation."
- 情景: "The singer stepped into the spotlight and began to sing."
