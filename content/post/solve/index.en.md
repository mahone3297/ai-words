## Pronunciation
/səˈloʊv/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A solution** (n): A method or answer to a problem, difficulty, or equation. For example: "The team developed a new solution to the complex issue."
- **A solver** (n): A person or tool capable of finding a solution. For example: "He's an excellent problem solver."

### Verb
- **To solve** (v): To find a way to deal with or resolve a problem, puzzle, or task. For example: "She solved the math problem quickly."
- **To resolve** (v): Similar to solving, but often implies a more final or permanent settlement. For example: "The conflict was finally resolved through negotiation."

## Phrases
- "Find a solution to" means to discover a way to address or fix a problem.
- "Solving a riddle" refers to deciphering a puzzle or enigma.
- "Solve a mystery" means to uncover the truth about a confusing or unknown situation.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The architect presented a viable solution for the design problem."
- **Verb**: "After hours of research, he finally solved the mystery of the ancient artifact."
- **Intransitive**: "The software is designed to solve complex calculations automatically."
