## Pronunciation
/ˈɜːrɡ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A strong feeling of desire or necessity to do something; a push or impulse. For example: "He had an urge to travel the world."
- **Verb**: To feel a strong desire or inclination to do something; to impel or motivate. For example: "She urged her team to work harder on the project."
- **Adjective**: Characterized by a strong desire or inclination; compelling. For example: "An urgent matter requires immediate attention."

## Phrases
- "Feel an urge to do" signifies a strong desire to perform an action.
- "urge someone to do" implies encouraging someone to take a particular action.
- "urge caution" is a warning to be careful or restrained.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The doctor urged her patient to quit smoking for their health's sake."
- **Verb**: "He urged his colleagues to contribute their ideas to the discussion."
- **Adjective**: "The emergency services were urgently needed due to the accident."
