## Word: Journal

## Pronunciation
/ˈdʒɜːrnəl/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A daily or weekly publication containing news, articles, and other informative pieces, often on a specific subject or profession. For example: "She writes a column for a popular science journal."
  - _Also_: A book or notebook in which one keeps a record of daily events or personal reflections, thoughts, or feelings; a diary. For example: "He maintained a travel journal during his trip around the world."
  
- **Verb**: To record something systematically, especially on a regular basis, in a journal or diary. For example: "She journals every day to reflect on her experiences."

## Phrases
- "Keep a journal" means to regularly write down one's thoughts or activities in a diary.
- "Journal entry" refers to a single recorded item in a journal or diary.
- "Academic journal" refers to a periodical that publishes scholarly articles, typically after a peer-review process.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (publication)**: "The latest issue of the medical journal includes groundbreaking research on cancer treatment."
- **Noun (diary)**: "Reading her old journal brought back many fond memories of childhood adventures."
- **Verb**: "As part of her therapy, she journaled about her emotions each night before bed."

- Chronicle
