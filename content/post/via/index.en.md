## Pronunciation
/vɪə/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: Through; by means of; in the manner of. For example: "The package was delivered via express mail."
- **Preposition**: By or through a particular route, means, or method. For example: "She traveled via train to get to the city."
- **Noun**: An intermediate point or stage, especially in a journey or communication. For example: "The conference will be held via video conference."
- **Pronoun**: (informal) A way of referring to oneself or others without specifying a name. For example: "Everyone had their own story to share via the platform."

## Phrases
- "Travel via plane" means to go by airplane.
- "Communicate via email" refers to sending messages electronically.
- "Learn via online courses" indicates studying remotely.

## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "I arrived at the meeting via the back door to avoid the crowd."
- **Preposition**: "They connected via satellite to participate in the global summit."
- **Noun**: "Students can access course materials via the school's digital library."
- **Pronoun**: "Each participant shared their experiences via 'us' in the discussion."
