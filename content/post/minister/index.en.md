## Pronunciation
/ˈmɪnɪstər/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A high-ranking government official responsible for a specific department or area of public affairs. For example: "The Minister of Finance presented the annual budget."
  - Also, in certain religious contexts, a person who conducts religious worship and performs other spiritual duties in a Christian church, especially a Protestant one. Example: "The minister delivered a moving sermon on Sunday."
- **Verb (v)**: To attend to the needs of someone or something; to serve or administer to. Example: "She ministers to the sick in the hospital."
- **Noun (historical)**: In some monarchies, a member of the cabinet or council that advises the sovereign.

## Phrases
- "Cabinet minister": A senior government official who is a member of the cabinet.
- "Minister to someone's needs": To care for or provide what is necessary for someone.
- "The Prime Minister": The head of government in many countries, especially those with a parliamentary system.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (government official)**: "The new Education Minister will be addressing the nation tonight."
- **Noun (religious context)**: "The minister officiated the wedding ceremony."
- **Verb**: "As a nurse, her role is to minister to the patients' medical and emotional needs."

## Synonyms
- Official (noun, government context)
- Clergyman/Clergywoman (noun, religious context)
- Serve (verb)
- Administer (verb)
- Care for (verb)
