## Pronunciation
/pɜːrəl/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A small, round, shiny object produced within the soft tissue (nacre) of certain mollusks, especially oysters and pearls, as a result of irritation or foreign material.
   - Example: "The necklace was adorned with a priceless South Sea pearl."
2. A precious gemstone often valued for its lustrous appearance and beauty.
   - Example: "She inherited a family heirloom, a flawless white pearl earring."

### Verb
1. To produce or acquire a pearl, often metaphorically referring to something valuable or rare.
   - Example: "The artist found inspiration in the pearl of a new idea."
2. To express admiration or appreciation, often using the phrase "as if it were a pearl."
   - Example: "Her performance was a true pearl of wisdom."

### Adjective
1. Referring to something of great beauty or value, like a pearl.
   - Example: "A pearl of wisdom is a valuable piece of advice."
2. Smooth and lustrous, like a pearl's surface.
   - Example: "Her skin had a delicate pearl-like sheen."

## Phrases
- "She cultivated a pearl of knowledge through years of study."
- "A pearl of a story, passed down through generations."
- "The pearl of her voice captured everyone's attention."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The diver found a stunning black pearl in the depths of the ocean."
- **Verb**: "After months of hard work, he finally polished the pearl of his career."
- **Adjective**: "Her eyes sparkled like a pair of precious pearls."

## Synonyms
- Noun: Bead, gemstone, treasure, jewel
- Verb: Acquire, cultivate, obtain, create
- Adjective: Lustrous, exquisite, rare, valuable
