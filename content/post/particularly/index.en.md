## Pronunciation
/pəˈtɪkəli/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: Used to emphasize that something is especially or particularly true or relevant. It can indicate a higher degree or intensity than might be expected. 

## Phrases
- "In particular" is used to specify a specific point or instance within a general statement.
- "Especially" or "more than usual" often have similar meanings when used to emphasize.
- "With particular interest" implies a heightened focus on a subject.

## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "She was particularly proud of her achievement."
- "The weather was beautiful today, particularly for a January day."
- "The teacher praised John's presentation, particularly his creativity."

## Synonyms
- Especially
- Specifically
- notably
- uniquely
- exceptionally
- prominently

## Antonyms
- Generally
- Normally
- Indifferently
- Not particularly (contrasts emphasis)
