## Pronunciation
/tɛmpt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Temptation**: An attractive but potentially harmful or undesirable thing that entices or urges someone to act. For example: "The forbidden fruit was a temptation for Adam and Eve."
- **Enticement**: Something that persuades or attracts someone to do something, often in a seductive manner. For example: "The promise of a promotion was a strong enticement for him to stay at the job."

### Verb
- **To tempt**: To attract or allure someone to do something, often with a sense of danger or sinfulness involved. For example: "She couldn't resist the temptation of a slice of chocolate cake."
- **To entice**: To persuade someone to do something by offering something desirable or appealing. For example: "The chef enticed the customers with the aroma of freshly baked bread."

## Phrases
- "Be tempted by" suggests being influenced by an attraction.
- "Resist the temptation" means to withstand the desire to do something.
- "Lure someone with temptation" implies using something attractive to draw someone in.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The siren's voice was a temptation he couldn't ignore."
- **Verb**: "She tried to resist the temptation to eat all the cookies in one sitting."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "He was tempted to cheat on the exam, but his conscience stopped him."
