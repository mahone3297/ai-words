## Word: Musical

## Pronunciation
/ˈmjuːzɪk(ə)l/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Relating to music or having a connection with music. For example: "She has a natural talent for musical instruments."
  - _The musical quality of her voice was remarkable._
- **Noun**: A play or film that includes songs, spoken dialogue, and often dance as part of the performance. For example: "They're going to see a Broadway musical tonight."
  - _West Side Story is a beloved American musical._

## Phrases
- "Musical theatre" refers to plays that incorporate songs, spoken dialogue, and often dance.
- "Musical genre" denotes a category of music characterized by similarities in form, style, or subject matter.
- "A musical prodigy" refers to a person with exceptional talent in music from a very young age.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The school offers a variety of musical classes, ranging from piano to violin."
- **Noun**: "Hamilton is a critically acclaimed historical musical that blends hip-hop with traditional show tunes."

## Synonyms
- Melodic (adj.)
- Harmonious (adj.)
- Operatic (adj., when referring specifically to opera-like musicals)
- Show (noun, informal for a musical production)
- Spectacle (noun, especially for grand or elaborate musicals)
