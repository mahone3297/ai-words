## Pronunciation
/tɛər/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A tear** (countable): A drop of liquid that comes from the eye when it is sad, emotional, or due to physical irritation. For example: "She wiped away a tear from her cheek."
- **A rip or gap**: A tear in clothing or a hole. For example: "The seam on my shirt has a small tear."

### Verb
- **To tear** (v): 
  - (1) To pull apart or rend with force, causing a break. For example: "The paper tore easily when I tried to fold it."
  - (2) To experience an emotional breakdown or be overcome with emotion. For example: "She tore up after hearing the sad news."
  - (3) To remove forcefully or roughly. For example: "Tear off the tape from the package."

### Adjective
- **Tearful**: Characterized by tears or causing tears. For example: "A tearful goodbye was said at the farewell ceremony."

### Adverb
- **Tearfully**: In a manner involving tears or expressing sadness. For example: "She cried tearfully as she remembered her grandmother."

## Phrases
- "Tear up a document" means to destroy or shred a piece of paper.
- "Tear down a wall" can metaphorically mean to break down barriers or preconceptions.
- "Tears of joy" describe happy tears.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She held back her tears as she read the letter."
- **Verb**: "The old man couldn't help but tear up when he saw his children's faces."
- **Adjective**: "The mother's tearful eyes showed her deep sorrow."
- **Adverb**: "He spoke tearfully about his lost childhood."
