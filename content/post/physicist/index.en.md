## Pronunciation
/ˈfɪzɪkɪst/

## Definitions
### Noun
A physicist is a scientist who studies the fundamental laws of nature, particularly related to matter, energy, and the universe at the atomic and subatomic level. They explore various phenomena such as mechanics, electromagnetism, thermodynamics, and quantum mechanics.

### Verb
To physicist (informal) can mean to apply the principles of physics or engage in physics-related research or analysis.

## Phrases
- "A renowned physicist" refers to a highly respected expert in the field.
- "Exploring new physics" involves researching uncharted territories in the scientific discipline.
- "Physicist's equation" may denote a significant or groundbreaking theory in physics.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The physicist's lecture was filled with complex theories and experiments."
- **Verb**: "The team of physicists is working on developing a new method to harness solar energy."
- **Informal**: "She has a natural physicist's mind, always questioning and seeking explanations."
