## Pronunciation

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A device that secures something, typically a door or container, by preventing it from being opened without a key or combination. Example: "She put the necklace in the safe and turned the lock."
- **Verb (v)**: To fasten or secure something with a lock so that it cannot be easily opened. Example: "I always lock my car when I leave it in the parking lot."
- **Verb (v)**: To become fixed or immovable due to mechanical jamming or obstruction. Example: "The door had swollen and wouldn't lock properly."
- **Verb (v)**: To fix someone's gaze or attention onto something. Example: "She locked her eyes on the stage during the performance."
- **Verb (v)**: In computing, to make data inaccessible or unmodifiable until a certain condition is met, often for security purposes. Example: "The user can lock their account to prevent unauthorized access."

## Phrases
- "Lock and key": The traditional mechanism for securing things, referring to both parts needed to operate a lock.
- "Lock down": To secure or confine someone or something tightly and restrict movement or access, often used in situations like prison lockdowns or COVID-19 lockdowns.
- "Lock horns": To engage in a heated argument or conflict.
- "Lock, stock, and barrel": Completely, entirely; every part of something.
