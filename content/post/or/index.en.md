## Word: Or

## Pronunciation
/ɔːr/

## Definitions
- **Conjunction**: A word used to connect alternatives, often indicating a choice between them. It can also be used to introduce an alternative case or condition. For example:
  - "You can have tea or coffee."
  - "If it rains, we'll stay home; or if it's sunny, we'll go to the park."

## Example Sentences
- **Conjunction (choice)**: "I'll take either pizza or pasta for dinner tonight."
- **Conjunction (alternative case)**: "Call me before you leave, or let me know if you need a ride."

## Phrases
- "Either...or": This phrase is used to present two alternatives where only one is possible. Example: "You can either finish your homework now, or wait until tomorrow morning."
- "Or else": This phrase introduces a consequence if the first action is not taken. Example: "Finish your chores, or else you won't get any screen time."

## Synonyms
- Either
- Alternately
- In the alternative
- Otherwise
- On the other hand

Note: The word "or" doesn't have direct synonyms when used as a conjunction because its function is unique in connecting alternatives. However, the phrases that include "or" may convey similar meanings as other expressions with different words.
