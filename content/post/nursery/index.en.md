## Word: Nursery

## Pronunciation
/ˈnɜːr-səri/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 

  - A room or place where young children are cared for and educated before they start attending school. _Example:_ "The children love playing with toys in the nursery."
  - A section of a garden where plants are grown from seeds or cuttings before being transplanted, often called a plant nursery. _Example:_ "They bought several rose bushes from the local nursery."

- **Adjective** (related to the noun): Referring to things associated with a nursery or childcare. _Example:_ "She decorated the room with bright, colorful, and nursery-themed wallpaper."

## Phrases
- "Nursery school": An educational establishment that provides care and education for very young children before they begin compulsory education.
- "Plant nursery": A place where trees, shrubs, flowers, and other plants are propagated and grown to be sold or planted elsewhere.
- "Nursery rhymes": Traditional poems or songs for children, often passed down orally and featuring simple language and repetition.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (childcare)**: "Parents dropped off their toddlers at the nursery before heading to work."
- **Noun (plants)**: "We spent the afternoon selecting new perennials at the local nursery."
- **Adjective**: "She picked out a set of nursery furniture for the baby's room."

## Synonyms
- Childcare center
- Daycare center
- Kindergarten (in some contexts)
- Creche
- Plant propagation center
- Garden center (for plant nurseries)
