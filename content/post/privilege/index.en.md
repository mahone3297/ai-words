## Pronunciation
/prɪˈvɪljəs/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A special right or advantage given to someone, often by authority or custom. Example: "The privilege of attending the exclusive event was reserved for VIPs."
2. An honor or favor bestowed upon someone. Example: "Being chosen for this prestigious research project was a great privilege."
3. Exclusive or favored treatment. Example: "She enjoyed the privilege of having a personal assistant."

### Verb
1. To have or exercise a special right or advantage. Example: "They were privileged to witness the signing of the treaty."
2. To enjoy a certain benefit or status. Example: "Students at this school are privileged with access to top-notch facilities."

## Phrases
- "Take advantage of a privilege" means to make use of a special right or opportunity.
- "Without privilege" refers to something that is not available or accessible to everyone.
- "Enjoying equal privileges" signifies fairness and equality in treatment.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Having access to the library's rare books was a privilege for the scholars."
- **Verb**: "Not everyone is priviledged with the ability to travel the world."
- **Adjective**: "The children were treated with all the privileges befitting their noble birth."
