## Word: Luggage

## Pronunciation
/ˈlʌɡɪdʒ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: Bags, cases, and containers used for carrying personal belongings during travel. It can also refer to the contents of such items. 

  - Example Sentence: "She packed her luggage with clothes and essentials for the week-long trip."
  - Another Example: "The airline lost my luggage, so I had to buy new clothes."

## Phrases
- "Check your luggage": To hand over one's suitcases or bags to be transported in the hold of an aircraft or other transport vehicle.
  - Example: "Before boarding the plane, passengers need to check their luggage at the counter."
- "Carry-on luggage": Refers to baggage that is small enough to be brought onto an aircraft and stored in the overhead compartment or under the seat in front.
  - Example: "His carry-on luggage contained his laptop, a book, and some snacks."
  
## Example Sentences
- Noun (as the item): "They wheeled their heavy luggage through the airport terminal."
- Noun (as the contents): "Her luggage included a swimsuit for the beach and formal wear for the gala dinner."
