## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈdiːd/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: (used to emphasize the truth of a statement) certainly, really, or truly. For example: "It was indeed a beautiful day."
- **Adjective**: (used for emphasis) actually; in fact. For example: "She is indeed an expert in her field."

## Phrases
- "In fact" can be used as a synonym for "indeed" when emphasizing something's truth or actuality. Example: "Indeed, he did finish his work on time; in fact, he finished it early."
- "Indeed so" is used to confirm agreement or to acknowledge the truth of a statement. Example: "The project was challenging. Indeed so, but we managed to complete it successfully."
- "To be indeed fortunate" means to be genuinely lucky or privileged. Example: "They were indeed fortunate to have such supportive parents."

## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "I am very happy, indeed, to be here today."
- "He did not merely attend the event; he spoke at it, indeed."
- **Adjective**: "This indeed is the house where I grew up."
