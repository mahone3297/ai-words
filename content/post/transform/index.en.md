## Pronunciation
/tɹænsˈfɔrm/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (transitive) To change the form, nature, or appearance of something; alter or convert from one state, shape, or substance to another. For example: "The magician transformed the card into a rabbit."
- **Noun**: The act or process of transformation. For example: "The transformation of a caterpillar into a butterfly is a remarkable natural phenomenon."
- **Adjective**: Characterized by or resulting in a change or transformation. For example: "A transformative experience can greatly impact a person's life."

## Phrases
- "Transform something" means to alter its physical or functional properties.
- "Transformational change" describes a significant and lasting alteration.
- "Transformative technology" refers to innovations that bring about major improvements.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The artist's new technique transformed her paintings."
- **Noun**: "The transformation of society from an agrarian to an industrial one took centuries."
- **Adjective**: "The program aimed to bring about transformative educational reforms."
