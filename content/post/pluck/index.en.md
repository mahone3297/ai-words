## Pronunciation
/plʌk/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A plant's fruit or flower**: The soft, usually edible part that grows on a plant and is often pulled off by hand. Example: "She plucked a ripe strawberry from the garden."
- **Pulling out forcefully**: The act of removing something, typically with force, from its position or attachment. Example: "He plucked the thorn from his finger."
- **Courage or determination**: An expression of bravery or confidence. Example: "She showed great pluck when she faced her fears."

### Verb
- **To remove or pick**: To take something, especially a small object, by pulling it off or through. Example: "He plucked a feather from the pillow."
- **To gather or obtain**: To acquire something, often with effort or determination. Example: "He plucked a solution to the problem from his memory."
- **To extract**: To take something out of a situation or state. Example: "She plucked herself up from the despair."

### Adjective
- **Determined or brave**: Characterized by courage and willingness to face challenges. Example: "He was a plucky little boy who never backed down."

### Adverb
- (Informal) Boldly or confidently. Example: "She spoke with a plucky determination."

## Phrases
- "pluck up the courage" to gather one's resolve or find the strength to do something difficult.
- "pluck a leaf" to take a small item or idea from a larger context.
- "pluck oneself together" to gather one's thoughts or emotions after a setback.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She picked plums from the tree in her backyard."

- **Adjective**: "Despite the fear, he showed a plucky attitude in the face of danger."
- **Adverb**: "She plucked up her courage to speak up during the meeting."
