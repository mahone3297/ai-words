## Pronunciation
/ˈstrʌgl/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The act of exerting oneself with great effort or difficulty to overcome an obstacle, problem, or resistance. "She struggled to lift the heavy box."
2. A state of conflict or difficulty, often mental or emotional. "Financial struggles can be challenging."

### Verb
1. To engage in a hard-fought battle or contest, often against an opposing force or adversity. "He struggled through the difficult hike."
2. To experience difficulty in performing an action or task. "She struggled to understand the complex concept."

### Adjective
1. Marked by difficulty or intense effort. "His struggle for survival was inspiring."
2. Characterized by contest or contention. "A political struggle for power."

### Adverb
1. In a manner that requires great effort or exertion. "He struggled slowly up the stairs."

## Phrases
- "Struggle with an issue" means to face difficulty or challenge in resolving a problem.
- "Struggle for equality" refers to the fight for equal rights.
- "Struggle against addiction" is the process of overcoming dependency.
