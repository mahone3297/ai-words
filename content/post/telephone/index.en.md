## Pronunciation
/telɪˈfəʊn/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A device that allows two or more people to communicate by transmitting sound over a wire or electromagnetic waves, enabling long-distance conversation.
   - Example: "The telephone revolutionized communication in the 19th century."
2. A system of telephonic communication, often through a network or cellular infrastructure.
   - Example: "I called my friend using the local telephone network."

### Verb
1. To use a telephone to make a call or communicate with someone.
   - Example: "She telephoned her family every Sunday."
2. To contact someone by phone, usually informally.
   - Example: "Don't hesitate to telephone if you need any help."

### Adjective
Referring to the technology or act of telephoning.
   - Example: "The telephone company announced new fiber-optic services."

## Phrases
- "Make a telephone call" means to initiate a conversation on the phone.
- "Dial a telephone number" is the action of inputting a number to connect with someone.
- "Answer the telephone" refers to picking up when the phone rings.
