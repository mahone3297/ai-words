## Pronunciation
/əmˈbʌŋkst/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Referring to one or more people or things that are among a group, indicating a position between others. For example: "She was the most talented amongst the group."
- **Preposition**: Indicating the relationship of one thing to another within a group, often implying comparison or relative position. For example: "The best book was amongst the new releases."

## Phrases
- "Amongst the winners" signifies those who have achieved a victory or recognition within a set.
- "Amidst the crowd" describes someone or something being situated in the middle of a group.
- "In amongst the chaos" implies being caught up in a confusing or disordered situation.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "Her painting stood out amongst the others due to its unique style."
- **Preposition**: "The tallest building was amongst the oldest structures in the city."

## Synonyms
- Amid
- Between (in the sense of positioning)
- Among
- Amongst (informal, British English)
- In the midst of
- Midway through

## Note:
- The use of "amongst" as a preposition is considered less common in American English, where "among" is more commonly used in this context. However, both are acceptable in British English.
