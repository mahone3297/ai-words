## Pronunciation
/pɪˈrekt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Prediction**: The act or process of forecasting or estimating future events or outcomes based on present evidence or knowledge. For example: "The meteorologist made a weather prediction for tomorrow."
- **Predictor**: A person or thing that forecasts or indicates what is likely to happen. For example: "This economic indicator is often a predictor of market trends."

### Verb
- **To predict**: To forecast or anticipate the occurrence of something with some degree of confidence. For example: "The scientist predicted the discovery of a new species."
- **To predetermine**: To decide beforehand or establish as a certainty. For example: "The winner was predetermined by their impressive performance."

### Adjective
- **Predictable**: Characterized by easily foreseen or expected behavior. For example: "Her routine is so predictable; you can almost set your watch by it."

### Adverb
- **Predictably**: In a manner that can be anticipated or expected. For example: "The results were predictable, given the circumstances."

## Phrases
- "Predict the outcome" means to forecast the result of an event.
- "Make a prediction" is the act of forming an estimate about the future.
- "A predictive model" refers to a system that estimates future events based on past data.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The economist's prediction for GDP growth was accurate."
- **Verb**: "The doctor predicted that the patient would recover within a week."
- **Adjective**: "The exam results were predictable, with top students scoring high."
- **Adverb**: "His behavior was predictably erratic during times of stress."
