## Pronunciation
/prækˈtɪkl/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Practicality**: The quality of being useful, suitable, or appropriate for real-life situations. For example: "The designer focused on creating a practical and durable kitchen."
2. **Practical person**: A person who is sensible, down-to-earth, and efficient. For example: "She's a very practical person who always finds a solution to problems."

### Verb
1. To apply practical methods or ideas to a situation. For example: "Let's be practical and find a cost-effective solution."
2. To make something functional or operational. For example: "They decided to put the old shed to practical use as a workshop."

### Adjective
1. Suitable for everyday use or situations. For example: "A practical car that saves on fuel."
2. Concerned with the actual effectiveness or efficiency of something. For example: "Her design was both stylish and practically efficient."

## Phrases
- "Be practical" suggests being realistic and avoiding impractical ideas.
- "A practical approach" refers to a method that focuses on functionality and effectiveness.
- "Put theory into practice" means to apply theoretical knowledge to real-world situations.

## Example Sentences

- **Verb**: "We need to put these plans into practical action."
- **Adjective**: "The course emphasized the practical skills necessary for the job."
