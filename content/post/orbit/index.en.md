## Word: Orbit

## Pronunciation
/ˈɔːrbɪt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: (n) The curved path that an object in space takes around a celestial body, such as a planet or star, under the influence of its gravity. Example: "The moon's orbit around Earth takes approximately 28 days."
  
  - Also used metaphorically to describe a sphere of influence or activity. Example: "The city has become a major player in the global economic orbit."

- **Verb**: (v) To move in a curved path around a celestial body or to rotate around something else. Example: "Satellites orbit the Earth transmitting data back to ground stations."

## Phrases
- "In orbit": Refers to an object that is currently moving along a circular or elliptical path around a celestial body. Example: "The International Space Station has been in orbit around Earth since 1998."
- "To put something into orbit": Means to launch it into space so that it begins circling a celestial body. Example: "NASA successfully put a new satellite into orbit last night."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Astronomers calculated the exact dimensions of Mars' orbit around the sun."
- **Verb**: "The spacecraft was designed to orbit Jupiter and study its moons."
