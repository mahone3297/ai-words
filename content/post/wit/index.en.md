## Pronunciation
/wɪt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **wit** (noun): A quality of being clever, humorous, or having a good sense of humor. It often involves the ability to make witty remarks or come up with clever solutions.
- **wit** (noun, obsolete): Intelligence or cleverness, especially in a person.

### Verb
- **to wit** (idiomatic): To say that, to mean that; used to emphasize or clarify a point.

## Phrases
- "Displaying a good wit" refers to showing intelligence or humor.
- "Wit is the wine of the mind" is a phrase highlighting the importance of wit.
- "With great wit" is used to describe something done cleverly or amusingly.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Her quick wit was the reason she always won the argument."
- **Verb**: "To wit, the answer lies in understanding the context."
- **Idiomatic**: "He told a joke with such wit that it had everyone laughing."
