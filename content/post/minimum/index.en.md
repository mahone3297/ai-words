## Pronunciation
/ˈmɪnɪməm/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: The least or smallest amount, quantity, or degree possible, required, or desirable; the lowest limit allowed or possible. For example: "The minimum wage was increased this year."
  - In mathematics: The smallest value that a function can take within a given interval or set.
  - In statistics: The smallest number in a set of data or the lower boundary of a range.

- **Adjective (adj)**: Being the least or smallest possible; representing the minimum level or amount. For example: "The job requires a minimum of two years' experience."

## Phrases
- "At a minimum": Meaning at least or the bare essentials needed.
- "Minimum requirement(s)": The least amount or level of something necessary to meet a certain standard or achieve a goal.
- "Minimum wage": The lowest salary legally payable to workers.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "They recommend drinking a minimum of eight glasses of water daily."
- **Adjective**: "This software upgrade requires a minimum system specification of 4GB RAM."
