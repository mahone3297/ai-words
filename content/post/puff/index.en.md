## Pronunciation
/pʌf/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Aerodynamic force:** An outward burst of air produced by blowing or forcing air into something, often causing it to rise or move. Example: "The helium balloon was lifted by a gentle puff."
- **Breath or exhalation:** A short, light inhalation or release of air. Example: "He took a deep puff of his cigar."
- **Temporary increase in size or volume:** A swelling or bulge, often used metaphorically. Example: "The pillow was puffed up with feathers."
- **An act of smoking:** The action of inhaling and exhaling smoke from a cigarette, cigar, or pipe. Example: "He took a long puff on his pipe."

### Verb
- **To blow air into (something):** To inflate or cause to expand by expelling air. Example: "She puffed up her cheeks to make herself look bigger."
- **To give a quick, light breath:** To take a small breath or puff. Example: "After running, he puffed a little to catch his breath."
- **To operate or manipulate something with short bursts:** To use a tool or device in a quick, repeated manner. Example: "He puffed the lighter to ignite the fire."

## Phrases
- "Puff up" means to swell or expand.
- "A puff of smoke" describes a small cloud of smoke.
- "Puff pastry" refers to a flaky, layered dough used in baking.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The wind puffed the sails, propelling the boat forward."
- **Verb**: "She puffed the life back into the deflated tire."
- **Interjection**: "Puff, puff, puff! The steam engine struggled up the hill."

## Synonyms
- Blow
- Inflate
- Exhale
- Sigh
- Sniff (informal, for a small puff)
