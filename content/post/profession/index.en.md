## Pronunciation
/profɪˈʃən/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A career or occupation to which a person is professionally committed, often involving specialized training and skills.
   - Definition: A line of work that someone has chosen to pursue and develop expertise in.
   - Example: "Teaching is her chosen profession."

2. An activity or pursuit that one engages in as a means of earning a living.
   - Example: "He works in the legal profession."

### Verb
1. To engage in or practice as one's occupation or career.
   - Example: "She aspired to pursue a profession in medicine."

2. To declare or describe (oneself) as belonging to a particular profession.
   - Example: "He proudly declared his profession to be an architect."

## Phrases
- "Enter the profession" means to start a career in a specific field.
- "Switching professions" refers to changing from one occupation to another.
- "Professional development" is the process of improving one's skills and knowledge within a career.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She spent her entire life dedicated to the performing arts as her profession."
- **Verb**: "After graduating, he began his professional journey as a software engineer."
- **Phrasal**: "Many professionals attend conferences to enhance their professional development."
