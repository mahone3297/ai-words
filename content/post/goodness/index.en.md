## Word: Goodness

## Pronunciation
/ˈɡʊdnəs/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The quality of being good, moral excellence, or virtue. It can also refer to the inherent goodness or positive aspects of something. Example: "The goodness of his heart was evident in his actions."
   - *Food-related meaning*: The state or quality of being good or beneficial, especially regarding the nutritional value or taste of food. Example: "This organic honey is full of natural goodness."

- **Interjection**: An expression of surprise, bewilderment, or emphasis, often used to convey mild annoyance or frustration. Example: "Goodness gracious, how did that happen?"

## Phrases
- "For goodness' sake": An idiomatic expression used to express impatience, frustration, or exasperation, similar to 'for heaven's sake.' Example: "For goodness' sake, hurry up!"
- "Goodness knows": This phrase is used to indicate uncertainty or emphasize a point, implying that only a higher power could know the answer. Example: "Goodness knows when he'll arrive."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Her unwavering commitment to honesty was a testament to the depth of her goodness."
- **Food-related Noun**: "Grandma's homemade apple pie is filled with the goodness of fresh fruit and natural ingredients."
- **Interjection**: "Goodness me, it's been ages since we last spoke!"

## Synonyms
- Virtue
- Morality
- Benevolence
- Kindness
- Decency (for moral goodness)
- Nutrient content
- Healthiness (for food-related goodness)
