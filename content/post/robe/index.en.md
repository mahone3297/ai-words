## Pronunciation
/rəʊb/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. A long piece of clothing, typically loose-fitting, that covers the body from the shoulders to the ankles, worn especially as formal wear or for special occasions. 2. (colloquial) A dressing gown or bathrobe.
- **Verb**: To dress in a robe, especially for a religious ceremony or as part of a theatrical role.
- **Adjective**: Characteristic of a robe, such as flowing or ceremonial.

## Phrases
- "Dress in a robe" or "put on a robe"
- "A white linen robe" (referring to a specific type)
- "robe oneself with authority" (figurative use)

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She wore a beautiful silk robe to the gala."
- **Verb**: "The priest donned his vestments and robe before the service."
- **Adjective**: "The altar boy looked dignified in his ecclesiastical robe."

## Synonyms
- Robe-like
- Gown
- Robe-shaped
- Liturgical attire
- Robe-style

## Related Words
- Robelet (a shorter, simpler version of a robe)
- Tippet (a short, pointed robe or cape worn over a surplice)
- Habit (a specific type of robe worn by religious orders)
- Robe of office (a ceremonial garment indicating a person's position of authority)
