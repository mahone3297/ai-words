## Pronunciation
/ˈkwɔːrt/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **quart** is a unit of volume commonly used in the United States and the United Kingdom, equal to one fourth of a gallon or approximately 32 fluid ounces (1.14 liters).

### Verb
To **quart** something means to measure or fill a container with a quart.

## Phrases
- "Fill a quart jar" refers to the act of pouring a liquid up to the quart mark.
- "Quart of milk" is a common expression for a specific quantity of milk.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "I bought a gallon of juice, which is equivalent to four quarts."
- **Verb**: "She carefully measured out two quarts of flour for the recipe."
- "I need to quart the gasoline into the car's tank."

## Synonyms
- Pint (half a quart)
- Quart jug
- Fourth of a gallon
- One-quart measurement

## Related Terms
- **Dry quart**: Refers to a non-measurable unit, often used informally to indicate a large amount, like "a quart of fun" or "a quart of laughter."
- **Liquid quart**: The standard unit of measurement for liquid volume in the US and UK.
