## Word: Learning

## Pronunciation
/ˈlɜːrnɪŋ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The acquisition of knowledge or skills through experience, study, or being taught. For example: "Continuous learning is essential for personal growth."
  - "Learning process": The steps taken or activities engaged in to gain new information or abilities.
  - "Learning curve": The rate at which someone learns something new, often represented graphically to show improvement over time.

Example Sentence: "Throughout his life, he was dedicated to the lifelong learning journey."

- **Verb**: To acquire knowledge or understanding of something; to study or be taught. For example: "She is learning French this semester."
  
Example Sentence: "Children learn best through play and exploration."

## Phrases
- "Learning experience": An event or activity that provides knowledge or understanding, often from direct involvement or observation.
- "Learning by doing": A hands-on approach to education where one acquires skills and knowledge through practical action rather than theoretical study.
- "Learning from mistakes": Acquiring wisdom or insight through recognizing and correcting errors.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Reading books has been a significant part of her learning journey."
- **Verb**: "They are learning how to code so they can develop their own apps."

## Synonyms

- Study
- Instruction
- Training
- Acquisition of knowledge
- Enlightenment
- Familiarization
- Mastery (in the context of gaining skill)
