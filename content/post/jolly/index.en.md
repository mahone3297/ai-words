## Word: Jolly

## Pronunciation
/dʒɑːli/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Characterized by high spirits and cheerfulness; merry or jovial. For example: "We had a jolly time at the Christmas party."
- **Adverb**: In a cheerful or lively manner. For example: "The children were singing jolly songs around the campfire."

### Definition with Examples
- **Adjective**: Describing something or someone as very happy, enjoyable, or pleasant. Example: "The atmosphere was jolly and festive."
  - "Grandpa was in his jolly element telling stories around the fireplace."
- **Adverb**: Used to emphasize that something is done in a particularly happy or enthusiastic way. Example: "He laughed jolly loudly when he heard the joke."

## Phrases
- "Jolly good" is a British informal expression meaning excellent or very satisfactory. Example: "That's jolly good news!"
- "Jolly well" is used to add emphasis to an action or intention, often expressing determination or approval. Example: "I'll do it jolly well on my own!"

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The jolly old man dressed as Santa Claus brought joy to the children."
- **Adverb**: "She sang jolly merrily during the carol concert."

- Cheerful
- Joyful
- Happy
- Festive
- Lively
- Jovial
- Gay (in the sense of being light-hearted and cheerful)
