## Pronunciation
/rəˈʃiəʊ/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Ratio**: The relative proportion or numerical relationship between two quantities, expressed as one number divided by another. For example: "The ratio of boys to girls in the class is 3:2."
2. **Proportion**: The degree to which something is similar or corresponds to something else. For example: "The design maintains a good ratio of form to function."

### Verb
1. **To ratio**: To compare or judge in terms of proportion or relative size. For example: "We ratioed the teams' scores to determine the winner."

### Adjective
1. **Rational**: Based on reason or logic. For example: "A rational decision-making process involves weighing the pros and cons."

## Phrases
- "In a 2:1 ratio" describes a specific proportion.
- "The ratio of success to failure" compares the frequency of positive and negative outcomes.
- "Maintain a healthy ratio" refers to achieving balance or equilibrium.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The recipe calls for a 1:1 ratio of flour to sugar."
- **Verb**: "They ratioed the materials needed for the project."
- **Adjective**: "Her thoughts were highly rational and well-argued."
