## Pronunciation
/ˈluːsn/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To make (something) less tight, firm, or compact; to release from a fixed or fastened state. For example: "She loosened the knot on her tie."
- **Verb**: (v) To become less tight, firm, or compact; to become released or detached. For example: "The soil loosens after a heavy rain."
- **Verb**: (figurative) To relax or ease (something that is tense, rigid, or strict). For example: "He needs to loosen up before giving his presentation."

## Phrases
- "Loosen up" means to relax or become less tense or formal.
- "Loosen one's grip" refers to releasing one's hold or control over something.
- "Loosen the reins" suggests allowing greater freedom or flexibility.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb (physical action)**: "Before he could remove his shoes, he had to loosen the laces."
- **Verb (figurative use)**: "After a few drinks, she began to loosen up and enjoy the party."
- **Verb (natural occurrence)**: "As the ice melts, it gradually loosens its grip on the rocks beneath."
