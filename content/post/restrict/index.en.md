## Pronunciation
/rɪˈstrɪkt/

## Definitions
### Noun
A noun form of the verb, referring to:
1. The act of limiting or controlling something to a certain extent or within specific boundaries. For example: "The government imposed strict restrictions on public gatherings."
2. A limitation or condition placed on something. For example: "The new policy imposed a restriction on the use of plastic bags."

### Verb
1. To limit or confine something to a particular scope or level. For example: "The company restricted access to certain areas."
2. To control or prevent something from happening or being done. For example: "They restricted their spending to save for a vacation."

### Adjective
Describing something that is limited in quantity, scope, or freedom. For example: "The restricted parking area is reserved for staff only."

### Adverb
Used to indicate how something is restricted, e.g., "The flight was restricted to non-stop travel."

## Phrases
- "Restrict access" means to limit who can enter or use a space or resource.
- "Under restricted conditions" refers to limitations imposed on a task or situation.
- "Freedom is restricted in this area" communicates that there are limits to what can be done.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The government imposed strict restrictions on the sale of firearms."
- **Verb**: "The teacher restricted the students' use of mobile phones during class."
- **Adjective**: "The restricted diet helped her manage her health condition."
- **Adverb**: "He had to drive under restricted speed due to roadworks."
