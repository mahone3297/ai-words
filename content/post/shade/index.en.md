## Pronunciation
/sheɪd/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **shade** (countable and uncountable): 
   - **Countable**: A partial or complete darkness caused by an object blocking light; an area of shadow. Example: "The tree provided shade from the hot sun."
   - **Uncountable**: The quality or state of being darkened or partially obscured from light. Example: "The room had a cool shade of blue."

### Verb
1. To provide shelter or protection from direct sunlight or heat. Example: "The awning shades the entrance."
2. To describe or categorize something in terms of its color or tone. Example: "The painting is a beautiful shade of green."

### Adjective
1. Describing a color that is darker than its base hue. Example: "A pale yellow with a subtle shade of orange."
2. Relating to a particular level or intensity of a quality, such as lightness or darkness. Example: "A soft shade of pink."

## Phrases
- "Seek shade" implies finding protection from the sun or heat.
- "In the shade" refers to a location that is relatively cooler and out of direct sunlight.
- "Add a pop of shade" means to introduce a contrasting color to create visual interest.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The old oak tree provided ample shade for picnickers."
- **Verb**: "She carefully chose a paint shade that would complement her living room walls."
- **Adjective**: "The flowers were a vibrant shade of purple in the garden."
