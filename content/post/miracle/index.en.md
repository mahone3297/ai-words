## Pronunciation
/ˈmɪrəkl/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: An event that appears inexplicable by the laws of nature and is therefore attributed to a supernatural agency or to divine intervention. For example: "The recovery was nothing short of a miracle; doctors had given up hope."
- **Noun**: Something extraordinary that surpasses all known human or natural powers and is hence considered as a manifestation of divine intervention. Example: "The birth of their child was a miracle after years of infertility."
- **Noun (figurative)**: Any highly improbable or unexpected event that brings about a positive outcome. Example: "It was a miracle they found her alive after being lost in the wilderness for days."

## Phrases
- "A miracle worker" refers to someone who achieves results that others consider unattainable or miraculous.
- "Miracle cure" denotes a medicine or treatment that produces unexpectedly rapid or complete recovery from an illness or condition.
- "By a miracle" implies that something happened only because of an extraordinary and unlikely occurrence.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Many people believe that his recovery from the coma was a genuine miracle."
- **Noun (figurative)**: "Winning the lottery was a financial miracle that turned their fortunes around."
