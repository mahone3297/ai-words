## Word: Likely

## Pronunciation
/ˈlaɪkli/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Having a strong probability of occurring or being true; probable. For example: "It's likely to rain today."
  - Usage: "Based on the forecast, it's likely that we'll have a sunny weekend."
- **Adverb**: In all probability; most probably. For example: "He will likely arrive late due to traffic."
  - Usage: "She's likely going to finish her project ahead of schedule."

## Phrases
- **As likely as not**: With equal probability of either outcome. For example: "As likely as not, he'll decide to stay home tonight."
- **Likely enough**: Probably, quite possibly. For example: "Likely enough, we'll have to postpone the meeting."
- **Be likely to do something**: To have a strong tendency or expectation of doing something. Example: "New students are likely to experience homesickness during their first semester."

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "Given his skills, he's the most likely candidate for the job."
- **Adverb**: "With the current wind direction, the fire is likely to spread towards the north."
