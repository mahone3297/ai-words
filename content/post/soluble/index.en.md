## Pronunciation
/səˈluːbəl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Capable of being dissolved or suspended in a solvent; capable of dissolving easily. For example: "The salt is soluble in water."
- **Noun**: A substance that can be dissolved; a solute. For example: "Sugar is a soluble solid."

## Phrases
- "Soluble substances" refers to materials that can dissolve in a given medium.
- "Insoluble compounds" are those that do not dissolve easily.
- "A soluble compound forms a solution" when it dissolves completely.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The soluble dye mixed easily with the water to form a vibrant color."
- **Noun**: "Many vitamins are soluble in fat, while others are soluble in water."

## Synonyms
- Dissolvable
- Hygroscopic (referring to substances that absorb moisture)
- Miscible (able to mix without separation)
- Solvent-friendly
- Water-soluble (specifically referring to solubility in water)

## Antonyms
- Insoluble
- Non-dissolvable
- Inert
- Immiscible (unable to mix)
