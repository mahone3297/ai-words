## Pronunciation
/ˈwɪtʃ/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A determiner used to introduce a relative clause, specifying one of several people, things, or possibilities: "The book on the table which you lent me was very interesting."
2. A word used to refer to a particular person or thing in a group: "Which team do you support? The Manchester United fans cheered loudly."

### Pronoun
1. Referring to a specific person or thing out of several alternatives: "Which one of these keys opens the door?"
2. Used as a question word to inquire about something specific: "Which color do you prefer?"

### Adjective
1. Designating one of several alternatives: "There are three rooms available, which one would you like?"
2. Indicating a particular quality or feature: "This dress has a unique design, with which color does it come?"

## Phrases
- "Which one is yours?" - asking for identification.
- "Which language do they speak?" - inquiring about a specific language.
- "Choose the one which suits you best." - offering options.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "I need to find the book which was left on my desk."
- **Pronoun**: "I'm not sure which decision to make between these two options."
- **Adjective**: "She wore a dress with a pattern that was both elegant and unique."
