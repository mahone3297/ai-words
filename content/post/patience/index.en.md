## Pronunciation
/pəˈteɪns/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: **1** The capacity to tolerate delay, difficulty, or adversity with composure and without becoming annoyed, frustrated, or upset. Example: "She showed great patience while waiting in line."
   **2** The quality or virtue of being able to endure pain, hardship, or delay without complaint. Example: "His patience was tested during the long journey."
- **Verb**: To bear with calmness or endurance; to tolerate. Example: "She patiently explained the instructions to the child."
- **Adjective**: Characterized by or showing patience; tolerating delays or difficulties with equanimity. Example: "A patient teacher allows time for questions."

## Phrases
- "Show patience" means to remain calm and understanding in a difficult situation.
- "Patience is a virtue" highlights the importance of being able to endure without getting upset.
- "In need of patience" implies that something requires perseverance or tolerance.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She displayed immense patience as she waited for her turn to speak."
- **Verb**: "He patiently listened to his friend's complaints without interrupting."
- **Adjective**: "The teacher's patient demeanor helped soothe the anxious students."
