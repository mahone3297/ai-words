## Pronunciation
/saɪt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 
    1. The ability to see; vision. "She has excellent sight."
    2. An instance or experience of seeing something; a visual perception. "The sight of the sunset left her speechless."
    3. A particular view or scene, often picturesque or impressive. "The tourists marveled at the historic sight."
- **Verb**: To perceive with the eye; to see. "I sighted a bird perched on a branch."
- **Adjective**: Characteristic of or relating to sight. "Sight tests check a person's visual acuity."

## Phrases
- "Get a good sight of something" means to see something clearly.
- "Out of sight, out of mind" implies that when something is no longer visible, it is also forgotten.
- "Sightseeing" is the activity of visiting interesting places to see their sights.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After adjusting my glasses, I could finally get a clear sight of the painting."
- **Verb**: "He sighted the ship in the distance."
- **Adjective**: "The sight of her old house brought back memories from her childhood."
