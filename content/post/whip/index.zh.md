## 读音
/whɪp/

## 释义
- **名词**: 一种鞭子，通常用于驱赶动物或惩罚。例如: "The jockey used a whip to urge the horse faster."
- **动词**: 用鞭子抽打；快速搅拌。例如: "He whipped the cream until it formed stiff peaks."
- **名词**: 在某些组织或活动中，负责监督和确保成员出席的人。例如: "In Parliament, a whip is an official who ensures party discipline."

## 短语
- "whip cream" 意为打发奶油。
- "whip someone into shape" 意味着训练或激励某人变得更有纪律或效率。

## 例句
- 名词: "The coach held a whip in his hand, ready to train the new recruits."
- 动词: "She whipped the eggs vigorously to create a frothy mixture."
- 名词: "The political whip made sure all party members voted according to the party line."
