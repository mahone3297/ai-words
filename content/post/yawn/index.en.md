## Pronunciation
/ˈjɔːn/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A deep, audible inhalation followed by an exhalation through the mouth, often indicating weariness or boredom. For example: "After a long day, she couldn't help but let out a yawn."
- **Verb**: To stretch the mouth wide open and breathe in deeply, typically involuntarily. For example: "He yawned widely as he woke up from his nap."
- **Adjective**: Characteristic of or showing a yawn; sleep-inducing. For example: "The boring lecture was so monotonous that it made people around yawn-y."

## Phrases
- "Yawned out loud" means to yawn audibly.
- "To stifle a yawn" is to try to prevent oneself from yawning.
- "A yawn is contagious" refers to the tendency for people to yawn when others do.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She felt her eyes getting heavy, and a yawn escaped her lips."
- **Verb**: "After staying up all night, he could barely contain his yawns during the meeting."
- **Adjective**: "The children were so tired that the classroom felt yawn-inducing."
