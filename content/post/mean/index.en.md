## Pronunciation
/miːn/

## Definitions
### Noun (n)
1. **Average**: A number that represents the middle value of a set of numbers; found by adding all the numbers together and dividing by the total count. Example: "The mean of these three numbers is 10."
2. **Intentions or purpose**: The intended meaning or intention behind something, especially a person's actions or words. Example: "Her intentions were noble, but her actions had unforeseen consequences."
3. **Arithmetic mean**: In mathematics, it refers to the sum of a list of numbers divided by the count of numbers in the list.
4. **Means**: Resources or methods used to achieve something. Example: "They had no means to support themselves financially."

### Verb (v)
1. **Meaning or intend**: To convey or have a particular sense or significance. Example: "I didn't mean to offend you with my comment."
2. **Average out**: To calculate the average of several quantities. Example: "We need to mean out the test scores to determine the class average."
3. **Be characterized by**: To typically exhibit a certain quality or feature. Example: "This harsh winter weather means cold temperatures and heavy snowfall."

### Adjective (adj)
1. **Unkind or spiteful**: Characterized by or displaying cruelty, unkindness, or ill will. Example: "His mean behavior towards animals upset everyone."
2. **Average**: Being approximately equal to an average; medium. Example: "The mean temperature this month was lower than last year."

### Phrases
- "By means of": Using or through the use of something. Example: "They achieved success by means of hard work and determination."
- "To mean business": To be serious about one's intentions or goals. Example: "When he said he would quit, he meant business."
- "To mean well": To have good intentions even if the outcome isn't ideal. Example: "Although her advice wasn't helpful, she meant well."
