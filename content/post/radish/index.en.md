## Pronunciation
/rəˈdɪʃ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A small, white or red bulbous root vegetable with a crisp texture and slightly peppery taste. Also known as mooli in some regions.
- **Verb**: (informal) To cultivate or consume radishes, often as part of a salad or in pickling.

## Phrases
- "A plate of fresh radishes" refers to a serving of radishes as a side dish.
- "Add some radishes to your garden" suggests incorporating radishes into a vegetable plot.
- "Radish sprouts in a salad" describes using radish seeds to grow edible greens.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She sliced some radishes and added them to her sandwich for a crunchy texture."
- **Verb**: "My grandmother loves to grow a variety of radishes in her backyard garden every summer."
- **Informal**: "I just harvested a batch of radishes, they're so refreshing in this hot weather."

## Synonyms
- Daikon (for the large white variety)
- White Icicle (a specific type)
- Watermelon radish (another variety)
- Salad radish
- Turnip radish

## Antonyms
- None (since radish is a vegetable and not inherently positive or negative)
