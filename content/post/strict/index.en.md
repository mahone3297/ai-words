## Pronunciation
/ˈstrɪkt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Strict** can be used as a noun to describe a rule or standard that is very exacting or not allowing any deviation. 
   - Example: "The school has strict discipline policies."
   
### Adjective
- As an adjective, **strict** means requiring adherence to rules or regulations without leniency; severe or demanding.
   - Example: "A strict teacher keeps a watchful eye on students' behavior."
   - Example: "The strict diet helped her lose weight."

### Adverb
- It can also function as an adverb, meaning with great severity or rigor.
   - Example: "He followed the instructions strictly."
   - Example: "She was strict in her approach to teaching."

## Phrases
- "Be strict with someone" implies holding them accountable or enforcing rules firmly.
- "Under strict conditions" refers to circumstances where rules are strictly enforced.
- "A strict upbringing" suggests a parent or family environment with high expectations and few allowances.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The company has a strict policy against tardiness."
- **Adjective**: "She's strict about keeping her workspace organized."
- **Adverb**: "The chef insisted on following the recipe strictly."
