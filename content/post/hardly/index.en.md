## Pronunciation
/ˈhɑːrdli/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: (degree) To a very small extent; almost not at all. For example: "She could hardly hear him over the noise."
  - **(modal)** Used to indicate that something is difficult to do or happens only with great difficulty. For example: "I could hardly believe my eyes when I saw the news."

## Phrases
- "Hardly ever": This means almost never. For example: "She hardly ever goes out on weekends."
- "Hardly had...when": This construction is used to describe two actions happening in quick succession, often emphasizing the immediacy of the second action after the first one. Example: "Hardly had I sat down when the phone rang."
  
## Example Sentences
- **Adverb (degree)**: "The storm was so loud that we could hardly sleep last night."
- **Modal**: "He had studied for weeks, but he still found the exam questions hard to answer; he could hardly understand some of them."

## Synonyms
- Barely
- Scarcely
- Virtually not
- Almost not
- Just about not
- Not quite
- In no way (in the sense of 'not easily')
