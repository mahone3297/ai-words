## Pronunciation
/ˈvedʒətəbl/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A plant or part of a plant that is eaten as food, typically one that is not fruit or a grain.
   - Example: "She likes to eat a variety of vegetables for her lunch."
2. A person who is unsociable, dull, or uninteresting.
   - Example: "He's a bit of a vegetable when it comes to socializing."

### Verb
1. To cultivate or grow plants for food, especially vegetables.
   - Example: "Farmers grow a wide range of vegetables on their land."
2. (informal) To be unresponsive or lack energy or enthusiasm.
   - Example: "She felt like a vegetable after a long day at work."

### Adjective
1. Characterized by or containing a high proportion of vegetables or plant-based foods.
   - Example: "A vegetarian diet is rich in fiber and vitamins from vegetables."
2. Lacking vitality or animation.
   - Example: "His tired face was a clear indication that he was feeling like a vegetable."

## Phrases
- "Add more vegetables to your diet" suggests increasing consumption of vegetable-based foods.
- "He's a vegetable on the couch" implies someone is idle or unengaged.
- "A vegetable garden" refers to a plot of land where vegetables are cultivated.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She prepared a colorful salad with a variety of vegetables for the picnic."
- **Verb**: "The gardener spends hours every day tending to his vegetable patch."
- **Adjective**: "Her skin looked radiant after incorporating more vegetables into her skincare routine."
