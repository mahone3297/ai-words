## Pronunciation
/ˈmɑːnɪtər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. A device or system that displays information (such as video images or data), often used for surveillance or control purposes. For example: "The security guard watched the monitors for any unusual activity." 
2. A person who supervises or checks something, especially for conformity to rules or standards. For example: "She was appointed as a classroom monitor to help maintain discipline."
3. In computing, it refers to a type of display screen or device that serves as the primary visual interface for a user with a computer system. For example: "I just bought a new 27-inch monitor for my desktop computer."
4. In audio equipment, it can refer to a device that controls or regulates the sound level or quality during recording or performance. For example: "The sound engineer adjusted the volume on the studio monitor."

- **Verb**: 1. To observe, check, or supervise (a process, activity, or situation) for a particular purpose, usually to ensure correct functioning, adherence to rules, or safety. For instance: "The nurses monitored the patient's vital signs throughout the night."
2. To keep track of or record changes in something over time. Example: "Scientists monitor climate change by collecting data from various sources."

## Phrases
- "To monitor someone/something closely": To watch or follow someone or something very carefully.
- "Computer monitor": The display screen of a computer system.
- "Heart rate monitor": A device used to measure and display one's heart rate in real-time.
