## Pronunciation
/ˈmedɪsɪn/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. The science or practice of diagnosing, treating, and preventing diseases or injuries using various methods such as drugs, surgery, and physical therapy. Example: "She studied medicine at the university before becoming a doctor."
   - 2. Drugs or other substances used to treat or prevent disease; medication. Example: "The patient was prescribed some pain medicine."
   - 3. (in alternative medicine) Practices or treatments that are not part of standard Western medical care but are believed by some to have healing properties. Example: "Herbal medicine has been used for centuries to treat various ailments."

## Phrases
- "Take your medicine" means to accept or endure something unpleasant that one deserves or needs, often referring to a punishment or necessary treatment. Example: "After breaking the rules, he had to take his medicine and face the consequences."
- "Medicine cabinet" refers to a storage place for medicines and first aid supplies usually found in a bathroom.
- "Practice of medicine" refers to the professional activity of diagnosing and treating patients.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (traditional medicine)**: "Modern medicine has made significant strides in eradicating certain diseases."
- **Noun (medication)**: "The doctor recommended a course of antibiotics as medicine for her infection."
- **Noun (alternative medicine)**: "Acupuncture is a form of traditional Chinese medicine that involves inserting needles into specific points on the body."
