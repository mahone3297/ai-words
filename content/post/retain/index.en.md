## Pronunciation
/rɪˈteɪn/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Retention**: The act or process of holding onto something, especially information or skills. For example: "The company places great importance on employee retention."
- **Retainer**: A person or thing that keeps something in place or prevents it from falling, often referring to a financial arrangement. For example: "The lawyer charges a retainer fee for their services."
- **Retention rate**: The percentage of individuals who continue to use a product or service over a given period. For example: "The school's high retention rate indicates student satisfaction."

### Verb
- **To retain**: To keep, hold, or maintain possession of something; to remember or recall information. For example: "The students are expected to retain what they learned in class."
- **To retain counsel**: To hire and keep a lawyer for legal advice. For example: "The defendant decided to retain counsel before entering a plea."

### Adjective
- **Retained**: Belonging or kept by someone, as in a retained employee or retained earnings. For example: "The retained earnings will be reinvested in the business."

## Phrases
- "Retain knowledge" means to remember or keep information in one's memory.
- "A retained employee" is one who has been offered a long-term contract or position.
- "Retention policy" refers to a company's guidelines for keeping data or records.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The company implemented a program to improve employee retention."
- **Verb**: "She managed to retain her composure during the stressful situation."
- **Adjective**: "The client chose a retained marketing agency for their advertising needs."
