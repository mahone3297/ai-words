## Pronunciation
/ˈsɪriəs/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: 1. Having a serious tone or attitude; grave, solemn, or important. For example: "The situation is quite serious."
- 2. Showing a deep concern or seriousness; earnest, sincere. For example: "She took her responsibilities very seriously."
- 3. Severe or harsh; causing harm or pain. For example: "The punishment was serious for the crime committed."

## Noun (rarely used):**
- A condition characterized by gravity or a lack of humor. For example: "The meeting was marked by a serious atmosphere."

## Adverb**: In a serious manner or with seriousness. For example: "He spoke seriously about the issue."

## Phrases
- "Be serious" means to be attentive or not joking around.
- "Take something seriously" implies giving it importance or considering it with care.
- "A serious injury" refers to a significant health issue.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "She wore a serious expression when discussing the project's challenges."
- **Noun**: "The doctor had a serious look on their face when delivering the diagnosis."
- **Adverb**: "He always takes his work seriously and never procrastinates."
