## Pronunciation
/trʌk/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A motor vehicle with large wheels, typically one with an open box-like body for carrying goods or passengers. Example: "The delivery truck arrived at 8 am."
2. A piece of machinery or equipment used for transporting goods or materials. Example: "The construction company has a fleet of trucks for moving heavy equipment."

### Verb
1. To transport goods or people using a truck. Example: "The company trucks the produce from the farm to the market."
2. To convey or move something using a truck. Example: "They trucked the old furniture to the charity."

## Phrases
- "Drive a truck" means to operate a truck as a profession.
- "Haul something in a truck" refers to loading and transporting items.
- "Truckload" is a unit of measurement for cargo capacity.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The truck driver made a round trip across the country."
- **Verb**: "The workers were busy unloading the truck at the warehouse."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "The construction crew loaded the truck with bricks for the new building."
