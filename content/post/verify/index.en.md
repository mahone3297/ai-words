## Pronunciation
/ˈvɛrɪfaɪ/

## Definitions
### Verb
1. **Transitive verb**: To confirm or check the accuracy, truth, or validity of something by comparison, examination, or investigation. For example: "Please verify my email address."
2. **Intransitive verb**: To become convinced or certain of something's truth or accuracy. For example: "After reviewing the documents, he verified the information."

## Phrases
- "Verify a fact" means to confirm its accuracy.
- "The authenticity was verified" indicates that the genuineness was confirmed.
- "I need you to verify my purchase" requests confirmation of a transaction.

## Example Sentences
- **Transitive**: "The bank verified my identity before processing the withdrawal request."
- **Intransitive**: "His suspicions were finally verified after receiving the evidence."
- "I verified the meeting time with the secretary to ensure it was correct."

## Synonyms
- Confirm
- Validate

- Establish

## Antonyms
- Disprove
- Deny

## Usage
- In formal contexts, "verify" is often used in professional or academic settings when checking details or confirming facts.
- In informal situations, "confirm" or "check" might be more commonly used.
