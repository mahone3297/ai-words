## Pronunciation
/ˈɪntəvəl/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A period of time between two events or points, especially one during which a particular action or process occurs. For example: "There was a ten-minute interval between each speaker's presentation."
  - *Music*: A pause or break between notes, measures, or phrases in music.
  - *Mathematics*: The difference between two values or numbers; also referred to as a distance on a number line.
  - *Theater*: A brief pause during a performance, usually for the audience to rest or for set changes.

Example sentences:
- **Noun (general)**: "There will be a short interval before the next session begins."
- **Noun (music)**: "The pianist left a significant interval between the chords to create tension."
- **Noun (mathematics)**: "The interval between 0 and 10 includes all the whole numbers from 1 through 9."
- **Noun (theater)**: "During the interval, patrons can visit the bar for refreshments."

## Phrases
- "At regular intervals": Happening or appearing at equal spaces of time.
- "Interval training": A type of exercise that involves alternating periods of high-intensity effort with periods of recovery.
- "Time interval": A specific length of time between two events or moments.
