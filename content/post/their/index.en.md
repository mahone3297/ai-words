## Pronunciation
/ðeər/

## Definitions
### Noun
- Possessive form of they, indicating ownership or belonging to a group. For example: "Their cars are parked outside."
- Referring to multiple people collectively. For example: "Their team won the game."

### Pronoun
- Refers to one or more people, typically plural, without specifying exactly who. For example: "Each person should take their turn."
- Used to avoid repetition or to refer to a previously mentioned subject. For example: "She gave them instructions, and they followed them."

### Adjective
- Referring to something that belongs or is associated with a particular group. For example: "Their house has a beautiful garden."

### Adverb
- Used to indicate possession or association. For example: "She was very proud of their accomplishments."

## Phrases
- "Their belongings" refers to possessions owned by a group or individuals.
- "Their interests" denotes the things that a group or people share.
- "Their opinion" signifies the collective view of a group.
