## Pronunciation
/ˈjɔːrz/

## Definitions
- **Pronoun**: Refers to something that belongs to someone else, typically without specifying who. For example: "Please take care of your belongings."
- **Possessive Adjective**: Shows ownership or possession. For example: "This book is hers; it's yours." 

## Phrases
- "Yours truly" is a formal closing used in letters.
- "Yours sincerely" is another formal closing.
- "Yours, [Your Name]" is a way to sign off on a letter.

## Example Sentences
- **Pronoun**: "The package with the red ribbon is yours; I know it's from your sister."
- **Possessive Adjective**: "She wore her favorite necklace, which was a gift from yours truly."

## Synonyms
- Mine
- Ours (collectively)
- His / Her / Their (depending on gender or possessive)
- Property

## Note
In some cases, "yours" can also function as a noun to refer to the people who own or possess something collectively, but this usage is less common than its primary roles as a pronoun and possessive adjective.
