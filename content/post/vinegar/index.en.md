## Pronunciation
/ˈvɪŋɡər/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Vinegar**: A liquid typically made by fermenting alcohol or fruit, known for its sour taste and acidic properties. It is often used as a condiment, in cooking, and for cleaning.
- **Acidulated water**: A solution of vinegar in water, often used for food preservation or as a mild cleaning agent.

### Verb
- **To vinegar**: To treat (food, especially vegetables) with vinegar for flavor or preservation.
- **To have a vinegar attitude**: To be grumpy or uncooperative.

### Adjective
- **Vinegary**: Having the taste or smell of vinegar; sour.

### Adverb
- (informal) **Vinegarily**: In a manner reminiscent of vinegar; sourly.

## Phrases
- "Add some vinegar to the recipe" means to incorporate vinegar into a dish for flavor.
- "She uses vinegar as a natural cleaner" highlights its non-toxic cleaning properties.
- "A splash of vinegar in your salad dressing" suggests a common culinary application.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She drizzled vinegar over her coleslaw for extra tang."
- **Verb**: "The old man always vinegars his carrots before storing them."
- **Adjective**: "The salad had a vinegary tang from the lemon juice and vinegar."
