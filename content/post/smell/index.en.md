## Pronunciation
/sməl/

## Definitions
### Noun
- The quality or property of having a distinctive odor; scent.
- A sensation or perception of a particular smell.
- In British English, a derogatory term for a person's body odor or hygiene.

### Verb
- To perceive the smell of something: She smelled the roses in the garden.
- To have a sensation or awareness of a particular odor.

### Adjective
- Characterized by a particular smell: The room had a musty smell.
- Foul-smelling: The fish had a strong, unpleasant smell.

### Adverb
- Describing the manner in which something is smelled: He sniffed the air to smell the approaching storm.

## Phrases
- "Smell something" means to detect or recognize an odor.
- "Get a whiff of" implies a brief, light smell: I could barely get a whiff of the garlic bread from across the street.
- "The smell of success" is often used metaphorically to describe a positive atmosphere or accomplishment.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: The old book had a faint smell of mildew.
- **Verb**: She sniffed the air, trying to smell if anyone was cooking dinner.
- **Adjective**: The bakery window displayed freshly baked bread with a mouth-watering smell.
- **Adverb**: He walked slowly, taking in the smell of the blooming flowers.
