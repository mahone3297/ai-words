## Pronunciation
/siːz/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To take hold of something suddenly and with force, often with the intention of keeping it; to capture or grab. Also used figuratively to indicate the start of an action or event. For example: "The police seized the suspect."
- **Noun**: The act of seizing; the taking of control or possession. For example: "The government seized control of the oil fields."

## Phrases
- "Seize the day" means to make the most of the present moment.
- "Seize an opportunity" implies grabbing a favorable chance.
- "Seized by inspiration" describes a sudden burst of creativity.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "He seized the ladder to reach the high shelf."
- **Noun**: "The thief was arrested after he tried to seize the jewelry store."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "The soldiers were ordered to seize the enemy's stronghold."
