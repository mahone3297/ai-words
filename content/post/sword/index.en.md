## Pronunciation
/sɔːrd/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **sword** is a long, bladed weapon with a hilt, typically made of metal and designed for slashing and thrusting. It was commonly used in medieval times and is often associated with knights and combat.

### Verb (transitive)
To **brandish** or **wield** a sword, especially as a symbol of power or in combat. For example: "The warrior brandished his sword in defiance."

### Adjective
- **Sword-like**: Referring to the shape or appearance of a sword. For example: "The sculpture had a graceful, sword-like curve."

## Phrases
- "Draw your sword" means to unsheath and prepare a sword for use.
- "A sword fight" refers to a duel using swords.
- "Sheathed his sword" denotes the act of putting away a sword after use.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The museum displayed an impressive collection of ancient swords."
- **Verb**: "In the reenactment, the knight expertly wielded his longsword."
- **Adjective**: "The sword's blade shone brightly in the sunlight."

## Synonyms
- Sabre
-刀剑 (dāo jiàn) (Chinese)
- Katana (Japanese)
- Claymore (Scottish longsword)
- Rapier (a slender, flexible sword)
- broadsword (a heavy, double-edged sword)

## Antonyms
- Dagger (a shorter, more versatile blade)
- Shield (a defensive weapon, not a sword)
