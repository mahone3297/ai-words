## Pronunciation
/səˈfɜːrʃəl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: 1. Lacking depth or seriousness; shallow. Example: "Her understanding of the topic was quite superficial."
- **Adjective**: 2. Surface-level; not going below the surface or delving into details. Example: "The conversation was superficial, barely touching on the real issues."
- **Adverb**: In a superficial manner; without exploring or understanding fully. Example: "He gave a superficial response to the question."

## Phrases
- "Superficially polite" implies being polite on the surface but not genuine.
- "A superficial injury" refers to a minor, skin-deep wound.
- "Superficial knowledge" is limited understanding without depth.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective (Noun)**: "Her interest in art was superficial, she never truly appreciated the complexity of the works."
- **Adjective (Adjective)**: "His concern for others seemed superficial, as he rarely followed through with his promises."
- **Adverb**: "She analyzed the problem superficially, missing the underlying complexities."
