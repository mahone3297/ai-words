## Pronunciation
/ˈbuːt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Boot**: 
   - A type of footwear that covers the foot and ankle, typically made of leather or other durable material, and fastened with laces or buckles.
   - In British English, a boot can also refer to the trunk of a car.
   - A computer startup or initialization process, as in "rebooting the system."

### Verb
- **To boot** (informal):
   - To start or restart a computer.
   - To cause (a person) to leave a location by physically removing them, as in "He was booted out of the meeting for being late."
   - To give someone a ride in a vehicle.

## Phrases
- "Put on your boots" means to wear your boots before leaving.
- "Boot up" refers to starting a computer after it has been turned off or restarted.
- "Boot camp" is a military-style training program, often for new recruits.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She wore her favorite cowboy boots to the rodeo."
- **Verb**: "I need to boot my laptop before I can check my emails."
- **Informal Verb**: "The kids begged their dad to take them to the amusement park, so he had to boot up the car."
