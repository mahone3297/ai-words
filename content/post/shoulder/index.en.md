## Pronunciation
/ˈʃəʊldər/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **A part of the body**: A broad, curved bone at the top of the arm that connects the torso to the arm, typically supporting the weight of the upper limb and allowing for movement of the arm.
   - Example: "She rested her elbow on the wooden shoulder."
2. **Shoulder of responsibility**: The task or burden of taking charge or making decisions, especially in a leadership role.
   - Example: "The team leader shouldered the responsibility for the project's success."

### Verb
1. **To support with the shoulder**: To hold or bear something, often figuratively, using the shoulder for stability or balance.
   - Example: "He shouldered the heavy box onto the shelf."
2. **To bear or endure**: To tolerate or carry a burden or hardship.
   - Example: "She shouldered the emotional pain of the loss."

## Phrases
- "Put one's shoulder to the wheel" to work hard or make an effort.
- "Shoulder-to-shoulder": Side by side, in close proximity.
- "Shouldering the blame" accepting responsibility for a mistake or misfortune.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The hiker felt a sharp pain in his shoulder after the long climb."
- **Verb**: "He shouldered his way through the crowded room."
- **Metaphorical Usage**: "The team shouldered their responsibilities to finish the project on time."
