## Pronunciation
/rɪˈsiːt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. A document acknowledging payment or delivery of goods or services, typically given by a shop or organization to the customer. 2. (informal) An acknowledgment or proof of something received. 3. (US) A type of lottery ticket that is purchased.
- **Verb**: To receive or obtain (a document or item) as proof of payment or delivery.

## Phrases
- "Request a receipt" means to ask for a written confirmation of a purchase.
- "Keep your receipt" is advice to save the proof of purchase for future reference or returns.
- "A receipt for your order" is what a merchant gives to a customer after completing a sale.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After purchasing the groceries, she asked for a receipt to file her expense report."
- **Verb**: "The customer signed for the package and was given a receipt by the courier."
- **Informal**: "Just to be safe, always keep your receipt when shopping online."

## Antonyms
