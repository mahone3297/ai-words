## Word: Ambiguous

### Pronunciation
/əˈmɪbjuːɡəs/

### Definitions
- **Adjective**: Having multiple possible meanings or interpretations, often leading to confusion or uncertainty. For example: "The instructions were ambiguous, causing some confusion among the team."
- **Noun**: A thing that is open to more than one interpretation or unclear meaning. For example: "The ambiguous nature of the poem led to much debate among scholars."

### Phrases
- "An ambiguous statement" refers to one with multiple possible meanings.
- "Ambiguity in language" highlights the potential for confusion in communication.
- "Resolving ambiguity" means clarifying something that has multiple interpretations.

### Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The ambiguous policy left many employees unsure about their responsibilities."
- **Noun**: "The ambiguous plot of the novel kept readers guessing until the end."
- **Verb**: "She tried to resolve the ambiguity in the contract to avoid misunderstandings."

### Synonyms
- Vague
- Unclear
- Indeterminate
- Obscure
- Doubtful
- Ambivalent
- Ambidextrous (in a figurative sense)
