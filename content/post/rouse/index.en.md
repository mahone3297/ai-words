## Pronunciation
/ˈraʊz/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: 
  - **Transitive verb**: To awaken, stimulate, or encourage someone or something to activity or action. For example: "The teacher roused the sleeping students with a loud bell."
  - **Intransitive verb**: To become active or alert after being dormant or sleepy. For example: "The sun rising roused the birds from their nests."
- **Noun**: An act of rousing or the state of being aroused. For example: "His passionate speech roused the crowd."

## Phrases
- "Rouse someone from sleep" means to wake someone up.
- "Rouse enthusiasm" refers to generating excitement or motivation.
- "She roused herself early for the morning jog."

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The coach roused the team to perform better in the second half."
- **Noun**: "The sudden storm roused memories of the past."
- **Phrasal verb**: "He tried to rouse his audience's interest with a captivating story."
