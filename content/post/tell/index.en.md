## Pronunciation
/tɛl/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: 
  - **Base form**: tell
  - **Past tense**: told
  - **Past participle**: told
  - **Infinitive**: to tell
  - **Transitive**: To communicate information to someone, often imparting knowledge or a story. For example: "She told me a secret."
  - **Intransitive**: To express or convey a message or news. For example: "The news spread quickly, as people told each other."
  - **Interrogative**: To inquire about something. For example: "Can you tell me the time?"
  - **Modal**: To request or permit someone to communicate. For example: "You should tell them the truth."

## Phrases
- "Tell me a story" asks for a narrative.
- "Tell me about yourself" seeks personal information.
- "Don't tell anyone" implies confidentiality.
- "Tell a lie" means to deceive with false information.
- "Tell the truth" requests honesty.

## Example Sentences
- **Transitive**: "She told her children the bedtime story."
- **Intransitive**: "The news was told to the entire staff during the meeting."
- **Interrogative**: "Can you tell me how you fixed the broken vase?"
- **Modal**: "You're not allowed to tell anyone where we're going."
