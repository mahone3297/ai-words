## Word: Palm

## Pronunciation
/pɑːm/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: The inner surface of the hand that extends from the wrist to the bases of the fingers. For example: "She placed the coin in the palm of her hand."
- **Noun (n)**: The flat part of the hand used for various actions such as grasping or supporting. Example: "He caught the ball with his palm."
- **Noun (n)**: A tree having a single stem/trunk growing to considerable height and bearing large leaves that form a crownshaft. For instance: "Date palms are common in desert regions."
- **Noun (n)**: The leaf of certain plants, especially those of palms, often used decoratively. Example: "The church was decorated with palm fronds on Palm Sunday."
- **Verb (v)**: To hold something in the palm of one's hand. Example: "He palmed the key so no one would see it."
- **Verb (v)**: To conceal something in one's hand, typically by sleight of hand. Example: "The magician palmed the coin before making it disappear."
- **Verb (v)**: In sports, to catch or handle a ball using the palm of the hand, especially when this is against the rules. Example: "The goalkeeper illegally palmed the ball outside the penalty area."

## Phrases
- "In the palm of your hand" - meaning under one's control or influence. Example: "She held the future of the company in the palm of her hand."
- "Palm off" - to deceive someone by passing something worthless as valuable or genuine. Example: "He tried to palm off a fake watch as an antique."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (hand)**: "She examined the ring closely, turning it over in the palm of her hand."
- **Noun (tree)**: "The coconut palms swayed gently in the ocean breeze."
- **Verb**: "He palmed the basketball deftly, dribbling past his defender."

## Synonyms
- Hand (noun, referring to the part of the body)
- Frond (noun, referring to the leaves of a palm tree)
- Conceal (verb, in the context of hiding something in the palm)
- Handle (verb, in the context of sports)
