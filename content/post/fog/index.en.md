## Word: Fog

## Pronunciation
/fɒɡ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A visible mass of tiny water droplets suspended in the atmosphere near the Earth's surface, typically reducing visibility. For example: "The morning mist gradually thickened into a dense fog."
- **Verb**: (fog up) To cover with or as if with fog; make obscure or indistinct. For example: "The windows fogged up due to the difference in temperature."

## Phrases
- "In a fog" - to be confused or unclear in one's thoughts; not able to think clearly.
- "Fog of war" - uncertainty in situational awareness experienced by participants in military operations.
- "Drive through the fog" - to navigate or proceed through difficult or confusing circumstances.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The ship navigated cautiously through the thick fog along the coast."
- **Verb**: "My glasses always fog up when I walk from the cold outside into a warm room."

## Synonyms
- Mist
- Haze
- Smog (in the case of pollution-induced fog)
- Murk (for thicker fog)
- Vapor (in certain contexts)

## Antonyms
- Clear (in the sense of weather conditions)
- Visibility (as fog reduces it) 

**Note:** The verb form does not have direct antonyms but could be contrasted with actions like "clearing up" or "defogging."
