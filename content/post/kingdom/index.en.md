## Word: Kingdom

## Pronunciation
/ˈkɪŋdəm/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A country, state, or territory ruled by a king or queen, or a monarchy as a form of government. Example: "The kingdom was known for its rich history and powerful monarchy."
  - In biology, it can refer to one of the taxonomic ranks used in classifying organisms, above the level of phylum and below domain. Example: "In the animal kingdom, mammals belong to the largest group."
  - Figuratively, it can also mean a domain or sphere of activity or influence. Example: "Music was his kingdom; he reigned supreme in that realm."

## Phrases
- "Kingdom of God": A term referring to the spiritual reign or rule of God over all creation, often seen in religious contexts.
- "Kingdom Come": An idiomatic expression meaning 'at some indefinitely distant time in the future', e.g., "I'll do it when the kingdom comes."
- "Animal Kingdom": Refers to the totality of all animals on Earth.
  
## Example Sentences
- **Noun (government)**: "The kingdom's prosperity was built on trade and diplomacy."
- **Noun (biology)**: "Biologists have classified humans as part of the Animal Kingdom."
- **Noun (figurative)**: "She rules her own little kingdom in the kitchen, crafting culinary masterpieces."
