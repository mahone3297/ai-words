## Pronunciation
/prɪns/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A male member of a royal family, typically the son and heir apparent to the throne. [Oxford](https://en.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/prince) / [Cambridge](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/prince)
- **Adjective**: Having qualities or characteristics associated with royalty, such as dignity or elegance. [Cambridge](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/princely)

## Phrases
- "Royal prince" refers to a prince who belongs to a royal family.
- "Princely behavior" denotes actions befitting a prince, often noble or dignified.
- "Prince charming" is a metaphor for an idealized, attractive man, especially in fairy tales.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The prince was seen attending a charity event last week."
- **Adjective**: "She had a princely manner, always treating others with respect."
- "The prince charming arrived at the ball, captivating everyone with his grace."

## Etymology
The word "prince" comes from the Old French "prince" or "princeps," which ultimately derived from the Latin term "princeps" meaning "first citizen" or "ruler."
