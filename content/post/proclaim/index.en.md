## Pronunciation
/prəˈklaɪm/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Proclamation**: An official statement or declaration made by a person or authority, often publicly, to announce or declare something. For example: "The mayor made a proclamation declaring a day of peace."
- **Announcement**: A public statement or declaration, typically about an event or piece of news. For example: "The school made an announcement about the new schedule."

### Verb
- **To proclaim**: To make a loud and public announcement, declare, or assert with authority. For example: "The president proclaimed the nation's commitment to climate action."
- **To declare**: To formally state or announce, often with emphasis on its significance. For example: "The judge declared the defendant not guilty."

### Adjective
- **Proclaimed**: Officially announced or declared. For example: "The proclaimed winner of the election."

## Phrases
- "Proclaim victory" means to publicly declare success or triumph.
- "Proclaimed sanctuary" refers to a place officially designated as safe.
- "Proclamation of independence" marks the start of a new nation.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The mayor issued a proclamation to honor the local heroes."
- **Verb**: "The president proclaimed the new tax reforms across the country."
- **Adjective**: "The proclaimed champion was crowned at the sports event."
