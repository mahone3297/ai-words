## Pronunciation
/ˈdɔːtər/

## Definitions
### Noun
A daughter is a female offspring, typically the child of a parent or parents, particularly one born to the mother. **[Etymology](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/daughter#Etymology)**: From Old French *dauter* (feminine form), from Latin *dētērum*, meaning "second-born".

### Verb
To be or act as a daughter, especially in terms of inheritance or familial relationship. **[Usage](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/daughter)**: She was the daughter who took care of her elderly parents.

## Phrases
- "Be a good daughter" is a request for loyalty and respect.
- "Daughter of the house" refers to a family member who is considered the head or caretaker.
- "Daughter's pride" symbolizes a parent's love and admiration for their child.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She was the youngest daughter in a large family."
- **Verb**: "As a daughter, she inherited her father's entrepreneurial spirit."
- **Phrasal Usage**: "She felt a deep sense of responsibility as the daughter of the house."
