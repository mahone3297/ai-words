## Pronunciation
/rɪˈplaɪ/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A noun referring to a response**: The act of answering or responding to something, typically a question or comment. For example: "He waited for her reply."
- **A message or answer**: A written or spoken response to a communication. For example: "I received no reply to my email."

### Verb
- **To respond**: To give an answer or reaction to something said or asked. For example: "She replied promptly to his question."
- **To answer**: To give a response to a query or request. For example: "Please reply to this message."

### Adjective
- **Relevant or appropriate**: Expressing a fitting or suitable response. For example: "Her reply was concise and to the point."

## Phrases
- "Reply to a message" means to send a response to a written communication.
- "I'm awaiting your reply" indicates that someone is expecting a response from you.
- "No reply needed" signifies that an answer isn't necessary or expected.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The customer's reply to our inquiry contained valuable feedback."
- **Verb**: "After reading the letter, he sat down to reply."
- **Adjective**: "His reply was well-researched and showed he understood the topic."
