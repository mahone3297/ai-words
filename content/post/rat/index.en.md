## Pronunciation
/ræt/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A small, typically brown or gray rodent with a pointed snout, long tail, and sharp teeth, often considered a pest.
   - Example: "The rat scurried across the kitchen floor."
   - Synonyms: vermin, rodent, Norway rat, sewer rat

2. [slang] A person, especially a man, who is cowardly or despicable.
   - Example: "He's such a rat, always betraying his friends."

### Verb
1. To act like a rat, betray, or be untrustworthy.
   - Example: "He ratted on his coworker for taking the office supplies without permission."
   - Synonym: inform, snitch

2. To escape or flee quickly, often in a panic.
   - Example: "The rats fled the burning building."

### Adjective
1. Deceitful or treacherous.
   - Example: "A rat deal won't last long."

2. Diseased or contaminated.
   - Example: "The food was tainted with rat poison."

## Phrases
- "Rat race" refers to a stressful, competitive lifestyle.
- "Get rid of the rat infestation" means to eliminate a population of rats.
- "Rat-a-tat-tat" is a sound imitating rapid gunfire or machine gun fire.
