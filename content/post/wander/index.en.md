## Pronunciation
/wɒndər/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A leisurely or aimless movement, often without a specific destination: *The children wandered through the park.*
2. An act of moving from one place to another without a fixed plan: *He spent his day wandering around the city.*
3. A train of thought or imagination that is not focused: *Her mind wandered during the boring lecture.*

### Verb
1. To move without a particular purpose or direction: *She wandered through the forest, lost in thought.*
2. To stray or deviate from a planned course or subject: *His thoughts wandered during the conversation.*
3. (figurative) To muse or reflect: *He wandered about philosophical questions.*

## Phrases
- "Wander off" means to go away without permission or without a clear destination.
- "Wanderlust" refers to a strong desire to travel or explore new places.
- "Lost in thought" implies someone is wandering their mind while physically present.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After the long hike, they collapsed on a bench, feeling the weariness of their wanderings."
- **Verb**: "The old man would often wander down by the river, reminiscing about his past."
- **Adverb**: "She wandered aimlessly through the aisles of the bookstore."
