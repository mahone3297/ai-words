## Pronunciation
/ˈmɪnərəl/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A naturally occurring, inorganic substance with a definite chemical composition and a crystalline structure. Minerals are typically formed through geological processes and are considered the building blocks of rocks. For example: "Iron is a common metallic mineral found in the Earth's crust."

  - _Example Sentence:_ "The region is rich in valuable minerals such as gold and silver."
  
- **Noun (n)**: Nutrient-rich substances that are essential for maintaining good health and proper bodily function. These include calcium, magnesium, and zinc, which can be obtained from dietary sources like fruits, vegetables, and water. Example: "Calcium is an important mineral for bone health."

  - _Example Sentence:_ "She takes daily supplements to ensure she gets all her essential minerals."

## Phrases
- "Mineral deposit": A natural accumulation of minerals in the earth's crust, often in economically viable quantities.
- "Mineral water": Water that contains various dissolved minerals, which may offer health benefits or give it a distinctive taste.
- "Vitamin and mineral supplement": A nutritional supplement that provides additional vitamins and minerals to one's diet.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The geologist was studying the formation and distribution of different minerals in the mountain range."
- **Noun**: "A balanced diet should include a variety of fruits and vegetables to supply the body with necessary minerals."
