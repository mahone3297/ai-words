## Pronunciation
/plænˈteɪʃ(ə)n/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 
1. A large area of land where trees, crops, or plants, especially those intended for commercial cultivation, are planted and managed together.
   Example: "The plantation was once a major source of cotton production."
2. An establishment or estate where slaves or indentured laborers were worked, typically in the context of the American South or British colonies.
   Example: "The historical plantation house still stands on the grounds."

- **Verb**: To establish or set up a plantation, often referring to the planting of a large scale agricultural operation.
   Example: "They planned to plantation a new orchard next year."

## Phrases
- "Plantation agriculture" refers to the large-scale cultivation of cash crops.
- "Slave plantation" is used to describe the system of forced labor on plantations.
- "Coffeeplantation" or "Tea plantation" denote specific types of plantation with a focus on a single crop.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The plantation's workers carefully tended to the sugar cane fields."
- **Verb**: "The family decided to start a small fruit plantation in their backyard."
- **Historical**: "The plantation system played a significant role in shaping the region's economy."
