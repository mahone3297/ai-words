## Pronunciation
/ˈsaʊnd/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The sensation of hearing, particularly as produced by a vibrating object, like a musical instrument or a spoken word. Example: "The sound of the ocean waves was soothing."
2. A particular auditory quality or characteristic of a voice, instrument, or noise. Example: "Each bird has its unique sound."
3. A noise or tone, often associated with a specific event or object. Example: "The sound of breaking glass echoed through the room."
4. A piece of music or a musical composition. Example: "She played a beautiful sound on her violin."

### Verb
1. To produce or emit sound, especially intentionally. Example: "The musician sounded the trumpet."
2. To perceive or become aware of a sound. Example: "I could hear the sound of footsteps approaching."
3. To cause or contribute to a particular effect or atmosphere with one's voice or sounds. Example: "Her soothing voice sounded like lullabies."

## Phrases
- "Make a sound" means to produce any audible noise.
- "Silent as a sound" is a paradoxical expression that implies something is completely quiet.
- "Soundproofing" refers to the reduction of sound transmission.
