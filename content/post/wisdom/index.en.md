## Pronunciation
/wɪzdəm/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Wisdom**: The ability to use knowledge and experience to make good judgments and decisions, often showing insight and understanding. For example: "She gained wisdom through her life experiences."
- **Counsel**: Advice or guidance given to help someone make a decision or understand a situation. For example: "Her mentor provided valuable counsel on her career path."

### Verb
- To impart wisdom: To share knowledge or advice that can guide or enlighten someone. For example: "The elder imparted wisdom to the young generation."
- To seek wisdom: To actively look for knowledge or guidance to improve oneself. For example: "Many people seek wisdom from books and mentors."

### Adjective
- Wise: Having or showing great knowledge and understanding, often indicating sound judgment. For example: "The wise old man offered sage advice."

## Phrases
- "Act with wisdom" encourages making well-considered decisions.
- "Seeking wisdom" implies a quest for knowledge and understanding.
- "The wisdom of the crowd" suggests the collective intelligence of many people.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "His wisdom was respected by all who knew him."
- **Verb**: "She decided to follow her heart after seeking wisdom from her elders."
- **Adjective**: "The wise leader made tough decisions based on sound judgment."
