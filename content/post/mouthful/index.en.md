## Word: Mouthful

## Pronunciation
/ˈmaʊθfʊl/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A quantity of food or drink that can be taken into the mouth at one time. For example: "The baby took a small mouthful of mashed peas."
- **Noun (figurative)**: A large amount of something that is difficult to say or swallow, often referring to information or opinions. For example: "His speech was a mouthful of complex economic theories."
- **Adjective**: Describing something that requires a lot of effort or capacity to manage or consume due to its size or complexity. For example: "The new vocabulary words were quite a mouthful for the beginner learners."

## Phrases
- "A mouthful to say": Refers to a word or phrase that is lengthy or difficult to pronounce.
- "A mouthful of marbles": An idiom used to describe someone who is speaking unclearly or with difficulty, possibly because their mouth is full or they have a speech impediment.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (literal)**: "She took a big mouthful of water to quench her thirst."
- **Noun (figurative)**: "Understanding quantum physics is quite a mouthful for most people."
- **Adjective**: "That long medical term is really a mouthful; let's find a simpler way to explain it."

## Synonyms
- Bite (literal sense)
- Swallow (literal sense)
- Chunk (of information)
- Load (figurative sense)
- Whopper (informal, for a large or complex statement)
