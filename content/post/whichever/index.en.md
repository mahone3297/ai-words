## Pronunciation
/ˈwaɪnəʊ/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Referring to one of several alternatives, without specifying which one. It can also be used to indicate preference or availability. For example: "Whichever dress you choose, it will look great on you."
- **Pronoun**: Used to refer to either of two or more people or things, without specifying which one. For example: "Whichever one arrives first, please let them know I'm expecting them."

## Phrases
- "Whichever of these" implies a choice between a few options.
- "Whichever suits your preference" indicates flexibility according to someone's liking.
- "Whichever comes first" refers to the first item or event that fulfills a condition.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "You can have whichever slice of cake you want; they're all equally delicious."
- **Pronoun**: "Whichever team wins, we'll celebrate together."
