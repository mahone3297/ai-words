## Pronunciation
/vəˈkæbjələri/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A collection of words, terms, or phrases that a person knows and uses to communicate, especially in a particular field or language: *She has an extensive vocabulary in French.*
2. The ability to use words effectively and with a wide range of expressions: *His vocabulary is rich and diverse.*

### Verb
1. To acquire or learn a set of words or expressions: *She's been working on expanding her vocabulary.*
2. To use words fluently and precisely: *Reading extensively helps to improve one's vocabulary.*

### Adjective
1. Relating to the set of words known by a person or group: *The curriculum focuses on developing students' technical vocabulary.*
2. Comprising a large or varied number of words: *A good writer has a broad and nuanced vocabulary.*

## Phrases
- "Expand your vocabulary" means to increase the range of words you know and use.
- "Increase one's vocabulary" refers to improving one's word knowledge.
- "Vocabulary learning" is the process of acquiring new words.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She had an impressive vocabulary, able to discuss complex ideas with ease."
- **Verb**: "Regular reading helps to build and maintain a strong vocabulary."
- **Adjective**: "The teacher emphasized the importance of having a rich vocabulary for writing."
