## Pronunciation
/ˈlɪbərəl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: (adj) 1. Open-minded or tolerant, especially free from prejudice or bias; characterized by a willingness to respect and accept different opinions, lifestyles, or conditions. Example: "He has a liberal attitude towards different cultures."
   2. Generous or bounteous, often referring to the giving or sharing of things in abundance. Example: "The millionaire was known for his liberal donations to charity."
   3. In politics, it refers to advocating individual freedom, social equality, and government intervention for the purpose of promoting social justice. Example: "She identifies with the liberal wing of the political party."

- **Noun**: (n) A person who holds such political views that support individual rights, civil liberties, and democratic reform. Example: "Many liberals argue for increased funding for education and healthcare."

## Phrases
- "Liberal arts" refers to academic studies that aim to develop general knowledge and intellectual abilities rather than professional or vocational skills.
- "Liberal democracy" is a form of government where power is held by the people and their elected representatives, with protection for minority rights and civil liberties.
- "Liberal policies" refer to those aimed at reducing economic inequality, protecting personal freedoms, and promoting social welfare.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "His liberal approach to parenting allowed his children to explore their own interests freely."
- **Noun**: "As a liberal, he supports progressive taxation and universal access to public services."
