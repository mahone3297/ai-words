# Layer

## Pronunciation
/ˈleɪər/

## Definitions
### Noun (n)
- A level or thickness of material that covers or underlies something else; often used in reference to a stratum in the earth's surface, the layers of an onion, or the construction materials in a building. For example: "The archaeologists discovered several ancient layers beneath the city."
- In technology, a layer can refer to a level or tier within a system, such as network layers or software architecture layers. Example: "Firewall rules were implemented at the application layer to enhance security."
- In art, it could mean a coat or level of paint or other medium applied to a surface. Example: "The artist meticulously added another layer of oil paint to the canvas."
- In fashion, it might denote an item of clothing worn over another, especially in colder weather. Example: "She wore multiple layers to protect herself from the cold."

### Verb (v)
- To arrange or stack things in layers. Example: "She layered the lasagna with alternating sheets of pasta and cheese."
- To apply something in layers. Example: "The decorator will layer the walls with a primer before painting."

## Phrases
- "Layer by layer": referring to the process of adding or removing parts in a systematic, step-by-step manner.
- "Onion layers": metaphorically used to describe something with many complex or hidden levels.
  
## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The cake had three layers of sponge and cream filling."
- **Verb**: "She layered the blankets on the bed to make it extra cozy."
