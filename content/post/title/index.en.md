## Pronunciation
/tɪˈtaɪl/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A heading or caption that identifies or describes the content of a book, article, or other written work.
   - Example: "The title of the novel is 'To Kill a Mockingbird.'"
2. The position or rank given to a person, especially in a competition or organization.
   - Example: "She won first place with her impressive title in the chess tournament."
3. A formal name or designation, often used to address someone.
   - Example: "Please address me by my title, Sir/Madam."

### Verb
1. To give or assign a title to something, such as a book or a person.
   - Example: "The author decided to title her next book 'The Unseen World.'"
2. To refer to or call someone by their title.
   - Example: "He introduced his friend, the Duke of Edinburgh, with great respect."

### Adjective
1. Characterizing or designating the main subject or topic of a work.
   - Example: "The title track on the album is a powerful ballad."
2. Describing a high rank or importance.
   - Example: "She holds a prestigious title within the company."

## Phrases
- "Choose a suitable title" means to select an appropriate name or heading.
- "She wrote a book titled 'The History of..." 
- "He was promoted to a higher title within the company."
