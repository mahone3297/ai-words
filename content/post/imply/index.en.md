# Imply

## Pronunciation
/ɪmˈplaɪ/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To suggest something without stating it directly; to indicate or convey a meaning indirectly.

### Examples
- **Verb**: In his statement, he implied that he was not happy with the decision.
  - Sentence: "The manager's silence implied her disapproval of the proposal."
  
- **Verb (law)**: To involve or entail by logical necessity, even if not explicitly stated.
  - Sentence: "The terms of the contract imply certain obligations on both parties."

## Phrases
- "Imply something" means to suggest or hint at something without actually saying it.
- "To be implied" means that something can be understood from what has been said or done, although it is not directly expressed.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "Her sarcastic tone implied that she was not impressed with the performance."
- **Verb (law)**: "The law implies a warranty of fitness for purpose when buying goods."

## Synonyms
- Suggest
- Indicate
- Convey
- Hint
- Signal
- Impute
- Stipulate (in the legal sense)
- Entail (in the legal sense)
