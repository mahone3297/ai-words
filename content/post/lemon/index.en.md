## Pronunciation
/ˈlɛmən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A citrus fruit with a bright yellow outer rind and sour, juicy inner flesh. It's widely used in cooking and for its juice, which has a tangy flavor. For example: "She added a slice of lemon to her water for a refreshing taste."
- **Adjective**: Relating to or having the flavor or aroma of lemons. For example: "The cake had a delightful lemon filling."

### Noun usage examples
- "The chef squeezed fresh lemon over the grilled fish to enhance its flavor."
- "She made a pitcher of lemonade using freshly squeezed lemons from her garden."

### Adjective usage examples
- "She loves the zesty scent of lemon-scented cleaning products."
- "The lemon sorbet was the perfect palate cleanser between courses."

## Phrases
- "When life gives you lemons, make lemonade": This idiomatic expression suggests that one should turn a difficult situation into something positive or beneficial.
- "To have a face like a lemon": An informal British expression describing a facial expression of disappointment or disapproval.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "I accidentally squirted lemon juice into my eye while preparing dinner."
- **Adjective**: "The lemon zest added a burst of flavor to the dessert."

## Synonyms
- Citrus limon
- Lemon tree (referring to the plant itself)
- Lemon juice (referring to the liquid extracted from the fruit)
- Lemon flavor (when referring to its taste)
- Lemon-colored (when referring to its bright yellow hue)
