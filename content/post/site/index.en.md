## Pronunciation
/saɪt/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A place where something occurs or is located; a location. **Example:** "The construction site is busy today."
2. (Internet) A specific web page or collection of related pages forming part of a larger website. **Example:** "The company's homepage is the main site."
3. A place where people gather for a particular activity or event. **Example:** "The archaeological site holds ancient artifacts."
4. A physical or virtual location where a business operates. **Example:** "The retail store has several branch sites across the city."

### Verb
1. To set up or establish a site. **Example:** "They are in the process of siting a new office building."
2. To choose or select a suitable location for something. **Example:** "The developers decided to site the solar farm in a sunny area."

### Adjective
1. Characterizing a particular place or location. **Example:** "The coastal site is known for its beautiful views."
2. Relating to the internet or websites. **Example:** "A site-specific artwork created for the web."

## Phrases
- "Visit a site" means to go to a particular location or website.
- "Off-site meeting" refers to a meeting held away from the usual workplace.
- "Site map" is a visual representation of a website's structure.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The archeological team excavated artifacts from the ancient site."
- **Verb**: "We need to find a suitable site for our community garden project."
- **Adjective**: "The site-specific installation was designed to interact with its surroundings."
