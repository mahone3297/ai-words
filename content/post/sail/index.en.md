## Pronunciation
/səˈleɪ/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Sail**: A large piece of fabric or membrane stretched on a frame, used to catch the wind and propel a boat or ship through the water.
- **Sailing**: The activity or sport of using sails to navigate a boat or ship.

### Verb
- **To sail**: To navigate a boat or ship by using sails, often referring to the act of hoisting or adjusting sails to catch the wind.
- **Sailed**: Past tense of "to sail," indicating the action of navigating with sails.

### Adjective
- **Sailing**: Relating to the use of sails for movement; pertaining to boats or ships that rely on wind power.

### Adverb
- **Sailing**: In a manner involving the use of sails; by means of sailing.

## Phrases
- "Sailing the open seas"
- "Sailing into the sunset"
- "Learning to sail"
- "Sailing along"

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The sailors hoisted the sails to catch the wind."
- **Verb**: "We decided to sail across the lake this weekend."
- **Adjective**: "The yacht was a sleek, modern sailing vessel."
- **Adverb**: "She sailed gracefully around the harbor."
