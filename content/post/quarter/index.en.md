## Pronunciation
/ˈkwɔːrtər/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **A unit of measurement** (used with countable nouns): A quarter of a dollar, a quarter of an hour.
   Example: "The pizza was cut into four equal quarters."
2. **One of four equal parts** of something, typically a circular shape or a period of time: The top quarter of the field, the first quarter of the year.
   Example: "She finished the first quarter of the marathon in record time."
3. **A person's share** in a group or team: Each player gets one quarter of the ball.
   Example: "The company is divided into four quarters, with each manager responsible for their respective teams."

### Verb
1. **To divide something into four equal parts**.
   Example: "They quartered the land among the four sons."
2. **To allocate or distribute something equally among four people or groups**.
   Example: "The cake was quartered among the guests."

## Phrases
- "Quarter of an hour" refers to 15 minutes.
- "Quarterfinals" are the last eight teams competing in a tournament, before the semifinals.
- "Quarterback" is the leader of the offensive team in American football.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She saved a quarter of her monthly salary for emergencies."
- **Verb**: "The teacher asked the students to quarter the apples before eating them."
- **Noun as verb**: "We'll meet in the middle of the playground, near the quarter pole."
