## Word: Mill

## Pronunciation
/mɪl/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A building or establishment equipped with machinery for grinding grain into flour or for processing other materials like paper, textiles, or metals. For example: "The old watermill still stands by the riverbank."
- **Noun (n)**: A machine used for grinding, crushing, or pulverizing, especially one that grinds grain into flour. For instance: "They restored the antique stone mill."
- **Noun (n)**: A factory or workshop where manufacturing takes place, often using heavy machinery. Example: "The steel mill employed many workers in the town."
- **Verb (v)**: To grind, crush, or process something using a mill. Example: "We mill our own wheat to make bread."
- **Verb (v)**: To move around in a circle, often repeatedly, typically in a confused or aimless manner. Example: "The children were milling about in the playground."
- **Verb (v)**: In sports, to maneuver an opponent strategically so as to create advantageous positioning. Example: "The quarterback successfully milled the defense before throwing the touchdown pass."

## Phrases
- "Put through the mill": To subject someone or something to a series of difficult tests or treatments. Example: "The new recruits were put through their paces at boot camp."
- "Run of the mill": Average or ordinary; not exceptional. Example: "This restaurant serves run-of-the-mill food but it's quite affordable."
  
## Example Sentences
- **Noun (as a building)**: "The village relied on the old mill for its daily bread."
- **Noun (as a machine)**: "The farmer feeds the corn into the mill to produce cornmeal."
- **Verb**: "The farmer mills his grains to ensure the highest quality flour for his bakery."
- **Verb (moving around)**: "After the concert, fans were milling outside the venue hoping for autographs."
- **Sports usage**: "The basketball player expertly milled around defenders to get an open shot."
