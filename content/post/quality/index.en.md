## Pronunciation
/ˈkwɒləti/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Quality**: 1. A characteristic or attribute that determines the value, excellence, or desirability of something. For example: "The hotel's quality of service was exceptional."
- 2. Standard or degree of excellence, often used in a comparative form (e.g., high quality, low quality). For example: "This product has superior quality compared to others on the market."

### Adjective
- **Of high quality**: Expressing excellence or superiority in terms of quality. For example: "A high-quality piece of furniture will last for years."
- **Good quality**: Demonstrating satisfactory or desirable characteristics. For example: "The ingredients used in this food are of good quality."

### Adverb
- **Qualitatively**: In terms of quality; regarding the nature or character. For example: "The results improved qualitatively after implementing new methods."

## Phrases
- "Maintain quality control" means to ensure that products or services meet a consistent standard.
- "Focus on quality over quantity" emphasizes the importance of producing well-made items rather than just producing many.
- "A commitment to quality" reflects a dedication to excellence in all aspects of work or life.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The restaurant's reputation relies on the quality of its cuisine."
- **Adjective**: "She chose a high-quality fabric for her dress."
- **Adverb**: "The teacher praised the students' work qualitatively, noting their progress."
