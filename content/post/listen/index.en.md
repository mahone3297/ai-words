## Pronunciation
/ˈlɪs·ən/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To give one's attention to sounds or words, especially by directing one's ears towards a speaker or source of sound, with the intent to understand or hear something clearly. For example: "Listen carefully to what I'm saying."
- **Noun (less common)**: An act of listening; a hearing. For example: "She gave him a patient listen before responding."

### Verb Usage Examples
- "The children were asked to listen attentively to the teacher's instructions."
- "I could hear her crying, but I couldn't listen because I was too busy."

### Noun Usage Example
- "His first public listen came after months of practice in his bedroom."

## Phrases
- "Listen up" - A command to pay close attention to what is about to be said.
- "Listen out for" - To be attentive to hear something specific, e.g., "Listen out for the postman."
- "Listen to reason" - To consider logical arguments or advice sensibly.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "We need to listen closely to our customers' feedback if we want to improve our service."
- **Noun**: "After the concert, we sat down for a listen to the live recording."
