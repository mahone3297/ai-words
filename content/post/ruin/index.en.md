## Pronunciation
/rɪˈnjuː/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. The complete destruction or devastation of something, often leaving it in a state of collapse or decay. 2. Ruins can also refer to the remains of a destroyed structure or site. 3. (figurative) A person's social standing or reputation, often ruined by misfortune or mistake.
- **Verb**: To cause the complete destruction or downfall of; to devastate. 2. To spoil or corrupt (a person, relationship, or situation).

## Phrases
- "In ruins" describes something that is severely damaged or destroyed.
- "She watched her dreams go up in smoke, and her life was left in ruins."
- "The ancient city lies in ruins after the devastating war."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The once-grand mansion had been reduced to nothing but a pile of ruins after the fire."
- **Verb**: "The reckless spending habits eventually ruined his financial security."
- **As a figure of speech**: "Her reputation was ruined by those false accusations."
