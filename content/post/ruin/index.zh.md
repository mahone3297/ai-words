## 读音
/ruːˈɪn/

## 释义
- **名词**: 倒塌的建筑物或遗迹；废墟。例如: "The ancient city is now just a ruin."
- **动词**: 摧毁，破坏，使破产。例如: "The flood ruined many homes in the region."
- **形容词**: 毁灭性的，破败不堪的。例如: "The storm left the town in a ruined state."

## 短语
- "in ruins" 意为成为废墟，破败不堪。例如: "After the war, the city was in ruins."
- "ruin one's reputation" 意为毁掉某人的名誉。例如: "His dishonesty could ruin his reputation."

## 例句
- 名词: "The archaeologists were exploring the ruins of an ancient civilization."
- 动词: "Heavy rain ruined our outdoor picnic plans."
- 形容词: "The old castle stood proudly, its walls still standing despite being in a ruined condition."
