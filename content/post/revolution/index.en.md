## Pronunciation
/rɪˌvəˈluːʃən/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A sudden, complete, or radical change in the political, social, or economic structure of a country, especially one that involves replacing one form of government with another.
   - Example: The French Revolution marked a significant turning point in French history.
2. A series of rapid and dramatic changes, often in a particular field or industry.
   - Example: The digital revolution transformed the way we communicate and work.

### Verb
1. To bring about or undergo a fundamental change, typically a social or political one.
   - Example: The society is striving for a peaceful revolution to address social inequalities.
2. To cause a sudden and dramatic change in a system or process.
   - Example: The new technology revolutionized the manufacturing industry.

### Adjective
1. Characterized by or causing a revolution; revolutionary.
   - Example: A revolutionary new approach to education

## Phrases
- "Bring about a revolution" means to initiate a major change.
- "Social revolution" refers to a transformation in societal structures.
- "Technological revolution" denotes a rapid advancement in technology.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: The Industrial Revolution sparked a period of unprecedented economic growth.
- **Verb**: She believes that this policy will revolutionize healthcare access.
- **Adjective**: The green energy movement is a revolutionary shift in our energy production.
