## Pronunciation
/sɪt/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (n) To place oneself in a seated position on a chair, bench, or other surface. For example: "Please have a seat."
- **Verb**: (v) To occupy oneself in an activity or wait for something to happen, often passively. For example: "She sat quietly reading a book."
- **Noun**: The act of sitting or the state of being seated. For example: "The children were sitting around the table."

## Phrases
- "Sit down" is an instruction to take a seat.
- "Sitting hours" refers to the time spent in a seated position, often at work.
- "To sit out" means to abstain from participating in an activity.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "After a long day, he collapsed onto the couch and sat."
- **Noun**: "The park was filled with people sitting on benches, enjoying the sunshine."
- **Verb**: "She decided to sit out the game due to an injury."
