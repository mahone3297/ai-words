## Pronunciation
/ˈrɒtn/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: 1. Having a bad smell or appearance due to decay or rot; spoiled. "The fruit was rotten." 
- 2. Diseased or unhealthy; corrupt. "His character is rotten."
- 3. Characterized by moral corruption or immorality. "A rotten system"
- 4. (informal) Very bad or extremely unpleasant. "That party was a rotten time."

## Phrases
- "Rotten eggs" is a metaphor for something of poor quality or unacceptable.
- "Rotten apple" is a similar metaphor for a person who has a corrupting influence.
- "Rotten to the core" describes someone who is fundamentally dishonest or corrupt.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The rotten fish had started to stink up the whole kitchen."
- **Adjective**: "After his betrayal, his once-promising career was rotten beyond repair."
- **Adverb**: "She couldn't stand the rotten smell coming from the compost heap."
