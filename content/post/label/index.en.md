## Word: Label

## Pronunciation
/ˈleɪbl/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A small piece of paper, fabric, plastic, or similar material attached to an object and giving information about it, often its name, manufacturer, instructions, or uses. For example: "The price tag was the only label on the product."
  - Also, a descriptive category or term applied to a person or thing, especially one that suggests a stereotype. Example: "They refused to be confined by society's labels."
- **Verb**: To attach a label to something as a means of identification or to describe it. Example: "She carefully labeled each jar with the type of jam it contained."
  - Also, to classify or identify someone or something according to a particular characteristic or category. Example: "The media tends to label certain politicians as 'liberal' or 'conservative'."

## Phrases
- "Labeling system": A method of organizing items using labels to denote categories, contents, or ownership.
- "Label maker": A device used to create and affix labels.
- "To label someone/something as": To categorize or identify someone or something in a specific way, which can sometimes carry a negative connotation if it involves stereotyping.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The nutrition label on the cereal box showed the ingredients and calorie count."
- **Verb**: "Each file needs to be correctly labeled before we put them in storage."
