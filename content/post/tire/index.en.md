## Pronunciation
/tɪr/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. A device with a rubber or pneumatic outer covering that provides traction on a vehicle's wheel, allowing it to move. 2. Fatigue or weariness, especially from driving or physical exertion. 3. (informal) A state of being bored or disinterested. 4. (US) A tireless person or thing.
- **Verb**: To become worn out or exhausted, typically due to continuous use or effort. 2. To replace or change a tire on a vehicle.

## Phrases
- "Tire pressure" refers to the air pressure inside a tire.
- "Tire rotation" is the process of changing the position of tires on a vehicle to ensure even wear.
- "Tire change" is the act of replacing a flat or worn tire with a new one.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The car needed new tires after the long journey."
- **Verb**: "I had to stop and tire the car's spare tire because of a puncture."
- **Adjective**: "The driver felt tired after a marathon of back-to-back meetings."
