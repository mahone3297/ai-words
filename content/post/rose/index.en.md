## Pronunciation
/rəʊz/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A [noun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noun) referring to the flower of the Rosa genus, often symbolizing love, beauty, or springtime. Example: "She planted a bed of roses in her garden."
- A [noun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noun) used informally to refer to the act of rising or coming up, especially from a low position. Example: "The sun rose early that morning."

### Verb
- [Verb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Verb) meaning to grow or come into existence, typically as a result of development or improvement. Example: "The company's profits have really risen over the past year."
- Occasionally used informally to mean to ascend or go up, as in the case of a person or object. Example: "The children rose to their feet when they heard the bell."

### Adjective
- An [adjective](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adjective) describing something that is pink, red, or having a sweet fragrance, like a particular type of flower. Example: "A fragrant rose filled the air with its aroma."
- Sometimes used metaphorically to describe a person who has improved or become better. Example: "Her confidence rose after receiving positive feedback."

### Adverb
- An [adverb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adverb) describing the manner in which something rises or grows. Example: "The temperature rose steadily throughout the day."

## Phrases
- "Grow from a rose bush" signifies growth or development from a simple beginning.
- "Rose to fame" describes gaining recognition or popularity.
- "Rise to the occasion" implies behaving well or adapting to a challenging situation.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She picked a delicate rose from the garden to give to her friend."
- **Verb**: "The stock market rose sharply after the release of good earnings reports."
- **Adjective**: "The bride carried a bouquet of red roses on her wedding day."
- **Adverb**: "The sun rose slowly over the horizon, casting a warm glow across the landscape."
