## Word: Justice

## Pronunciation
/ ˈdʒʌstice /

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The quality of being fair and reasonable in the way people are treated; fairness and impartiality, especially in the administration of law. For example: "The court's decision upheld the principles of justice."
- **Noun (Law)**: The administration of the law or authority given to judges to administer the law; the legal system that maintains social order through the punishment of crimes. Example: "The trial was conducted with due process and justice was served."
- **Noun (Philosophy)**: A moral principle dictating that people receive what they deserve, whether it be reward or punishment. Example: "Injustice anywhere is a threat to justice everywhere."
  
## Phrases
- "Justice system": The network of courts, judges, lawyers, and laws responsible for upholding justice in society.
- "Seek justice": To pursue a fair resolution or to right a wrong.
- "Social justice": Fairness and equity in society, particularly concerning access to wealth, opportunities, and privileges.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (general meaning)**: "Their tireless work for justice has inspired many activists around the world."
- **Noun (law)**: "The judge’s verdict was seen as a triumph for justice in a case marred by corruption."
- **Noun (philosophy)**: "True justice would require not only punishing the guilty but also rehabilitating them."
