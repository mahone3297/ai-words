## Pronunciation
/ˈθɪətə(r)/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **theatre** (also spelled theater in American English) is a building or space where plays, musicals, operas, or other performances are presented to an audience. It can also refer to the art or profession of performing such productions.

### Verb
To **theatricalize** or **put on a show** means to present a performance or create a dramatic atmosphere, often in a playful or exaggerated manner.

### Adjective
- **Theatrical** describes something that is overly dramatic or artificial, resembling a stage production.

### Adverb
- **Theatrically** refers to a dramatic or theatrical manner.

## Phrases
- "Opening night at the theatre" refers to the first public performance of a play or show.
- "A live theatre experience" denotes attending a performance in person.
- "The theatre of the absurd" is a term coined by Jean-Paul Sartre, referring to a genre of literature that challenges traditional narrative structures.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "I'm going to see a new play at the local theatre this weekend."
- **Verb**: "She decided to theatricalize her presentation to make it more engaging."
- **Adjective**: "His acting was so theatrical that it drew attention away from the plot."
- **Adverb**: "She entered the room theatrically, drawing gasps from the audience."
