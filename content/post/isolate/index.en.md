## Word: Isolate

## Pronunciation
/ˈaɪsəleɪt/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To separate or set apart from others; to place or keep alone or apart from contact with others, especially for the purpose of preventing the spread of disease or contamination. For example: "The patient was isolated in a quarantine facility."
- **Noun**: (n) An individual thing or person that has been separated or stands alone from others; a detached unit or entity. For example: "Each isolate of the virus was tested separately in the lab."

### Verb Usage Examples
- "The government has decided to isolate all international travelers for a period of 14 days."
- "To study its properties, the scientist had to isolate the chemical from the mixture."

### Noun Usage Examples
- "After the outbreak, several isolates of the bacteria were collected for further analysis."
- "The village, being remote, felt like an isolate from the rest of the world."

## Phrases
- "Isolate oneself": To deliberately distance oneself from others emotionally or physically.
- "Infectious disease isolation": The process of separating infected individuals from non-infected ones to prevent the spread of illness.
  
## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "It's crucial to isolate the faulty component to fix the system."
- **Noun**: "The new strain of the virus was first identified through analyzing patient isolates."

## Synonyms
- **Verb**: Separate, seclude, insulate, segregate, detach, quarantine
- **Noun**: Separatist, loner, recluse, hermit, solitary, outcast (in certain contexts)
