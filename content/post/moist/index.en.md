## Pronunciation
/mɔɪst/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: (adj) Having a slightly wet or damp texture; neither dry nor soaking wet. It can also refer to something that has a slightly humid or sticky feel due to a small amount of liquid present. For example:
  - _The soil was moist after the rain._
  - _She dabbed her face with a moist towel._

- **Verb**: (v, rare) To make or become moistened or wet. For example:
  - _The gardener moistened the soil before planting the seeds._
  - _The air soon began to moisten as fog rolled in._

## Phrases
- "Moisturize": The act of applying a substance to the skin or hair to prevent it from becoming dry and to keep it moist.
- "Moisture content": The amount of water or other liquid contained within a material or substance.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: _The baker explained that a moist cake is a sign of proper baking._
- **Verb**: _She moistened the stamps before sticking them on the envelopes._

## Synonyms
- Damp
- Humid
- Wet
- Slightly wet
- Juicy (in certain contexts)
- Hydrated
- Dewy (for skin or surfaces)
- Moistened

Please note that while "moist" can be used as a verb, it's less common than its use as an adjective in everyday language. Also, the word "moist" can sometimes have negative connotations when used to describe certain textures or conditions, particularly in informal settings.
