## Word: Orange

## Pronunciation
/ˈɔːrɪndʒ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A round citrus fruit with a bright orange skin and sweet, juicy segments. It can also refer to the color that resembles the hue of this fruit. Example: "She peeled an orange for breakfast." / "The walls were painted a vibrant orange."
- **Adjective**: Relating to or having the color of an orange. Example: "She wore an orange dress to match her shoes."

### Noun usage examples
- "He sliced open an orange to share its refreshing scent."
- "The sunset filled the sky with shades of red and orange."

### Adjective usage examples
- "The autumn leaves turned a brilliant shade of orange."
- "Her scarf was a bold orange that added a pop of color to her outfit."

## Phrases
- "Orange juice" - a beverage made from squeezed oranges.
- "A basket of oranges" - indicating multiple oranges.
- "To have orange fingers" - typically referring to the staining effect after peeling or handling many oranges.

## Synonyms
- Tangerine (for the fruit)
- Amber, vermilion, burnt orange (for the color) 
- Clementine, mandarin (for other types of orange fruits)

Note: As an English color name, "orange" does not have verb or interjection uses like the example provided for "hello".
