## Pronunciation
/proʊˈfɪt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. The financial gain or benefit derived from a business, investment, or other activity, after expenses have been subtracted. 2. A successful outcome or achievement. *Example: The company's profits soared last quarter.*
- **Verb**: To make a profit; earn a financial gain. *Example: They planned to increase their profit margins by optimizing production processes.*
- **Adjective**: Characterized by or resulting in profit. *Example: The venture was highly profitable.*

## Phrases
- "Profit margin" is the difference between revenue and costs expressed as a percentage.
- "Operating profit" refers to earnings before interest and taxes.
- "Profit-sharing" is a system where employees receive a portion of the company's profits.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The new product line significantly boosted our annual profits."
- **Verb**: "She analyzed the data to determine if they could increase their profit."
- **Adjective**: "The company's stock price has been on a steady upward trajectory, reflecting its consistently profitable operations."
