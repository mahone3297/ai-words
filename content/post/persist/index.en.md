## Pronunciation
/pərˈsɪst/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The quality or action of continuing steadfastly without giving up, often despite difficulty or opposition. **Example:** "Her persistence paid off when she finally landed the job."
2. A persistent person or thing that keeps recurring or existing over time. **Example:** "The teacher praised the students' persistence in their studies."

### Verb
1. To continue with something, especially through difficulties or opposition, showing determination. **Example:** "She persisted in her research even after facing numerous setbacks."
2. To maintain or enforce consistently. **Example:** "The government persists in its efforts to combat corruption."

### Adverb
1. Characterizing a verb, indicating continued action without interruption. **Example:** "He persisted tirelessly in his work."
