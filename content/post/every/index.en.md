## Word: Every

## Pronunciation
/ˈɛvri/

## Definitions
- **Determiner**: Used to refer to all members of a group considered individually; each one. Example: "Every student in the class passed the exam."
- **Adjective**: Occurring or existing at all times or in all cases; always. Example: "There's something new to learn every day."

### Phrase
- "Every now and then": Occasionally, from time to time. Example: "I like to visit the beach every now and then."
- "Every which way": In all directions, everywhere. Example: "The wind was blowing leaves every which way."
- "Every so often": At intervals, occasionally. Example: "We should meet up for coffee every so often."

## Example Sentences
- **Determiner**: "Every member of the team has to attend the meeting."
- **Adjective**: "She reads a new book every week to expand her knowledge."

## Synonyms
- Each
- All
- Every one
- Each and every
- Every single (one)
