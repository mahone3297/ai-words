## Pronunciation
/aməz/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To cause great surprise or wonder; astonish. (v)
- **Adjective**: Causing great surprise or admiration; astonishing. (adj)

## Phrases
- "Be amazed by something" implies experiencing wonder at a particular sight or event.
- "She amazed everyone with her performance."
- "The magician's tricks never cease to amaze the audience."

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "Her quick thinking amazed the police, leading them to solve the case."
- **Adjective**: "The view from the top of the mountain was absolutely amazing."
- **To be amazed**: "No one could believe the transformation they had undergone."

## Synonyms
- Astound
- Stun
- Amaze
- Shock
- Startle
- Entrance
- Captivate

## Antonyms
- Disappoint
- Bore
- Frustrate
- Dull
- Unimpress
- Unamused

## Usage Notes
- The word "amaze" can be used both as a verb and an adjective, expressing the action of surprising or the state of being amazed, respectively.
- It is often followed by "by" when referring to the cause of the amazement.
