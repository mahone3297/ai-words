## Pronunciation
/ˈʌtər/

## Definitions
### Noun
- Utterance: The act or instance of saying something, especially when it is considered significant or complete. For example: "Her utterance was filled with wisdom."
- Expression: A way of conveying meaning through words or gestures. For example: "A simple utterance can convey a lot of emotion."

### Verb
- To utter: To speak or pronounce words, especially deliberately or forcefully. For example: "He uttered a heartfelt apology."
- To say or declare: To express something clearly and distinctly. For example: "She uttered her intention to resign."

### Adjective
- Utterly: Completely, entirely. For example: "The utter darkness made it difficult to see."
- Complete: Giving all necessary information or details. For example: "An utter description of the event."

### Adverb
- Utterly: In a complete or total manner. For example: "She was utterly exhausted after the long journey."

## Phrases
- "Utter nonsense" refers to something that doesn't make sense.
- "To utter a secret" means to reveal it.
- "In utter disbelief" means completely shocked.

## Example Sentences

- **Adjective**: "Her utterly calm demeanor helped others feel at ease."
- **Adverb**: "He was utterly absorbed in his book."
