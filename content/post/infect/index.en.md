## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈfekt/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To spread a disease, virus, or harmful organism from one living thing to another; to cause something to be affected by a disease or bad influence. For example: "The flu virus infected many people during the winter season."
- **Noun**: (n) The act of infecting or the state of being infected; a disease or infection. For example: "The early detection of the infection was crucial to prevent its spread."

## Phrases
- "Infectious disease" refers to a disease that can be transmitted from one person to another through contact, air, bodily fluids, or vectors.
- "To infect with enthusiasm" is a metaphorical usage meaning to inspire someone with excitement or eagerness.
- "To infect the mind" suggests influencing someone's thoughts or beliefs negatively.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "She worried she might have infected her coworkers after coming down with the flu."
- **Noun**: "The patient's recovery was delayed due to a severe chest infection."

## Synonyms
- Contaminate (v)
- Transmit (v)
- Spread (v)
- Impair (v)
- Disease (n)
- Illness (n)
- Sickness (n)
- Infection (n) 

Note: As a verb, "infect" doesn't have direct synonyms in the context of spreading diseases, but words like 'contaminate', 'transmit' and 'spread' are often used in similar contexts. As a noun, "infection", "disease", "illness", and "sickness" denote similar concepts.
