## Definitions
- **Noun**: A word or expression that can take on different values or meanings, often referring to a quantity that may change. For example: "The equation has several variables."
- **Adjective**: Characterized by variation or being subject to change. For example: "The weather is highly variable from day to day."
- **Verb**: To change or be changed according to circumstances or factors. For example: "The price of the product can vary depending on location."
- **Adverb**: In a varying or uncertain manner. For example: "Her mood was variable; one moment she was happy, the next sad."

- "Variable data types" in programming refer to data that can hold different values.
