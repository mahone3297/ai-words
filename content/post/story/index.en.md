## Pronunciation
/stɔːri/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **story** can refer to:
1. A sequence of events that are narrated or imagined, often with a beginning, middle, and end, and often intended for entertainment or instruction: *Told her a bedtime story about a brave knight.*
2. A fictional or non-fictional account of past events: *The epic tale of their journey across the ocean.*
3. A unit of information in the news or media: *The top story on the evening news.*

### Verb
To **tell a story** or **spin a yarn** means to narrate or recount an event or series of events: *She told a thrilling story about her adventure in the jungle.*

### Noun (figurative)
In metaphorical use, it can denote a narrative or explanation: *His life's story is one of perseverance and resilience.*

## Phrases
- "Tell a story" or "share a story" means to communicate a narrative.
- "Share your story" invites personal anecdotes or experiences.
- "Lost in a good story" implies being fully absorbed in a narrative.
- "From the horse's mouth" when referring to the original story or source.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "He loved to read bedtime stories to his nephews every night."
- **Verb**: "She sat around the campfire, telling stories under the stars."
- **Noun (figurative)**: "Her story of success inspired many young entrepreneurs."

### Derived Terms
- Storytelling: The act of narrating or sharing stories.
- Plot: The sequence of events in a story.
- Character: A person or animal in a story.
- Arc: The development of a story from beginning to end.
