## Pronunciation
/dɪˈsiːv/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To intentionally mislead or trick someone, causing them to believe something that is false or misleading. (n. deceit)
- **Noun**: The act of deceiving or the state of being deceived; deception.

## Phrases
- "Deceived by appearances" implies being misled by how things appear.
- "Beware of false promises" warns against being deceived.
- "Caught red-handed" describes being caught in the act of deceiving.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "He deliberately deceived his friends about his whereabouts."
- **Noun**: "She felt betrayed after falling for his elaborate deception."
- **Idiomatic**: "He was so cunning that he deceived even the most astute detective."
