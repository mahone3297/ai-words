## Pronunciation
/liːst/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Superlative form of 'little,' used to indicate the smallest amount or degree; minimum. For example: "This is the least expensive option."
- **Adverb**: In the smallest degree; to the slightest extent. For example: "She cares the least about what others think."
- **Noun**: The smallest quantity or amount possible, often used in the context of comparison or limitation. For example: "He eats the least among his siblings."

## Phrases
- "At least": Meaning 'the minimum' or 'no less than'. For example: "I need at least five minutes to prepare."
- "Least of all": Indicating that something or someone is unlikely or unexpected. For example: "Least of all did I expect her to arrive early."
- "The least you can do": Expressing the minimum action expected from someone. For example: "After all the trouble you've caused, the least you can do is apologize."

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "This is the least difficult problem on the test."
- **Adverb**: "She worries least when she's spending time with her family."
- **Noun**: "In this group, he has the least experience with public speaking."
