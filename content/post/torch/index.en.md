## Pronunciation
/tɔːrtʃ/

## Definitions
### Noun
A torch is a device that produces light, typically through a burning fuel source, such as a wick or gas, enclosed within a protective casing. It can also refer to a person carrying or holding a lighted torch.

### Verb
To torch (informal) can mean to set fire to something, especially as an act of vandalism or protest.

## Phrases
- "Carrying a torch" symbolizes a strong emotional attachment or admiration for someone or something.
- "Lighting a torch" signifies starting a fire or initiating a signal.
- "Torchbearer" refers to someone who carries a torch during a ceremony or event.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The firefighter carried a torch to guide people through the dark tunnel."
- **Verb**: "Protesters torched several police cars during the demonstration."
- **Implied verb**: "The Olympic torch relay is a symbolic tradition where torchbearers carry the flame from city to city."
