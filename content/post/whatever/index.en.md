## Pronunciation
/ˈwɛərɛvɛə(r)/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. Whatever (pronoun): An expression used to indicate a lack of choice or indifference, often followed by a question or clause. For example: "I'll wear whatever you pick out for me."
2. Whatever (adjective): Indicating anything, no matter what; unspecified. For example: "She'll do whatever it takes to succeed."

### Verb
1. To use "whatever" as a pronoun, to refer to something without specifying. For example: "He'll take whatever job comes his way."
2. To imply acceptance or allowance of any condition or choice. For example: "I'll go with whatever plan you prefer."

### Adverb
1. Expressing a lack of concern or interest in the specifics. For example: "She doesn't care what time we meet – whatever works for you."

## Phrases
- "Whatever floats your boat" is a colloquial expression meaning something that suits a person's preferences.
- "I don't care, whatever" is used to show indifference or lack of interest.
- "Do whatever you want" expresses permission or allowance for someone to act as they please.
