## Pronunciation
/rɪˈeɪn/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Reign**: 1. The period during which a monarch or other ruler holds power, especially their rule over a country or empire. Example: "The Tudor dynasty's long reign ended with Queen Elizabeth I's abdication."
- 2. A reign of something (often weather): a period when a particular condition or phenomenon dominates, often used metaphorically. Example: "There was a long reign of drought in the region."

### Verb
- **To reign**: To exercise power or rule over a kingdom, empire, or area. Example: "The king reigned with wisdom and justice."
- (figurative) To prevail or dominate in a particular field or situation. Example: "Technology has reigned supreme in recent years."

## Phrases
- "Rule a kingdom" or "rule the roost"
- "During his/her reign"
- "A golden/reign of peace/prosperity"

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The Roman Empire experienced several distinct reigns under various emperors."
- **Verb**: "Queen Victoria's reign lasted for over 60 years."
- **Metaphorical**: "His ideas have reigned supreme in the literary world."
