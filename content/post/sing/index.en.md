## Pronunciation
/sɪŋ/

## Definitions
### Noun
A noun related to the word "sing" can be:
1. The act or art of producing musical sounds by modulating the voice: *She has a beautiful singing voice.*
2. A song, especially one that is performed: *He sang a popular tune at the karaoke night.*
3. A particular performance of a song: *The concert was a sell-out, with multiple sing-alongs.*

### Verb
1. **To sing**: The action of producing sound through the voice, typically in a musical manner: *She sings in the church choir every Sunday.*
2. **To perform or recite (a song, poem, etc.)**: *The children sang a lullaby to their baby brother.*
3. **To express or convey (an emotion, message, etc.) through song**: *His lyrics beautifully sing about love and loss.*

### Adjective
1. Characteristic of singing or having a pleasant singing quality: *Her voice is a soprano, and her singing style is very melodic.*
2. Expressive or emotional when spoken or sung: *The song's lyrics are very lyrically sung.*

### Adverb
Not a common adverb form of "sing."

## Phrases
- "Singing contest" refers to a competition where participants showcase their singing abilities.
- "Sing along" encourages people to join in the chorus of a song being performed.
- "Sung praises" means expressing admiration or gratitude through song.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Listening to her sing was a real treat for the ears."
- **Verb**: "The children were singing joyfully in the schoolyard."
- **Adjective**: "She has a voice that truly sings of happiness."

## Synonyms
- Vocalize
- Perform
- Render (a song)
- Warble
- Chant

Note: The adverb form is not extensive, as "singingly" could be used to describe a singing manner, but it's less common.
