## Pronunciation
/sænˈdɪʃ/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **sandwich** is a food dish typically consisting of two or more slices of bread or a single slice acting as a wrapper, with various fillings between them. The fillings can be made from meats, cheeses, vegetables, spreads, or condiments.

### Verb
To **sandwich** something means to place it between two other things, often in a parallel or sequential manner, as if forming a sandwich.

### Adjective
A **sandwiched** situation can refer to being caught or trapped between two opposing forces or circumstances.

## Phrases
- "Make a sandwich" or "build a sandwich"
- "A classic sandwich" or "a BLT (bacon, lettuce, and tomato) sandwich"
- "The triple-decker sandwich" or "a double-decker club sandwich"
- "A cold sandwich" or "a hot sandwich"

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "I grabbed a turkey sandwich on my way to work."
- **Verb**: "The menu offered a variety of sandwich options to choose from."
- **Adjective**: "She felt sandwiched between her demanding job and family responsibilities."
