## Pronunciation
/rɪˈpiːt/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The act of saying or doing something again: *The teacher asked the students to repeat their answers.*
2. A repetition of a word, phrase, or sound: *The song had a catchy repeat chorus.*
3. A statement or fact that is restated or recalled: *The history teacher often repeated key dates in class.*

### Verb
1. To say or do something again: *Please repeat after me: 'I promise to be honest.'*
2. To perform (a task) repeatedly: *The machine is programmed to repeat the process automatically.*
3. To replay or reproduce (a sound or recording): *The DJ repeated the hit song multiple times.*

### Adjective
1. Characteristic of being done or said more than once: *A repetitive pattern can be annoying.*
2. Requiring repetition for learning: *A repeat password is stronger than a simple one.*

## Phrases
- "Repeat after me" is used to instruct someone to mimic a phrase.
- "Repeat performance" refers to a similar event happening again.
- "Repeat offender" describes someone who has done something wrong repeatedly.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The students were asked to repeat their calculations on the whiteboard."
- **Verb**: "She repeated her apology several times to make sure he understood."
- **Adjective**: "The lesson was designed to avoid repetitive exercises."
