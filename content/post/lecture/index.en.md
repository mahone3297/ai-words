# Lecture

## Pronunciation
/ˈlɛktʃər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A formal talk or discourse on a particular subject, usually given by a teacher or expert to an audience. For example: "The professor delivered an engaging lecture on quantum physics."
  - _In education context_: A teaching session at a college or university where information is presented to students. Example: "She attended three lectures every week during her first semester."
- **Verb**: To give a formal talk or discourse on a specific topic. Example: "The visiting scholar will lecture on the history of art tomorrow afternoon."

## Phrases
- "Lecture series": A set of consecutive lectures on related topics given over a period of time.
- "Lecture hall": A large room or auditorium specifically designed for delivering lectures.
- "Lecture notes": Written or printed material summarizing the content of a lecture.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The guest speaker's insightful lecture on climate change captivated the audience."
- **Verb**: "She lectures part-time at the local community college in addition to her research work."
