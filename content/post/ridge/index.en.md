## Pronunciation
/rɪdʒ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. A long, narrow, elevated landform with steep sides, typically running parallel to a mountain range. Example: "The alpine ridges offered stunning views."
   2. A line or raised edge on a surface, often indicating a division or boundary. Example: "The book has a leather ridge along the spine."
   3. A prominent feature on the face, particularly the nose or cheekbones. Example: "She had a strong facial ridge that added character to her appearance."

- **Verb**: 1. To form a ridge or series of ridges. Example: "The wind eroded the landscape, creating new ridges over time."
   2. (figurative) To distinguish oneself or stand out in a particular way. Example: "His expertise formed a mental ridge in her mind."

## Phrases
- "Ridge line" refers to the contour where two adjacent ridges meet.
- "Run along a ridge" means to follow the path of a ridge.
- "A ridge of clouds" describes a line of clouds extending across the sky.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The hiker followed the winding path up the mountain ridge."
- **Verb**: "The soil tends to accumulate along the ridges, making them fertile spots."
- **Figure of speech**: "His ideas stood out like a mental ridge in the debate."
