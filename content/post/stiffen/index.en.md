## Pronunciation
/stɪfən/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: 
  - **Transitive verb**: To become harder or more rigid in texture, shape, or attitude; to strengthen or firm up. Example: "The wood stiffened after being left outside in the rain."
  - **Intransitive verb**: To become tense or unyielding, especially emotionally or mentally. Example: "She stiffened when she heard the bad news."

## Phrases
- "Stiffen up" means to become more rigid or upright.
- "Stiffen with cold" refers to becoming stiff due to cold temperatures.
- "Stiffen the competition" can mean to increase the challenge or make others work harder.

## Example Sentences
- **Transitive**: "The paint on the old house had begun to stiffen from exposure to the elements."
- **Intransitive**: "His resolve stiffened when faced with adversity."
- "The muscles in her neck stiffened as she read the difficult passage."
