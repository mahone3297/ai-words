## Pronunciation
/səˈraʊnd/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The act of surrounding; encircling or encompassing something. "The city is surrounded by a lush forest."
2. The group or people surrounding a person or object. "A crowd surrounded the famous artist."
3. Environmental factors or conditions that surround an area or situation. "Her home was surrounded by tranquility."

### Verb
1. To encircle or go around something, typically completely. "The troops surrounded the enemy's camp."
2. To be in close proximity to; enclose. "The mountains surrounded the valley."
3. To furnish with or create an atmosphere or feeling of. "The party was beautifully surrounded by fairy lights."

### Adjective
1. Characterizing something that is all around; encircling. "A garden surrounded by a high wall."
2. Providing a surrounding context or environment. "Her childhood memories were surrounded by love and laughter."

## Phrases
- "Surround someone/something" means to be on all sides of or next to.
- "Be surrounded by" describes being in the presence of or having a certain quality all around.
- "Surround yourself with positivity" implies creating a supportive environment.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The old house was surrounded by a tall stone wall."
- **Verb**: "The soldiers quickly surrounded the escaping prisoners."
- **Adjective**: "She felt safe and secure, surrounded by her family at the beach."
