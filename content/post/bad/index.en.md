## Pronunciation
/bæd/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Bad**: 1. Of poor quality or unsatisfactory. "The weather was really bad that day."
- 2. Unfavorable; adverse. "His health has been bad lately."
- 3. Unlucky or unfortunate. "She had a bad run of luck."

### Verb
- **To be bad**: To be of poor quality or condition. "This cake doesn't taste good; it's bad."
- 2. To cause harm or damage. "His actions had bad consequences."
- 3. To behave poorly or morally wrong. "Don't be bad to your little sister."

### Adjective
- **Bad**: 1. Opposite of good. "A bad grade on the exam."
- 2. Wicked or immoral. "He's a bad person."
- 3. Intense or severe. "A bad headache."

### Adverb
- **Badly**: In a poor manner or to an extent. "He performed badly in the race."

## Phrases
- "Feel bad about something" means to feel remorse or regret.
- "Be bad for you" implies that something is harmful or detrimental to health.
- "Worse than bad" describes something extremely negative.
