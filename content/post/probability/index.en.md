## Pronunciation
/proʊbəˈbɪləti/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A measure of the likelihood or chance that something will happen, usually expressed as a number between 0 and 1, with 1 being certain and 0 having no chance. For example: "The probability of rain tomorrow is 40%."
- **Verb**: To assign or estimate the likelihood of an event occurring. For example: "We need to probability the success of our new project."

## Phrases
- "Probability distribution" refers to the statistical function describing the likelihood of different outcomes.
- "Conditional probability" is the likelihood of one event occurring given that another event has already occurred.
- "High probability" means a strong likelihood, while "low probability" indicates a weak likelihood.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The probability of winning the lottery is very low, but some people still buy tickets."
- **Verb**: "Scientists calculated the probability of the species surviving under changing environmental conditions."
- **Phrasal**: "They adjusted their strategy based on the conditional probability of the opponent's move."
