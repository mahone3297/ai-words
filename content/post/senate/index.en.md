## Pronunciation
/senˈteɪ.ni/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A body of elected officials, typically in a federal government, that advises and helps make decisions.**
- **In ancient Rome, a high council consisting of two consuls and various senators who advised the Roman magistrates.**

## Phrases
- "Attend the Senate session"
- "The upper chamber of Congress"
- "A senator from California"

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The senator proposed a new bill to tackle climate change in the Senate."
- **Noun**: "The Senate voted on the confirmation of the Supreme Court nominee."
- **Noun**: "She was proud to serve on the Senate judiciary committee."

## Verb
- **To represent or act as a senator in a legislative role.**
- **To participate in the decision-making process of a Senate.**

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "He will soon be taking office as a senator."
- **Verb**: "The senator deliberated on the budget proposal in a committee meeting."

## Synonyms
- **Senators**
- **Members of the Senate**
- **Legislators**
- **Representatives**
- **Upper house members** (referring to bicameral systems)

## Antonyms
- **Representative (from the lower house)**
- **Member of the House**
- **Legislative assistants**
- **Councillors** (local government context)
