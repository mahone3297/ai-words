## Pronunciation
/weɪst/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Waste**: 1. Unwanted or unused material that is discarded, thrown away, or no longer serves a purpose. "The factory produces a lot of waste products every day."
- 2. Excess or unnecessary use of something, often referring to time or resources. "Don't waste your time on trivial matters."

### Verb
- **Waste**: 1. To dispose of or get rid of something without using it effectively. "They wasted a lot of food at the picnic."
- 2. To spend (time, effort, or money) in a useless or inefficient manner. "She wasted hours trying to fix the broken toy."

### Adjective
- **Wasteful**: Causing or characterized by excessive use or consumption, often of resources. "Their manufacturing process was found to be wasteful."

### Adverb
- **Wastefully**: In a wasteful manner; unproductively. "He worked wastefully on the project, without a clear plan."

## Phrases
- "Reduce, reuse, and recycle" is a popular phrase promoting waste reduction.
- "Prevent waste" emphasizes the importance of avoiding unnecessary consumption.
- "Waste not, want not" is an old saying encouraging frugality.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The city has implemented a waste management program to reduce the amount of trash produced."
- **Verb**: "He vowed to stop wasting his talent by choosing a better career path."
- **Adjective**: "The government aims to make its operations less wasteful through energy-efficient practices."
- **Adverb**: "She worked wastefully on the assignment, leaving it incomplete."
