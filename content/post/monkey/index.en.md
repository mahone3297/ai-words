## Pronunciation
/ˈmʌŋki/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A small to medium-sized primate with a long tail, typically having a hairy, agile body and limbs, and often characterized by a pointed face and large eyes. For example: "The zoo had several species of monkeys swinging from tree to tree."

  - As a colloquialism: A mischievous or playful person. Example: "He's such a little monkey, always getting into things he shouldn't."

- **Verb (informal)**: To mimic or imitate someone, usually in a mocking or playful manner. Example: "The kids were monkeying around, copying each other's dance moves."

## Phrases
- "Monkey business": Mischievous or deceitful activity, especially when undertaken playfully or unseriously. Example: "There was some monkey business going on behind the scenes."
- "Monkey around": To behave in a mischievous, playful, or unproductive manner. Example: "The children were warned not to monkey around near the pond."
- "Monkey see, monkey do": This phrase suggests that people tend to imitate others without thinking, just like monkeys. Example: "Young children often learn behaviors through monkey see, monkey do."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The capuchin monkey displayed its intelligence by using tools to extract food from a crevice."
- **Verb**: "When they were left unsupervised, the kids couldn't resist monkeying around with the new toys."
