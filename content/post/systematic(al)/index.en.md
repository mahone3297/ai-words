## Pronunciation
/systeməˈtæmik/ (noun) /ˌsɪstəˈmætɪk/ (adjective)

## Definitions
### Noun
A systematic approach or methodical organization, often in a particular field or discipline. For example: "The school has implemented a systematic curriculum."

### Adjective
1. Following a regular, ordered pattern or method. For example: "A systematic study of the subject was conducted."
2. Careful and thorough, with a logical progression or structure. For example: "Her systematic approach to problem-solving proved effective."

## Phrases
- "Apply a systematic approach" means to tackle something methodically.
- "Systematic review" refers to a thorough examination of existing research.
- "Systematic error" is a mistake that consistently occurs due to a flaw in the process.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The scientist designed a systematic experiment to test the hypothesis."
- **Adjective**: "His systematic analysis revealed patterns in consumer behavior."
- **Adjective**: "She followed a systematic cleaning routine to maintain her home."

## Synonyms
- Methodical
- Systematical (informal, less common)
- Structured
- Organized
- Methodic
