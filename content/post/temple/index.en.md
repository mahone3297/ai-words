## Pronunciation
/tˈemp(ə)l/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A place of religious worship, especially one dedicated to a particular god or gods, often with a sanctuary or main building.
   - Example: "The ancient temple was adorned with intricate carvings."
2. A building or structure dedicated to a historical or cultural significance, not necessarily religious.
   - Example: "The national monument served as a temple to democracy."

### Verb
1. To visit or go to a temple for religious purposes or to pay homage.
   - Example: "Many tourists visit temples to meditate and learn about Buddhism."
2. To honor or show respect through a ritual or ceremony.
   - Example: "The community came together to consecrate the new temple."

### Adjective
1. Referring to a place of worship or religious importance.
   - Example: "A Hindu temple complex is a sacred site for believers."

## Phrases
- "Pay respects at the temple" means to visit and offer reverence.
- "Visit the temple for peace" suggests seeking spiritual calmness.
- "The temple's architecture is awe-inspiring" highlights its impressive design.
