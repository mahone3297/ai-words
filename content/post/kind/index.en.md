## Pronunciation
/kwaɪnd/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A category of people or things having common characteristics; a sort or type. For example: "There are many different kinds of dogs."
  - Also used to describe a person's nature or disposition: "She has a kind heart."
- **Adjective**: Characterized by benevolence, sympathy, and consideration for others; showing kindness. For example: "He was always kind to his neighbors."
  - Describing something that is of a particular type or variety: "What kind of music do you like?"
  
## Phrases
- "Kind of": This phrase can be used as an informal way to indicate approximation or similarity. For example: "It's kind of cold today."
- "A kind gesture": An act of generosity or thoughtfulness towards others.
- "Kind regards": A polite closing in written communication, expressing friendliness and respect.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She's a kind person who never hesitates to help those in need."
- **Adjective**: "The teacher was kind enough to explain the concept again when we were struggling."
- "That dog breed is a kind known for its intelligence and loyalty."

## Note on Verb Usage
While 'kind' is not typically used as a verb in modern English, it was once used as such with the meaning 'to make kind', which is now archaic. In contemporary language, the action of being kind is usually expressed through verbs like 'be kind' or 'show kindness'.
