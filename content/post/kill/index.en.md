## Pronunciation
/kɪl/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To cause the death of (a person, animal, or other living thing) intentionally or unintentionally; to put an end to something's existence or functioning. _For example:_ "The hunter killed the deer with a single shot."
  - _figurative usage_: "She killed her chances of promotion by making that mistake."
- **Verb**: To destroy or neutralize bacteria, germs, or viruses, often by chemical or heat treatment. _Example:_ "Boiling water kills most harmful bacteria."
- **Verb**: To severely affect someone emotionally; to devastate or deeply upset. _Example:_ "The news of her father's death killed her spirits."
- **Noun**: The act of causing death or destruction; a killing. _Example:_ "There was a brutal murder, and the police are investigating the kill."
- **Noun** (informal): A successful or impressive achievement. _Example:_ "That goal was an absolute kill in the match."

## Phrases
- "Kill two birds with one stone" means to achieve two objectives with a single action.
- "Make a kill" refers to a successful hunt or accomplishment, especially in business.
- "Kill time" means to spend time on unproductive activities while waiting.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The soldier was ordered to kill the enemy."
- **Noun**: "The detective pieced together the details of the crime scene where the kill took place."
- _Figurative usage_: "The harsh words from his boss killed any enthusiasm he had for the project."
