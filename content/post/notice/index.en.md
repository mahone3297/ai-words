## Pronunciation
/ˈnəʊtɪs/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: (n) An official announcement or notification, often written, that conveys information or instructions. For example: "The company sent out a notice of termination to its employees."
- **Noun**: (n) The act of observing or taking note of something, often with attention or interest. For example: "He took notice of the new painting on the wall."
- **Verb**: (v) To become aware of or perceive something, usually after paying attention. For example: "I just noticed that the flowers have bloomed."
- **Verb**: (v) To officially inform or notify someone about something. For example: "The landlord has to give tenants a 30-day notice before raising the rent."

## Phrases
- "Take notice" means to become aware of or pay attention to something.
- "Noticeable difference" refers to something that can be easily observed or detected.
- "Without notice" implies that something happened suddenly or unexpectedly.
