## Pronunciation
/stʌmək/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A muscular, sac-like organ in the abdominal cavity of a human or animal, where food is temporarily stored and partially digested before being passed on to the intestines.
   - **Example**: "The stomach is an essential part of the digestive system."
   - **Synonyms**: gut, abdomen, tummy

### Verb
1. To experience physical discomfort or pain in the stomach.
   - **Example**: "After eating too much, he started to feel a stomachache."
   - **Synonyms**: upset, ache, churn

2. To have a feeling of unease or apprehension (metaphorical use).
   - **Example**: "She had a stomach for adventure."
   - **Synonyms**: unease, apprehension

### Adjective
1. Relating to or affecting the stomach or digestive system.
   - **Example**: "A stomach-friendly diet"
   - **Synonyms**: gastric, digestive

## Phrases
- "Ease one's stomach" means to alleviate an upset stomach.
- "Stomach flu" refers to a common illness affecting the digestive system.
- "Stomach acid" refers to the hydrochloric acid produced by the stomach.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After the surgery, his recovery was slow due to the pain in his stomach."
- **Verb**: "She tried to ease her baby's stomach by giving him a warm bottle."
- **Adjective**: "The medication helped to calm his stomach cramps."
