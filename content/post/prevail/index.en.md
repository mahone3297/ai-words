## Pronunciation
/preɪˈveɪl/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To become dominant or triumph over another force, opinion, or argument; to overcome or dominate. For example: "The new policy prevailed after much debate."
- **Adjective**: (adj) Prevailing or dominant, especially in terms of influence or acceptance. For example: "The prevailing wind direction is from the east."

## Phrases
- "prevail upon someone" means to persuade or convince someone to do something.
- "prevail against" signifies overcoming an obstacle or opposition.
- "prevailing wisdom" refers to what is generally accepted as true or correct.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "His determination prevailed, and he finally completed the marathon."
- **Adjective**: "The prevailing mood at the meeting was one of optimism."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "Despite initial resistance, the new technology eventually prevailed against traditional methods."
