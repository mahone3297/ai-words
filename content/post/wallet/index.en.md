## Pronunciation
/wɒlɪt/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **A wallet** (n): A small, usually leather or fabric container used to carry money, credit cards, identification, and other small personal items.
   - Definition: An item that holds financial documents and personal belongings, typically carried in one's pocket or purse.
   - Example: "I put my wallet in my back pocket before leaving the house."

2. [Slang] **A person's finances** (n): Refers to their money and assets.
   - Definition: The sum of money and assets a person possesses, often metaphorically used.
   - Example: "After losing his job, he had to downsize his wallet."

### Verb
- Not a common verb in its standard form, but can be used in a figurative sense to describe managing or organizing one's finances.
   - Example: "She's really good at keeping her financial wallet in order."

## Phrases
- "Carry a wallet" or "hold a wallet"
- "Keep your wallet secure"
- "Empty your wallet" (to spend all one's money)

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "He took out his wallet to pay for the coffee."
- **Slang**: "After a few months on a tight budget, she had to cut back on her spending to lighten her financial wallet."
- **Verb**: "She's known for being frugal, always keeping a close eye on her wallet."

## Synonyms
- Purse (for women)
- Billfold
- Portfolio (for carrying important documents)
- Wallet case
- Cashbox
- Finances (in a general sense)
