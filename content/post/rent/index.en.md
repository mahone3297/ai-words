## Pronunciation
/rɪnt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **1.** The money paid regularly for the use of something, especially real estate, as a tenant. Example: "He was responsible for paying rent on the apartment."
- **2.** (informal) The act of hiring or borrowing something for a period, often with the agreement to pay a fee. Example: "She rented a car for her vacation."

### Verb
- **1.** To lease or hire something for a specified period, typically with regular payments. Example: "The landlord agreed to rent us the house for a year."
- **2.** (informal) To hire a person for a short time or on an as-needed basis. Example: "They rented a graphic designer for their marketing campaign."

### Adjective
- Describing the quality of being suitable for temporary use or occupancy without ownership. Example: "The rental car was in good condition."

## Phrases
- "Pay rent on time" means to fulfill the financial obligation of renting a property.
- "Rent control" refers to government regulations limiting the amount landlords can charge for rent.
- "Rent-to-own" is a scheme where a tenant has the option to buy the property after a certain period.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "He had to find a new place to rent after his lease expired."
- **Verb**: "She rents out her spare room to a college student."
- **Adjective**: "The rental properties in the city are usually well-maintained."
