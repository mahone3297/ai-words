## Definitions
- **Adjective**: 1. Having a relatively small length or height; not tall or extended. For example: "The child was short for their age."
- 2. Lacking in duration or extent; brief. For example: "The meeting was a short one."
- 3. Insufficient in quantity or quality. For example: "There wasn't enough time for a proper discussion."
- 4. Below average or inferior in some respect. For example: "His performance was short on creativity."
- **Adverb**: 1. In a short manner; briefly. For example: "She answered quickly, in short."
- 2. (informal) Very; extremely. For example: "He's short-tempered, so be careful around him."

## Phrases
- "Be short for" means to be shorter in size or stature than something else.
- "Cut to the chase" is often used to describe a situation where someone wants to get to the point quickly without unnecessary details, which can be shortened.
- "A short break" refers to a brief pause or rest.
