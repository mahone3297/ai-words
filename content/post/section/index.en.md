## Pronunciation
/həˈleɪʃn/

## Definitions
### Noun
A section or division within a book, document, or website, usually marked by a distinctive heading or title, containing related information or content. For example: "The history section provides an overview of the ancient civilization."
- **Specialized**: Segments, chapter, topic, part, module, topic, portion

### Verb
To divide or allocate a part of something, especially a written work, into sections. For example: "The author divided the novel into five main sections."
- **Related**: Organize, structure, arrange, partition, chunk

### Adjective
Characterizing or pertaining to a specific section or part. For example: "Each section of the museum has its own unique exhibits."

## Phrases
- "Section 3.1" refers to a particular subsection within a numbered list.
- "Browse through the sections" means to go through different parts of a document or website.
- "Read the introduction before delving into the main section" is a suggestion to familiarize oneself with the context.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The library's non-fiction section is packed with books on history."
- **Verb**: "She carefully organized her notes into separate sections for each subject."
- **Adjective**: "The garden design features several distinct sections, each with a different theme."
