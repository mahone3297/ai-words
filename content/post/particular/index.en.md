## Pronunciation
/pərˈtɪkju.lər/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A particular thing**: A specific item or instance among others. For example: "She was very interested in that particular painting."
- **A detail**: Something specific or unique about a situation. For example: "The instructions were clear, but he wanted a more particular explanation."
- **Preference**: A favored choice or inclination. For example: "Her favorite color is a particular shade of blue."

### Adjective
- **Specific**: Referring to one thing rather than general. For example: "Each student has a particular learning style."
- **Exceptional**: Standing out from the rest due to uniqueness. For example: "That restaurant offers a particularly delicious dessert."
- **Detailed**: Carefully specified or exact. For example: "The report provided particular data on the project's progress."

### Adverb
- **Specifically**: In a particular manner or with special focus. For example: "She thanked him specifically for his help."
- **Particularly**: Especially or unusually. For example: "The weather was particularly cold that day."

## Phrases
- "Be particular about" implies being selective or choosy.
- "Take particular notice of" means to pay close attention to something.
- "Not everyone shares your particular views" means some people don't agree with your specific opinions.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Each child had their own particular toy they loved to play with."
- **Adjective**: "The teacher praised her for her particular talent in mathematics."
- **Adverb**: "He was particularly grateful for the birthday gift."
