## Pronunciation

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A person who is held prisoner or confined, especially against their will. Also used figuratively for something that is restricted or immobile. Example: "The captive audience listened intently to the speaker."
- **Verb**: To capture or seize someone or something, making them prisoner. Example: "The enemy captured the enemy soldiers."
- **Adjective**: Held captive, imprisoned, or restricted in some way. Example: "Her imagination was captive by the story's twists and turns."

## Phrases
- "Captive audience" refers to a group of people who are forced to listen to a particular performance or presentation.
- "Escape from captivity" describes the act of freeing oneself from imprisonment or captivity.
- "Captive mind" implies complete absorption or fixation on a particular thought or idea.
