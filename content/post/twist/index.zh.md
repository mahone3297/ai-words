## 读音
/twɪst/

## 释义
- **名词**:
  - 形状或结构上的弯曲或扭结；转折点。例如: "The rope had several twists in it."
  - 一个观点、解释或故事的不同版本。例如: "There's a surprising twist in the plot of the movie."

- **动词**:
  - 使弯曲或扭动。例如: "She twisted the wire around the pole."
  - 变形、曲解（事实、信息等）。例如: "He twisted the facts to suit his argument."

- **形容词**:
  - 扭曲的、弯曲的。例如: "a twist necktie"
  - 独特的、非传统的。例如: "a twist on classic recipes"

## 短语
- "Give something a twist" 意为给某事添加新的元素或变化。
- "Put a twist on" 意味着以不同的方式解释或处理某事。

## 例句
- 名词: "The tree branch had a natural twist that made it perfect for a swing."
- 动词: "He twisted the key to open the lock."
- 形容词: "The restaurant offered a twist on traditional Italian cuisine."
