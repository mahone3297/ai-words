## Word: Merry

## Pronunciation
/ˈmɛri/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Characterized by joy, happiness, or festivity; cheerful. For example: "The guests had a merry time at the party."
  
  > "We had a merry Christmas celebration with family."
- **Noun (archaic)**: A period of enjoyment or festivity. For example: "They spent many a merry during the holiday season."

## Phrases
- "Merry Christmas" - A greeting expressing wishes for a joyful and festive Christmas holiday.
- "Make merry" - To enjoy oneself in a lively and happy manner, usually with food, drink, and entertainment.
- "Merry-go-round" - A fairground ride with seats on which people can sit and go round and round to music.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The children were in high spirits, singing and dancing in a merry mood."
- **Noun (archaic)**: "After the feast, they continued their merrys late into the night."

## Synonyms
- Joyful
- Jovial
- Festive
- Cheerful
- Happy
- Gay (in the sense of being lighthearted and carefree)
