## Pronunciation
/tuːrˈbaɪn/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A large mechanical device that converts the kinetic energy of a fluid (such as steam, water, or wind) into mechanical work, often used to generate electricity or pump fluids. Example: "The power plant relies on steam turbines to produce electricity."
- **Adjective**: Characterized by or related to a turbine, as in a turbine blade or turbine flow. Example: "A gas turbine engine has high efficiency compared to a piston engine."

## Phrases
- "Drive a turbine" means to operate or control a turbine mechanism.
- "Turbine generator" refers to an electrical generator powered by a turbine.
- "Steam turbine" is a specific type of turbine using steam as its working fluid.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The hydroelectric dam's turbine spun rapidly as the water flowed through it."
- **Adjective**: "The modern aircraft's jet turbine produces immense thrust."
- "The wind turbine's blades spun gracefully in the breeze."

## Synonyms
- Turbine-driven
- Turbomachine
- Turbine-powered
- Kinetic-energy converter
- Whirling machine

## Antonyms
- Static (in reference to non-moving or non-rotating)
- Non-turbine (opposite of a turbine mechanism)
