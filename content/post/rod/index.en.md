## Pronunciation
/rɒd/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A long, straight piece of wood, metal, or other rigid material, typically used for support, leverage, or as a tool.
   - Example: "The carpenter used a wooden rod to measure distances."
2. A thin, cylindrical object, often with a handle, used for various purposes, such as fishing or stirring.
   - Example: "He cast his fishing rod into the lake."
3. A device that transmits electrical signals, like a cathode ray tube in an old television set.
   - Example: "The cathode ray tube was replaced by a digital flat-screen display."
4. A person's backbone or spine.
   - Example: "She stood up straight, proud as a rod."

### Verb
- (Informal) To use a rod as a means of punishment or correction.
   - Example: "Parents might rod their children for misbehaving."

### Adjective
- (Slang) Very straight, rigid, or strict.
   - Example: "She had an iron rod of a spine."

## Phrases
- "Hit someone with a rod" (informal, refers to punishment)
- "Rod and reel" (fishing equipment)
- "Rodent-proofing" (blocking rodents with a barrier or rod)

## Example Sentences

- **Verb**: "The teacher used a pointer rod to emphasize important points on the board."
- **Adjective**: "He maintained a rod-like discipline in his classroom."
