## Pronunciation
/ˈʃɔːr/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A strip of land along the edge of a sea, lake, or river, especially where it meets the water.
- The area near the water's edge where waves break or where boats can be landed.
- A line or boundary; a dividing line.

### Verb
- To reach or approach the shore; go to the edge of the water.
- To border or encircle (a body of water).
- To come close to realizing or achieving something; get near.

### Adjective
- Relating to or situated on the shore.
- Bordering a body of water.

## Phrases
- "Come down to the shore" for going to the beach or waterfront.
- "The shorebirds feed along the shore" to describe birds that live near the water.
- "The shore has a beautiful view" to describe a scenic coastal location.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "We spent the day walking along the sandy shore of the ocean."
- **Verb**: "The fishermen anchored their boat just off the shore."
- **Adjective**: "The shore houses were built with sturdy materials to withstand the harsh coastal weather."
