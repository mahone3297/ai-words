## Pronunciation
/suːt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A suit** (n): A set of clothing, typically consisting of a jacket and trousers or a dress, worn together for formal or professional occasions. Example: "He wore a well-tailored suit to the job interview."
- **Suit of clothes** (n): A coordinated outfit for men, often including a matching jacket and pants. Example: "The suit was a perfect match for his wedding."

### Verb
- **To suit** (v): To be suitable or appropriate for a particular purpose or situation. Example: "This job requires a strong work ethic, which suits her personality."
- **To fit** (v, transitive): To be the correct size or shape for someone, especially when referring to clothing. Example: "These shoes suit her feet perfectly."

## Phrases
- "Suit up" means to get ready for a specific activity, often implying dressing formally or appropriately.
- "It doesn't suit me" expresses that something is not suitable or comfortable for an individual.
- "A suit and tie" is a colloquial expression for formal attire.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "He wore a classic black suit for the business meeting."
- **Verb**: "The climate here doesn't suit my tropical wardrobe."
- **Transitive verb**: "The new haircut suits you."
