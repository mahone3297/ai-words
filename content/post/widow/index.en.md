## Pronunciation
/wɪdəʊ/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **widow** is a woman who has lost her husband, typically through death. It can also refer to the estate left behind by a deceased spouse.

### Verb (informal)
To **become a widow** is to become a widow after the death of one's spouse. To **be a widow** is to be in this state.

### Adjective
Sometimes, "widowed" is used as an adjective to describe someone who is widowed, such as "a widowed mother."

## Phrases
- "Become a widow" signifies the loss of a spouse.
- "A widow's story" often refers to the experiences of a woman after the death of her partner.
- "Widowed life" can describe the post-spousal lifestyle.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She was a strong, independent widow raising her two children alone."
- **Verb**: "After her husband's passing, she had to adjust to being a widow."
- **Adjective**: "The widowed community often comes together for support."

## Synonyms

-寡妇 (in Chinese, pinyin "guǎfù")
- Bereaved (describing someone who has lost a loved one)
-鳏居 (state of being widowed)
- Widower (the male equivalent, but not a synonym for widow)
