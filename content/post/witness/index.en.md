## Pronunciation
/wɪˈtnɪs/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **witness** (noun) refers to a person who has seen, heard, or been present during an event, typically a crime or accident, and can provide testimony about it.

### Verb
To **witness** (verb) means to observe or be present at an event, often as a spectator or with the intent to testify.

## Phrases
- "Witness a crime" or "witness an event" means to see something happen firsthand.
- "Eye-witness account" refers to a personal, firsthand description of an event.
- "Being a witness to history" means to experience significant events firsthand.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The police interviewed several witnesses after the car accident."
- **Verb**: "I was lucky enough to witness the birth of my child."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "Her testimony as a key witness led to the conviction of the suspect."
