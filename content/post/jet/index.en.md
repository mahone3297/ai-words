## Word: Jet

## Pronunciation
/dʒɛt/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A high-speed aircraft powered by one or more jet engines, typically used for commercial air travel or military purposes. Example: "The jet landed smoothly on the runway."
- **Noun (n)**: A stream of matter forced out under high pressure, especially a narrow, fast-moving column of water, steam, or gas. Example: "A powerful jet of water from the fire hose extinguished the flames."
- **Noun (n)**: A dark, hard variety of lignite coal that can be polished and is often used for ornamentation. Example: "The fireplace mantel was adorned with a carved piece of jet."
- **Verb (v)**: Travel by jet aircraft. Example: "They jetted off to Paris for their honeymoon."
- **Verb (v)**: Move or travel very rapidly. Example: "The sports car jetted down the highway."
- **Adjective (adj)**: Relating to or propelled by a jet engine. Example: "Jet propulsion is the technology behind modern air travel."

## Phrases
- "Jet set": A fashionable group of international socialites who travel frequently by jet for pleasure or work.
- "Jet lag": A temporary disruption of the body's sleep-wake cycle caused by traveling across multiple time zones.
- "Private jet": A small aircraft designed for personal or business use, usually owned or chartered.
