## Pronunciation
/ˈlʌvər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A person who has a strong affection or passion for something or someone. For example: "She's a true music lover; her collection spans decades of hits." 
- **Noun (informal)**: A person deeply in love with another, often romantically. Example: "They have been lovers since college."
- **Noun (archaic)**: A friend or associate. Example: "He was a dear lover and companion during their travels."

## Phrases
- "Love of one's life" refers to a person's most beloved romantic partner.
- "Lover's quarrel" describes a disagreement between two people in a romantic relationship.
- "Animal lover" denotes someone who cares deeply about animals and their welfare.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (affection for something/someone)**: "The art lover spent hours admiring the masterpieces in the gallery."
- **Noun (romantic partner)**: "Their love story began when they were just teenagers, and now they're inseparable lovers."
- **Noun (friend or associate)**: "Old friends and lovers of literature gathered around the fireplace to share stories."
