## Pronunciation
/sənˈsənd/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To temporarily stop or delay (a process, activity, or punishment). For example: "The school suspended the classes due to bad weather."
- **Noun**: Suspension (as in a temporary halt or removal from an activity). For example: "The student's suspension was for three days."
- **Adjective**: Characterized by suspension or being held up. For example: "The bridge was suspended high above the river."

## Phrases
- "Suspend judgment" means to hold back one's opinion until more information is available.
- "Suspended animation" refers to a state where a person's vital functions are slowed down to preserve life during medical procedures.
- "Suspend disbelief" is a term used in literature to encourage readers to accept fictional elements.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The teacher suspended the class's outdoor activity until the rain stopped."
- **Noun**: "His license was suspended after he received too many speeding tickets."
- **Adjective**: "The artist designed a stunning building with a suspended ceiling."
