## Pronunciation
/rɪˈlaɪəbl/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Reliable**: An adjective that describes something or someone that can be trusted to be dependable or consistently good in quality or performance.
- Example: "The old car was a reliable mode of transportation."

### Verb
- **To be reliable**: The ability to be dependable or trustworthy; to fulfill expectations consistently.
- Example: "She's known for being a reliable team member who always finishes her tasks on time."

## Phrases
- "A reliable source" refers to an accurate and trustworthy information provider.
- "A reliable friend" is someone who can be counted on in times of need.
- "Dependable service" indicates a consistent level of excellence in customer care.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The company hired a new employee with a proven track record of reliability."
- **Verb**: "My neighbor is so reliable; I can always ask them to watch my plants when I'm away."
- **Adjective**: "The weatherman predicted a reliable sunny day, and it actually turned out that way."
