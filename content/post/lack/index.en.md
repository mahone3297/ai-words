## Word: Lack

## Pronunciation
/læk/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The state or condition of not having something or being deficient in something. For instance: "The project suffered from a lack of funding."
  - Example Sentence: "There's no lack of enthusiasm among the team members, just a lack of resources."
  
- **Verb**: To be without or not have enough of something; to be deficient in. 
  - Example Sentence: "She lacks confidence when speaking in public."

## Phrases
- "Lack of" is commonly used to denote absence or deficiency of something, e.g., "a lack of sleep can affect productivity."
- "For lack of a better word" is used when one cannot find a more suitable term to describe something, e.g., "I'll call it a 'device' for lack of a better word."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Despite his many talents, he felt a lack of direction in his career."
- **Verb**: "The company's success does not lack due to innovation, but rather its poor marketing strategy."
