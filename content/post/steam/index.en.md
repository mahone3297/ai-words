## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Steam** (n): A gaseous form of water produced by boiling or vaporization, often seen rising from hot surfaces or used in various industrial processes.
   - Example: "The engine was emitting thick steam from the exhaust."
2. **Steam power** (n): The energy derived from steam, historically used to drive machinery and engines.
   - Example: "The locomotive was powered by steam during the Industrial Revolution."

### Verb
1. **To steam** (v):
   - (Cooking) To heat food with steam, typically in a steamer or on a moist surface.
      - Example: "I like to steam vegetables to retain their nutrients."
   - (Cleaning) To use steam for cleaning purposes.
      - Example: "A steam cleaner is great for removing dirt from carpets."
   - (Thermal process) To expose something to steam for therapeutic or medical purposes.
      - Example: "She took a steam bath to relax her muscles."

### Adjective
1. **Steamy** (adj): Characterized by or containing steam; having a misty appearance.
   - Example: "The bathroom was steamy after a long shower."

### Adverb
1. **Steamily** (adv): In a steaming manner; with steaminess.
   - Example: "The tea was steamily poured into the cup."
