## Pronunciation
/lɔs/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: The state or feeling of having lost something or someone valuable; deprivation. For example: "The company suffered huge financial losses last year."
- **Noun (n)**: The amount by which something is diminished or decreased; reduction. For example: "Weight loss is a common goal for many people starting a new exercise routine."
- **Noun (n)**: In sports or games, the situation where one side or player has fewer points than the opposing side. For example: "Their team suffered another loss in yesterday's match."
- **Verb (v)**: To fail to keep or maintain; mislay or be without after having had. For instance: "I seem to have loss my keys again."
- **Verb (v)**: To experience death, destruction, or failure. Example: "She feared the loss of her family's ancestral home."
- **Verb (v)**: To cause a diminishment or decrease in size, quantity, or quality. Example: "Years of neglect have led to the loss of many historic buildings."

## Phrases
- "At a loss": Unable to decide what to do or find a solution. Example: "After his sudden departure, we were all at a loss about how to proceed."
- "Loss leader": A product sold at a low price to attract customers and encourage them to buy other items. 
- "Take a loss": To accept defeat or financial setback. Example: "The investor decided to cut his losses and sell his shares."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The emotional loss was almost too much to bear after her pet passed away."
- **Verb**: "He was devastated when he realized he'd loss the precious family heirloom."
- **Noun (in sports context)**: "The championship title slipped from their grasp with a crushing loss to their rivals."
