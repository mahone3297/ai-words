## Word: Owe

## Pronunciation
/əʊ/

## Definitions
- **Verb (v)**: To have a moral or legal obligation to give, repay, or do something for someone else, typically because of receiving something from them or having been helped by them. For example: "I owe you a debt of gratitude for your help."
  - **Infinitive form**: to owe
  - **Past tense**: owed
  - **Present participle**: owing
  - **Past participle**: owed

### Phrases
- **Owe someone money**: To be in debt to someone financially.
- **Owe someone an explanation/apology**: To have a responsibility to provide an explanation or apology to someone.
- **Owe it to oneself**: To have a duty or responsibility towards oneself to do something beneficial or right.
- **Pay what one owes**: To fulfill financial or other obligations.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "I owe you $50 for that concert ticket."
- "She still owes her friend a favour after borrowing her car last week."
- "He felt he owed his success to his parents' unwavering support."

## Synonyms
- Be indebted to
- Have a debt to
- Are under an obligation to
- Must pay back
- Are liable for
- Are responsible for
- Need to repay or return
- Due (money) to
