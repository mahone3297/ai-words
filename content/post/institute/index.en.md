# Institute

## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈstɪtjuːt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: (n) An organization founded for a specific purpose, often educational or research-oriented, such as a college, university, or research center. Example: "He studied at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology."
- **Verb**: (v) To establish or start something on a permanent basis, especially an organization or system. Example: "The government institutes new policies to tackle climate change."

### Noun Usage Examples
- "The company plans to donate funds to several research institutes."
- "They are founding a new language institute to promote cultural exchange."

### Verb Usage Examples
- "A new law was instituted to protect consumer rights."
- "She instituted a series of reforms to improve the school's curriculum."

## Phrases
- "Institute of higher learning": Refers to a college or university.
- "Institute proceedings": To initiate legal action against someone.
- "Institute a policy/change": To introduce and put into effect a new policy or change.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The art institute is hosting a prestigious exhibition this month."
- **Verb**: "After years of discussion, they finally decided to institute a recycling program in the city."
