## Pronunciation
/rɪsk/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Risk**: A possibility of harm, damage, or loss, especially when associated with some action or event. [Definition source: Oxford Dictionaries]
   - Example: "Taking on too much financial risk can be detrimental to one's future."
2. **Risky behavior**: An action that involves a high probability of negative consequences. [Definition source: Merriam-Webster]

### Verb
1. **To take a risk**: To undertake a potentially dangerous or uncertain action. [Definition source: Cambridge Dictionary]
   - Example: "He decided to start his own business, taking a big risk."
2. **To assess a risk**: To evaluate the likelihood and potential consequences of a situation. [Definition source: Collins Dictionary]

### Adjective
1. **Risky**: Characterized by the likelihood of danger or uncertainty. [Definition source: Macmillan Dictionary]
   - Example: "The new project was considered risky due to market fluctuations."

## Phrases
- "Assume the risk" means to accept the possibility of danger or loss.
- "Manage risk" is the process of identifying, evaluating, and controlling potential hazards.
- "Risk aversion" refers to a tendency to avoid actions with potential negative outcomes.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She was willing to take the financial risk to invest in her education."
- **Verb**: "The team decided to take a calculated risk by launching a new product."
- **Adjective**: "The adventurer was known for his willingness to take on risky challenges."
