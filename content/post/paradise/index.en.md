## Pronunciation
/ˈpærədaɪs/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: (n) A place or state of perfect happiness; an idyllic or blissful retreat or realm. For example: "They dreamed of finding their own little paradise on earth."
- **Noun**: (n) In religious contexts, it refers to the Garden of Eden or Heaven, considered the final resting place of the righteous souls. For example: "The faithful believe that they will enter Paradise after death."
- **Noun**: (figuratively) Any delightful or beautiful place offering great pleasure or contentment. For example: "The island was a tropical paradise with crystal-clear waters and pristine beaches."

## Phrases
- "A taste of paradise": refers to experiencing something so pleasurable that it feels like a momentary glimpse of heaven.
- "Paradise lost": from John Milton's epic poem, metaphorically used to describe the loss of a perfect state or situation.
- "Go to paradise": often used colloquially to mean 'to die' with the implication that the person will go to a better place after death.
