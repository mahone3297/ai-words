## Pronunciation
/sɪˈkəndli/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: Used to indicate the sequence of events or to emphasize something that comes after another point or item in a list. It can also imply comparison or contrast.

## Phrases
- "Firstly, and secondly" is a structure used to list points or reasons.
- "In conclusion, secondly..." signals a continuation after summarizing main points.
- "Secondly, it's important to..."

## Example Sentences
- **Comparison**: "She has two advantages: firstly, her language skills; secondly, her experience in the field."
- **Sequence**: "Let's discuss the problem. Firstly, we need to understand the cause; secondly, we'll find a solution."
- **Contrast**: "While some prefer coffee, secondly, others opt for tea as their morning beverage."

## Synonyms
- Next
- Secondly, however
- Moreover
- Furthermore
- Also
- In addition
- Then
- Subsequently

## Note:**
As "secondly" is primarily an adverb, it does not have direct noun, verb, or adjective forms. The examples provided above focus on its usage as an adverb in various contexts.
