## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈdʒʊər/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To cause physical harm or damage to someone or something; to hurt or wound. For example: "The fall from the ladder seriously injured his leg."
- **Verb**: To cause damage or harm to something non-physical, such as a reputation or relationship. For example: "His reckless behavior could injure the company's reputation."
  
  > Injure can also be used transitively, meaning it takes an object. E.g., "The accident injured several passengers."
  
## Phrases
- "Injure oneself": To accidentally hurt oneself. Example: "She injured herself while lifting weights."
- "Injure another person": To purposefully or accidentally cause harm to someone else. Example: "The driver failed to stop at the red light and ended up injuring two pedestrians."
  
## Example Sentences
- **Verb (physical injury)**: "He was taken to the hospital after being injured during the football match."
- **Verb (non-physical injury)**: "Her insensitive remarks deeply injured her friend's feelings."

## Synonyms
- Wound
- Hurt
- Damage
- Harm
- Bruise
- Traumatize
- Maim (more severe injuries)
- Afflict
- Impair
- Disfigure (in cases of permanent physical damage)
