## Pronunciation
/həˈləʊ/

## Definitions
### Noun
A term used to greet or address someone. For example: "She waved a friendly hello at the passerby."
### Verb
To say "hello" or greet someone. For example: "He greeted the teacher with a hearty hello in the morning."
### Adjective
Referring to something that is related to or expressing a greeting. For example: "The store had a cheerful hello sign outside."
### Interjection
Used to greet, attract attention, or express surprise. For example: "Oh, hello there! I didn't expect to see you."

## Phrases
- "Give someone a hello" means to acknowledge or greet them.
- "A hello from afar" signifies a distant greeting.
- "Hello, old friend" is a familiar way of addressing someone you haven't seen in a while.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She heard a chorus of hellos as she entered the classroom."
- **Verb**: "After a long day, he was happy to hear his family's hello from the airport."
- **Adjective**: "The postcard had a big, colorful hello on the front."
