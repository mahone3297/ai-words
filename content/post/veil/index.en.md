## Pronunciation
/veɪl/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Veil**: A lightweight fabric worn over the head, especially by women, as a covering or decoration, often for religious or cultural reasons.
   - Example: "The bride wore a delicate white wedding veil."
2. **Veil of secrecy**: An obscurity or concealment surrounding a particular subject or event.
   - Example: "The government kept the details of the negotiations under a veil of secrecy."

### Verb
1. **To veil**: To cover or conceal something, usually with a veil or a metaphorical one.
   - Example: "She tried to veil her disappointment behind a smile."
2. **To obscure**: To make something less visible or clear.
   - Example: "The fog veiled the mountain peak."

### Adjective
1. **Veiled**: Concealed, hidden, or indirect.
   - Example: "Her veiled criticism was aimed at the new policy."

## Phrases
- "Lift the veil" means to reveal or uncover something.
- "A veiled threat" refers to a subtle indication of potential harm or punishment.
- "Veiling one's emotions" means to hide feelings or expressions.

## Example Sentences

- **Verb**: "The detective tried to unveil the truth behind the crime."
- **Adjective**: "His veiled expression suggested he was hiding his true intentions."
