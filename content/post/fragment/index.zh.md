## 读音
/ˈfræɡmənt/

## 释义
- **名词**:
   - 一片，碎块，碎片：A small part of something that has been broken or separated from the rest. 例如: "The vase shattered into many fragments."
   - （文学、艺术作品的）片段：A section or excerpt from a larger work. 例如: "He read a fragment of his new novel."

- **动词**:
   - 分成碎片，破碎：To break or cause something to break into pieces. 例如: "The explosion fragmented the window."
   - 取得片段：To obtain or create a part of something larger. 例如: "The computer program fragmented the file into smaller parts for easier transfer."

## 短语
- "Fragment of a sentence" 指句子的一部分。
- "Fragmented society" 指分裂或破碎的社会结构。

## 例句
- 名词: "The archaeologists found fragments of ancient pottery in the excavation site."
- 动词: "The rock was hit by a hammer and fragmented into tiny pieces."
