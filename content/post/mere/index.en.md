## Word: Mere

## Pronunciation
/ˈmɪər/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: 1. Merely; only (used to emphasize how small or insignificant something is). For example: _It's a mere formality to sign this document._
   - 2. Barely sufficient or minimal; extremely small or limited. For instance: _The child was but a mere toddler when he learned to read._
   - 3. Referring to a body of water that is shallow, often a lake or pond. Example: _They swam across the mere._

- **Noun**: A small lake, especially one that is broad and shallow. Example: _The village overlooked a tranquil mere._

## Phrases
- "A mere matter of time": This phrase implies that something is bound to happen sooner rather than later.
- "Mere words": Implies that the spoken or written words lack substance or action behind them.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: _The hike up the mountain was no mere stroll in the park._
- _She dismissed his accomplishments as mere luck._
- _We camped by the mere during our weekend getaway._
  
- **Noun**: _The old mill stood picturesquely by the edge of the mere._

## Synonyms
- Adjective: Simply, barely, just, no more than, nothing but
- Noun (body of water): Lake, pond, pool, lagoon
