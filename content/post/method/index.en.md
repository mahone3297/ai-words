## Word: Method

## Pronunciation
/ˈmeθəd/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A set of procedures, techniques, or rules that are used in a particular area of study, activity, or problem-solving process, often involving a systematic approach. For example: "The scientific method involves formulating hypotheses and testing them through experiments."
  - _Example Sentence_: "Her teaching method emphasizes hands-on learning."
- **Verb (rarely used)**: To treat or deal with in a certain manner; to handle systematically. For example: "He methoded the issue by breaking it down into smaller parts."

## Phrases
- "Methodology": The system of methods and principles used in a particular discipline or field of study.
- "Trial and error method": An approach to problem-solving where one tries various solutions until finding the correct one.
- "By methodical means": Through a systematic or orderly process.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The team adopted a new project management method to streamline their workflow."
- **Verb**: "He methodically went through each step of the algorithm to ensure accuracy."
