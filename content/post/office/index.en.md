## Pronunciation
/ˈɒfɪs/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A room or set of rooms where people work, typically in an administrative capacity. It can also refer to the building that houses such rooms. _Example_: "The company's headquarters has a spacious main office with several departments."
  - _As a collective noun_: The staff or organization associated with a particular type of work or function. _Example_: "She works for the sales office."
- **Verb (transitive)**: To assign or allocate to a particular position or duty within an office or organization. _Example_: "After graduation, he was officed in the New York branch."

## Phrases
- "In office": Refers to holding a position or being actively engaged in one's duties in an official capacity. _Example_: "The president has been in office for two terms."
- "Office hours": The designated time during which a professional, usually a teacher or professor, is available to meet with students or clients.
- "Office politics": The often complex interpersonal relationships and strategies employed by individuals to gain advantage or influence within an organization.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "I need to go to the post office to send this package."
- **Verb**: "They decided to office the new employee in the corner cubicle for maximum concentration."
