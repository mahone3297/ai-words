## Word: Offer

## Pronunciation
/ˈɔːfər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A proposal or suggestion made with the intention of being accepted, often involving a price or conditions. For example: "The company made a generous job offer to the candidate."
- **Verb**: To present something (such as a product, service, or proposition) for acceptance or rejection; to propose or suggest something. For example: "They offered to help with the move."

### Noun Usage Examples
- "I received an offer on my house that was too good to refuse."
- "She couldn't turn down the job offer from her dream company."

### Verb Usage Examples
- "He offered his assistance when he saw us struggling with the heavy boxes."
- "The seller is offering a discount if we buy multiple items."

## Phrases
- "Make an offer" - To propose a deal or suggest a price.
- "Counteroffer" - A response to an initial offer, typically suggesting different terms.
- "Offer condolences" - To express sympathy, especially after someone's death.
- "An offer you can't refuse" - An idiom referring to an extremely attractive or compelling proposal.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The real estate agent presented several offers for the property."
- **Verb**: "We will offer our support in any way we can during this difficult time."
