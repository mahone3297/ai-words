## Pronunciation
/stɪkɪ/

## Definitions
### Noun
A sticky substance that adheres to surfaces, typically with a certain degree of resistance when pulled away. For example: "The tape left a sticky residue on the table."
### Verb
To become stuck or adhere to something, often in a troublesome manner. For example: "The page was so sticky that it wouldn't come out of the book."
### Adjective
1. Characterized by a tendency to stick or adhere: "A sticky note on my computer screen."
2. Having a high viscosity or resistance to flow: "A sticky syrup."
3. (informal) Tending to be clingy or persistent: "A sticky relationship."

## Phrases
- "Get something unstuck" means to free an object from being stuck.
- "A sticky situation" refers to a difficult or problematic scenario.
- "A sticky finger" is a colloquial term for a finger that has become covered in something sticky.

## Example Sentences
