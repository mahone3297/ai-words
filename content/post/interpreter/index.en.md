## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈtɜːprɪtər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A person who translates spoken or written words from one language into another. For example: "The conference hired professional interpreters for the foreign delegates."
- **Noun (figurative)**: A person or device that explains or deciphers information that might be difficult to understand otherwise. For example: "A good teacher acts as an interpreter between complex theories and students' understanding."

## Phrases
- "Simultaneous interpreter" refers to an interpreter who translates orally while someone speaks, typically used in conferences or meetings.
- "Sign language interpreter" is someone who facilitates communication between deaf people and those who can hear by translating between sign language and spoken language.
- "Interpreter service" refers to the provision of interpreting services, often provided by agencies or professionals for various settings such as legal, medical, or business contexts.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (language)**: "She's been working as a French-English interpreter at the United Nations for years."
- **Noun (figurative)**: "The new software update includes an interpreter feature that helps users decode technical jargon."

## Synonyms
- Translator (in the context of converting languages)
- Interpreter (in the figurative sense, may be replaced with 'mediator', 'explainer', 'decoder')
