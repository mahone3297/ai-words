## Pronunciation
/pəˈtrəʊliəm/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A noun referring to a complex mixture of hydrocarbons found beneath the Earth's surface, primarily used as fuel for transportation and various industrial processes. Also known as crude oil.
- **Verb**: To extract or refine petroleum from the ground. For example: "The oil company is planning to drill for more petroleum reserves."

## Phrases
- "Petroleum-based products" encompasses items made from crude oil, such as gasoline and plastics.
- "Fossil fuels" often include petroleum, coal, and natural gas.
- "Oil spill" refers to an accidental release of petroleum into the environment.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The country relies heavily on petroleum exports for its economy."
- **Verb**: "The refinery is processing large quantities of crude petroleum to produce diesel and gasoline."
- **Adjective**: "Petroleum-fueled vehicles are a major source of greenhouse gas emissions."
