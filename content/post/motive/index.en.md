## Pronunciation
/ˈməʊtɪv/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A reason for doing something, often hidden or underlying; an incentive or driving force. For example: "The main motive behind his decision was financial gain."
- **Noun (Law)**: Grounds or reasons that prompt a person to act in a certain way, especially those leading to a criminal offense. For instance: "Detectives were trying to uncover the murderer's motive."
- **Adjective**: Relating to or serving as a cause or reason; having the power to move to action. Example: "His motive powers were ambition and love of learning."

## Phrases
- "Motive force" refers to something that stimulates action or change.
- "Hidden motive" suggests a concealed reason or agenda behind someone's actions.
- "Mixed motives" implies that there are multiple reasons driving a person's behavior.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Her primary motive for accepting the job offer was to further her career."
- **Noun (Law)**: "The prosecution had to prove beyond reasonable doubt that the defendant had a motive to commit the crime."
- **Adjective**: "His motive-driven approach to work has earned him numerous accolades."
