## Pronunciation
/pɪn/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A small, usually cylindrical object with a pointed end, used for fastening things together or marking a position.
- (informal) A person, especially a woman, who is considered attractive or sexually appealing.
- In games like bowling, a small ball with one pin-shaped end that is knocked over by the thrower.

### Verb
- To affix or fix with a pin, often metaphorically.
- To secure or hold in place with a pin.
- To indicate or designate something as the best or most preferred choice.

### Preposition
- To put or attach something with a pin, as in "pinning up a notice."

## Phrases
- "Pin something down" means to secure or fix it in place.
- "Pin a thought" is a figurative expression to remember or hold onto an idea.
- "To pin a blame" is to assign responsibility for an issue or mistake.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The tailor pinned the fabric to the pattern to check the fit."
- **Verb**: "I need to pin this map to the wall so it doesn't fall down."
- **Preposition**: "She pinned her hopes on winning the competition."
