## Pronunciation
/ˈʃæm-pəʊn/

## Definitions
### Noun
A shampoo is a hair care product designed to cleanse, nourish, and condition the scalp and hair. It is typically applied wet and rinsed off. 

### Verb (transitive)
To shampoo (one's hair) means to wash it using shampoo, usually for cleaning and conditioning purposes.

### Adjective
Shampoo can also be used as an adjective to describe a type of hair treatment or a characteristic of a product that cleanses hair effectively.

## Phrases
- "Shampoo your hair" is the act of washing it with shampoo.
- "Dry shampoo" is a product used for refreshing hair between washes without water.
- "Sulfate-free shampoo" refers to a formula without harsh detergents to avoid stripping natural oils.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She uses a gentle shampoo to prevent her sensitive scalp from irritation."
- **Verb**: "I need to shampoo and condition my hair before going out tonight."
- **Adjective**: "The baby has fine, silky hair that requires a moisturizing shampoo."

Shampoo comes in various formulations, such as foaming, volumizing, color-safe, or targeted for specific hair concerns like dandruff, oily hair, or dryness. Some people also use it as a body wash or for pets.
