## Pronunciation
/stɪf/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A stiff person is one who is inflexible, unyielding, or rigid in their movements or opinions.
- A stiff piece of material, such as cardboard or paper, is hard and unyielding.
- In card games, a stiff hand refers to a poor hand with few high-ranking cards.
- A stiff competition or challenge is one that is difficult or closely contested.

### Verb
- To stiffen (sth.) means to make something harder or stiffer, often by adding a solidifying agent.
- To stiff someone (usually in business) is to refuse to pay them, typically for goods or services provided.

### Adjective
- Stiff can describe a person who is unresponsive or unemotional.
- It can also mean cold or uncomfortable, as in "a stiff breeze" or "a stiff neck from sleeping poorly."
- In terms of clothing, it refers to tight-fitting or formal attire.

### Adverb
- Stiffly describes movement that is awkward, uncoordinated, or lacking flexibility.

## Phrases
- "To take something stiffly" implies doing so with rigidity or formality.
- "A stiff upper lip" is a British expression meaning to remain composed and resilient in the face of adversity.
- "A stiff drink" refers to a strong alcoholic beverage.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The old man had a stiff back from years of manual labor."
- **Verb**: "She tried to straighten the sofa cushions, but they were already quite stiff."
- **Adjective**: "The ceremony was conducted with a stiff formality."
- **Adverb**: "He danced stiffly at the party, clearly uncomfortable on the dance floor."
