## Pronunciation
/trʌst/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Trust**: 1. Confidence in someone or something, often based on reliability or past experience. "She has complete trust in her friend's judgment."
   - **Synonyms**: confidence, faith, reliance, belief, dependence

2. The act of placing reliance on someone or something for care or management. "The children were placed in the trust of their grandparents."
   - **Synonyms**: custody, guardianship, fiduciary responsibility, delegation

3. A legal arrangement in which a person (the trustee) holds assets for the benefit of another person (the beneficiary). "A trust fund is established for the education of the beneficiaries."

### Verb
- **To trust**: To have faith or confidence in someone or something. "I trust her with important decisions."
   - **Synonyms**: believe, rely, depend, entrust, confide

### Adjective
- **Trusted**: Believed to be reliable or honest. "She is a trusted colleague."
   - **Synonyms**: dependable, reliable, honest, credible

### Adverb
- **Truly**: In a true or genuine manner. "Truly, he was grateful for her help."

## Phrases
- "Trust but verify" is a maxim advising caution before accepting something as true.
- "In her trust" signifies reliance or confidence placed in someone.
- "Placing trust in a system" refers to having faith in its functionality.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The family's future was placed in the trust of the financial advisor."
- **Verb**: "She decided to trust her instincts about the situation."
- **Adjective**: "He is a truly trusted friend."
- **Adverb**: "She trusted him with the secret truly."
