## Word: Meat

## Pronunciation
/miːt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The flesh of animals (e.g., beef, pork, lamb, poultry) used as food. For example: "They served a juicy steak for the main course of meat."
- **Noun (figurative)**: The most important or substantial part of something; essence. For example: "The meat of the argument was buried deep within the lengthy essay."

### Noun Examples
- "Vegetarians don't consume meat products."
- "The butchers were cutting and packing various types of meat in the store."
- "After a long hunt, they finally had fresh meat for their winter stores."

## Phrases
- "Meat and potatoes" refers to basic or substantial food items, often indicating a preference for simplicity or traditional values.
- "Where's the beef?" is an idiomatic expression questioning where the substance or value is in a situation.
- "Bring home the bacon" means to earn money to support one's family, referring metaphorically to meat as sustenance.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "At the barbecue, we grilled several different kinds of meats, including burgers and sausages."
- **Noun (figurative)**: "In his speech, he got straight to the meat of the matter without any unnecessary preamble."
