## Pronunciation
/ɒd/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Not even; not divisible by two without a remainder; not capable of being evenly paired off. Example: "There's one odd sock left after the laundry."
  - Also used to describe something that is unusual, peculiar, or unexpected. Example: "Her behavior was rather odd lately."
- **Noun**: A number that cannot be expressed as the sum of two equal integers; an odd number. Example: "All the odd numbers between 10 and 20 are 11, 13, 15, 17, and 19."
  - In gambling, it can refer to an unlikely event or bet. Example: "The odds were against him winning, but he placed an odd on it anyway."

## Phrases
- "Odd man out": The person who does not fit in or match with others in a group, often due to a numerical disparity. Example: "In a group of four friends, he felt like the odd man out."
- "At odd hours": At irregular or unpredictable times. Example: "She would work on her art projects at odd hours during the night."
- "By odd coincidence": When something happens unexpectedly and surprisingly coincidentally. Example: "By odd coincidence, we both wore the same dress to the party."

## Example Sentences
