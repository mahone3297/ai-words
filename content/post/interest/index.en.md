## Pronunciation
/ˈɪntrɛst/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A feeling of curiosity or concern about something or someone; a desire to learn or know more. Example: "She has a great interest in ancient history."
- **Noun (n)**: The benefit or advantage that one gets from something, especially money earned on an investment. Example: "The bank pays a high rate of interest on savings accounts."
- **Noun (n)**: Something that holds a person's attention or arouses their curiosity because it is enjoyable, entertaining, or valuable. Example: "Reading was his main interest."
- **Verb (v)**: To cause someone to have a feeling of curiosity or concern about something. Example: "Her stories always interest people from all walks of life."
- **Verb (v)**: To invest money in order to earn a financial return. Example: "They decided to interest some of their savings in stocks and shares."
  
## Phrases
- "Take an interest in something": To develop a liking or enthusiasm for something. Example: "He started taking an interest in gardening during lockdown."
- "In the interests of...": For the benefit or protection of something or someone. Example: "The new regulations were introduced in the interests of public safety."
- "To have a vested interest": To have a personal stake or involvement in something that can influence one's actions or decisions. Example: "As shareholders, they have a vested interest in the company's success."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "His primary interest lies in understanding the intricacies of the human mind."
- **Verb**: "This novel really interests me; I can't put it down."
- **Noun (financial)**: "The company borrowed a large sum at a fixed rate of interest."
