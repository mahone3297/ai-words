## Pronunciation
/vəʊˈləʊt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A measure of electric potential difference between two points in a circuit, usually measured in volts (V). It represents the force that causes electric current to flow.
- **Adjective**: Relating to or denoting voltage or electrical pressure.

## Phrases
- "Voltage drop" refers to a decrease in voltage across a conductor.
- "High voltage" designates a high level of electrical potential.
- "Stable voltage" indicates a constant and reliable electrical supply.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: The power source provides a stable 12-volt voltage to run the electronic devices.
- **Adjective**: The transformer stepped up the low voltage to high voltage for transmission over long distances.
- **Phrasal Verb**: The electrician checked for any voltage fluctuations in the wiring.

## Synonyms
- Electric potential
- Electromotive force (EMF)
- Electrical pressure
- Voltaginal force
- Potential difference

## Antonyms
- Ground (zero voltage)
- Low voltage
- Insulation (no flow of voltage)
