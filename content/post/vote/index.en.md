## Pronunciation
/vəʊt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A countable noun**: A formal expression of preference or decision, typically in a political or organizational context. For example: "The citizens cast their votes in the election."
- **An uncountable noun**: The act of expressing one's preference by choosing between options. For example: "The students were asked to vote on the best activity for the field trip."

### Verb
- To express one's choice or approval in a decision-making process. For example: "Everyone in the meeting was invited to vote on the new proposal."
- To approve or agree with something. For example: "The proposal was voted through by a large margin."

## Phrases
- "Cast a vote" means to exercise your right to choose or decide by voting.
- "Vote for/against" is used to indicate support or opposition in an election or decision.
- "Unanimous vote" signifies that all members agree on a particular decision.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After the debate, the senator waited for the final vote on the bill."
- **Verb**: "The shareholders will vote on whether to approve the merger."
- **Phrasal**: "The board of directors made a unanimous vote to approve the new CEO."
