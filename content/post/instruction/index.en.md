## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈstrʌkʃən/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A set of directions or guidelines meant to be followed to complete a task, understand a process, or operate something. For example: "The instructions for assembling the furniture were easy to follow."
  - Also, the act of teaching or directing; guidance. Example: "The teacher's clear instructions helped her students understand the concept."
- **Verb (v)**: To direct, advise, or teach someone how to do something. Example: "The coach instructed the team on the new play."
- **Noun (legal)**: A formal order or direction, especially one given by a court or other authority. Example: "The judge issued specific instructions to the jury."

## Phrases
- "Follow instructions": To adhere to the provided guidelines or directions.
- "Instruction manual": A booklet that accompanies a product, containing step-by-step instructions for use or assembly.
- "Under instruction": Being taught or directed to perform a certain action or task.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Read the instructions carefully before beginning the experiment."
- **Verb**: "The chef instructed the apprentice on how to prepare the perfect soufflé."
- **Legal Noun**: "The judge read out the instructions to the jury before they began their deliberations."
