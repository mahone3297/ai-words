## Pronunciation
/perˈfekt/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. Complete without flaw or defect; flawless: "A perfect day at the beach."
2. Conforming exactly to a standard or ideal: "Her performance was close to perfection."
3. (mathematics) Having no factors other than 1 and itself: "The number 7 is a prime, therefore perfect."
4. (music) In a key that contains no sharps or flats: "The song is written in a perfect key."

### Verb
1. To be or make something completely without flaws or errors: "She perfected her technique after months of practice."
2. To achieve a state of excellence or completeness: "He perfected the recipe for his famous cake."

### Adjective
1. Without fault or error: "The plan was a perfect fit for their needs."
2. Ideal or optimal: "The solution was a perfect balance between efficiency and simplicity."
3. Complete or total: "They had a perfect understanding of the situation."
