## Pronunciation
/θrɔŋ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A large group of people gathered together, often in a crowd or moving in a mass. ["A throng of spectators filled the stadium."](https://www.dictionary.com/browse/throng)
- **Verb**: To gather in a large, dense group; swarm. ["The festival attracted a throng of visitors from all over the world."](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/throng)

## Phrases
- "Crowd together" means to form a dense group.
- "A sea of people" describes a large throng.
- "Throngs of admirers" signifies many people who are fans or admirers.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The city square was thronged with holiday shoppers."
- **Verb**: "The concert venue was thronged with fans eager to see the performance."
- **Adjective**: "The museum entrance was guarded by a throng of reporters."

## Synonyms
- Crowd
- Swarm
- Gathering
- Hostile horde (informal, implies a dense, possibly unruly group)
- Mob (slight negative connotation, suggesting an unruly or aggressive group)
