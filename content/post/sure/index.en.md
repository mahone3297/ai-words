## Pronunciation
/ʃʊə(r)/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. Certainty or confidence: *I'm sure that he'll come.*
2. Assured knowledge or belief: *She was sure of his innocence.*
3. A person who is certain or confident: *The host was a sure presence at every event.*

### Verb
1. To be confident or convinced: *I'm sure that we'll win the game.*
2. To confirm or assure: *He's sure to let us know the result soon.*
3. (informal) To affirm or state with certainty: *I'm sure you'll enjoy the concert.*

### Adjective
1. Absolutely certain: *There's no surefire way to solve this problem.*
2. Reliable or dependable: *A sure bet is someone you can count on.*
3. Without doubt or hesitation: *Her answer was a sure yes.*

### Adverb
1. Definitely or certainly: *He's sure to arrive on time.*
2. Positively or confidently: *She spoke her mind with sure conviction.*

## Phrases
- "Be sure about something" means to have confidence in a decision or fact.
- "Sure enough" is used to confirm a prediction or expectation.
- "No problem, sure thing" expresses willingness to help or agree.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "He was absolutely sure of his future career path."
- **Verb**: "She's sure to get an A in the math exam if she studies hard."
- **Adjective**: "The weather forecast is looking very sure for sunny skies tomorrow."
- **Adverb**: "He's always sure to remember our anniversary."
