## Word: Innocent

## Pronunciation
/ˈɪnəsnt/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Free from guilt, sin, or immorality; not responsible for wrongdoing. For example: "The child's innocent eyes sparkled with curiosity."
- **Noun**: A person who is free from guilt, especially one who has not committed a specific crime. For example: "The accused was proven innocent by the jury."
- **Adjective (Law)**: Not legally guilty of a crime or offense. For example: "After reviewing the evidence, the judge declared him innocent."
- **Adjective (Naive)**: Lacking worldly experience, knowledge, or sophistication; unsophisticated. For example: "She had an innocent belief that everyone tells the truth."

## Phrases
- "Innocent until proven guilty": The principle that a person accused of a crime is considered not guilty until evidence proves otherwise.
- "Innocent bystander": A person who is uninvolved in a conflict or situation but is affected by it nonetheless.
- "Guilty conscience needs no accuser; an innocent life laughs at threats": A proverb emphasizing that those who have done nothing wrong have nothing to fear.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective (Guiltlessness)**: "Despite their suspicious behavior, they were entirely innocent of any involvement in the theft."
- **Noun**: "As the sole innocent among the guilty parties, he was released without charge."
- **Adjective (Naivety)**: "His innocent assumption that all people are inherently good led to his downfall."
