## Pronunciation
/ˈpɒnd/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A body of water typically small, shallow, and often surrounded by land**: A natural pond filled with lilies or a man-made pond for fishing.
- **An enclosed area of water, often artificial, used for ornamental purposes or as a habitat**: The community decided to create a wildlife pond in the park.

### Verb
- **To deposit something into a pond or pool**: She ponded the rainwater in a barrel.
- **To accumulate or collect (liquid) in a small pool**: The melting snow ponded on the roof.

## Phrases
- "Toss a stone in the pond" refers to making a small splash or disturbance in the water.
- "The pond's ecosystem" describes the plants and animals living together in that water body.
- "Pond life" can refer to the creatures that inhabit a pond.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The children loved feeding the ducks in the nearby pond."
- **Verb**: "After a long walk, he stopped to pond his sweat."
- **As a Verb**: "The gardener ponded the excess water from the garden hose."
