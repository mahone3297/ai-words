## Pronunciation
/səʊˈkɔːld/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Referring to something that is commonly known by a particular name but may have a different or more accurate description. For example: "The so-called experts often disagree."
- **Adverb**: In a so-called manner; typically used to emphasize that something is not genuinely considered or acknowledged. For example: "She's living in a so-called mansion, but it's really just a large house."

## Phrases
- "So-called fact" implies that something is not genuinely a fact.
- "The so-called best" suggests that a label is given without proper validation.
- "A so-called authority" refers to someone whose credibility is questionable.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The so-called miracle cure turned out to be a placebo."
- **Adverb**: "He's a so-called success, but his methods are questionable."
- "Her so-called talent was exposed after a poor performance."
