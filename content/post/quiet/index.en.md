## Pronunciation
/ˈkwaɪət/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Characterized by a lack of noise or disturbance; peaceful and calm. For example: "The library is a quiet place to study."
- **Noun**: Silence or stillness; a state of being quiet. For example: "The baby fell asleep amidst the soft quiet of the night."
- **Verb**: To make or become quiet; to silence. For example: "The teacher asked the students to quiet down."
- **Adverb**: In a quiet manner; softly. For example: "She whispered to avoid waking the baby, but her voice was still quite quiet."

## Phrases
- "Keep quiet" means to remain silent or not speak.
- "Speak quietly" is a request for someone to lower their volume.
- "A quiet moment" refers to a brief pause or a serene atmosphere.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The children were quiet after their mother's stern look."
- **Noun**: "She enjoyed the quiet of the countryside during her vacation."
- **Verb**: "The baby quieted down when she heard her favorite lullaby."
- **Adverb**: "Please be quiet while we listen to the lecture."
