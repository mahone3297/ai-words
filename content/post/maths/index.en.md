## Word: Maths

## Pronunciation
/ˈmæθs/ (British English), /ˈmæθ/ (American English)

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The branch of knowledge that deals with numbers, quantities, and space, either as abstract concepts (pure mathematics) or as applied to other disciplines such as physics and engineering. It encompasses arithmetic, algebra, geometry, calculus, and various other related studies.

Example Sentence:
- _Maths is his favorite subject in school._

Synonyms:
- Mathematics
- Arithmetic
- Algebra
- Geometry
- Calculus
- Quantitative Reasoning

### Phrases
- "Do your maths homework" implies working on mathematical exercises assigned by a teacher.
- "Maths skills" refers to proficiency and ability in solving mathematical problems.
- "Mathematical thinking" denotes the logical and analytical thought processes characteristic of math.

### Example Sentences
- **Noun**: _Improving her maths skills became a priority for Jane._
- _The complexity of higher maths often intimidates beginners._
  
Note: In American English, the singular form "math" is commonly used instead of the British English plural form "maths." However, the essence of its meaning remains the same across both variations.
