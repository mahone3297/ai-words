## Pronunciation
/shəʊt/

## Definitions
### Noun
A loud cry or exclamation to attract attention or express strong emotion. For example: "He let out a loud shout to alert the others."
- **Synonyms**: yell, cry, exclamation, outburst

### Verb
1. To utter a loud cry or sound, especially in anger or excitement. For example: "The children shouted with joy when they received their presents."
2. To express or communicate a message by shouting. For example: "He shouted instructions to the crowd during the protest."

### Adverb
In a manner of shouting or loudly. For example: "She shouted her answer across the room."

## Phrases
- "Shout out" means to call out or announce something publicly.
- "To shout encouragement" is to cheer someone on vociferously.
- "Loud shout of laughter" describes a hearty, boisterous laugh.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The crowd erupted in a shout of approval."
- **Verb**: "He shouted his name to be heard over the music."
- **Adverb**: "She shouted commands to her team during the game."
