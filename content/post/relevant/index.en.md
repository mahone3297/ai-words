## Pronunciation
/rɪˈvɛlənt/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Relevant** (adj): Pertaining to something that is important, applicable, or connected to a particular context or issue. For example: "The new policy is highly relevant to our company's goals."
2. **Relevance** (noun): The quality of being pertinent or related to a specific topic or situation. For example: "The discussion focused on the relevance of historical events to current issues."

### Adjective
3. **Relevantly** (adv): In a way that is related or applicable. For example: "The data was analyzed relevantly to draw meaningful conclusions."

## Phrases
- "Be relevant to" implies connection or significance to a particular subject.
- "Keep it relevant" suggests staying on point or maintaining focus.
- "Relevant information" denotes data or facts that are essential or useful.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "His suggestions were highly relevant to the project's success."
- **Adjective**: "The course content was designed to be relevantly challenging for students of all levels."
- **Adverb**: "She presented her findings in a way that was relevantly engaging."
