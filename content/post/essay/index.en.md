## Pronunciation
/ˈeseɪ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A short piece of writing on a particular subject, often written as a school or college assignment. _Example:_ "She submitted her persuasive essay on climate change."
  - Also, an attempt or effort to do something. _Example:_ "His first essay at painting was surprisingly good."

- **Verb**: To try or attempt (to do something). _Example:_ "She decided to essay a new approach to solving the problem."

## Phrases
- "Essay question": A question in an exam that requires an extended answer in the form of an essay.
- "To essay a role": In acting, it means to take on a character or part in a play or film.
  
## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The student spent hours perfecting his history essay before submitting it."
- **Verb**: "He decided to essay a solo journey across Europe to challenge himself."

## Synonyms
- Noun (for written piece): Composition, paper, dissertation, thesis, article
- Verb (to attempt): Try, endeavor, undertake, assay

Note: The verb form of "essay" is less commonly used than its noun form in everyday language. When used as a verb, it often carries a sense of undertaking a task with some difficulty or novelty, rather than just performing a routine action.
