## Pronunciation
/sevrəl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: 1. More than two but not many; a few but not all. For example: "There were several people waiting for us."
- **Determiner**: Used to indicate an indefinite number, typically more than one. For example: "Several books are on the table."
- **Adverb**: In a quantity or number that is not specified; a few or various. For example: "She ate several cookies."

## Phrases
- "Several days later" refers to a period after some time has passed.
- "Several attempts" means multiple tries.
- "Several reasons" implies multiple causes or explanations.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "I bought several new books for my book club."
- **Determiner**: "There were several ideas discussed during the meeting."
- **Adverb**: "She made several phone calls before finding the right contact."

## Synonyms

- Several dozen (to indicate a specific, approximate number)
- A number of
- Multiple
- Some
- A couple

## Antonyms
- One
- None
- Few (implying only a small number)
