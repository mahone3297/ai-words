## Definitions
### Noun
- **Static electricity**: A form of electric charge that remains relatively stationary on the surface of a material, often causing a temporary shock when touched. Example: "The carpet can accumulate static during dry winter months."
- **Static content**: Unchanging or fixed material in a website or software application. Example: "The header of a webpage usually contains static content like the logo and navigation menu."

### Verb
- To remain unchanged or unresponsive. Example: "The radio station's signal was static during the storm."
- To describe or characterize something as being motionless or unchanging. Example: "The atmosphere at the event was static and unexciting."

### Adjective
- Fixed, unchanging, or immovable. Example: "The static position of stars in the night sky is a familiar sight."
- Pertaining to electricity that is stationary or at rest. Example: "The device has a built-in static filter to reduce interference."

### Adverb
- In a static manner; without movement or change. Example: "She sang in a static voice, without any emotion."

## Phrases

- "Avoid static cling" refers to preventing clothes from sticking together due to static electricity.
- "A static analysis" is a preliminary examination of code or data without running it.
