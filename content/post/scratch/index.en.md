## Pronunciation
/skrætʃ/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Scratch** (noun): 
   - The act of making a light, rapid movement with a sharp tool or the fingernails, causing a surface to be scored or removed.
   - A superficial mark or blemish on the skin or surface.
   - In music, a rhythmic pattern or loop created by scratching records, often associated with DJing.
   - A small amount of money or goods given as a reward or prize.

Example: "The cat scratched the furniture when it was startled."

### Verb
1. **To scratch** (verb):
   - To make a scratching motion or mark with a tool or nails.
   - To cause itching or irritation.
   - To create or produce music by manipulating records or digital audio.
   - To seek or attempt to obtain something through informal means.

Example: "The artist scratched out an unwanted line on the canvas."

### Adjective
1. **Scratched** (adjective):
   - Marked or damaged by scratches.
   - Having a rough, uneven surface due to scratches.
   - Belonging to or involving scratching or DJing.

Example: "The scratched vinyl records had a nostalgic charm."

## Phrases
- "Scratch an itch" means to relieve an itching sensation.
- "Scratch the surface" implies only a superficial investigation or understanding.
- "Scratch card" refers to a card with a code to be redeemed for a prize.
