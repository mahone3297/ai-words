## Pronunciation
/sɔːs/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A liquid or semi-liquid mixture usually containing spices, herbs, or other flavorings, used to enhance the taste of food.
   - Example: "The dish was served with a rich tomato sauce."
2. A condiment or spread, often made from fermented ingredients, like mayonnaise or mustard.
   - Example: "I put some ketchup on my sandwich for extra sauce."

### Verb
1. To apply or coat food with a sauce.
   - Example: "She sauced the chicken with a tangy barbecue sauce."
2. (figurative) To provide a complement or additional element to something.
   - Example: "This story adds the perfect sauce to the overall narrative."

### Adjective
1. Characterizing or flavored by a sauce.
   - Example: "The creamy sauce gave the pasta a luxurious texture."

## Phrases
- "Add some sauce to your plate" means to serve or pour sauce over your food.
- "A saucepan of spaghetti sauce" refers to a pot of prepared sauce for pasta.
- "Sauce it up" can mean to make something more flavorful or attractive.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The steak was cooked medium-rare, with a flavorful garlic herb sauce."
- **Verb**: "She sauced the vegetables with a homemade pesto before serving."
- **Adjective**: "The salad had a delightful lemon and dill sauce dressing."

## Synonyms

- Dressing
- Relish
- Gravy
- Glaze
