## Pronunciation
/rɪˈbɜːr/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **robber** is a person who forcibly takes property or money from another person, often using violence or intimidation. In legal terms, it refers to an individual committing the crime of robbery.

### Verb
To **rob** is to take something by force or theft from someone else, typically with violence or threat of violence.

## Phrases
- "Rob a bank" or "Robbery took place" refers to the act of committing a robbery.
- "Caught the robber red-handed" describes finding someone in the act of committing a robbery.
- "Be robbed of your peace" implies having one's tranquility stolen.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The police were investigating the recent string of robberies in the neighborhood."
- **Verb**: "The thief was sentenced to ten years in prison for robbing an elderly couple."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "He was robbed of his wallet while jogging in the park."
