## Pronunciation
/tɛkˈstʊbʊk/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A book designed for use as a primary source of study or instruction, typically containing a systematic presentation of a subject. 
- **Verb**: To serve as a textbook or be used as one, providing comprehensive information on a subject. 

## Phrases
- "Use a textbook for learning" indicates the purpose of studying from a book.
- "A standard textbook" refers to a widely accepted resource in a particular field.
- "Textbook case" is a perfect example or situation that illustrates a concept well.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The high school students relied heavily on their biology textbook for assignments."
- **Verb**: "This new course will follow a textbook approach, with chapters covering different topics."
- "The textbook's diagrams made it easier to understand complex theories."

## Synonyms
- Textbook material
- Study guide
- Reference book
- Educational text
- Coursebook

## Antonyms
- Non-fiction (opposite of a general fiction book)
- Unstructured (contrasted with a well-organized text)
- Informal (in contrast to a formal educational resource)
