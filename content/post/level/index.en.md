## Word: Level

## Pronunciation
/ˈlɛvəl/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 
  - A horizontal plane or line that serves as a reference point for measuring height or depth. Example: "The water reached a dangerous level."
  - A position on a graded scale, especially one of a series of stages in a process or hierarchy. Example: "She's achieved a new level of success in her career."
  - A device used to establish or indicate a horizontal plane. Example: "A carpenter's spirit level."
  
- **Verb**:
  - To make something horizontal or flat. Example: "We need to level the ground before we build the house."
  - To aim or direct something horizontally. Example: "He leveled his gaze at the speaker."
  - To bring to a common standard or degree; equalize. Example: "The new policy aims to level the playing field for small businesses."

- **Adjective**:
  - Having no part higher than another; flat or even. Example: "The land is fairly level around here."
  - Being of the same rank, status, or ability. Example: "They're on a level footing with their competitors."

## Phrases
- "On a level playing field": In a situation where all participants have an equal chance of succeeding.
- "Level up": To advance to a higher rank or stage, often used in video games or metaphorically in life or career situations.
- "Level best": One's utmost effort or ability; trying one's hardest.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The rising sea levels pose a threat to coastal cities."
- **Verb**: "He took a moment to level his camera before taking the shot."
- **Adjective**: "The road stretches out across the level landscape."

## Synonyms
- Flat (for adjective meaning)
- Horizontal (for noun meaning related to plane or line)
- Equal (for verb meaning related to bringing to the same standard)
- Balanced (in certain contexts)
