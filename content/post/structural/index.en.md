## Pronunciation
/ˈstrʌktʃərəl/

## Definitions
### Noun
A structural adjective that describes something related to the arrangement, organization, or composition of a system, building, or design. It can also refer to the supporting framework or elements that give a structure stability.

### Verb
To describe or analyze the structure of something, often in a systematic or formal manner. This can include the way components are arranged or connected.

### Adjective
1. Characterizing or concerned with the arrangement or composition of parts in a system or object, typically to ensure stability or functionality.
2. Of or relating to the basic framework or constitution of a thing, such as a building or a theory.

## Phrases
- "Structural changes" imply modifications to the fundamental structure.
- "The structural integrity" refers to the strength and stability of a structure.
- "Structural analysis" is the process of studying how different parts work together.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: The architect paid close attention to the structural details of the bridge.
- **Verb**: We need to conduct a structural assessment of the old house before renovating.
- **Adjective**: The new software has a more intuitive structural layout than the previous version.
