## Pronunciation
/slaɪd/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A sliding object or surface**: A piece of material that moves along a track or surface, often used for presentations or entertainment. Example: "The children were playing on the park slide."
- **A downward movement**: The act of going down a slope or incline, typically with ease. Example: "She glided down the slide at the playground."
- **A decrease or decline**: A reduction in value or position. Example: "There was a slide in the stock market."

### Verb
- **To move smoothly down a surface**: To go down a slide, typically for fun or as part of an activity. Example: "The kids slid down the hill on their sleds."
- **To change gradually or steadily**: To progress from one level to another, usually with a decline. Example: "The company's profits have been sliding over the past few quarters."
- **To pass or move from one position to another**: In a context of technology or presentation, to change slides in a presentation software. Example: "Click the arrow to slide to the next page."

### Adjective
- Describing a smooth and easy motion: "A glassy slide on the river."
- Expressing a decline or loss: "The company's sales have experienced a steady slide."
