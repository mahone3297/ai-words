## Pronunciation
/weɪkˈniː/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To make something less strong, powerful, or effective; to cause a decline or reduction in intensity, force, or effectiveness. For example: "The storm weakened the old bridge."
- **Noun**: The act of becoming less strong or powerful; a decline in strength or intensity. For example: "The patient's condition showed signs of weakening."

## Phrases
- "Weaken a resolve" means to make someone's determination or commitment less firm.
- "Weakened defenses" indicate a decreased ability to protect oneself.
- "Weakened by fatigue" suggests exhaustion has reduced someone's strength.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "Exercise can help to strengthen your muscles and prevent them from weakening over time."
- **Noun**: "The economy was weakened by the global recession."
- "His resolve began to weaken after several consecutive failures."
