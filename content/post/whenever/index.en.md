## Pronunciation
/ˈwenˈevə(r)/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: Indicating a particular time or condition at which something occurs or is done. For example: "I'll call you whenever you're free."
- **Prepositional phrase**: A combination of a preposition and its object, functioning as an adverb. For example: "She arrived whenever the sun set."

## Phrases
- "whenever possible" suggests a preference for a certain time or situation.
- "whenever necessary" implies doing something when it's required or appropriate.
- "whenever one pleases" indicates that someone can do something at their own convenience.

## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "I'll let you know whenever there's an update on the project."
- **Prepositional phrase**: "She would often read books whenever the weather was fine."

## Synonyms
- At any time
- Whenever it suits
- As the occasion arises
- At liberty
- On demand

## Antonyms
- Never
- Specifically
- Only at certain times (opposite of always)
