## Pronunciation
/ˌʌndərˈteɪk/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A task or undertaking**: A project or activity that one agrees to do or take responsibility for. For example: "The company assigned him a major undertaking to complete by the end of the month."
- **An agreement to do something**: A commitment or promise to carry out a particular action or deal with a situation. For example: "She undertook to help with the renovation project."

### Verb
- **To assume or agree to do something**: To begin or engage in a task, often with a sense of commitment. For example: "He undertook the challenge of climbing the mountain."
- **To assume the risk or liability for**: To take on the responsibility for an action or its consequences. For example: "The business owner undertook the debt."

## Phrases
- "undertake a project"
- "undertake a journey"
- "undertake a mission"
- "undertake a challenge"

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The team was given the daunting task of undertaking a large-scale software rewrite."
- **Verb**: "After much contemplation, he decided to undertake the arduous climb up the Himalayas."
- **Verb (with risk)**: "The investor was willing to undertake the high-risk investment in a startup."
