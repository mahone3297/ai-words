## Word: Modest

## Pronunciation
/ˈmɑːdɪst/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Having or showing a moderate or humble estimate of one's merits, importance, etc.; not arrogant or boastful. Example: "She was a modest woman who never sought the limelight."
- **Adjective**: (of clothing, decoration, etc.) Not showy or excessive; unpretentious. Example: "The bride wore a simple, yet elegant and modest dress."
- **Adjective**: Limited in size, amount, or scope; small. Example: "They lived in a modest house on the outskirts of town."

## Phrases
- "Lead a modest life": Living without extravagance or seeking excessive attention or possessions.
- "Modest proposal": An ironic or satirical suggestion that often highlights societal issues by proposing an extreme solution.
- "Modesty is a virtue": This phrase suggests that having humility and avoiding boasting is a commendable quality.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective (personality)**: "Despite his many accomplishments, he remained modest about his achievements."
- **Adjective (clothing)**: "She preferred to wear clothes that were both comfortable and modest for her religious beliefs."
- **Adjective (size)**: "His ambition was to build a modest home where his family could live happily."

## Synonyms
- Unassuming
- Humble
- Self-effacing
- Reserved
- Conservative (in terms of appearance)
- Moderate (in terms of quantity or size)
