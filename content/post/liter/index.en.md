## Word: Liter

## Pronunciation
/ˈlɪtər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The basic unit of measurement for volume in the metric system, equal to 1,000 cubic centimeters or approximately 0.264 U.S. gallons. For example: "The fuel tank can hold ten liters of gasoline."
- **Noun (informal)**: A container that holds one liter of liquid, especially a bottle or can. For instance: "She bought a liter of milk from the store."

## Phrases
- "A liter of water a day keeps dehydration away" – emphasizing the importance of daily water intake.
- "Fill it up with five liters" – when referring to filling a container with a specific volume of liquid.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (measurement)**: "The recipe calls for two liters of broth."
- **Noun (container)**: "He recycled the empty plastic liter bottles."

## Synonyms
- litre (British English)
- Metric quart
- 1,000 milliliters
- About 33.8 ounces (US fluid ounces)
