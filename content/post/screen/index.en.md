## Pronunciation
/skriːn/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **1** (countable) A device with a surface for displaying images or text, typically electronic, used for watching movies, playing video games, or working on a computer.
   - Example: "The TV is the main screen in our living room."
   - **2** (countable) A partition or surface that separates one area from another, often with a translucent material.
      - Example: "A window acts as a screen between the inside and outside."
   - **3** (uncountable) The act of screening or the process of filtering out unwanted content or substances.
      - Example: "The movie went through multiple rounds of screen before its release."

### Verb
- To display something on a screen, especially a computer monitor or television.
   - Example: "The software will automatically screen the incoming emails."
- To examine or review something carefully, typically in search of flaws or hidden information.
   - Example: "The security team screens all luggage before it enters the airport."

### Adjective
- Referring to the surface or appearance of a screen.
   - Example: "The glass screen had a sleek design."

### Adverb
- In a manner that involves a screen or uses a screen.
   - Example: "He worked remotely, screen-sharing with his colleagues."

## Phrases
- "On the screen" refers to a visual display.
- "Behind the screen" implies what happens offstage or unseen.
- "Screening tests" are medical examinations done to detect potential issues.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The audience watched the film on a large cinema screen."
- **Verb**: "The IT specialist was screen-casting her desktop to the remote team."
- **Adjective**: "Her eyes were fixed on the bright screen, absorbed in the game."
- **Adverb**: "Behind the screen, the technicians were busy setting up the equipment."
