## Pronunciation

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Adjective**: In good health or condition; fit. For example: "She's feeling very well today."
- **Adverb**: To a satisfactory degree; adequately. For example: "He did his work well."
- **Adverb of manner**: With skill or effectiveness. For example: "She sang well."

### Verb
- **Adverb**: In a healthy or favorable state. For example: "The patient is recovering well."
- **Adverb**: To perform or do something competently. For example: "He did a good job well."
- **Adverb**: To experience or feel something comfortably. For example: "I slept well last night."

### Adverb
- **Interjection**: Expressing satisfaction, approval, or agreement. For example: "Well done!"
- **Preposition**: Indicating a measure or extent. For example: "The water was not well mixed."

## Phrases
- "Take care of yourself" implies maintaining good health.
- "Do it well" emphasizes the importance of quality.
- "Not well" is used to indicate poor health or condition.
