## Pronunciation
/səˈɡnətʃə(r)/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A person's distinctive mark or style, usually consisting of their name written in a particular manner, often appended to documents or letters.
   - Example: "The artist signed each painting with their unique signature."
2. A characteristic or quality that identifies or distinguishes someone or something.
   - Example: "Her writing style is easily recognizable by her signature flourish."

### Verb
1. To affix one's signature to a document, indicating agreement or authorization.
   - Example: "He carefully signed the contract before sealing it with a wax seal."
2. To mark or indicate one's presence, approval, or support.
   - Example: "The celebrity signed autographs for fans at the event."

### Adjective
1. Characteristic of a person's handwriting or style.
   - Example: "A cursive signature is more personal than a printed one."
2. Serving as a symbol or indicator.
   - Example: "The logo on the product is a signature design element."

## Phrases
- "Sign your name" is the act of placing one's signature on a document.
- "A signature piece" refers to something that is uniquely identifying or representative of an individual.
- "Signed off" indicates that someone has agreed or acknowledged something by signing.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She proudly displayed her new artwork, featuring her signature blend of colors."
- **Verb**: "After finishing the project, he signed off on the final report."
- **Adjective**: "His signature style of photography is instantly recognizable."
