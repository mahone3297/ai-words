## Pronunciation
/wʌri/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **noun** (v. **worry**): An emotion characterized by uneasiness, anxiety, or concern about a problem or situation. For example: "Her constant worries over her job prospects were affecting her mental health."

### Verb
A **verb** (v. **worry**): To be concerned or anxious about something, often to an excessive degree. For example: "I'm worried about the upcoming exam."
- **Gerund**: To worry oneself (e.g., "He worries himself sick over small details.")

### Adjective
An **adjective** (adj. **worried**): Describing a state of being anxious or concerned. For example: "She looked worried when she heard the news."

### Adverb
An **adverb** (adv. **worriedly**): Describing the manner in which someone worries, typically with anxiety. For example: "He worriedly scanned the report for any errors."

## Phrases
- "Worry about something" means to have concern or fear regarding a matter.
- "Ease someone's worries" is a way to alleviate their anxiety.
- "Don't worry, it will be fine" is a reassurance given to calm someone's concerns.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She couldn't shake off the worries that had been plaguing her mind."
- **Verb**: "The parents worry constantly about their child's safety."
- **Adjective**: "His worried expression showed how much he was troubled by the situation."
- **Adverb**: "He worriedly bit his lip as he considered the problem."
