## Pronunciation
/sənˈtəns/

## Definitions
### Noun
A sentence is a grammatical structure that expresses a complete thought, idea, or statement, typically consisting of a subject, verb, and (often) an object. In written or spoken language, it is the basic unit of communication that conveys meaning.

### Verb
To sentence, or to pronounce a sentence, is to issue a formal judgment, declare a punishment, or to express a complete thought in the form of a grammatically correct statement.

### Noun (colloquial)
Sometimes used informally to refer to a group of words that convey a message, even if not a complete grammatical sentence.

## Phrases
- "Formulate a sentence"
- "Write a sentence that summarizes the main point"
- "Sentence structure"
- "Run-on sentence" (a sentence that joins two or more independent clauses without proper punctuation)

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The teacher asked the students to write a five-sentence paragraph about their vacation."
- **Verb**: "The judge sentenced the defendant to six months in prison."
- **Colloquial**: "I need to clear my head before I can string together a coherent sentence."
