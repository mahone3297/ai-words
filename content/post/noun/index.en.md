## Word: Noun

## Pronunciation
/naʊn/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A word (except a pronoun) that represents a person, place, thing, quality, action, or idea, and is typically used as the subject or object of a verb, or as the object of a preposition. For example: "The cat sat on the mat." In this sentence, "cat" and "mat" are nouns.

### Examples
- **Common Noun**: "A dog barked loudly."
- **Proper Noun**: "London is the capital of England." Here, "London" is a specific place.
  
## Phrases
- "Concrete noun" refers to tangible things that can be perceived through the senses, e.g., "apple."
- "Abstract noun" refers to ideas, concepts, emotions, e.g., "love" or "happiness."
- "Collective noun" is a noun that refers to a group of individuals, e.g., "herd" for a group of animals.
  
## Example Sentences
- **Common Noun**: "The bird flew over the tree."
- **Proper Noun**: "William Shakespeare wrote many plays."
- **Abstract Noun**: "Honesty is the best policy."
- **Collective Noun**: "The team won the championship."

## Synonyms
- Thing
- Entity
- Concept
- Object
- Being
- Person
- Place
- Idea

Note: The concept of 'noun' itself doesn't have synonyms as it is a part of speech rather than a lexical item with a meaning equivalent to other words. However, examples above demonstrate different types of nouns one might encounter in language use.
