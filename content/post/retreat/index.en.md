## Pronunciation
/rɪˈtrɪt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. An act of withdrawing or drawing back, especially from an enemy or a difficult situation. 2. A place of refuge or seclusion, often a temporary one. 3. A military withdrawal or evacuation. 4. (informal) A vacation or break from work or responsibilities.
- **Verb**: 1. To withdraw or move back, typically in response to a threat or for safety. 2. To withdraw from an engagement or commitment. 3. To go on a retreat for rest or spiritual purposes.

## Phrases
- "Retreat from" indicates a withdrawal or retreat from a particular location or situation.
- "Retreat to" implies going to a specific place for rest or seclusion.
- "Desertion or retreat" refers to abandoning a post or duty.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After the battle, the soldiers retreated to a nearby hill for cover."
- **Verb**: "In the face of criticism, he decided to retreat from the public eye."
- **As a noun**: "The team went on a retreat to improve their communication skills."
