## Pronunciation
/ˈmɛməriəl/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: (n) An object, such as a monument, statue, or plaque, established to remind people of a person or event from the past; something intended to preserve the memory of someone or something. For example: "They unveiled a memorial to the war heroes in the town square."
- **Adj**: (adj) Relating to or serving as a memorial; commemorative. For example: "The memorial service was held to honor her late father."

### Noun Usage Examples
- "The 9/11 Memorial & Museum commemorates the victims of the terrorist attacks."
- "The family erected a memorial bench in the park where their loved one often sat."

### Adjective Usage Examples
- "The memorial concert celebrated the musician's life and work."
- "A memorial plaque on the wall lists all the names of those who died in the disaster."

## Phrases
- "Memorial Day" - a US public holiday observed on the last Monday of May to remember and honor those who have died while serving in the military.
- "Memorial service" - a ceremony conducted in memory of a deceased person, typically without the remains present.
  
## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The community gathered around the war memorial to pay their respects."
- **Adjective**: "The memorial fund was set up to support research into the disease that took his life."
