## Pronunciation
/ˈmjuːtʃuəl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Relating to or involving two or more parties, with each having equal or reciprocal rights and obligations. For example: "They have a mutual understanding about their roles in the project."

  - **Mutual respect**: A shared recognition and admiration between people.
  - **Mutual fund**: An investment vehicle that pools money from many investors to purchase a diverse portfolio of stocks, bonds, or other assets.
  - **Mutual friend**: A person who is a friend to two or more individuals.

## Phrases
- **Mutual agreement**: When both parties consent to the same terms or conditions.
- **Mutual benefit**: A situation where both sides gain something from an interaction or relationship.
- **Mutual trust**: The reliance and confidence that exists between two or more people.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "Their mutual love for books sparked a strong friendship."
- "After some discussion, they reached a mutual agreement on how to divide the workload."
- "Investing in a mutual fund can help diversify your financial portfolio."
