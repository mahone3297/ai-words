## Pronunciation
/kəˈnædə/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: **[1]** The second largest country in the world, located in North America, bordered by the United States to the south and the Arctic Ocean, Atlantic Ocean, and Pacific Ocean to the north, east, and west. **[2]** The official name of the country, referring to both the federal state and its capital, Ottawa.
- **Adjective**: Characteristic of or relating to Canada. Example: "A maple leaf is a symbol of Canadian pride."

## Phrases
- "Call/e-mail someone from Canada" refers to contacting someone from the country.
- "Canadian cuisine" encompasses various dishes popular in the nation.
- "The Great White North" is a colloquial term for Canada, emphasizing its vastness and northern climate.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "We're planning a road trip across Canada this summer."
- **Adjective**: "The maple syrup in Canadian breakfasts is known worldwide."
- **Adjective**: "She's a proud Canadian, having grown up surrounded by stunning landscapes."

## Synonyms
- [Noun] - Commonwealth, Canada-US, Maple Land
- [Adjective] - Canadian, from Canada

## Note:
1. The first definition is the most common usage when referring to the country.
2. The second definition is specific to the adjective form, which is often used to describe something associated with Canada.
