## Pronunciation
/ʃɪft/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A shift** can refer to:
  - A change in position or direction: "The wind shifted directions during the storm."
  - A change in work schedule or duty: "She's taking a night shift at the hospital."
  - A political or social change: "The political landscape has undergone a significant shift."
  - A guitar playing technique: "He executed a smooth chord shift on the piano."

### Verb
- To change from one thing to another: "The company decided to shift its focus to e-commerce."
- To transfer responsibility or control: "The shift manager handed over the duties to the new employee."
- To alter one's behavior or attitude: "She shifted her approach after receiving feedback."

### Adjective
- Flexible or adaptable: "The new system allows for easy shifts between different settings."

### Adverb
- In a shifting manner: "The clouds drifted across the sky, casting shifting shadows on the ground."

## Phrases
- "Shift gears" means to change one's mindset or approach.
- "Shift workload" implies redistributing tasks among team members.
- "Shift responsibility" indicates passing tasks to someone else.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The worker was relieved from his day shift and headed home."
- **Verb**: "After the exam, he had to shift his study habits to prepare for the final."
- **Adjective**: "The adaptable shift schedule ensures everyone gets enough rest."

## Synonyms
- Change
- Transition
- Shiftiness (informal, primarily referring to a tendency to change)
- Reconfiguration
- Alteration
