## Definitions
### Noun
1. A collection of water, often used for swimming, cooling, or entertainment purposes, typically with a shallow end and a deep end.
   - Example: "The community center has a large outdoor pool for public use."
2. A group of people or things considered together, often sharing a common characteristic or interest.
   - Example: "A talent pool refers to a group of skilled professionals available for job opportunities."
3. An area of liquid that accumulates, often from rain or overflow.
   - Example: "The storm left a puddle on the street."

### Verb
1. To gather or accumulate in a pool or container.
   - Example: "The water pooled at the bottom of the garden hose."
2. To form a group or collection of people or resources.
   - Example: "The company is pooling their resources to fund a new project."

### Adjective
1. Designated for or involving a shared resource or activity.
   - Example: "A hotel with a poolside bar."
2. A term used to describe a type of game where players take turns sinking balls into a designated area.
