## Word: Imprison

## Pronunciation
/ɪmˈprɪzn/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To confine someone in a prison or place them under arrest, typically as a punishment for a crime or for detention pending trial. 

  > Example Sentence: He was imprisoned for his role in the fraud scheme.
  
- **Noun (Derived Form): Imprisonment**
  > Definition: The act of being confined in a prison; the state of being imprisoned.
  > Example Sentence: His sentence included a year's imprisonment.

## Phrases
- "To be imprisoned": To be put in jail or held captive.
- "Imprison one's mind": Figuratively, to restrict someone's thoughts or creativity.
- "Do time": To serve a prison sentence.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The accused was immediately imprisoned after the court pronounced its verdict."
- **Noun**: "His experience during his long years of imprisonment had profoundly changed him."
