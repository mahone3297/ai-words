## Pronunciation
/wɔːrn/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: n. **[warn]** (v) To give notice or advice about a possible danger, risk, or negative consequence; to caution. For example: "The teacher warned the students about the exam difficulty."
- **Noun**: The act of giving such a warning or the message conveyed. For example: "The warning signal sounded."

## Phrases
- "Warn someone against something" to advise against a particular action or situation.
- "Issue a warning" to formally notify about a potential issue.
- "Heeded warning" indicates that someone has taken notice and acted on the given advice.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The lifeguard warned swimmers about the strong currents."
- **Noun**: "Her warning about the approaching storm saved many lives."
- **Adverb**: "She spoke in a stern tone to give a strong warning."
