## Word: Locomotive

## Pronunciation
/ˌloʊkəˈmōtɪv/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A self-propelled railway vehicle that pulls a train along the tracks. It usually produces power through a diesel engine or electric motors. For example: "The steam locomotive was once the driving force of the industrial revolution."
  - _Example sentence_: "The locomotive's whistle echoed across the valley as it approached the station."
  
- **Adjective**: (less commonly) Describing something that has the ability to move itself, especially in a powerful or controlled manner. For instance: "The new robotic vacuum cleaner is a highly locomotive device."

## Phrases
- "At the head of the train" implies that the locomotive is leading the train.
- "Under its own power" can refer to a locomotive operating independently without being towed or pushed.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The engineer inspected the locomotive before setting off on the cross-country journey."
- **Adjective**: "The experimental locomotive vehicle demonstrated unprecedented agility and speed."

## Synonyms
- Engine (in the context of trains)
- Train engine
- Power car
- Diesel
- Electric (referring to the type of locomotive)
- Steam engine (for older models)
