## Pronunciation
/ˌʌndəˈɡrɑːdʒt/

## Definitions
### Noun
An **undergraduate** is a student who is pursuing a bachelor's degree at a college or university, typically in their first three or four years of study, before becoming a graduate student. 

### Verb
To be or act as an **undergraduate** can mean to attend or participate in undergraduate studies.

## Phrases
- "Freshman year" often refers to the first undergraduate year.
- "Upperclassmen" are typically third- or fourth-year undergraduates.
- "Graduating undergraduate" is someone about to complete their bachelor's degree.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She was excited to start her undergraduate studies at the prestigious university."
- **Verb**: "As an undergraduate, he joined several clubs to broaden his knowledge."
- **Adjective**: "The vibrant undergraduate community organized a charity event."

## Synonyms
- Bachelor's student
- Collegian
- Junior (in terms of academic level)
- Freshman or Sophomore (specifically in the first or second year)
- Young professional (implying someone just starting their career after completing undergraduate studies)

## Antonyms
- Graduate (a post-undergraduate student)
- Postgraduate (a student pursuing a higher degree after completing an undergraduate program)
