## Pronunciation
/stʌmbl/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A noun referring to an awkward or unsteady movement, typically caused by tripping or losing one's balance. Example: "He stumbled over a loose rock on the trail."
- A momentary loss of coordination or focus, often figurative, that hinders progress or understanding. Example: "She stumbled on the complex concept, needing time to digest it."

### Verb
- The action of moving with difficulty or carelessly, often due to a misstep or lack of balance. Example: "He stumbled down the stairs in the dark."
- To experience difficulty or confusion while speaking, thinking, or grasping a topic. Example: "She stumbled over her words during the presentation."

### Adverb
- Describes how something was stumbled upon, usually indicating clumsiness or unintentionality. Example: "He stumbled upon an old photo while cleaning out the attic."

## Phrases
- "Stumble across" means to accidentally discover something.
- "Stumble through" refers to struggling to accomplish something, usually due to difficulty.
- "Stumbling block" is a hindrance or obstacle that impedes progress.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "He regained his footing after a sudden stumble."
- **Verb**: "She stumbled over the revelation that her friend had lied to her."
- **Adverb**: "He stumbled through the interview, clearly nervous."
