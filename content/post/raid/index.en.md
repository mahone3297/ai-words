## Pronunciation
/ˈreɪd/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A noun referring to an act**: An armed attack on a place, especially one carried out suddenly and secretly. For example: "The police conducted a raid on the drug lab."
- **An event**: A sudden visit or inspection, often without notice. For example: "The school principal made an unannounced raid on a student's locker."
- **Informal**: A social gathering or party. For example: "We had a raucous raid on the local arcade."

### Verb
- **To carry out a raid**: To invade or seize something suddenly and often forcefully. For example: "The military raid on the enemy base was successful."
- **To inspect or search**: To examine something thoroughly, often with a specific purpose. For example: "The customs officers raided the cargo containers for contraband."

## Phrases
- "Be prepared for a raid" means to anticipate a surprise visit or inspection.
- "A raid on the cookie jar" is a colloquial expression for taking food without permission.
- "Police raid on a crime scene" signifies a swift investigation.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The special forces conducted a raid on the terrorist hideout."
- **Verb**: "The firefighters raided the burning building to rescue trapped victims."
- **Informal**: "We decided to throw a raid party for our friends."
