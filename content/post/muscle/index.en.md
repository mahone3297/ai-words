## Pronunciation
/ˈmʌsəl/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A body tissue composed of cells or fibers that can contract and produce movement, typically attached to bones and serving to effect motion. For example: "She developed strong muscles through regular exercise."
  - Also used metaphorically to refer to power or influence, e.g., "The government flexed its economic muscle."
  
- **Verb (v)**: To exert strength or force; to strain or make tense one's muscles. For example: "He had to muscle the heavy box onto the shelf."
  - Also used figuratively, meaning to use power or coercion to achieve something, e.g., "They muscled their way into the industry."

## Phrases
- "Flex your muscles": To show off one's strength or power, either physically or metaphorically.
- "Muscle memory": The ability to perform a learned motor task without conscious thought, due to repetition and practice.
- "Work out your muscles": Engage in physical activity to strengthen or tone the muscles.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Regular weightlifting helps build bigger and stronger muscles."
- **Verb**: "The athlete muscled past his opponents to take the lead in the race."
