## Pronunciation
/ˈʌnjən/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A bulbous plant with a pungent taste and smell, having hollow green leaves that surround a single edible white bulb composed of many layers. Example: "She diced a red onion to add to the salad."
- **Noun (n)**: The edible bulb of this plant, which is commonly used as a vegetable in cooking. Example: "Onions form the base for many savory dishes."

## Phrases
- "To bring tears to one's eyes": Refers to the effect chopping onions can have due to the release of sulfur compounds that irritate the eyes. Example: "Chopping onions always brings tears to my eyes."
- "Layer upon layer": This phrase is metaphorically used, often referring to complexity or depth, inspired by the layered structure of an onion. Example: "The mystery had layer upon layer like an onion."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "I'm going to sauté some onions and garlic before adding the other vegetables."
- "The aroma of caramelized onions filled the kitchen."
