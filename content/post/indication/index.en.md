## Pronunciation
/ˌɪndɪˈkeɪʃən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A signal, sign, or hint pointing towards something; a piece of information that suggests or indicates a fact or situation. For example: "There were several indications that the project was going off track."
  - Medical context: A physical symptom or a change that may suggest the presence of a disease or condition. Example: "The patient's high fever is an indication for further testing."
- **Verb**: (indicate) To point out, show, or make known indirectly or suggestively. Example: "The sales figures indicate a significant increase in demand."

## Phrases
- "Give an indication": To provide some information or a clue about something. Example: "The survey results will give an indication of public opinion."
- "No indication of": To not have any sign or evidence suggesting something exists or will happen. Example: "There's no indication of foul play in the incident."
  
## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Her silence was an indication of her disapproval."
- "His elevated heart rate was a clear indication that he had been exercising."
- **Verb**: "The report indicates that consumer confidence has improved this quarter."
