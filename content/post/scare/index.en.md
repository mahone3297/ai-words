## Pronunciation
/skeə/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To cause fear or alarm in someone. For example: "The loud noise scared the children."
- **Noun**: An incident or action that causes fear or anxiety. For example: "The haunted house was filled with terrifying scares."
- **Adjective**: Designed to provoke fear or startle. For example: "A scary movie is perfect for Halloween."

## Phrases
- "Scare someone silly" means to frighten someone so much that they become silly or lose their composure.
- "Get a scare" means to receive a sudden and unexpected fright.
- "Scared stiff" describes being extremely frightened, causing one's body to feel rigid.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The thief tried to scare the witness into silence."
- **Noun**: "She still has nightmares from the scary dream she had last night."
- **Adjective**: "The haunted house had many scary decorations and effects."
