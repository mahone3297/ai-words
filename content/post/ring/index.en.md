## Pronunciation
/rɪŋ/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A circular band of metal, bone, or other material worn on the finger as a sign of engagement, marriage, or as a decoration.
   - Example: "She wore a beautiful diamond ring on her wedding day."
2. An instance of a sound, typically produced by striking a bell or similar object, that signals a call or summons.
   - Example: "The phone rang, signaling an incoming call."
3. A series of sounds or events that create a pattern, often associated with a musical instrument or a cycle.
   - Example: "The doorbell rang three times before she answered."

### Verb
1. To produce a ringing sound, especially from a bell or electronic device.
   - Example: "The clock chimed five as he rang the doorbell."
2. To make a sound like a ring; to tinkle.
   - Example: "The wind chimes rang softly in the breeze."
3. To send or make a signal using a telephone or similar device.
   - Example: "I'll ring you back when I get home."

### Adjective
1. Characteristic of a ring or having the sound of a ring.
   - Example: "The ring tone was a cheerful jingle."
2. Circular or having a round shape.
   - Example: "The circular garden had a large iron fence surrounding it."

## Phrases
- "Ring a bell" means to evoke a memory or seem familiar.
- "Ring out" signifies a loud, clear sound ending something.
- "Ring true" suggests something is genuine or believable.

## Example Sentences
