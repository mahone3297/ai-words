## Pronunciation
/plænt/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A living organism, typically with a root, stem, leaves, and flowers, that absorbs water and nutrients from the soil and converts them into organic matter through photosynthesis.
   - Example: "The garden is filled with various plants, including roses and tomatoes."
2. The process of placing something in the ground or embedding it so that it grows.
   - Example: "She planted the tree in the spring to give it time to establish itself."
3. An establishment where plants are cultivated for sale or scientific study.
   - Example: "The botanical garden is a popular tourist attraction."

### Verb
1. To put (seeds or seedlings) in the ground to grow.
   - Example: "He will be planting vegetables in his backyard this weekend."
2. To establish or set up a company, organization, or system.
   - Example: "They are planning to plant a new branch office in New York."

### Adjective
1. Relating to a living organism or its growth.
   - Example: "A hydroponic plant system doesn't require soil."
2. Fixed or immovable, as in furniture or equipment.
   - Example: "The heavy machinery is firmly planted on the ground."
