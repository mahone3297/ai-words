## Pronunciation
/wɪls/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: Used to indicate that something happens while another action is taking place or during a specific period. It can also mean 'although' or 'while' in some cases.

## Phrases
- "Whilst working on the project" describes an action happening concurrently.
- "I'll visit my grandparents whilst they're still awake" implies a temporary overlap of events.
- "She was studying whilst listening to music" shows simultaneous activities.

## Example Sentences
- **Simultaneous action**: "He cooked dinner whilst watching the news."
- **Temporal contrast**: "Although it was raining, we decided to go for a walk."
- **Contrasting clauses**: "She was tired, but she carried on working whilst drinking coffee."

## Synonyms
- During (temporal context)
- While (temporal context)
- Although (contrast)
- Though (contraction of "although")

## Antonyms
- After (sequential order)
- Before (sequential order)
- Except when (specific condition)
