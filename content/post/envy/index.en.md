## Word: Envy

## Pronunciation
/ˈenvi/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A feeling of discontent or resentful longing aroused by someone else's possessions, qualities, or luck. For example: "She felt a pang of envy when she saw her friend's new car."
  
  > _Example sentence:_ "His success has bred envy among his colleagues."
  
- **Verb**: To feel or show envy towards (someone); to wish that one had a quality, possession, or experience enjoyed by another. For example: "She envied her sister's confidence."

  > _Example sentence:_ "I envy those who can speak multiple languages fluently."

## Phrases
- "Green with envy": A phrase indicating intense feelings of envy, often used metaphorically referring to the idea that jealousy can make one look pale or sickly green.
- "Envy someone their success": To feel envy towards someone because of their achievements.
  
## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Her lavish lifestyle was the object of much envy."
- **Verb**: "She envies the freedom that comes with being self-employed."

## Synonyms
- Jealousy
- Covetousness
- Longing
- Yearning
- Resentment
- Admiration tinged with envy
- Enviousness
- begrudging
- Emulation (in a sense of striving to equal or surpass what one envies)
