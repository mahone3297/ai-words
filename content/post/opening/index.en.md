## Pronunciation
/ˈəʊpənɪŋ/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: The act of making something accessible or available by moving something that blocks it, or the space thus created. For example: "The store had a grand opening yesterday."
  - Also refers to the initial part or stage of an event, process, or activity. Example: "The play's opening scene was breathtaking."
  - In a building, it can refer to an aperture, gap, or entrance through which one can enter or pass. Example: "The front door has a large window in the opening above it."
  - In a letter, document, or book, it can mean the introductory part. Example: "The novel's opening chapter sets the tone for the entire story."
  - In business or employment, it signifies a new job opportunity or vacancy. Example: "There's an opening for a manager in the company."

- **Verb (v)**: To move something so as to make a space or passage through; to start or initiate something. Example: "We're opening the conference with a keynote speech."
  - Also used to describe the action of starting or beginning an event, session, or period. Example: "The exhibition opens at 10am tomorrow."
  - When referring to a business, it means to start operating for the day or for the first time. Example: "The shop opens at 9am every morning."

## Phrases
- "Opening night" refers to the first public performance of a theatrical production.
- "Opening hours" are the times during which a business or institution is open to the public.
- "Opening remarks" are the initial comments made at a meeting or event.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The ribbon-cutting ceremony marked the official opening of the new library."
- **Verb**: "She opened her eyes to find the sun shining brightly through the window."

## Synonyms
- Noun: commencement, debut, inauguration, outset, inception, launch, debutante, gateway, portal, vacancy
- Verb: initiate, commence, start, begin, launch, unlock, reveal, disclose, uncover, admit
