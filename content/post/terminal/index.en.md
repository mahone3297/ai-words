## Pronunciation

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Terminal**: (1) A device or place where a communication system, such as a train or airline, ends or connects to other routes. Example: "The airport terminal is bustling with travelers."
- (2) The final stage of a process or series of events, often marked by the end or conclusion. Example: "The project reached its terminal phase before the deadline."
- (3) In computing, a software program that provides access to a network or the internet. Example: "A web browser is a common terminal application."
- (4) (Medicine) The end point of a disease or treatment, often marked by death. Example: "The patient's terminal illness progressed rapidly."

### Verb
- To reach or arrive at a terminal or endpoint. Example: "The train terminated at the city center."
- To conclude or finish a process, especially abruptly. Example: "Their conversation terminated after a disagreement."

### Adjective
- Designating the last or final stage of something. Example: "A terminal stage of cancer."
- Reaching or situated at the end of a line or route. Example: "A terminal station on the subway."

### Adverb
- (Computing) In a manner that indicates the end of a process. Example: "The command was executed terminal."

## Phrases
- "At the terminal" refers to the end point or check-in area at an airport or train station.
- "Terminal condition" describes a severe and incurable medical state.
- "Terminal velocity" is the maximum speed reached by an object falling through the air without further acceleration due to air resistance.
