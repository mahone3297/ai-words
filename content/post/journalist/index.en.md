## Pronunciation
/ˈdʒɜːrnəlɪst/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A person who collects, writes, edits, photographs, or otherwise reports news or other current information to the public, typically through newspapers, magazines, radio, television, or online media platforms. For example: "She has been working as a journalist for over a decade, covering political events worldwide."

### Example Sentences
- "The investigative journalist broke the story on corruption within the local government."
- "After studying journalism in college, he landed a job as a freelance journalist for several renowned magazines."

## Phrases
- "Journalist on the ground": Refers to a reporter who is physically present at an event or location to cover it firsthand.
- "Citizen journalist": An amateur reporter who gathers, reports, or analyzes news and information, often using digital technology such as social media.
- "Journalistic ethics": The principles of conduct for journalists that guide them in making professional decisions, such as accuracy, fairness, and objectivity.
