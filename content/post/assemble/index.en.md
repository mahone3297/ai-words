## Pronunciation
/əˈsæmbəl/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Assembly**: The act or process of bringing together various parts to form a whole. For example: "The team was assembling the robot for the competition."
- **Collection**: A group of items gathered together. For example: "A collection of antique books was displayed at the museum."
- **Assembly line**: A manufacturing setup where products move continuously along a series of workstations. For example: "The assembly line workers were efficiently assembling cars."

### Verb
- **To assemble**: To put together or combine parts to form a whole. For example: "We'll need to assemble the furniture before we can move in."
- **Compile**: To gather and organize information or components to create a final product. For example: "The programmer assembled all the code into a functional program."

### Adjective
- **Assembled**: Made up of separate parts that have been brought together. For example: "The team arrived with their assembled equipment."

## Phrases
- "Assemble something" means to put together the parts of a product.
- "Assembled by": Indicates that a product was put together by a specific party.
- "Disassemble to inspect" is the process of taking apart to check for damage or maintenance.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The volunteers worked tirelessly to assemble the charity event's decorations."
- **Verb**: "The engineer carefully disassembled the machine to find the source of the problem."
- **Adjective**: "The pre-assembled kit made it easy for beginners to build the model."
