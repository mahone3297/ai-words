## Pronunciation
/ənˈtɪl/

## Definitions
- **Preposition**: A part of speech that indicates the point up to which something continues or the time when an action stops. For example: "She will work until 5 PM."
- **Adverb**: Used to indicate the extent or duration of an action or until a particular point in time. For example: "I'll wait until you finish your meal."

## Phrases
- "Until tomorrow" signifies a time limit until the next day.
- "Until further notice" means until a new instruction or arrangement is made.
- "Not until" is a common construction meaning "only after" or "only when".

## Example Sentences
- **Preposition**: "I have to study until my exam on Friday."
- **Adverb**: "She decided to stay home until the rain stopped."
- "I won't go outside until the sun comes up."
