## Pronunciation
/rɪˈbɛl/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A person who opposes or resists authority or established norms, often violently or openly.
2. [Slang] A person who defies expectations or rules, typically in a non-violent manner.

### Verb
1. To rise up against authority or established order, often in opposition.
2. To act in defiance or disobedience, especially against rules or conventions.

## Phrases
- "Rebel against" indicates resistance or opposition.
- "He's a political rebel" describes someone who challenges the status quo politically.
- "Born to be a rebel" is a phrase used to describe someone who seems naturally inclined to defy authority.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The young rebels marched through the streets, demanding change."
- **Verb**: "She rebelled against her father's strict rules."
- **Sentence with rebel as a verb**: "After years of working in a corporate job, he decided to rebel and start his own business."
