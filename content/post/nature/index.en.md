## Word: Nature

## Pronunciation
/ˈneɪtʃər/

## Definitions
### Noun (n)
- The inherent characteristics, qualities, or tendencies of a person, animal, thing, or phenomenon; the essential aspects that determine its identity or behavior. Example: "The nature of a lion is to be fierce and territorial."
- The phenomena of the physical world collectively, including plants, animals, the landscape, and other features and products of the earth, as opposed to humans or human creations. Example: "Exploring the untouched beauty of nature was refreshing."
- The natural world considered as a system functioning independently of human interference or control. Example: "The balance of nature is fragile and needs protection."
- A person's character or basic disposition; temperament. Example: "His nature is one of kindness and generosity."

### Verb (v)
*(Nature can also be used in certain contexts as a verb meaning 'to give something its fundamental character or quality,' but this usage is less common)*

### Adjective (adj)
- Occurring or existing in nature rather than being made or caused by people; not artificial. Example: "Nature trails offer hikers the chance to experience the natural beauty up close."

## Phrases
- "In nature": Referring to things as they exist in reality rather than in theory or under controlled conditions. Example: "The species thrives in nature but struggles in captivity."
- "Nature vs. nurture": A concept referring to the debate about whether inherited traits or environmental influences have a greater impact on an individual's development.
- "Take a walk in nature": To spend time outdoors in natural surroundings.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The calmness of her nature helped her navigate through difficult times."
- **Adjective**: "They decided to use only organic, nature-based ingredients in their skincare products."
