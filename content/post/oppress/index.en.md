## Pronunciation
/əˈpres/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To keep someone down by unjust use of one's authority or power; to subject to prolonged cruel or unjust treatment, especially mental suffering; to weigh heavily upon; to crush or burden. Example: "The dictator oppressed his people with harsh laws."
- **Noun**: (n) The act or state of oppressing; the feeling of being heavily burdened, mentally or physically. Example: "Their struggle against oppression became a symbol of freedom."

## Phrases
- "Oppress the masses": To dominate and mistreat a large group of people.
- "Lift the yoke of oppression": To free someone from a burdensome or controlling situation.
- "Systemic oppression": When oppressive policies, customs, or beliefs are ingrained within a society or institution.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "Throughout history, many groups have been oppressed due to their race, religion, or social status."
- **Noun**: "The fight against oppression has been a central theme in her political activism."
