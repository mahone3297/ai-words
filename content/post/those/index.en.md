## Pronunciation
/ðəʊz/

## Definitions
### Noun
**Those** is a demonstrative pronoun used to refer to people or things previously mentioned or easily identified. It can also be plural and possessive.

### Adjective
**Those** can function as an adjective when modifying plural nouns, referring to a specific group or batch of them.

### Determiner
In certain contexts, 'those' can be used as a determiner, specifying a particular set or kind of objects.

## Phrases
- "Those over there" points to a group of people or objects in a specified direction.
- "Those who agree" refers to the individuals supporting a particular idea or proposal.
- "Those are my books" identifies a specific set of belongings.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "I saw those children playing in the park."
- **Adjective**: "The books on those shelves are outdated."
- **Determiner**: "Those are the best apples from the tree."

## Synonyms
- Those folks
- Such
- That lot
- Those individuals
- The ones

## Note
When used as a plural form of 'that' (e.g., "That's those books"), it can sound informal or colloquial in some contexts. In more formal writing, 'those' is typically preferred to avoid ambiguity.
