## Pronunciation
/pərˈsuːd/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To convince or influence someone to do something, typically by giving reasons or using arguments. 
- **Noun**: The act of persuading or the ability to persuade.

## Phrases
- "Persuade someone to do something" means to convince them to take a particular action.
- "Failed to persuade" indicates that someone was unable to convince another person.
- "Persuasive argument" refers to a convincing reason or explanation.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "She persuaded her team to accept the new project proposal."
- **Noun**: "His confident presentation had a persuasive effect on the audience."
- **Phrasal verb**: "The salesperson tried hard to persuade the customer to buy the product."

## Usage
- Persuasion can be used in both formal and informal contexts, often involving a skilled communication strategy.
- It's common to use persuasive language when trying to influence someone's opinion or decision-making process.
