## Pronunciation
/ˌɪmplɪˈkeɪʃən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The conclusion that can be drawn from something although it is not explicitly stated; a logical consequence. For example: "The implication of his statement was clear - he would have to resign."
  - **Noun**: In law, a suggestion or indication of something, especially something unfavorable, without direct assertion. For instance: "There were implications of guilt in his behavior."
- **Verb**: (to imply) To suggest or indicate something without stating it directly. Example: "Her silence implied her disagreement with the proposal."

## Phrases
- "Implication of something": Refers to the inferred meaning or consequence of an action, event, or statement.
- "Have implications for": Means that something will affect or have consequences on something else. Example: "This new discovery has huge implications for our understanding of the universe."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The company's decision to downsize has serious implications for job security."
- **Verb**: "His refusal to comment implies that he might be involved in the scandal."
