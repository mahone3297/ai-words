## Pronunciation
/lɒɡ/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A record of events, typically one that is kept as a written or electronic document. For instance: "The captain maintained a detailed log of their journey across the ocean." 
- **Noun (n)**: A section of a tree trunk cut lengthwise, often used as fuel or building material. Example: "They chopped wood to gather logs for the fireplace."
- **Verb (v)**: To write down information or events in a systematic manner, usually in a logbook. Example: "The pilot logged the flight data before landing."
- **Verb (v)**: To cut down trees and remove their trunks. Example: "Logging operations have been halted due to environmental concerns."
- **Verb (v)**: In computing, to make a record of actions or data, often for troubleshooting purposes. Example: "The software automatically logs your activity on the system."

## Phrases
- "Log in/log out": To enter or exit a computer system or online account after providing credentials.
- "To log onto something": To access a computer network or website by signing in.
- "Log cabin": A dwelling constructed from logs, especially associated with rustic living or pioneering history.
- "Logarithm": In mathematics, the power to which a number must be raised to produce another given number.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (logbook)**: "The ship's log contained entries about sea conditions and sightings of marine life."
- **Noun (tree trunk)**: "A fallen log blocked the hiking trail."
- **Verb (writing records)**: "Make sure you log your hours worked accurately each day."
- **Verb (cutting trees)**: "The company has been logging old-growth forests for decades."
- **Verb (computing)**: "Users need to log in with their username and password to access their accounts."
