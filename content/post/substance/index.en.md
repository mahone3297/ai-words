## Pronunciation
/subˈstəns/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A material with a defined chemical composition, having properties that distinguish it from other substances, often with a specific use or application.
   - "The experiment tested the properties of various chemical substances."
2. The essential nature or character of something; essence.
   - "The essence of art lies in its substance."
3. The main part or content of a literary work, film, or discussion.
   - "The book's substance comes from the author's personal experiences."

### Verb
1. To be the essential part or essence of something.
   - "Water is the substance of life."
2. To fill or supply with a substantial amount or quality.
   - "This project will substance the company's growth plans."

### Adjective
1. Fundamental; essential.
   - "The substance of their argument was well-supported."
2. Concrete or substantial; not vague or empty.
   - "Her proposal was full of substance."

## Phrases

- "Get to the substance" means to get to the heart of the matter.
- "A substantial amount" indicates a significant quantity.
