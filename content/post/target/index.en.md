## Pronunciation
/tɑːrɡɪt/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A person or thing that is the intended recipient or focus of an action, effort, or interest: "The marketing campaign's main target audience is young adults."
2. The object or goal of a weapon or missile: "The missile struck its target with precision."
3. (Business) A specific product, service, or market segment that a company aims to serve: "Our company has multiple targets for expanding our customer base."

### Verb
1. To aim or direct something, such as an attack or attention, at a particular person or thing: "The company targeted their new product towards eco-conscious consumers."
2. To set a goal or objective for oneself or others: "We need to target higher sales figures this quarter."

## Phrases
- "Target audience" refers to the specific group a product, service, or message is designed for.
- "Hit the target" means to achieve the intended goal or outcome.
- "Set a target" implies establishing a goal or benchmark.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The architect designed the building to be visually appealing to its target demographic."
- **Verb**: "The athlete trained rigorously to target a world record."
- **Verb**: "The marketing team's primary target is to increase website traffic by 50%."
