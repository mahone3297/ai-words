## Word: Mist

## Pronunciation
/mɪst/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A visible suspension of fine water droplets in the air near or close to the ground, typically resulting from cool air passing over a warmer surface such as a lake or after rain. For example: "The morning mist obscured the view across the valley."
  
  - **Verb**: (transitive) To cover or envelop with or as if with mist. For example: "Mist draped over the hills early in the morning."

## Phrases
- "In the mist": Describes something happening or being within an area covered by mist.
- "Through the mist": Refers to navigating or seeing something unclearly due to the presence of mist.
- "A mist of confusion": A metaphorical use, suggesting a state of confusion or vagueness akin to a physical mist.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The dense mist made driving conditions hazardous."
- **Verb**: "Fog misted the windshield, making it difficult to see the road ahead."

## Synonyms
- Fog
- Vapor
- Sprinkle
- Haze
- Murk
- Drizzle (when used in the context of 'to mist' meaning lightly wetting something)
- Aerosol (in scientific contexts referring to a fine water spray)
