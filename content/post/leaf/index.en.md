## Pronunciation
/lēf/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: One of the usually green, flat, and broad organs that grow from the stem of a plant, often being specialized for photosynthesis and the storage of food. For example: "The tree shed its leaves in autumn."
  - Also used metaphorically, such as "the book has 500 pages, each page a new leaf to explore."
- **Verb (v)**: To move through or over something by turning pages or layers. For example: "He leafed through the magazine quickly."
  - Alternatively, it can refer to plants shedding or growing leaves: "The tree began to leaf out in the spring."

## Phrases
- "Turn over a new leaf": To make a fresh start or change one's behavior significantly for the better.
- "Take a leaf out of someone's book": To imitate someone because they have good qualities or methods.
- "Leaf through something": To look through a book, magazine, or document quickly but not thoroughly.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She collected a handful of colorful autumn leaves."
- **Verb**: "He leafed through the old photo album, reminiscing about his childhood."
