## Word: Majority

## Pronunciation
/ˌmərəˈdʒɔri/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The greater number or part; more than half of a total. It typically refers to people within a group who hold a particular view or vote in favor of something. For example: "The majority of the population supports this reform."
  - _In legislative contexts:_ The number by which the votes in favor exceed those against a proposition.
  - _In statistics or demographics:_ The portion of a group that forms more than 50% of its total members.
- **Adjective**: Relating to or constituting the greater number; prevalent or dominant. Example: "The majority opinion on the issue was clear from the survey results."

## Phrases
- "The majority of": This phrase is used when referring to most of something or most people in a group. Example: "The majority of students prefer online learning."
- "A majority vote": When more than half of the voters support a decision or proposal. Example: "The bill passed with a clear majority vote."
- "In the majority": Being part of the larger or prevailing group. Example: "After the election, his party found itself in the majority."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The majority of the committee members agreed on the new policy."
- **Adjective**: "Her views were in the minority, as the majority opinion favored a different approach."
