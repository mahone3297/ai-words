## Pronunciation
/kənˈskɒns/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A noun referring to an individual's moral sense or an inner voice that guides them regarding right and wrong. For example: "Her conscience was bothering her over the decision."
- **Adjective**: Reflecting one's sense of morality or ethics. For example: "A conscience-stricken person may feel guilty about their actions."

## Phrases
- "Act on one's conscience" means to do what one believes is morally right.
- "To clear one's conscience" signifies resolving a feeling of guilt or remorse.
- "Conscienceless" describes someone without a sense of guilt or moral responsibility.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "He couldn't sleep knowing he had neglected his conscience."
- **Adjective**: "Her conscience-admonished choice was to donate the money to charity."
- **Adverb**: "She spoke with a heavy conscience, aware of the harm she had caused."

## Synonyms
- Moral compass
- Inner voice
- Sense of right and wrong
- Gut feeling
- Conscience-pricking

## Antonyms

- Immoral
- Dishonest
- Unethical
- Guilt-free (opposite of conscience-stricken)
