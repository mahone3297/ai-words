## Pronunciation
/tɪn/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Tin** (noun): A thin sheet or rolled metal, usually aluminum, used for making containers, cans, or other packaging.
- **Tin can** (informal): A container made of tin.
- **Tin whistle** (music): A small, simple wind instrument made from a tin tube.

### Verb
- **To tin** (archaic or technical): To cover with tin, as in roofing or coating.

### Adjective
- **Tinny** (adjective): Having a thin, harsh, or metallic sound; also referring to something made of cheap or low-quality tin.

## Phrases
- "Tin foil" for the shiny, reflective metal sheet.
- "Tin-plated" to describe a surface covered with a thin layer of tin.
- "Tin whistle tune" for a song played on a tin whistle.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The old house had a roof made of galvanized tin."
- **Verb**: "The artist carefully tinned the edges of the canvas to prevent rust."
- **Adjective**: "The old radio sounded tinny due to the worn-out speaker."

## Synonyms
- Aluminum (for the material)
- Tinplate (for the process of covering)
- Flatware (informal, for tableware made of tin)

## Antonyms
- Solid (for the material, contrasting with its thin nature)
- Warm (for the adjective sense, contrasting with tinny sound)
