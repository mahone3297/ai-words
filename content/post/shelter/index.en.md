## Pronunciation
/sheɪltər/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **1** A structure providing protection from weather, danger, or other adverse conditions, typically for people or animals: "They sought shelter from the rain in a nearby cave."
- **2** A place or situation that offers refuge or support: "The homeless found shelter at the local charity."
- **3** (pl. shelters) A refuge or sanctuary for a particular group or purpose: "The animal shelter took care of abandoned pets."

### Verb
- To seek or provide protection or refuge in a shelter: "The refugees were forced to shelter in a makeshift camp."
- To offer or give shelter to someone: "The family opened their home to the homeless stranger."

### Adjective
- Sheltered: "A protected environment; a sheltered upbringing."
- Sheltering: "Offering protection; sheltering from the wind."

## Phrases
- "Seek shelter" implies finding a place to protect oneself from something.
- "Provide shelter for" means to make available a place of refuge.
- "Undercover shelter" refers to a hidden or concealed location.
- "Temporary shelter" denotes a short-term place to stay.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The hikers found shelter in a hollow tree during the storm."
- **Verb**: "The organization aims to shelter victims of domestic violence."
- **Adjective**: "The children felt sheltered and secure at their grandparents' house."
- **Adjective (as an adjective)**: "The sheltering trees provided a cool respite on a hot day."
