## Pronunciation
/pəˈliːs/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Police**: A force responsible for maintaining law and order, typically in a town or city. Example: "The police were called to the scene of the accident."
2. **Police officer**: A member of a police force. Example: "The officer politely asked for our IDs."
3. **Traffic police**: Officials who enforce traffic rules. Example: "The traffic police directed the flow of vehicles."

### Verb
1. To enforce the law or maintain public safety. Example: "Police officers are patrolling the streets."
2. To inquire about someone's well-being or presence. Example: "Please police the area for any suspicious activity."

## Phrases
- "To be on police duty" means to perform one's job as a police officer.
- "Keep an eye on the police car" refers to observing a patrol vehicle.
- "The police have issued a warning" communicates a formal alert.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The police cordoned off the area after the explosion."
- **Verb**: "The detective decided to police the suspect's background."
- **Interjection**: "Police, over here! There's been a disturbance."
