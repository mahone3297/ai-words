## Pronunciation
/səʊ/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: To an extent; to such a degree. For example: "The weather was so hot that we decided to go swimming."
- **Adjective**: Used to emphasize a quality or fact. For example: "She is so talented, it's unbelievable."
- **Interjection**: Expressing agreement, emphasis, or mild surprise. For example: "So, you're going on vacation this week?"

## Phrases
- "So what?" implies indifference or disbelief.
- "That's so cool!" expresses admiration.
- "It's not so bad once you get used to it." suggests initial resistance but eventual acceptance.

## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "I was so tired after the long flight, I fell asleep immediately."
- **Adjective**: "The cake was so delicious, I had to have seconds."
- **Interjection**: "So, did you hear about the new restaurant downtown?"

## Synonyms
- Thus
- Consequently
- Hence
- Accordingly
- Rather
- Very (to emphasize degree)

## Antonyms
- Not so (contrasts with "so")
- Unusually (when emphasizing the opposite)
