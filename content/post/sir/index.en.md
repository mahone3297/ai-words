## Pronunciation
/sɜː/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A formal or polite title used to address someone with respect, typically a man. For example: "He addressed the crowd as 'Ladies and gentlemen, sir.'"
- **Adjective**: Designating or used to address a male person with respect. For example: "The butler bowed to his master, addressing him as 'Sir.'"
- **Interjection**: An expression of respect or politeness, often used to start a conversation. For example: "May I have your attention, please, sir?"

## Phrases
- "Address someone as sir" means to formally refer to or speak to a man with respect.
- "Sir, yes, sir" is a military or formal response to a command.
- "After you, sir" is an expression of courtesy, indicating that someone should go first.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The officer saluted the commander, calling him 'sir.'"
- **Adjective**: "The teacher addressed the class, asking the sir for his thoughts."
- **Interjection**: "Excuse me, sir, do you know where the restroom is?"

## Synonyms
- Gentleman
- Sirrah (informal, sometimes disrespectful)
- Ma'am (formal, used for women)
- Sirs (plural, addressing multiple men)
- Mister (less formal than sir)
