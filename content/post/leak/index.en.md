## Pronunciation
/lik/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A hole, crack, or other imperfection through which liquid, gas, or light can pass without permission or control. _Example_: "There's a leak in the roof that needs fixing."
- **Verb**: (intransitive) To pass liquids, gases, or information unintentionally or without authorization from a container or source. _Example_: "The pipe started leaking water all over the floor."
- **Verb**: (transitive) To disclose confidential or secret information to others without authorization. _Example_: "Someone in the company has been leaking sensitive data to the press."

## Phrases
- "Plug the leak": To stop or repair something that is leaking.
- "Leak information": To disclose or reveal secret or private details.
- "A leaky bucket": An idiom describing something that loses its contents before it can be fully utilized.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "They discovered a serious oil leak near the engine."
- **Verb (intransitive)**: "The faucet is old and tends to leak when left on overnight."
- **Verb (transitive)**: "The whistleblower leaked documents proving corruption within the organization."

## Synonyms
- Noun: Seepage, spill, emission, discharge
- Verb: Spill, seep, dribble, escape, disclose, reveal
- Adjective (describing something that leaks): Porous, permeable, unsealed, not watertight, insecure
