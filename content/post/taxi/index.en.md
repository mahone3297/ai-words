## Pronunciation
/tæksi/

## Definitions
### Noun
A taxi is a type of vehicle designed for hire, typically with a driver, that operates on public roads to transport passengers from one place to another, usually for a fee.

### Verb
To taxi (as a noun): To ride or drive a taxi. To maneuver a vehicle, especially an aircraft, in preparation for takeoff or landing, or to move along a road or area by short, controlled movements.

To taxi (as a verb): To arrange for or hail a taxi for transportation.

### Adjective
Taxi-like: Having the characteristics or appearance of a taxi.

## Phrases
- "Get into a taxi" means to enter a taxi for a ride.
- "Taxi service" refers to the business of providing transportation by taxi.
- "Taxi stand" is a designated area where taxis wait for passengers.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "I hailed a taxi and gave the driver my destination."
- **Verb**: "The pilot taxied the plane to the runway for takeoff."
- **Adjective**: "The old taxi was painted in a distinctive yellow color, typical of many cities."

## Synonyms
- Cab
- Carriage
- Vehicle (when referring to a taxi specifically)
- Ride-hailing (e.g., Uber, Lyft)
- Shuttle (in specific contexts)
