## Pronunciation
/vest/

## Definitions
### Noun
A vest is a piece of clothing that covers the chest and upper part of the torso, typically with sleeves but without a collar or buttons. It is often worn as an undergarment, a part of a uniform, or as a layering piece.

### Verb (transitive)
To vest someone with a particular role, authority, or responsibility, usually in an organizational context. For example: "The board vested him with the power to make important decisions."

### Verb (intransitive)
To vest oneself, to put on or assume a vest as part of one's attire.

## Phrases
- "Wear a vest" means to physically put on a vest.
- "Vest with a specific function" implies assigning a purpose or role to something.
- "vesting rights" refers to the transfer of ownership or privileges.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "He wore a tailored vest over his shirt for the formal event."
- **Verb (transitive)**: "The company vestes new employees with benefits upon joining."
- **Verb (intransitive)**: "She vests herself in her favorite old sports jacket before going out."
