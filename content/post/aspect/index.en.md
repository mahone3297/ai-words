## Pronunciation
/ˈæspekt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Aspect**: In grammar, it refers to a particular way in which a verb is inflected to show time, manner, direction, or other grammatical features. For example: "The progressive aspect shows continuous action."
- **Phenomenon**: A general feature or characteristic of something, often referring to a quality that can be observed or experienced. For example: "Climate change is a global aspect of our modern world."

### Verb
- **Aspectualize**: To express the aspect (time or manner) of a verb in a sentence. For example: "The teacher aspectualized the verb 'to learn' to show the progressive aspect."
- **Considerate**: To show thoughtfulness or attentiveness to another's needs or feelings, often in terms of how actions are performed. For example: "A considerate person pays attention to the aspects that matter to others."

### Adjective
- **Aspects**: Referring to various features or qualities of something. For example: "The course covers different aspects of history."
- **Temporal**: Relating to time or duration. For example: "The project has several temporal aspects to manage."

## Phrases
- "Aspectual markers" are linguistic elements that indicate verb aspects.
- "Cultural aspects" refers to the features of a society or tradition.
- "Focus on the positive aspects" means to concentrate on the good points.
