## Word: Moon

## Pronunciation
/moon/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: The natural satellite of Earth, orbiting around it and appearing as a bright, shining object in the night sky. It's the fifth largest moon in the solar system. Example: "The full moon illuminated the dark forest."
- **Noun (n)**: A celestial body that orbits around another planet, often used in the context of other planets having moons. Example: "Jupiter has many moons."
- **Noun (n)**: A month, especially a lunar month based on the phases of the moon. Example: "The festival takes place once a moon."
- **Verb (v)**: To gaze at or admire the moon. Example: "They spent the evening mooning over the ocean."
- **Verb (v)**: (informal) To expose one's buttocks publicly as a prank or gesture. Example: "The mischievous boys were caught mooning passersby from the school roof."
  
## Phrases
- "Shoot for the moon": Aim ambitiously high.
- "Once in a blue moon": Rarely or infrequently.
- "Under the moonlight": In the light of the moon, often suggesting romance or mystery.
- "Moon landing": The act of a spacecraft reaching the surface of the moon.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (moon as Earth's satellite)**: "The astronauts planted the flag of their country on the moon."
- **Noun (moon as a celestial body)**: "Titan is the largest moon of Saturn."
- **Verb (admiring the moon)**: "We sat outside under the stars, mooning over the beauty of the night sky."
- **Verb (informal sense)**: "His juvenile behavior included mooning people from the bus window."
