## Pronunciation
/pʌblɪˈkeɪʃ(ə)n/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The act or process of making something available to the public, especially a printed or digital work, such as a book, magazine, or article.
   - Definition: A piece of writing or art that is published and distributed for public consumption.
   - Example: "The newspaper publishes daily news and opinions."
2. A specific issue or installment of a periodical, like a magazine or journal.
   - Example: "Issue 3 of the monthly magazine had several interesting publications."
3. The product of publication, such as a book or article.
   - Example: "The publication received critical acclaim for its investigative reporting."

### Verb
1. To make something available for public distribution or viewing.
   - Example: "The author will soon publish her new novel."
2. To announce or declare the availability of (a product or information).
   - Example: "The company announced the publication of their quarterly financial report."

## Phrases
- "Publish a book" means to release a written work to the public.
- "Publication date" refers to when a work becomes available for purchase or viewing.
- "Self-publishing" is the act of producing and distributing a work without a traditional publisher.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The author's latest publication was a groundbreaking research paper on climate change."
- **Verb**: "The publishing house has decided to cancel the planned publication of the novel due to poor sales projections."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "After months of hard work, the manuscript finally saw publication."
