## Pronunciation
/ˈmɪnɪstrɪ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: (n) The department or branch of government that deals with a specific area of public administration or affairs, such as defense, education, or health. Example: "The Ministry of Health is responsible for implementing new healthcare policies."
- **Noun**: (n) The period during which a person holds office as a minister in charge of such a department. Example: "His long ministry saw significant reforms in the educational sector."
- **Noun**: (n) In religious contexts, it refers to the work or service of a religious minister, priest, or other clergy member. Example: "He devoted his life to ministry within the local church community."

## Phrases
- "In the ministry": Refers to being involved or working in a governmental or religious position of authority or service.
- "To take up ministry": To begin serving in a religious or governmental capacity.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (government)**: "The newly appointed minister will head the Ministry of Foreign Affairs."
- **Noun (religious)**: "After completing his theological studies, he entered the ministry and became a pastor."
- **Noun (period of service)**: "During her distinguished ministry, the Education Secretary managed to implement several key reforms."

## Synonyms
- Department (in the context of government)
- Service (in the context of religious duties)
- Clergy (referring to the collective body of religious ministers)
- Administration (for the act of managing government affairs)
- Priesthood (specifically for religious leadership roles)
