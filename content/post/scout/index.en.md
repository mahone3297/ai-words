## Pronunciation
/skaʊt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A person who is sent or appointed to explore an area, gather information, or find potential new members, especially for a sports team or organization.
- A young person who participates in scouting activities, such as the Boy Scouts or Girl Scouts.

### Verb
- To search or explore an area to discover details or potential opportunities.
- To look for or investigate something or someone, often secretly.

## Phrases
- "Scout out a location" means to examine or survey an area beforehand.
- "Be a scout for a company" refers to finding potential clients or talent for a business.
- "Scouting report" is a summary of information gathered by a scout about a player or prospect.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The scout spent weeks in the forest, gathering data on wildlife."
- **Verb**: "The real estate agent scouted several properties before settling on the perfect one for her client."
- **Scouting mission** might be "The team embarked on a scouting mission to find new business opportunities."

## Synonyms
- Scout around
- Reconnoitre
- Probe
- Explore
- Survey

## Antonyms
- Hide (opposite of searching)
- Uncover (opposite of discovering)
