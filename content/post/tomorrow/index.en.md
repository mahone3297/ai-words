## Pronunciation
/təˈmɔrəʊ/

## Definitions
### Noun
- Tomorrow refers to the day that immediately follows the current day in the time sequence, typically used to indicate a future event or appointment.

### Verb
- To use "tomorrow" as a verb can imply planning or scheduling something for the next day, e.g., "I'm meeting him tomorrow morning."

### Adjective
- It can also be used as an adjective to describe the quality of being related to or occurring the next day, e.g., "The weather forecast for tomorrow promises sunshine."

### Adverb
- Less commonly, it can function as an adverb to express the time when something will happen, e.g., "Let's discuss it tomorrow."

## Phrases
- "Meet you tomorrow" = an agreement to meet at a specific time on the following day.
- "What are your plans for tomorrow?" = asking about someone's schedule for the next day.
- "See you tomorrow" = a farewell with the intention to meet again the next day.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "I'll finish my work today so I can relax tomorrow."
- **Verb**: "We're having a barbecue at home tomorrow."
- **Adjective**: "I'm looking forward to a sunny tomorrow after this week's rain."
