## Pronunciation
/ˌenʤɪˈniər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A person who designs, builds, maintains, or improves complex systems, machinery, structures, or public works, typically having specialized knowledge in areas such as civil, mechanical, electrical, chemical, or software engineering. Example: "She's a software engineer at a tech company."
- **Verb**: (transitive) To design, build, or manage something using scientific and technical knowledge, especially in the fields of construction, manufacturing, or technology. Example: "They engineered a new bridge that would withstand extreme weather conditions."

### Noun Examples
- "The lead engineer on the project has over 20 years of experience."
- "Aerospace engineers design spacecraft and satellites."

### Verb Examples
- "They engineered a solution to reduce pollution from factory emissions."
- "He engineered the product to be more energy-efficient."

## Phrases
- "To engineer a solution": To design and implement a method to solve a problem.
- "Reverse engineer": To take apart and analyze an object or system to understand how it works or to replicate it without prior knowledge of its internal workings.
