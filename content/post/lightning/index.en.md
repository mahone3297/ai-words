## Definitions
- **Noun**: An intense flash of light caused by a discharge of atmospheric electricity, often accompanied by thunder, typically during a rainstorm. For example: "The lightning illuminated the sky during the storm."
- **Noun (figurative)**: A sudden burst or flash of something, often brilliance, insight, or energy. For example: "Her quick thinking was like a bolt of lightning in solving the problem."

## Phrases
- "Lightning bolt": A visible streak of electricity that occurs during a thunderstorm.
- "Lightning strike": The event of lightning hitting the ground or an object.
- "Lightning speed": Extremely fast pace; used metaphorically to describe rapidity or swiftness. For example: "She finished her work with lightning speed."
- "A bolt from the blue": An unexpected event or piece of news, often with negative consequences, akin to a sudden lightning strike out of a clear sky.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (literal)**: "The house was struck by lightning during the storm last night."
- **Noun (figurative)**: "His wit was like lightning, striking with unexpected brilliance."
