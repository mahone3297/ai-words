## Pronunciation
/tɪp/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A small amount of money given as a reward or as an additional payment for good service, especially in a restaurant.
   - Example: "The waiter left a tip on the table after the meal."
2. A hint or piece of advice.
   - Example: "She gave me a tip on how to improve my public speaking."

### Verb
1. To give something, usually money, as a gratuity to a service provider.
   - Example: "Please don't forget to tip the taxi driver."
2. To provide a hint or suggestion.
   - Example: "He tipped me off about the upcoming meeting."

### Adjective
1. Characteristic of or giving a slight touch or movement.
   - Example: "The dancer's tips were light and graceful."

## Phrases
- "Leave a tip" means to pay extra money for a service.
- "A tip of the hat" is an expression of gratitude or respect.
- "Tip-off" refers to a hint or warning.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "I always leave a 20% tip for exceptional service."
- **Verb**: "He thanked the bartender for tipping him off about the secret menu item."
- **Adjective**: "She did a quick tip of her fingers to get my attention."

## Synonyms
- Gratuity
- Taster
- Token
- Small change
- Reward (in the context of service)
