## Pronunciation
/pəˈsɪv/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Passive voice** (grammar): A grammatical construction in which the subject of the sentence is acted upon by the verb, often involving the use of a form like "is/was + past participle." For example: "The book was read by Sarah."
2. **Passive attitude**: Submission or acceptance without active resistance or initiative. For example: "He had a passive approach to conflict resolution."

### Verb
1. To act or be acted upon in a passive manner. For example: "The machine was repaired without our intervention."
2. To undergo a process or treatment passively. For example: "The patients were given their medications in a passive manner."

## Phrases
- "In a passive voice" describes a sentence structure where the agent (performer) is not the focus.
- "Passive resistance" refers to nonviolent opposition to authority.
- "Be passive about it" means to accept something without taking action.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The new policy was introduced in a passive voice to avoid offending anyone."
- **Verb**: "The children were being taught to be more proactive rather than passive in their learning."
- **Active vs. Passive**: "He painted the house (active) vs. The house was painted (passive)."
