## Pronunciation
/prɪˈviːəs/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Occurring or coming before in time, order, or sequence; preceding. For example: "The previous year's sales figures."
- **Adverb**: In a preceding manner; previously. For example: "She had already discussed the topic with her colleague previously."
- **Noun**: A former time or event; something that happened earlier. For example: "I remember the previous meeting."

## Phrases
- "Previous experience" refers to past knowledge or skills in a particular area.
- "Previous to that" indicates something that happened before another event.
- "Previous work" denotes work done before the current project or task.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The previous applicant had more relevant qualifications."
- **Adverb**: "He mentioned the previous discussion during the meeting."
- **Noun**: "Can you review the previous decisions made by the committee?"

## Synonyms
- Former
- Past
- Earlier
- Prior
- Antecedent
- Preceding
- Formerly

## Usage Notes
- "Previous" can also be used as a comparative form of the adjective "more previous" or superlative "most previous," meaning "earlier" or "more preceding."
- When referring to people, "previous" may imply a specific individual rather than a general sense of time. For example, "My previous boss was very strict."
