## Pronunciation
/pɪktʃər/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Picture**: A representation of something, typically visual, created using paint, photography, digital media, or other means, often to depict a scene, person, or object. For example: "The painting on the wall is a beautiful picture of a sunset."
- **Image**: A mental representation of something, often based on memory or imagination. For example: "The image of her childhood home stayed with him forever."
- **Snapshot**: A quick, informal photograph taken without much planning. For example: "I took a snapshot of my friends during our vacation."

### Verb
- **To picture**: To imagine or visualize something in one's mind. For example: "Can you picture what the city will look like after the renovations?"
- **To illustrate**: To show or explain something by providing a visual aid, such as a drawing or photograph. For example: "The diagram illustrates the complex process clearly."

### Adjective
- **Pictorial**: Having to do with pictures or images, often used to describe content or style. For example: "The magazine had a pictorial spread on famous landmarks."

## Phrases
- "Paint a picture" means to vividly describe or create a mental image.
- "A picture is worth a thousand words" is a popular saying suggesting that a single image can convey a lot of information.
- "Frame a picture" means to arrange or present something attractively.
