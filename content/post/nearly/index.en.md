## Pronunciation
/ˈnɪərli/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: To a very great extent or almost completely; close to but not exactly. It indicates that something falls just short of being complete or exact.

Example sentence (adverb): 
- _The answer was nearly correct._
- _She finished her work nearly on time._

## Phrases
- "Nearly done": Close to finishing something.
- "Nearly there": Close to arriving at a destination.
- "Nearly perfect": Almost without any flaws or mistakes.
  
## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: 
  - _It's nearly midnight, we should get going._
  - _I was nearly asleep when the phone rang._

## Synonyms
- Almost
- Virtually
- Essentially
- Roughly
- Approximately
- About
- Just about
- Nearly enough
- All but

## Usage in Idiomatic Expressions
- "Nearly enough" can be used to emphasize that something is sufficient but not quite ideal: "We have nearly enough supplies to last the week."
- "Missed it by a hair/nearly missed it" implies coming close to achieving or reaching something but ultimately falling short: "He nearly missed the bus this morning."
