## Pronunciation
/ləʊf/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A large, usually oblong mass of bread that is baked and often has a firm texture. For example: "She bought a freshly baked loaf of bread from the bakery."
- **Verb (v)**: To spend time idly or lazily; to lounge around. For example: "After lunch, he decided to loaf around the house and read a book."
- **Noun (informal, slang)**: A person who avoids work or effort. For example: "Don't be such a loaf all day; help with the chores!"

## Phrases
- "A loaf of bread": This is a common phrase used when referring to a single unit of bread.
- "Loaf around": To spend time aimlessly or without purpose, often in a relaxed manner.
  
## Example Sentences
- **Noun (bread)**: "I sliced the warm, crusty loaf of sourdough to make sandwiches."
- **Verb**: "On Sundays, he likes to loaf on the couch watching old movies."
- **Slang Noun**: "He's just a lazy loaf; he never helps with anything."

## Synonyms
- Bread (for the noun meaning a large mass of baked dough)
- Idle (for the verb meaning to spend time lazily)
- Lounge (for the verb meaning to relax or rest indolently)
