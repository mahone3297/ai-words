## Pronunciation
/preˈfeɪs/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A written introduction or explanation placed before a book, especially one by the author or another expert on the subject, providing context or background information.
2. [figurative] An introductory part or chapter; a foreword.

### Verb
1. To write or insert a preface into a book or document.
2. To give a preface (as in a speech or greeting).

## Phrases
- "Preface a book" means to write an introduction for a book.
- "Include a preface" signifies adding a forward or an introductory note.
- "Read the preface" refers to reading the introductory part of a text.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The author wrote a lengthy preface discussing the inspiration behind their novel."
- **Verb**: "The editor decided to preface each chapter with a brief summary."
- **Prepositional phrase**: "After the main content, he found a fascinating preface by a historian."

## Synonyms
- Foreword
- Prologue
- Introduction
- Epistle (informal)
- Acknowledgments (when referring to the section after a preface)
- Prefatory remarks

## Antonyms
- Epilogue (if used to mean the closing part instead of the introduction)
