## Pronunciation
/rɪˈbɛlɪən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: **v.** (rebel) / **n.** An act of open and often violent resistance against an authority or established system. / **n.** A group of people who rebel against such authority. / **n.** Disobedience or defiance, especially as a form of protest.
- **Verb**: To rise up in opposition or defiance, usually against an authority figure or established order.

## Phrases
- "A rebellion against the government"
- "Join the rebellion"
- "The silent rebellion within"

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The rebellion was fueled by widespread discontent among the people."
- **Verb**: "Many young people rebelled against their parents' strict rules."
- **As a noun**: "The rebels were able to capture strategic locations during the civil war."

## Synonyms
- Insurrection
- Uprising
- Revolt
- Resistance
- Mutiny
- Disruption
- Rebellion (as a form of protest)

## Antonyms
- Compliance
- Submission
- Obedience
- Loyalty
- Acceptance

## Etymology
Rebellion comes from the Latin "rebella," meaning "to turn back, resist," which in turn is related to the verb "rebellare," meaning "to rebel."
