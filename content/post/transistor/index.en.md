## Pronunciation
/transˈzɪstər/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A small electronic component that amplifies or switches electronic signals and is widely used in electronic devices, especially in computers and televisions.
   - Definition: A device that can amplify or switch electrical signals with minimal power consumption.
   - Example: "The heart of a computer's processor contains millions of transistors."

2. [Obsolete] A person who serves as an agent or intermediary between two parties.
   - Obsolete Definition: A go-between or intermediary.
   - Example (obsolete): "He acted as a transistor between the buyer and seller."

### Verb
- To control or regulate the flow of electricity through a transistor.
   - Example: "The circuit was transistorized to increase efficiency."

- To transfer or move from one place or medium to another, often metaphorically.
   - Example: "New technologies transistorized information sharing."

## Phrases
- "Transistor technology" refers to the advancements in this field.
- "A transistor-based amplifier" describes a device using transistors for amplification.
- "The transistor revolutionized electronics" highlights its significant impact on the industry.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The invention of the transistor marked a major milestone in the history of electronics."
- **Verb**: "The new smartphone uses a highly efficient transistor design for better battery life."
- **Obsolete Verb**: "He served as a transistor between the rival companies, negotiating their terms."

## Synonyms
- Solid-state relay
- Field-effect transistor (FET)
- Bipolar junction transistor (BJT)
- Transconductance amplifier (TCA)
- Switching transistor (for controlling current)
