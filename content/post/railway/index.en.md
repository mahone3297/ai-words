## Pronunciation
/rɪˈleɪlwei/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. A system of fixed tracks with trains running on them, used for public transport or freight transportation. 2. A railway company or network. 3. A train or series of connected cars pulled by a locomotive. 4. The industry related to railways, including construction, operation, and maintenance.

- **Verb**: To travel or commute using a railway service.

## Phrases
- "Take the railway" means to use a train for transportation.
- "The railway station" refers to a place where trains stop for passengers to board or disembark.
- "High-speed railway" is a modern train system designed for fast travel.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The railway connects two major cities, making travel between them much faster."
- **Verb**: "I usually take the railway to work every day."
- **Prepositional phrase**: "The train arrived at the railway station right on time."

## Synonyms

- Train line
- Railroad track

## Antonyms
- Roadway (for ground transportation)
- Airline (for air transportation)
- Cruise (for water transportation)
