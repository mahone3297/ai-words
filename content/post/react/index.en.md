## Pronunciation

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Chemical reaction**: A process in which one or more substances change into new substances, often accompanied by energy release or absorption. Example: "The mixture underwent a chemical reaction when exposed to heat."
- **Physical reaction**: An immediate response to a stimulus, typically emotional or physiological. Example: "Her fear triggered a physical reaction in her muscles."
- **Psychological reaction**: An individual's thought, feeling, or behavior in response to a situation. Example: "People had mixed psychological reactions to the news."

### Verb
- **React to**: To respond or behave in a particular way in response to a particular stimulus or situation. Example: "The teacher asked a question, and the students quickly reacted to it."
- **React with**: To undergo a chemical reaction when combined with another substance. Example: "Iron reacts with oxygen to form rust."
- **React against**: To oppose or resist something. Example: "Many people reacted against the proposed tax hike."

### Adjective
- **Reactive**: Quick to respond or show a response to stimuli. Example: "A reactive immune system can fight off infections effectively."
- **Non-reactive**: Not responding or showing a reaction to something. Example: "This material is non-reactive with most chemicals."
