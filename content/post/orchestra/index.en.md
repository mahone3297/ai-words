## Pronunciation
/ˈɔːrkɪstrə/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A large group of musicians who play various instruments together, usually under the direction of a conductor. For example: "The philharmonic orchestra performed Beethoven's 5th Symphony with great passion."
- **Noun (metaphorical)**: A well-coordinated group of people working together towards a common goal. For instance: "The team operated like a finely tuned orchestra."

### Noun Examples
- "The conductor raised his baton, and the orchestra began to play."
- "The school orchestra rehearsed every day after classes to prepare for their upcoming concert."

## Phrases
- "Sit in the orchestra pit": The area in a theatre where the orchestra plays during a performance, often below the stage level.
- "Lead the orchestra": To guide, manage, or coordinate a group effectively, often metaphorically referring to any organization or project.
