## Pronunciation
/ˌʌpˈtuːd/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Current; not outdated or behind; kept informed. For example: "She always has an up-to-date knowledge of the latest technology."
- **Adverb**: In a current or recent manner; keeping pace with developments. For example: "The company is committed to staying up-to-date with industry trends."

## Phrases
- "Stay up-to-date" means to remain informed or knowledgeable about the latest developments.
- "Keep up with the times" is a similar expression, suggesting being current or modern.
- "Up-to-date software" refers to programs that have the latest updates and patches.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "I made sure to install the up-to-date antivirus software on my computer."
- **Adverb**: "She's always up-to-date on the latest news in the field."
- "He was determined to be financially up-to-date by paying his bills promptly."
