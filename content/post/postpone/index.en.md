## Pronunciation
/pəˈstəʊn/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To delay or put off until a later time. For example: "The meeting was postponed until next week."
- **Noun**: The act of delaying or putting something off. For example: "The decision to postpone the project was a tough one."

## Phrases
- "Postpone an event" means to reschedule an activity for a later date.
- "Postponement" refers to the act of delaying something.
- "Postpone indefinitely" means to not set a new date for the action.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "Due to unforeseen circumstances, the concert has been postponed until next month."
- **Noun**: "His wedding was postponed due to the COVID-19 pandemic."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "She decided to postpone her vacation until after the exams."

## Synonyms
- Suspend
- Reschedule
- Delay
- Postponement
- Put off
- Rearrange

## Antonyms
- Advance
- Accelerate
- Initiate
- Schedule
- Proceed

## Usage
- **Formal**: It's common to use "postpone" in formal contexts, such as business meetings or official announcements.
- **Informal**: In informal conversations, people might use "delay" or "put off" interchangeably.

## Notes
- "Postpone" can also be used with specific deadlines or appointments, while "delay" is often used more generally for any interruption or slowdown in progress.
