## Pronunciation
/prəˈtiːn/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Protein**: A complex organic compound that constitutes a significant part of living organisms and serves as a vital component for various biological processes, including growth, repair, and immune function. It is made up of amino acids and has diverse structures and functions.
- **Amino Acid**: One of the building blocks of proteins, contributing to their unique properties and functionalities.

### Verb
- **Protein synthesis**: The process by which cells produce proteins, involving transcription of genetic information into messenger RNA (mRNA) and translation into a protein chain.

## Phrases
- "Essential amino acids": The nine amino acids that cannot be synthesized by the body and must be obtained through diet.
- "Protein source": A food item rich in proteins, such as meat, dairy, or plant-based alternatives.
- "Structural protein": Proteins that provide strength and support in cells, like collagen in skin or keratin in hair.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The diet should include a variety of proteins to ensure a balanced intake of essential nutrients."
- **Verb**: "The cell's machinery is constantly synthesizing new proteins to maintain its functions."
- **Phrasal**: "Fish are an excellent protein source for vegetarians looking to boost their protein intake."
