## Pronunciation
/unˈhæpɪ/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Expressing or characterized by a lack of happiness or satisfaction; sorrowful, dissatisfied, or gloomy. For example: "She felt very unhappy after receiving the news."
- **Adverb**: In an unhappy manner; unhappily. For example: "He spoke unhappily about his recent loss."

## Phrases
- "Be unhappy with something" means to feel dissatisfaction or disappointment.
- "An unhappy childhood" refers to a difficult or unhappy upbringing.
- "Unhappy ending" describes a conclusion that leaves one feeling sad or unsatisfied.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "She had an unhappy expression on her face, knowing she had failed the exam."
- **Adverb**: "He sighed unhappily when he realized he had forgotten his keys."

## Synonyms
- Unhappy = Sad, discontent, miserable, gloomy, dejected
- Discontented = Unsatisfied, uneasy, grumpy, irritable
- Miserable = Wretched, sorrowful, pitiful, depressed
- Depressed = Low-spirited, disheartened, downcast
- Uncheerful = Gloomy, somber, unsmiling

## Antonyms
- Happy = Joyful, content, pleased, elated
- Cheerful = Lighthearted, optimistic, upbeat
- Satisfied = Content, pleased, fulfilled
- Optimistic = Hopeful, positive, expectant
