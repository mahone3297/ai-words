## Pronunciation
/pəˈrʌchəs/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The act of buying something, especially goods or services. **Example**: "I made a purchase at the store."
2. The thing bought, often used with a definite article. **Example**: "The car was my most significant purchase."

### Verb
1. To buy or acquire something, typically after making an agreement. **Example**: "She purchased a new laptop for her studies."
2. To engage in the process of buying, often implying a decision-making process. **Example**: "I'm going to make a purchase online later."

## Phrases
- "Make a purchase" means to buy something.
- "Complete a purchase" signifies finishing the buying process.
- "Purchase on credit" refers to buying with borrowed money.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After months of saving, she finally made a large purchase for her home."
- **Verb**: "He purchased the tickets for the concert in advance."
- **Verb**: "I'll be back in a few minutes to finalize my purchase."
