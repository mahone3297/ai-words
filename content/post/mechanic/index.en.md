## Word: Mechanic

## Pronunciation
/ˈmɛkənɪk/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A skilled worker who repairs and maintains vehicles, machinery, or equipment. For example: "The car wouldn't start, so I took it to a mechanic."
- **Adjective**: Relating to the practical application of scientific and mathematical principles to design, construction, and operation of machines or structures. Example: "Mechanic engineering involves understanding how machines work."

### Noun (Detailed Definition)
- A person whose occupation is repairing and maintaining motor vehicles, aircraft, or other mechanical equipment.
- In a broader sense, a mechanic can also refer to someone adept at using tools or working with machines in general; they may specialize in areas like electronics, robotics, or HVAC systems.

## Phrases
- "Take it to the mechanic" implies taking a vehicle or equipment to be repaired or serviced.
- "A mechanic's lien" is a legal claim on property by someone who has provided labor, materials, or services but has not been paid.
  
## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The airplane mechanic meticulously checked every part of the engine before giving it clearance for takeoff."
- "She decided to become a mechanic after discovering her passion for fixing motorcycles."

## Synonyms

- Technician
- Repairman
- Engineer (in some contexts)
- Artisan
- Maintenance worker
- Garage hand
