## Pronunciation
/ˈkɪləɡræm/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The base unit of mass in the International System of Units (SI), defined as being equal to the mass of a particular international prototype made of platinum-iridium alloy, approximately equivalent to the mass of one liter of water at its freezing point under standard atmospheric pressure. For example: "The weight of that bag of sugar is 2 kilograms."

## Phrases
- "A kilogram of apples" - Refers to a quantity of apples weighing one kilogram.
- "Lose kilograms" - To reduce body weight by specified amounts in kilograms.
- "The kilogram is the fundamental unit of mass" - Stating the scientific definition and importance of the kilogram in measurement systems.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The newborn baby weighed just over three kilograms at birth."
- "She decided to buy five kilograms of potatoes for the weekend's cooking."
- "After his workout routine, he managed to lose two kilograms in a month."

## Synonyms
- Kg (abbreviation)
- Kilo (informal)
- One thousand grams

Note: Kilogram does not have verb or other part-of-speech usages commonly found in everyday language. It is primarily used as a unit of measurement for mass or weight.
