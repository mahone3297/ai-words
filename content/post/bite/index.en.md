## Pronunciation
/baɪt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A part of the mouth that can be closed around something, used for biting or taking small pieces of food. Example: "The dog took a healthy bite out of the apple."
- **Verb**: To take a quick, sharp nip or strike with the teeth, often causing pain or damage. Example: "She bit into her sandwich hungrily."
- **Verb (figurative)**: To grasp or hold on to something firmly or with determination. Example: "He bit onto the opportunity with all his might."
- **Verb (idiomatic)**: To respond to a challenge or criticism. Example: "Don't bite back; just calmly explain your point."

## Phrases
- "Take a bite out of" means to make a small but significant impact or consume a portion of something.
- "Bite your tongue" is a warning to refrain from saying something impolite or unwise.
- "Bite the bullet" is a colloquial expression to endure a difficult situation with courage.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The chef sliced the steak into small, bite-sized portions."
- **Verb**: "She winced as the dentist's drill bit into her tooth."
- **Verb (figurative)**: "He bit into the project, determined to finish it on time."
- **Verb (idiomatic)**: "When he was criticized, he decided not to bite back but instead offered constructive feedback."
