## Pronunciation
/spɑːrk/

## Definitions
### Noun
A spark is a small burst of energy, light, or heat that ignites or initiates something. It can also refer to an idea, inspiration, or a small flame.

### Verb
To spark (v): To ignite, stimulate, or set off a reaction, often an interest or enthusiasm. It can also mean to cause a spark to occur.

### Adjective
Sparkling or electric: Characterized by energy, liveliness, or brilliance.

### Adverb
Sparks-like: In a manner reminiscent of or suggesting sparks.

## Phrases
- "Spark a conversation" means to initiate a discussion.
- "A spark of creativity" refers to a sudden burst of inspiration.
- "Spark joy" is a phrase used to describe something that brings happiness.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The match sparked a fire in the fireplace."
- **Verb**: "Her passion for music sparked a new interest in her son."
- **Adjective**: "She wore a sparkling dress to the gala."
- **Adverb**: "His eyes sparkled with excitement."
