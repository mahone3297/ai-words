# Jaw

## Pronunciation
/dʒɔː/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The lower part of the face that holds the teeth and is moved to chew food or to speak. For example: "He clenched his jaw in anger."
- **Noun (figurative)**: A person's ability to withstand hardship or difficulty; resilience. For example: "The team showed real jaw during the tough match."
- **Noun (plural form "jaws")**: The opening or mouth of something, such as a tool or machine. For example: "The pliers have strong steel jaws."

## Phrases
- "Drop one's jaw" means to be very surprised or shocked, causing one's mouth to open wide.
- "Set one's jaw" refers to the act of firmly resolving oneself to endure something difficult.
- "Lockjaw" is a medical condition characterized by a spasm of the muscles around the jaw, making it difficult to open the mouth.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (anatomy)**: "The surgeon reset the patient's broken jaw after the accident."
- **Noun (figurative)**: "She had the kind of jaw that suggested she could handle any challenge thrown her way."
- **Noun (plural form)**: "The alligator's powerful jaws snapped shut with a loud clap."
