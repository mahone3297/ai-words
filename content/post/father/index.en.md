## Pronunciation

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A man who has a child or children; the male parent. For example: "He was a loving and devoted father to his two daughters."
- **Noun (figurative)**: A founder, originator, or creator. For example: "Henry Ford is considered the father of the modern assembly line."
- **Noun (religious)**: God, especially when considered as the first person of the Christian Trinity. For example: "The Lord's Prayer begins with 'Our Father.'"
- **Noun (honorific title)**: A respectful form of address for an older man, especially one in authority or with whom one has a spiritual relationship. For example: "Father, can you give us your guidance?"
- **Verb**: To beget or sire a child. For example: "He fathered three sons during his lifetime."

## Phrases
- "Father figure": A man who serves as a guiding or protective influence in someone's life, often in the absence of a biological father.
- "Like father, like son": This phrase suggests that sons tend to resemble their fathers in behavior or character.
- "Father Time": A metaphorical representation of time, often depicted as an old man carrying a scythe.
