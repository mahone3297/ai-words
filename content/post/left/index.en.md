## Pronunciation
/lɛft/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Situated on the side that is usually opposite to the right hand; toward the west when one is facing north. Example: "The door is on the left side of the house."
- **Noun**: The direction or position on the left side. Example: "Stay to the left on this path."
- **Verb**: Past tense and past participle of leave; to go away from or abandon. Example: "She left her keys on the table."
- **Preposition**: Indicating movement or direction to the left side of something. Example: "Turn left at the next intersection."
- **Noun (informal)**: The political left, referring to liberal or socialist ideologies. Example: "His views are firmly on the left."

## Phrases
- "On the left": Referring to the position or direction.
- "To the left of": Indicating something's location relative to another object.
- "Left behind": To be forgotten or not taken along with others; also metaphorically meaning being outdated or surpassed.
  
## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "Please sit on the left side of the auditorium."
- **Noun**: "Keep going straight until you reach the fork in the road, then take a left."
- **Verb**: "I left my phone charger at home."
- **Preposition**: "The post office is just left of the library."
- **Noun (informal)**: "The candidate's policies appeal to those on the political left."

## Synonyms
- Adjective: Southern, port (when referring to the sides of a ship), sinistral
- Noun (direction): Portside (in maritime context)
- Verb (past tense of leave): Departed, exited
- Preposition: To the port (in maritime navigation)
