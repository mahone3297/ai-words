## Pronunciation
/vɪˈzɪt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A noun referring to a visit**: An act of going to see or pay a call on someone or something, often for a short time. For example: "We had a pleasant visit with our grandparents."
- **A noun referring to interest**: An expression of curiosity or desire to learn about something or someone. For example: "The museum received many visits from art enthusiasts."

### Verb
- **To go and see someone or somewhere**: To go with the purpose of spending time or communicating. For example: "I'm going to visit my friend tomorrow."
- **To engage in a social interaction**: To spend time with someone, often informally. For example: "We visited the local market every weekend."

### Adjective
- **Describing an action**: Expressing the act of paying a visit or attending. For example: "The annual visit to the doctor is required."

## Phrases
- "Pay a visit": To go and see someone, typically for a short time.
- "Visit someone's home": To go to someone's residence.
- "Virtual visit": A meeting or communication through digital means, especially when distance prevents physical presence.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "We had a wonderful visit at the beach."
- **Verb**: "My parents are planning to visit us next month."
- **Adjective**: "The museum's annual visitation numbers have been rising."
