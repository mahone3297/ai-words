## Word: Little

## Pronunciation
/ˈlɪtəl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Small in size, amount, or degree; not large or significant. For example: "The little kitten curled up on the sofa."
  - *Comparative*: littler /ˈlɪtlər/
  - *Superlative*: littlest /ˈlɪtlɪst/
  - "There's still a little milk left in the fridge."
- **Adverb**: To a small extent or degree; not much or often. For example: "She slept little last night due to her excitement."
- **Noun (informal)**: A small child or young person. For example: "The little ones were fast asleep in their beds."

## Phrases
- "A little goes a long way": Suggests that even a small amount of something can be effective or sufficient.
- "Little by little": Gradually, in small increments.
- "Think big, start small": Encourages starting with little steps towards achieving a larger goal.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "This little book contains all the wisdom I've gathered over the years."
- **Adverb**: "He speaks little about his past."
- **Noun**: "The teacher asked the little ones to line up for recess."

## Synonyms
- Small
- Petite
- Tiny
- Minor
- Young (when used as a noun)
- Diminutive
- Infant (when referring to a young child)
