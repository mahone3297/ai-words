## Pronunciation
/unˈles/

## Definitions
- **Preposition**: A part of speech that introduces a condition or exception, typically followed by a clause. It is used to indicate that something will happen if a certain condition is not met or is true. For example: "I'll come if you invite me."
- **Conjunction**: In this context, "unless" is used as a coordinating conjunction to connect two clauses, often in a conditional statement. For example: "I'll stay if it doesn't rain."

## Phrases
- "Unless stated otherwise" means that a particular rule or assumption applies unless there is an explicit mention to the contrary.
- "I'll do it unless..." is commonly used to express a condition for doing or not doing something.
- "She won't go unless..." is a common way to introduce a necessary condition for a person's action.

## Example Sentences
- **Preposition**: "The meeting will be canceled unless we receive enough RSVPs."
- **Conjunction**: "If you study hard, you'll pass the exam; unless you don't."
