## Pronunciation
/swɪm/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Swimming**: The activity of moving through water using one's arms and legs for propulsion, typically for exercise or leisure.
- **Swimmer**: A person who engages in swimming as a sport or recreational activity.
- **Swimwear**: Clothing designed for swimming, such as bathing suits.

### Verb
- **To swim**: Move through water by alternately propelling oneself with limbs.
- **Swimming lessons**: Instruction given to learn how to swim.

### Adjective
- **Swimmingly**: In a smooth or effortless manner, like swimming.
- **Aquatic**: Relating to water, suitable for swimming.

### Adverb
- **Swimmingly**: In a way that is easy, comfortable, or enjoyable (as in "ate dinner swimmingly").

## Phrases
- "Take a dip" means to go swimming for a short time.
- "Learn to swim" refers to the process of acquiring swimming skills.
- "Open water swimming" is swimming in natural bodies of water, not a pool.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She was a skilled swimmer, able to glide effortlessly through the lake."
- **Verb**: "The children were having fun splashing around in the pool, learning to swim."
- **Adjective**: "The hotel had a beautiful outdoor swimming pool with a view of the ocean."
- **Adverb**: "He swam swimmingly across the river, enjoying the cool water on a hot day."
