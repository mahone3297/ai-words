## Pronunciation
/unˈləʊd/

## Definitions
### Noun
**Unload** (noun): The act of removing goods or materials from a vehicle, ship, or container; the process of discharging items from storage or a freight hold.

### Verb
**Unload** (verb): To take off or remove goods, cargo, or items from a vehicle, vessel, or container; to transfer or dispatch something from a loaded state.

### Adjective
**Unloaded** (adjective): Not loaded; having no weight or burden; free from cargo or freight.

## Phrases
- "Unload the truck" means to take the goods out of a truck.
- "Unloading dock" refers to a designated area for unloading cargo.
- "An unloaded mind" implies a relaxed or clear mental state.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The crew spent hours unloading the crates from the ship."
- **Verb**: "After the delivery, he had to unload the boxes onto the warehouse floor."
- **Adjective**: "The unloaded train was ready to proceed on its journey."
