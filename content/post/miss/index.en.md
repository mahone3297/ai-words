## Pronunciation
/miss/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To fail to hit, reach, or meet something; to not be successful in aiming or attaining. For example: "She missed the target while practicing archery."
- **Verb**: (v) To fail to attend or participate in something, especially an event or appointment. For example: "He missed the important meeting due to illness."
- **Verb**: (v) To feel the lack or absence of something or someone; to long for or regret their departure. For example: "She really misses her hometown after moving away."
- **Verb**: (v) To fail to notice or recognize something or someone. For example: "I almost missed seeing the small note at the bottom of the page."
- **Noun**: (n) A failure to hit, catch, or achieve something intended or desired. For example: "The basketball player had too many misses this game."
- **Noun**: (n) The act of failing to attend or participate. For example: "Her frequent misses from work raised concerns about her commitment."

## Phrases
- "Miss out on something" means to fail to experience or benefit from an opportunity.
- "Miss the point" means to misunderstand or fail to grasp the main idea or purpose.
- "A miss is as good as a mile" is a proverb meaning that even a small mistake can have significant consequences.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "I'm going to miss the train if I don't hurry up."
- **Noun**: "His repeated misses during the penalty shootout cost his team the match."
- **Noun (used for addressing unmarried women)**: "Miss Smith, could you please pass the salt?"
