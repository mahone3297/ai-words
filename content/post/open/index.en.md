## Pronunciation
/ˈoʊpən/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Not closed or locked; able to be entered or accessed. For example: "The door was left wide open."
- **Verb**: (transitive) To move something so that it is no longer closed or obstructed; to unfasten or unlock. For example: "She opened the window to let in some fresh air."
- (intransitive) To become open. For example: "The flowers began to open in the morning sun."
- (transitive) To establish or start something officially or formally. For example: "They opened the new store with a grand ceremony."
- (transitive) To make something available for use or access. For example: "The museum opens daily at 10 AM."
- (figurative) To disclose or reveal something, such as information or feelings. Example: "She opened up about her struggles."

## Phrases
- "Open sesame": An idiomatic expression suggesting that a command or action will produce immediate or magical results.
- "Keep an open mind": To remain receptive to new ideas or perspectives.
- "An open secret": Information that is known by many people but not talked about openly.
- "Leave the door open": To allow the possibility of something happening in the future.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The shop is open until late tonight."
- **Verb**: "I opened the letter and read its contents."
- **Figurative usage**: "The conference opened opportunities for international collaboration."
