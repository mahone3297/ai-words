## Word: Merchant

## Pronunciation
/ˈmɜːrʧənt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A person who buys or sells goods in large quantities, especially one who trades with foreign countries or supplies merchandise to retailers. Example: "The spice merchant traveled from port to port to sell his exotic wares."

Example Sentence: 
- "The wealthy silk merchant was renowned for his fair trade practices throughout the region."

Phrases:
- "Merchant navy": The commercial fleet of ships that carries cargo and passengers internationally.
- "Merchant bank": A financial institution that deals with underwriting, issuing, and trading securities for corporations and governments.
- "Merchant of Venice": A famous play by William Shakespeare about a Jewish moneylender named Shylock.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "During the Renaissance, Italian merchants played a significant role in the economic growth of their cities."
- "She decided to open her own boutique, becoming a merchant of unique handmade jewelry."
