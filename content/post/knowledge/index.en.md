## Word: Knowledge

## Pronunciation
/ˈnɑːlɪdʒ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The information, understanding, and skills acquired through education or experience. It refers to awareness or familiarity with someone or something, such as facts, truths, principles, or skills. 
  - Example Sentence: "*Her vast knowledge of history made her lectures captivating.*"
  
- **Noun (figurative)**: An acquaintance or familiarity gained by personal experience. 
  - Example Sentence: "*He has firsthand knowledge of the local culture from living there for years.*"

## Phrases
- "Knowledge is power": This proverb emphasizes that having information and understanding can lead to control, influence, or advantage.
- "In the realm of knowledge": This phrase is used when referring to a specific area of expertise or study.
- "To possess knowledge about something": To have information or understanding on a particular subject.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "*A good education equips students with valuable knowledge and critical thinking skills.*"
- **Noun (figurative)**: "*His practical knowledge of farming techniques helped him succeed in his agricultural business.*"
