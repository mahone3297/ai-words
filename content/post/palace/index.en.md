## Word: Palace

## Pronunciation
/ˈpælɪs/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A large and impressive building that is the official residence of a king, queen, emperor, or other high-ranking nobleperson, often containing administrative offices and reception rooms. For example: "The Queen resides in Buckingham Palace."
- **Noun (figurative)**: A grand or magnificent place or thing regarded as equivalent to a royal residence. For instance: "The museum was a palace of art."

## Phrases
- "Palace of Versailles" - A famous French royal palace and UNESCO World Heritage Site.
- "Palace intrigue" - Refers to secretive and often manipulative political activities within a government or organization.
- "A child's palace" - A metaphorical expression for a place where a child feels safe, secure, and happy, often their own home.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The lavish decorations inside the ancient palace were breathtaking."
- **Noun (figurative)**: "The library, with its towering shelves and ornate architecture, was truly a palace of knowledge."
