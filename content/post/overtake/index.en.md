## Pronunciation
/ˌoʊvərˈteɪk/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (transitive) To catch up with and pass another vehicle, person, or thing that was ahead. For example: "The faster car soon overtook the slower one on the highway."
  - **Definition Extension**: It can also mean to surpass someone in a competition or in terms of development, progress, or achievement. Example: "Her dedication to practice allowed her to overtake her opponents in the race."
  
## Phrases
- "Overtake from behind" – to pass something or someone by going around them while they are in front.
- "Be overtaken by events" – to be rendered irrelevant or unnecessary because of subsequent developments.
- "To overtake someone's position" – to take over or surpass someone's rank, status, or accomplishments.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb (in traffic context)**: "The bus managed to safely overtake the lorry on the straight stretch of road."
- **Verb (in competitive context)**: "Through sheer determination, he finally overtook the leader in the last lap of the marathon."
- **Idiomatic use**: "Their innovative technology quickly overtook that of their competitors in the market."
