## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈsʌlt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: An expression or act that causes someone to feel offended, hurt, or belittled; a disrespectful or scornful remark or action. For example: "His words were intended as an insult to her intelligence."
- **Verb**: To treat with disrespect or contempt by making offensive remarks or gestures; to demean or degrade someone. For example: "She couldn't believe he had insulted her in front of their friends."

### Phrases
- "To offer/throw an insult": To say or do something that is meant to be offensive.
- "Insult to injury": An additional source of harm or irritation following an initial setback or problem. For example: "Having lost the game was bad enough, but losing it to our arch-rivals was adding insult to injury."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "His constant insults eventually led her to end the friendship."
- **Verb**: "Don't insult my intelligence by lying to me."
