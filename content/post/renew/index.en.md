## Pronunciation
/rɪˈnjuː/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The act of restoring or regenerating something to its original state or freshness; a renewal or revival.
   - Example: "The lease on the apartment will need to be renewed."
2. A certificate or document that allows the holder to continue using or possessing something, often with specified conditions.
   - Example: "He received a library card renewal."

### Verb
1. To restore or refresh (a license, contract, or legal status) for another period.
   - Example: "She renewed her passport before leaving for her trip."
2. To give new life, vigor, or strength to (a person, idea, or relationship).
   - Example: "The project has been renewed with fresh ideas."

### Adjective
1. Characterizing or relating to a renewal or restoration.
   - Example: "The company's renewed focus on sustainability."

## Phrases
- "Renew one's vows" to repeat a marriage ceremony or commitment.
- "Renew interest" to show a renewed interest or enthusiasm.
- "Renewable energy" refers to sources of energy that can be replenished naturally.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After the storm, the community began the process of renewing their homes."
- **Verb**: "He renewed his membership in the gym to stay fit."
- **Adjective**: "Their renewed efforts led to improved performance."
