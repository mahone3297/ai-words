## Pronunciation
/ðə/

## Definitions
- **Determiner**: A word used to specify or identify a particular noun, often indicating a previously mentioned or implied noun. It is usually placed before the noun to provide more information about it. In some cases, "the" can also function as a noun, referring to a specific, known entity.
- **Article**: A type of determiner, specifically the one used before singular, non-descriptive nouns to indicate a general reference.

## Phrases
- "The one on the left" refers to a specific object among several.
- "The answer to the question" indicates a particular solution.
- "The Great Wall of China" denotes a well-known historical structure.

## Example Sentences
- **Determiner**: "I saw the dog playing in the park."
- **Article**: "The car needs a new tire."
- **Noun**: "History is full of stories about the famous artist."

## Synonyms
- This
- That
- These
- Those
- A (when used as a singular indefinite article)
- An (when used before a vowel sound)

## Notes
- "The" is often omitted when referring to well-known objects or concepts, such as "the sun" or "the sky."
- It can also be used to emphasize or introduce a statement: "The truth is, I was late."
