## Pronunciation
/sæˈtaɪlɪt/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A celestial body that revolves around a larger body, such as a planet or moon, and often serves as a platform for scientific research, communication, or navigation.
   - Example: "The International Space Station is a major Earth satellite."
2. A smaller device or object that orbits, hovers, or moves alongside another, often for support or monitoring purposes.
   - Example: "A GPS satellite provides location data to smartphones."

### Verb
1. To orbit or travel in a satellite-like manner around something.
   - Example: "The satellite tracked the weather patterns closely."
2. To function as a satellite, providing support or assistance.
   - Example: "The communication satellite relays signals between Earth and spacecraft."

### Adjective
1. Relating to or resembling a satellite.
   - Example: "Satellite imagery helps scientists study the Earth's surface."
2. Dependent or secondary.
   - Example: "Her role in the project was somewhat satellite to the main team's efforts."
