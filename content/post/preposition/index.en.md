## Pronunciation
/prepəˈzɪʃ(ə)n/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A part of speech that relates words to other parts of a sentence, indicating their relationship in terms of location, time, direction, or manner. For example: "The book is on the table."
- **Verb**: To place or introduce something with respect to another element in a sentence. For example: "She positioned the vase on the shelf."
- **Adjective**: Characterizing a word that describes how a noun or pronoun relates to other elements in a sentence. For example: "in preparation" or "under the table."

## Phrases
- "With a preposition" refers to using a preposition to clarify the relationship.
- "Prepositional phrase" consists of a preposition plus its object, often functioning as a modifier.
- "Misuse of prepositions" occurs when a preposition is incorrectly used in a sentence.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The cat sat under the tree during the rain."
- **Verb**: "She hid her surprise behind a prepositional phrase."
- **Adjective**: "The plan was well-prepared for the meeting."

## Synonyms
- Adverbials
- Relators
- Pre指示词 (in Chinese)
- Conjunctions of place, time, or manner
- Postpositions (in some languages)

## Antonyms
- None (prepositions don't have direct antonyms, but they can be replaced with other prepositions or conjunctions to indicate different relationships)
