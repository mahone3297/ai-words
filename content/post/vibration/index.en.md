## Pronunciation
/vəˈbaɪʃn/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. The act or motion of oscillating or moving back and forth, typically with a regular frequency. 2. A sensation produced by such movement, especially in one's body. 3. (Physics) The transfer of energy from one object to another in the form of oscillations. 4. (Technology) The detection or measurement of such oscillations, often used to monitor machinery or communication signals.
- **Verb**: To cause to vibrate, move in a rhythmic or oscillatory manner. 3. To sense or perceive vibrations, particularly in the context of music or sound waves.

## Phrases
- "Body vibration" refers to movement within the body.
- "Mechanical vibration" is a term in engineering.
- "Resonant vibration" occurs when an object vibrates at its natural frequency.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The engine's vibration was causing the car to shake."
- **Verb**: "The musician adjusted the tuning pegs to minimize the string vibration."
- **Phrasal**: "The earthquake produced strong seismic vibrations throughout the city."
