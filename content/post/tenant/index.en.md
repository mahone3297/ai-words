## Pronunciation
/ˈtɛnənt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A person who occupies or rents a house, apartment, or other living space from its owner or landlord, typically on a long-term basis.
- In British English, a tenant may also refer to a person occupying agricultural land or business premises as a lessee.

## Phrases
- "Tenant rights" refers to the legal protections and obligations that a tenant has regarding their rental property.
- "Subtenant" is a term used for a person who rents a living space from a tenant, not directly from the landlord.
- "Leasehold tenant" is one who holds a lease, a contract giving them exclusive rights to use the property for a specified period.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The tenant was responsible for maintaining the garden according to the terms of their agreement."
- **Verb**: "The landlord was discussing maintenance issues with his new tenant over the phone."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "The landlord evicted the tenant for non-payment of rent."
