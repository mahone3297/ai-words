## Word: Inquire

## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈkwaɪər/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To ask for information or details about something; to make a formal or official investigation. 
  - Example Sentence: "The detective inquired about the suspect's whereabouts on the night of the crime."
- **Verb**: To request information from someone, often politely or formally. 
  - Example Sentence: "She inquired whether the store had any more stock of the item she wanted."
  
## Phrases
- "To inquire after someone": To ask how someone is or what they have been doing, typically when they have been ill or absent.
  - Example: "I called to inquire after your health."
- "Inquire within": A phrase often seen on signs outside businesses or establishments, inviting potential customers or clients to enter and ask for more information.
- "Inquiry process": A systematic investigation or research into a specific subject.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb (asking for information)**: "The reporter inquired deeply into the corruption scandal."
- **Verb (inquiring about someone's well-being)**: "I inquired about her feelings after hearing the news."
