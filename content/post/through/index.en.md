## Pronunciation
/θruː/

## Definitions
### Preposition
- **Through**: A preposition indicating movement across or from one side to the other side of something, or completion of an action via a particular medium or means. 

### Verb
- (informal) **To go through**: To experience, complete, or pass through a process, situation, or space. 

### Adverb
- **Throughout**: Throughout the entire duration or extent of something.

### Adjective
- **Through**: Intact or reaching all the way through; not just on the surface. For example, "a tunnel that runs through the mountain."

## Phrases
- "Go through the gate" means to pass through a gateway or barrier.
- "She saw it happen through the window."
- "The project was completed through teamwork."
- "Throughout history, humans have explored the world."

## Example Sentences
- **Preposition**: "I drove through the city on my way home."
- **Verb**: "I went through a lot of challenges during my internship."
- **Adverb**: "The book's insights apply throughout the industry."
- **Adjective**: "The wire runs through the hollow core of the pipe."
