## Pronunciation
/west/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. The western direction or side, opposite east. 2. A geographical region, often characterized by dry climate and cowboy culture. 3. (U.S.) The western United States. 4. (Brit./Austral.) A part of a country's mainland towards the west coast, e.g., "the West Midlands."
- **Adjective**: Western; located or situated in the west. 3. Characteristic of the west; pertaining to western customs or traditions.

## Phrases
- "Go west" can mean to travel or move towards the west or pursue new opportunities.
- "The Wild West" refers to the period in U.S. history marked by lawlessness and frontier life.
- "Western front" is a military term referring to a line of battle, particularly in World War I and II.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "We decided to take a road trip across the American West."
- **Adjective**: "The sun sets in the west, casting a warm glow over the landscape."
- **Adverb**: "The cowboy rode towards the west, following the sun."
