## Pronunciation
/pəˈluːt/

## Definitions
### Noun
**Pollution**: The presence or introduction of substances or pollutants into the environment, often causing harm to living organisms or ecosystems. For example: "The river was severely polluted due to industrial waste."

### Verb
**To pollute**: To contaminate (an environment) with harmful substances or waste, or to introduce pollutants into it. For example: "The factory is polluting the air with its toxic emissions."

### Adjective
**Polluted**: Contaminated by harmful substances or waste, often affecting water, air, or land. For example: "The polluted water made it unsafe for swimming."

## Phrases
- "Air pollution" refers to the contamination of the atmosphere.
- "Water pollution" is the contamination of water bodies.
- "Reduce, reuse, and recycle" is a popular slogan emphasizing pollution prevention.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The city's air pollution levels have been on the rise."
- **Verb**: "The new regulations aim to reduce industrial pollution."
- **Adjective**: "The once-clear lake has become a polluted wasteland."
