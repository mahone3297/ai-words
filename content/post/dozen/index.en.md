## Pronunciation
/dʌzən/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **dozen** is a measure of twelve items, typically used for countable objects like eggs, bottles, or people. It is an archaic term derived from the Old French word 'douze' meaning twelve.

### Verb
To **dozen** (as in "dozen something") can be used informally to mean to give a large quantity or number of something, often in a joking or exaggerated sense.

## Phrases
- "A dozen eggs"
- "A dozen steps"
- "A dozen times over"

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The bakery sold dozens of fresh-baked bread loaves."
- **Verb**: "She had to dozen her friends with invitations for the party."
- **Informal**: "He must have dozened the cookies, they were everywhere!"

## Synonyms

- Score
- Batch

- Quartet

## Notes
- The use of "dozen" as a verb is informal and often replaced by more specific terms when precise quantities are needed.
- In some contexts, "a dozen and one" or "a baker's dozen" can refer to thirteen items, with the baker's dozen being an extra given as a gift or to avoid a fine.
