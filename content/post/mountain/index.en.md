## Pronunciation
/ˈmaʊntn/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A large natural elevation of the earth's surface rising abruptly from the surrounding level; usually higher and steeper than a hill. For example: "The climbers reached the summit of the snow-capped mountain."
- **Noun (figurative)**: Something that is difficult to overcome or surmount, like an obstacle or challenge. For example: "Her biggest challenge was the mountain of debt she had accumulated."

## Phrases
- "Move mountains" - to achieve something great or difficult, often requiring considerable effort. For example: "She managed to move mountains by completing her degree while working full-time."
- "On top of the world/mountain" - feeling elated, successful, or triumphant. For example: "After winning the gold medal, he felt like he was on top of the world."
- "Mountains out of molehills" - to exaggerate the importance or severity of a problem. For example: "Don't make mountains out of molehills; it's just a small mistake."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (literal)**: "The cabin nestled in the shadow of the towering mountain provided a stunning view."
- **Noun (figurative)**: "His alcohol addiction was a mountain he needed to conquer to regain his health."
