## Word: Introduce

## Pronunciation
/ˌɪntrəˈdjuːs/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: 
  - To present or make known (a person) to another, typically by name or with some information about them. For example: "He introduced us to his new girlfriend."
  - To bring forward or announce (something new or unfamiliar) for the first time; to present or begin something. Example: "The company introduced a new product line."
  - In biology, to bring or place (an organism) into a new environment or habitat. Example: "The invasive species was accidentally introduced to the region."
  
## Phrases
- "Introduce yourself" means to tell someone your name and possibly other relevant information about you when meeting them for the first time.
- "To introduce a topic" means to start talking or writing about a subject.
- "Make introductions" refers to the act of presenting one person to another, often in a social setting.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb (people)**: "I'd like to introduce my colleague, Sarah, who will be leading the project."
- **Verb (ideas/products)**: "The professor introduced the concept of quantum physics to the class."
- **Biology context**: "The park rangers worked hard to prevent the introduction of non-native plants that could harm the ecosystem."

## Synonyms
- Present
- Acquaint
- Bring forward
- Announce
- Unveil
- Launch (in the context of products)
- Familiarize (with)
- Initiate (a relationship or discussion)
