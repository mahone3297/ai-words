## Pronunciation
/ˌɪnˈsaɪd/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The inner part, space, or contents of something; the area within. For example: "The inside of the house was beautifully decorated."
  - Also used to refer to information that is not widely known, especially about a situation or organization. Example: "She had inside knowledge about the company's upcoming projects."

- **Adjective**: Located or occurring within; internal or inner. Example: "The inside door handle was broken."

- **Verb**: (informal) To be located or placed within. Example: "The book is inside the drawer."

## Phrases
- "Inside out": completely reversed or turned so that the inner side faces outwards, often used metaphorically to describe a thorough understanding or reversal of a situation. Example: "She knew the project inside out."
- "On the inside": having access to private or confidential information or being part of a group. Example: "He was on the inside when it came to the political campaign."
- "Keep it inside": to hold back emotions or information from being expressed or shared. Example: "She kept her feelings inside."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The painting depicted a cozy scene from the inside of a cottage."
- **Adjective**: "Make sure to clean the inside of the oven too."
- **Verb**: "I'll make sure to put the fragile items inside the box carefully."

## Synonyms
- Interior (noun)
- Inner (adjective)
- Within (adverb)
- Inside of (prepositional phrase)  
- Internally (adverb)
