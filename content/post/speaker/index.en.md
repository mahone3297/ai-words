## Pronunciation
/sˈpɛkrər/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A person who speaks, especially as a profession or specialist in public addresses or presentations. **Example**: "The keynote speaker was an expert on climate change."
2. A device or system that produces sound, often used for making announcements or presentations. **Example**: "The conference room was equipped with a high-quality speaker system."

### Verb
1. To use one's voice to communicate thoughts, ideas, or information. **Example**: "The teacher asked the students to speak up during class discussions."
2. To deliver a speech or presentation. **Example**: "The candidate will be speaking at the rally tomorrow."

### Adjective
1. Characterizing someone who is skilled in expressing themselves verbally. **Example**: "The speaker was articulate and engaging."
2. Designating a device that produces sound. **Example**: "A Bluetooth speaker allows wireless audio playback."

## Phrases
- "Give a speech" means to make a formal or informative address.
- "Speakerphone" refers to a telephone feature that allows multiple people to hear the speaker.
- "Speaker notes" are prepared remarks for a speech or presentation.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The conference featured several notable speakers from different industries."
- **Verb**: "After the ceremony, the mayor thanked the honored guest for her inspiring speech."
- **Adjective**: "The speaker's expertise in marketing was evident in their presentation."
