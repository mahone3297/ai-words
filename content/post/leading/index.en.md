## Pronunciation
/ˈliːdɪŋ/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Most important or prominent; having a commanding or guiding role. For example: "The company's leading product has seen a surge in sales."
  - *Leading edge*: The foremost or most advanced part, often referring to technology or innovation.
  - *Leading figure*: A person who holds a significant or influential position in a particular field.

- **Verb (Present Participle of Lead)**: Directing or guiding something or someone ahead. For example: "The leading actor captivated the audience with his performance."
  - *Leading by example*: Setting a precedent or demonstrating behavior that others should follow.
  - *Leading the charge*: Initiating action or taking the forefront in a movement or endeavor.

## Phrases
- "Leading role": The main character or position in a play, film, or other production.
- "Leading indicator": An economic or financial statistic that predicts future trends.
- "Leading question": A type of question phrased to suggest a particular answer or influence the response.

## Example Sentences

- **Verb**: "His expertise led him to become a leading expert in the field of artificial intelligence."
