## Pronunciation
/ˈkwiːz/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **quiz** is a type of test or game that typically consists of a series of questions, often multiple-choice, designed to assess knowledge, skills, or understanding on a particular subject. It can be used for educational purposes or as a form of entertainment.

### Verb
To **quiz** means to ask a series of questions, usually for testing or evaluating someone's knowledge, or to engage in a friendly competition or discussion.

## Phrases
- "Take a quiz" implies completing a test or questionnaire.
- "Quiz yourself" suggests self-assessment or learning through practice questions.
- "Quiz show" refers to a television program where contestants answer questions for prizes.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The students were given a history quiz to prepare for the exam."
- **Verb**: "She quizzed her friends on their favorite books over lunch."
- **Verb (intransitive)**: "He spent the evening quizzing his family about their travels."

## Synonyms
- Test
- Examination
- Assessment
- Quiz night
- Trivia session

## Antonyms
- Relaxation
- Entertainment (opposite of a serious quiz)
- Unwinding (unlike studying or testing)
