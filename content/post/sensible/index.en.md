## Definitions
### Noun
A **sensible** person or thing is one that is reasonable, practical, or showing good judgment. It can also refer to something easily understood or perceivable. For example:
- "She's always making sensible decisions."
- "A sensible choice would be to wear warm clothes on a cold day."

### Verb
To **sense** or **make sense** means to perceive or understand something clearly or logically. For example:
- "It finally made sense after I read it aloud."
- "The plan makes perfect sense when you consider the circumstances."

### Adjective
- Describing something that is logical, reasonable, or appropriate. For example: "A sensible budget helps with financial planning."
- Referring to a feeling or sensation. For example: "He had a sudden sensible feeling that he was being watched."

### Adverb
- Used to describe how something is done, often implying clarity or understanding. For example: "She speaks sensibly about her goals."
