## Pronunciation
/rɪˈspɒns/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Response**: A reaction or reply to something, typically in words or actions. For example: "Her response to the question was immediate."
- **Answer**: An answer given in reply to a question or request. For example: "He gave a detailed answer to her query."

### Verb
- **To respond**: To reply or react to something, usually verbally or in writing. For example: "She quickly responded to his email."
- **React**: To have an emotional or physical reaction to a particular situation. For example: "The audience reacted positively to the performance."

### Adjective
- **Responsive**: Quick to react or answer, especially to communication. For example: "A responsive customer service team."
- **Replyable**: Capable of receiving or giving a reply. For example: "This phone is replyable to voice messages."

### Adverb
- **Responsibly**: In a manner showing care and consideration for one's duties or obligations. For example: "He handled the task responsibly."

## Phrases
- "Response time" refers to the duration it takes to provide a reaction.
- "No response" indicates that no reply has been received.
- "Prompt response" means an immediate or timely reply.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The teacher asked for a written response to the assignment."
- **Verb**: "Please respond to my message as soon as possible."
- **Adjective**: "Her answers were thoughtful and responsive."
- **Adverb**: "He gave a responsible response to the project deadline."
