## Word: Literature

## Pronunciation
/ˈlɪtərətʃər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The written works, especially those considered of superior or lasting artistic merit, such as novels, plays, poetry, and essays. _Example_: "Shakespeare's plays are cornerstones of English literature."
- **Noun (in broader sense)**: Writings on a particular subject. _Example_: "The literature on climate change is vast and constantly growing."
- **Verb (less common usage)**: To study or be well-versed in literature. _Example_: "She decided to literature herself in classical Greek texts."

## Phrases
- "World literature": Refers to literary works from all cultures across the globe.
- "Literature review": A comprehensive summary and critical evaluation of existing research on a specific topic.
- "The classics of literature": Refers to works that are highly esteemed for their enduring literary value.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Jane Austen's 'Pride and Prejudice' is a staple of 19th-century British literature."
- **Verb**: "His passion for literature drove him to read extensively and become a professor in the field."
