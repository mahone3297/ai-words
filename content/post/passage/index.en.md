## Pronunciation
/pəˈsɪdʒ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A section of written or spoken text that conveys a particular idea, topic, or story; a continuous flow of words or ideas. For example: "The passage discussed the history of the city."
- **Verb**: To go through or move from one part to another, often while reading or understanding a text. For example: "She carefully read through the passage to grasp its meaning."
- **Adjective**: Flowing or coherent in thought or expression. For example: "The author's writing had a smooth and eloquent passage."

## Phrases
- "Reading comprehension passage" refers to a text used for testing understanding.
- "Passage of time" signifies the progression of moments or events.
- "Passage of legislation" means the process by which a bill becomes law.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The passage in the novel was particularly emotional and impactful."
- **Verb**: "After finishing the chapter, he took a moment to reflect on the passage he just read."
- **Adjective**: "Her speech contained a series of well-structured passages that flowed seamlessly."
