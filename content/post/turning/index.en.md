## Pronunciation
/ˈtɜːrnɪŋ/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A change in position or direction**: The act or process of turning; rotation. For example: "The wheel was turning smoothly."
- **A change in one's opinion or feelings**: Conversion or shift in attitude. For example: "Her thoughts began to turn towards a new career path."
- **A phase or stage in a process**: A point in time or development. For example: "The turning point in the game came with a crucial goal."
- **A person's turn**: The opportunity to take part or act. For example: "It's your turn to bat now."

### Verb
- **To rotate or move around a central axis**: She turned the doorknob to open the door.
- **To cause to change direction or face**: He turned to face the approaching danger.
- **To change one's mind or opinion**: He couldn't decide, so he turned his attention elsewhere.
- **To hand over control or responsibility**: The teacher turned the class over to the substitute.

### Adjective
- **In the process of changing**: The turning leaves on the trees signal autumn.
- **Changing or undergoing transformation**: The economy is currently in a turning point.
- **Requiring a decision or choice**: The situation presented a turning point in their lives.

### Adverb
- **Describing the manner of turning**: She turned slowly to avoid tripping.
- **Indicating a change in direction**: He turned left at the intersection.

## Phrases
- "Take a turning": To make a sudden change in direction or plan.
- "Turn of events": An unexpected development in a situation.
- "Turning point": A critical juncture that leads to a significant change.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: The car took a sharp turning on the winding road.
- **Verb**: After months of contemplation, he decided to turn his life around.
- **Adjective**: The view from the top of the mountain offered a stunning turning of the landscape.
- **Adverb**: He took a sudden turning and disappeared from sight.
