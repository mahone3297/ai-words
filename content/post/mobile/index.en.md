## Word: Mobile

## Pronunciation
/ˈməʊbaɪl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: (adj) Capable of moving or being moved easily from one place to another; not fixed in place. Example: "A mobile phone can be carried around easily."
  
  > "She bought a mobile desk that could be wheeled from room to room."
  
- **Noun**: (n) An object, often decorative, suspended so that it can move freely in the air. Example: "The baby was fascinated by the colorful mobile hanging above his crib."

  > "They hung a beautiful kinetic mobile above the atrium."

- **Noun**: (n) A mobile device, especially a mobile phone. Example: "Smartphones are now an essential part of our daily lives, with their many mobile applications."

  > "With the new mobile, he could stay connected on-the-go."

## Phrases
- "Mobile app" - a software application designed to run on smartphones, tablets, and other mobile devices.
- "Mobile home" - a prefabricated dwelling that is movable and usually rests on a permanent site in a designated mobile home park.
- "Mobile unit" - a vehicle or structure that can be moved from place to place for a specific purpose, like a mobile library or medical clinic.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "Thanks to modern technology, we now have access to mobile banking services."
- **Noun (decorative object)**: "The artist crafted a unique mobile made entirely out of recycled materials."
- **Noun (mobile device)**: "His job requires him to be constantly on his mobile responding to clients' queries."

## Synonyms
- Portable (for adjective sense)
- Movable
- Transportable (for the sense related to objects)
- Cellphone (for the sense related to mobile phones)
- Smartphone (for the sense referring to advanced mobile devices)
