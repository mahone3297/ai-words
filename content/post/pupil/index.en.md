## Pronunciation
/pjuːˈpɪl/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **A person who is learning to read and write, especially a child attending school.** (informal)
2. **The central, dark spot in the eye, containing the pupil of the iris and sensitive to light.**

## Phrases
- "The pupil's eyes were wide with curiosity."
- "A bright young pupil."
- "The teacher was impressed by the pupil's quick progress."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The pupil was fascinated by the demonstration in science class."
- **Noun**: "As the teacher shone a flashlight, the pupils dilated in response to the light."
- **Adjective**: "The pupil's artwork displayed exceptional creativity."

## Verb
1. **To be the student of; learn under.**
2. **To focus one's gaze on; pay attention to.**

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "She has been tutoring new pupils in math."
- **Verb**: "The teacher asked the pupils to focus their attention on the blackboard."

## Interjection
- Not commonly used as an interjection, but it can be used informally to address or refer to a pupil.

## Synonyms (for Noun)
- Student
- Learner
- Apprentice
- Scholar
- Child (informal)

## Synonyms (for Verb)
- Attend
- Study under
- Learn from
- Engage in instruction

Note: The verb form of "pupil" is not commonly used as an interjection to greet someone, as it is primarily associated with the act of being a student or learning. If you're looking for a greeting, synonyms like "Hi," "Hey," or "Hello" would be more appropriate.
