## Pronunciation
/ˈlɪtərəri/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Relating to literature, books, or writing, often implying a concern with artistic or scholarly aspects. For example: "She has a degree in literary studies."
  - *Also used to describe something that is characteristic of literature or written works*: "The novel contains many literary allusions."
- **Noun (plural form 'literaries')**: Refers to literary works or writings collectively; can also refer to literary people or activities. For example: "Her interests include attending literary events and reading the latest literaries."

## Phrases
- "Literary canon": The body of works considered to be the most important and influential in a particular culture or genre.
- "Literary analysis": The process of examining a piece of literature to understand its meaning, themes, and techniques used by the author.
- "Literary devices": Techniques used by authors to convey meaning, such as metaphor, simile, personification, etc.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The author's use of metaphors is one of the literary highlights of the book."
- **Noun**: "She immersed herself in the literary circles of New York City."
