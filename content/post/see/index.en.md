## Pronunciation
/siː/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To perceive with the eyes or have knowledge or awareness of something. (n. sense: the act of seeing)
- **Noun**: Vision; the ability to perceive things visually. For example: "She has excellent sight."
- **Adjective**: Manifesting or expressing perception or understanding. For example: "A clear vision of the future."
- **Adverb**: In a manner that can be seen; visibly. For example: "He saw the sunset from the balcony."

## Phrases
- "See something" means to notice or become aware of an object or situation.
- "I can't see that" indicates lack of visibility or understanding.
- "To catch a glimpse of" refers to a brief but noticeable sighting.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "I saw a shooting star streak across the sky last night."
- **Noun**: "Her eyesight is failing with age."
- **Adjective**: "He had a deep-seated sense of injustice he could no longer see ignored."
- **Adverb**: "The children were playing outside, visible through the open window."
