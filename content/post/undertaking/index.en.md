## Pronunciation
/ˌʌndərˈteɪkɪŋ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 
1. An enterprise, project, or task that one undertakes, often involving effort or risk. For example: "She embarked on a major undertaking to renovate her house."
2. An agreement or commitment to do something. For example: "The company signed an undertaking to deliver the project on time."

- **Verb**: To take on a task or responsibility, especially one that is challenging or risky. For example: "He undertook a leadership role in the community project."

## Phrases
- "undertake a project" means to start or engage in a task or mission.
- "undertaking a journey" refers to embarking on a trip or expedition.
- "undertaking great responsibility" implies taking on a significant or demanding duty.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After months of planning, he finally embarked on his ambitious scientific undertaking."
- **Verb**: "She was willing to undertake the daunting task of learning a new language."
