## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈkluːd/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To contain something as a part of something else; to have within its scope or boundaries. For example: "The package includes breakfast."
  - Also used transitively to indicate that someone or something is part of a group, list, or category. For example: "This tour includes visits to three major museums."
- **Verb**: (v) To consider or treat someone or something as part of something else. For example: "We should include all team members in the decision-making process."
  
## Phrases
- "Include something in something": To put or add something as a part of something else. For instance: "Don't forget to include your contact details in the email."
- "Be included": To be considered as a part of something. Example: "All taxes and fees are included in the price."
- "To include but not limited to": An expression indicating that a list or examples that follow are not exhaustive. Example: "The job duties include, but are not limited to, data entry and customer service."

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The recipe includes a variety of fresh vegetables."
- "Your monthly subscription fee includes access to all premium content."
- "Make sure to include a cover letter with your job application."
