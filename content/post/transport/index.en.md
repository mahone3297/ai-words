## Pronunciation
/ˈtrænspɔrt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Transport**: The act or system of moving people or goods from one place to another, often using vehicles or infrastructure. For example: "The city's public transport network includes buses and trains."
- **Transportation**: The means by which people or goods are conveyed from one location to another, such as cars, airplanes, or ships. For example: "She uses various modes of transportation to get to work."

### Verb
- **To transport**: To carry or convey (people or goods) from one place to another. For example: "The company is transporting thousands of boxes to the new warehouse."
- **To ship**: To send goods by water or air for delivery. For example: "The books will be transported by ship to the international market."

### Adjective
- **Transport-related**: Relating to transportation or its systems. For example: "The transport sector plays a crucial role in the economy."

## Phrases
- "Public transport" refers to shared vehicles and services provided by governments or private companies.
- "Cargo transport" is the movement of goods by commercial means.
- "Door-to-door transport" means direct delivery from sender to recipient without intermediate stops.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The new high-speed rail connects two major cities, improving transport between them."
- **Verb**: "The rescue team transported the injured victims to the hospital immediately."
- **Adjective**: "The transport-related innovations have significantly reduced travel times."
