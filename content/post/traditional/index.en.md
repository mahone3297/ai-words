## Pronunciation
/trəˈdɪʃənl/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Adjective**: Belonging to or characteristic of a particular culture, period, or society; customary or usual in a particular place or time. For example: "The traditional clothing of the region is colorful and intricate."
2. **Noun**: A custom, practice, or belief that has been passed down through generations. For example: "They follow a traditional wedding ceremony."

### Verb
1. To adhere to or continue using customs, practices, or beliefs that have been handed down. For example: "The family has traditionally celebrated Christmas with a big feast."
2. To maintain or uphold in a traditional manner. For example: "He decided to keep the house in its traditional style."

## Phrases
- "Traditional values" refers to long-standing beliefs or customs.
- "A traditional diet" includes foods that are typical of a specific culture or region.
- "Traditional arts and crafts" encompass skills and techniques passed down through generations.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The festival showcased traditional dances from various parts of the country."
- **Verb**: "She wanted to pass on her grandmother's traditional recipes to the next generation."
- **Adjective**: "The village retained its traditional charm despite modern developments."
