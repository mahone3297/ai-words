## Pronunciation
/tlænsleɪt/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The act of converting text, words, or speech from one language to another. **Example:** "The translator skillfully translated the complex sentence into Spanish."
2. The process of interpreting or expressing something in different terms or concepts. **Example:** "The translator had to find equivalent expressions for idiomatic phrases."

### Verb
1. To convert (words or text) from one language to another. **Example:** "She translated the novel from French to English."
2. To interpret or convey the meaning of something in a different context or medium. **Example:** "He translated his feelings into a beautiful piece of art."

## Phrases
- "Translate a text" means to convert written words from one language to another.
- "Translate this expression" asks for the equivalent meaning in another language.
- "Translate into sign language" refers to communicating without spoken words.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The translator's job is to ensure accurate translations between languages."
- **Verb**: "I need you to translate this email for our international clients."
- **Verb**: "After years of study, he can now translate ancient texts fluently."
