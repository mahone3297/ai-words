## Pronunciation
/ðɪs/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: 1. Referring to that one; this particular thing or person. For example: "This book is on sale today."
- **Determiner**: 2. Used to introduce a singular, non-specific noun, often indicating proximity or familiarity. For example: "This house is where we live."
- **Pronoun**: 3. Used to refer to something previously mentioned or understood, typically in a relative clause. For example: "The key to the door was on the table, which he took."

## Phrases
- "This way" indicates the direction to follow.
- "This one" is used to point out a specific item among others.
- "This is" is a common way to introduce or present something new.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "I love this time of year when the leaves change color."
- **Determiner**: "This is the most beautiful sunset I've ever seen."
- **Pronoun**: "She asked about the person I was with, whom I introduced as this old friend."

## Synonyms

- This here (informal)
