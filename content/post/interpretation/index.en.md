## Pronunciation
/ɪnˌtɜːrprɪˈteɪʃən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The act of explaining the meaning of something, especially a text, artwork, or sign; a particular explanation or translation. For example: "The interpreter's interpretation of the poem was quite insightful."
- **Noun**: A performance or rendition of a creative work such as a piece of music, a play, or a dance. For example: "Her interpretation of Beethoven's sonata was highly praised by critics."
- **Verb**: To explain the meaning of something, often by translating it from one language into another. For example: "She interpreted the French novel into English."
- **Verb**: To give a personal or artistic rendering of something, like a dramatic role or a musical score. For example: "The actor interpreted the character with great depth and emotion."

## Phrases
- "Literal interpretation": Taking words or actions in their most basic, exact sense without metaphorical or symbolic understanding.
- "Free interpretation": Rendering something with considerable freedom and individual expression, not bound strictly to the original text or score.
- "Legal interpretation": The process of explaining or construing the meaning of laws, contracts, or other legal instruments.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "His interpretation of the data suggested a different conclusion than what the team initially thought."
- **Verb**: "She interpreted the ancient hieroglyphics to unlock the secrets of the pharaohs' tombs."
- **Verb (artistic)**: "The ballet dancer interpreted the composer's intentions beautifully through her expressive movements."
