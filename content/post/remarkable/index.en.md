## Pronunciation
/rɪˈmɑːrkəbl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Characterized by being worthy of notice or attention due to its unusual quality, significance, or excellence. For example: "The view from the mountain was truly remarkable."
- **Adverb**: In an exceptional or outstanding manner; notably. For example: "She sang the song remarkably well."

## Phrases
- "Be remarkable" implies standing out in a positive way.
- "A remarkable achievement" highlights an extraordinary accomplishment.
- "To be remarkable at something" means to excel in a particular skill or field.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The artist's paintings were remarkable for their vivid colors and intricate details."
- **Adverb**: "She made a remarkable recovery from her illness."
- "His performance was remarkable considering his age."
