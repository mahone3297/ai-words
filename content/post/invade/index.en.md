## Word: Invade

## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈveɪd/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (1) To enter a country, region, or territory by military force with the aim of conquering or occupying it. For example: "The army invaded the neighboring country last week."
   - (2) To intrude into someone's life, privacy, or personal space in an unwelcome or intrusive manner. For example: "Her boss seemed to invade her private time with constant work emails."

### Phrases
- "Invade someone's privacy": To intrude on or violate someone's private matters without permission.
- "Invade a place": To enter and take control of a location forcefully or without invitation.
- "Invasion": The act or instance of invading, especially the entrance of an armed force into a territory to conquer or occupy it.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb (military sense)**: "In 1941, Germany invaded the Soviet Union during World War II."
- **Verb (intrusion sense)**: "His nosy neighbor had a habit of invading his peace and quiet every weekend with loud music."

## Synonyms
- Raid
- Occupy
- Conquer
- Intrude
- Penetrate
- Storm
- Invective (in the context of privacy invasion)
- Breach (as in breach of privacy)
