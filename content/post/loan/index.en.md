## Pronunciation
/loʊn/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A sum of money or other valuable asset given to someone for a period of time with the expectation that it will be repaid in full, usually with interest. For example: "She took out a student loan to pay for her education."
- **Noun (n)**: The act of lending something, especially money, to someone. Example: "The bank specializes in home loans."
- **Verb (v)**: To lend something, typically money, to someone temporarily. Example: "They have agreed to loan us the money to buy a new car."
- **Verb (v)**: To borrow something, typically money, from someone or a financial institution. Example: "He decided to loan the book to his friend for a week."

## Phrases
- "Loan shark": An informal lender who charges extremely high-interest rates.
- "Loan agreement": A legal document specifying the terms and conditions of a loan.
- "Payday loan": A short-term, high-interest loan intended to bridge the borrower's finances until their next payday.
  
## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The company secured a substantial business loan to expand its operations."
- **Verb**: "I can loan you my car while yours is in the shop."
- **Verb**: "She needed to loan some money to cover her rent until her paycheck arrived."
