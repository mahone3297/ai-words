## Pronunciation
/telɪskəʊp/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **telescope** (n) is a scientific instrument designed to observe distant objects by collecting light or other electromagnetic radiation, typically through an objective lens or mirror, and focusing it onto an eyepiece or detector. It allows users to magnify and study celestial bodies, terrestrial features, or even examine small details with great resolution.

### Verb
To **telescope** (v) can mean to use a telescope for observation or to compress or condense something in time, often metaphorically. For example, "He telescoped his vacation to fit it within a shorter period."

## Phrases
- "Observe through a telescope" describes the act of viewing distant objects with this instrument.
- "Telescopic view" refers to the extended or magnified perspective.
- "Telescope one's activities" implies doing multiple tasks simultaneously or efficiently.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The amateur astronomer spent hours gazing at the stars through his telescope."
- **Verb**: "After a long day at work, he telescoped his evening routine to catch the sunset."
- **Phrasal**: "The new satellite imagery provided a telescopic view of the city's infrastructure."
