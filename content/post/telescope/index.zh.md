## 读音
/ˈtɛlkəspɪs/

## 释义
- **名词 n**: 一种光学仪器，通过它可以观察远处的物体，使它们看起来更近。常用于天文观测。例如: "He used a telescope to observe the stars."
- **名词 n** (比喻): 指任何能扩展视野或洞察力的事物。例如: "Her new book is a literary telescope that magnifies the past."

## 短语
- "through the telescope" 通过望远镜看。
- "space telescope" 太空望远镜，如哈勃太空望远镜（Hubble Space Telescope）。
- "telescope into" （时间）压缩，紧凑。例如: "Their schedule telescoped all the activities into one day."

## 例句
- 名词: "The astronomer pointed the powerful telescope towards the moon."
- 比喻名词: "Her insights provided a telescope into the minds of historical figures."

## 同义词
- Binoculars (双筒望远镜)
- Microscope (显微镜) (在某些语境下，比喻意义的同义词)
- Optical instrument (光学仪器) (更广泛的类别，包括望远镜)
- Viewer (观察器) (在特定上下文中)
