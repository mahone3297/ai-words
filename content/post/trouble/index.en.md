## Pronunciation
/trʌbl/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **trouble**: 1. An issue, difficulty, or problem that causes difficulty or hardship. "She was having financial trouble paying her bills."
   - **Synonyms**: difficulty, adversity, hardship, issue, problem, predicament

2. A state of disturbance or disorder, often causing concern. "The area has been experiencing political trouble lately."

3. A person's act of causing annoyance or disruption. "His constant mischief caused no end of trouble."

### Verb
- **trouble**: 1. To cause difficulty or annoyance for someone. "Don't trouble yourself with it; I can handle it myself."
   - **Synonyms**: harass, vex, inconvenience, bother, disturb

2. To seek or engage in a conflict or dispute. "They were having trouble resolving their differences."

### Adjective
- **troublesome**: Causing problems or difficulty. "Her troublesome behavior disrupted the class."

### Adverb
- **troubled**: 1. Characterized by difficulty or worry. "He had a troubled expression on his face."
   - **Synonym**: distressed

2. Causing trouble or difficulty. "The project was troubled by numerous setbacks."

## Phrases
- "Get into trouble" means to encounter difficulties or consequences.
- "Ease someone's trouble" is to help alleviate their problems or difficulties.
- "Free from trouble" describes a peaceful or uneventful situation.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The company faced financial trouble due to the recession."
- **Verb**: "She tried to trouble the kids into doing their homework."
- **Adjective**: "The troubled teenager sought counseling to address his issues."
- **Adverb**: "His troubled past made it hard for him to trust people."
