## Word: Most

## Pronunciation
/məʊst/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Greatest in amount, extent, or degree; the majority of. _Example:_ "Most people prefer coffee over tea."
  - _Superlative form of 'many' or 'much'_ : "That's the most beautiful sunset I've ever seen."
- **Adverb**: To the greatest extent or degree; almost all. _Example:_ "She was most impressed by his performance."
  - _Used for emphasis_ : "Most certainly, I will attend the meeting."
- **Determiner**: The greater part or majority of a group or thing. _Example:_ "Most students passed the exam."

## Phrases
- "The most important thing" refers to something that holds the highest level of significance.
- "In most cases" implies that something happens or applies to nearly all situations.
- "At most" denotes the maximum number or quantity of something. _Example:_ "There were at most ten people present."

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "This book is the most interesting one I've read this year."
- **Adverb**: "Most likely, we'll have clear skies tomorrow."
- **Determiner**: "Most evenings, he spends time reading before bed."

## Synonyms
- Majority
- Greatest
- Mainly
- Primarily
- Chiefly
- Virtually (when used as an adverb)
- Almost (when used as an adverb)
