# Intention

## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈtenʃən/

## Definitions
### Noun (n)
- **A plan or course of action that a person intends to follow through**: The intention behind his actions was to help the community. _"She had every intention of finishing her degree."_
- **Purpose or aim**: His intention was to write a novel about his life experiences. _"The intention of this project is to raise awareness on environmental issues."_
- **Mental state of having a purpose**: She acted with good intentions but unfortunately caused some confusion. _"His intentions were pure, even if the outcome wasn't what he expected."_

### Verb (v)
- **To intend; to have in mind as a purpose or goal**: They intended to launch the product by next month. _"I'm intending to apply for that job."_
  
## Phrases
- **With the best intentions**: Meaning to have noble or well-meaning objectives, even if the results are not ideal. _"She meant it with the best intentions, but her advice ended up causing more problems."_
- **Intentions vs. actions**: This phrase suggests that one's actual behavior can differ from their intended plans or goals. _"His intentions were admirable, but his actions did not reflect them."_
  
## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Despite his initial intentions to stay for just a short visit, he ended up staying the whole summer."
- **Verb**: "The company intends to expand its operations globally within the next five years."
