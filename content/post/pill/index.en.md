## Pronunciation
/pɪl/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **pill** (countable) refers to:
1. A small, usually round, medicated tablet that is taken orally for treatment or relief. For example: "The doctor prescribed me some painkillers."
2. A small object or idea that seems easy to swallow but has a significant impact or side effect. For example: "The marketing strategy was a sweet pill to swallow, but it came with unexpected consequences."

### Verb
To **take a pill** means to consume a medicinal tablet or capsule. For example: "I need to take my vitamins before breakfast."
**Pill popping** is a colloquial term for frequently taking medication. For example: "He's been pill popping for weeks to manage his insomnia."

### Adjective
- **Pill-shaped**: Describing an object that resembles a pill in form. For example: "The USB drive had a sleek pill-shaped design."
- **Pillowy**: Soft and plump like a pill. For example: "The cushion was pillowy comfortable."

## Phrases
- "Take a pill" for consuming medication.
- "Morning pill" or "before-bed pill" to indicate a specific time of day for medication.
- "Pillbox" or "pill organizer" refers to a container for storing daily medications.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She swallowed two pills every day to manage her anxiety."
- **Verb**: "I need to take my allergy pill before going outside."
- **Adjective**: "The fluffy pillow was pillowy soft on the bed."
