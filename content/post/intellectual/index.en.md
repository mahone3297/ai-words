## Pronunciation
/ˌɪntəˈlektʃuəl/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A person possessing a highly developed intellect; a person who uses their mental capacities for abstract reasoning, critical thinking, and creativity, often in academic or artistic pursuits. Example: "She's an intellectual who has written several books on philosophy."
- **Adjective**: Relating to the intellect or intellectual activities, processes, or pursuits; characterized by a high degree of mental activity or intelligence. Example: "Intellectual curiosity drove him to read extensively on various subjects."

### Noun Usage Examples
- "The intellectuals gathered for a symposium to discuss contemporary art movements."
- "He was an influential intellectual whose work challenged traditional social norms."

### Adjective Usage Examples
- "Her intellectual abilities have earned her numerous accolades in the field of science."
- "An intellectual discussion on the nature of consciousness took place during the seminar."

## Phrases
- "Intellectual property": Refers to creations of the mind, such as inventions, literary and artistic works, designs, and symbols, names, images, and trademarks used in commerce.
- "Intellectual capital": The collective knowledge, skills, and experience possessed by individuals and organizations, which can be leveraged for economic gain or competitive advantage.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The university attracts intellectuals from all over the world to its faculty."
- **Adjective**: "She possesses an intellectual prowess that sets her apart from her peers."
