## Pronunciation
/praɪz/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 
  - A reward given for excellence, achievement, or competition. (e.g., "The winner received a cash prize.")
  - Something valued, especially a cherished possession. (e.g., "Her grandmother's jewelry was a prized heirloom.")

- **Verb**: To award or bestow a prize. (e.g., "The judges will prize the most innovative design.")
- **Adjective**: Valuable or esteemed. (e.g., "The prize-winning painting was displayed prominently.")

## Phrases
- "Claim the prize" means to receive or take the reward.
- "Win a prize" signifies achieving victory and receiving a reward.
- "Set a prize" or "offer a prize" are ways to motivate people to participate in a contest or challenge.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After months of hard work, he finally won the grand prize at the science fair."
- **Verb**: "The charity organized a raffle to prize donations for their cause."
- **Adjective**: "The prize-winning author's book sold millions of copies worldwide."
