## Pronunciation
/pʊʃ/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: 
  - **Noun**: A physical force exerted to move an object or person forward or upward. For example: "He pushed the cart towards the checkout."
  - **Transitive verb**: To apply force to something, causing it to move or change position. For example: "She pushed the door open."
  - **Intransitive verb**: To express a desire or intention, often with a connotation of urging or forcing. For example: "He pushed for a change in policy."
  - **Gerund**: Pushing, as in the act of pushing itself, e.g., "Pushing against the wall for leverage."

## Phrases
- "Push through": To continue despite obstacles or difficulties. For example: "She pushed through the crowd to get to the stage."
- "Push the envelope": To go beyond what is considered normal or acceptable. For example: "The artist was known for pushing the envelope with their avant-garde work."
- "Push your limits": To test and surpass one's capabilities. For example: "Athletes often push their limits to improve their performance."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The weight of the boxes was too much for him to push up the stairs."
- **Verb**: "He pushed the lever to start the machine."
- **Intransitive**: "She felt a strong urge to push for a new project."
