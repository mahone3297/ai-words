## Pronunciation
/ˈʃɜːrt/

## Definitions
### Noun
A shirt is a garment for men, women, and children, typically made of cloth, with a collar, sleeves, and a closed front, worn on the upper part of the body. It can be long-sleeved or short-sleeved, and made of various materials like cotton, linen, or synthetic fabrics.

### Verb
To shirt (informal) means to wear a shirt, especially as part of one's daily attire.

### Adjective
Shirt can also function as an adjective, describing something related to or resembling a shirt, such as a shirt collar or shirttail.

## Phrases
- "Wear a shirt" for indicating the act of putting on a shirt.
- "A shirt and tie" is a formal attire combination.
- "Button up your shirt" is an instruction to fasten the shirt buttons.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "He wore his favorite blue shirt to the interview."
- **Verb**: "It's cold outside, make sure to shirt up before leaving."
- **Adjective**: "The shirt collar was stiff from starch."
