## Pronunciation
/rɪˈpent/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: n. & v. 1. To feel or express remorse or contrition for past wrongs, sins, or mistakes; regret deeply. 2. To change one's behavior or attitude because of such remorse; reform.

## Phrases
- "Repent one's sins" means to acknowledge and seek forgiveness for past wrongdoings.
- "Turn over a new leaf" often implies a person has repented and decided to make significant changes.
- "Seek redemption" signifies a desire to atone for past actions through repentance.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb (to repent)**: "After years of addiction, he finally repented and sought help."
- **Verb (as a change in behavior)**: "She repented her impulsive decision and apologized to her friends."
- **Noun**: "The priest encouraged the penitent to engage in acts of charity as part of their repentance process."

## Synonyms
- Repentance
- Remorse
- Forgiveness-seeking
- Contrition
- Amendment
- Reformation

## Antonyms
- Denial
- Insistence
- Persistence
- Unrepentant
- Indifference

## Note: Repent can also be used as a noun to refer to the act of feeling remorse or the state of being sorry, but it's less common than its verb form.
