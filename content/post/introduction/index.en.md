## Word: Introduction

### Pronunciation
/ˌɪntrəˈdʌkʃən/

### Definitions
- **Noun**: (n) A formal statement that presents or explains something or someone new, often preceding a speech, book, performance, or the like. For example: "The author wrote a lengthy introduction to the novel."
  - Also refers to the act of introducing one person to another or to a group. Example: "The host made the introductions before the dinner party began."
  
- **Verb**: (v) The act of presenting or bringing something or someone into use or operation; to introduce something/someone to others. Example: "The professor introduced the topic by giving a brief history."

**Phrases**
- "Make an introduction" – To present one person to another, usually in a social or professional context.
- "Introduction to a subject" – A preliminary explanation or overview of a particular topic.
- "Self-introduction" – A brief statement about oneself when meeting new people or starting a presentation.

### Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "In her introduction, the speaker outlined the main points of her argument."
- **Verb**: "The company recently introduced a new line of eco-friendly products."
