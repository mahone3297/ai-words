## Pronunciation
/theərəˈθetɪkəl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Relating to or based on theory rather than practical application or observation. For example: "Theoretical physics explores concepts beyond what can be observed directly."
- **Noun**: A theoretical concept or framework. For example: "The course focused on theoretical economics."

## Phrases
- "Theoretical explanation" refers to a hypothesis or understanding without empirical evidence.
- "A theoretical approach" denotes a method that relies on principles rather than practical experience.
- "Theoretical knowledge" is the understanding gained through abstract thinking and reasoning.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "Theoretical models help scientists predict the behavior of complex systems."
- **Noun**: "Her research was based on a theoretical foundation of quantum mechanics."
- **Adjective (used as a noun)**: "The seminar discussed various theoretical frameworks for understanding human behavior."
