## 读音
/stæmp/

## 释义
- **名词**:
1. 邮票：A small adhesive label with a picture and value, used to show that the required fee for sending a letter or package has been paid. 例如: "She carefully affixed a stamp to the envelope."
2. 印章，戳记：An impression or device used to make a mark or design on a surface, often as a sign of authentication. 例如: "The company's official stamp was visible on every document."
3. 脚印，足迹：The mark left by something that has pressed into a surface. 例如: "There were footprints in the fresh snow."

- **动词**:
1. 盖章：To impress or attach a stamp onto something. 例如: "He stamped the letter with his signature seal."
2. 印记，铭记：To impress or imprint something firmly in the mind or memory. 例如: "The experience will forever stamp itself upon her heart."

## 短语
- "Stamp collection" 指集邮活动。
- "Stamp out" 意为彻底消除或根除。例如: "They are working to stamp out corruption in the government."
- "Put your stamp on something" 意为在某事物上留下个人特色或印记。例如: "She wanted to put her own stamp on the new project."

## 例句
- 名词: "The rare stamp was highly sought after by collectors."
- 动词: "The customs officer stamped our passports before we could enter the country."
