## Pronunciation
/səˈleɪər/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **sailor** is a person who works on a ship, typically as a member of the crew, and is trained in navigation, seamanship, and handling sails or other forms of propulsion. In naval contexts, it may also refer to a professional military personnel serving on a ship.

### Verb (transitive)
To **sail** is to navigate a ship using wind power or, metaphorically, to embark on a journey or career with confidence and determination.

### Adjective
Sailing can be used as an adjective to describe the activity or practice of using sails for propulsion, as in "sailing boats" or "sailing competitions."

## Phrases
- "Set sail" means to begin a voyage.
- "Sail the seven seas" is a metaphor for exploring various parts of life or the world.
- "A sailor's life" refers to the adventurous or challenging nature of a seafarer's existence.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The sailor navigated the stormy waters with skill and courage."
- **Verb**: "After months at sea, the sailors finally set sail for home."
- **Adjective**: "The sailing festival attracted enthusiasts from all over the country."

## Synonyms
- Seaman
- Marine
- Deckhand
- Oceanic voyager
- Nautical professional

## Antonyms
- Landlubber (a person not familiar with or skilled at sailing)
