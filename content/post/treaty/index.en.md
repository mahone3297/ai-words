## Pronunciation
/treɪtɪ/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **treaty** is a formal, written agreement between two or more nations, states, or parties, usually concerning matters of international law, politics, or commerce. It often establishes rules, obligations, or resolves disputes between the involved parties.

### Verb
To **treaty** can mean to negotiate, make arrangements, or enter into a treaty with another party.

## Phrases
- "Treaty of peace" signifies a formal agreement ending a conflict.
- "Treaty rights" refer to the privileges granted by a treaty.
- "Open negotiations" imply the process of entering into treaty talks.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The Treaty of Paris ended the Revolutionary War between France and America."
- **Verb**: "Both countries are currently treaty-bound to cooperate on climate change efforts."
- **Negotiating a treaty** means working on an agreement between nations.
