## Pronunciation
/ˈtuːl/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Tool**: A device or implement used for a specific purpose, often manual, to perform tasks or work on objects. For example: "The carpenter's toolbox contained various tools for his trade."
2. **Software Tool**: A program or application designed to help users accomplish a particular task, such as a word processor or graphic design software. For example: "Microsoft Excel is a popular spreadsheet tool for data analysis."

### Verb
1. To use or employ a tool for a specific purpose. For example: "He used a screwdriver to tighten the screws."
2. To provide or offer a tool for someone else's use. For example: "She provided her students with the necessary tools for their project."

### Adjective
Referring to something that facilitates a task or serves as a means to an end. For example: "The company's marketing strategy was a powerful tool for customer acquisition."

## Phrases
- "A tool for the job" signifies a suitable instrument or method for a particular task.
- "Using the right tool for the job" emphasizes selecting the appropriate instrument or approach.
- "A Swiss Army Knife is a multitool" highlights a versatile tool with many functions.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The plumber brought a variety of tools to fix the leak."
- **Verb**: "I need to find a better tool to remove the nails."
- **Adjective**: "Her writing skills were a valuable tool in her career."
