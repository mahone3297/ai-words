## Pronunciation
/ˌpæsɪˈkɒlədʒɪk/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Relating to the mind or mind processes, especially those affecting behavior and emotions. For example: "The psychological impact of trauma can be long-lasting."
- **Noun**: The branch of science that deals with the study of mental processes, behaviors, and emotions. For example: "She has a degree in psychology."
- **Adverb**: In a psychological manner or concerning the mind. For example: "He analyzed the situation psychologically."

## Phrases
- "Psychological factors" indicate elements influencing mental or emotional aspects.
- "Undergo psychological evaluation" refers to a professional assessment of one's mental health.
- "Cognitive psychology" focuses on mental processes like perception and memory.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "Her anxiety was rooted in deep-seated psychological issues."
- **Noun**: "The therapist specializes in treating psychological disorders."
- **Adverb**: "She tried to understand his motivations psychologically."
