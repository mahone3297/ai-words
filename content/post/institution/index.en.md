## Word: Institution

## Pronunciation
/ɪnstɪˈtjuːʃən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: (n) A social or organizational structure founded for a religious, educational, professional, or similar purpose. It can also refer to a practice, custom, or law that has become established and accepted within a society or community. Example: _"The university is one of the country's most respected institutions."_
- **Noun**: (n) A place where people are cared for or receive treatment due to illness, disability, or other needs. Example: _"She was admitted to a mental health institution for treatment."_
- **Noun**: (n) A long-established practice, convention, or custom. Example: _"Marriage is a social institution with deep historical roots."_

## Phrases
- "Institutional memory": The collective knowledge and experience held by an organization or group, especially when referring to how it affects decision-making and operations.
- "Educational institution": Schools, colleges, or universities where education is provided.
- "Financial institution": Banks, credit unions, or investment firms that deal with financial transactions.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After graduating from high school, he enrolled in a prestigious academic institution."
- **Noun**: "The government vowed to reform outdated institutions that no longer served their intended purposes."
- **Noun**: "His grandmother spent her final years in a well-respected care institution."
