## Pronunciation
/preɪˈfɜːrəbl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: More favorable or desirable than another option; preferred. For example: "The vegetarian meal was considered the more preferable choice."
- **Adverb**: In a more favorable or desirable manner. For example: "She handled the situation more preferably than her colleague."

## Phrases
- "Preferable to" indicates a comparison where one thing is better.
- "Be preferable for" suggests that something is a better fit or option.
- "His solution was clearly the more preferable."

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The quiet location was more preferable for studying."
- **Adverb**: "She chose the preferable method to solve the problem."
- "He found the apartment with a balcony to be more preferable overall."
