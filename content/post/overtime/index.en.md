## Word: Overtime

## Pronunciation
/ˈoʊvərˌtaɪm/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: Extra work hours beyond the regular schedule, usually with additional pay. For example: "Working overtime has become a routine for many employees during the busy season."
- **Adjective**: Relating to or occurring after the normal working hours. For example: "Overtime pay is calculated based on the number of hours worked beyond the standard 40-hour week."

## Phrases
- "Work overtime": To continue working beyond one's regular hours.
- "Overtime pay": Compensation given to employees for hours worked beyond their standard workweek, often at an increased rate.
- "In overtime": In sports, it refers to a period of play that extends beyond the scheduled time when the game ends in a tie; for instance, "The match went into overtime."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "To meet the deadline, he had to put in several hours of overtime last night."
- **Adjective**: "Employees are entitled to overtime compensation for any hours worked over 40 in a week."

## Synonyms
- Supplementary hours
- Extended hours
- Additional hours
- Beyond regular hours
- Unpaid overtime (when extra hours are worked without extra pay) 
- Time-and-a-half (referring to the common method of calculating overtime pay)
