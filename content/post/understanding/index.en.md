## Pronunciation
/ˌʌndərˈstændɪŋ/

## Definitions
### Noun
**Understanding**: The act or ability to comprehend or grasp the meaning, purpose, or intention of something, often including empathy and the capacity to share someone else's thoughts or feelings. For example: "Their deep understanding of each other's perspectives made their collaboration successful."

### Verb
**To understand**: To perceive or grasp the meaning, logic, or significance of a concept, idea, or situation. For example: "I'm trying to understand the complex math problem."

### Adjective
**Understandable**: Easy to comprehend or follow; clear. For example: "The instructions were written in an understandable manner."

## Phrases
- "Gain a better understanding" means to improve one's comprehension or knowledge.
- "Lack of understanding" refers to a deficiency in grasping a concept.
- "Mutual understanding" indicates shared comprehension between two or more parties.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After several discussions, they finally reached a mutual understanding."
- **Verb**: "I need to take some time to understand your perspective."
- **Adjective**: "The teacher explained the lesson in a way that made it easily understandable."
