## Pronunciation
/prəˈpɔːrt/
(pronounced as proh-port)

## Definitions
### Noun
A **portrait** is:
1. A painting, drawing, or photograph that represents a person, usually focusing on their face.
2. A likeness or depiction of someone's character or personality.
3. A formal or artistic representation of a subject, often capturing their essence or style.

### Verb
To **portray** is:
1. To depict or represent (a person, character, or situation) in art, literature, or film.
2. To present or illustrate (an idea or quality) through actions or description.

### Adjective
- **Portrait-style** describes something having the appearance or characteristics of a portrait, such as a photograph taken in a formal pose.
- **Portraiture** refers to the art of creating portraits.

## Phrases
- "Paint a portrait" means to create a visual representation of someone.
- "A family portrait" is a group photo showcasing family members.
- "Portraying a role" means acting out a character or position.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The artist spent months working on the portrait, striving for a realistic representation of his subject."
- **Verb**: "The author portrayed the main character as a complex and evolving figure throughout the novel."
- **Adjective**: "Her portrait-style photography captured her subjects with a unique and intimate touch."
