## Pronunciation
/səˈleɪri/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A regular payment made by an employer to an employee for work done, typically on a weekly or monthly basis. [**Etymology**: Late Middle English, from Old French saillier, from saillir, to leap, pay.]

## Phrases
- "Salary package" refers to the total compensation a person receives, including base salary and benefits.
- "Cost of living adjustment (COLA)" adjusts salaries based on inflation.
- "Minimum wage" is the lowest amount an employer can legally pay for a worker's hourly or annual salary.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "He negotiated a higher salary after demonstrating exceptional performance at work."
- **Verb**: "The company announced a raise for all employees, which boosted their annual salaries."
- "Her salary covers her basic expenses but leaves little for savings."

## Synonyms

- Compensation

## Antonyms

- Loss
- Deduction
