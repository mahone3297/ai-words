## Pronunciation
/tɪˈvɪzɪən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A device that receives and displays moving images and sound, typically through electronic signals transmitted by radio, cable, or satellite. Also used as a mass medium for broadcasting programs to a large audience.
- **Verb**: To watch or receive television programming. For example: "We usually watch television in the evening."
- **Noun**: The content or medium of television programming, including news, entertainment, and documentaries. For example: "Television has evolved significantly over the years."

## Phrases
- "Switch on the television" means to turn on the TV.
- "Cable television" refers to subscription-based TV service.
- "Live television" denotes a broadcast happening in real-time.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The entire family gathered around the television to watch the big game."
- **Verb**: "She tuned in to her favorite show after dinner."
- **Noun**: "The latest news was featured on the evening television broadcast."
