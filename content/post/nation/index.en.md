## Word: Nation

## Pronunciation
/ˈneɪʃən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A large group of people who share the same history, culture, language, descent, or territory, often under the same government and occupying a particular geographical area. It usually has its own distinctive identity and institutions. For example: "The United States is a nation built by immigrants from all over the world."

  - **Derived Terms**: National (adj.), Nationality (noun), Nationalism (noun)

**Example Sentences**
- _Noun_: "The entire nation came together to celebrate their independence day."
- "They aimed to build a strong, unified nation with equal opportunities for all citizens."

## Phrases
- "Nation-state": A political entity where the population is composed primarily of a single nation.
- "Nation-building": The process of constructing or strengthening the national identity and the institutions of a country.
- "One nation under God": A phrase often found in national pledges or anthems, symbolizing unity and shared values.
