## Word: Metric

## Pronunciation
/ˈmɛtrɪk/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A system or standard of measurement, especially the International System of Units (SI), which includes meters, kilograms, seconds, etc., used widely in science, industry, and everyday life. Example: "The metric system allows for easy conversion between units like kilometers and meters."
- **Noun**: A numerical measure that assesses or describes a particular characteristic, quality, or phenomenon. Example: "Customer satisfaction metrics are crucial for evaluating a company's performance."
- **Adjective**: Relating to measurement or metrics. Example: "The metric data showed a significant improvement in website traffic."

## Phrases
- "In metric terms" - Referring to measurements using the metric system.
- "Key performance indicators (KPIs)" - Also known as metrics, these are specific measures used to evaluate how effectively an organization is achieving its key business objectives.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (measurement system)**: "Canada adopted the metric system as its official system of measurement in the 1970s."
- **Noun (measure)**: "A vital metric for assessing the success of a social media campaign is engagement rate."
- **Adjective**: "The metric scale on the ruler helps scientists make precise measurements."
