## Pronunciation
/ˌelɪmənˈteri/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Relating to the basic or fundamental aspects of something; not complex or advanced. For example: "Elementary mathematics includes addition, subtraction, multiplication, and division."
  - _In education:_ Pertaining to the first few years of formal schooling, typically covering basic subjects. Example: "Elementary school prepares children for more advanced learning."
  - _In science:_ Relating to the simplest or most fundamental constituents of matter or energy. Example: "Elementary particles make up all known matter in the universe."

- **Noun (rarely used)**: The elements or rudiments of a subject; basics. Example: "The teacher started with the elementary of chemistry before moving on to complex theories."

## Phrases
- "Elementary, my dear Watson": A famous quote from Arthur Conan Doyle's Sherlock Holmes stories, used by Holmes when he has solved a mystery that seems simple but may have eluded others.
