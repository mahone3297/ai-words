## Word: Nap

## Pronunciation
/nap/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A short sleep, usually taken during the day. For example: "After lunch, he took a quick nap to refresh himself."
- **Verb**: (1) To sleep lightly or briefly, especially during the day. For example: "I think I'll just nap for a while before dinner." (2) To cover with a soft layer by rubbing, typically with a cloth, to produce a smooth or polished surface. For example: "She napped the fabric with velvet to give it a luxurious feel."

## Phrases
- "Power nap": A short sleep intended to quickly revitalize the sleeper.
- "Cat nap": A brief sleep, often referring to the short sleeps that cats take.
- "Take a nap": To have a short sleep, usually during the day.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The baby's afternoon nap allowed her parents some time to relax."
- **Verb (sleep)**: "After a long night shift, he decided to nap on the couch before heading to bed."
- **Verb (polish)**: "To maintain the shine, he regularly naps his shoes with a soft cloth."

## Synonyms
- **Noun (sleep)**: Doze, siesta, snooze
- **Verb (sleep)**: Doze off, catch some Zs, rest briefly
- **Verb (polish)**: Felt, brush, rub down, polish
