## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈsɪst/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To state firmly and persistently, especially in demanding something or asserting one's rights; to refuse to give up a point or claim despite opposition or resistance. For example: "He insisted on speaking to the manager."
  - _Insist upon_ (phrasal verb): To demand something strongly and persistently. E.g., "She insisted upon seeing the contract before signing it."
- **Noun**: The act of insisting or the thing insisted upon. Less commonly used. E.g., "Their insistence on perfection led to delays in the project."

## Phrases
- _Insist on something_: To be firm about needing or wanting something specific. E.g., "I must insist on a full refund."
- _Insist that_: To strongly maintain that something is true or necessary. E.g., "He insists that he was home all night."
  
## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The teacher insisted that all students turn off their phones during class."
- "Despite objections from her colleagues, she insisted on presenting her own proposal."
- **Noun**: "Her insistence on proper grammar irritated some of her peers, but it improved overall communication."
