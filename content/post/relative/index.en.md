## Pronunciation
/rɪˈlætɪv/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A relative is a person who is connected to another through blood ties, marriage, or adoption. Example: "My cousin is my first cousin, a relative on my mother's side."
- In a comparative context, it refers to something related or compared to another thing. Example: "The new car is more fuel-efficient than its relative model."

### Adjective
- Relative describes something that is in relation to something else. Example: "The climate here is relatively mild."
- It can also be used to indicate a degree of likeness or proximity. Example: "Her skills are relatively strong in comparison to her peers."

### Adverb
- Used to modify verbs, adjectives, or other adverbs, indicating a relationship to another entity or situation. Example: "She is relatively inexperienced in this field."

## Phrases
- "Be related to" means having a familial connection.
- "Relatives' houses" refers to the homes of family members.
- "Relative age" discusses someone's age in comparison to others.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "My relatives from out of town are arriving soon."
- **Adjective**: "The view from the top of the mountain is relatively stunning."
- **Adverb**: "She performed the task relatively well, given her lack of experience."
