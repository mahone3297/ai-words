## Word: Mechanics

## Pronunciation
/ˈmɛkənɪks/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The branch of science concerned with the properties of matter and energy and the relationships between them, especially concerning forces and motion. For example: "The course covered the basic principles of mechanics."
- **Noun (plural)**: People who repair or maintain machinery, vehicles, or other mechanical equipment. For instance: "The car mechanics fixed the engine problem quickly."
- **Noun**: The techniques or principles involved in operating something, especially a system or technique used in a particular field such as literature, music, or politics. Example: "The mechanics of writing a compelling story involve creating believable characters and an engaging plot."

## Phrases
- "Mechanics of the universe" refers to the laws governing the physical behavior of celestial bodies.
- "Get down to the mechanics" means to deal with the practical details or procedures of something.
- "Know the ins and outs of mechanics" implies having a thorough understanding of how something works.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (science)**: "Newton's laws of mechanics form the foundation of classical physics."
- **Noun (people)**: "She took her car to the local mechanics for its annual service."
- **Noun (principles)**: "Understanding the mechanics of marketing can help boost sales."

## Synonyms
