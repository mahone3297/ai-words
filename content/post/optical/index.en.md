## Word: Optical

## Pronunciation
/ˈɑːptɪkl/

## Definitions
### Noun (optical)
- Relating to sight or vision; pertaining to the eyes or the sense of sight. Example: "The patient required optical assistance due to his deteriorating eyesight."
- Referring to devices or instruments designed to aid or improve vision, such as glasses, lenses, or telescopes. Example: "She purchased new optical equipment for her astronomical observations."

### Adjective (optical)
- Concerning light or the properties of light, especially its behavior and interactions with matter. Example: "Optical fibers transmit data by using pulses of light."
- Of or relating to optics, the branch of physics that deals with the properties and behavior of light. Example: "His research focuses on the optical properties of different materials."

## Phrases
- "Optical illusion": A visually perceived image that differs from reality, often caused by the visual system's misinterpretation of what it receives.
- "Optical device": A tool or instrument that works with or manipulates light, such as microscopes, cameras, or binoculars.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The store specializes in selling a variety of optical frames for prescription glasses."
- **Adjective**: "The advanced camera has superior optical zoom capabilities."

- Visual
- Ocular
- Optic
- Luminous (in the context of light-related properties)
