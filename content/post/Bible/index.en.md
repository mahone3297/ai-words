## Pronunciation
/bəˈbaɪl/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Bible** (countable): The sacred book of Christianity, containing the Old and New Testaments, considered by Christians to be the word of God as revealed through the prophets and Jesus Christ.
   - Example: "The Bible is the foundation of many religious teachings."
2. **Holy Bible**: An alternative term for the Christian scriptures, often referring to the King James Version.
   - Example: "The Holy Bible is a source of spiritual guidance for millions."

### Proper Noun
1. **Bible**: Refers to specific translations or editions, e.g., the New International Version or the Revised Standard Version.
   - Example: "The NIV Bible has become popular worldwide."

### Verb
1. **To study the Bible**: The act of reading, interpreting, or examining its contents for religious or educational purposes.
   - Example: "Many devout Christians spend hours each day studying the Bible."
2. **To quote the Bible**: To refer to a passage or verse from the text.
   - Example: "She often quotes the Bible to support her arguments."

## Phrases
- "Read the Bible" means to engage in religious study or personal devotion.
- "Scriptures of the Bible" refers to the religious texts it contains.
- "Biblical reference" denotes a quotation or allusion from the Bible in a conversation or writing.
