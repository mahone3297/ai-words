## Word: Limitation

## Pronunciation
/ˌlɪmɪˈteɪʃən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A restriction on the size, amount, or number of something; a confining or bounding condition. For example: "There's a time limitation on the exam."
- **Noun (legal context)**: A provision or rule that restricts or regulates some activity or right. For example: "The new law places limitations on freedom of speech."
- **Verb**: The act of limiting something or the state of being limited. For example: "This policy aims at the limitation of carbon emissions."

## Phrases
- "Within the limitations": Within the set boundaries or restrictions.
- "Push the limitations": To try to exceed or surpass existing boundaries.
- "Legal limitations": Refers to the legal restrictions or constraints imposed by laws or regulations.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Due to financial limitations, we cannot afford to expand the project."
- **Legal Noun**: "The court upheld the validity of those contractual limitations."
- **Verb**: "The company has been working tirelessly to limitation its environmental impact."
