## Pronunciation
/ˈpleʒə(r)/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A feeling of great satisfaction or happiness derived from an enjoyable activity or experience. "The beauty of nature provided her with immense pleasure."
2. A source of enjoyment; something that brings delight. "Reading a good book is one of life's simple pleasures."

### Verb
1. To derive or experience pleasure from something. "She took great pleasure in cooking for her family."
2. To express pleasure, often informally. "He thanked me with a hearty pleasure."

### Adjective
1. Giving or expressing pleasure or satisfaction. "Their company was a pleasure to be around."

### Adverb
1. In a manner that is pleasurable or delightful. "We spent the evening in great pleasure."

## Phrases
- "Seeking pleasure" implies a pursuit of enjoyable activities.
- "Take pleasure in" means to derive satisfaction from.
- "In the pleasure of the moment" refers to enjoying the present.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Listening to music was his greatest pleasure during his free time."
- **Verb**: "She took pleasure in watching the sunset on the beach."
- **Adjective**: "The dinner party was a pleasure for all the guests."
- **Adverb**: "They danced with great pleasure at the wedding reception."
