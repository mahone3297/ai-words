## Pronunciation
/təˈwɔrdz/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: Directed or moving in the direction of; towards. For example: "She walked toward the store."
- **Preposition**: Expressing motion or direction towards something or someone. For example: "She headed toward the sunset."
- **Verb**: To move or aim in the direction of; proceed towards. For example: "He turned toward the sound of music."
- **Adjective**: Relating to or in the direction of; approaching. For example: "The wind was blowing toward the shore."

## Phrases
- "Move toward a goal" implies progression or progress towards a target.
- "Face toward the sun" suggests positioning oneself to receive its warmth.
- "His thoughts drifted toward the future" indicates a mental focus on what's coming.

## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "The storm clouds were gathering darkly, threatening to rain down from the sky."
- **Preposition**: "She sat down on the bench, looking toward the mountains in the distance."
- **Verb**: "He decided to walk toward the library to return a book."
- **Adjective**: "As the day wore on, her mood grew increasingly optimistic toward the project."
