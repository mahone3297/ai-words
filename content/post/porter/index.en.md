## Pronunciation
/prəˈpɔːtə(r)/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A person who carries luggage, especially at a hotel or station.
   - A porter at the airport helps with passengers' bags.
2. A waiter or server who brings food and drinks to tables in a restaurant.
3. (figurative) A person who does menial or unskilled work.
   - The porter was responsible for maintaining the cleanliness of the building.

### Verb
1. To carry luggage or other items for someone.
   - The porter loaded the luggage onto the train.
2. To serve as a porter or waiter at a location.
   - He decided to porters at a local café during his spare time.

## Phrases
- "porter assistance" refers to help provided by a porter
- "porter's lodge" is the entrance area of a building where porters typically work
- "porter service" denotes the act of providing porter services

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The bellhop greeted us with a friendly porter's smile."
- **Verb**: "After a long flight, the porter swiftly unloaded our baggage."
- **Porter's Role**: "As a porter, he was known for his efficiency and strong back."

## Synonyms
- Bagman
- Bellhop
- Attendant

- Loader

## Antonyms
- None (since a porter is a specific job role rather than a general term with opposite meaning)
