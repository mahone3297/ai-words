## Pronunciation
/rɪˈvər/

## Definitions
### Noun
A river is a large natural watercourse flowing towards an ocean, sea, lake, or another river. It often forms a boundary between regions or serves as a source of water, transportation, and habitat for various organisms.

### Verb
To river (as in "rivered through") can mean to flow through a river or move along its course.

### Adjective
1. Relating to a river or its features: "The riverbank was lined with willow trees."
2. Following a river's path: "The hikers decided to follow the river downstream."

## Phrases
- "Travel down the river" implies going along the river's course.
- "Live by the river, die by the river" suggests a connection to one's environment or fate.
- "A river runs through it" often symbolizes a central theme or connection in a story.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The Amazon River is the largest in the world by volume."
- **Verb**: "The boat glided gently along the river, admiring the scenery."
- **Adjective**: "The riverbank was ablaze with wildflowers during the spring."
