## Pronunciation
/rɪˌspɒnsəˈbɪləti/

## Definitions
### Noun
**Responsibility**: The state or fact of being responsible for something; the obligation to deal with or take care of something. 

**Etymology**: From Old French responsabilité, derived from Latin responsibilis (answerable), from respondere (to answer).

### Verb
**To assume responsibility**: To take on the duty or obligation for something. 

**To逃避 responsibility**: To avoid accountability for one's actions or decisions.

### Adjective
**Responsible**: Having the ability or willingness to be accountable for one's actions or decisions.

**Accountable**: Being responsible for the consequences of one's actions.

## Phrases
- "Take responsibility for" means to assume the duty or ownership of something.
- "Shift the responsibility" implies transferring the obligation to another person or entity.
- "Act responsibly" refers to behaving with care and consideration for consequences.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Each team member has a clear sense of responsibility for their project's success."
- **Verb**: "He was forced to take responsibility for the accident."
- **Adjective**: "The manager expects all employees to be responsible for their work."
