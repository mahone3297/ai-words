## Pronunciation
/taɪprɪtər/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **A machine for typing characters on paper, typically having keys that correspond to letters, numbers, and punctuation marks.**
   - Example: "The old-fashioned typewriter was replaced by a computer keyboard."
2. **An operator of such a machine, especially one skilled in using it proficiently.**
   - Example: "She was a talented typewriter, able to type at a rapid pace."

### Verb
1. **To type text using a typewriter or similar device.**
   - Example: "He spent hours typing out a letter on his antique typewriter."
2. **To produce written work with a typewriter or computer.**
   - Example: "The journalist typed up a draft of the article before submitting it."

## Phrases
- "Typing on a typewriter" refers to the act of producing text using the machine.
- "Retyping a document" means reproducing a text on a typewriter.
- "Learning to type on a manual typewriter" implies acquiring the skill of operating the device.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The typewriter keys were worn from years of constant use."
- **Verb**: "She finished her thesis by typing each page on her trusty typewriter."
- **Action**: "He started a new chapter by typing the words 'Once upon a time' on the typewriter."

## Synonyms
- Typewriter operator
- Composer (of text)
- Keyboardist (informal)
- Operator (in the context of typing)
- Dater (on a vintage typewriter, referring to the act of signing a document)
