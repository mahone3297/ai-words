## Pronunciation
/ˈriːdər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**:
    - A person who reads written material, such as books, newspapers, or screens. For example: "The avid reader spends hours every day with a novel."
    - A device or software that processes text and displays it, often for visual consumption. For example: "A Kindle is a popular e-reader for book lovers."

- **Verb**:
    - To look at and understand the meaning of written words. For example: "She reads the instructions carefully before using the new appliance."
    - To interpret or comprehend data from a screen, like a computer or phone. For example: "The app reads the QR code and pulls up relevant information."

## Phrases
- "To be a voracious reader" implies having a strong appetite for reading.
- "Reading comprehension" refers to the ability to understand written text.
- "Skimming a reader" means quickly scanning through content for key points.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She's a lifelong reader who devours books on various topics."
- **Verb**: "He read the email several times to ensure he understood its message."
- **As a verb in a passive construction**: "The news was read by millions of people online."
