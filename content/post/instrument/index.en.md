## Pronunciation
/ˈɪn.strə.mənt/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A tool, device, or piece of equipment designed for a specific purpose, especially one used in scientific research, music, or surgery. For example: "The musician played her favorite instrument, the violin."
- **Noun (n)**: A means by which something is achieved, done, or expressed; a method or agency. For instance: "Education is an instrument for social change."
- **Verb (v)**: To equip or supply with instruments or tools. Example: "The laboratory was well-instrumented for advanced experiments."
- **Verb (v)**: To use something as a means to achieve an end. Example: "She instrumented her plan effectively to secure the promotion."

## Phrases
- "Musical instrument": An object used to create musical sounds, e.g., piano, guitar.
- "Instrument of torture": A device historically used to inflict pain or punishment.
- "Instrumental in": Playing a crucial role in a process or event. E.g., "His research was instrumental in the discovery of the new chemical compound."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (tool/device)**: "The doctor picked up the surgical instrument and began the operation."
- **Noun (method/agency)**: "Legislation can be an effective instrument in reducing poverty."
- **Verb**: "They have instrumented the spacecraft with several high-tech sensors for deep space exploration."
