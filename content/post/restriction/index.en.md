## Pronunciation
/rɪˈstrɪkʃn/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **restriction**: A rule or limitation that prevents something from happening or being done freely. For example: "The airport has strict security restrictions."
- **Constraint**: Something that restricts or controls a person's actions or choices. For example: "Her financial constraints limited her options for travel."
- **Barrier**: A hindrance or obstacle that prevents movement or access. For example: "The language barrier made communication difficult."

### Verb
- **to restrict**: To limit or confine something to a certain scope or condition. For example: "The company restricted employee access to sensitive data."
- **to curb**: To control or lessen the extent of something, often negatively. For example: "The government is trying to curb air pollution."

## Phrases
- "Freedom from restriction" implies liberation from limitations.
- "Imposed restrictions" indicates limitations imposed by others.
- "Lifting restrictions" signifies removing previously imposed limitations.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The new policy imposes stricter restrictions on public gatherings."
- **Verb**: "The government is gradually lifting COVID-19 restrictions as cases decline."
- **Adjective**: "His creativity was restricted by his strict upbringing."
