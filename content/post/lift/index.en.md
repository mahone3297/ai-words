## Pronunciation
/lɪft/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A device for raising or lowering people or objects to different levels, typically consisting of a platform or compartment that moves vertically in a shaft. For example: "They took the lift to the top floor."
- **Verb**:
  - **Transitive Verb**: To raise or move something upwards using physical force or mechanical means. For example: "He lifted the heavy box onto the shelf."
  - **Intransitive Verb**: To rise or move upwards. For example: "The hot air balloon began to lift off the ground."
  - **Informal Verb**: To improve or enhance something; to make better. For example: "The renovation has really lifted the look of the house."

## Phrases
- "Give someone a lift": To offer someone a ride in a vehicle, especially when they are going in the same direction. For example: "Can you give me a lift to the station?"
- "Lift one's spirits": To make someone feel happier or more optimistic. For example: "Listening to music always lifts my spirits."
- "Lift weights": To exercise with weights to build muscle strength. For example: "He goes to the gym every day to lift weights."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The maintenance team was called to fix the broken lift in the office building."
- **Verb (Transitive)**: "She carefully lifted the fragile vase from the table."
- **Verb (Intransitive)**: "As the sun rose, the mist gradually lifted from the valley."
- **Verb (Informal)**: "A promotion at work really lifted her morale."
