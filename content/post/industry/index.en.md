## Word: Industry

## Pronunciation
/ˈɪndəstrē/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The production of goods or services within an economy, typically on a large scale involving many processes and organized effort. It can also refer to a specific sector or branch of such activity, e.g., the automobile industry or the entertainment industry. _Example_: "The technology industry has seen rapid growth in recent years."
- **Noun (figurative)**: The quality or state of being diligent, hardworking, and efficient in one's work or pursuits. _Example_: "She demonstrated remarkable industry in completing her project ahead of schedule."

### Phrases
- "Heavy industry": Refers to industries that involve large-scale manufacturing or resource extraction, often with high capital intensity.
- "Light industry": Relatively less capital-intensive industries, usually producing consumer goods or involving fewer raw materials.
- "Industry standards": Norms, guidelines, or protocols widely accepted and adhered to within a particular business or industrial sector.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (economy)**: "The government implemented policies to boost the competitiveness of domestic industries."
- **Noun (figurative)**: "His tireless industry was the key to his success in the field."
