## Word: Northwest

## Pronunciation
/ˌnɔːθˈwest/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Situated in, moving toward, or facing the direction between north and west; the compass point that is one-quarter of the way around from due north, at 315°. Example: "The house is located to the northwest of the city center."
- **Noun**: The direction or point on the compass lying halfway between north and west. Example: "They sailed towards the northwest in search of new lands."

### Phrase
- **Northwest passage**: A hypothetical sea route through the Arctic Ocean, along the northern coast of North America, connecting the Atlantic and Pacific Oceans.
- **In the northwest corner**: Referring to a location situated in the area where the two sides meet at the northwest point, e.g., "The old lighthouse stands in the northwest corner of the island."

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The strong winds blew in from the northwest."
- **Noun**: "The plane took off and headed in a northeasterly direction towards the opposite side of the country."

## Synonyms
- Nor'west (informal)
- N.W. (abbreviation)
- Upper left quadrant (geographical reference) 

Note: While 'northwest' primarily functions as an adjective and noun describing direction, it can also be used metaphorically to describe a region or area in the corresponding direction relative to a specific location. For instance, "the northwest region of the United States."
