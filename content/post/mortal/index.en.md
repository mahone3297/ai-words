# Mortal

## Pronunciation
/ˈmɔːrtl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Relating to or characteristic of human beings as having limited duration of life; subject to death. _Example:_ "Mortal beings are destined to age and eventually pass away."
  - _Also used metaphorically_ to describe something with deadly consequences or capable of causing death: "The venom of this snake is highly mortal."
  
- **Noun**: A human being considered as subject to death; a person. _Example:_ "All mortals must face their mortality someday."

### Phrases
- "Mortal enemy": An archenemy or one with whom one has irreconcilable differences, often suggesting a conflict that could lead to death.
- "Mortal sin": In religious contexts, it refers to a gravely sinful act that can lead to damnation if not absolved before death.
- "For all intents and purposes": This idiomatic expression suggests something is effectively true, even though it may not be literally so; originally from the fuller form "for all intents, constructions, and purposes," emphasizing the permanence of something despite its potential impermanence due to human mortality.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "Fearing his own mortality, he began to appreciate every moment of life."
- **Noun**: "The gods looked down upon the mortals with a mixture of envy and pity."

## Synonyms
- Human (as a noun)
- Temporal (adjective, referring to human life)
- Earthly (adjective, referring to humans or things pertaining to human existence)
- Deadly (adjective, when referring to something capable of causing death)
- Finite (adjective, describing the limited lifespan of humans)
