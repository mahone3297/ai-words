## Pronunciation
/ˈfeləʊ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: (n) 1. A man or boy; a male person. e.g., "The young fellow was helping his mother with groceries." 2. A person of the same kind or group; a comrade or associate. e.g., "They were all good fellows and got along well." 3. (formal or old-fashioned) A member of a learned society or profession. e.g., "He's a fellow of the Royal Society."
- **Adjective**: (adj) 1. Of the same kind or group; belonging together. e.g., "We're fellow students at the same university." 2. Used to express camaraderie or shared experience. e.g., "Fellow travelers on this journey through life."

## Phrases
- "Fellow traveler": Someone who shares similar views or is on the same journey or course of action.
- "Fellows of a college": Members of a collegiate institution who hold a specific status or rank.
- "One of the lads/fellows": A friendly way to refer to a man as part of a group.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "He was elected as a fellow of the prestigious academy."
- **Adjective**: "We're all fellow humans trying to make sense of this world."
