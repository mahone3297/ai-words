## Pronunciation
/taɪˈfuːn/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A large tropical cyclone that forms over the western Pacific Ocean, characterized by strong winds, heavy rainfall, and potential for devastating damage. (Example: "The typhoon brought widespread destruction to the region.")
- **Adjective**: Referring to a strong tropical storm with similar characteristics to a hurricane. (Example: "The typhoon-force winds were fierce.")

## Phrases
- "Be hit by a typhoon" implies experiencing a severe storm.
- "Prepare for a typhoon" means taking precautions before a storm arrives.
- "Typhoon season" refers to the time of year when these storms are most common.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The residents were advised to evacuate as the typhoon approached their coastal city."
- **Adjective**: "The typhoon warning triggered panic among the tourists visiting the island."
- **None**: Since "typhoon" is primarily a noun, it does not have a direct verb form in this context.

## Synonyms
- Hurricane (mainly in American English)
- Cyclone (primarily in Australia and South Asia)
- Tornado (distinctly different, referring to a twister)
- Storm (less specific, can refer to any intense weather event)
