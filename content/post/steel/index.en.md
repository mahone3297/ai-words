## Pronunciation
/stiːl/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Steel**: A strong, hard, metallic alloy, primarily composed of iron with carbon, used extensively in construction, machinery, and manufacturing. For example: "The bridge was built with high-quality steel."
- **Steel industry**: The sector that produces and processes steel. For example: "The decline in the steel industry led to job losses."

### Verb (Transitive)
- To manufacture or produce steel. For example: "The factory produces steel sheets for construction."
- To reinforce or strengthen with steel. For example: "They steel-reinforced the foundation to ensure stability."

### Adjective
- Robust or durable, often implying strength or resilience. For example: "The steel door is very sturdy."
- Made of steel. For example: "The stainless steel sink is corrosion-resistant."

### Adverb
- In a manner resembling steel; firmly or resolutely. For example: "He stood steel-cold in the face of adversity."

## Phrases
- "Steel oneself": To prepare mentally or emotionally for a difficult situation.
- "Carbon steel": A type of steel containing less than 2% carbon.
- "High-strength steel": A steel with enhanced tensile strength for heavy-duty applications.
