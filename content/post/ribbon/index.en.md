## Pronunciation
/ˈrɪbiːn/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. A long, thin strip of material, usually with a decorative pattern or color, used for tying around objects, attaching tags, or as part of a gift, ornament, or decoration. 2. (computing) A type of data structure used to store and manipulate text or binary data.

- **Verb**: To attach a ribbon to something, often as a decoration or to mark a specific area.

## Phrases
- "Tie a ribbon around it" signifies decorating or finishing an object with a ribbon.
- "A ribbon of color" describes a continuous line or band of a particular hue.
- "Award ribbon" is a common term for a medal or prize attached with a ribbon.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: She tied a bow with a red ribbon on the present for her friend's birthday.
- **Verb**: The florist adorned the bouquet with a delicate blue ribbon.
- **Adjective**: The nursery was filled with rows of colorful ribbon-wrapped plants.
