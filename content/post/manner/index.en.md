## Pronunciation
/ˈmænər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A way in which something is done, conducted, or performed; a mode of action, behavior, or conduct. For example: "She did it in a polite manner."
  - Also refers to a person's habits, bearing, or way of conducting themselves: "His gentlemanly manner impressed everyone."
- **Noun (archaic)**: A way of acting or behaving that conforms to socially accepted norms or etiquette: "They were taught good manners from a young age."

## Phrases
- "In a manner of speaking": Indicating that what is said should not be taken too literally. For example: "In a manner of speaking, he was right."
- "Mannerisms": The distinctive ways in which a person behaves or moves, especially if these are considered to be peculiar or affected.
- "Good manners": Refers to polite social behavior and conduct.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She completed the task with great efficiency and professionalism, showing her impeccable work manner."
- **Noun (behavior)**: "His quiet and unassuming manner made him popular among his colleagues."
- **Noun (archaic)**: "Their upbringing ensured they displayed the utmost courtesy and good manners at all times."
