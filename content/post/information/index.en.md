## Word: Information

## Pronunciation
/ˌɪnfərˈmeɪʃən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: Data, facts, or details about events, ideas, or people that can be communicated or learned. It's often processed, structured, or presented in a meaningful context.

### Examples:
- **Noun**: "The information he provided was crucial to solving the case."
- "She gathered all the necessary information before making her decision."
- "The internet has become the primary source of information for many people."

## Phrases
- "Access to information": The ability or right to obtain or view data or knowledge.
- "Information technology (IT)": The use of computers and telecommunications equipment to store, retrieve, transmit, and manipulate data.
- "Reliable information": Data that can be trusted and verified.
- "Informed consent": Agreement to something based on full disclosure of relevant facts and understanding.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The company's database stores vast amounts of customer information."
- "Based on the latest information from the weather service, we decided to cancel the outdoor event."
- "A library is a treasure trove of information on countless subjects."
