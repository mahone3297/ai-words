## Pronunciation
/moʊmənt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A brief period of time; a minute portion of time. For example: "Wait for a moment while I fetch my coat."
  - Also, a particular point in time regarded as being critical or significant. Example: "This is a defining moment in history."
  - Additionally, it can refer to a specific experience or happening, especially one that is highly emotional or meaningful. Example: "That moment when she graduated was the proudest of her life."

- **Adjective**: Occurring or coming immediately before or after something else; imminent. Example: "The momentary pause before he answered revealed his uncertainty."

## Phrases
- "At this moment": Referring to the present time. Example: "At this moment, we have all the necessary resources to start the project."
- "Moment of truth": A critical or decisive point, often where the truth of a situation becomes clear. Example: "The moment of truth arrives when the results are announced."
- "In a moment": Soon, shortly, or quickly. Example: "I'll be with you in a moment."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She cherished every moment spent with her family."
- **Adjective**: "There was a momentary lapse in concentration during the game."
