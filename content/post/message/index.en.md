## Pronunciation
/ˈmɛsɪdʒ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A verbal, written, or recorded communication sent from one person or group to another; information conveyed. For example: "She received an important message on her phone."
  - _Idiomatically_, it can also refer to the content or meaning behind something expressed indirectly: "The message of the film is about perseverance."
  
- **Verb**: (transitive) To convey information, thoughts, or feelings through a message. For example: "She messaged him to confirm the meeting time."
  - _In modern context_, it often refers to sending a digital message via text, email, or social media: "I'll message you the details later."

## Phrases
- "Get the message" means to understand or grasp the intended meaning or implications.
- "Message board" refers to an online platform where users can post messages and communicate with each other.
- "Leave a message" suggests recording a voice or text message when the recipient is unavailable.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Please write down the message before you forget it."
- **Verb**: "She decided to message her friend about the exciting news."
- **Idiomatic usage**: "His silence was a clear message that he wasn't interested in the proposal."
