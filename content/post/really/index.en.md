## Pronunciation
/rɪˈliːli/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: Denotes emphasis or degree of truthfulness. For example: "I really love this book."
- **Adverb**: In a true or absolute sense; without exception. For example: "She really did her best."

## Phrases
- "Really?" can be used to express disbelief or seek confirmation.
- "It's really cold today" emphasizes the severity of the cold weather.
- "That's really impressive" shows admiration.

## Example Sentences
- **Emphasis**: "I really need to finish this project by tonight."
- **Degree of truth**: "She really believes she can win the marathon."
- **Absolute sense**: "They really haven't seen each other in years."

## Synonyms
- Truly
- Honestly
- Absolutely
- Definitely
- Actually

## Antonyms
- Not really
- Sort of
- Kind of (when used to downplay something)
