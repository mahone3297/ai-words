## Pronunciation
/rɪˈlɛktn/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Unwilling or hesitant to do something; slow to take action or make a decision. For example: "He was reluctant to accept the invitation."
- **Verb**: To feel or act unwilling to do something;不愿意. For example: "She reluctantly agreed to help."
- **Adverb**: Reluctantly, meaning with hesitation or unwillingness. For example: "He reluctantly stepped forward to speak."

## Phrases
- "Reluctant acceptance" implies a forced agreement.
- "A reluctant hero" describes someone who isn't initially willing but ends up stepping up.
- "Reluctantly agreeing" indicates a lack of enthusiasm.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "Despite her initial reluctance, she eventually joined the team."
- **Verb**: "He reluctantly handed over his wallet when asked."
- **Adverb**: "She reluctantly opened the box, revealing its contents."
