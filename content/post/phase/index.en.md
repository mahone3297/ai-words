## Pronunciation
/feɪz/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Astronomy**: A period during which the Moon or a planet has a particular appearance from Earth, marked by its position relative to the Sun and stars.
   - Example: "The moon is in the first quarter phase."
- **Physics**: A part of a cycle or series of events, often in reference to a repeating pattern or development.
   - Example: "The water cycle goes through several phases: evaporation, condensation, and precipitation."
- **Technology**: In the context of computer science, a specific stage in the development or lifecycle of a software application.
   - Example: "The project is currently in the alpha testing phase."

### Verb
- To go through a series of stages or steps.
   - Example: "The project will phase out the old system over the next few months."
- To be in a particular stage or condition.
   - Example: "The company is phasing in new equipment to improve efficiency."

## Phrases
- "In its final phase"
- "Phase transition"
- "Phase out" (to gradually replace something with a newer version)
- "Phase in" (to introduce something gradually)

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "During the full moon phase, the nocturnal animals become more active."
- **Verb**: "The manufacturing process is now phasing in automated machines."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "The government is planning to phase out fossil fuel subsidies."
