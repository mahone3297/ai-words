## Pronunciation
/ˌmæɡ.nəˈtɪk/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Relating to or having the properties of a magnet; able to attract iron or steel: "The magnetic force between two magnets."
  - _Example Sentence_: "The child was fascinated by the magnetic pull of the refrigerator magnets."
  
- **Noun**: The force that attracts iron and other metals, or the ability of a material to produce such a force: "The earth's magnetic field."
  - _Example Sentence_: "The compass needle aligns with the Earth's magnetic north pole."
  
- **Noun**: (informal) A charismatic quality that attracts people: "Her personality had a magnetic charm."
  - _Example Sentence_: "The new CEO possesses a magnetic presence that inspires confidence."

## Phrases
- "Magnetic field": An area around a magnet or electric current within which magnetic forces can be detected.
- "Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI)": A medical imaging technique using magnetic fields to create detailed images of internal body structures.
- "Magnetic attraction": A strong emotional or physical draw between two people or things.

## Example Sentences
- _Adjective_: "The ship's navigational system relies on a highly accurate magnetic sensor."
- _Noun (force)_ : "Scientists discovered that certain rocks have a weak magnetic polarity."
- _Noun (quality)_ : "His magnetic energy filled the auditorium during his performance."
