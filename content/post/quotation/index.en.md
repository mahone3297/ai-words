## Pronunciation
/ˈkwaɪ.ə.tən/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **quotation** is
1. The act of quoting or reproducing a piece of text, speech, or expression by someone else, often as evidence or support for an argument.
2. A passage from a written work that is excerpted and presented as a separate unit, typically attributed to its author.
3. A collection of such passages, often compiled by an editor.
4. A price offered for goods or services, especially one that is higher than the usual or expected rate.

### Verb
To **quote** is to
1. Express the exact words or meaning of something said or written by another person.
2. Supply a price or estimate based on the current market conditions or specifications.
3. Indicate the value or significance of something by mentioning it or referring to it.

### Adjective
* Quotable: Containing material suitable for being quoted or remembered.

### Adverb
* Quotably: In a manner that can be quoted or cited.

## Phrases
- "Quote someone" means to repeat their words verbatim or summarize them.
- "Cite a quotation" is to refer to a quote in an academic or written work.
- "Price quote" is a formal offer for a product or service with specific terms.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She included several famous quotes in her essay to support her argument."
- **Verb**: "The auctioneer quoted a high price for the rare painting."
- **Adjective**: "His speeches were filled with quotable lines that inspired many."
