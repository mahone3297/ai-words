## Definitions
### Noun
- **Zero**: A numerical digit representing the absence of quantity or the starting point in counting. In mathematics, it is often denoted by the symbol 0. For example: "The sum of two and zero equals two."
- **Zero gravity**: The condition where gravitational forces appear to be negligible, as experienced in space. For example: "In space, astronauts float due to zero gravity."
- **Zero tolerance**: A policy that imposes immediate consequences for any violation, without exception. For example: "The school has a zero-tolerance policy towards bullying."

### Verb
- **To zero in**: To focus on something or adjust something to a precise point, often in aiming or targeting. For example: "The archer zeroed in on the bullseye."
- **Set the zero**: To calibrate a device or instrument to a baseline or starting point. For example: "I need to set the oven temperature to zero before baking."

### Adjective
- **Zero out**: To cancel or nullify something completely. For example: "The account was zeroed out after all debts were paid."
- **Zero-based budgeting**: A financial management approach that starts each budget period with no assumed funds, requiring justification for every expense. For example: "The company uses a zero-based budgeting system."

## Phrases
- "Start from zero" implies beginning anew or without prior knowledge.
- "Zero-sum game" describes a situation where one person's gain comes at the expense of another.
- "Going for zero waste" is a commitment to minimizing waste production.
