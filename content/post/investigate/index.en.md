## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈvɛstɪɡeɪt/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To conduct a formal inquiry or systematic examination to discover facts or information about something, often by research or questioning. For example: "The detective investigated the crime scene thoroughly."
- **Noun**: (n) The act of investigating; an investigation. For example: "The investigation was thorough and led to the discovery of new evidence."

## Phrases
- "Investigate a matter": To delve deeply into a subject to uncover the truth.
- "Under investigation": Describes something that is currently being examined or researched.
- "To carry out an investigation": To perform a systematic search for information or answers.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The police are investigating the theft at the museum."
- **Noun**: "After a long and complex investigation, the team finally found the source of the problem."
