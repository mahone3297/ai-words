## Word: Job

## Pronunciation
/dʒɑːb/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A paid position of regular employment; a profession, occupation, or trade. For example: "She found a new job as a software engineer."
  - Also used informally to refer to a task or piece of work, e.g., "Painting the house was quite a job!"
- **Verb**: (informal) To criticize or scold someone severely. For example: "The boss really jobbed him for being late again."
- **Noun** (archaic): A piece of work done or to be done; a task or project. For example: "The carpenter's job was to build a cabinet."

## Phrases
- "On the job": Engaged in one's work or duties.
- "Job hunting": The process of seeking employment.
- "A tough job": Refers to a difficult or demanding task or situation.
- "Do the job": To accomplish a task effectively.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (employment)**: "After graduating from college, he landed his dream job at a top tech firm."
- **Verb (criticize)**: "His poor performance on the field got him jobbed by the coach during halftime."
- **Noun (task)**: "Fixing the leaky faucet was a messy job, but it had to be done."
