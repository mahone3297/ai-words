## Pronunciation
/acˈkwɪr/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To obtain or gain possession of something, usually through effort or purchase. For example: "She acquired a new skill through practice."
- **Noun**: The act of acquiring; acquisition. For example: "The company's acquisitions have expanded their market share."
- **Adjective**: Characterizing something that has been obtained or gained. For example: "The acquired knowledge was crucial for her success."

## Phrases
- "Acquire a skill" means to learn or develop a particular ability.
- "Acquire property" refers to buying or gaining ownership of real estate.
- "Acquired taste" describes something initially unappealing that becomes enjoyable after repeated exposure.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "After months of study, he acquired a deep understanding of the subject."
- **Noun**: "The business acquired several smaller companies to bolster its operations."
- **Adjective**: "Her acquired expertise in marketing proved invaluable to the team."
