## Pronunciation
/səˈsteɪn/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Sustain**: (noun) The ability to maintain or support something over time; endurance or resilience. For example: "The forest relies on a sustainable management plan."
- **Maintenance**: (noun) The act of keeping something in good condition or repair, typically over a period of time. For example: "Regular maintenance is crucial for a car's longevity."

### Verb
- **To sustain**: (verb) To keep up, support, or endure; to maintain or continue. For example: "The economy is sustained by strong consumer spending."
- **To sustain oneself**: (verb) To provide enough energy or resources to carry on. For example: "She sustains her passion for painting through daily practice."

### Adjective
- **Sustainable**: (adjective) Capable of being maintained at a certain level without being depleted or causing harm to the environment. For example: "A sustainable lifestyle reduces carbon emissions."

## Phrases
- "Sustain a tradition" means to keep a custom or practice alive.
- "Sustainable agriculture" refers to farming methods that don't deplete natural resources.
- "Sustain the fire" is an idiom meaning to keep something going, like a conversation or enthusiasm.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The company's success is largely sustained by its innovative products."
- **Verb**: "She knew she had to sustain her energy levels during the marathon."
- **Adjective**: "The city aims to achieve carbon neutrality through sustainable practices."
