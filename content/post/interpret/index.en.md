## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈtɜːrpriːt/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To explain or tell the meaning of something, especially by translating from one language into another or by analyzing and explaining the significance of events, actions, or symbols. For example: "She interpreted the dream as a sign of good luck."
  - In law: To construe or explain the meaning of a statute, contract, or other legal document.
  - In art or performance: To present or perform a creative work such as a piece of music, a play, or a dance, according to one's understanding or personal expression.
  - "He interpreted the classical music piece with great sensitivity."

- **Noun**: (n) A particular explanation or understanding of something; a translation or rendition. For instance: "There are many different interpretations of this poem."

## Phrases
- "To put one's own interpretation on something" means to give a personal or unique explanation or meaning to it.
- "Linguistic interpretation" refers to the act of translating spoken or written words from one language to another.
- "Legal interpretation" involves the process of construing and applying the meaning of laws.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The interpreter interpreted the foreign minister's speech fluently into English."
- **Noun**: "Her interpretation of the character was so vivid that it brought tears to our eyes."
