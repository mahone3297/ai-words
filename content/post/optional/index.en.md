## Word: Optional

## Pronunciation
/ˈɑːpʃənl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Not necessary; left to personal choice or preference. It describes something that can be chosen but is not required.

### Examples
- _**Optional activity**: Attending the after-class workshop is optional for students._
- _**Optional feature**: The premium package includes several optional features like heated seats and a sunroof._

## Phrases
- **Optional extras**: Additional items or services that can be chosen by a customer but are not included in the standard price.
- **Optional course**: A class or subject that a student may choose to take but is not required for their degree or program.
- **Make something optional**: To allow people to decide whether they want to do something or include it, rather than mandating it.

## Example Sentences
- _The company offers flexible working hours, with Fridays being an optional work-from-home day._
- _In the survey, participants could answer the last question, which was optional._

## Synonyms
- Elective
- Voluntary
- Non-compulsory
- Discretionary
- Unobligatory
