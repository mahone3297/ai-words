## Pronunciation
/rɪˈtʃ/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The act or ability to stretch out one's hand or arm to touch or grasp something, especially to attain or get something: *She reached for the book on the top shelf.*
2. The extent or degree to which something extends or can be extended: *His voice had a reach that filled the entire auditorium.*

### Verb
1. To stretch or extend oneself physically to touch or grasp something: *He reached across the table to pick up the fork.*
2. To attain or come within reach of something: *Her goal is to reach financial independence by 30.*
3. To communicate or connect with someone: *She tried to reach her friend on the phone but couldn't get through.*

### Adjective
1. Extending a certain distance: *The armrests on the long reach desk.*
2. Achievable or accessible: *This task is within reach for most students.*

## Phrases
- "Reach out" implies making contact or offering help.
- "Reach a consensus" means to agree on a decision.
- "Reach a conclusion" means to arrive at a final understanding or opinion.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "He couldn't reach the cookie jar on the high shelf."
- **Verb**: "I need you to reach the light switch for me."
- **Adjective**: "The athlete's long reach allowed them to touch the bar during the pole vault."
