## Pronunciation
/tɪpɪkˈæl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Characteristic of a particular type or group; representing or exhibiting the usual or normal features of something. For example: "The weather here is typically mild during spring."
- **Adverb**: In a typical manner or way; usually or generally. For example: "She studies diligently, typically spending hours in the library each day."

## Phrases
- "Typical response" refers to a common or expected reaction.
- "Atypical behavior" denotes something that is not normal or usual for someone or something.
- "Not so typical" suggests something that deviates from what is expected.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The company's typical office layout includes an open-plan area and private offices."
- **Adverb**: "It's typical for her to arrive early to meetings."
- "Her injury was atypical for someone of her age."
