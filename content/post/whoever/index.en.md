## Pronunciation
/hoʊˈevər/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Referring to anyone, without specifying who; uncertain or unknown. For example: "The instructions apply to whoever is responsible."
- **Pronoun**: Used to refer to an unknown or unspecified person, typically as the subject of a clause. For example: "Whoever left the door open should close it."

## Phrases
- "Anyone who" introduces a relative clause that specifies a group of people.
- "Whichever one" can be used as a synonym for "whoever" when referring to options.
- "No matter who" implies that the action applies regardless of the identity of the person.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "I'll leave the key under the doormat for whoever needs it."
- **Pronoun**: "Whoever wins the prize will receive a cash reward."
