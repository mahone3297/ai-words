## Pronunciation
/ˈdʒʊri/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A group of people selected to hear evidence and make a decision in a legal case, either civil or criminal. The decision they reach is usually referred to as a verdict. _Example_: "The jury deliberated for three days before reaching a unanimous decision."

  - _Also_ (informal): A body of individuals who judge or evaluate something, often used metaphorically. _Example_: "A jury of our peers will decide the winner of the art competition."

## Phrases
- "Jury duty": The legal obligation to serve on a jury in a court of law.
- "Jury trial": A legal proceeding where the facts of the case are decided by a jury.
- "A hung jury": When the members of a jury cannot reach a unanimous decision, leading to a mistrial.
- "Twelve men and women of the jury": A traditional phrase referring to the number and gender composition of jurors in some jurisdictions.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After listening to all the testimony, the jury found the defendant guilty."
- "In the US, every citizen may be called upon for jury duty."
- "Their design was judged by a jury of industry experts."
