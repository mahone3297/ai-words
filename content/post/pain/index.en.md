## Word: Pain

## Pronunciation
/peɪn/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: An unpleasant physical sensation caused by illness or injury. For example: "She felt a sharp pain in her side after the fall."
- **Noun (figurative)**: Emotional distress or suffering. For example: "The loss of his wife brought him great pain."
- **Verb**: To cause physical or emotional distress to someone or something. For example: "The accident left him badly pained and unable to walk."
- **Verb (archaic)**: To feel pain; experience discomfort. For example: "She pained to hear about his misfortune."

## Phrases
- "Physical pain" refers to discomfort felt in the body due to injury or illness.
- "Emotional pain" refers to mental or psychological distress.
- "Painkiller" is a medication that alleviates pain.
- "In pain" describes the state of experiencing pain. Example: "She was clearly in pain after the surgery."
- "To be a pain in the neck (or backside)" is an idiomatic expression meaning someone or something is causing annoyance or irritation.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (physical)**: "The doctor prescribed medicine for his persistent headache and joint pain."
- **Noun (emotional)**: "The thought of leaving her hometown brought her immense pain."
- **Verb**: "The harsh words pained her deeply."
- **Verb (archaic)**: "He pained himself to complete the marathon despite his injured leg."
