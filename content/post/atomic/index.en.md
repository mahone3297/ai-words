## Pronunciation
/əˈtɒmɪk/

## Definitions
### Noun
An atomic refers to:
1. The smallest unit of a chemical element, consisting of a nucleus surrounded by electrons, constituting the basic building block of matter.
2. (Physics) A fundamental particle that cannot be divided or destroyed, forming the basis of all matter.

### Verb
To atomic can mean:
1. To have the nature or properties of something atomic; to be fundamental or elemental.
2. To break down into atomic components or processes.

### Adjective
Atomic describes something that is:
1. Fundamental or basic, like an atomic component.
2. Of or relating to atoms or atomic processes.

### Adverb
Atomically refers to:
1. In an atomic manner, on a fundamental level.
2. Extremely or completely.

## Phrases
- "Atomic structure" denotes the arrangement of protons, neutrons, and electrons in an atom.
- "Atomic force microscopy" is a technique used to observe atomic-scale features.
- "Atomic clock" is an extremely precise timekeeping device based on atomic vibrations.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The periodic table arranges elements according to their atomic numbers."
- **Verb**: "The scientist's research involved atomic-level analysis of molecules."
- **Adjective**: "The material has an atomic bond, making it incredibly strong."
- **Adverb**: "He performed the task atomically, without missing a step."
