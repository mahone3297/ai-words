## Pronunciation
/rɪˈzɪstənt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A noun referring to** resistance: The ability to withstand or oppose something, typically force, pressure, or change. For example: "The new material is highly resistant to water damage."
- **A noun denoting** immunity or tolerance: The state of being immune to a disease or unaffected by a particular condition. For example: "The bacteria have developed antibiotic resistance."

### Verb
- **To resist**: To withstand or oppose forcefully, typically an action or influence. For example: "She was able to resist the temptation to eat sweets."
- **To be resistant**: To not easily give in or comply with a suggestion or demand. For example: "The child is quite resistant to learning a new skill."

## Phrases
- "Resistant to": Expressing the ability to withstand or be unaffected by something. For example: "The plants are resistant to pests."
- "Chemical resistance": The ability to withstand certain chemicals. For example: "This paint is known for its chemical resistance."
- "Psychological resistance": The act of opposing or resisting change in thoughts or behavior. For example: "He showed strong psychological resistance to therapy."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The bacteria's antibiotic resistance made treatment challenging."
- **Verb**: "The gardener was careful to cultivate plants resistant to drought."
- **Adjective**: "The soldiers were trained to show physical and mental resistance in battle."
