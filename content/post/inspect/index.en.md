## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈspɛkt/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To examine or scrutinize something carefully, often for a specific purpose or to discover any flaws, issues, or details. For example: "The inspector came to inspect the building's safety features."
- **Noun**: (n) The act of examining something closely or formally; a thorough examination or inspection. For example: "The annual vehicle inspection ensures that all cars on the road meet safety standards."

## Phrases
- "Inspect something for something": To look over something thoroughly with the intention of finding or verifying a particular aspect. Example: "They inspected the house for signs of damage after the storm."
- "Under inspection": Being examined or checked. Example: "The factory is currently under inspection by health and safety officials."
- "To pass/fail inspection": To be approved or disapproved after being inspected. Example: "The restaurant failed the health inspection due to unsanitary conditions."

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The customs officer meticulously inspected each package entering the country."
- **Noun**: "After the home inspection, the couple decided to negotiate a lower price due to necessary repairs."
