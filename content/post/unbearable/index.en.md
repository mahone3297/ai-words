## Pronunciation
/unˈbærəbl/

## Definitions
### Noun
**Unbearable** is an adjective that describes something that is extremely difficult to bear, tolerate, or endure. It implies a level of intensity that is almost intolerable.

### Verb
The verb form, "to make/unbearable," refers to causing extreme discomfort or suffering.

### Adjective
1. **Extreme discomfort**: The noise was unbearable, causing everyone to cover their ears.
2. **Intolerable condition**: Living in such poverty was an unbearable situation for many families.

## Phrases
- "Bearable/unbearable circumstances"
- "Enduring/unbearable pain"
- "An unbearable workload"

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: The heat was unbearable on that hot summer day.
- **Verb**: His constant complaining made the atmosphere unbearable.
- **Adjective**: She couldn't stand the unbearable silence in the room.
