## Word: Library

## Pronunciation
/ˈlaɪbrəri/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A building or room containing a collection of books, periodicals, and sometimes films and music for use by the public or the members of an institution. For example: "She spent her afternoons studying in the quiet atmosphere of the library."
- **Noun (informal)**: The collection of books or other materials itself, regardless of where it is housed. For example: "His personal library includes rare first editions and signed copies."
- **Noun (computing)**: A collection of precompiled software routines that a program can use. For example: "The new software update adds several useful libraries for data processing."

## Phrases
- "Visit the library" means to go to a library to read, study, or borrow books.
- "Check out a book from the library" refers to borrowing a book to read outside the library premises.
- "Return books to the library" means giving back borrowed items within their due date.
- "In the stacks" refers to the area in a library where the books are physically stored.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (traditional)**: "The local library hosts weekly storytime sessions for children."
- **Noun (computing)**: "The developer integrated the machine learning library into the application for enhanced functionality."
