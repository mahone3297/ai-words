## Definitions
### Noun
- **A swan** is a large, long-necked waterfowl belonging to the family Anatidae, known for its graceful appearance and distinctive white feathers, often with a black head. Example: "The swan gracefully glided across the lake."

### Verb (transitive)
- To **swan** can refer to imitating the movement or behavior of a swan, usually in a non-serious or showy manner. Example: "The child swanned around the playground, pretending to be a swan."

### Verb (intransitive)
- In British English, "swan" can also mean to spend time idly or without purpose. Example: "He often spends his weekends swanning around the town square."

## Phrases
- "Swan song" is a term for a final performance or effort, especially before retirement or death.
- "To go like a swan" is a metaphor for leaving gracefully or dying with dignity.
