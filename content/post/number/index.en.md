## Word: Number

## Pronunciation
/ˈnʌmbər/

## Definitions
- **Noun (num)**: A mathematical value used to represent a quantity, often expressed in digits; for example, "The number 7 is considered lucky by many." It can also refer to an amount or total count, such as "A number of people attended the concert."
- **Noun**: A symbol, word, or group of words designating a specific thing, person, or concept, as in "Her phone number is 555-1234."
- **Verb (v)**: To assign a number or numerical value to something, like "Number the pages in the report consecutively."
- **Verb**: To count or include as part of a total, "We numbered the cars passing by on the highway."
- **Adjective (adj)**: Relating to numbers or quantities, "Mathematics is her favorite subject because she loves working with numbers."

## Phrases
- "By the numbers" means to follow instructions precisely or sequentially.
- "In number" refers to the quantity of something.
- "A magic number" refers to a significant or optimal figure in a particular context.
- "To pick up the tab (or check) for the whole number" means to pay the entire bill.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (mathematical value)**: "Add the two numbers together to get the sum."
- **Noun (designation)**: "Please provide your passport number at the check-in counter."
- **Verb**: "Each contestant was numbered according to their starting position."
- **Adjective**: "There's been a marked increase in the number of students enrolling this year."
