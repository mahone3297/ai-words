## Pronunciation
/oʊl/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. Soil is the upper layer of Earth's surface that supports plant growth, consisting of mineral particles, organic matter, water, and air. It is a mixture of rock fragments, dead plant material, and microorganisms that provide nutrients for plants.
   - Definition: A fertile layer of earth containing nutrients for plant life.
   - Example: The garden's rich soil attracted a variety of plants.

2. (informal) A general term for the ground or earth, especially in a particular area or region.
   - Example: The soil in this area is prone to erosion.

### Verb
1. To prepare or enrich the soil for planting by adding fertilizers or turning it over.
   - Example: Farmers till the soil before sowing seeds.

2. To apply or introduce something to the ground, typically with a view to cultivation or improvement.
   - Example: They scattered seeds in the soil around the tree.

### Adjective
1. Characteristic of the ground or earth, especially in terms of quality or fertility.
   - Example: A soil-rich forest floor.

## Phrases
- "Improve soil quality" means to enhance the fertility or structure of the soil.
- "Test the soil pH" is a process to determine the acidity or alkalinity of the soil.
- "Soil erosion" refers to the wearing away of the topsoil due to wind or water.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: The farmer tilled the soil to loosen it for planting.
- **Verb**: She planned to add compost to improve the soil in her backyard garden.
- **Adjective**: The fertile soil in the valley supported lush vegetation.
