## Word: French

## Pronunciation
/frentʃ/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Relating to France, its people, or their language. For example: "She's fluent in French."
  - Also used to describe things that originate from or are characteristic of France, such as "French cuisine" or "French fashion".
- **Noun**: The Romance language spoken in France and in many other countries, especially as an official or second language. For example: "She teaches French at the high school."
- **Noun (plural form)**: People from France collectively. For example: "The French are known for their love of wine and cheese."

## Phrases
- "Parlez-vous français?" translates to "Do you speak French?" and is a common phrase when asking if someone speaks the French language.
- "À la française" means "in the French manner" or "the French way", used to describe something done according to French customs or styles.
- "French leave" is a colloquial expression meaning to depart without permission or notice.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "He adopted a French accent when reciting Baudelaire's poetry."
- **Noun**: "Learning French grammar was challenging but rewarding."
- **Noun (collective)**: "The French Revolution marked a significant period in European history."

## Synonyms
- Gallic (adjective referring to things related to France)
- Francophone (pertaining to the French language or speakers)
- French-speaking (as an adjective describing people or places where French is spoken)

Note: Depending on the context, no direct synonyms may exist for "French" when it refers specifically to the nationality or language. However, words like "Francophile" can be used to describe someone who admires or has an affinity for French culture.
