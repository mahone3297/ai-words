## Pronunciation
/rɪˈkɔːrd/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A **record** is a document, piece of information, or item that serves as evidence or documentation, especially one that is kept for reference or historical purposes.
   - Example: "The school keeps a record of students' grades."
2. A **personal best or high score** in a competition or activity.
   - Example: "She set a new record in the 100-meter race."
3. An instance of something being recorded, such as a music album or audio file.
   - Example: "The band's latest record has topped the charts."

### Verb
1. To **record** means to write down, register, or note information.
   - Example: "The journalist recorded the details of the interview."
2. To create a sound recording, usually on a device like a phonograph or digital audio recorder.
   - Example: "They recorded a live concert for a future release."

## Phrases
- "Set a new record" signifies achieving the best result or score in a competition.
- "Keep a record of" implies maintaining documentation or a history of something.
- "Recorded history" refers to the written or documented past.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The scientist meticulously recorded all her observations in the lab notebook."
- **Verb**: "The sports commentator described how the athlete broke the world record."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "He decided to record his thoughts before forgetting them."
