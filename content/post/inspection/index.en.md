## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈspekʃən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: (n) The act of inspecting something carefully, typically for evaluating its quality, condition, or conformity with regulations. Example: "The house underwent a thorough inspection before it was put on the market."
- **Noun**: (n) An official visitation or examination, often by an authority figure or professional. Example: "The school inspection will assess the educational standards and facilities."
- **Noun**: (n) A formal or periodic review or assessment, such as a military inspection or a vehicle inspection. Example: "The annual safety inspection of the factory revealed several issues that needed addressing."

## Phrases
- "Pass inspection": to meet the required standards after being inspected. Example: "The restaurant failed to pass the health inspection due to unsanitary conditions."
- "Under inspection": currently being examined or evaluated. Example: "The suspect's home is under inspection by the police."
- "Subject to inspection": open to or awaiting careful examination or scrutiny. Example: "All bags are subject to inspection before boarding the plane."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "A building inspector conducted a meticulous inspection of the property for any signs of structural damage."
- "The food inspector arrived unannounced for a routine inspection of the kitchen."
- "After the fire, the damaged bridge underwent a comprehensive inspection to determine if it was safe to use again."
