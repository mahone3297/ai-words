## Definitions
- **Noun**: A vast body of salt water that covers most of the earth's surface. It's interconnected with smaller bodies called seas and often contains marine life and natural resources. Example: "The deep blue ocean stretches out to the horizon."
  - _Oceanography_ (noun): The branch of science that deals with the physical, chemical, and biological aspects of the ocean.
  - _Ocean liner_ (noun): A large passenger ship designed for long-distance travel across oceans.
  
  Example sentences:
  - _Noun_: "The Titanic was a luxurious ocean liner that tragically sank in the North Atlantic Ocean."
  - _Derived from 'ocean'_ (adjective): "Oceanic currents play a significant role in regulating global climate."

## Phrases
- "Across the ocean": Refers to traveling a great distance, usually internationally. Example: "She moved across the ocean to start a new life in Australia."
- "To sail the seven seas/oceans": This idiomatic expression denotes extensive travel or adventure. Example: "As a young man, he dreamed of sailing the seven oceans."
- "The ocean floor": Refers to the bottom of the ocean, where various geological features and living organisms exist.
