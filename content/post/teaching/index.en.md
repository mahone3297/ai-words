## Pronunciation
/ˈtiːtʃɪŋ/

## Definitions
### Noun
**Teaching**: The act or profession of imparting knowledge, skills, or values to others, usually in an educational setting. For example: "She has a passion for teaching children."
- **Method**: A particular way of teaching, such as direct instruction or inquiry-based learning.
- **Curriculum**: The content or course of study taught in a school or educational program.

### Verb
**To teach**: To give instruction or guidance to someone, helping them learn or acquire knowledge. For example: "The teacher patiently taught the students how to solve complex problems."
- **Coach**: To guide or mentor someone in a specific skill or activity, often sports-related.
- **Instruct**: To give clear directions or explanations to help someone understand or perform.

### Adjective
**Teachable**: Capable of being taught or open to learning. For example: "The young boy was eager to learn and proved himself very teachable."
- **Pedagogical**: Relating to the theory or practice of teaching.

## Phrases
- "Teach a subject" means to instruct in a specific academic area.
- "Teach by example" suggests showing rather than telling through one's actions.
- "Teaching materials" encompasses textbooks, lesson plans, and other resources used in the teaching process.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Her teaching style inspired a love for learning in her students."
- **Verb**: "The experienced teacher taught her students how to think critically."
- **Adjective**: "The student demonstrated excellent teachable qualities under his guidance."
