## Pronunciation
/ˈʃɔːrtɪdʒ/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **shortage** refers to a lack or insufficiency of something, often material goods, resources, or personnel, when there is not enough to meet the demand or requirements. 

### Verb
To **experience a shortage** is to have a deficiency or run low on something, as in a supply or resource.

### Adjective
- **Shortage-prone** describes something that is prone to experiencing a lack or deficiency.
- **In short supply** means there is not enough of a particular item available.

## Phrases
- "Experience a shortage of" denotes the state of having inadequate supplies.
- "Mitigate the shortage" refers to finding ways to alleviate or reduce a lack.
- "Avoiding shortages" is the act of preventing or managing a lack of resources.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The region is currently facing a water shortage due to drought."
- **Verb**: "Due to the pandemic, many businesses experienced a shortage of essential workers."
- **Adjective**: "The company had to ration its food supply because it was in short supply."
