## 读音
/traɪfl/

## 释义
- **名词**: 不重要的事物；琐碎的事；少量。例如: "That's just a trifle, not worth worrying about."
- **动词**: 轻视；对待…不认真。例如: "He trifled with her feelings by not taking their relationship seriously."
- **形容词**: 微不足道的；琐碎的。例如: "She has a trifle of work left to finish."

## 短语
- "a mere trifle" 意味着极其微小或不重要的东西。
- "trifle with someone's affections" 指玩弄某人的感情。

## 例句
- 名词: "The disagreement was a trifle compared to the main issue they needed to address."
- 动词: "Don't trifle with your health; see a doctor if you're feeling unwell."
- 形容词: "His concerns were trivial, just a trifle in the grand scheme of things."

## 同义词
- insignificant
- petty
- insignificant detail
- slight
- negligible
