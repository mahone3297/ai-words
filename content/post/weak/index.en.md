## Pronunciation
/ˈwɛk/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Lacking strength or power; feeble; fragile. For example: "The old man had a weak constitution."
- **Adverb**: In a weak manner; poorly. For example: "She lifted the heavy box with a weak effort."
- **Verb**: To be less strong or powerful than usual; to be susceptible to disease or infection. For example: "His immune system is weak, so he catches colds easily."

## Phrases
- "A weak signal" indicates a low power or strength in communication.
- "Weakness in the knees" describes feeling unsteady or faint.
- "A weak link" refers to a person or component that is vulnerable or prone to failure.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "Her arms were too weak to lift the weight."
