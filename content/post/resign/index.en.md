## Pronunciation
/rɪˈzaɪn/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A resignation, especially from a position or post.
- The act of giving up or stepping down from a job or office.
- The written notice of such a decision.

### Verb
- To give up one's job or position voluntarily.
- To resign oneself to a situation or outcome.
- To cease actively participating or supporting something.

## Phrases
- "Resign from a position" means to officially leave a job.
- "Resignation letter" is a formal document announcing one's intention to leave a job.
- "Resign oneself to" implies accepting a situation with disappointment or acceptance.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After months of internal conflict, the CEO submitted his resignation."
- **Verb**: "She decided to resign from her stressful job to pursue a more fulfilling career."
- **To resign oneself to**: "He resigned himself to the fact that he would have to work overtime this weekend."

## Synonyms
- Quit
- Resignation
- Surrender
- Relinquish
- Step down

## Antonyms
- Continue
- Stay
- Persist
- Maintain

## Usage
- *Noun*: The board accepted the director's resignation with regret.
- *Verb*: After much contemplation, he finally resigned himself to his fate.
- *Adjective*: She felt a sense of relief after making the difficult decision to resign.
