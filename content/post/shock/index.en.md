## Pronunciation
/ʃɒk/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Shock**: 1. A sudden and intense feeling of surprise, horror, or grief. "The news of her death came as a shocking blow."
   - 2. An electric current that causes a person to feel a sudden jolt or lose consciousness. "The device was designed to administer a mild electrical shock for medical purposes."
   - 3. A physiological response to injury or extreme stress, characterized by increased heart rate, blood pressure, and metabolic activity. "He recovered quickly from the physical shock."

### Verb
- **Shock**: 1. To cause surprise, shock, or amazement in someone. "Her revelation shocked the entire audience."
   - 2. To stun or overwhelm with a sudden change or event. "The sudden loss of her job left her completely shocked."
   - 3. (Medicine) To treat with an electric shock, especially to revive a person who has stopped breathing. "The paramedics shocked the patient to restore his heartbeat."

### Adjective
- **Shocking**: 1. Causing great surprise or horror; morally or socially unacceptable. "The graphic violence in the film was quite shocking."
   - 2. Intense or severe. "The cold shock of the water woke him up immediately."

### Adverb
- **Shockingly**: In a shocking manner; unexpectedly. "The results of the election were shockingly unexpected."

## Phrases
- "Receive a shock" means to be surprised or startled by something.
- "A rude awakening" can describe a sudden, shocking realization or situation.
- "In a state of shock" refers to being overwhelmed by a traumatic event.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The discovery of the stolen artifacts came as a shocking revelation."
- **Verb**: "His sudden departure shocked his friends and family."
- **Adjective**: "The severity of her injuries was shocking to witness."
- **Adverb**: "The economy's rapid decline was shockingly swift."
