## Word: Organism

## Pronunciation
/ˈɔːrɡənɪzəm/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A living being, such as an animal, plant, fungus, or microorganism, that has the ability to grow, reproduce, maintain homeostasis, respond to stimuli, and adapt to its environment.

### Noun Examples
- _"The human organism is composed of trillions of cells."_
- _"Microorganisms play a crucial role in breaking down organic matter in ecosystems."_
- _"Scientists discovered a new species of deep-sea organism during their exploration."_

## Phrases
- "Complex organism": Refers to a living being with intricate biological structures and functions, such as humans or mammals.
- "Single-celled organism": An organism made up of just one cell, like bacteria or amoeba.
- "Organismic theory": A philosophical or scientific concept referring to the idea that organisms have an inherent drive toward self-organization and development.

## Example Sentences
- _"Plants are organisms capable of producing their own food through photosynthesis."_
- _"Viruses are often debated whether they should be considered living organisms because they cannot replicate without host cells."_
- _"Understanding how organisms adapt to changing environments is key in evolutionary biology."_
