## Pronunciation
/waɪ/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: Expressing the reason or cause for something; serving as an explanation or justification. For example: "I'm staying late because I have to finish this report."
- **Interrogative pronoun**: Used to ask the reason or cause for something. For example: "Why are you leaving early?"
- **Preposition**: In some cases, used with "because" to introduce a reason. For example: "She decided to study because she wanted to improve her grades."

## Phrases
- "Why not?" suggests permission or encouragement for a particular action.
- "Why bother?" implies that the effort is unnecessary.
- "Why don't you...?" is a suggestion or invitation to do something.

## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "I asked him why he was absent from the meeting."
- **Interrogative pronoun**: "She wondered why they hadn't received a response yet."
- **Prepositional phrase**: "He explained the reason why he was so stressed."

## Synonyms
- Because
- As
- For
- Inasmuch as
- The reason being

## Antonyms
- Why not? (Contrast: But why?)
- Without reason (Contrast: With no explanation)
