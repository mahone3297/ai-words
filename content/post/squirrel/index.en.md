## Pronunciation
/səˈkrɪl/

## Definitions
### Noun
A small, tree-dwelling mammal with a bushy tail, typically brown or gray, that feeds on nuts and seeds. **Example:** "The squirrel scampered up the tree, storing acorns for winter."

### Verb
To gather or hoard food, often referring to squirrels' behavior. **Example:** "Squirrels are known for their habit of squirreling away nuts for the season."

### Adjective
1. Relating to squirrels or their actions. **Example:** "The squirrel-themed garden was a delight to behold."
2. Characteristic of being active or energetic. **Example:** "Her squirrelly energy never seemed to fade."

### Adverb
In a manner resembling a squirrel's activity or agility. **Example:** "The squirrelily nimble climber managed to escape the trap."

## Phrases
- "Squirrel about" means to be restless or fidgety.
- "Squirrel away" signifies saving or hiding something.
- "Squirrel out" is slang for getting lost or disoriented.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The children watched the squirrel play in the park."
- **Verb**: "The gardener squirrelled seeds in different sections of the garden."
- **Adjective**: "Her squirrelly nature made her the life of the party."
- **Adverb**: "The squirrelishly quick burglar slipped through the security measures."
