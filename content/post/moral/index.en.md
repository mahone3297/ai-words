## Word: Moral

## Pronunciation
/ˈmɔːrəl/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A set of principles or values that guide actions, decisions, and behaviors; ethics or morality. Example: "The moral of the story is to always be honest."
  - Also, the lesson or message that a story or experience conveys about right and wrong behavior. Example: "The moral of the fable is that hard work pays off."

- **Adjective**: Relating to principles of right and wrong conduct; ethical. Example: "A moral dilemma."
  - Also, teaching or exhibiting goodness or correctness of character and behavior. Example: "Moral education is essential for children's development."

## Phrases
- "Moral compass" refers to an individual's internal sense of what is right and wrong that guides their decision-making.
- "Moral high ground" refers to holding a position regarded as morally superior to others'.
- "Moral support" denotes encouragement and emotional backing rather than tangible assistance.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "In many fairy tales, the moral is often clear and straightforward."
- **Adjective**: "His moral integrity was never in question during his long career in public service."
