## Pronunciation
/ˈmeʒərəmənt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The action, process, or system of measuring something; the size, quantity, or amount of something ascertained by this process. For example: "The scientist took precise measurements of the chemical reaction."
  - **Noun (figurative)**: A standard or means by which something can be judged or assessed. For instance: "Beauty is a subjective measurement."

## Phrases
- "Take measurements" refers to the act of determining the dimensions of something using a tool such as a ruler, tape measure, or weighing scale.
- "Make measurements" implies the same process but may also connote recording or documenting the data obtained.
- "Inaccurate measurements" suggests that the determined size, quantity, or amount deviates from the true value.
- "Measurements for success" indicates specific criteria used to evaluate whether a goal has been achieved.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (literal usage)**: "Before ordering new drapes, we need to take accurate measurements of the window."
- **Noun (figurative usage)**: "Customer satisfaction ratings serve as a key measurement of our company's performance."
