## Pronunciation
/preɪˈper/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Preparation**: The act or process of making something ready or suitable for use. Example: "She spent hours preparing for the exam."
- **Cook**: A dish that is ready to be served or eaten after being prepared. Example: "The chef prepared a delicious pasta dish."
- **Disposition**: Mental state or readiness for action. Example: "His calm preparation for the challenge showed his good disposition."

### Verb
- **To prepare**: To get ready or make something ready for a particular purpose or event. Example: "She's preparing a presentation for the meeting."
- **To cook**: To process food by applying heat, often in a kitchen. Example: "He prepared a hearty meal for the family."
- **To equip**: To make someone or something ready with necessary tools or supplies. Example: "They prepared the team with new equipment."

### Adjective
- **Prepared**: Ready or having done the necessary groundwork. Example: "The hiker was well-prepared for the long hike."
- **Ready-made**: Finished and immediately usable without further preparation. Example: "I bought a few ready-made meals for busy days."

### Adverb
- **Preparatory**: Beforehand or in advance of something. Example: "The preparatory work for the project began months ago."

## Phrases
- "Prepare for a task" means to get ready to undertake it.
- "Prepare oneself" implies getting mentally or emotionally ready.
- "Prepare a recipe" involves gathering ingredients and steps to cook a meal.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She was diligently preparing her garden for spring planting."
- **Verb**: "The chef prepared the fish by cleaning and marinating it."
- **Adjective**: "The well-prepared students felt confident before the exam."
- **Adverb**: "His preparatory lectures were very informative."
