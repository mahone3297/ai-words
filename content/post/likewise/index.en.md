## Pronunciation
/ˌlaɪkˈwaɪz/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: In a similar manner; correspondingly; equally. For example: "The same rules apply to children and adults alike; they should all be treated fairly."
  - *She worked hard, and he did likewise.*
  
- **Conjunction**: Used to introduce a clause that reinforces or adds to what has already been stated. For example: "She enjoys reading books, and likewise finds pleasure in writing them."
  - *I love hiking, and likewise enjoy camping.*

## Phrases
- "In like manner" means in the same way or similarly.
  - "He treats his friends with kindness, and in like manner, respects his colleagues."

- "Likewise, too" can be used to emphasize agreement or similarity.
  - "I'm attending the conference this year, and my colleague will likewise attend as well."

## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "If you need help, just ask; I'll be glad to assist you, and your classmates will likely do likewise."
- **Conjunction**: "I prefer coffee in the morning; likewise, I often drink tea in the evening."
