## Pronunciation
/ˈrəʊteɪt/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: n. & v. To move around in a circular or rotary motion, typically around a central axis or point. Also, to change position or order regularly or repeatedly.
- **Noun**: A rotation, the act of turning or moving through a full circle.

## Phrases
- "Rotate an object" means to cause it to turn around a central axis.
- "Rotate the tires" refers to changing their position on a vehicle.
- "Annual rotation" implies a regular cycle of tasks or responsibilities.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The fan rotates slowly, keeping the air cool."
- **Noun**: "In a game, players rotate positions every few minutes."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "The employees take turns rotating duties during their shift."

## Usage
### Noun
Rotation can be used as a countable or uncountable noun, depending on the context. As a countable noun, it refers to a specific instance of rotation, while as an uncountable noun, it describes the action in general.

### Verb
Rotate is often used transitively, followed by an object, and can also be used as a transitive or intransitive verb, with or without an explicit subject. It can be both a regular or irregular verb in the present tense (rotate, rotated, rotating) but requires irregular forms in past participle (rotated) and past tense (rotated).
