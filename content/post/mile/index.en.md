## Word: Mile

## Pronunciation
/maɪl/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A unit of length commonly used in the United States and the United Kingdom, equivalent to 1,760 yards or approximately 1,609 meters. For example: "They ran a mile every morning."
- **Verb (informal)**: Travel or cover a distance of one mile. For example: "We've already mile-ed this route several times."
- **Noun (idiomatic)**: A great distance, often used hyperbolically to emphasize the magnitude of a gap or difference. For example: "We're miles apart in our opinions."

## Phrases
- "A mile a minute": At a very fast pace or speed.
- "Go the extra mile": To make a special effort beyond what is expected or required.
- "Mile marker": A sign along a road indicating the distance traveled in miles from a fixed point, often used as a reference for navigation.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (unit of length)**: "The marathon covers a distance of 26.2 miles."
- **Verb (informal)**: "She bikes to work every day, so she's used to mile-ing that route by now."
- **Idiomatic**: "Our tastes in music differ by miles."

## Synonyms
- Kilometer (in metric systems)
- League (a historical unit longer than a mile)
- Length (in general terms)
- Distance (as a more abstract term)
