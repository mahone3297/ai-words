## Pronunciation
/prəˈnaʊns/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To articulate the sounds of words correctly according to their written form or a particular pronunciation rule. For example: "She can pronounce foreign names flawlessly."
- **Noun**: The act of saying a word or sound with clarity and accuracy. For example: "The correct pronunciation of 'château' is shuh-tow."
- **Adjective**: Characterizing or relating to the way a word is spoken, often with a specific accent or dialect. For example: "The British pronunciation of the word 'schedule' is different from the American one."

## Phrases
- "Pronounce a name" or "Enunciate a word"
- "Correct pronunciation"
- "Regional pronunciation"

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "He practiced his pronunciation to improve his public speaking skills."
- **Noun**: "She taught her child the proper pronunciation of difficult words."
- **Adjective**: "The British teacher emphasized the correct pronunciation of vowel sounds."
