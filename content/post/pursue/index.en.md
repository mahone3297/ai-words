## Pronunciation
/puːˈsaʊ/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To follow, chase, or seek after with the intention of catching or obtaining; to pursue a course of action or a goal. [n. 1] [v. transitive]
- **Noun**: The act of pursuing, pursuit, or the state of being pursued. [n. 2]

## Phrases
- "Pursue a career" means to work towards a chosen profession.
- "Pursue an education" refers to continuing one's studies.
- "Pursuit of happiness" is a phrase from the Declaration of Independence, referring to the right to seek well-being.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The detective decided to pursue the criminal through the city."
- **Noun**: "His lifelong pursuit of knowledge led him to become a renowned scholar."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "After graduating, she pursued her passion for art."

## Idiomatic expressions
- "Pursue relentlessly" - to continue chasing without stopping.
- "Drop pursuit" - to stop actively trying to catch or find something.
- "Lost in pursuit" - deeply focused or absorbed in a pursuit.
