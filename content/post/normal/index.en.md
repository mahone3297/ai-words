## Pronunciation
/ˈnɔːr-məl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Conforming to a standard; usual, typical, or expected. For example: "A normal working day lasts 8 hours."
- **Noun**: The usual or average state, condition, or degree; a standard or model. For instance: "The new design deviated from the norm by introducing unique features."

### Adjective Examples
- "It's not normal for it to rain this heavily in June."
- "Her temperature is now back to normal after her fever subsided."

### Noun Examples
- "This product claims to restore your skin's natural pH balance to its normal level."
- "In statistics, the mean is often considered the normal for a set of data."

## Phrases
- "Return to normal" implies reverting to a regular or standard state after a disruption.
- "Business as usual" or "back to normal" suggests that things are functioning as they typically do.
- "Normal distribution" is a statistical term referring to a bell-shaped curve where data points are symmetrically distributed around a central value.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "After the storm, it took weeks for life to return to normal."
- **Noun**: "In many societies, the nuclear family has been considered the social norm."
