## Pronunciation
/sɔːrt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A type or category**: A way to classify or arrange items based on shared characteristics. For example: "Sort the books by author."
- **The act of arranging**: The process of putting things in order. For example: "She sorted the mail into different piles."
- **Sorting out**: Resolving or determining something. For example: "We need to sort out the logistics for the event."

### Verb
- **To arrange in a particular order**: Place items in a specific sequence or classification. For example: "Please sort the laundry according to color."
- **To determine the quality or character of**: Judge or evaluate something. For example: "He sorted through the applicants based on their qualifications."

### Adjective
- **Ordered or organized**: Having a clear structure or arrangement. For example: "The garden was beautifully sorted with rows of flowers."

## Phrases
- "Sort through" means to examine or go through items one by one.
- "Sort of" is an informal way to indicate approximate agreement or uncertainty. For example: "It's sort of like that."
- "Get sorted" is a colloquial expression meaning to get organized or settled.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The teacher asked the students to sort the marbles by size."
- **Verb**: "I need to sort the files on my computer before I can start working."
- **Adjective**: "Her room was sorted and tidy, with everything in its place."
