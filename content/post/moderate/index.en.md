## Word: Moderate

## Pronunciation
/ˈmɑːdərət/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Not extreme, kept within reasonable or average limits; not large or great in amount, intensity, or degree. For example: "They chose a moderate pace for the hike."
  - *Politics*: Belonging to or supporting a political party or group that occupies a middle position between the extremes of left-wing and right-wing politics. Example: "The moderate wing of the party was able to sway the vote."
- **Verb**: To make something less extreme, severe, or intense; to limit or control something. Example: "Moderate your drinking habits."
- **Noun**: A person who holds or advocates moderate views, especially in politics. Example: "The moderates were key in negotiating the peace treaty."

## Phrases
- "In moderation": Refers to having or doing something within reasonable limits, avoiding excess. Example: "Eating chocolate in moderation won't harm your health."
- "Moderate your behavior": To control or adjust one's actions or conduct to be less extreme or more acceptable. Example: "The teacher had to remind students to moderate their behavior during class."
  
## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The climate in this region is generally quite moderate, with mild winters and cool summers."
- **Verb**: "The mediator worked hard to moderate the tension between the two parties."
- **Noun**: "The moderates in the senate played a pivotal role in passing the bipartisan bill."
