## Pronunciation
/ˌɪntərˈfɪər/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To involve oneself in something in a way that causes disruption, obstruction, or hindrance; to intervene without invitation or necessity, often leading to negative consequences. Example: "The noisy construction work next door was interfering with my ability to concentrate."
  - To clash or compete with something else, often causing confusion or incompatibility. Example: "Her personal beliefs interfered with her ability to carry out the task assigned."
  
- **Noun**: (n) The act of interfering, meddling, or obstructing. Example: "His constant interference in our project caused several delays."

## Phrases
- "Interfere with": To disrupt or obstruct something or someone's progress or action. Example: "Don't interfere with the experiment while it's running."
- "Without interference": This implies no disruption or intervention from external factors. Example: "The experiment was conducted without any interference."

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "I wish my little brother wouldn't interfere when I'm studying."
- "The government vowed not to interfere in the private affairs of its citizens."
- **Noun**: "Their constant interference in our plans made it difficult to reach our goals on time."
