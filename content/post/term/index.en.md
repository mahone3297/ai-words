## Pronunciation
/tɜːrm/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A word or phrase that refers to a particular subject, topic, or concept. *Example: The 'term' in 'mathematics term' refers to a specific mathematical concept.*
2. A period of time during which something is in effect or studied, often in an educational context. *Example: Students are evaluated at the end of each semester on their academic term.*
3. A condition or phase of a disease or medical condition. *Example: She was prescribed antibiotics for a short term to treat her infection.*

### Verb
1. To give or use a specific name or label for something. *Example: They defined the term 'ecology' in their science lesson.*
2. To refer to or describe a particular period or stage. *Example: The company is working on a long-term plan for growth.*

### Adjective
1. Characteristic of a particular period or phase. *Example: The term climate change describes the long-term shift in weather patterns.*
2. Fixed or established. *Example: The term deposit is required to secure the apartment.*

## Phrases
- "Use a term for something" implies giving it a specific name or classification.
- "Term paper" is a common academic assignment.
- "Long-term goals" are objectives that extend over a significant period.
