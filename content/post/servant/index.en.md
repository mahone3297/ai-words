## Pronunciation
/sɜːˈvnt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. A person who serves another, often in a domestic or professional capacity, such as a butler, maid, or cook. 2. (slang) A person who works in a low-ranking position, often without much autonomy. 3. An inferior or subordinate in a relationship or organization.

## Phrases
- "Serve as a servant" implies taking on a role of assistance or duty.
- "A life of servitude" describes a life spent in bound service or labor.
- "Be subservient to" means to be obedient or submissive to someone else's authority.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The butler was a highly skilled servant, anticipating his master's every need."
- **Verb**: "In her former life, she had been a loyal servant to the family for decades."
- **Adjective**: "The servant's quarters were small and cramped, reflecting their status."

## Etymology
The word "servant" comes from the Old French word "serviteur," derived from the Latin "servus," meaning slave or bond servant. Over time, its meaning has evolved to include hired workers beyond the realm of slavery.
