## Word: More

## Pronunciation
/mɔːr/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: (comparative form of 'much' or 'many') Indicating a greater quantity, extent, or degree. For example: "I need more time to finish this project."
- **Adverb**: To a greater extent or degree; further, additional, or again. For example: "She wanted to know more about the topic."
- **Determiner**: Used to refer to a greater amount or number than previously mentioned. For example: "She asked for more tea after finishing her first cup."
  
## Phrases
- "More than": Expressing that one quantity is greater than another. For example: "I have more than enough money to buy this."
- "No more": Referring to the end or absence of something. For example: "There will be no more delays."
- "More often than not": Usually; mostly. For example: "More often than not, he arrives early to meetings."

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "If you need more information, please consult the reference section."
- **Adverb**: "He worked harder and more diligently than ever before."
- **Determiner**: "Would you like some more cake? There's plenty left."
