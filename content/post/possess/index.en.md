## Pronunciation
/pəˈzɛs/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Possess** (noun): The act or state of having ownership or control over something. For example: "The child has a toy that he possesses with great joy."
- **Possession** (noun): The state of owning or having legal rights to something. For example: "The house was seized by the government due to unpaid taxes."

### Verb
- **To possess** (verb): To have physical or legal ownership of something; to hold or control. For example: "She possesses a vast collection of rare books."
- **To be possessed by** (verb): To be influenced or controlled by a supernatural force, typically used metaphorically. For example: "He was possessed by an intense passion for art."

## Phrases
- "In possession of" indicates that someone has something in their keeping or has legal title to it.
- "To possess knowledge" means to have understanding or familiarity with a subject.
- "Be in possession of a secret" refers to having confidential information.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The museum's collection is a treasure trove of ancient artifacts that they possess."
- **Verb**: "After winning the lottery, he found himself possessed of a large fortune."
- **Metaphorical**: "She was possessed by the desire to travel the world."
