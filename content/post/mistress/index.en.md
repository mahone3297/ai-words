## Word: Mistress

## Pronunciation
/ˈmɪstrɪs/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A woman who has power, control, or authority over something; especially one in charge of a household or establishment. Example: "She was the mistress of her own destiny."
- **Noun (archaic or informal)**: A woman with whom a man has a long-term extramarital sexual relationship, often maintaining a continuing association. Example: "He kept a mistress in a house on the outskirts of town."
- **Noun (historical)**: A woman who employs servants or governs a household. Example: "The lord's wife and his mistress both had separate responsibilities in managing the manor."

## Phrases
- "Mistress of ceremonies": A woman who presides over a formal event, introducing speakers or performers.
- "Love mistress": An archaic or literary term for a woman who is a lover outside of marriage.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (authority)**: "She became the mistress of the art gallery after years of dedicated work."
- **Noun (extramarital relationship)**: "Despite his reputation, the affair with his mistress remained secret for many years."
- **Historical context**: "The lady of the manor, acting as mistress, oversaw the daily operations and welfare of her estate's inhabitants."

## Synonyms
- Paramour (in the context of an extramarital relationship)
- Lover (in a non-marital romantic relationship)
- Governess (historically, a woman in charge of a household)
- Companion (in some cases, referring to a long-term partner outside of marriage)
