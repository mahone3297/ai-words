## Pronunciation
/unˈlʌkɪ/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Having an unfortunate series of events or bad luck; ill-fated. For example: "She had an unlucky day, losing her wallet and keys."
- **Adverb**: Unluckily; in an unfortunate manner. For example: "Unluckily, it started raining just as we arrived."

## Phrases
- "Be unlucky to" implies having an unfortunate experience with something. For example: "It was unlucky to draw the short straw."
- "An unlucky streak" refers to a continuous run of bad luck. For example: "He seems to be on an unlucky streak lately."
- "To wish someone good luck, but they're still unlucky" highlights the irony of bad luck despite well wishes.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "She felt very unlucky after failing the exam despite studying hard."
- **Adverb**: "Unluckily, the team lost the championship game due to a last-minute error."
- "Despite being an experienced driver, he had an unlucky accident."
