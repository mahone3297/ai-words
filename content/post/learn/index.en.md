## Pronunciation
/lɜrn/

## Definitions
- **Verb (v)**: To acquire knowledge or skill by studying, practicing, being taught, or experiencing something. For example: "He's learning how to play the guitar."
- **Verb (v)**: To come to understand or accept something through experience or information. For example: "She learned that hard work pays off."
- **Verb (v)**: To commit to memory; to memorize. For example: "The students learned their multiplication tables."
- **Noun (n)**: The process of acquiring knowledge or skill. For example: "Learning a new language can be challenging but rewarding."

## Phrases
- "Learn the ropes": To become familiar with the procedures or techniques required for a particular job or activity. For example: "It took him a while to learn the ropes at his new job."
- "Learn by heart": To memorize something perfectly. For example: "I had to learn the poem by heart for tomorrow's recital."
- "To learn from one's mistakes": To gain understanding or wisdom from past errors or misjudgments. For example: "After failing the exam, he vowed to learn from his mistakes."

## Example Sentences

- **Verb**: "Through trial and error, they learned what strategies worked best."
- **Noun**: "Continuous learning is essential for personal and professional growth."
