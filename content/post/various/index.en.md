## Pronunciation
/ˈveəriəs/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Having many different forms, qualities, or aspects; varied. For example: "The classroom had various decorations."
- **Noun**: A number or kind; diversity. For example: "The book offers a variety of perspectives on the topic."

## Phrases
- "A variety of" indicates a range or assortment of something. For example: "There's a variety of fruits available at the market."
- "Various reasons" means different or numerous reasons. For example: "People have various reasons for choosing to work remotely."
- "Various opinions" reflects a range of viewpoints. For example: "In a debate, participants expressed various opinions on the issue."

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The students displayed various talents during the talent show."
- **Noun**: "The garden has a variety of colorful flowers."
- **Adverb**: "Her approach to problem-solving is very various."
