## 读音
/rʌˈmɔːr/

## 释义
- **名词**: 不正式的消息或传闻，通常未经证实。例如: "There were rumours of a possible strike by the workers."
- **动词**: 传播或听到谣言。例如: "The news soon rumoured throughout the city."

## 短语
- "Rumour has it that..." 表示有传言说。例如: "Rumour has it that the company is going to lay off some employees."
- "Start a rumour" 意味着发起或制造一个谣言。例如: "Some people deliberately started a rumour to damage his reputation."

## 例句
- 名词: "The rumour of his resignation caused a stir in the office."
- 动词: "She rumoured that the celebrity was getting married, but it turned out to be false."

## 同义词
- Gossip
- Whisper
- Talk
- Report
- Slander
