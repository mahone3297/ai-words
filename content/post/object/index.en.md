## Pronunciation
/ˈɑːbdʒekt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 
  - (n) A thing that can be seen and touched; a material entity. For example: "The vase on the table is a beautiful object."
  - (n) Something that is the focus of attention, interest, or activity. For instance: "The main object of the exercise was to improve fitness."
  - (n) In computing, an item that has distinct properties and actions associated with it, often data that a program manipulates. Example: "In OOP, a class defines how objects of a certain type behave."
  - (n) In grammar, the noun or noun phrase that receives the action of a verb or shows the relationship of a preposition. Example: "In the sentence 'She saw the bird', 'the bird' is the direct object."

- **Verb**:
  - (v) To oppose or resist something forcefully. Example: "Many people object to the proposed changes in the law."
  - (v) To express disapproval or dissent. Example: "I must object to your characterization of events."

## Phrases
- "Object of desire" refers to something or someone that one desires intensely.
- "Object lesson" is a practical demonstration or example used to teach a moral or principle.
- "Take objection to" means to disagree with or challenge something.

## Example Sentences

- **Verb**: "He strongly objected to the suggestion that he had been dishonest."
