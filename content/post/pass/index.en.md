## Definitions
### Noun
- A [verb](#verb) that involves the transmission or movement from one person or place to another, often through a medium or with the assistance of something.
- A certificate or document allowing entry or travel: "He showed his passport at the border."
- The act of going past or by: "The bus passed the stop."
- A momentary visit or stay: "We had a brief pass through the museum."

### Verb
- To move from one place to another, usually temporarily: "The car passed the traffic lights."
- To transfer possession or control: "She passed the baton to her teammate."
- To complete a required course or examination: "After months of study, he passed the driving test."
- To convey or communicate information: "The news was passed on to the higher authorities."

### Adjective
- Permitting or allowing passage: "The gate had a pass for visitors."
- Not requiring special permission or access: "A general admission pass."

### Adverb
- In a manner that allows or facilitates passage: "The corridor was wide enough to pass easily."
- Temporarily or briefly: "He'll be back in a few days, just passing through."
