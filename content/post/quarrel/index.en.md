## Pronunciation
/ˈkwɒrəl/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A disagreement or argument between people, often characterized by angry words or raised voices. *Example*: "Their constant quarrels were causing problems in the family."
- **Verb**: To engage in a dispute or argument with someone. *Example*: "She decided to quarrel with her roommate about the mess."
- **Adjective**: Causing or marked by conflict or disagreement; contentious. *Example*: "The quarrelsome atmosphere made it hard to have a productive meeting."

## Phrases
- "Start a quarrel" means to initiate an argument.
- "Put an end to the quarrel" signifies resolving a disagreement.
- "No more quarrels allowed" is a statement to discourage arguments.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After the long day at work, they couldn't avoid starting a quarrel over dinner."
- **Verb**: "The friends had a brief quarrel but quickly made up."
- **Adjective**: "The manager's quarrelsome behavior led to tensions among employees."
