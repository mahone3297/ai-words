## Pronunciation
/distres/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. Suffering or anxiety caused by severe or prolonged hardship, misfortune, or danger. 2. An expression of sympathy or concern for someone's suffering. 3. A state of great worry or agitation. [Oxford](https://en.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/distress)
- **Verb**: 1. To cause someone to experience distress or suffering. 2. To seek help or assistance in times of trouble or need. [Merriam-Webster](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/distress)

## Phrases
- "In great distress" indicates someone is in a very difficult situation.
- "Ease someone's distress" means to alleviate their suffering or worry.
- "Call for immediate assistance" is a request to come to someone's aid when they are in distress.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The refugees were in constant distress due to the harsh living conditions."
- **Verb**: "The first responder rushed to the scene to try to distress the injured person."
- **Interjection**: "Oh no, my car broke down! I'm in such distress."
