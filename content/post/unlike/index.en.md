## Pronunciation
/unˈlaɪk/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Contrasting in nature or quality; differing from. For example: "The houses were unlike any others in the neighborhood."
- **Preposition**: Expressing contrast or difference; in a manner different from. For example: "Unlike her sister, she doesn't like coffee."

## Phrases
- "Unlike many others" suggests a contrast with a general group.
- "Unlike what you might expect" implies a deviation from a typical assumption.
- "Unlike before" denotes a change from a previous state or situation.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "Her handwriting was unlike anything I had ever seen, with intricate loops and flourishes."
- **Preposition**: "Unlike his strict upbringing, he prefers a more relaxed lifestyle."
