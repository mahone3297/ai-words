## Pronunciation
/pɛər/

## Definitions
### Noun
A pear is a round, fruit-bearing tree in the rose family, with a single stem, and edible fruits that are usually yellow, green, or brown when ripe. The fruit has a firm, juicy flesh and a slightly sweet, crunchy texture.

### Verb (informal)
To "pear" or "pear-shaped" can be used humorously to describe someone's body shape resembling a pear, often with a wider base than top.

### Fruit
- A type of edible fruit, botanically classified as Pyrus communis.

### Tree
- The Pyrus tree, known for its pears.

## Phrases
- "A juicy pear" describes a ripe and flavorful pear.
- "A bowl of pears" refers to a collection of pears for eating.
- "Grow a pear tree" implies starting something from scratch or nurturing it.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The farmer picked a ripe pear from the tree."
- **Verb**: "She had a pear-shaped figure, but she was fit and healthy."
- **Fruit**: "I sliced the pears and added them to the fruit salad."

## Synonyms
- Bartlett
- Comice
- Anjou
- Seckel (small variety)
- Pome (collectively referring to stone fruits like pears and apples)

## Related Terms
- Pomaceous fruit
- Culinary use: pear sauce, pear tart, poached pears
- Nutrient-rich: fiber, vitamin C, antioxidants
