## Pronunciation
/wɒʃ/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Wash**: A cleaning process using water and soap or detergent to remove dirt, stains, or sweat from clothes, dishes, or surfaces. For example: "I need to do a load of laundry this weekend."
- **Washing machine**: A household appliance used for washing clothes. For example: "The washing machine was humming loudly as she loaded it with clothes."
- **Washbasin**: A sink typically used for washing hands or small items. For example: "She washed her face at the hotel's washbasin before going to bed."

### Verb
- **To wash**: The act of cleaning with water and soap or detergent. For example: "She meticulously washed the car to remove the grime."
- **Hand wash**: Cleaning by hand, often without a machine. For example: "He decided to hand wash his delicate fabrics to preserve them."
- **Machine wash**: Using a washing machine. For example: "I'll machine wash these towels since they're heavily soiled."

### Adjective
- **Washed**: Clean or having undergone the washing process. For example: "The freshly washed sheets smelled of lavender."
- **Washable**: Capable of being washed. For example: "These curtains are machine-washable, which is convenient."

### Adverb
- **Washed clean**: Completely cleaned. For example: "The rain washed the streets clean after the storm."

## Phrases
- "Give something a wash" means to clean it.
- "Wash up" is a colloquial term for cleaning one's hands or face.
- "Wash out" can mean to fade or lose color, or to no longer be effective.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She did several loads of wash over the weekend to prepare for spring."
- **Verb**: "He carefully washed the mud off his boots before entering the house."
- **Adjective**: "Her newly washed hair shone under the sun."
- **Adverb**: "The paint on the wall had washed out after years of exposure to sunlight."
