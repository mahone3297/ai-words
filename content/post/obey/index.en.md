## Word: Obey

## Pronunciation
/əˈbeɪ/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To comply with or follow the orders, rules, instructions, or wishes of someone or something. It can also refer to submitting oneself to a recognized authority or law.

Example sentences:
- **Verb**: "Children must obey their parents."
- "The soldiers were trained to immediately obey commands."
- "She promised to obey her doctor's advice on diet and exercise."

### Phrases
- "To obey one's conscience" means to act according to what one believes is morally right.
- "Obey the law" refers to adhering to the rules and regulations set by the government.
- "Obey the call" could mean responding positively to a summons, invitation, or inner prompting.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "I will obey your instructions carefully to avoid any mistakes."
- "They had no choice but to obey the court order."
- "He was praised for his willingness to obey without question."
