## Pronunciation
/pəˌsɪbələti/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The state or fact of being possible; the capability of occurring or being true. Example: "The possibility of winning the lottery is slim."
2. A feasible or likely outcome or event. Example: "There's a high possibility that it will rain today."

### Verb
1. To have the potential or be capable of (something). Example: "It's possible to learn a new language in a year with dedication."
2. To consider or entertain the likelihood of something. Example: "We should carefully consider the possibilities before making a decision."

### Adjective
1. Characterizing or denoting something that might occur or is feasible. Example: "The project has many possibilities for innovation."

### Adverb
1. In a possible or probable manner. Example: "She might arrive late, but it's possible."

## Phrases
- "Consider the possibility" means to think about or contemplate an option.
- "Beyond possibility" indicates something that cannot happen or is highly unlikely.
- "In the realm of possibility" suggests something that could conceivably happen.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The scientists explored the biological possibilities of cloning."
- **Verb**: "She pondered the possibility of moving abroad for work."
- **Adjective**: "The invention was a technological possibility that no one had ever imagined."
- **Adverb**: "His quick thinking saved them from a situation that seemed impossible."
