## Pronunciation
/ˈkʌvər/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A **covering** that protects or conceals something: *The book has a beautiful leather cover.*
2. The outermost layer or part of something: *The car's hood is covered in dust.*
3. A page or section at the front or back of a book, typically with illustrations or information about the author or contents: *The title page is the cover of a book.*
4. A type of insurance that pays for damages or losses: *He had comprehensive car insurance to cover any accidents.*
5. A person who acts as a representative or spokesperson: *She was hired as the face cover for the brand.*

### Verb
1. To place something over or on top of another object: *Cover the pot with a lid.*
2. To conceal or hide: *The snow covered the ground completely.*
3. To provide a surface or material for a publication: *The magazine will feature a cover story next month.*
4. To serve as a replacement or substitute: *He covered for his sick colleague at work.*

### Adjective
1. Covering or providing protection: *A waterproof cover for the umbrella.*
2. Complete or sufficient: *A full-page cover story.*

### Adverb
1. In a covering manner: *He covered the wound with a bandage.*
2. To a great extent or fully: *The blanket covers the entire bed.*

## Phrases
- "Cover up" means to conceal or hide something.
- "Cover charge" refers to an extra fee for entry to a venue.
- "Cover song" is a version of a popular song performed by another artist.
- "Cover story" is a prominent article in a magazine or newspaper.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: *The old house needed new roof cover after the storm.*
- **Verb**: *She covered her eyes with her hands when she saw the scary scene.*
- **Adjective**: *The book's cover design caught the reader's eye.*
- **Adverb**: *He covered the distance quickly, arriving early to the meeting.*
