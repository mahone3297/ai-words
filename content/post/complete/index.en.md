## Pronunciation
/kəmˈpliːt/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: 1. Having all the necessary parts or elements; fully formed or finished. For example: "The project is now complete."
   - **Noun**: 1. The act of finishing or the state of being finished; completion. For example: "We celebrated the completion of the building."
   - **Verb**: To make something complete or finish it. For example: "She completed the puzzle in record time."

## Phrases
- "Bring something to completion" means to finish it.
- "Complete a task" means to finish a job or assignment.
- "In its entirety" refers to the whole thing, without any omissions.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The kitchen is completely renovated with new appliances."
- **Noun**: "After years of hard work, he finally achieved his goal of completing the marathon."
