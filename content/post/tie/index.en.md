## Pronunciation
/taɪ/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A piece of fabric worn around the neck, often with a knot, to fasten clothing or to hold a shirt in place.
- A formal or informal way to refer to a specific thing or event, such as "a tennis tie" or "a political tiebreaker".
- A connection or link between people, teams, or organizations, often in a competitive context.

### Verb
- To fasten or secure with a tie, typically around the neck.
- To form a tie or equal position in a competition or ranking.
- To associate or be connected with someone or something.

### Adjective
- Having a close relationship or connection.
- Linked or related, especially by similarity or shared interests.
- Tied up or occupied, as in "I'm tied up with work".

## Phrases
- "Tie a knot" means to fasten a tie securely.
- "Tie-breaker" refers to a deciding game or round in a competition.
- "A necktie" is a formal type of tie worn with a suit.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "He wore a bright red tie to the office party."
- **Verb**: "In the soccer match, both teams ended in a tie."
- **Adjective**: "The companies are closely tied in the global market."
