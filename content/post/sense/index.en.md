## Pronunciation
/həˈləʊ/

## Definitions
### Noun
A term used to greet or address someone. Often accompanied by a friendly gesture or expression. For example: "She waved a friendly hello at her neighbor."

### Verb
To say "hello" or greet someone in a friendly manner. Can be formal or informal, depending on the context. For example: "The teacher greeted the students with a cheerful hello each morning."

### Interjection
An expression used to draw attention, initiate a conversation, or convey surprise or pleasure. Often spoken alone or followed by a comma. For example: "Oh, hello, there! How's it going?"

## Phrases
- "Give someone a hello" means to extend a greeting to them.
- "Drop a hello" is a casual way of saying to check in with someone.
- "Hello, my dear" is a romantic way of addressing someone.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "As he entered the office, he heard his colleagues' hellos fill the air."
- **Verb**: "After a long day, she greeted her family with a big hello and a hug."
- **Interjection**: "Hello, sunshine! It's so good to see you today!"
