## Definitions
### Noun
A word or phrase that expresses an action, occurrence, or state, often denoting an event or a mental or physical activity. For example: "The verb 'run' describes the action of moving quickly on foot."

### Verb
1. To perform an action or execute a function: "She verb-ed the task efficiently."
2. To express or communicate using words or symbols: "He verb'd his feelings clearly."
3. To indicate or signify (a particular meaning or purpose): "The sign verb'd 'no parking.'"
4. To have a particular function or role: "This word verb's as a linking verb in a sentence."

### Adjective
Characterizing or denoting an action: "A transitive verb is one that requires an object."

## Phrases
- "To perform a verb" means to carry out a specific action.
- "Regular verbs" have a predictable pattern of past tense and participle forms.
- "Infinitive form" refers to the base form of a verb, typically used without a subject (to run, to eat).
