## Pronunciation
/prəˈsɪktɪkli/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: In a practically or nearly practical manner; almost or effectively. It can also mean without any real change or difference. For example: "They completed the project practically on time."
- **Adjective**: Characterized by or being nearly practical; feasible or workable. For example: "The solution was practically unattainable."

## Phrases
- "Practically speaking" is used to introduce a statement based on practical considerations.
- "It's practically impossible" indicates that something is extremely unlikely to happen.
- "Practicality is key" emphasizes the importance of functionality or realism.

## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "She learned to ride a bike practically without any training."
- **Adjective**: "The plan was practically foolproof, but some unexpected issues arose."
- "They were practically out of money, so they had to cut back on expenses."
