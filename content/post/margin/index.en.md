## Word: Margin

## Pronunciation
/ˈmɑːrɡɪn/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: The space that is left blank around the edge of a page or sheet of paper, as in a book or on a printed document. Example: "The margins on this page are quite narrow."
- **Noun (n)**: The area between the boundary of a playing field and the spectators' seats. Example: "The ball went out of bounds and landed in the margin."
- **Noun (n)**: In finance or economics, it refers to the difference between the cost of a product or service and its selling price. Example: "The company increased its profit margin by reducing production costs."
- **Noun (n)**: In statistics, it's the difference between the upper and lower limits of an interval estimate or a measure of error. Example: "The poll results have a margin of error of plus or minus 3%."
- **Verb (v)**: To make or leave a margin on or around something. Example: "She decided to margin her notes with a red pen for easier reference."
  
## Phrases
- "Profit margin": The percentage of profit made from each sale.
- "Margin call": A demand from a broker to a trader to deposit additional funds to cover potential losses.
- "Within margin of error": A statistical result that falls within the range of uncertainty.
  
## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The essay should be typed with double-spacing and one-inch margins all around."
- **Noun (finance)**: "Increasing productivity can lead to an increase in profit margins."
- **Verb**: "The designer carefully margined the graphics so they wouldn't bleed off the page."

## Synonyms
- Edge
- Border
- Blank space
- Perimeter (in context of physical boundaries)
- Profitability (in financial contexts) 
- Leeway (in the sense of room for error or variation)
