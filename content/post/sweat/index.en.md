## Pronunciation
/sɛt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Sweat**: (noun) 1. The natural salty fluid produced by the body's glands as a response to heat, physical exertion, or stress, which evaporates to cool the skin. 2. A profuse production of this fluid, often causing a feeling of being hot or uncomfortable. 3. [informal] An expression of nervousness or anxiety, often accompanied by visible perspiration.

### Verb
- **To sweat**: (verb) 1. To produce sweat, especially as a result of physical activity or stress. 2. [informal] To worry or be anxious about something.

### Adjective
- **Sweaty**: (adjective) Characterized by or covered in sweat.

### Adverb
- **Sweating**: (adverb) In a manner that causes sweating, especially as a result of physical effort.

## Phrases
- "Break a sweat" means to work hard and produce a lot of sweat.
- "Sweat it out" refers to overcoming a difficult situation by enduring it until completion.
- "Sweat glands" are the specialized organs responsible for producing sweat.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After the long hike, he was covered in sweat from head to toe."
- **Verb**: "The athlete was sweating profusely after finishing the marathon."
- **Adjective**: "The dog was lying on the hot pavement, panting heavily and looking sweaty."
- **Adverb**: "He was sweating so much that his shirt was drenched."
