## Pronunciation
/ˈmɪrər/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A smooth or highly polished surface (usually made of glass) that reflects light in such a way as to give an image of whatever is in front of it. For example: "She checked her appearance in the mirror before leaving home."
- **Verb (v)**: To reflect or reproduce exactly, often used metaphorically to describe imitation or copying. For example: "The lake mirrors the surrounding mountains perfectly."
- **Noun (figurative)**: Something that gives a true representation or reflection, such as a medium through which one can see or understand something clearly. For example: "History is a mirror that reflects the mistakes and triumphs of the past."

## Phrases
- "Mirror image": An exact copy or reflection, but reversed left to right.
- "Through the looking glass": Refers to entering a strange or distorted reality, derived from Lewis Carroll's novel 'Alice Through the Looking Glass'.
- "To hold up a mirror to society": To show or reveal the truth about society, often by highlighting its flaws.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The dressing table was adorned with a large ornate mirror."
- **Verb**: "The calm water mirrored the cloudless sky above."
- **Figurative Noun**: "Literature serves as a mirror reflecting the values and beliefs of a culture."
