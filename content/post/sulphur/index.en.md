## Pronunciation
/səˈlfər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A chemical element with the symbol S and atomic number 16, known for its yellowish-gray color and characteristic smell. It is a nonmetallic solid that occurs naturally in volcanic regions and is used in various industrial processes and compounds, such as matches, gunpowder, and fertilizers.
- **Adjective**: Relating to or containing sulfur; sulfurous. For example: "The volcanic fumes emitted a pungent sulphur smell."

## Phrases
- "Sulphuric acid" is the scientific name for a strong mineral acid derived from sulfur.
- "Sulfur dioxide" (SO2) is a gas emitted during volcanic eruptions and industrial processes.
- "Sulfuric fertilizers" are used to enhance soil fertility.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The mine yielded vast reserves of sulfur, which were then processed for use in various industries."
- **Adjective**: "The cave walls were stained with a faint sulphur hue."
- "The sulfur springs near the town provided a source of thermal energy."

## Synonyms

- Sulfurous

- Elemental sulfur
