## Pronunciation
/nəʊˈstɔːrm/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **snowstorm** is a violent or intense precipitation event characterized by heavy snowfall, strong winds, and reduced visibility, typically occurring in cold climates during winter months. 

### Verb
To **be caught in a snowstorm** (informal) means to experience or find oneself in the midst of such a weather condition.

### Adjective
- **Snowy** describes something covered or influenced by snow, as in "The landscape was snowy after the snowstorm."

### Adverb
- **Stormily** can be used to describe the intensity or chaotic nature of a snowstorm, as in "The storm raged on stormily throughout the night."

## Phrases
- "Be prepared for the snowstorm" means to anticipate and get ready for the incoming harsh weather conditions.
- "Stay safe during the snowstorm" is a reminder to take precautions in such weather.
- "The power went out during the snowstorm" highlights the potential disruptions caused by heavy snowfall.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The children were building snowmen after the recent snowstorm."
- **Verb**: "We were fortunate enough to avoid the snowstorm that hit the city."
- **Adjective**: "The trees were weighed down by the snowy branches after the snowstorm."
- **Adverb**: "The drivers struggled to navigate the stormily winding roads due to the snow."
