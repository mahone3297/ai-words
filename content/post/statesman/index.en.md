## Pronunciation
/stətsmən/

## Definitions
### Noun
A statesman is a **noun** referring to an experienced and respected leader, often in politics, who is known for their wisdom, integrity, and ability to navigate complex situations. Statesmen are typically associated with diplomacy, leadership, and the maintenance of national interests.

### Verb
The term "statesman" can also be used as a **verb** to describe the act of exhibiting qualities and behavior typical of a great leader or diplomat, such as making wise decisions and representing one's country effectively.

## Phrases
- "Display statesmanship" means to demonstrate strong, principled leadership.
- "He exhibited exceptional statesmanship during the crisis."
- "A true statesman knows when to compromise and when to stand firm."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "He was hailed as a revered statesman for his role in negotiating peace between the warring nations."
- **Verb**: "The new prime minister is expected to exhibit strong statesmanship in addressing the nation's economic challenges."
- **Sentence combining noun and verb**: "The former president's legacy as a great statesman was celebrated at the ceremony."
