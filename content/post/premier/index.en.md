## Pronunciation
/prɪˈmɪr/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. The most important or senior member of a government or organization, typically the prime minister. For example: "The premier announced new economic policies."
   2. (informal) The first or leading position in a series or competition. For example: "He was the premier player on the team."
- **Adjective**: 1. Rank or position as the most important or senior. For example: "The premier's residence is a symbol of power."
   2. Of high quality or excellence. For example: "The premier performance was highly acclaimed."

## Phrases
- "Hold the premier position" means to be in the top spot.
- "In his first premier address" refers to a leader's inaugural speech.
- "Premier league teams" signifies the top tier of sports leagues.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The premier was invited to speak at the international summit."
- **Adjective**: "The premier painting in the gallery attracted a lot of attention."
- **Adjective (of events)**: "The premier film festival showcases the best international cinema."

## Synonyms
- Prime minister
- Chief executive
- Leader
- Top-ranked
- Best-in-class
- Highest-ranking

## Note: The word "premier" can also function as a noun in British English to mean the opening night or first showing of a play or movie. This usage is not included in the above definitions but may be relevant in specific contexts.
