## Pronunciation
/səˈfaɪər/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To fulfill or meet a need, desire, or requirement; to give satisfaction or contentment. For example: "The meal satisfied her hunger."
- **Noun**: The act of satisfying, fulfillment, or contentment. For example: "His achievement brought a sense of satisfaction."

## Phrases
- "Satisfy one's needs" means to fulfill the requirements or desires of a person.
- "Satisfied customer" refers to a person who is happy with a product or service.
- "Satisfactory results" indicate that something meets the desired level of quality or performance.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "Her hard work resulted in a satisfactory grade on the exam."
- **Noun**: "After a long day, she just wanted to go home and relax, feeling satisfied."
- **Adjective**: "The garden was a visual delight, providing ample satisfaction to the gardener."

## Antonyms

## Usage Notes
- "Satisfy" can also be used in the passive voice, e.g., "Her needs were satisfied by the generous donation."
- It can be extended to abstract concepts like emotional or intellectual fulfillment, such as "The book satisfied his thirst for knowledge."
