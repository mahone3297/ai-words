## Pronunciation
/lēp/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A sudden, powerful jump or spring, typically with the legs bent and the arms outstretched forward. For example: "The athlete made a magnificent leap over the high hurdle."
- **Verb**: 
  - **Transitive Verb**: To jump over or across (an obstacle), especially with one powerful bound. For example: "She leaped the stream in a single bound."
  - **Intransitive Verb**: To move suddenly and quickly through the air by jumping; to spring clear of the ground. For example: "The cat leaped onto the windowsill."
  - **Figurative**: To make a sudden, large advance or increase; to undergo a rapid change. For example: "Technology has leaped ahead in recent years."
  
## Phrases
- "Leap of faith": An act of belief or trust in something without proof or evidence.
- "Take a leap": To decide to do something that involves risk or a significant change.
- "Leap year": A calendar year containing one additional day (February 29) to keep the calendar synchronized with Earth's revolutions around the Sun.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "His incredible leap secured him the gold medal in the long jump competition."
- **Verb**: 
  - "The gazelle leaped gracefully away from the approaching predator."
  - "She decided to take a leap and start her own business."
