## Pronunciation
/stɜrn/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **1.** The rear part of a ship or boat, especially the part below the deck: *The ship's stern was elegantly designed.*
- **2.** (informal) A strict or severe attitude or manner: *She had a no-nonsense stern demeanor.*
- **3.** (figurative) The direction or focus of one's authority or control: *The captain took command from the stern of the situation.*

### Verb
- **To stern**: To direct or guide someone or something with authority or severity; to enforce discipline: *The teacher sternly corrected the students' behavior.*

### Adjective
- **Stiff**: Having a firm, unyielding quality; strict or severe: *A stern disciplinarian.*
- **Impenetrable**: Unyielding or unapproachable; difficult to penetrate or understand: *The professor's stern gaze was impenetrable.*

## Phrases
- "Keep to the stern" means to stay on course or maintain focus.
- "Deal with matters sternly" implies a serious and strict approach.
- "Stern warning" signifies a strong, authoritative caution.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The sailor grasped the tiller, guiding the ship's stern through the stormy waters."
- **Verb**: "The captain sternly instructed the crew to follow safety protocols."
- **Adjective**: "Her stern expression indicated that there would be no leniency for tardiness."
