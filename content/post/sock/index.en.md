## Pronunciation
/ˈsɒk/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **A sock**: A close-fitting foot covering, typically made of a fabric such as wool, cotton, or synthetic materials, worn to provide warmth, comfort, or protection for the feet.
   - Example: "I need to buy a new pair of socks for work."
2. **Socks as a plural**: A collection of socks, often used collectively.
   - Example: "She washed a load of socks in the washing machine."

### Verb
- **To sock**: (informal) To hit someone with a sock or to strike them forcefully.
   - Example: "The child accidentally socked their brother on the arm."
- **Socks someone**: (informal, humorous) To give someone a playful punch or hard pat.
   - Example: "He socked his friend on the shoulder to get their attention."

## Phrases
- "Put on a pair of socks" means to wear socks.
- "Matched socks" are pairs of socks that have the same design or color.
- "Roll up your socks" is an expression for getting ready to do some physical activity by rolling up the cuffs of your pants to reveal your socks.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She sorted her socks by color before putting them in the drawer."
- **Verb**: "He took off his wet socks and dried them by the radiator."
- **Informal Verb**: "When he heard the noise, he quickly socked the door closed."
