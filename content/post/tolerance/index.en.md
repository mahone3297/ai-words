## Pronunciation
/təˈlɒrəns/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: **1** The capacity to tolerate, accept, or endure something, especially differences or hardships. **2** A policy or practice of allowing a certain amount of something, often with a view to avoiding conflict or promoting harmony. **3** (informal) The act of being patient or understanding towards others' mistakes or opinions. 

- **Verb**: To tolerate, bear, or accept something with patience or without complaint.

## Phrases
- "Show/tolerate no/low tolerance for" indicates a strong disapproval or intolerance.
- "Have high/low tolerance for" describes one's ability to cope with a particular situation or behavior.
- "Educational tolerance" emphasizes the acceptance of diverse ideas in a learning environment.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She has a high tolerance for noise, which helps her concentrate on her work."
- **Verb**: "The government has shown a growing tolerance towards alternative lifestyles."
- **Phrasal**: "Their marriage survived years of financial difficulties due to his high tolerance for stress."
