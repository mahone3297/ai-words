## Pronunciation
/rɪˈkəɡnaɪz/

## Definitions
### Noun
- Recognition: The act of acknowledging or identifying someone or something. For example: "The team's success received widespread recognition."
- Award: A formal acknowledgment of achievement or excellence. For example: "She was honored with a lifetime achievement award."

### Verb
- To acknowledge or identify: The act of perceiving and understanding the identity or qualities of something. For example: "I recognized her voice on the phone."
- To acknowledge: To give credit or accept the existence of something. For example: "He recognized the error in his calculations."

### Adjective
- Perceptive: Having the ability to notice and understand things well. For example: "She has a perceptive eye for detail."
- Familiar: Known or recognized well. For example: "The music sounded familiar to him."

### Adverb
- Clearly: In a manner that allows easy understanding or identification. For example: "She recognized him clearly despite the distance."

## Phrases
- "Recognize a pattern" refers to perceiving a repeated structure or sequence.
- "Lack of recognition" can mean not being acknowledged or appreciated.
- "Visual recognition" is the ability to identify something by sight.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "His dedication to the project earned him recognition from his colleagues."
- **Verb**: "She recognized her old friend walking down the street."
- **Adjective**: "His exceptional musical talent allows for instant recognition of his performances."
- **Adverb**: "The detective recognized the suspect's handwriting clearly on the note."
