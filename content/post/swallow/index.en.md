## Pronunciation
/swɒl/

## Definitions
### Noun
A swallow is a small, typically migratory bird of the family Hirundinidae, known for its ability to catch insects on the wing. It can also refer to the act of swallowing something whole. Example: "The swallow built its nest under the eaves of the house."

### Verb
To swallow (v) means to take food or liquid into one's mouth and move it down the throat without chewing, often used metaphorically. Example: "He swallowed his pride and apologized for his mistake."

### Adjective
In some contexts, "swallow" can be used as an adjective, describing something that is easily accepted or swallowed whole. Example: "The children eagerly swallowed the story of the magic garden."

### Adverb
"Swallow-like" describes something done with the same rapidity or efficiency as a swallow consumes its prey. Example: "The snake moved swallow-like through the grass."

## Phrases
- "Swallow a lie" means to accept or pretend to believe something that is not true.
- "Swallow hard" refers to making a conscious effort to suppress emotions or difficulty, especially when making a difficult decision.
- "Swallow your anger" means to restrain or control one's anger.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The swallow was perched on a branch, waiting for its next meal."
- **Verb**: "After a long debate, he finally swallowed his reservations and agreed to the proposal."
- **Adjective**: "Her advice was swallowed up by the excitement of the moment."
