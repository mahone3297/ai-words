## Pronunciation
/ˈrɔɪəl/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A noun referring to the quality or state of being regal, belonging to a king, queen, or other member of a royal family, or associated with royalty.
- A monarch or their descendants, as in "the British royal family."
- A style of rule characterized by dignity, splendor, or formality.

### Verb
- To bestow or confer a royal title, honor, or attribute.
- To act in a regal manner or with great dignity.
- To officially recognize or appoint someone to a royal position.

### Adjective
- Characteristic of a monarch or their court; grand, majestic, or opulent.
- Having high rank or privilege, often by birth or appointment.
- Showing respect or admiration for something considered prestigious or noble.

## Phrases
- "Royal title" signifies an honorific bestowed by a monarch.
- "Royal wedding" is a ceremony uniting a royal couple.
- "Royal flush" (in card games) is the highest-ranking hand.
- "Acting in a royal manner" implies dignified conduct.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: The newly crowned king attended numerous royal engagements.
- **Verb**: The queen bestowed a knighthood upon the local hero.
- **Adjective**: The luxurious palace exuded a sense of royal grandeur.
