## Pronunciation
/ˈfɔːrməl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Relating to or following established customs, rules, or standards, often associated with ceremonial, official, or professional occasions. For example: "The event required formal attire."
  - In mathematics or logic, denoting a precise, explicit, and rigorous treatment or formulation. For example: "A formal proof of the theorem."
- **Noun**: A formal event or occasion that adheres to a specific dress code or protocol. For example: "They attended a formal dinner at the embassy."

## Phrases
- "In formal dress" refers to wearing clothing appropriate for a formal event.
- "Formal education" denotes structured and recognized schooling rather than informal learning.
- "Formal language" is a type of communication characterized by its use of standard grammar, lack of contractions, and avoidance of slang or colloquialisms.

## Example Sentences
