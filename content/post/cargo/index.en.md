## Definitions
### Noun
- **Cargo**: A noun referring to goods, typically transported by ship, plane, or truck, that are not passengers or living beings. For example: "The ship was loaded with a diverse cargo of perishable goods."
- **Payload**: In the context of transportation, this term can also refer to the load carried by a vehicle or aircraft, excluding passengers and crew.

### Verb
- **Cargo**: Occasionally used as a verb to describe the act of loading or unloading cargo, or the goods being transported. For example: "The workers were busy cargoing the containers onto the ship."
- **Haul**: Another verb meaning to transport goods, often in a more general sense than 'cargo.'

### Adjective
- **Cargo**: Less commonly, this term can be used to describe something that has been transported or carried, like "cargo pants" or "cargo shorts." 

## Phrases
- "Carry cargo" means to transport goods or items.
- "Unload cargo" refers to the process of removing goods from a vehicle or vessel.
- "Cargo hold" designates the area on a ship or aircraft where cargo is stored.
