## Pronunciation
/həʊli/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Sacred or consecrated; dedicated to a religious purpose or deity. For example: "The holy scriptures were read during the ceremony."
  - Also used to describe something that is considered pure, perfect, or morally right: "She leads a holy life devoted to helping others."
- **Noun**: (usually capitalized) Refers to God or gods, especially in Christian contexts: "The Holy Trinity consists of the Father, Son, and Holy Spirit."
  
## Phrases
- "Holy place" refers to a location considered sacred or hallowed by a religion.
- "Holy water" is water that has been blessed and is often used in religious rituals.
- "Holy day" is another term for a religious holiday or festival.
- "Holy grail" metaphorically refers to an elusive or highly sought-after object or goal.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The church's holy atmosphere was palpable during the midnight mass."
- "This ancient book is considered one of the holiest texts in their faith."
- **Noun**: "Many pilgrims journey to Jerusalem, known as the Holy City."
- "The priest sprinkled holy water on the congregation."

## Synonyms
- Sacred
- Sanctified
- Divine
- Blessed
- Hallowed

For the verb form of "holy", it's less common but can be seen in some dialects or informal uses meaning to make or declare something as holy:
- Consecrate
- Sanctify
- Bless
- Purify
