## Pronunciation
/ˈwɜːθ/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Worth**: The value or importance of something, often in terms of money or usefulness. For example: "This painting is worth millions of dollars."
- **Merit**: The quality that makes something valuable or deserving of respect. For example: "His hard work has earned him great worth in the company."
- **Deserving**: Belonging to someone by right or having enough quality to be considered valuable. For example: "She is well worth the effort to help."

### Verb
- **Be worth**: To have a certain value or significance. For example: "The book is worth reading twice."
- **Deserve**: To be considered or regarded as meriting something, like reward or attention. For example: "Your dedication deserves recognition."

### Adjective
- **Worthy**: Having qualities that make it deserving of admiration or respect. For example: "A worthy opponent is someone who challenges you."
- **Valuable**: Possessing significant worth or usefulness. For example: "The antique vase is a valuable addition to the collection."

## Phrases
- "Be worth the effort" suggests that the outcome justifies the investment of time or energy.
- "Worth noting" indicates something important or noteworthy.
- "Not worth the trouble" means that the inconvenience or cost outweighs the benefit.

## Example Sentences

- **Verb**: "Her dedication to the project proved her worth to the team."
- **Adjective**: "The film's ending was a worthy conclusion to the story."
