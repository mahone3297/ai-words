## Word: Mainland

## Pronunciation
/ˈmeɪnlænd/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The main or principal island of a group, especially one that is much larger than any nearby islands, or the part of a country that is not on an island but constitutes the majority of its landmass, excluding smaller islands and offshore territories. 

  - Example Sentence: "After a week on the small island, they finally returned to the mainland."
  
- **Adjective**: Relating to or denoting the main island or contiguous part of a country, as opposed to islands or overseas territories.
  
  - Example Sentence: "Mainland China refers to all the regions under the jurisdiction of China except Hong Kong, Macau, and Taiwan."

## Phrases
- "Cross over to the mainland": To go from an island to the main part of a country.
- "The mainland economy": Refers to the economy of the main part of a country, not counting its insular dependencies or overseas territories.
  
## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "From the coastal town, it's just a short ferry ride to the mainland."
- **Adjective**: "There are cultural differences between the island communities and the mainland cities."

## Synonyms
- Continent (in some contexts)
- Motherland (figuratively)
- Heartland (depending on context)
- Inland (to contrast with coastal areas)
