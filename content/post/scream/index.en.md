## Pronunciation
/skriːm/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. A loud, high-pitched cry of fear, pain, or alarm. 2. An intense emotional outburst or exclamation. 3. (colloquial) A humorous or exaggerated shout or yell, often for attention or entertainment.
- **Verb**: 1. To utter a loud, high-pitched cry, typically of fear or pain. 2. To express strong emotion or excitement by shouting or crying out.

## Phrases
- "Let out a scream" means to emit a loud cry.
- "Scream in terror" describes reacting with a fearful cry.
- "Scream your lungs out" implies shouting or crying very loudly.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The child screamed when they saw the spider." 
- **Verb**: "She screamed in delight when she won the race."
- **Interjection**: "Oh no! He screamed when he tripped on the banana peel."

## Synonyms
- Yell
- Shout
- Wail
- Sigh
- Groan (for a less intense scream)
- Screech (for a high-pitched scream, often from animals)
