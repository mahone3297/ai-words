## Pronunciation
/ˌoʊvərˈlʊk/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To fail to notice or observe something, often unintentionally; to neglect or miss something important. For example: "I overlooked a few errors when proofreading my essay."
- **Verb**: (v) To supervise or survey from above; to have a commanding view of something. For example: "The balcony provided an excellent overlook of the city skyline."
- **Noun**: (n) A high point or vantage point from which one can survey or look down upon a landscape or scene. For example: "The castle's battlements offered a stunning overlook of the surrounding countryside."

## Phrases
- "Overlook something/someone": To not take notice of or forgive a mistake or fault. Example: "She decided to overlook his tardiness this time."
- "From an overlook": Describing a viewpoint that provides a panoramic or extensive view. Example: "We admired the sunset from the scenic overlook."

## Example Sentences
- **Verb (neglect)**: "In their haste, they overlooked several key details in the contract."
- **Verb (supervise)**: "The manager oversees the daily operations of the company from her office on the top floor."
- **Noun**: "The national park has several hiking trails that lead to breathtaking overlooks."

## Synonyms
- **Verb (to neglect)**: Ignore, miss, neglect, disregard, omit, bypass.
- **Verb (to supervise)**: Supervise, oversee, manage, direct, monitor, survey.
- **Noun**: Viewpoint, vantage point, scenic viewpoint, panorama.
