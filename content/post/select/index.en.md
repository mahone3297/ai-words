## Pronunciation
/sɪˈlekt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. The act of choosing or picking something out from a group, typically based on preference or criteria. For example: "The selector chose the best candidate for the job."
   2. A device or mechanism that allows you to choose between options, often found in electronic devices. For example: "The remote has a selection of channels."
- **Verb**: To choose or pick someone or something from a group, often deliberately. For example: "She selected a new computer model for her office."
- **Adjective**: Characterized by choice or discrimination, especially in taste or preference. For example: "Their menu offers a wide selection of dishes."

## Phrases
- "Make a selection" means to choose something from available options.
- "Preferential selection" refers to choosing based on a preference.
- "Random selection" involves choosing items without bias.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The programmer used a software tool for automatic selection of bugs."
- **Verb**: "The customer selected five books for their reading list."
- **Adjective**: "The restaurant has a diverse selection of vegetarian options."
