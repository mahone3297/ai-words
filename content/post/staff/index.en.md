## Pronunciation
/stæf/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A group of employees who work together in an organization, typically divided into different departments or roles. [nouns denoting a group of people](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/staff)
   Example: The hospital staff consists of doctors, nurses, and administrative workers.

2. A collection of implements or tools used for a particular purpose. [a group of items used for a specific task](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/staff_1)
   Example: A golf course maintenance staff keeps the greens in top condition.

3. A thin, flexible rod or cylinder, often made of metal, used for writing or drawing. [writing utensil](https://www.dictionary.com/browse/staff)
   Example: The artist used a quill pen from the antique writing staff.

### Verb
To provide or assign a group of people to perform specific tasks or duties. [to organize or assign employees to work](https://www.macmillandictionary.com/us/dictionary/british/staff_2)
   Example: The company will need to staff the new branch with experienced personnel.

### Preposition
1. With regard to or in connection with a specific department or role. [relating to a particular area or function](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/staff%20(in))
   Example: She was responsible for staff development in the HR department.

2. Supporting or sustaining, as in providing resources or supplies. [providing or maintaining](https://www.oxfordlearnersdictionaries.com/definition/english/staff_3)
   Example: The charity provides food and shelter for homeless staff.

## Phrases
- "Form a staff" means to create or assemble a group of people for a specific purpose.
- "Staff meeting" refers to a gathering of employees to discuss matters related to their work.
- "Management staff" denotes the group of executives or supervisors in an organization.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: The teaching staff organized a workshop for new teachers.
- **Verb**: The company has been hiring additional staff to meet the growing demand.
- **Preposition**: The project required additional staff to complete it on time.
