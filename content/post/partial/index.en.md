## Pronunciation
/pərˈsəl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: 1. Not complete or total; lacking in full extent or scope. For example: "The view was partial due to the trees blocking part of it."
   - **Verb**: 1. To judge or favor someone or something to an unfair degree; be biased. For example: "The jury was partial towards the defendant."
   - **Noun**: 1. A part of something, especially one that is not the whole. For example: "This painting shows only a partial representation of the city."

## Phrases
- "Be partial to" indicates a preference or bias towards something.
- "A partial understanding" means an incomplete grasp of a concept.
- "A partial blackout" refers to a temporary loss of consciousness or vision.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "Her opinion on the matter was partial because she had a personal stake in the outcome."
- **Verb**: "His judgment was considered partial since he was friends with one of the witnesses."
- **Noun**: "I only have access to the partial data, so my analysis might be incomplete."
