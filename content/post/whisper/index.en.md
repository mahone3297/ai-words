## Pronunciation
/ˈwɪsə(r)/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A soft or hushed sound made with one's voice, often to convey something secretly or softly to someone's ear. Example: "The children were whispering secrets in the corner."
- **Verb**: To speak in a low, soft voice, typically to avoid being heard by others. Example: "She whispered a bedtime story to her child."
- **Adjective**: Characterized by or producing a whisper; quiet or hushed. Example: "A whisper of wind rustled through the leaves."
- **Adverb**: In a whispering manner; softly or secretly. Example: "He whispered instructions so no one else would hear."

## Phrases
- "Whisper a secret" implies sharing something confidentially.
- "Whispered rumors" refer to unconfirmed or private information.
- "Whispering gallery" is a room or space where sound echoes, allowing people to hear whispers from a distance.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The library was filled with the soft whispers of readers."
- **Verb**: "In the dark theater, he could barely make out her whispered reply."
- **Adjective**: "The quiet of the night was punctuated by the occasional whisper of crickets."
