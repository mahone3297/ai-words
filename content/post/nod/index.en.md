## Pronunciation
/nɑd/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A quick, downward movement of the head as a sign of greeting, agreement, or acknowledgement. For example: "He gave a slight nod to show his approval."
- **Verb**: (1) To move one's head downward briefly, typically in assent, greeting, or understanding. For example: "She nodded when I suggested going out for dinner." (2) To cause something to move up and down with a similar motion. For example: "The horse nodded its head in rhythm with the music."

## Phrases
- "A nod's as good as a wink to a blind horse": This idiomatic expression means that a subtle hint or signal will be understood by someone who is already aware of the situation.
- "Give a nod to something/someone": To acknowledge or approve something or someone informally. For example: "The new design got a nod from the CEO."
- "Nod off": To fall asleep or become drowsy, especially unintentionally. For example: "During the long meeting, he nodded off several times."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She caught my eye and gave a friendly nod from across the room."
- **Verb**: "When asked if he agreed, he simply nodded his head."
- **Verb (idiomatic)**: "After a long day at work, he nodded off on the couch while watching TV."
