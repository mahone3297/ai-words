## Word: Jail

## Pronunciation
/dʒeɪl/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A place where people are legally confined as a punishment for crimes they have committed or while awaiting trial. _Example_: "He was sent to jail for theft."
- **Verb**: To put someone in prison or a similar place of confinement as a punishment or while awaiting trial. _Example_: "The police jailed the suspect pending further investigation."

### Noun Usage Examples
- "The new jail has improved facilities for rehabilitation programs."
- "She was released from jail after serving her sentence."

### Verb Usage Examples
- "The defendant could be jailed for up to five years if found guilty."
- "If he fails to appear in court, a warrant will be issued for his arrest and he could be jailed."

## Phrases
- "Go to jail": To be imprisoned as a consequence of one's actions.
- "Spend time in jail": To be confined in jail for a certain period.
- "Do time": Informal expression meaning to serve a prison sentence.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Conditions inside the old city jail were deplorable."
- **Verb**: "The judge decided not to jail the first-time offender but instead ordered community service."
