## Pronunciation
/dəˈnʌki/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A domesticated mammal**: A small, usually gray, horse-like animal with a long ears and a distinctive bray. It is often used for transportation or work.
- **Informal term**: Sometimes used colloquially to refer to a foolish or stubborn person, especially in a derogatory manner.

### Verb
- **To ride**: To sit on and control a donkey, often for transportation.
- **To imitate a donkey's bray**: To make a loud, grunting sound in jest or imitation.

## Phrases
- "Donkey work" refers to menial or tedious tasks.
- "Donkey's years" is a British expression meaning many years, often implying great age.
- "Donkey show" is a low-quality or outdated entertainment event.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The farmer was leading his donkeys along the dusty road."
- **Verb**: "The children were having fun riding the donkeys at the fair."
- **Imitative**: "The child mimicked the donkey's bray, making everyone laugh."
