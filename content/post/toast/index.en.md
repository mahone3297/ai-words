## Pronunciation
/təʊst/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A type of food**: A slice of bread that has been toasted, often served with butter, jam, or cheese, and sometimes as an accompaniment to meals.
- **Toasting ceremony**: A formal or informal event where a toast is made to honor or celebrate someone or something.
- **Toastmaster**: A person who presides over a toast-making session at a gathering.

### Verb
- **To raise a toast**: To propose a drink or expression of goodwill in honor of someone or something, often followed by clinking glasses.
- **Toasted**: (of bread) Grilled or browned on one side, as by exposure to heat.

## Phrases
- "Make a toast" or "raise a toast" means to propose a toast.
- "Cheers to your health/to your success/to friendship" are common toasts.
- "Toasting someone's birthday" is a tradition to honor a person on their special day.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "I put a piece of bread in the toaster and waited for it to turn golden brown."
- **Verb**: "At the wedding reception, the couple toasted each other's love and commitment."
- **Toasting**: "Let's raise a glass to our new business venture, cheers!"

## Synonyms
- Salute
- Raise a glass
- Clink glasses
- Drink to
- Honor
- Celebrate

## Note: In addition to these definitions, "toast" can also be used metaphorically, referring to praise or admiration expressed in words, as in "He was the toast of the town."
