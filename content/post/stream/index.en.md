## Pronunciation
/ˈstriːm/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A continuous flow or sequence of events, items, or data: "The river flowed in a gentle stream."
2. A series of moving water: "A mountain stream provided fresh drinking water."
3. An audio or video transmission: "He listened to the live stream of the concert."
4. A line of people or objects moving in the same direction: "The parade was a long stream of colorful floats."

### Verb
1. To flow continuously: "The river streamed through the valley."
2. To transmit (audio or video) over the internet: "They streamed the movie on their platform."
3. To follow a course or sequence: "The students streamed into the classroom."

### Adjective
1. Characterized by a continuous flow: "A steady stream of customers entered the store."
2. Internet-based: "The streaming service offers a vast library of content."

## Phrases
- "Stream of consciousness" refers to a train of thoughts without apparent structure.
- "Streamline a process" means to make it more efficient by removing unnecessary steps.
- "Watch a live stream" means to view a real-time event online.

## Example Sentences

- **Verb**: "She decided to stream her favorite podcast while cooking dinner."
- **Adjective**: "The company's new marketing strategy aimed to increase website traffic through streaming ads."
