## Pronunciation
/ˈtrænsfərər/

## Definitions
### Noun
A transformer is a **n** (electric engineering) device that **transforms** electrical energy from one voltage or current to another through electromagnetic induction. It is commonly used in power distribution systems to step up or down voltages.

### Verb
To transform (a material, signal, or data) means to change its form or structure, often using electronic equipment like a transformer. For example: "The computer can transform digital signals into analog sounds."

### Noun (Science Fiction)
In science fiction, a transformer is a character or creature that can change shape or appearance, often with a focus on the Transformers franchise.

## Phrases
- "Step-up transformer" increases voltage.
- "Power transformer" in electrical systems.
- "Transformer core" refers to the central component of the device.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The transformer on the power line efficiently stepped down the voltage."
- **Verb**: "The transformer was designed to transform low-frequency AC into high-frequency AC for radio transmission."
- **Science Fiction**: "The Autobots could transform from robots into vehicles."
