# large

## Pronunciation
/lɑːrdʒ/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Of considerable or great size; big. For example: "The elephant was a large animal."
  - *Formal definition*: Having more than average dimensions, extent, quantity, or number; spacious, extensive, or substantial.
- **Adjective (figurative)**: Great in scale, scope, or intensity. For example: "He has a large influence on the team."
- **Adjective (economics)**: Relating to or involving a significant amount of money, capital, or resources. For example: "Large corporations often dominate the market."
  
## Phrases
- "In large part": Mostly, to a significant extent. Example: "Her success was due in large part to her dedication."
- "At large": Without restriction or confinement; generally, widely. Example: "The suspect is still at large."
- "On a large scale": On a grand or extensive level. Example: "They began manufacturing the product on a large scale."
  
## Example Sentences
- **Adjective (size)**: "We need a larger house to accommodate our growing family."
- **Adjective (extent)**: "The company has a large customer base around the world."
- **Adjective (economics)**: "Investors were attracted to the large returns promised by the new venture."
