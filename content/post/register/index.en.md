## Pronunciation
/rɪˈzɛɡəstər/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A list or record of names, items, or information, often kept in a book or on a computer: *The school's register of students.*
2. (Computing) The device that stores data temporarily during computer operations: *The CPU retrieves data from the memory register.*
3. An official record of events or transactions: *The ship's log is the captain's register of daily happenings.*

### Verb
1. To enroll or record someone's name or details in a register: *Students must register for classes at the start of each semester.*
2. To enter data into a register or system: *The software automatically registers sales at checkout.*

### Adjective
1. Official or formal: *A registered trademark.*
2. Designating something that has been officially entered or accounted for: *A registered vote.*

## Phrases
- "Keep a register" means to maintain a record.
- "Register for an event" means to sign up or enroll.
- "Check the register" is to look up a record or transaction.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The teacher asked each student to write their name in the attendance register."
- **Verb**: "I need to register my laptop with IT before I can use it on campus."
- **Adjective**: "The company provides registered nurses to care for patients."
