## Word: Murder

## Pronunciation
/ˈmɜːrdər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The unlawful killing of one human being by another, especially with malice aforethought. For example: "The police were investigating the brutal murder case."
  - *As a mass noun*: The act or practice of killing people in a cruel or violent way. Example: "The serial killer was known for his heinous murders."
- **Verb**: To kill (someone) unlawfully and with malicious intent. Example: "She was murdered in her sleep."
  - *Figurative use*: To ruin or spoil something completely. Example: "The new regulations will murder the chances of small businesses to thrive."

## Phrases
- "First-degree murder" refers to a premeditated and intentional killing, which carries the most severe penalties.
- "Murder mystery" is a genre of fiction or entertainment that involves a crime where someone is murdered, and the plot revolves around discovering who committed the crime.
- "To commit murder" means to carry out the act of murdering someone.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The jury found the defendant guilty of first-degree murder."
- **Verb**: "The suspect confessed to murdering the victim during a heated argument."
