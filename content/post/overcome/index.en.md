## Pronunciation
/ˌoʊ.vərˈkəm/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (transitive) To successfully deal with and defeat a problem, difficulty, or enemy; to prevail over something that challenges or constrains one. For example: "She managed to overcome her fear of public speaking."
  - _Phrasal Verb_: overcome by (something) - to be overwhelmed or defeated by an emotion or situation. Example: "She was overcome by grief when she heard the news."
- **Verb**: (intransitive) To succeed in moving past or getting through a physical obstacle or barrier. Example: "The hiker finally overcame the steep mountain pass."

## Phrases
- "Overcome adversity": To successfully face and conquer challenging situations or problems.
- "Overcome with emotion": To be strongly affected or overwhelmed by an emotional experience.
- "Overcome obstacles": To successfully tackle and move beyond any difficulties or hindrances.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb (defeating a challenge)**: "Through sheer determination, he has overcome his addiction to smoking."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "She was completely overcome with joy when she received the award."
- **Verb (physical barrier)**: "They overcame the rugged terrain and completed their journey across the wilderness."
