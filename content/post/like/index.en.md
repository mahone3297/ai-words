## Pronunciation
/laɪk/

## Definitions
- **Verb (v)**: To have a preference or inclination towards something; to enjoy or approve of something. Example: "I like pizza."
  - Also used transitively to express similarity: "This painting looks like a Monet."
- **Preposition (prep)**: In the sense of 'such as' or 'for example'. Example: "Fruits like apples and bananas are good for health."
- **Adjective (adj)**: Similar to, resembling. Example: "She has a like-minded friend who shares her passion for travel."
  
## Phrases
- "Like so": Demonstrating how something should be done or positioned.
- "Be like": Used informally to quote or imitate someone's words or behavior. Example: "She was all like, 'I can't believe this happened!'"
- "As likes go": Referring to the standard or typical features of something within its category. Example: "As pets go, dogs are generally more loyal than cats."

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "Do you like playing football?"
- **Preposition**: "She bought books on various topics, like history and astronomy."
- **Adjective**: "The new dress is very much like the one she wore yesterday."

## Synonyms
- Enjoy
- Love
- Prefer
- Adore
- Approve
- Resemble
- Similar to

Note that "like" also commonly functions as a discourse marker in informal conversation, often to introduce examples or to indicate uncertainty ("I'm, like, really tired"), but this usage is not considered standard in formal writing or speech.
