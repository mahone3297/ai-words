## Pronunciation
/dɔːr/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A structure that provides an entrance or exit to a building, room, or vehicle, typically consisting of a hinged panel that swings open.
- **Example**: "The main door to the house was locked."
- **Synonyms**: entrance, gateway, aperture, exit

### Verb
- To open and close (a door) as part of entering or leaving a place.
- **Example**: "I closed the door behind me before turning on the lights."
- **Synonyms**: open, shut, unlatch, bolt

### Adjective
- Referring to a door or its function.
- **Example**: "The wooden door creaked as it opened."
- **Synonyms**: entry-level, portal-like, hinged

### Adverb
- In a manner related to doors; typically when referring to movement between rooms or buildings.
- **Example**: "She stepped through the door quickly."
- **Synonyms**: doorward, entry-wise, exitantly

## Phrases
- "Open the door" means to lift the latch or turn the handle to allow entry.
- "Keep the door closed" is a request to maintain privacy or control the temperature.
- "Knock on the door" is a gesture to announce oneself before entering.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She rested her hand on the cold metal doorknob."
- **Verb**: "He slammed the door shut, frustrated with the situation."
- **Adjective**: "The old house had solid wooden doors that were well-preserved."
