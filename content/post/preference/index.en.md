## Pronunciation
/preˈfɜːrəns/

## Definitions
### Noun
A preference is a **noun** that refers to a strong liking or inclination towards one thing over another, often indicating a choice or a bias. It can be a personal or subjective opinion. For example:

- "I have a strong preference for coffee over tea."
- "Her dietary preference is for vegetarian meals."

### Verb
To prefer (something) is a **verb** that means to hold a higher regard or like something more than another option. For example:

- "She prefers reading books to watching TV."
- "After much consideration, he decided to prefer the simpler solution."

## Phrases
- "Expressing a preference" means to clearly state what one likes or dislikes.
- "Making a preference" implies making a conscious decision about what one wants.
- "No preference" can mean having no strong liking or no particular choice.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "His preference for adventure led him to choose a career in travel."
- **Verb**: "After trying both, she now has a clear preference for the new software."
- **Sentence with preference**: "When ordering food, it's important to know your guests' dietary preferences."
