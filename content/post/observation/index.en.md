## Pronunciation
/ˌɑːbzərˈveɪʃən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The action or process of observing something or someone carefully or systematically. For example: "The scientist made meticulous observations of the plants' growth patterns."
  - Also used for the result of such an action: "Her years of patient observation led to groundbreaking discoveries."
- **Noun (legal)**: A formal watching, monitoring, or checking of a situation, especially by law enforcement or as part of a legal process. For example: "The police made routine observations on the suspect's movements."
  
## Phrases
- "Make an observation": To notice or comment on something.
- "Observation deck": A platform built for people to observe from, often found at high points like skyscrapers or stadiums.
- "Observation period": A set time during which something is continuously monitored or studied.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Through careful observation, the astronomer was able to identify a new comet."
- **Noun (legal)**: "During the surveillance operation, officers made several key observations that were later presented in court."
