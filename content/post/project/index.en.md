## Pronunciation
/proʊˈdʒekt/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A task or undertaking that requires planning, organization, and execution, typically one carried out to achieve a specific aim or goal. For example: "The school project involved researching local history."
2. An individual's display or demonstration of a particular skill or talent, often for evaluation or entertainment. For example: "Her art project was a stunning representation of her creativity."
3. A proposal or presentation made to investors or stakeholders. For example: "He submitted a business project outlining his new product idea."

### Verb
1. To initiate or carry out a planned activity or assignment. For example: "We're going to start working on the project next week."
2. To visualize or imagine something in detail. For example: "She projected herself as the lead character in the play."

### Adjective
1. Related to or involving a plan or organized effort. For example: "The project-based learning approach engages students more."
2. Large or extensive in scope. For example: "The construction project is expected to take two years."

## Phrases
- "Work on a project" means to engage in a task or assignment.
- "Collaborate on a project" involves working together with others.
- "The project timeline" refers to the schedule or plan for completing a project.
- "The project manager" is the person responsible for overseeing a project.
