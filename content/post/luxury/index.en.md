## Pronunciation
/ˈlʌkʃəri/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The state of great comfort and extravagant living, often involving expensive and high-quality items or services. For example: "They indulged in the luxury of a five-star hotel during their vacation."
- **Adjective**: Describing something that is considered non-essential but provides pleasure or comfort if one can afford it. For example: "A luxury car with all the latest features."

### Noun Examples
- "The cruise ship offered every conceivable luxury to its passengers."
- "Their new home was a testament to their love for luxury and elegance."

### Adjective Examples
- "She treated herself to some luxury chocolates on her birthday."
- "This spa offers a range of luxury treatments designed to pamper and relax."

## Phrases
- "Luxury item": A product or service that is not necessary but is highly desirable due to its exclusivity or prestige.
- "Live in luxury": To enjoy a lifestyle characterized by wealth, comfort, and ease.
- "Luxury brand": A company or product line known for producing high-end, premium goods.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After years of hard work, he could finally afford the luxury of early retirement."
- **Adjective**: "That designer bag is a luxury purchase; not everyone can afford it."
