## Word: Notebook

## Pronunciation
/ˈnʊtbʊk/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A small book with blank pages for writing notes, often used by students for taking down class notes, making lists, or jotting down ideas. 

  - Example sentence: She pulled out her notebook and began taking meticulous lecture notes during the history class.
  
## Phrases
- "Keep a notebook" means to maintain a record of information, thoughts, or ideas in a notebook.
- "Notebook PC" or "laptop" refers to a portable personal computer that can be easily carried and used in different locations.
- "A notebook full of doodles" suggests a journal or diary filled with drawings or sketches done while the user was engaged in another activity.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: The detective flipped through his worn notebook, scanning for clues he might have missed earlier.
- "In his schoolbag, he had neatly arranged his textbooks alongside his spiral-bound notebook."
- "Her notebook was a treasure trove of creative ideas and musings."

## Synonyms
- Journal
- Diary
- Notepad
- Memorandum book
- Scrapbook (for storing notes, clippings, etc.)
- Planner (in the context of scheduling and organizing)
- Logbook (in certain professional contexts like science or navigation)
