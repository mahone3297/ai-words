## Pronunciation
/ˈnɛkləs/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A piece of jewelry worn around the neck, typically made of precious metals and often adorned with gems, beads, or other decorative elements. For example: "She wore a beautiful diamond necklace to the gala."

### Phrases
- "A string of pearls" refers to a necklace made of pearls.
- "Necklace of office" denotes a special necklace that symbolizes a position of authority or membership in a specific organization.
- "Break the necklace" can metaphorically mean to disrupt or destroy a connected series or chain.

### Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The princess's most prized possession was a gold necklace inherited from her grandmother."
- "After winning the championship, he presented his wife with a tennis bracelet and matching necklace."

## Derived Forms
- Necklace-making (noun): The craft of creating necklaces.
- Necklace-like (adj): Resembling or suggesting a necklace in form or arrangement; for example, "the necklace-like pattern of the lights around the Christmas tree." 

## Idiomatic Expressions
- "To have a necklace of bruises" implies having multiple bruises around one's neck, often due to injury or violence.
- "Something old, something new, something borrowed, something blue, and a silver sixpence in her shoe" - In this wedding tradition, "something old" could be a family heirloom necklace.
