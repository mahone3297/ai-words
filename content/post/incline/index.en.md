## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈklaɪn/

## Definitions
- **Noun** (n): A sloping surface or angle; a gradient. For example: "The path takes a steep incline towards the summit."
- **Verb** (v): To have a tendency or disposition towards something; to lean or bend in a particular direction. For instance: "She inclines her head when listening attentively."
- **Verb** (v): To cause to slope or slant. Example: "The architect designed the roof to incline slightly for rainwater runoff."
  
## Phrases
- "On an incline": Describing something that is positioned or happening on a slope or upward slope.
- "Incline towards": To tend towards or favor something. For example: "His opinions incline towards liberal views."
  
## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "We had to walk up a steep incline to reach the top of the hill."
- **Verb**: "As he aged, he began to incline more towards spending time with his family."
- **Verb**: "Make sure the treadmill is set on an incline to simulate uphill walking."
