## Pronunciation
/ˈɪnˌsɪdənt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: An event or occurrence, typically one that is unexpected, unplanned, or unwanted, and which may have a negative impact or cause harm. For example: "The car accident was a tragic incident."
- **Noun (legal)**: A particular action or event that is under investigation, often involving possible wrongdoing or criminal activity. For example: "The police are looking into the theft incident."
- **Verb**: To happen or occur, especially unexpectedly or as a minor accompaniment to something else. For example: "Several incidents of plagiarism were noted during the academic year."

## Phrases
- "In the aftermath of the incident": Refers to the period following an event, particularly when dealing with its consequences.
- "Incident response": The process of dealing with an unexpected event, especially in IT security where it involves managing and mitigating the effects of a cyber attack.
- "Without incident": Describes a situation where an event proceeded smoothly and without any problems or issues.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "A fire broke out last night, causing significant damage to the building in what has been described as a major incident."
- **Legal Noun**: "The company reported several data breaches to regulatory authorities as separate incidents."
- **Verb**: "Despite heavy rain, the parade went ahead incident-free."
