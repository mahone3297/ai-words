## Pronunciation
/səˈpəʊz/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: **v**. To assume or suppose something to be true or likely, without proof or certainty; to think or consider. Example: "I suppose it will rain later today."
- **Verb**: **n**. A hypothetical or imagined situation for consideration or discussion. Example: "Let's suppose we had unlimited resources."
- **Adverb**: **adv**. Used to indicate that something is imagined or hypothetical. Example: "Supposing the worst happens, what would you do?"

## Phrases
- "Suppose that..." introduces a hypothetical scenario.
- "What if we suppose..." suggests a speculative thought experiment.
- "I suppose I should..." indicates a realization or acceptance of a possibility.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "I suppose you're right, I should apologize."
- **Verb**: "Suppose you were in my shoes, how would you react?"
- **Adverb**: "Supposing the train is delayed, we'll have to find another way home."
