## Pronunciation
/ˈlɔːn.dri/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The washing, drying, and ironing of clothes and linens, often done commercially or in a separate room or area of a house; also refers to a place where this is done. For example: "She spends her weekends doing the laundry."
- **Noun (US slang)**: Clothes that need to be washed or that have been washed. For example: "I have a basket full of laundry waiting to be folded."

## Phrases
- "Do the laundry": To wash, dry, and possibly iron clothes or linens.
- "Laundry list": A long, often exhaustive list of items or complaints. This metaphorical usage comes from the idea of listing all the items that need to be laundered.
- "Take the laundry to the cleaners": To bring one's clothes to a professional cleaning service.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The hotel offers a same-day laundry service for its guests."
- **Noun (slang)**: "I need to sort out my darks and whites before putting them in the laundry."
  
## Synonyms
- Wash
- Washing
- Laundering
- Cleaners (in the context of a commercial establishment)
- Washroom (when referring to a designated area for doing laundry)
- Laundromat (North American English)
