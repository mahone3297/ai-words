## Pronunciation
/steɪt/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **A state** (n): A political entity, usually characterized by a government, a defined territory, and a population. Example: "The United States is a federal state with 50 constituent states."
2. **Condition or situation** (n): The current state of affairs or circumstances. Example: "The economy is in a stable state."
3. **Mental or emotional condition** (n): A particular mindset or feeling. Example: "She was in a contemplative state after the news."
4. **Phase or level of being** (n): A distinctive stage in development or progress. Example: "The project is in its final state."

### Verb
1. **To state** (v): To declare or assert something clearly. Example: "She stated her position on the issue."
2. **To be in a state** (v): To experience a particular emotional state. Example: "He was in a state of panic when he realized he'd lost his keys."

## Phrases
- "In a state of" describes a specific condition or emotion. Example: "The child was in a state of exhaustion."
- "Declare a state of emergency" refers to official action taken to respond to a crisis.
- "Be in the same state of mind" means to share the same thoughts or perspective.

- **Verb**: "After the long journey, they were in dire need of a state of rest."
