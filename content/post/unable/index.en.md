## Pronunciation
/unˈeɪbl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Having the inability to do something; lacking the power or capacity to perform a particular action. For example: "He was unable to lift the heavy box."
- **Verb**: To be incapable of doing something; to lack the ability or means to do something. For example: "She was unable to complete the task on time."

## Phrases
- "Unable to do" indicates incapacity for a specific action.
- "Unable to cope" suggests a lack of ability to manage a situation.
- "Being unable" describes a continuous state of inability.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "Due to his injury, he was unable to run in the race."
- **Verb**: "The flood made it impossible for them to travel to the airport."
- **Sentence with 'being unable'**: "Being unable to find a solution, they decided to seek professional help."
