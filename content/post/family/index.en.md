## Word: Family

## Pronunciation
/ˈfæməli/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A group of people who are related by blood, marriage, adoption, or who live together as a household. For example: "We had a big family reunion last summer."
  - Also refers to all the descendants of a common ancestor; a lineage or clan. Example: "The royal family has ruled this country for centuries."
  - In a broader sense, it can also refer to a group of people closely associated for any reason, like a work family, school family, or church family.
  
- **Adjective**: Relating to or characteristic of a family. Example: "Family values are important to us."
- **Noun (figurative)**: A group of things that are related or belong together, e.g., "The dog breed belongs to the terrier family."

## Phrases
- "Family tree": A diagram showing the relationships among members of a family over several generations.
- "Family bond": Strong emotional ties between family members.
- "Family matters": Issues or concerns related to one's family life.
- "Blended family": A family formed when two people with children from previous relationships marry.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (relatives)**: "I come from a large, close-knit family."
- **Adjective**: "This restaurant offers a family-friendly atmosphere."
- **Noun (group of related things)**: "Dolphins belong to the cetacean family."
