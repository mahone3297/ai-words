## Pronunciation
/ˈtʃiːp/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: 1. Having a low price or cost; inexpensive. 2. Of poor quality or standard. 3. Showing little value or respect.

## Phrases
- "A cheap meal" refers to a meal that is inexpensive.
- "She bought a cheap car" means the vehicle was affordable but possibly of lesser quality.
- "Don't sell yourself short; you deserve something better than a cheap imitation."

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective (price)**: "The discount store had many cheap items on sale."
- **Adjective (quality)**: "The apartment was advertised as cheap but turned out to be shabby."
- **Adverb**: "He gave a cheap imitation of the famous actor's performance."

## Note
The word "cheap" can sometimes be used pejoratively to imply a lack of value or quality, while it can also simply refer to affordability. Be cautious when using it to avoid negative connotations.
