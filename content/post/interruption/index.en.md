## Pronunciation
/ˌɪntəˈrʌpʃən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: An act of interrupting something, especially a continuous activity, speech, or process; a break in continuity or a disturbance. For example: "There was an unexpected interruption during his speech."

  > *The phone rang, causing an interruption in our meeting.*
  
- **Noun (Derived sense)**: Something that interrupts or disturbs, such as an unforeseen event or an obstacle. For example: "Noise from the construction site has been a constant interruption to their work."

  > *Her frequent coughing fits were an unwelcome interruption to the lecture.*

## Phrases
- "Without interruption": Describing a continuous action that isn't stopped by anything.
  > *"They worked on the project without interruption for several hours straight."*
- "To cause an interruption": To break into or disturb something ongoing.
  > *"His sudden entrance caused an interruption in the film screening."*

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The power outage caused a temporary interruption to our online class."
- **Noun (Derived sense)**: "The new policy aims to reduce unnecessary interruptions during office hours."
