## Word: Marriage

## Pronunciation
/ˈmærɪdʒ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The legally or formally recognized union of two people as partners in a personal relationship, often with religious, social, or legal sanction. For example: "They celebrated their 25th wedding anniversary this year."
  - **Noun (figurative)**: A close union or combination of two things, especially abstract concepts. Example: "The marriage of technology and art produced some stunning visual effects."
  
## Phrases
- "Marriage ceremony": The formal event where two people get married.
- "Marriage license": A legal document authorizing a couple to marry.
- "Marriage proposal": When one person asks another to marry them.
- "Child marriage": A marriage where one or both parties are under the age of 18.
- "Marriage of convenience": A union based on practical considerations rather than love or affection.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Their marriage was a symbol of enduring love and commitment."
- "The merger represented a strategic marriage between two industry giants."
