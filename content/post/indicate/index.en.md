## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈdɪkeɪt/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To show, point out, or make known something clearly; to give information about something, often by means of signs, symbols, or words. For example: "The arrows on the map indicate the direction to follow."
  - Also used to mean: To suggest or imply the existence, presence, or truth of something without stating it directly. For example: "His silence indicates his disapproval."

## Phrases
- "Indicate one's preference": To show which option or choice one likes or prefers.
- "Indicate a turn": In driving contexts, to signal that you intend to turn left or right.
- "Indicator light": A device, often a light, that indicates the status or condition of something (e.g., a warning light indicating low fuel).

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The red sky at night indicates good weather tomorrow." 
- "She indicated her agreement by nodding her head."
- "Please indicate your name and address on the form."
