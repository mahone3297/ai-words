## Pronunciation
/ˌəˈpɒn/

## Definitions
### Preposition
- **Upon**: A preposition indicating a position or action on or in relation to something. It can be used to show location, time, or condition. 

### Verb
- **Upon (with 'to')**: Expresses an action that takes place as a result or upon completion of another action. For example: "I will act upon your advice."

### Adverb
- **Upon**: Occasionally used as an adverb to emphasize the immediacy or directness of an action. For example: "She immediately acted upon his suggestion."

## Phrases
- "Upon arriving" indicates when something happens after arrival.
- "Upon seeing him" describes an action triggered by seeing someone.
- "Upon reflection" suggests thinking about something before taking action.
- "Upon request" means in response to a specific request.

## Example Sentences
- **Preposition**: "The book is upon the table."
- **Verb**: "Upon hearing the news, she burst into tears."
- **Adverb**: "Upon reaching the top of the mountain, the view was breathtaking."

## Synonyms
- On
- Above
- Over
- At
- In
- Upon (less commonly)

## Usage Notes
- "Upon" can be followed by a noun, gerund, or clause, depending on the context.
- It's often used in idiomatic expressions like "upon entering," "upon reflection," or "upon waking up."
