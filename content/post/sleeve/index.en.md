## Pronunciation
/sliːv/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Sleeve**: A part of a garment extending from the shoulder to the wrist, usually on the upper arm.
   - Definition: The sleeve protects the arm and can be made of various materials, such as cotton, silk, or leather.
   - Example: "She rolled up her long sleeves to help with the gardening."

2. **Sleeveless**: Without sleeves; having no covering for the arms.
   - Example: "The sleeveless dress was perfect for the summer heat."

### Verb
- (Informal) To put on or remove the sleeve of a garment.
   - Example: "I need to sleeve up my jacket before going outside."

### Adjective
- Sleeveless: Describing a garment without sleeves.
   - Example: "The sleeveless top was a popular choice for the beach party."

## Phrases
- "Roll up your sleeves" means to get ready to work or be more involved.
- "Long sleeves" or "short sleeves" describe the length of a sleeve on a garment.
- "Button-up sleeves" refers to the type of closure on a shirt or blouse.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She carefully removed the sleeve from the dry cleaning bag."
- **Verb**: "He rolled up his sleeves and got down to work."
- **Adjective**: "The sleeveless dress was a refreshing change from her usual attire."
