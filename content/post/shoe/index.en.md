## Pronunciation
/uːˈʃuː/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A foot covering, typically having a sole and a heel, designed to protect and support the wearer's foot.
- **Example**: "She wore a pair of stylish leather shoes to the office."
- **Synonyms**: boot, sandal, sneaker, pump, bootie

### Verb
- To put on or wear a shoe, especially one's footwear.
- **Example**: "I usually shoe up before leaving the house."
- **Synonym**: lace up, slip on, don

### Adjective
- Referring to something related to or made of shoes.
- **Example**: "The shoe store had a wide variety of footwear."
- **Synonym**: shoey, footwear-related

## Phrases
- "Put on your shoes" means to get dressed by putting on your footwear.
- "Shoe fit" refers to how well a shoe fits a person's foot.
- "Shoe sale" is an event where shoes are sold at reduced prices.

## Example Sentences

- **Verb**: "He carefully shooed away the birds from the garden."
- **Adjective**: "She admired the colorful shoe collection displayed in the store window."
