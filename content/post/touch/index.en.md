## Definitions
### Noun
- **Touch**: The physical contact with something or someone; a sensation perceived through the skin or body. For example: "The soft fur of the cat was a gentle touch."
- **Tactile sense**: The ability to perceive pressure, temperature, texture, and other physical sensations through the skin.

### Verb
- **To touch**: To make contact with someone or something by physically laying a hand or limb on it; to handle or manipulate. For example: "She touched the vase gently to avoid breaking it."
- **To touch someone emotionally**: To have an impact on someone's feelings or emotions. For example: "His words touched my heart."

### Adjective
- **Tactile**: Relating to the sense of touch; involving physical contact. For example: "A tactile surface provides a soothing feel."
- **Intimate**: Having a close, personal connection; involving physical touch. For example: "Their intimate dance showed a deep emotional bond."

### Adverb
- **Tactfully**: In a considerate or sensitive manner, avoiding causing offense. For example: "She handled the situation tactfully."
