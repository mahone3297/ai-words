## Word: Interrupt

## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈtɜːrpt/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: 
  - (transitive) To break into or disturb the continuity or progress of (an action, speech, thought, etc.). For example: "The ringing phone interrupted our conversation."
  - (intransitive) To break off what one is doing by some extraneous act, event, or thought. For example: "I was interrupted while reading my book."
  - (computing) To suspend the normal processing of a program temporarily, usually in response to an external signal or event.

## Phrases
- "Interrupt someone's flow": To break the concentration or train of thought of someone.
- "Interrupt a process": In computing, to halt the normal execution of a program or task.
- "Interrupt politely": To try to get someone's attention without being rude or intrusive.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb (transitive)**: "Please don't interrupt when I'm speaking; it's quite rude."
- **Verb (intransitive)**: "She was interrupted from her work by a knock on the door."
- **Computing**: "The system can automatically interrupt a running program if it detects an error."
