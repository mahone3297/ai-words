## Word: Orphan

## Pronunciation
/ˈɔːrfən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A child whose parents are dead or have permanently abandoned them. For example: "The charity focuses on providing shelter and education for orphans."
- **Verb** (archaic): To make someone an orphan; to deprive of parents. For example: "The war orphaned many children."
  
## Phrases
- "Orphanage": An institution that provides care for orphans.
- "Double orphan": A child who has lost both parents.
- "Orphan drug": A pharmaceutical agent developed specifically to treat a rare medical condition, often due to lack of profitability for widespread use.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The protagonist was an orphan raised by her grandmother after her parents' untimely death."
- **Verb**: "The epidemic tragically orphaned countless children throughout the region."
