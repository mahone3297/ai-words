## Pronunciation
/rɪˈzɑːlv/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The act or process of determining something with firmness and determination; a settled purpose or decision. **Example:** "After much contemplation, he finally resolved to change his career path."
2. A solution to a problem or conflict; a settlement or conclusion. **Example:** "The committee reached a resolution on the budget issue."

### Verb
1. To come to a conclusion or make a firm decision about something. **Example:** "She resolved to start a new exercise routine after the New Year."
2. (Computing) To resolve a URL or domain name into its IP address. **Example:** "The server resolved the domain name to its corresponding IP address."

### Adjective
1. Firmly set or determined; resolute. **Example:** "His resolve was unwavering in the face of adversity."
2. (Mathematics) Having a unique value or answer. **Example:** "The equation has a simple algebraic resolution."

## Phrases
- "Resolve a conflict" means to find a way to end a disagreement or dispute.
- "Resolve an issue" refers to finding a solution to a problem.
- "A personal resolution" typically refers to a commitment made at the beginning of a year or a specific goal to achieve.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "His final resolution was to donate half his inheritance to charity."
- **Verb**: "After months of negotiations, they finally resolved their differences."
- **Adjective**: "Her resolve to succeed was evident in her determination to study hard."
