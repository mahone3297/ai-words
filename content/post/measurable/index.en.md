## Pronunciation
/ˈmeʒərəb(ə)l/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Able to be measured; having definite or ascertainable dimensions, quantity, or extent that can be determined by mathematical or empirical methods. 

Example sentence: 
- *The effectiveness of the new drug is measurable through various clinical trials.*
- *This project's success can be measurable in terms of its impact on the local community.*

## Phrases
- "Measurable outcomes": Specific results that can be quantitatively assessed to determine if a goal has been achieved.
- "Measurable quantities": Amounts that can be precisely calculated or estimated using a standard unit of measurement.
- "Achieving measurable progress": Refers to advancing towards a goal in a way that allows one to track and quantify improvements.

## Example Sentences
