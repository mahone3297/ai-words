## Pronunciation
/ˈmɪlk/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A white liquid produced by female mammals (such as cows, goats, or sheep) as food for their young. For example: "She poured a glass of milk for her son's breakfast."
- **Verb**: To extract milk from a mammal, typically through the process of milking. For example: "The farmer rises early each day to milk the cows."
- As a verb, it can also mean to draw something out or obtain something beneficial. For instance: "We need to milk this opportunity for all its worth."

## Phrases
- "Milk and cookies" refers to a traditional snack often enjoyed together.
- "Don't cry over spilled milk" is an idiom meaning not to be upset over something that cannot be undone.
- "To milk something dry" is an expression meaning to take full advantage of something until no benefit is left.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Drinking a glass of warm milk before bed can help promote sleep."
- **Verb**: "She learned how to milk the goats on her uncle's farm."
- **As an idiomatic verb**: "He's been milking his fame ever since he won the championship."
