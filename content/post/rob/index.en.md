## Pronunciation
/rɒb/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A crime in which someone takes something valuable (such as money or property) from another person, often by force or threat. [Oxford](https://en.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/rob)
- **Verb**: To take something (especially money or possessions) from someone by force or illegally. [Cambridge](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/rob)
- **Verb**: (informal) To deceive or cheat someone, especially by taking something that belongs to them. [Merriam-Webster](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/rob)

## Phrases
- "Rob a bank" refers to committing a robbery at a financial institution.
- "Rob Peter to pay Paul" is a phrase meaning to take from one person to give to another, often implying unfairness.
- "Robust" (adjective) describes something that is strong and healthy.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The police were investigating a series of armed robberies in the city."
- **Verb**: "He was robbed of his wallet while jogging in the park."
- **Verb (informal)**: "The company accused him of robbing them of their intellectual property."
