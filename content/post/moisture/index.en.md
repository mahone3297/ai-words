## Pronunciation
/ˈmɔɪsʧər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The presence of water in the air or in a substance, usually in minute quantities, often referring to the dampness or wetness of something. For example: "The high moisture content in the air made it feel humid."
  - "Moisture on her brow indicated that she had been exercising."
- **Verb (Infinitive Form: moisten)**: To make something slightly wet or damp, or to become slightly wet or damp. For example: "The rising temperature caused the ground to moisture with dew."
  - "She moistened her lips before speaking."

## Phrases
- "Moisture retention" refers to the ability of a material to hold or absorb moisture.
- "Moisture-wicking" describes fabrics that draw sweat away from the skin to keep the wearer dry.
- "Moisture content" is the amount of water present in a given substance, often used in contexts like food, soil, or wood.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Too much moisture in the basement can lead to mold growth."
- **Verb**: "She took a sip of water to moisten her parched throat."
