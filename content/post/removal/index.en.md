## Definitions
### Noun
- **Removal**: The act of taking something away or off; the process of removing an object or person from a particular place or position. For example: "The removal of old furniture made room for new decorations."
- **Erasure**: The act of wiping out or deleting written or printed text. For example: "The sensitive information was removed from the document for security reasons."
- **Deletion**: The action of taking out or eliminating data or content. For example: "Unnecessary files were removed to free up storage space."

### Verb
- **To remove**: To take away, detach, or撤除; to clear out. For example: "Remove the old wallpaper before painting the walls."
- **Displacement**: The act of moving something from one place to another without its destruction. For example: "The furniture was removed to make room for a new carpet."

### Adjective
- **Removable**: Capable of being taken away or detached easily. For example: "This shelf is removable, making it easy to clean behind."

### Adverb
- **Remotely**: In a distant or not direct manner. For example: "The event will be broadcast remotely to reach a wider audience."
