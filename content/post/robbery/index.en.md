## Pronunciation
/ˈrɒbəri/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **robbery** is the act of committing a theft, typically by force or threat, with the intent to steal property or money from a person, establishment, or vehicle. It is a crime characterized by violence or the fear of violence.

### Verb
To **rob** (someone) is to forcibly take their possessions or money, often through illegal means.

## Phrases
- "Pull a robbery" refers to committing a robbery.
- "Robbery prevention" is about measures taken to avoid such crimes.
- "Barely escaped a robbery" implies a narrow escape from a criminal attempt.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The police were investigating a string of recent robberies in the neighborhood."
- **Verb**: "The thief was caught red-handed after attempting to rob the convenience store."
- **Sentence**: "The victim recounted how she narrowly escaped a daylight robbery on the street."
