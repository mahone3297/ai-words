## Pronunciation
/ˈleʒər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: Time that is free from work or duties, especially time that one can use for rest, recreation, or personal activities. For example: "In my leisure time, I enjoy reading books and painting."
  - Leisure activities: Pursuits or hobbies engaged in during one's free time, e.g., "Playing chess is one of his favorite leisure activities."
- **Adjective**: Relating to or denoting time that is free from work or duties; having spare time or being at leisure. For instance: "Leisure wear is designed for comfort during off-duty hours."

## Phrases
- "At leisure": Having enough free time to do something without hurry or pressure. E.g., "I'll finish the book when I have time at leisure."
- "Leisure class": A social group with substantial disposable income and ample leisure time, often associated with the wealthy elite.
- "Leisurely pace": Describing an unhurried speed or tempo, as in "We took a leisurely stroll through the park."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After retirement, he had plenty of leisure to pursue his lifelong passion for gardening."
- **Adjective**: "The resort offers a range of leisurely activities for guests to unwind, such as yoga classes and spa treatments."
