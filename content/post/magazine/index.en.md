## Word: Magazine

## Pronunciation
/ˌmæɡəˈziːn/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A publication that is issued periodically (weekly, monthly, quarterly, etc.) and contains articles, stories, pictures, or other content on various subjects or by different authors. Example: "I just bought the latest issue of my favorite fashion magazine."
- **Noun**: A storehouse or supply chamber, especially for ammunition on a warship or aircraft. Example: "The soldiers loaded magazines into their rifles before heading out."
- **Noun** (obsolete): A place for keeping things, such as a warehouse or storage room. Example: "The old warehouse served as a magazine for storing goods."

## Phrases
- "Read it in a magazine": Refers to obtaining information from a magazine article.
- "Gun magazine": Refers specifically to the part of a firearm that holds bullets or cartridges.
- "Subscribe to a magazine": Means to arrange for regular delivery of a magazine.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (publication)**: "She contributed several articles to the popular science magazine."
- **Noun (ammunition)**: "After emptying his gun's magazine, he quickly replaced it with a new one."
- **Obsolete usage**: "The museum curator unlocked the old magazine where they kept valuable artifacts."

## Synonyms
- Periodical
- Journal
- Publication
- Bulletin
- Review (for specialized magazines)
- Clip (informal for a single article from a magazine)
- Ammo clip/mag (for the firearm meaning)
