## Pronunciation
/lʊk/

## Definitions
- **Verb (v)**: To direct one's gaze toward something or someone, often with focus or attention. For example: "He looked out the window at the passing cars."
  - Also used transitively to mean directing an action towards something: "Look at that painting; it's breathtaking."
- **Noun (n)**: The act of directing one's gaze to see or examine something. For instance: "She gave the room a quick look before leaving."
  - Also used metaphorically for a certain appearance or impression: "The house had a worn-out look."
  
## Phrases
- "Take a look" means to glance or inspect something. Example: "Can you take a look at this report before we send it?"
- "Give someone a dirty look" implies disapproval or annoyance through facial expression. Example: "She gave him a dirty look when he interrupted her."
- "If looks could kill" is an idiom used to describe a very angry or hostile expression. Example: "When he saw the mess, he gave me a look that if looks could kill, I'd be dead."

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "She looked up from her book when she heard the doorbell ring."
- **Noun**: "I got a good look at the suspect before he ran away."

### Additional Meanings and Phrases
- **Verb**: To appear or seem to be a particular way. Example: "The situation looks promising."
  - Phrase: "It looks like rain," meaning it appears that rain is about to happen.
- **Adjective (adj)**: Describing the appearance of something or someone. Example: "She chose a dress with a classic look."
- **Idiom**: "To look forward to something" means to anticipate it with pleasure or excitement. Example: "I'm really looking forward to the vacation."
