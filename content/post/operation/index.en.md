## Pronunciation
/ˌɑːpəˈreɪʃən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: (n) A medical procedure involving physical intervention to treat or diagnose a condition, often performed by a surgeon. For example: "The patient underwent a heart operation yesterday."
  - _Also used metaphorically_: The action of operating or functioning; working procedure, especially of a machine or system. "The factory operates on a 24-hour basis."
  - In mathematics or logic, it can refer to a process of calculation or transformation. "This mathematical operation involves adding then multiplying the numbers."
  - In military contexts, an organized activity or campaign designed to achieve a specific objective. "The military operation was meticulously planned."

## Phrases
- "Undergo an operation" means to experience surgery.
- "Military operation" refers to a strategic mission or campaign.
- "Operational procedures" are the established methods of conducting tasks within an organization.
- "In the course of operation" suggests while something is functioning or being executed.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The doctor explained the risks associated with the brain operation."
- "During the IT system's operation, any technical issues should be reported immediately."
- "Their successful marketing operation significantly boosted sales."
