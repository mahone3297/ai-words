## Pronunciation
/səˈspɪʃən/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A **noun** (noun): A feeling or belief that something is likely to be true without proof, often based on indirect evidence or intuition. For example: "She had a suspicion that he was lying."
- An **unfounded belief or guess** about something: "There was a suspicion of foul play in the case."

### Verb
- To **suspect** (verb): To have a feeling that something may be true, without concrete evidence. For example: "The detective began to suspect the defendant of the crime."
- To entertain a doubt or believe something is likely: "She suspected that her neighbor was up to something suspicious."

### Adjective
- **Suspicious** (adjective): Characterized by or showing a lack of trust or confidence; marked by doubt or mistrust. For example: "His behavior was highly suspicious."
- Causing or arousing suspicion: "The package was found to be suspiciously heavy."

## Phrases
- "Suspicions arose" means doubts or suspicions started to form.
- "Under suspicion" refers to being considered guilty or involved in something questionable.
- "Without any suspicion" means without any doubt or disbelief.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Her face fell when she heard the accusations, her suspicion confirmed."
- **Verb**: "The police began to suspect the link between the robberies after reviewing surveillance footage."
- **Adjective**: "The man's secretive behavior made him seem highly suspicious."
