## Pronunciation
/wɪŋ/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Aircraft**: The fixed, usually branched structure that extends from the fuselage of an airplane and supports the wings, providing lift.
   > "The plane's wings were painted in vibrant colors."
- **Body part**: In birds and some insects, a lateral extension of the body that serves for flight or balance.
   > "The bird spread its wings to take off."
- **Attribute**: A side or extremity, as in a house with two wings.
   > "The mansion had three wings, each with its own entrance."

### Verb
- **To fly**: To move through the air using wings or an aircraft.
   > "The eagle spread its wings and soared high."
- **To support**: To give assistance or contribute to something, often metaphorically.
   > "Her encouragement was a wing to his success."

### Adjective
- **Airborne**: Relating to or occurring in the air, especially in flight.
   > "The airborne troops landed behind enemy lines."
- **Covering**: Providing protection or shelter, like a wing on a house.
   > "The roof has extended wings to protect the garden."

## Phrases
- "Winged creatures" refers to animals with the ability to fly using wings.
- "Get your wings" is a metaphorical expression for gaining the skills or experience needed to do something well.
- "Wings of mercy" symbolize compassion and protection.
