## Pronunciation
/rɪˈðəm/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Rhythm**: A natural pattern or flow of sounds, movements, or events that repeat in a regular way, often with a sense of musical beat or timing.
- **Metrical rhythm**: The arrangement of stressed and unstressed syllables in poetry or music.
- **Internal rhythm**: The natural cadence or flow within a sentence or piece of writing.

### Verb
- **To have rhythm**: To exhibit a natural sense of timing or flow, especially in movement or speech.
- **To maintain rhythm**: To keep a consistent pattern or pace.

## Phrases
- "Rhythmic beat": A regular and repeating pattern of sound or movement.
- "Syncopated rhythm": A rhythmic structure that emphasizes off-beat sounds.
- "Rhythm section": In music, the group of instruments responsible for maintaining the beat (usually drums, bass, and piano).

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Her dance moves had a mesmerizing rhythm."
- **Verb**: "He tried to teach his child how to tap their foot to the rhythm of the song."
- **Internal rhythm**: "The author's prose flowed smoothly with a natural internal rhythm."

## Synonyms
- Beat
- Cadence
- Flow
- Tempo
- Ticker
- Pulse

## Antonyms
- Dissonance
- Lack of rhythm
- Staccato (lacking a continuous flow)
