## Pronunciation
/ˈnuːkliər/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Relating to the nucleus of an atom; atomic. For example: "Nuclear energy is generated by the fission of uranium atoms."
  - _Also used metaphorically_ for anything having a central or fundamental role: "The nuclear family consists of parents and their children."

- **Noun**: A nuclear weapon or device. For example: "The threat of a nuclear war loomed over the region."

## Phrases
- "Nuclear energy": The energy released from the nucleus of an atom, typically through nuclear reactions like fission or fusion.
- "Nuclear family": A social unit consisting of two parents and their children, considered as a basic unit in society.
- "Nuclear option": A strategy that may have extreme or far-reaching consequences, often used in political contexts to refer to a decisive but risky move.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The country is investing heavily in nuclear power generation to reduce its reliance on fossil fuels."
- **Noun**: "The international community has been working tirelessly towards global disarmament of nuclear weapons."

## Synonyms
- Atomic (adj.)
- Nuclear-powered (adj.)
- Thermonuclear (adj., referring specifically to fusion reactions)
- Nuclear bomb/bombardment (n.)
- Nuclear reactor/fusion reactor (n.)
