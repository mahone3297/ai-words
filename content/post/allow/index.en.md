## Pronunciation
/əˈləʊ/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: n. v. To give permission or approval for something to happen; to permit. Also, to enable (a person) to do something. *Example:*
  - "The teacher allowed the students to use their phones during breaks."
  - "The boss allows flexible working hours."

- **Noun**: Permission or authorization given to do something. *Example:*
  - "With his parent's allowance, he could buy that new video game."
  - "The company has a strict policy on alcohol consumption during work hours; no allowance is granted."

- **Adjective**: Permissive or not restrictive. *Example:*
  - "The school has a lenient policy on tardiness, allowing some leeway."

## Phrases
- "Allow for" means to consider or accommodate a certain factor or possibility.
- "Allowance" refers to a sum of money given regularly for personal expenses.
- "Not allowing for" indicates something that does not take into account or consider a particular aspect.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "After careful consideration, the supervisor allowed the project deadline to be extended."
- **Noun**: "As a reward for good grades, she received a larger allowance this month."
- **Adjective**: "The new policy doesn't allow for any exceptions to the rules."
