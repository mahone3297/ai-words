## Pronunciation
/səˈpraɪz/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Surprise**: A sudden, unexpected event or occurrence that causes amazement or shock. For example: "The party was a complete surprise."
- **Sudden realization**: A sudden understanding or awareness of something. For example: "A moment of surprise dawned on her as she read the letter."

### Verb
- **To surprise**: To cause astonishment or catch someone off guard by doing something unexpected. For example: "He surprised everyone with his announcement."
- **To be surprised**: To experience feelings of astonishment or amazement. For example: "She was surprised to find her old friend at the door."

## Phrases
- "Surprise someone with a gift" means to give someone an unexpected present.
- "Be prepared for a surprise" alerts others that something unexpected may happen.
- "Surprise attack" refers to a military strategy where forces launch an assault without prior notice.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Her birthday was filled with delightful surprises from friends."
- **Verb**: "He surprised his family by graduating early."
- **Interjection**: "What a surprise to see you here!"
