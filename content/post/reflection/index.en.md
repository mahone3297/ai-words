## Pronunciation
/rɪˈflekʃ(ə)n/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A **reflection** is a(n) **noun** that refers to an image formed when light bounces off a surface, often seen in mirrors or water. It can also be a mental or emotional response to something, or a thought or idea considered after an event.
- Example: "The lake's calm surface reflected the surrounding trees."
- Synonyms: mirror image, reflection, echo, contemplation

### Verb
- As a **verb**, "to reflect" means to consider, contemplate, or show an image back, usually of a surface or situation.
- Example: "The glass reflects the beauty of the sunset."
- Synonyms: contemplate, think about, mirror, show, portray

### Adjective
- Sometimes, "reflective" is used as an **adjective** to describe something that has a reflective surface or quality, or that shows thoughtfulness.
- Example: "A reflective person often pauses to consider their actions."
- Synonyms: reflective, contemplative, pensive, meditative

## Phrases
- "Reflect on one's experiences" means to think about past events.
- "Reflective surfaces" refers to materials that bounce light back, like mirrors.
- "Reflecting on a problem" implies analyzing it before making a decision.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She gazed into the mirror, admiring her reflection."
- **Verb**: "After the meeting, he took some time to reflect on the discussion points."
- **Adjective**: "The garden was surrounded by reflective, shimmering pools."
