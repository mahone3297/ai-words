## Pronunciation
/siːm/

## Definitions
### Verb
1. **Verb (transitive)**: To give the appearance of; to suggest or imply something without being certain. ["The weather seems fine today."]
2. **Verb (intransitive)**: To appear or be perceived as such; to have a particular quality or characteristic. ["Her words seemed sincere."]

### Adjective
1. **Describing perception**: Seeming or appearing to be true or real. ["It seems like a good idea."]
2. **Expressing probability**: Giving the impression that something is likely to happen. ["It seems likely that they will arrive soon."]

## Example Sentences
- **Verb (transitive):** "The old house seemed abandoned, but we later learned it was just unoccupied."
- **Verb (intransitive):** "Her explanation seemed reasonable, so I accepted it."
- **Adjective:** "His success seems almost effortless."
- **Phrases:** "The situation seems dire" or "The solution seems straightforward."
