## Pronunciation
/profəˈsɔːr/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A person who teaches at a university or college, typically at a high level and with a doctorate degree. *Example: The professor delivered an engaging lecture on history.*
- **Adjective**: Designating a person holding a rank or position of teaching and research in a university or college. *Example: She was appointed as a tenured professor.*
- **Verb**: To teach or instruct, especially at a high academic level. *Example: He is scheduled to professor a course on quantum physics.*

## Phrases
- "Teach a professorship" refers to taking on a full-time teaching position.
- "A visiting professor" is a scholar from another institution who temporarily teaches at a different one.
- "The professor's office hours" are the set times when students can meet with their instructor.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The professor's expertise in chemistry was widely recognized."
- **Adjective**: "She became a renowned professor after years of hard work."
- **Verb**: "He invited the professor to mentor his research project."
