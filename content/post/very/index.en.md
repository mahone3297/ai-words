## Pronunciation
/ˈvɛri/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: 1. To a great extent; extremely. For example: "She was very tired after the long journey."
- **Adjective**: 1. Having a high degree of a particular quality; extremely. For example: "The cake was very delicious."
- **Adjective**: 2. (used with comparative adjectives) More so than usual or expected. For example: "This book is much more interesting than the previous one."

## Phrases
- "Very well" means in excellent condition or state.
- "Very good" is used to express approval or satisfaction.
- "Not very" is a negation indicating something is not extreme or significant.

## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "She worked very hard on her project."
- **Adjective**: "The weather was very cold that day."
- **Adjective (comparative)**: "I'm feeling very happy today compared to yesterday."
