## Pronunciation
/kæɪt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Skate**: A device with blades attached to a boot, used for gliding on ice or other surfaces like roller rinks or skateboard parks.
- **Skater**: A person who skates, typically as a sport or hobby.

### Verb
- **To skate**: Perform actions on skates, such as moving, glide, or perform tricks.
- **Skateboarding**: A sport that involves riding a skateboard, often with similar movements to ice skating.

### Adjective
- **Skateboard**: Referring to the equipment used in skateboarding.
- **Skated**: Done or experienced on skates.

## Phrases
- "Skate on thin ice" warns of precarious situations.
- "Skate park": A facility specifically designed for skateboarding and inline skating.
- "Skating season" refers to the time when ice skating is commonly done.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She loved to skate on the frozen lake every weekend."
- **Verb**: "The kids were skating around the rink, showing off their skills."
- **Adjective**: "The skateboarder rode the skateboarded-friendly ramp with ease."

## Synonyms
- Roll (for gliding on skates)
- Skate about (to move on skates)
- Slide (informal term for skating)
- Ice skate (specifically for ice)
- Roller skate (for roller skating)
