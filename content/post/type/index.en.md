## Pronunciation
/həˈloʊ/

## Definitions
### Noun
A term used to greet or address someone. For example: "She waved a friendly hello at the passerby."

### Verb
To say "hello" or greet someone. For example: "He greeted his colleagues with a hearty hello every morning."

### Adjective
Adjective form, not commonly used as a standalone word, but can be found in compound words like "hello-world" (introducing a new software project).

### Interjection
Used to greet, attract attention, or express surprise. For example: "Oh, hello there! What a pleasant surprise!"

## Phrases
- "Give someone a hello" means to acknowledge or greet another person.
- "Hello, how are you?" is a standard greeting to inquire about someone's well-being.
- "Hello, my friend!" is a warm greeting directed towards a familiar acquaintance.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Her cheerful hello brightened up the dreary day."
- **Verb**: "As he entered the room, he heard a chorus of hellos from his friends."
- **Interjection**: "Hello, I've been looking for you all day!"
