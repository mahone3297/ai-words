## Pronunciation
/sɪŋk/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **A device with a basin for holding water, typically one fixed in a countertop or built into a floor, used for washing hands, dishes, or food.**
   Example: "The kitchen sink was full of dirty dishes."
2. **A low point or downward slope in the ground or surface.**
   Example: "The garden had a gentle sink at the edge."
3. **To descend or move down below a certain level or position.**
   Example: "The ship sank to the bottom of the ocean."

### Verb
1. **To go down below the surface, typically under water.**
   Example: "The submarine sank deep into the abyss."
2. **To lose or decrease in value, level, or effectiveness.**
   Example: "The stock price sank after the disappointing earnings report."
3. **To be absorbed or assimilated by something.**
   Example: "The oil soaked into the porous wood."

## Phrases

- "Sink with a sinking feeling" describes a sudden realization of disappointment or worry.
