## Pronunciation
/rɪˈmembər/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Memory**: The faculty or process of holding information in the mind, especially for later recall. For example: "She has a remarkable memory for names."
- **Recollection**: The act of recalling something that one has previously known or experienced. For example: "His recollection of the event was hazy."
- **Retention**: The ability to keep information in one's mind over time. For example: "The students need to retain what they've learned in class."

### Verb
- **To remember**: To have knowledge or awareness of something that has been learned or experienced before. For example: "I'll remember to bring the keys when I leave."
- **To recall**: To call back to mind, especially something that had been forgotten. For example: "He couldn't recall where he left his phone."
- **To retain**: To keep or hold onto (information) in one's mind. For example: "She retained all the details from the lecture."

### Adjective
- **Memorable**: Characterized by being easily remembered or recalled. For example: "The concert was a memorable experience."
- **Foresighted**: Having the ability to remember past experiences and apply them to future situations. For example: "A foresighted leader considers past mistakes when making decisions."

## Phrases
- "Remember to do something" means to keep something in mind as a task or responsibility.
- "Long-term memory" refers to memories that persist over an extended period.
- "Erase from memory" is an idiomatic expression meaning to forget completely.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Her childhood memories were filled with laughter and adventure."
- **Verb**: "I'll remember to call you later."
- **Adjective**: "The book had many memorable quotes."
