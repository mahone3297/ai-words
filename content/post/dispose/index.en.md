## Pronunciation
/dɪˈspəʊz/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Dispose** (countable): The act of getting rid of something, often by throwing it away or arranging for its disposal. For example: "They disposed of their old furniture."
- **Disposed** (past participle, adjective): Tended or having a disposition to; ready or willing to do something. For example: "The garden was beautifully disposed with colorful flowers."

### Verb
- **Dispose** (transitive verb): To get rid of, throw away, or manage the disposal of something. For example: "The company will dispose of the outdated equipment."
- **Dispose of** (phrase): To deal with or manage the final disposition of something, often implying a permanent removal. For example: "They need to dispose of the hazardous waste properly."

### Adjective
- **Disposable** (describing): Intended to be used once and then thrown away. For example: "Disposable razors are convenient for travel."

## Phrases
- "Dispose of waste" means to manage or remove trash.
- "dispose oneself" can mean to settle oneself comfortably or to prepare for a task.
- "dispose of a property" refers to selling or transferring ownership of a piece of real estate.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The company has a dedicated team for disposing of electronic waste."
- **Verb**: "After the renovation, they disposed of the old cabinets."
- **Adjective**: "The hotel provided disposable napkins for guests."
