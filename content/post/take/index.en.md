## Pronunciation
/take/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Meaning**: The act of taking something, usually from someone else's possession or control. For example: "I took the last slice of pizza."
- **Derivation**: From the verb "take," it can also refer to a portion or quantity. For example: "The cake was divided into equal takes."

### Verb
- **Meaning**: To seize, grasp, or receive something, physically or metaphorically. For example: "Take this book and read it."
- **Verb Inflections**: (take, took, taken)
- **Phrasal Verbs**: "take over," "take down," "take apart," "take responsibility"

### Adjective
- **Meaning**: Having the power or ability to do something. For example: "She took charge of the project."
- **Adverb**: In a determined or forceful manner. For example: "He took a deep breath before starting."

## Phrases
- "Take something for granted" assumes something as true without considering its value.
- "Take a break" means to pause or rest for a while.
- "Take a risk" involves acting despite potential danger or uncertainty.
