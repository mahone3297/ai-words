## Pronunciation
/dɛns/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: 1. Having a large number or concentration of particles, people, or things per unit volume, making something appear solid or thick. For example: "The forest was dense with tall trees."
- 2. (Math) Characterized by a high degree of complexity or density in data or information. For example: "A dense dataset requires sophisticated analysis."
- 3. (Physics) Referring to a material with a low permeability to light or other electromagnetic waves. For example: "A dense fog blocked the view."

## Noun (rarely used)
- A quality or condition that is dense, especially when describing something intangible. For example: "The book's subject matter is very dense."

## Adverb
- In a dense manner; closely or thickly. For example: "The crowd was densely packed for the concert."

## Phrases
- "Dense foliage" describes thick vegetation.
- "A dense network" refers to a tightly connected system.
- "Dense packing" means efficient use of space.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The jungle was so dense that it was hard to navigate."
- **Noun**: "The professor explained the dense theory with patience."
- **Adverb**: "She carefully studied the dense data to extract valuable insights."
