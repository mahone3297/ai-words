## Pronunciation
/rɪˈlɔɪdˌrɑːd/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A system of fixed tracks with trains, used for transporting passengers and goods on land, especially over long distances. Example: "The railroad connected two cities, facilitating trade."
- **Verb**: To transport people or goods using a railroad system. Example: "The train was scheduled to railroad passengers from New York to Chicago."
- **Noun (plural, informal)**: An informal term for a railroad company or its employees. Example: "He works for the local railroad."

## Phrases
- "Built a railroad" means constructing a railway.
- "Railroaded a suspect" (informal) implies forcing or manipulating someone into a decision or confession.
- "Switching railroad" refers to the process of moving trains between tracks.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The historic railroad bridge still stands over the river."
- **Verb**: "The freight cars were railroaded across the country."
- **Noun (plural, informal)**: "The railroad workers went on strike to improve their conditions."
