## Pronunciation
/tɛkst/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A written or printed form of words, usually consisting of sentences, that conveys information or ideas: *The document contained several pages of text.*
2. The content of a written work, such as a book, article, or website: *The main text discusses historical events.*
3. The material that fills a book or publication, excluding headings, illustrations, or other non-text elements: *The book's text is dense with details.*

### Verb
1. To write or compose (words, especially in a specific style or format): *She quickly texted a response.*
2. To send or transmit (a message) using a messaging app or service: *He texted his friend good morning.*

### Adjective
1. Relating to or consisting of written words rather than images or symbols: *A text-based game.*
2. In the form of written communication: *Text messages are becoming more common.*

## Phrases
- "Text someone" means to send a message to another person via their phone or messaging app.
- "Textbook" refers to a book used as the primary source of learning material in a course.
- "Text mining" is the process of extracting useful information from large bodies of text data.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The professor asked us to read the entire text before class."
- **Verb**: "I'll text you the directions when we meet up."
- **Adjective**: "Her report was a lengthy text filled with statistics."
