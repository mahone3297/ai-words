## Pronunciation
/mæsk/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A covering for all or part of the face, often worn for disguise, protection, entertainment, or to prevent the spread of infection. For example: "She wore a decorative mask to the masquerade ball."
- **Verb (v)**: To cover or conceal with or as if with a mask. For example: "During the pandemic, it's mandatory to mask your face when entering public spaces."
- **Verb (v)**: To hide or disguise (one's feelings, character, etc.). For example: "She masked her fear with a smile."
- **Verb (v)**: In chemistry, to apply a protective coating to (a surface). For example: "The metal was masked before being spray painted."

## Phrases
- "Put on a brave face/mask": To pretend to be confident or untroubled despite feeling the opposite.
- "Behind the mask": Refers to the true identity or intentions that are hidden beneath a facade.
- "Mask up": A colloquial phrase meaning to put on a protective face mask.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Doctors and nurses wore medical masks during surgery to maintain a sterile environment."
- **Verb**: "In the play, the actor masked his features with a white Venetian mask."
- **Verb**: "He tried to mask his disappointment when he heard the news."
