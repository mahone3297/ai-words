## Pronunciation
/ˈwaɪdθ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A measure of the breadth or extension from side to side of something, typically a flat or rectangular object. For example: "The width of the room is 12 feet."
- **Adjective**: Characteristic of being wide or broad. For example: "The river had a generous width for swimming."

## Phrases
- "Width of a hair" describes an extremely narrow margin or difference.
- "The width of a doorway" refers to the dimensions needed to pass through.
- "Breath width" is used to describe the breadth of someone's breathing.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The bridge was designed to accommodate the maximum width of the passing vehicles."
- **Adjective**: "The artist stretched the canvas to achieve a dramatic width-to-height ratio."
- "The child measured the width of the river with a stick."
