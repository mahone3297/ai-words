## Word: Inherit

## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈhɛrɪt/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To receive or acquire (property, titles, rights, etc.) as a result of a family member's death, especially from one's parents. It can also refer to receiving characteristics or traits genetically. For example:
  - "She inherited a fortune from her grandfather."
  - "He inherited his mother's artistic talent."

- **Noun**: (n) Something that is passed down or received from ancestors, such as property, characteristics, or a title. For example:
  - "The house was part of his inheritance."
  - "The disease is an unfortunate inheritance from a distant relative."

## Phrases
- "Inherit the Earth" refers to gaining possession or reward in a future life, often used metaphorically to describe a positive outcome after persevering through adversity.
- "Inheritance tax" is a tax paid on the value of property or money that someone inherits.
- "To inherit something from someone" means to legally or naturally obtain it upon their passing or due to their will.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "When her father passed away, she inherited his entire estate."
- **Noun**: "The antique furniture was part of the family inheritance."

## Synonyms
- Bequeath
- Devolve
- Pass down
- Receive by succession
- Gain through heritage
- Acquire by inheritance
- Inheritorship
- Genetic transmission (in the context of traits)
