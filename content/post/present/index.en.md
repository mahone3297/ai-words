## Pronunciation
/presnt/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The current time or period, as opposed to the past or future. "The present moment."
2. Something that is given or offered now, as opposed to a future or past gift. "A present for your birthday."
3. A form of payment or settlement given in return for goods or services. "He paid for the meal in cash, with a present check."

### Verb
1. To give or offer something as a gift, typically at a particular time or occasion. "She presented him with a bouquet of flowers."
2. To exhibit or show (something) publicly. "The museum will present a new exhibition next month."
3. To be currently available or occurring. "The book is currently available for purchase."

### Adjective
1. Existing or happening now. "The present situation is challenging."
2. Characteristic of the present time or place. "Modern technology is a defining feature of the present era."
3. Given or offered as a gift. "A present-day wedding tradition."

### Adverb
1. At the present time; currently. "We will discuss the matter presently."

## Phrases
- "Be present" means to be fully engaged in the current moment.
- "Present company excluded" is a humorous way to exclude oneself from a general statement.
- "Present tense" refers to verbs describing actions happening now.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She cherished the present she received on her birthday."
- **Verb**: "The artist will present her latest work at the gallery opening."
- **Adjective**: "The present weather conditions are ideal for outdoor activities."
- **Adverb**: "Let's meet presently in the conference room."
