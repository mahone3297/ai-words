## Pronunciation
/rɪˈmeɪdi/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. A means of curing or correcting a problem or fault; a cure or solution. "The doctor prescribed a natural remedy for her headache."
   2. A medicine or preparation used to treat a disease or ailment. "The patient took the recommended remedy twice a day."

- **Verb**: To apply a cure or treatment to; to administer or provide a remedy for. "She tried several remedies before finding one that worked."

## Phrases
- "Find a remedy for" implies searching for a solution.
- "A home remedy" refers to a traditional or simple solution often found at home.
- "No known remedy exists" means no cure has been discovered.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The herbal remedy seemed to alleviate his symptoms."
- **Verb**: "He tried various remedies to soothe his sore throat."
- **Intransitive Verb**: "Her new workout routine proved to be a quick remedy for her insomnia."

## Synonyms
- Treatment
- Cure
- Solution
- Prescription
- Antidote
- Panacea (a universal cure)
- Remedy (informal or archaic)

## Antonyms
- Aggravate
- Worsen
- Exacerbate

## Note:
In British English, the verb form might be "remedy oneself" instead of "to remedy for."
