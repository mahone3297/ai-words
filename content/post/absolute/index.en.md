## Pronunciation
/ˈæbsəluːt/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: 
  - Complete and total, without any exception or qualification. For example: "The evidence points to an absolute truth."
  - Not relative; independent of external factors or conditions. For example: "Absolute zero is the lowest possible temperature."
  - (of power or a ruler) Unrestricted by constitutional or other limits. For example: "The king ruled with absolute power."

- **Noun**: 
  - The ultimate standard or principle. For example: "Morality has no absolute."
  - (usually in plural form) An absolute value. For example: "Find the absolutes of -3 and 3."

## Phrases
- "Absolute certainty" refers to something known with complete confidence.
- "Absolute beginner" denotes someone who has no prior knowledge or experience in a particular subject.
- "In absolute terms" means considering something without comparison or relation to other factors.
