## Pronunciation
/bləʊ/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A violent force of air**: The act or force of blowing air out; a gust. Example: "The wind blew the leaves off the trees."
- **An instance of expelling air from one's lungs**: A breath; a puff. Example: "He took a deep breath and blew out the candles."
- **A sudden burst or release**: A sudden event or happening. Example: "Her success was a big blow to her competitors."

### Verb
- **To expel air forcibly**: To exhale; to breathe out. Example: "He blew out the candles on the cake."
- **To hit or strike with force**: To deliver a hard hit or push. Example: "The football player blew past the defender."
- **To cause damage or disruption**: To have a negative impact. Example: "The news of the cancellation was a blow to their plans."

### Adjective
- **Impressive or forceful**: Characterized by strength or power. Example: "His performance was a real blow to the audience."
- **Casual or informal**: A relaxed, laid-back manner. Example: "We had a blowout party last weekend."

### Adverb
- **With force or explosiveness**: With great speed or intensity. Example: "She blew the whistle loudly."

## Phrases

- "Blow away" (to be impressed or overcome by something)
- "Blow someone a kiss" (to gesture affectionately)
