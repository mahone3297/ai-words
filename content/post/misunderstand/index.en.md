## Word: Misunderstand

## Pronunciation
/ˌmɪsʌndərˈstænd/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To fail to understand something correctly; to have a mistaken idea about the meaning or intention of something.

### Examples
- **Verb**: "I think there's been a misunderstanding; I didn't mean to imply that you were lying."
  - "They completely misunderstood the instructions and ended up assembling the furniture incorrectly."
  
## Phrases
- "Misunderstand something": To comprehend something inaccurately or wrongly.
- "Misunderstand someone": To not grasp what a person meant or intended when they spoke or acted.
- "Clear up a misunderstanding": Resolve or rectify a situation where there has been a misinterpretation or confusion.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "It was clear from her reaction that she had misunderstood the joke."
- "There seems to be a misunderstanding between us regarding the project deadline."
