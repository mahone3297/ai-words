## Pronunciation
/ˈpɛərənt/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Parent**: A person who brings up or gives birth to a child, or one of the two individuals (usually a mother and a father) who combine to produce offspring.
- **Legal parent**: A person recognized by law as having parental rights and responsibilities, such as custody or guardianship.

### Verb
- **To parent**: To act as a parent, providing care, guidance, and support to a child or child-like individual.
- **To parentify**: To treat someone, especially an adult, as if they were a child or to imbue them with a sense of being a parent.

## Phrases
- "Parental responsibility" refers to the duties and obligations that come with raising a child.
- "Single-parent household" describes a family where only one parent is present to raise children.
- "Co-parenting" refers to the cooperative arrangement between divorced or separated parents in sharing parenting responsibilities.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Both the mother and father are devoted parents to their three children."
- **Verb**: "As a single parent, she had to juggle work and childcare responsibilities."
- **Phrasal verb**: "After the divorce, they worked out a co-parenting plan for their kids."
