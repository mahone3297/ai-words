## Pronunciation
/prɪˈzɜrv/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The act or process of preserving food, artifacts, or other items by preventing decay or damage through methods like pickling, canning, or freezing.
   - Example: "The museum preserves historical artifacts from various cultures."
2. The state of being preserved or protected from harm or destruction.
   - Example: "The old house was lovingly preserved by the family."

### Verb
1. To keep something in its original condition or freshness by using preservation techniques.
   - Example: "She decided to preserve the vintage furniture for future generations."
2. To maintain or conserve (a resource, tradition, or quality).
   - Example: "We must preserve the delicate ecosystem for future wildlife."

## Phrases
- "Preserve food" means to prepare it to last longer without spoiling.
- "Preserve a memory" refers to keeping a cherished recollection alive.
- "Preserved history" refers to well-preserved records or remnants of the past.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The local community has a program to preserve their natural heritage."
- **Verb**: "She carefully preserved her grandmother's old letters in a safe place."
- **As a verb**: "The gardeners are working hard to preserve the native plants in the area."
