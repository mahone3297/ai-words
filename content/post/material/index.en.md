## Pronunciation
/ˈmætərɪəl/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: (n) 1. Substance or matter from which something can be made or composed; stuff. E.g., "The chair was made of solid wood material."
   2. Information, facts, or ideas that form the basis of a subject, discussion, or study. E.g., "The course covered a lot of material on environmental science."
   3. Goods, supplies, or resources needed for a particular purpose or activity. E.g., "Building materials were delivered to the construction site."
   4. (in the arts) The content or substance of a creative work. E.g., "The film's raw and powerful material explores themes of love and loss."

- **Adjective**: (adj) Relating to physical substances or the practical aspects of something rather than spiritual or intellectual matters. E.g., "Material possessions don't guarantee happiness."

## Phrases
- "Material goods": tangible items of economic value.
- "Material evidence": physical proof or documentation that supports a claim or argument.
- "Materialistic": having a strong desire for material wealth or possessions.
