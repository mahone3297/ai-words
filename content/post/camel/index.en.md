## Pronunciation
/kæməl/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Camel** (noun, plural: camels): A large, long-necked, hump-backed, and long-limbed mammal native to arid regions, particularly Africa and Asia. It is known for its ability to store fat in its humps and tolerate long periods without water.
2. [CamelCase](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CamelCase) (computing): A writing style in which words are connected without spaces, with the first letter of each word capitalized, e.g., "ThisIsACamelCaseExample".

### Verb
- Not directly used as a verb in standard English, but "to ride a camel" is a common phrase.

### Adjective
- Not commonly used as an adjective in English.

## Phrases
- "Ride a camel" or "ride on a camel's back"
- "A dromedary camel" (referring to the one-humped variety)
- "Bactrian camel" (referring to the two-humped variety)

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The nomads relied heavily on their camels for transportation across the desert."
- **Adjective**: "The dromedary camel's unique features make it well-suited to its harsh environment."
- **Verb**: "The tourists took turns riding the camels through the Sahara Desert."
