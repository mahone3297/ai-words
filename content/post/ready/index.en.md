## Pronunciation
/rɪˈdeɪ/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. **Ready** (adj): In a state that is prepared or able to perform a task or respond to a situation. For example: "The team is ready to start the game."
2. **Ready-made** (adj): Pre-assembled or produced without customization. For example: "I bought a ready-made suit from the store."

### Verb
1. **To be ready** (phrasal verb): To have completed all necessary preparations or be in a position to act. For example: "We're ready to leave when you are."
2. **To get ready** (infinitive): To prepare oneself for an activity or event. For example: "She gets ready for bed every evening at 10 PM."

### Adverb
1. **Readily** (adv): Without difficulty or hesitation. For example: "She readily agreed to the proposal."

## Phrases
- "Be ready by 5 PM" means to be prepared to arrive at 5 PM.
- "Ready and willing" expresses a person's eagerness to participate or cooperate.
- "Ready to face the day" signifies being prepared for whatever challenges might come.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The kitchen is ready for the guests to serve themselves."
- **Verb**: "After a long day at work, it's nice to finally be ready for bed."
- **Adverb**: "She speaks English readily, having lived abroad for several years."
