## Pronunciation
/ˈmʌltɪplaɪ/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To calculate or find the product of two or more numbers. For example: "To multiply 4 by 5, you get 20."
  - Also used metaphorically to mean increase in quantity, size, or number rapidly. Example: "The population of the city multiplied over the last decade."
- **Adjective**: (adj) Relating to multiplication or involving multiple instances. Example: "Multiplying factors can affect the outcome of the experiment."

## Phrases
- "Multiply by" means to calculate the product of one number with another. Example: "To solve this equation, we need to multiply 8 by 3."
- "Multiply oneself" refers to making many copies or reproducing on a large scale. Example: "Bacteria can multiply themselves very quickly under the right conditions."
  
## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The teacher asked us to multiply 7 and 9, which equals 63."
- "In business, it's essential to invest in ventures that will multiply your initial investment."
- **Adjective**: "The multiplying effects of compound interest can significantly grow your savings."
