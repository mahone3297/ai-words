## Pronunciation
/pəˈlɪsmən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A member of a police force, typically one who patrols an area to maintain law and order. [Oxford](https://en.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/policeman)
- **Verb**: To act as a policeman or policewoman, enforcing laws and maintaining public safety. [Cambridge](https://dictionary.cambridge.org/dictionary/english/policeman)

## Phrases
- "On duty as a policeman" refers to being actively engaged in law enforcement work.
- "A traffic policeman" is a specialist responsible for traffic control.
- "Off-duty policeman" indicates a police officer not currently in uniform or performing official duties.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The policeman saw a suspicious person lurking around the corner."
- **Verb**: "The retired policeman now volunteers at a local community center."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "He quickly called for backup when he realized he was dealing with a dangerous situation."

## Synonyms
- Policewoman
- Law enforcement officer
- Cop
- Officer
- Constable
- Patrolman
- Sheriff (in some regions)

## Antonyms
- Criminal
- Suspect
- Offender
- Lawbreaker

## Notes
- The term "policeman" is primarily used in British English, while "police officer" is more common in American English.
