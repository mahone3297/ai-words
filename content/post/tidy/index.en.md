## Pronunciation
/taɪˈdi/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A adjective describing a well-organized and clean state or appearance. For example: "The house was very tidy after the cleaning."
- A habit or inclination to keep one's living or working space organized. For example: "She has a very tidy mind, always putting things in their place."

### Verb
- To make something neat and orderly, especially with respect to personal belongings or a living space. For example: "I'll tidy up the kitchen before we have guests over."
- To put away or organize items. For example: "She tidied up her desk after finishing her work for the day."

### Adjective
- Characterizing someone who is meticulous or careful in maintaining order. For example: "He is a very tidy person, always keeping his room spotless."

## Phrases
- "Keep things tidy" means to maintain cleanliness and organization.
- "Tidy up" implies straightening and cleaning.
- "A tidy budget" refers to a well-managed financial plan.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Her room was always a tidy sanctuary from the chaos of the rest of the house."
- **Verb**: "I need to tidy up the garage before the sale this weekend."
- **Adjective**: "The children were encouraged to be tidy and responsible with their belongings."
