## Pronunciation
/ˈsʌm/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A sum** is the total amount of something, usually money or numbers, when added together: "The total sum of the bills was $100."
- In mathematics, it refers to a series or collection: "The geometric sum of a sequence is the sum of all its terms."

### Verb
- **To sum up** means to summarize or give a brief account: "Let's sum up the main points from the presentation."
- **To add up** signifies the act of calculating the total: "All the numbers add up to 50."

## Phrases
- "Sum up the day" is a request to briefly recollect events.
- "The sum of our efforts" represents the collective accomplishment.
- "Pay the sum owed" means to settle a financial debt.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The accountant tallied the sum of all the receipts."
- **Verb**: "After discussing the issue, we finally summed up our disagreements."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "Her grades don't quite add up, there must be an error."
