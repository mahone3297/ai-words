## Pronunciation
/ˈʃɔːrtkʌmɪŋ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A noun referring to a deficiency, limitation, or imperfection; a shortcoming or weakness. For example: "Her biggest shortcoming was her lack of self-confidence."
- **Adjective**: Characterizing something as having a defect or not being adequate in some aspect. For example: "The design had several technical shortcomings."
- **Verb**: To point out or refer to a shortcoming, fault, or imperfection. For example: "The teacher highlighted the students' shortcoming in time management."

## Phrases
- "Addressing a shortcoming" means to focus on or work to improve a deficiency.
- "Recognize one's own shortcoming" involves acknowledging personal weaknesses.
- "Mitigate shortfalls" means to compensate for or reduce the effects of a deficiency.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "His inability to speak a second language was a significant shortcoming in his job application."
- **Adjective**: "Despite her talent, her performance lacked the creativity that would have made it truly outstanding, due to its technical shortcomings."
- **Verb**: "The mentor encouraged the student to actively work on overcoming their shortcoming in public speaking."
