## Pronunciation
/rɪˈvaɪl/

## Definitions
### Noun
A rival is a person or thing that competes with another, often for the same goal or recognition. It can refer to an equal or opponent in a contest, market, or relationship.

### Verb
To rival (as a noun) can be used transitively, meaning to compete with or be a match for someone or something in terms of quality, importance, or impact.

### Adjective
Rivalry can describe a situation where two or more parties are in competition or strive to outdo each other.

## Phrases
- "Rival companies" indicate businesses that compete directly.
- "Their academic rivalry" signifies a competition between individuals or institutions in academics.
- "She has a rival for the job position."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "His new invention is a serious rival to the established product."
- **Verb**: "The two athletes are evenly matched, each trying to rival the other's record."
- **Adjective**: "The city's soccer teams have a fierce rivalry that dates back decades."
