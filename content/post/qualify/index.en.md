## Pronunciation
/ˈkwɒlɪfaɪ/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Qualification**: A standard or requirement that must be met in order to be considered suitable or acceptable for a particular purpose or position. For example: "A degree is a necessary qualification for this job."
- **Qualified**: Having the necessary skills, knowledge, or experience to perform a task adequately. For example: "She is a qualified teacher with years of experience."

### Verb
- **To qualify**: To meet the conditions or requirements needed for something, such as a job, certification, or eligibility. For example: "Many candidates will need to qualify for the scholarship after submitting their applications."
- **To qualify for**: Be eligible to receive or participate in something based on specific criteria. For example: "Employees who work over 40 hours per week may qualify for overtime pay."

## Phrases
- "Qualify for promotion" means to meet the criteria for advancing to a higher position.
- "Qualifying exam" is an assessment that one must pass to become eligible for a certain status or opportunity.
- "Lack of qualifications" refers to not having the necessary skills or credentials for a role.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She was proud to have earned her master's degree, which was a significant qualification for her career."
- **Verb**: "After months of hard work, he finally qualified for the marathon."
- **Interjection**: "Congratulations, you've qualified for the final round!"
