## Pronunciation
/ˈnjuːklɪəs/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: The central part of an atom that contains protons and neutrons, around which electrons orbit. It's the core of an atom where most of its mass is concentrated. For example: "The nucleus of an atom is made up of protons and neutrons."
- **Noun (n)**: In biology, it refers to the central part of a cell that contains most of the genetic material. For example: "The nucleus controls many of the functions of the cell by regulating gene expression."

### Example Sentences
- **Atomic Nucleus**: "Understanding the structure of the nucleus is crucial to nuclear physics."
- **Cell Nucleus**: "In eukaryotic cells, the nucleus houses the DNA within a double membrane structure called the nuclear envelope."

## Phrases
- "Nuclear family": A social unit consisting of two parents and their children, often considered the basic unit in society, with the parents acting as the nucleus around which the family revolves.
- "Nucleus accumbens": A region in the basal forebrain that plays a significant role in reward, pleasure, and addiction; often referred to in neuroscience.

## Synonyms

- Kernel (metaphorical use, referring to the essence or central part)
