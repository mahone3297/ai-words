## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈfɜːr/

## Definitions
- **Verb (v)**: To deduce something as logical consequence from specific facts or evidence; to arrive at a conclusion based on reasoning rather than direct evidence. For example: "From his body language, I inferred that he was not interested in the conversation."
  
  > "The detective inferred from the footprints that the suspect had been at the scene."
- **Verb (v)**: In a more formal or technical context, to derive by reasoning; to conclude or interpret information indirectly. For example: "Scientists inferred patterns of migration from DNA analysis."

## Phrases
- "To infer something from something else": To draw a conclusion based on what one has observed or learned.
- "To make an inference": The act of deducing or inferring a conclusion based on premises.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "Based on the witness's testimony, the jury was able to infer the defendant's guilt."
- "She inferred from his silence that he was upset about the news."
