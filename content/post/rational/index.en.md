## Pronunciation
/rəˈtʃuənl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Of, relating to, or guided by reason or logic; sensible and based on sound thinking. For example: "Her decision was entirely rational."
- **Noun**: A rational person or principle. For example: "A rational investor considers market trends before making decisions."
- **Verb**: To think or act rationally; to base one's actions on reason. For example: "It's important to rationalize your emotions when making big decisions."

## Phrases
- "Act rationally" implies making a logical decision or response.
- "Rational explanation" refers to a clear and logical account of something.
- "A rational choice" is one made after careful consideration of the available options.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "His decision to save money was a perfectly rational one."
- **Noun**: "The economist provided a rational analysis of the economic situation."
- **Verb**: "She tried to rationalize her fear before facing her phobia."
