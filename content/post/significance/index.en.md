## Pronunciation
/sɪgˈnɪfɪkəns/

## Definitions
### Noun
- The importance, meaning, or relevance of something; the quality of being significant or notable.
- A measure or degree of how important something is.
- **Example**: The significance of this discovery cannot be overstated.
- **Synonyms**: importance, meaning, value, relevancy, consequence

### Verb
- To attribute importance or relevance to something; to consider something as having significance.
- To understand or interpret the meaning or implications of something.
- **Example**: The event holds great significance in our history.
- **Synonyms**: assign significance, recognize, interpret, perceive

### Adjective
- Characterized by or expressing importance or meaning; having or showing significance.
- **Example**: The study had profound significance for the field.
- **Synonyms**: meaningful, substantial, important, relevant

## Phrases
- "The significance of..." introduces a discussion about the importance or meaning of a topic.
- "This decision carries great significance."
- "To overlook its significance would be a mistake."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: The significance of his words was lost on some of the audience.
- **Verb**: She pondered the significance of her dream before sharing it with her therapist.
- **Adjective**: The treaty's significance was widely acknowledged.
