## Pronunciation
/rɪˈpʌblɪk/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. A form of government in which power is held by the people, typically organized with elected representatives, and not a monarch or an absolute ruler. 2. A country or state that has this type of government. Example: The United States is a federal republic.
- **Adjective**: Characterized by or typical of a republic, especially one without a hereditary ruler. Example: A republic's constitution often emphasizes the rule of law.

## Phrases
- "Form a republic" means to establish a republic as a political system.
- "Representative republic" describes a form of government where citizens elect representatives to govern on their behalf.
- "Republicanism" is a political ideology based on the principles of a republic.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The newly elected president of the republic pledged to uphold the constitution's values."
- **Adjective**: "Many Latin American countries have a long history of democratic republicanism."
- **Verb**: "The citizens of the republic participated actively in the national election process."
