## Pronunciation
/nəʊ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A white, crystalline solid formed by the freezing and deposition of water, falling from clouds; also used figuratively for any heavy precipitation or flurry of particles. For example: "The children built a snowman in their backyard."
- **Verb**: To fall as snow, or to cover with snow. For example: "The ground was snowing heavily after the winter storm."
- **Adjective**: White, powdery, or consisting of ice crystals, typical of winter weather. For example: "A blanket of fresh snow covered the trees."
- **Noun**: (informal) An event or situation causing excitement or distraction. For example: "The sudden snowstorm caused chaos on the roads."

## Phrases
- "It's snowing heavily" describes heavy snowfall.
- "A dusting of snow" refers to a light layer of snow.
- "Snowball fight" is a playful activity involving throwing snowballs.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She watched the snowflakes dance in the moonlight."
- **Verb**: "The farmer plowed through the freshly fallen snow."
- **Adjective**: "The winter landscape was blanketed in crisp, white snow."
- **Noun**: "The news of the unexpected snow caused a stir among the community."
