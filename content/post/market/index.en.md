## Pronunciation
/ˈmɑːrkɪt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A place where goods are bought and sold, often outdoors; a regular gathering of people for the purchase and sale of provisions, livestock, etc. For example: "The farmers' market sells fresh produce every weekend."
- **Noun**: The activity or process of buying and selling goods and services; the system by which this is done. For example: "The global financial market experienced a downturn last year."
- **Noun**: A group of people or organizations that buy a particular product or service, considered as a whole. For example: "They are targeting the youth market for their new video game."
- **Verb**: To sell (goods) in a market; to make available for sale to the public. For example: "The company markets its products worldwide."
- **Verb**: To promote or advertise (a product, idea, etc.) so as to increase sales or popularity. For example: "They will market the new app through social media campaigns."

## Phrases
- "Market share": The proportion of total sales in an industry or market that a company accounts for.
- "Black market": An illegal traffic or trade in goods, especially one conducted without government approval.
- "In the market for something": Looking to buy a specific item or type of product.
- "Marketing strategy": A plan to promote and sell a product or service.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The weekly market was bustling with vendors selling handmade crafts and local delicacies."
- **Verb**: "They have marketed their eco-friendly products successfully to environmentally conscious consumers."
- **Noun (as a target audience)**: "This new smartphone is specifically designed for the tech-savvy market."
