## Pronunciation
/pəˈneɪtr/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The act of passing through, drilling, or forcing one's way into something, usually a solid object or medium. Example: "The needle penetrated the fabric."
2. Influence or impact that reaches an extent or level. Example: "Her words penetrated his understanding."

### Verb
1. To go through, pass, or make one's way into something, often with force or effort. Example: "The sunlight penetrated the thick forest."
2. To understand or comprehend something deeply. Example: "His explanation penetrated her confusion."

### Adjective
1. Capable of entering or passing through something. Example: "The pen is a highly penetrative tool."
2. Having a deep or profound effect. Example: "Her voice had a penetrating quality."

### Adverb
1. In a penetrating manner, reaching to the core or essence. Example: "He spoke in a penetrating tone."

## Phrases
- "Penetrate the barrier" means to break through a protective layer or obstacle.
- "Penetrating gaze" describes a look that seems to see through someone or something.
- "Penetration testing" is a cybersecurity practice to assess system vulnerabilities.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The rainwater penetrated the roof, causing leaks."
- **Verb**: "The spy satellite penetrated enemy territory undetected."
- **Adjective**: "Her insights were particularly penetrating."
- **Adverb**: "His voice was penetrating as he announced the news."
