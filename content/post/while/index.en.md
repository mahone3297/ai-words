## Pronunciation
/wɪl/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: Expressing duration or condition during which something occurs; typically used with 'while' in clauses to indicate that an action takes place during another action. For example: "I'll wait here while you finish your work."
- **Preposition**: In some cases, used to introduce a clause that contrasts with the main clause, often indicating a concurrent or contrasting action. For example: "While he was sleeping, she studied for her exam."

## Phrases
- "While I was cooking, the phone rang."
- "While you're away, I'll take care of the pets."
- "While the others were celebrating, she remained quiet."

## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "While we were walking in the park, it started raining."
- **Preposition**: "While he was busy, his friends came over unannounced."

## Synonyms
- Concurrently
- At the same time
- In the meantime
- During that time
- In the process

## Contrasting Examples (Antonyms)
- Until (denotes time until a condition is met)
- After (indicates time following an action)
- Despite (despite the fact) (contrast in meaning, not time)
