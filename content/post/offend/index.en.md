## Pronunciation
/ənˈdend/

## Definitions
- **Verb (v)**: To cause annoyance, anger, or resentment in someone; to violate a moral or social code or law. For example: "His rude comments really offended her."
  - Also, to transgress against someone's religious or moral beliefs. Example: "The play was considered offensive by some members of the community because it contained explicit language."
  
- **Noun (n)**: The act of offending or hurting someone's feelings, principles, or sensibilities. Example: "She offered an apology for her unintentional offense."

## Phrases
- "Offend against": To go against or violate something. Example: "He offended against the company's policies with his unethical behavior."
- "Take offense": To become upset or angry due to something said or done. Example: "She took offense when he made a joke about her appearance."
- "Without offense to": Said to indicate that one does not intend to upset or offend anyone with what they're about to say or do. Example: "Without offense to anyone, I think we need to reconsider this plan."

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "His insensitive remarks offended many people in the audience."
- **Noun**: "She was deeply sorry for any offense her words might have caused."
