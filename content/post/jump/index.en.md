## Pronunciation
/dʒʌmp/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A sudden upward movement using one's legs to propel oneself through the air. For example: "The athlete executed a perfect jump over the hurdle."
- **Verb**:
  - (intransitive) To leap suddenly or quickly upwards or forwards with force from a standing or seated position. For example: "The child jumped excitedly when they saw the presents."
  - (transitive) To propel oneself through the air by pushing off the ground with one's feet. For example: "She jumped across the stream."
  - (figurative) To move or act suddenly or quickly. For example: "Sales jumped after the new product launch."
  
- **Adjective**: (informal) Describing something that causes a sudden increase or burst of energy. For example: "The music was so upbeat and jump, it got everyone on their feet dancing."

## Phrases
- "Jump for joy" means to show extreme happiness by jumping up and down.
- "Take the jump" can mean to commit to a significant decision or action, often with risk involved.
- "Jump start" refers to giving a boost to a vehicle's battery or figuratively to give a quick boost to a project or idea.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The high jump event showcased some incredible athleticism."
- **Verb**: "The frog jumped into the pond as soon as it sensed danger."
- **Adjective**: "The band's new single has a real jump feel to it, reminiscent of old swing music."
