## Pronunciation
/ˈɒpəreɪt/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To control or manage the functioning of a machine, system, or organization. For example: "The operator has to know how to operate this complex machinery."
  - _Also used figuratively:_ "She operates on the principle of honesty and fairness."
- **Noun**: (n) The act of operating something; a procedure or process, especially in medicine. For example: "The patient underwent a delicate surgical operation."
- **Noun**: (n) A business or professional activity. For example: "They run a successful transportation operation."

## Phrases
- "Operate on someone/something": To perform surgery or carry out a mechanical or technical procedure on someone or something. Example: "The surgeon will have to operate on his knee."
- "Operate under [conditions/rules]": To function or work within certain constraints or principles. Example: "The company operates under strict environmental guidelines."
- "Get up and running/operational": To start functioning or become active. Example: "It took us several weeks to get the new software fully operational."

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "He's been trained to operate the crane safely."
- **Noun (procedure)**: "The IT team performed a system update operation overnight."
- **Noun (business)**: "Their logistics operation spans across three continents."
