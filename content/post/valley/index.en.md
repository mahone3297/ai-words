## Pronunciation
/ˈveɪli/

## Definitions
### Noun
A valley is a low area between hills or mountains, typically with a river or stream flowing through it. It can also be used metaphorically to describe a low point or a depression in something. 

### Verb (Transitive)
To valley, or valley oneself, can mean to descend into a valley or pass through one, often figuratively in terms of a situation or experience.

### Adjective
1. Geographical: Characterized by a valley, having a low, elongated depression.
2. Metaphorical: Describing a decline or downturn in a process, trend, or condition.

## Phrases
- "Valley of the gods" refers to a scenic, picturesque valley.
- "Sink into a valley of despair" implies a deep emotional low.
- "Life in the valley" can describe a peaceful or sheltered existence.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The hikers reached the base of the mountain, admiring the beauty of the valley below."
