## Pronunciation
/sprɛɪ/

## Definitions
### Noun
- A **spray** is a substance, often in the form of a fine mist or particles, that is emitted from a container or device for various purposes, such as cleaning, marking, or protection.
- In gardening, it can refer to a type of fertilizer or pesticide.
- A **spray bottle** is a container with a nozzle for dispensing a spray.

### Verb
- To **spray** means to apply a liquid in a fine mist or stream, typically using a can or pump.
- Can also refer to the act of emitting or dispersing something, like gas or particles.

### Adjective
- **Spray-coated** describes a surface that has been treated with a spray application.
- **Spray-dried** refers to food products that have been dried by a spraying process.

## Phrases
- "Spray paint" for artistic or marking purposes.
- "Hose spray" for water from a hose.
- "Air freshener spray" for home or car deodorization.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The gardener used a hose spray to water the plants."
- **Verb**: "She sprayed the furniture with a protective coating."
- **Adjective**: "The newly spray-painted wall looked vibrant."

## Antonyms
- Solid (for the substance being emitted)
