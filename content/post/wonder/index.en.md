## Pronunciation
/wʌndər/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **wonder** (noun): A feeling of surprise, admiration, or curiosity at something extraordinary or difficult to understand. For example: "The child's imagination was filled with wonders of the universe."
- **amazement** (noun): A strong sense of wonder and surprise at something impressive. For example: "Their achievement sparked amazement among their peers."
- **奇迹** (Chinese): A remarkable event that seems unlikely or impossible.

### Verb
- **to wonder** (verb): To think or feel unsure about something, or to be curious or puzzled by it. For example: "She couldn't help but wonder how they managed to finish the project so quickly."
- **to marvel** (verb): To be filled with wonder and admiration over something. For example: "They marveled at the breathtaking view from the mountain top."

### Adjective
- **wonderful** (adjective): Extremely good or pleasing; delightful. For example: "The weather was wonderful for a picnic."
- **marvelous** (adjective): Impressive or extraordinary in quality. For example: "The magician's performance was absolutely marvelous."

## Phrases
- "Wonders of the world" refers to remarkable or impressive natural or human-made features.
- "Wonder what..." is used to express curiosity or speculation. "I wonder what she's thinking."
- "Childhood wonder" describes the sense of amazement and excitement felt during early years.
