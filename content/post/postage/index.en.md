## Pronunciation
/postɪdʒ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: (countable) The amount of money paid for the conveyance of letters, packets, or goods by mail, usually according to weight or size. For example: "The cost of international postage has increased recently."
- **Verb**: To affix or pay for the postage on a letter or package. For example: "Please remember to post the letter before leaving."

## Phrases
- "Buy stamps for postage" means to purchase the necessary stamps to send mail.
- "Collect postage" refers to the process of accumulating stamps for mailing purposes.
- "First-class postage" denotes higher-quality or faster mail service with additional fees.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "I need to buy some stamps to cover the first-class postage for my package."
- **Verb**: "She went to the post office to post her letters and packages."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "The post office requires an additional surcharge for express postage."
