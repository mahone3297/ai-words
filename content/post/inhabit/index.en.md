## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈhæbɪt/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To live or dwell permanently or continuously in a place; to reside. For example: "They have inhabited this village for generations."
  - *Transitive Verb*: To populate or occupy (a place) with inhabitants. For example: "The land was inhabited by various tribes."
- **Noun**: The act of inhabiting or dwelling in a place; residence. For example: "The duration of their inhabit in the city was brief."

## Phrases
- "Inhabit a house" means to live in or make a home in a particular building.
- "Inhabitants of a city" refers to the people who live in that city permanently or on a long-term basis.
- "To inhabit different worlds" can metaphorically mean to have very different lifestyles or perspectives.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "She decided to inhabit the countryside after retiring from her busy city life."
  - "The forest was inhabited by a wide variety of wildlife."
- **Noun**: "The old mansion had been abandoned for years until new inhabitants moved in."
