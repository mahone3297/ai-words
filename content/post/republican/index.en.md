## Pronunciation
/rɪˈpʌblɪkən/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **republican** refers to a person who adheres to the principles of a republic, a form of government where power is held by the people, typically through elected representatives. It can also be used to describe a political affiliation supporting a republic rather than a monarchy or other forms of absolute rule.

### Adjective
As an adjective, **republican** describes something related to or characteristic of a republic, such as its political system or values.

### Adjective (US political usage)
In US politics, specifically, it refers to a member of the Republican Party, which is one of the two major political parties in the country.

## Phrases
- "He's a strong republican" means he supports the principles of a republic.
- "The democratic and republican parties" usually refers to the Democratic and Republican Parties in the US.
- "A republicanism movement" denotes a campaign or advocacy for a republic.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She was proud to be a lifelong republican."
- **Adjective**: "The country has a long history of republican ideals."
- **Adjective (US political)**: "Many republicans advocated for lower taxes and limited government."

## Synonyms
- Democratic (in US context, the opposite of republican)
- Liberal (though not always interchangeable, as liberal can also refer to left-wing politics)
- Conservative (again, in US context, often contrasts with republicans)
- Secular
- Democratic-republican (specifically referring to the historical era)
