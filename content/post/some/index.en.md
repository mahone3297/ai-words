## Pronunciation
/səm/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: 1. Some, a quantity or number of; a few or several. For example: "There are some apples on the table."
   - **Interrogative**: Used to inquire about the presence or quantity of something. For example: "Are there any cookies left?"
   - **Adverb**: In some cases; occasionally. For example: "In some situations, it might be necessary."

## Phrases
- "Some of the best" refers to a part considered to be the finest or most outstanding.
- "Some people believe..." indicates that certain individuals hold a particular opinion.
- "Some days are better than others" acknowledges variation in daily experiences.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "I bought some new books for the library."
- **Interrogative**: "Have you seen any of my keys? I can't seem to find some."
- **Adverb**: "Not everyone agrees, but some argue that climate change is real."

## Synonyms
- A few
- Several
- Certain
- A number of
- A couple

## Antonyms
- None (as "some" does not have a direct opposite when referring to a quantity)
- All (when referring to inclusiveness)
