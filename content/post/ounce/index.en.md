## Word: Ounce

## Pronunciation
/ˈaʊns/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A unit of weight equal to 1/16 of a pound or approximately 28.35 grams. For example: "The recipe calls for two ounces of chocolate chips."
- **Noun (historical)**: A former silver coin of England, equal to one-twelfth of a Troy pound.
- **Noun (informal)**: A small amount or quantity. For example: "She only needed an ounce of patience to wait for the results."

## Phrases
- "A drop in the ocean": Refers to something very small or insignificant compared to a larger whole, like an ounce in relation to the vastness of the ocean.
- "An ounce of prevention is worth a pound of cure": A proverb suggesting that taking action to prevent a problem is better than trying to fix it later on.

## Example Sentences
- **Unit of weight**: "The baby weighed eight pounds and three ounces at birth."
- **Historical Coinage**: "During medieval times, the value of goods was often measured in shillings and ounces."
- **Informal Usage**: "Even an ounce of kindness can make a huge difference in someone's day."

## Synonyms
- oz. (abbreviation)
- fluid ounce (for measuring liquid volume)
- small amount
- tiny bit
- modicum
- scrap
- trace

Note: The context determines whether "ounce" refers to a unit of weight or a small amount. In the case of its use as a historical coin, no direct synonyms exist as it's a specific type of currency from the past.
