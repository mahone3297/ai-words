## Pronunciation
/unɪ'versɪtɪ/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A large, autonomous institution of higher education offering degrees and carrying out research, especially one that has a college of arts and sciences and one or more professional schools.
   - Example: "The university is renowned for its medical school and research programs."
2. An association of students, teachers, and administrators at a university, often with a particular branch or department.
   - Example: "He joined the university's debating society."

### Verb
1. To enroll or admit as a student at a university.
   - Example: "She will be university-bound next year."
2. To attend a university, often as a regular part of one's education.
   - Example: "He universityed in London before returning home."

### Adjective
Referring to a university or its educational system.
   - Example: "The university's curriculum is designed to be rigorous and comprehensive."

## Phrases
- "Apply to university" means to submit an application for admission.
- "Graduate from university" is the act of completing a degree program and earning a diploma.
- "University life" refers to the experience of studying at a university.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She completed her degree at Harvard University."
- **Verb**: "After high school, he decided to university in Europe."
- **Adjective**: "The university's facilities are state-of-the-art."
