## Pronunciation
/rɪˈliːs/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The act of making something available for public use, distribution, or viewing; a letting go of control or custody. Example: "The new film will be released in theaters next month."
2. An official statement or announcement, often about a product or event. Example: "The company issued a release regarding their quarterly earnings."
3. (Music) The issuance of a recording from a record label. Example: "The album's first single was released as a digital download."

### Verb
1. To make something accessible or available for use or purchase. Example: "The software developer will release an updated version soon."
2. To set free from confinement or restriction. Example: "The prisoners were released after serving their sentences."
3. (Computing) To distribute or publish a software update. Example: "The app will be released with several bug fixes."

## Phrases
- "Release date" refers to the scheduled time when something becomes available for public consumption.
- "Unveil/release" is used to announce or introduce a new product.
- "Early access/release" allows users to try a product before its official launch.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The movie's release sparked a lot of excitement among fans."
- **Verb**: "The team has released a new security patch to address the vulnerabilities."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "The museum will release exclusive prints at their anniversary event."
