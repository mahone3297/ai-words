## Pronunciation
/ˌmænjuːˈfækʧər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The process of making goods or products using tools, machinery, and labor; the act of producing something on a large scale. For example: "The company specializes in the manufacture of electronic devices."
- **Verb**: To make (something) by combining raw materials through mechanical, chemical, or other industrial processes; to produce on a large scale. For example: "They manufacture high-quality furniture in their local factory."

### Noun usage examples
- "The automobile industry involves the manufacture of vehicles and parts."
- "Improving the efficiency of the manufacturing process can reduce costs."

### Verb usage examples
- "Several countries have started to manufacture vaccines domestically."
- "The company manufactures a wide range of consumer products."

## Phrases
- "Manufacturing sector": refers to the part of the economy that deals with the production of goods.
- "Mass manufacture": the production of goods in large quantities often using assembly-line techniques.
- "Custom manufacture": creating goods according to specific customer requirements.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The manufacture of textiles was once a dominant industry in this region."
- **Verb**: "We will be responsible for designing and manufacturing the new product line."
