## Pronunciation
/pʌnɪʃmənt/

## Definitions
### Noun
**Punishment** refers to the act of imposing a penalty or suffering on someone as a consequence for their actions, often as a form of discipline or retribution.

### Verb
To **punish** is to impose a penalty or inflict a disagreeable experience upon someone in response to their wrongdoing or breach of rules.

## Phrases
- "Administer punishment" implies the act of imposing a penalty or correction.
- "Serve a punishment" means to carry out the assigned penalty.
- "Merciful punishment" suggests a lesser or more lenient form of punishment.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The criminal received a severe punishment for his crimes."
- **Verb**: "The teacher punished the students who cheated on the exam."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "The judge decided to punish the offender with community service."
