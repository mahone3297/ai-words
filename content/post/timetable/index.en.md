## Pronunciation
/tɪmˈtaɪbəl/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A schedule or arrangement showing times for events, usually public transport or school lessons. Also known as a 'timetable plan' or 'schedule'.
- **Verb**: To create or follow a timetable, especially in terms of organizing one's activities or a public service. For example: "The train follows a strict timetable."

## Phrases
- "Stick to a timetable" means to adhere to a planned schedule.
- "Timetable changes" refers to alterations to a planned schedule.
- "Public transport timetable" is the list of scheduled routes and departures.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The students referred to the school timetable to plan their extracurricular activities."
- **Verb**: "The airline updated the flight timetable due to weather conditions."
- **Prepositional phrase**: "She arrived on time, as she always does, following her daily work timetable."
