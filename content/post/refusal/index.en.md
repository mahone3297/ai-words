## Pronunciation
/rɪˈfjuːzəl/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A refusal is when someone rejects or declines something, often verbally or in writing. It can be a refusal of an offer, request, or invitation. For example: "Her refusal to attend the event was unexpected."
- **Verb**: To refuse or decline something, typically with a negative response. For example: "He refused to accept the job offer."

## Phrases
- "Make a refusal" means to formally reject something.
- "Refusal to cooperate" indicates unwillingness to participate or comply.
- "No refusal policy" refers to a rule that does not allow for turning down a request or offer.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "His refusal to compromise on the matter led to conflict."
- **Verb**: "She politely refused their invitation to dinner."
- **Negation**: "He didn't refuse the suggestion outright; instead, he hesitated."
