## Pronunciation
/skɪm/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The act of reading quickly through a text or document, looking for important points or overall meaning without stopping to read each word or sentence in detail.
   - "After skimming the article, she found the main argument to be about climate change."

### Verb
1. To read or look at something quickly, usually to get a general idea without studying it thoroughly.
   - "Before starting the project, he skimmed through the instructions."

### Adjective
1. Relating to or involving a superficial or brief examination.
   - "Her analysis was a skim of the topic, not a deep dive."

## Phrases
- "Skim the surface" means to only glance at or touch the surface without delving deeper.
- "Skimming for key points" is the act of finding important information in a hurry.
- "Skim reading" is a technique often used for rapid information gathering.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She skimmed the pages of the book to find the index."
- **Verb**: "To save time, he skimmed the news headlines every morning."
- **Adjective**: "His understanding was based on a skim of the subject matter."

## Synonyms
- Glance
- Browse
- Scanned
- Quick read
- Peruse (with a sense of speed)
