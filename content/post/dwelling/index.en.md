## Pronunciation
/ˈdwelɪŋ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A place where a person lives; a residence or habitation. It can also refer to a period of living in a particular place or state. 

  > *For example:*
  > - _"They bought a new dwelling in the suburbs."_
  > - _"The old mansion had been a dwelling for generations of the family."_
  > - _"His mind was dwelling on the past events."_

## Phrases
- "Place of dwelling": The location where one resides.
- "Dwelling house": A building used primarily as a home.
- "Permanent dwelling": A residence intended for long-term occupancy.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (residence)**: "After years of saving, they finally moved into their dream dwelling."
- **Noun (state of being)**: "She couldn't help dwelling on her mistakes instead of focusing on the future."
  
## Synonyms
- Residence
- Abode
- Home
- House
- Lodging
- Habitation
- Accommodation
- Dwelling-place
- Living quarters

Note that while "dwelling" often refers to a physical structure, it can also be used metaphorically to describe a mental state of remaining on a particular thought or subject.
