## Pronunciation
/riːd/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A book, article, or other written work**: "I just finished reading a thrilling novel."
- **The act of interpreting written symbols**: "Reading comprehension is essential for students."
- **Understanding from text**: "She read between the lines to understand his true feelings."
- **The ability to read**: "He has excellent reading skills."

### Verb
- **To look at and interpret written words**: "She reads newspapers every morning."
- **To interpret or comprehend the meaning of written or printed material**: "He read the instructions carefully."
- **To perceive or understand something**: "He could read her emotions from her expression."

### Adjective
- **Relating to written language**: "She's a keen reader of poetry."
- **Skilled at reading**: "He's an experienced reader of financial reports."

### Adverb
- **In a manner of reading**: "She reads slowly to ensure understanding."
- **To the extent of reading**: "The book was so captivating that he read it twice."

## Phrases
- "Reading aloud" means to read a text out loud.
- "Reading list" is a collection of books or articles recommended for study or leisure.
- "Reading glasses" are worn to improve close-up vision for those with age-related vision problems.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She spent the whole day curled up with a good book, engrossed in her reading."
- **Verb**: "After finishing the book, he shared his thoughts on the plot."
- **Adjective**: "She is a voracious reader, consuming several books per week."
- **Adverb**: "He read the map several times before starting his journey."
