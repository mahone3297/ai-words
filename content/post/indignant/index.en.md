## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈdɪɡnənt/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Feeling or showing anger or annoyance at what is perceived as unfair treatment. For example: "She was indignant about the way her complaint had been dismissed."

Example sentence: 
- _Adjective_: "The citizens were indignant when they learned about the corruption scandal involving public funds."

## Phrases
- "To feel indignant": To experience a strong sense of righteous anger or moral outrage.
- "Indignant protest": A demonstration or objection that arises from a feeling of deep resentment against injustice or unfairness.
- "Indignant response": A reply characterized by strong feelings of anger or annoyance due to perceived mistreatment or injustice.

## Example Sentences
- _Adjective (phrase)_: "Upon reading the offensive comments online, she wrote an indignant response defending her position."
- _Adjective (sentence)_: "When his boss took credit for his work, he felt indignant and decided to confront him."
