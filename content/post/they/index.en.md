## Pronunciation
/ðeɪ/

## Definitions
- **Pronoun**: Refers to one or more people, often used when the gender is unknown or unimportant. Can be singular (they) or plural (they/them). Can also be used impersonally to refer to a group or an action. For example: "They are coming soon."
- **Subject pronoun**: Acts as the subject of a sentence, taking the place of a noun or noun phrase. For example: "They will finish the project by Friday."
- **Reflexive pronoun**: Used when the subject and object of the verb are the same. For example: "They were looking at themselves in the mirror."

## Phrases
- "They're coming" indicates that a group is on their way.
- "It's up to them" means the decision or responsibility lies with the mentioned group.
- "Each other" is a common usage to indicate mutual action between two individuals.

## Example Sentences
- **Subject pronoun**: "The team decided that they would take a break."
- **Reflexive pronoun**: "The children taught themselves how to ride a bike."
- **Impersonal use**: "Everyone, please take your seats; they're about to start the presentation."
