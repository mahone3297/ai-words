## Word: Kid

## Pronunciation
/kɪd/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A young person, typically between infancy and puberty; a child. For example: "The kids were playing in the park."
- **Noun (informal)**: A young goat. For example: "The farmer raised several kids on his farm."
- **Verb (informal)**: To tease or joke with someone, often affectionately. For example: "He likes to kid his friends about their fashion choices."

## Phrases
- "Just kidding" or "I'm just kidding": Used to indicate that one's previous statement was not serious but intended as a joke.
- "To play the kid": To act playfully or mischievously like a child.
- "To have a kid or kids": To give birth to or parent a child/children.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The teacher asked the kids to line up for recess."
- **Noun (informal)**: "The nanny bottle-fed the baby goats, or kids, every evening."
- **Verb**: "Don't take offense; I'm only kidding when I call you 'old man'."

## Synonyms
- Child (noun)
- Offspring (noun)
- Lamb (for the informal meaning of young goat)
- Tease (verb)
- Joke (verb)
