## Pronunciation
/meɪl/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: (n) A system for sending letters, packages, and other postal items, typically through a public or private postal service. Example: "I sent the package via regular mail."
  - Also, electronic messages sent over the internet: "She checks her email every morning before work."
- **Verb**: (v) To send something, usually a letter or package, through the postal system. Example: "I'll mail the invitations tomorrow."
  - Also, to transmit information or messages electronically: "He mailed the report to his boss."
  
## Phrases
- "Mail carrier" refers to a person who delivers mail.
- "Bulk mail" refers to large quantities of mailings often done by businesses or organizations.
- "Snail mail" is a colloquial term for traditional postal mail that arrives more slowly than electronic communication.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The postman delivered our mail late today."
  - "Make sure you check your spam folder in your email."
- **Verb**: "I need to mail this package internationally."
  - "Don't forget to mail your tax returns on time."

## Synonyms
- Post (noun and verb)
- Correspondence (noun, referring to written communications)
- Dispatch (noun or verb, often formal usage)
- Send (verb, general term for delivering something)
- Transmit (verb, especially for electronic communications)
