## Definitions
- **Noun**: In British and historical American English, a former unit of currency, equal to 1/20 of a pound (or 12 old pence). Now largely replaced by the pound, but still used informally or in certain contexts. For example: "The antique shop had a collection of vintage shillings."
- **Adjective**: Referring to something priced in shillings, especially in the past. For example: "The old shilling coin was intricately designed."

## Phrases

- "A shilling note" referred to a banknote worth a shilling.
- "Shilling change" meant small coins or currency.
