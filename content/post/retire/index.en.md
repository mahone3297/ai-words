## Pronunciation
/ˈrɪtɪər/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Retirement**: The act of formally ceasing one's work, typically when reaching a certain age or after a long period of service.
- **Retiree**: A person who has retired from their job or career.

### Verb
- To **retire**: To stop working or leave a job, often permanently, usually because of old age or retirement benefits.
- To **withdraw from**: To step back or remove oneself from an activity or situation.

### Adjective
- **Retired**: Having stopped working or no longer engaged in paid employment.

### Adverb
- (rarely used) **Retiredly**: In a manner characteristic of someone who is retired, leisurely, or relaxed.

## Phrases
- "Retire from a profession" means to end one's career in that field.
- "Early retirement" refers to leaving work before the usual retirement age.
- "Living off retirement savings" describes financial independence after retiring.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After 40 years, he finally retired from his job as a teacher."
- **Verb**: "She decided to retire gracefully at the age of 65."
- **Adjective**: "The retired couple enjoys spending their days gardening."
- **Adverb**: "He now spends his retired life traveling the world."
