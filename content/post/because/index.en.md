## Pronunciation
/bɪˈkɒz/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: Used to introduce a reason or cause for something. For example: "I stayed home because I was feeling sick."
- **Prepositional phrase**: A combination of a preposition and a noun or pronoun that functions as an adverb, often introducing a reason. For example: "She decided to study abroad because of the opportunity to learn a new language."

## Phrases
- "Because of" is used to indicate the cause or reason for an action.
- "Due to" can also be used interchangeably with "because" to express a similar reason.
- "As a result" or "consequently" are often used to connect a cause and its effect.

## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "She missed the deadline because she had to help her sister move."
- **Prepositional phrase**: "The party was canceled because of the bad weather."
- "They decided to go on vacation because they needed a break from work."

## Synonyms
- For the reason that
- Owing to
- Thanks to
- Since
- Consequently
- Ergo

## Note
While "because" can function as an adverb, it's primarily recognized as a preposition in this context, leading to the formation of prepositional phrases. However, in informal spoken English, it might be used more like an adverb to provide a reason.
