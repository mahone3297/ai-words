## Pronunciation
/vɒlt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: **voltage** (also spelled as volt) 1. A unit of electric potential difference or electromotive force, equal to the energy per unit charge; symbol: V. 2. The amount of electrical pressure or tension in a circuit. 3. A measure of the strength of an electric field.

- **Verb**: To measure or specify voltage; apply voltage to.

## Phrases
- "Apply a high voltage" means to set a high electrical potential.
- "The voltage dropped" signifies a decrease in electrical pressure.
- "A steady voltage source" refers to a stable power supply.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The battery provides a constant voltage of 12 volts."
- **Verb**: "The engineer carefully calibrated the voltage regulator to maintain stability."
- **In context**: "The surge protector protects against voltage fluctuations."

## Synonyms
- Electric potential
- Electromotive force
- Tension
- Potential difference
- Electrical pressure

## Antonyms
- Ground (zero voltage)
- Low voltage
- Open circuit (no voltage flow)
