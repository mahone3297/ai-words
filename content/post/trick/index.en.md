## Pronunciation
/trɪk/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Trick**: A deceptive or surprising action, often intended to deceive or amuse. For example: "The magician performed a series of impressive tricks."
- **Practical joke**: A playful deception meant to amuse or surprise. For example: "He pulled a prank on his friends by filling their lockers with balloons."
- **Skill**: A particular ability or technique, especially one requiring dexterity or ingenuity. For example: "She has a great trick shot in billiards."

### Verb
- **To trick**: To deceive or mislead someone, often by a ruse or a deceptive act. For example: "He tried to trick the waiter into giving him a free dessert."
- **To perform a trick**: To execute a skill or deception, often in a show or game. For example: "The kids loved watching the dog perform tricks."

### Adjective
- **Tricky**: Difficult to understand or accomplish due to its complexity or subtlety. For example: "This math problem is quite tricky."
- **Illusory**: Giving the appearance of something without being real or genuine. For example: "The painting had an illusory depth."

## Phrases
- "Pull a trick" means to deceive or play a prank.
- "A magic trick" refers to a performance involving deception and illusion.
- "It's no trick" is used to emphasize that something is real or genuine.
