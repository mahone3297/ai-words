## Pronunciation
/rəˈzɔr/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **razor** is a sharp-edged tool or instrument, typically with a blade attached to a handle, used for shaving, trimming hair, or cutting other materials like beard hair or fabric.

### Verb
To **razor** can mean to shave using a razor, but it can also metaphorically refer to cutting something close or making a precise, efficient cut.

### Adjective
A **razor-sharp** adjective describes something that is extremely sharp, like a razor's edge.

### Adverb
**Razors** can also be used as an adverb to indicate a very close or precise level, as in "he made a razor-thin margin."

## Phrases
- "Shave with a razor" is the primary action associated with the noun.
- "A clean shave with a razor" implies a smooth and close shave.
- "He had a razor-sharp intellect" highlights someone's exceptional mental acuity.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "I bought a new razor to replace my old one."
- **Verb**: "The barber gave him a close shave with his straight razor."
- **Adjective**: "Her argument was razor-sharp, leaving no room for error."
- **Adverb**: "He made a razor-like decision, considering every detail."
