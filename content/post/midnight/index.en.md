## Word: Midnight

## Pronunciation
/ˌmɪdˈnaɪt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: The middle of the night, occurring at 12 o'clock after sunset and before sunrise, usually considered the transition point between one day and the next. For example: "The train arrives at midnight."

Example sentence: "We're throwing a midnight party to celebrate New Year's Eve."

- **Adjective**: Referring to or happening at the time of midnight. For example: "The clock struck midnight, signaling the start of a new day."

Example sentence: "There's a midnight showing of the movie at the local cinema."

## Phrases
- "At midnight": This phrase denotes something happening precisely at 12 o'clock at night. Example: "The full moon rose at midnight."
- "Midnight oil": Idiomatically referring to working late into the night, often beyond usual bedtime. Example: "To finish the project on time, we had to burn the midnight oil for several nights."
- "Midnight snack": A light meal or snack eaten late at night, typically around midnight. Example: "I couldn't resist having a midnight snack before going to bed."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "It was already past midnight when they finally wrapped up their discussion."
- **Adjective**: "The midnight sun is a natural phenomenon that occurs during summer solstice in certain regions."

## Synonyms
- 12 o'clock at night
- Dead of night
- Witching hour
- Nocturnal hour
- Late-night
- Bedtime (in some contexts)
- Darkness (figurative)
