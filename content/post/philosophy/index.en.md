## Pronunciation
/ˌfɪləˈsɒfɪ/
## Definitions
### Noun
- Philosophy: The study of general and fundamental questions about existence, knowledge, ethics, values, reason, mind, and language, often seeking to provide ultimate answers and guiding principles.
- System of beliefs: A set of ideas, principles, or doctrines that guide an individual's or group's actions and attitudes, especially in matters of ethics or conduct.

### Verb
- To contemplate or engage in philosophical inquiry: She spends her weekends philosophizing about life's meaning.
- To adopt a philosophical stance or worldview: He philosophized about the nature of reality.

### Adjective
- Reflecting or concerned with philosophy: A philosophy seminar.

### Noun (pl.) - Philosophies
- Different ways of thinking or approaching life: Various philosophical schools of thought.

## Phrases
- "Philosophical debates" are discussions on abstract concepts.
- "A philosophical approach" involves deep thinking and introspection.
- "To ponder the philosophy of X" means to reflect on the underlying principles of a subject.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The course introduced students to various philosophical traditions."
- **Verb**: "She found solace in the philosophical writings of ancient thinkers."
- **Adjective**: "His life was guided by a stoic philosophical philosophy."
