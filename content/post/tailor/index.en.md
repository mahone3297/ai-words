## Pronunciation
/tˈeɪlər/

## Definitions
### Noun
A tailor is a person who specializes in making, altering, or repairing clothing, especially men's suits. They are skilled in working with fabrics and understanding patterns.

### Verb
To tailor (a garment) means to adjust or fit it specifically to an individual's measurements, often providing custom alterations. It can also refer to adapting content or style to a particular audience or need.

## Phrases
- "Tailor-made suit" refers to a garment that is specifically designed for a person's measurements.
- "Tailoring services" are offered by professional tailors for bespoke clothing.
- "Tailoring a presentation" means adjusting the content to suit the audience.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The tailor spent hours carefully cutting and sewing the fabric to create a perfect fit."
- **Verb**: "I took my old jacket to the tailor to have it tailored to my new body shape."
- **Adjective**: "Her writing was so well-tailored to her readers' interests that it became a bestseller."

## Synonyms
- Sewer (informal)
- Tailorer
- Custom-fitter
- Alteration specialist
- Designer (in a clothing context)

## Related Words
- Bespoke (tailoring for a specific client)
- Patternmaker
- Seamstress (female tailor)
- Tailcoat (a long, formal coat worn by men)
- Suit (a set of matching clothes, often including a jacket and pants)
