## Pronunciation
/kəmˈkreɪd/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. A term of friendship or respect used to address fellow members of a group, especially a political or military one. 2. A person regarded as a friend or comrade-in-arms. 
- **Adjective**: Characterizing a member of a communist or socialist organization.

## Phrases
- "Comrade solidarity" refers to the unity and support among comrades.
- "Comrade brothers" is a term of endearment for close friends or comrades.
- "Red Comrade" is an informal way to refer to a committed communist.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "We formed strong bonds with our comrades during the training."
- **Adjective**: "The comrade soldiers fought side by side in the battle."
- **Usage**: "They were known as the 'vanguard of the comrade revolutionaries.'"

## Synonyms
- Fellow
- Buddy
- Comrade-in-arms
- Colleague
- Companion

## Note: Comrade is primarily associated with historical or political contexts, and its usage may be limited in modern, informal English. It is more commonly used in literature, history, or when discussing historical figures and movements.
