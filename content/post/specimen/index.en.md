## Pronunciation
/spɪˈsɛmən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 
  - 1. A single representative item or example of a particular species, kind, or class, often preserved for study or exhibition. Example: "The museum displayed a rare botanical specimen."
  - 2. In science or research, a particular instance or case serving as a basis for analysis or comparison. Example: "The biologist collected data from multiple specimens to form a hypothesis."

## Verb (Transitive):
- To collect, gather, or prepare a specimen for study or examination. Example: "The scientist carefully collected soil samples to create a geological specimen."

## Adjective:
- Characteristic or typical of a particular group or type. Example: "The building's design was a fine specimen of Art Deco architecture."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The paleontologist studied the dinosaur fossil as a key specimen in their research."
- **Verb**: "After observing the behavior, the biologist made a specimen of the insect for further analysis."
- **Adjective**: "The garden featured various species of plants, each a distinct specimen of their kind."

## Phrases
- "A specimen collection" denotes a group of items gathered for study or display.
- "Identify a specimen" means to determine the species or characteristics of a sample.
- "Keep a record of specimens" involves maintaining documentation about collected samples.
