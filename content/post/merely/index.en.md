## Pronunciation
/ˈmɪrli/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: Used to emphasize that something is no more than what is stated; only or just. For example: "He was merely suggesting a solution."
  
  **Example Sentence**
  - "I merely pointed out a mistake in her calculations."
  
## Phrases
- "Merely for the sake of": Done solely for a particular reason without any other purpose. Example: "He attended the event merely for the sake of networking."
- "Merely a matter of": Expressing that something is not complex or difficult, but rather straightforward. Example: "Winning this game is merely a matter of strategy."

## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "She merely nodded in response to his question, indicating agreement."
- "It's merely a temporary setback; we'll find a way around it."
