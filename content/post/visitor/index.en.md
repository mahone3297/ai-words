## Pronunciation
/ˈvɪzɪtə(r)/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A person who visits someone or someplace**: A visitor is an individual who comes to a place temporarily, often with the purpose of seeing, being entertained by, or participating in an event or activity. Example: "The museum attracted many visitors from around the world."

### Verb
- **To go and see someone or someplace**: To visit is to travel to a location to spend time with someone or explore, often for leisure or business purposes. Example: "I'm planning to visit my grandparents this weekend."

## Phrases
- "Pay a visit" means to go and see someone or a place as a guest.
- "Be a regular visitor" refers to someone who frequently visits a particular place.
- "Welcome visitors" is a phrase used to express hospitality and openness to people coming to a location.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The hotel had a special arrangement for the long-term visitors."
- **Verb**: "After the conference ended, the attendees visited local attractions."
- "The teacher welcomed the new visitors to the classroom."
