## Pronunciation
/prəˈpɔːrʃənl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Having the same ratio or comparative size or amount as something else; corresponding in proportion. For example: "The size of the house is proportional to the number of people living in it."
- **Adverb**: In a proportional manner; according to the ratio or degree. For example: "Their salaries are paid proportionally to their experience."

## Phrases
- "Proportional representation" refers to a system where the number of representatives in a group reflects the distribution of support for different parties or groups.
- "Keep a proportional balance" means to maintain a fair and even distribution of resources or quantities.
- "Proportional growth" describes a steady increase in relation to a base or initial value.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The expenses were distributed proportionally among the team members."
- **Adverb**: "Their investment returns have grown proportionally over the years."
- "The budget allocation was made proportionally based on each department's needs."
