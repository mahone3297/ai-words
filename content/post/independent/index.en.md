## Word: Independent

## Pronunciation
/ˌɪndɪˈpendənt/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Not influenced or controlled by others; free from outside control or support; self-reliant. For example: "She is an independent thinker who never blindly follows trends."
  
  > *Example Sentence*: "After graduation, he decided to live independently, supporting himself without financial assistance from his parents."
  
- **Noun**: A person who is not affiliated with any particular group, especially in politics. For instance: "Many independents voted for the third-party candidate."

  > *Example Sentence*: "As an independent, she can vote for whichever candidate aligns best with her views."

## Phrases
- "Independent variable": In science or statistics, a variable that is manipulated or changed by the experimenter to observe its effect on another variable (dependent variable).
- "Independent contractor": A self-employed individual hired to complete specific tasks or projects for a client but not as an employee.
- "Go independent": To break away from a larger organization, political party, or group to work or act alone.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The country gained independence from its colonial ruler in the early 20th century."
- **Noun**: "Several independents were elected to Congress, promising to bring fresh perspectives to Washington."
