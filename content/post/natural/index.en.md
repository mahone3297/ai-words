## Word: Natural

## Pronunciation
/ˈnætʃrəl/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: (adj) 1. Relating to or caused by nature; not made or caused by humans. For example: "The water from the natural spring was crystal clear."
   - 2. Existing in or derived from nature; not artificial or man-made. Example: "She only uses natural ingredients in her cooking."
   - 3. Having a quality or characteristic that is usual for a particular species or thing; not altered or distorted. Example: "His natural talent for music became apparent at an early age."
   - 4. Involving no pretense or affectation; sincere and straightforward. Example: "She has a natural charm that draws people to her."
   - 5. Belonging to one's family by birth rather than adoption or marriage. Example: "She's his natural daughter."

- **Noun**: (n) 6. A person with an inherent ability or aptitude for something. Example: "She's a natural at tennis; she never had any formal training."

## Phrases
- "In its natural habitat": Refers to where a living organism typically lives or is found.
- "Natural selection": A fundamental concept in evolutionary biology referring to the process by which certain traits become more or less common in a population over time due to their effect on survival and reproduction.
- "Act naturally": To behave in a way that comes easily and without pretense.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The garden was designed to blend seamlessly with the natural surroundings."
- **Noun**: "Even among experienced athletes, he stood out as a natural leader."
