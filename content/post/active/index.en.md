## Pronunciation
/ˈæktɪv/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Active** (noun): Refers to a person, thing, or situation that is energetic, participating, or exercising power or influence. For example: "The children were actively engaged in the game."
- **Active voice** (grammar): In a sentence where the subject performs the action, not the object. For example: "The dog chased the cat."

### Verb
- **To be active** (verb): To participate in activities, show initiative, or have a role in something. For example: "She is an active member of the community club."
- **Activate** (verb): To start functioning or become operative. For example: "I need to activate my new phone."

### Adjective
- Describing a person who is dynamic, energetic, or showing interest or involvement. For example: "He is an active listener during meetings."
- Describing a substance that reacts with others or is ready to undergo a chemical change. For example: "The active ingredient in the medicine helps reduce pain."

### Adverb
- Describing how something is done, often with energy or enthusiasm. For example: "She sang actively, engaging the audience."

## Phrases
- "Stay active" encourages regular exercise and physical activity.
- "An inactive lifestyle" refers to a lack of participation in activities.
- "Active listening" involves fully concentrating on what someone is saying.

## Example Sentences

- **Adjective**: "The volcano showed signs of active lava flow."
- **Adverb**: "She danced actively at the party, filling the dance floor."
