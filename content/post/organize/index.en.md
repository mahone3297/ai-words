## Pronunciation
/ˈɔːrɡənaɪz/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To arrange or structure systematically; to plan and coordinate the elements of (an activity, task, project, etc.) so as to achieve a specific goal or purpose. For example: "They're organizing a charity event next month."
    - _Also used with 'to'_: "She's been busy organizing her files to make them more manageable."
- **Noun**: The action or process of organizing something, or the state of being organized. For example: "The organization of the conference was impeccable."

### Phrases
- "Get organized": to start arranging one's belongings, tasks, or schedule in a systematic and efficient manner.
- "Organize an event": to plan and execute all aspects of a gathering or function.
- "Organize your thoughts": to arrange one's ideas or arguments in a coherent and logical order.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "I need to organize my closet because it's getting too cluttered."
- "The team leader was praised for his exceptional ability to organize the workflow."
- **Noun**: "Effective time management begins with good organization."
