## Word: Might

## Pronunciation
/maɪt/

## Definitions
- **Modal Verb**: Expressing possibility or ability in the past, present, or future. It can also express permission or suggestion. 
  - **Possibility**: "They might come to the party tonight." (It's possible they will attend.)
  - **Ability**: "With enough practice, you might master this skill." (You have the potential to master it.)
  - **Permission**: "You might leave early if you finish your work." (It's permissible to leave early.)
  - **Suggestion**: "We might try a different approach." (Suggesting to consider another method.)

## Phrases
- "Might as well": To suggest that something is a good idea or worth doing because the circumstances make it logical or convenient. Example: "Since we're already here, we might as well take a look around."
- "If I might": Polite way to ask for permission or suggest something. Example: "If I might interrupt, there's an important point to consider."

## Example Sentences
- **Possibility**: "It might rain later, so bring an umbrella."
- **Ability**: "I thought I might be able to lift it, but it was too heavy."
- **Permission**: "You might want to take tomorrow off; you've been working very hard lately."
- **Suggestion**: "We might reconsider our strategy after reviewing the feedback."
