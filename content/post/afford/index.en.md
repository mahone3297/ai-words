## Pronunciation
/əˈfɔːrd/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The financial means to buy or support something: *I can't afford that expensive car.*
2. Capacity or ability to undertake or provide (sth.): *She has the ability to afford a vacation this year.*
3. Possession or access to sufficient resources: *They can't afford healthcare due to their low income.*

### Verb
1. To have enough money or resources to buy, pay for, or manage (sth.): *We can't afford the rent increase.*
2. To be able to provide or do something without strain: *She afforded herself a day off after weeks of hard work.*

### Adjective
1. Capable of being purchased or provided for: *An affordable apartment.*

## Phrases
- "Can't afford it" expresses inability to purchase or pay for something.
- "Afford to take a risk" means having enough resources to bear the consequences of a risky decision.
- "Be unable to afford" highlights lack of financial capability.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "I'm struggling to afford my student loans."
- **Verb**: "She could barely afford to eat out once a month."
- **Adjective**: "The apartment was within his affordable price range."
