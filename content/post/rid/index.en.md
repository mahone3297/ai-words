## Pronunciation
/rɪd/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The act of getting rid of something or someone, usually permanently. "The farmer was rid of pests with the help of a pesticide."
2. The state of being free from something unwanted or troublesome. "After months of hard work, she finally felt rid of her debt."

### Verb
1. To remove or dispose of (something) completely. "She rid the kitchen of clutter by donating old appliances."
2. To free oneself or someone else from a burden, problem, or influence. "He tried to rid himself of his addiction."

### Adjective
1. Free from something; unburdened. "The garden was rid of weeds after a thorough weeding session."

## Phrases
- "Get rid of" implies removing something unwanted.
- "Be rid of" suggests liberation from a negative situation.
- "Rid oneself of" refers to overcoming or eliminating a habit, emotion, or possession.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The gardener successfully rid the garden of invasive plants."
- **Verb**: "After years of procrastination, he decided to rid his life of bad habits."
- **Adjective**: "The house felt so much lighter once we were rid of all the furniture."
