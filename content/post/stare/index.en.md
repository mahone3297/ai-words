## Pronunciation
/stɛr/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: v (stared, stared, staring)
  - **Transitive**: To look fixedly at someone or something with intense interest or without moving one's eyes away. Example: "She stared at the painting in awe."
  - **Intransitive**: To look intently or fixedly, often for an extended period. Example: "He was staring out the window, lost in thought."
- **Noun**: An act or instance of staring, especially when prolonged or considered rude. Example: "Her stare was unsettling."

## Phrases
- "Stare at something" implies focusing your gaze on an object.
- "Stare blankly" describes an expression of confusion or lack of comprehension.
- "Stare into the distance" suggests deep contemplation or daydreaming.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "He couldn't tear his eyes away from the beautiful sunset, staring in wonder."
- **Noun**: "The stranger's stare made her feel uncomfortable."
- **Intransitive**: "She sat there, staring off into space, lost in her thoughts."
