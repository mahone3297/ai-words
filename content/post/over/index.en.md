## Word: Over

## Pronunciation
/ˈoʊvər/

## Definitions
- **Preposition**: In a higher position than; above. For example: "The bird flew over the tree."
- **Adverb**: From one side to the other side of (a place, area, or thing). For example: "She walked over the bridge."
- **Adverb**: Beyond the top or upper surface of something. For example: "The water level rose over the banks."
- **Adverb**: To the end of (a period of time); throughout. For example: "They worked over the weekend."
- **Adjective**: Finished or done with; completed. For example: "The project is finally over."
- **Verb**: To go or come to the other side of something. For example: "We drove over the hill."
- **Verb** (phrasal): Finish or complete something successfully. For example: "I'll just finish this report over lunch."

## Phrases
- "Over and over again": Repeatedly, many times.
- "All over the place": Spread or scattered widely, disorganized.
- "Get over something/someone": Recover from an emotional upset or shock; stop being affected by something or someone.
- "Go over something": Review or examine something carefully.
- "Hand something over": To pass or transfer something to someone.

## Example Sentences
- **Preposition**: "The plane flew over the city."
- **Adverb**: "She looked over the fence to see what was happening."
- **Adjective**: "The meeting is now over, and we can all leave."
- **Verb**: "Let's go over to the other side of the park."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "She managed to get over her fear of public speaking."
