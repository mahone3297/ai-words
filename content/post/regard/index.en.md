## Definitions
### Noun
- **Regard**: The act of paying attention or giving consideration to someone or something. For example: "She held his regard with great interest."
- **Esteem**: A high opinion or respect for someone's abilities or qualities. For example: "He has earned the regard of his peers for his dedication."
- **Attention**: The state of being mindful or focused on something. For example: "She asked for his regard while explaining the project."

### Verb
- **To regard**: To consider or think about someone or something with respect or interest. For example: "She regarded the painting with admiration."
- **To treat**: To handle or behave towards someone with a particular attitude or respect. For example: "He regards his employees with fairness and kindness."
- **To pay heed**: To listen carefully and take notice. For example: "The teacher asked her students to regard his instructions carefully."

### Adjective
- **Regarded**: Describing something that is considered or treated in a certain way. For example: "The restaurant was highly regarded for its cuisine."
- **Worthwhile**: Deserving of attention or effort due to its value or significance. For example: "His work was regarded as worthwhile by his colleagues."
