## Pronunciation
/ˈwɪtsəl/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. A thin, high-pitched sound produced by blowing air through pursed lips, typically used to signal or attract attention.
   - Example: "He blew a sharp whistle to get the children's attention."
2. A device that produces this sound, often used for signaling, signaling danger, or as a musical instrument.
   - Example: "The referee blew the whistle to start the game."

### Verb
1. To produce a whistle sound using one's mouth.
   - Example: "The children whistled a tune while playing outside."
2. To use a whistle as a signal or to communicate.
   - Example: "The hiker whistled a distress signal to alert rescuers."

### Adjective
1. Characterized by or producing a whistle-like sound.
   - Example: "The wind whistled through the trees."

### Adverb
1. In a whistling manner.
   - Example: "He whistled softly as he worked."

## Phrases
- "Whistle a tune" means to sing or play a melody on a whistle.
- "Whistle-blower" refers to someone who exposes wrongdoing.
- "To whistle past the graveyard" is a colloquial expression meaning to ignore danger or a difficult situation.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The train conductor blew his whistle to warn passengers of an upcoming stop."
- **Verb**: "He whistled a happy tune while walking down the street."
- **Adjective**: "The wind was so strong that it made a whistle through the open window."
- **Adverb**: "She whistled past the challenge, pretending it wasn't there."
