## Word: Mute

## Pronunciation
/ˌmyuːt/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A person who cannot speak or one who is silent by choice, often due to a physical disability or social constraint. Example: "The main character was a mute witness to the crime."
- **Verb**: (1) To silence, suppress, or make quiet. Example: "The teacher muted the background noise with a stern look." (2) To turn off or reduce the sound volume of something, typically electronic devices like TVs or microphones. Example: "He muted his phone during the meeting."
- **Adjective**: (1) Silent, unable to speak. Example: "A mute protest from the audience." (2) Lacking sound or producing no sound; quiet. Example: "The television was on mute."

## Phrases
- "Mute button": A button that allows one to temporarily turn off or lower the sound on a device.
- "Mute testimony": A silent but powerful expression or evidence of something.
- "To fall mute": To suddenly become silent, usually because of shock or fear.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The village had a special school for teaching sign language to mutes."
- **Verb**: "He muted the TV so he wouldn't disturb others while they slept."
- **Adjective**: "In awe, he stood there mute, unable to articulate his feelings."

## Synonyms
- Noun: Silent person, non-speaking individual
- Verb: Silence, hush, quieten, dampen, squelch
- Adjective: Silent, voiceless, speechless, soundless, quiet
