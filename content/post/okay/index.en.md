## Pronunciation
/ˌoʊˈkeɪ/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Acceptable, satisfactory, or good; expressing agreement or consent. For example: "The teacher said the essay was okay."
- **Adverb**: Used to indicate acceptance, agreement, or permission. For example: "Okay, you can go now."
- **Noun**: An expression of assent or acceptance. For example: "A nod is as good as a 'yes' in this context; a simple okay will do."
- **Interjection**: Used to express assent, agreement, or to acknowledge something. For example: "Okay, let's get started."

## Phrases
- "Be okay with something": To accept or be satisfied with something. Example: "I'm okay with staying in tonight."
- "Okay then": Used to confirm agreement or acceptance of a situation or suggestion. Example: "If that's what you want, okay then."
- "Okay, but...": Acknowledging a point while introducing a counterpoint or reservation. Example: "Okay, but we need to leave early tomorrow."

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "The weather today is just okay, not great."
- **Adverb**: "I'm feeling a bit down, but I'll manage, okay?"
- **Noun**: "We're still waiting on his final okay before proceeding."
- **Interjection**: "Okay, everyone, gather around for the meeting."
