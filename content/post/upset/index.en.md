## Pronunciation
/ʌpˈset/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Upset**: (noun) 1. A feeling of anger, disappointment, or disturbance caused by something that upsets someone. 2. An upset in sports, where an unexpected team wins. 3. A disruption or change in one's routine or plans. Example: "She was deeply upset by the news of her friend's illness."

### Verb
- **Upset**: (verb) 1. To disturb or discompose someone's emotions, thoughts, or balance. 2. To cause a defeat or loss for a favored competitor in a competition. 3. To overturn or disarrange something, often accidentally. Example: "The bad weather upset their carefully planned picnic."

### Adjective
- **Upset**: (adjective) Tending to cause distress or emotional disturbance. Example: "The upset child cried after losing their favorite toy."

### Adverb
- **Upset**: (adverb) In an upset manner; unexpectedly or unexpectedly causing upset. Example: "He won the race in an upset victory."

## Phrases
- "Be upset about something" expresses dissatisfaction or concern.
- "To upset someone's expectations" means to exceed or fall short of their anticipation.
- "Stay calm, don't let the little things upset you" advises resilience.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She was so upset after hearing her grades that she couldn't stop crying."
- **Verb**: "The underdog team upset the champions in the final match."
- **Adjective**: "The news of the cancellation left her feeling quite upset."
- **Adverb**: "His sudden departure upset the entire routine of the office."
