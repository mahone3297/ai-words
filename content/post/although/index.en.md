## Pronunciation
/əˈθoʊ/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: Used to introduce a clause that contrasts or qualifies another clause, often with "but" or "yet". It shows a contrary or unexpected fact. For example: "Although it was raining, we decided to go for a walk."

## Phrases
- "In spite of..."
- "Despite..."
- "Though..."
- "While..."
- "As...still..."

## Example Sentences
- **Contrast**: "Although he was exhausted, he stayed up late to finish the project."
- **Qualifying**: "Although the competition was tough, she managed to win the gold medal."
- **Contrastive clause**: "She was happy, although her friends were not there."

## Synonyms
- Despite
- In spite of
- Though
- Despite the fact that
- Although this may be true

## Antonyms
- Because
- Consequently
- Therefore (in a contrasting sense)
- So

Note: The examples provided use "although" primarily as an adverb in this context, but it can also function as a subordinating conjunction in complex sentences, connecting clauses.
