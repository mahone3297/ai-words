## Pronunciation
/rɪˈprəʊtʃ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A rebuke or censure for one's actions or words; criticism or disapproval. [Oxford](https://en.oxforddictionaries.com/definition/reproach)
- **Verb**: To criticize or blame someone for something they have done or said; to find fault with. [Merriam-Webster](https://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/reproach)

## Phrases
- "Reproach someone for their behavior"
- "Take (someone) to task for their actions"
- "Rebuff or scold someone"

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She felt reproached for not completing her work on time."
- **Verb**: "The father reproached his son for lying about his grades."
- **Intransitive**: "His silence reproached her for her harsh words."

## Synonyms
- Scold
- Rebuke
- Chide
- Fault
- Criticize
- Reprimand
- Upbraid

## Antonyms
- Praise
- Compliment
- Approve
- Endorse
- Indulge

## Usage Notes
- In British English, "reproof" is often used interchangeably with "reproach," but it may carry a slightly milder connotation of advice or warning rather than strong disapproval.
