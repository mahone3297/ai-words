## Pronunciation
/probəˈbli/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: Used to express the likelihood or probability of something being true or occurring. It modifies a verb, adjective, or other adverbs. For example: "It's probably going to rain later."
- **Adjective**: Refers to the likelihood or possibility of something happening, often with a certain degree of uncertainty. For example: "The chances of winning are probably low."

## Phrases
- "With a high probability" indicates a strong likelihood.
- "There's a good chance" means it's likely but not certain.
- "It's probable that" expresses a general belief in the likelihood of an event.

## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "I'll probably finish the project by tomorrow."
- **Adjective**: "She's probably the best candidate for the job."
- "He's probably on his way home now."
