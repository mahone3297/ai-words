## Pronunciation
/startl/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: (v) To cause someone to jump or be startled, usually by a sudden noise or unexpected event. **Noun**: A feeling of shock or surprise caused by something unexpected.

## Phrases
- "Startled by the loud noise"
- "Startled awake"
- "Startle sb. out of their wits"

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The sudden thunderstorm startled the birds from their perches."
- **Noun**: "She felt a startle when he touched her shoulder."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "He was startled out of his sleep by the ringing phone."

## Synonyms
- Surprise
- Shock
- Start
- Jolt
- Alarm
- Startle shock

## Antonyms
- Calm
- Relax
- Unperturbed
- Unaffected
- Unstartled

## Usage notes
- The verb "startle" typically implies a sudden, unexpected event that causes fear or surprise, while the noun "startle" refers to the emotional response itself.
- It can also be used in a figurative sense to describe a sudden realization or revelation.
