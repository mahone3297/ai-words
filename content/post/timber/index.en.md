## Pronunciation
/tɪmˈbər/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Timber**: 
   - 1. Wood cut into planks, beams, or logs for construction or other purposes.
   - 2. (informal) Large quantities of wood, especially as a resource or material.
   - 3. (US) The industry that deals with the harvest, processing, and distribution of wood.

### Verb
- **To timber**:
   - (archaic) To fell or cut down trees for lumber.
   - (figurative) To supply or stock up with a large amount of wood.

## Phrases
- "Timber from old growth forests" refers to wood harvested from ancient trees.
- "Building with timber" is a traditional method of construction using wood.
- "Timber export" denotes the trade of wood products internationally.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The house was built entirely of locally sourced timber."
- **Verb**: "The carpenters had to clear a large area of timber to make way for the new development."
- **Informal**: "The伐木工人 were busy timbering the forest."
