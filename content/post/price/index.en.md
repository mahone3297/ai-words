## Pronunciation
/prɪs/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. The amount of money required to buy something, usually expressed in monetary units. 2. A cost or value associated with a product or service. 3. An asking or estimated value, as in auction or negotiation. 4. A price tag or label.
- **Verb**: To set the price of something. 2. To quote or demand a specific price. 3. To evaluate or appraise the worth of something.

## Phrases
- "Market price" refers to the value determined by supply and demand.
- "Set a price on something" means to assign a value to it.
- "Negotiate a price" involves discussing terms to agree on a cost.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The new car had a steep price tag of $50,000."
- **Verb**: "The seller decided to raise the price of the house after receiving multiple offers."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "I couldn't afford the price they were asking for the antique."
