## Pronunciation
/saɪ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: An expression of weariness, disappointment, or sadness, typically accompanied by a deep breath. "A sigh of relief"
- **Verb**: To exhale deeply with a feeling of weariness, sadness, or resignation. "She sighed heavily after a long day at work."
- **Adjective**: Characterized by sighing; expressing weariness or discontent. "A sigh-filled silence"

## Phrases
- "Let out a sigh" to release a sigh of emotion.
- "Sigh of exasperation" for a sigh expressing annoyance or frustration.
- "Sigh of contentment" or "sigh of happiness" to express satisfaction or pleasure.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After a long hike, he collapsed onto the couch, letting out a deep sigh."
- **Verb**: "She sighed as she looked at the never-ending to-do list."
- **Adjective**: "The room was filled with a sigh-like quiet after the argument."

## Synonyms
- Exhale
- Groan
- Heave (a sigh)
- Whistle (a sigh)
- Murmur (of disappointment)
