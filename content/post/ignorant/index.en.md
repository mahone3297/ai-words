## Word: Ignorant

## Pronunciation
/ˈɪɡnərənt/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Lacking knowledge or information about a particular subject; uninformed. For example: "He was ignorant of the latest scientific discoveries."
- **Adjective (informal)**: Unwilling to learn or consider new information; unenlightened. For example: "Her ignorance on the topic was frustrating, as she refused to read any materials that might challenge her beliefs."

## Phrases
- "Ignorance is bliss": This proverb suggests that it can be better not to know certain things because knowledge may bring discomfort or worry.
- "To remain ignorant": To continue to lack knowledge or information about something despite opportunities to learn.
- "In one's ignorance": Due to a lack of knowledge or understanding about something.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "Despite being highly educated, he was ignorant of the local customs when he moved to the new country."
- **Adjective (informal)**: "Her arrogant attitude stemmed from her ignorance about the complexities of the issue."
