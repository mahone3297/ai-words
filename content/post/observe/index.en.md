## Pronunciation
/əbˈzɜːrv/

## Definitions
- **Verb (v)**: To watch or perceive attentively; to notice or detect something through one's senses, especially sight. For example: "The scientists observed the behavior of the animals."
  - _Also Verb_ (legal): To comply with or abide by a law, rule, or agreement. Example: "All participants must observe the terms of the contract."
- **Noun (n)**: An act of observing or watching; a comment or assessment based on what one has seen, heard, or noticed. Example: "Her observations were crucial to solving the case."

## Phrases
- "Make an observation" means to notice and point out something.
- "Keep under observation" means to monitor someone or something continuously.
- "Observation deck" is a platform where people can safely view a scene, often from a high elevation like in a building or stadium.
- "To observe a moment of silence" is to remain silent for a period to show respect or remembrance.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "The astronomer carefully observed the stars through his telescope."
- **Noun**: "Based on her careful observations, the botanist was able to classify the new species."
