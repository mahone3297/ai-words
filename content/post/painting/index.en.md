## Word: Painting

## Pronunciation
/ˈpeɪntɪŋ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A visual art form created by applying paint, pigment, color, or other medium to a solid surface such as canvas, paper, or wood. It usually refers to the finished artwork itself. Example: "The gallery showcased several Impressionist paintings."
  - Also used to describe the action or activity of creating such artworks. Example: "Her favorite pastime is oil painting on weekends."
- **Verb**: The act of applying paint or colors to a surface to create an image, design, or artistic expression. Example: "The artist spent hours painting the landscape onto the canvas."

## Phrases
- "A picture is worth a thousand words" implies that a painting can convey complex ideas or emotions more effectively than a large amount of descriptive text.
- "Painting the town red" is an idiom meaning to have a lively night out filled with entertainment and excitement.
- "To put a new coat of paint on something" metaphorically or literally suggests refreshing or improving its appearance.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Van Gogh's 'Starry Night' is one of the most recognized paintings in the world."
- **Verb**: "After years of practice, he has finally mastered the skill of painting with watercolors."
