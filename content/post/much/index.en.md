## Word: Much

## Pronunciation
/mʌtʃ/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: A quantifier indicating a large but unspecified amount or degree; to a great extent or degree. For example: "There's much work to be done."
  - "She was much happier after her vacation."
- **Adverb**: To a great extent or degree; very. For example: "I couldn't care much less about that issue."
  - "The project has improved much since last month."

## Phrases
- "Much ado about nothing": A phrase used to describe a situation where there has been a lot of fuss or excitement over something that turned out to be unimportant.
- "How much": An inquiry about the quantity or price of something.
- "Much to one's surprise": Expressing that something happened unexpectedly.
  
## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "There isn't much time left before the deadline."
- **Adverb**: "She works much harder than anyone else on the team."

## Synonyms
- Many (when referring to countable nouns)
- A lot (informal)
- Plenty
- Considerable
- Substantial
- Great deal
- Extensive
- Significantly (as an adverb)
