## Word: Effective

## Pronunciation
/ɪˈfektɪv/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Having the intended or expected outcome; producing a strong impression or result. It can also refer to something that is in operation or has force at the present time. 

  - **Example Sentence**: _The new marketing strategy proved to be highly effective._
  
- **Noun (rarely used)**: The power to produce a desired effect; effectiveness. E.g., _The drug lost its effectiveness over time._

## Phrases
- "Effective immediately": This means that something starts to apply or function from the moment it is announced.
  - Example: "The new company policy comes into effect immediately."
- "To be effective in": This phrase indicates the area or context in which something is successful or impactful.
  - Example: "The teacher's methods were particularly effective in engaging reluctant learners."

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: _The team leader implemented an effective communication system to ensure everyone was on the same page._
- **Noun (rare usage)**: _The vaccine's effectiveness against the virus has been proven by numerous studies._
