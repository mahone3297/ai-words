## Pronunciation
/stiˈdɪli/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: In a steady manner; consistently without interruption or variation. For example: "She was working steadily all day."
- **Adjective**: Firm and unchanging; constant. For example: "The ship sailed steadily through the calm waters."
- **Verb**: To move or progress steadily; maintain a consistent pace. For example: "He steadily improved his skills over time."

## Phrases
- "Maintain a steady pace" refers to keeping a consistent rhythm or speed.
- "Steadily increasing" indicates a gradual and continuous rise.
- "Steady hands" is a metaphor for someone who is calm and not easily distracted.

## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "She typed steadily on her keyboard, finishing the report in no time."
- **Adjective**: "The car drove steadily down the highway."
- **Verb**: "Over time, he steadily gained confidence in public speaking."
