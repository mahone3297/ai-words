## Pronunciation
/ˈskɑːlə(r)/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **scholar** is a person who is widely learned, especially in a particular field of study, and is often engaged in academic research or teaching. They are known for their deep knowledge and intellectual pursuits.

### Verb
To **scholar** can mean to study with great dedication or to display a high level of knowledge in a subject. It can also refer to the act of earning an academic degree or title.

## Phrases
- "Academic scholar" refers to a highly respected expert in their field.
- "Research scholar" denotes someone actively involved in scholarly research.
- "A true scholar at heart" describes someone with a passion for learning.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The university appointed a renowned scholar to lead the department."
- **Verb**: "After years of diligent scholarship, he finally earned his PhD."
- **Adjective**: "His extensive scholarship on ancient history was widely admired."
