## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈveɪʒən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: (n) A military assault or entrance by force into a country or region to conquer or subdue it; the act or instance of invading. For example: "The enemy launched a swift invasion, catching the defenders off guard."
- **Noun**: (figurative) The arrival or spread of something unwanted or harmful that disrupts or takes over an area or environment. For example: "An invasion of privacy"; "The forest suffered from an invasion of non-native species."

## Phrases
- "Invasion of" is often used to denote the intrusion or encroachment upon something. For example: "An invasion of personal space."
- "Military invasion" specifically refers to armed forces entering another country with hostile intent.
- "Invasion fleet" refers to a group of warships sent to invade a territory.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (military)**: "World War II began with the Nazi invasion of Poland."
- **Noun (figurative)**: "The sudden invasion of noise from the street disturbed the peaceful neighborhood."
