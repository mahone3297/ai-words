## Pronunciation

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Spoil (n)**: 1. To damage or corrupt something, often through excessive care or indulgence: "The children were spoiled by their grandparents." 2. A reward or treat given to someone, especially as a sign of affection or love: "She was spoilt with presents on her birthday."
- **Spoils (plural)**: The rewards or prizes gained from a victory or competition: "After the war, the spoils were divided among the victorious soldiers."

### Verb
- **Spoil (v)**: 1. To ruin or damage something by improper handling or excessive use: "He accidentally spoiled the cake by leaving it out too long." 2. To indulge or pamper excessively: "Parents should not spoil their child's independence."
- **Spoil (v) (informal)**: To intentionally cause harm or upset: "She was trying to spoil their plans."

### Adjective
- **Spoiled (adj)**: 1. Having been overindulged or pampered: "A spoiled child who expects everything they want." 2. Corrupted or ruined: "The spoiled food had to be thrown away."
- **Spoiling (adj)**: Causing damage or corruption: "The spoiled atmosphere of privilege."

### Adverb
- **Spoilingly (adv)**: In a spoiling manner: "She was spoilingly generous with her time."

## Phrases
- "Spoil for a fight" means to be eager or ready for a confrontation.
- "Spoil someone rotten" is an informal expression to describe giving someone too many treats or privileges.
- "Spoils of war" refers to the goods and property acquired by a victorious side after a conflict.
