## Pronunciation
/ɡreɪs/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: (n) A quality of moving with elegance, poise, and ease; beauty or smoothness of form, movement, or execution. For example: "The ballerina performed with such grace and poise."
- **Noun**: (n) The free and unmerited favor of God, often manifested in kindness towards people. For instance: "Through His grace, he found redemption."
- **Noun**: (n) A period officially allowed for payment of a debt or fulfillment of an obligation, usually after it has technically become due. Example: "They granted us a grace period of 30 days to pay the rent."
- **Verb**: (v) To bestow honor, credit, or favor upon someone or something; to confer dignity or distinction. Example: "The award graced her already impressive résumé."
- **Verb**: (v) To add beauty, charm, or dignity to something. Example: "The garden was graced by a stunning array of flowers."
  
## Phrases
- "By the grace of God": Expressing that something happened thanks to divine intervention or assistance.
- "With good grace": If someone does something with good grace, they do it willingly and without complaint.
- "Saving grace": A redeeming quality that counterbalances negative aspects.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun (elegance)**: "Her every move exhibited a natural grace that captivated the audience."
- **Noun (divine favor)**: "Their unwavering faith led them through life's trials, sustained by the grace of God."
- **Noun (grace period)**: "Customers can enjoy a grace period of six months before their loan repayments begin."
- **Verb**: "The princess gracefully graced the occasion with her presence."
