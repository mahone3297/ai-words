## Pronunciation
/ɪn/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A small hotel or tavern, often one providing lodging and food for travelers. For example: "We stopped at a cozy inn by the roadside for the night."
- **Noun (Historical)**: A large establishment where travelers could rest and find meals and entertainment during medieval times. Example: "The knight sought refuge at the old coaching inn after a long journey."
- **Verb (Rarely used)**: To house or lodge in an inn. Example: "The weary travelers decided to inn for the night before continuing their journey."

## Phrases
- "To check in at an inn": To register and take up residence at an inn.
- "An innkeeper": The owner or manager of an inn who provides accommodations and services for guests.
- "The inn of court": Refers to one of the professional associations for barristers in England and Wales.
