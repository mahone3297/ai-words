## Pronunciation
/ˌɔːrɡənaɪˈzeɪʃən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: (n) A structured group of people united for a particular purpose, such as a business, society, association, or government body. It can also refer to the process of planning and coordinating resources and activities effectively to achieve a specific goal.

Example sentences:
- *The United Nations is an international organization that aims to maintain global peace and security.*
- *"She's in charge of the organization's fundraising efforts."*
- *"Effective time management requires good organization skills."*

## Phrases
- "Nonprofit organization": A type of organization that uses its surplus revenues to further achieve its purpose rather than distributing it as profit.
- "Top-down organization": A hierarchical structure where decisions are made by upper management and then implemented by lower levels.
- "Join an organization": To become a member of a group with a shared purpose or interest.

## Example Sentences
- *After graduation, he joined a professional organization to network and advance his career.*
- *"The company underwent a major organizational restructuring last year."*
- *"They founded a charity organization to help underprivileged children in their community.*
