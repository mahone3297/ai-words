## Pronunciation
/rɪˈmeɪn/

## Definitions
### Noun
1. The act or state of remaining; continued presence or existence.
   - "The furniture remains in the old house."
2. The part that is left after something else has been taken away or used.
   - "There was only a small amount of food remaining."

### Verb
1. To stay in the same place or condition; not change or move.
   - "The partygoers remained until late into the night."
2. To stay behind or be left after others have gone.
   - "Only a few students remained after the exam."

### Adjective
1. Existing or staying in the same position or state.
   - "The house still remains in good condition."
2. Remaining; not consumed or depleted.
   - "The cake has a few slices remaining."

### Adverb
1. In a manner that continues to be present or unchanged.
   - "She remained calm in the face of adversity."

## Phrases
- "Remain silent" means to keep quiet.
- "Remains to be seen" suggests that it's uncertain what will happen next.
- "Remained faithful" describes loyalty over time.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After the storm, the power lines remained down for several days."
- **Verb**: "Despite the challenges, they remained committed to their goal."
- **Adjective**: "The old house, with its original features, remains a cherished family heirloom."
- **Adverb**: "She remained steadfast in her decision, regardless of the consequences."
