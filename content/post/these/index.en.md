## Pronunciation
/ðiːz/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Referring to one or more of a specified group or number, typically closer to the speaker. For example: "These books belong to me."
- **Determiner**: Used before a noun to indicate a particular group or number. For example: "Take these apples; they're ripe."

## Phrases
- "These items" refers to a specific set of things.
- "These days" indicates a recent period of time.
- "These instructions" mean the ones provided or being discussed.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "I need you to pick up these packages at the post office."
- **Determiner**: "These are the keys you were looking for."
- "These children are playing in the park."

## Synonyms
- These ones
- Such (informal)
- That/those (when referring to a specific group)
- A few/a couple

## Antonyms
- Those (referring to a different group)
- None (if no specific group is intended)
