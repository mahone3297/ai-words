## Word: License

## Pronunciation
/ˈlaɪsns/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: An official document giving permission to do something legally, especially to drive a vehicle or operate a business. For example: "He had to renew his driver's license every year."
  - _Also_ (in a figurative sense): Freedom or permission to act, speak, or think as one wants. For example: "She took her new job as a license to experiment with new ideas."

- **Verb (v)**: To give official permission for an activity to take place, often by issuing a license. For example: "The city council licensed several new restaurants last month."
  - _Also_ (figuratively): To permit or authorize (someone) to do something. For example: "The app must be licensed before it can be downloaded."

## Phrases
- "License to kill": A colloquial expression (usually from fiction) referring to permission to use lethal force.
- "Licensed to practice": This phrase is used when referring to professionals who have obtained the necessary qualifications and permissions to work in their field, such as doctors or lawyers.
- "Under license": This indicates that a product or service is being produced or provided with the authorization of the owner or inventor.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "After passing the exam, he finally got his driving license."
- **Verb**: "The government has licensed the company to import and distribute pharmaceuticals."
