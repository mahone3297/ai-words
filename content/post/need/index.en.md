## Pronunciation
/niːd/

## Definitions
- **Noun (n)**: A requirement or necessity; something that is essential or wanted. For example: "The company has a great need for skilled programmers."
- **Verb (v)**: To require or feel a lack of something, often implying it's essential or desirable. For example: "She needs a vacation to recharge her batteries."
- **Verb (v)**: To have a duty or responsibility to do something. For example: "We need to finish this project by tomorrow."
- **Verb (v)**: To want or desire something, sometimes suggesting a lack without which one cannot continue or be satisfied. For example: "I really need a cup of coffee before I start work."

## Phrases
- "In need of" indicates a deficiency or requirement for something. For example: "The house is in need of repair."
- "There is no need" implies that something isn't necessary or required. For example: "There's no need to rush; we have plenty of time."
- "If need be" suggests that something may be necessary or done if required. For example: "I'll stay late at work if need be."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Food, water, and shelter are basic human needs."
- **Verb**: "They needed help with their homework."
- **Verb**: "It's important that we understand what our customers truly need."
- **Verb**: "I need to go to the store before it closes."
