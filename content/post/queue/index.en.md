## Pronunciation
/ˈkjuː/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. A line of people standing, often waiting their turn to be served or to enter a space. *Example*: "The queue at the movie theater was quite long."
   - 2. A sequence or series, often in computer programming. *Example*: "The code processes elements in a queue one by one."

- **Verb**: To form a queue or join a line, typically to wait for something. *Example*: "People queued up patiently for their turn to buy tickets."

## Phrases
- "Stand in line" means to form a queue.
- "Get in line" is a colloquial way of telling someone to join the queue.
- "First come, first served" describes a system where items or services are given to those who arrive first, typically indicated by forming a queue.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The children were queuing up to go on the roller coaster."
- **Verb**: "We had to queue for an hour before we could order our food."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "The guests quickly queued outside the reception area."

## Synonyms
- Line
- Wait
- Formation
- Series
- Stack (in a computer context)
- Queueing (as a process)

## Antonyms
- Disperse
- Scatter
- Jostle (to push past without waiting)
