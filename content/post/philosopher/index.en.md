## Pronunciation
/ˈfələsəfər/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A person who practices philosophy, studies or teaches about fundamental principles, ethics, and the nature of reality. **Adjective**: Characteristic of philosophers or their ideas. **Verb**: To engage in philosophical thinking or discourse.

## Phrases
- "Philosopher's stone" (Metaphorical) - A legendary substance that was sought after for its supposed ability to transmute base metals into gold.
- "Philosophical inquiry" - The process of seeking knowledge through critical thinking and reflection.
- "Philosophical debate" - An argument or discussion about abstract concepts or ideas.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The philosopher pondered the meaning of life in his quiet study."
- **Adjective**: "Her writings were deeply philosophical, challenging conventional wisdom."
- **Verb**: "We had a philosophical conversation over dinner, discussing the nature of existence."
