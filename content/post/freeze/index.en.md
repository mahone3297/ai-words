## Word: Freeze

## Pronunciation
/friːz/

## Definitions
- **Verb (v)**: To change from a liquid or a gas to a solid by reducing the temperature below its freezing point. For example: "The water in the pond froze overnight."
  - Also used metaphorically to indicate stopping or halting something suddenly and completely: "Time seemed to freeze when they heard the news."
- **Noun (n)**: The state of being frozen or the process of becoming frozen. For example: "There was a severe freeze last night that damaged many crops."
  - As a colloquial term: "Let's have a freeze on hiring new employees until further notice."

## Phrases
- "To put something on ice" means to postpone or delay something indefinitely, often using 'freeze' metaphorically.
- "Freeze frame" refers to a still image taken from a video or film, where all motion appears frozen.
- "To freeze up" can mean to become immobilized with fear or panic, or for machinery to stop functioning due to extreme cold or malfunction.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "I accidentally left the milk out, and it froze solid."
- **Noun**: "The sudden freeze caught farmers unprepared, causing significant losses."
- **Metaphorical usage**: "When he saw the police car behind him, his heart froze in terror."

## Synonyms
- Solidify
- Chill
- Cool down
- Congeal
- Icify
- Petrify (in a figurative sense)
- Halt (for the metaphorical sense)
