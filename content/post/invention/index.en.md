## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈvɛnʃən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A new product, idea, or method created after studying and experimenting, typically one that is considered original or novel. For example: "The light bulb was a remarkable invention that changed the world."
    - The act of inventing something; creation or origination. Example: "His invention of a solar-powered car was a major breakthrough."
- **(Archaic)** Finding or discovery (not frequently used in this sense).

## Phrases
- "Invention of the wheel" refers to the seminal innovation in human history where humans developed the first wheel.
- "Patent an invention" means to obtain exclusive rights over an invention through legal means.
- "A stroke of genius/invention" implies a sudden, brilliant idea or creation.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "Alexander Graham Bell's invention of the telephone revolutionized communication."
- "Her latest invention promises to significantly reduce energy consumption in households."
- "The steam engine marked a pinnacle in the industrial inventions of the 18th century."
