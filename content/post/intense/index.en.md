## Word: Intense

## Pronunciation
/ɪnˈtens/

## Definitions
- **Adjective**: Having or showing strong feelings or opinions; very serious or powerful. For example: "She had intense loyalty towards her friends."
  - *Intensity*: The quality of being intense, often referring to emotions, focus, or force.
    > Example: "The athletes displayed incredible intensity during their training sessions."
- **Adjective (Physics)**: Extremely great in degree or strength; concentrated or high in amount. For instance: "An intense beam of light."

## Phrases
- "Intense emotion": Describes a deep, powerful feeling such as love, hate, fear, or joy.
- "Intense debate": A heated and passionate argument where both sides strongly advocate their viewpoints.
- "Intense workout": A physically demanding exercise session that pushes one's limits.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective (Emotion)**: "Their intense passion for music drove them to practice for hours every day."
- **Adjective (Focus)**: "He maintained an intense concentration while solving the complex math problem."
- **Adjective (Physics)**: "The experiment required an intense heat source to ignite the chemical reaction."
