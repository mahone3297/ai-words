## Pronunciation
/tɛmˈpeɪʃ(ə)n/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **temptation** is:
1. An attractive but potentially harmful or undesirable thing that tempts one to act on it, often with negative consequences: *The forbidden fruit was a temptation for Adam and Eve.*
2. An enticement or strong desire to do something: *The promise of a promotion was too great a temptation for him to resist.*

### Verb
To **tempt** someone means:
1. To allure or persuade someone to do something they might later regret: *The chef tempted the customers with his delicious-looking desserts.*
2. To offer something enticing as a way to persuade or influence: *The company tempted new employees with a lucrative signing bonus.*

## Phrases
- "Lose oneself to temptation" describes giving in to a strong desire without self-control.
- "Resist the temptation" means to withstand the urge to do something tempting, usually unwise.
- "A test of one's willpower" refers to a situation where strong temptation is present.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The siren song of the casino was a constant temptation for him."
- **Verb**: "She tried to resist the temptation to indulge in chocolate all day."
- **Verb (passive)**: "The ad campaign was designed to tempt potential customers."

## Synonyms
- Temptation: Inducement, lure, enticement, allurement, solicitousness
- Be tempted: Yield, give in, succumb, be attracted, be tempted by

## Antonyms
- Temptation: Repulsion, aversion, resistance,自制力, discipline
