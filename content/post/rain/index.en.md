## Pronunciation
/rɛɪn/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Rain**: 1. Water that falls from the sky in liquid form due to atmospheric condensation, often accompanied by clouds. 2. (countable) Precipitation in general. Example: "The rain fell heavily all day."
- **Rainfall**: The amount of rain that falls over a specific area or period. Example: "That region receives an annual rainfall of over 40 inches."

### Verb
- **To rain**: 1. To fall from the sky as water droplets. 2. (figurative) To pour down, especially in terms of emotions or events. Example: "It started raining cats and dogs just as we were about to leave."
- **Rain out**: To cancel an event because of rain or bad weather. Example: "The baseball game was rained out."

### Adjective
- **Rainy**: Characterized by or occurring with rain. Example: "We decided to stay inside on a rainy day."
- **Rainproof**: Resistant to rain or preventing rain from entering. Example: "This umbrella is rainproof."

### Adverb
- **Rainily**: In a manner that is rainy or wet. Example: "The leaves rustled rainily in the wind."

## Phrases
- "It's raining cats and dogs" (a colloquial expression meaning it's raining very heavily).
- "Get out of the rain" (an instruction to come indoors to avoid the precipitation).
- "Under the rain cover" (referring to a shelter or protection from rain).

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The children were playing in the puddles after a heavy rain."
- **Verb**: "The concert was canceled due to the heavy rain."
- **Adjective**: "She wore her raincoat to protect herself from the drizzle."
- **Adverb**: "He ran quickly under the awning to escape the rainily falling water."
