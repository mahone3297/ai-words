## Definitions
### Noun
1. The speed at which something is done, measured, or occurring: *The car's top speed is 150 miles per hour.* 
2. A price or amount per unit: *The hotel charges a nightly rate of $100.*
3. A degree of quality or value: *This painting is of a high artistic rate.*
4. A ratio or comparative measurement: *The exchange rate between dollars and euros fluctuates daily.*

### Verb
1. To evaluate or assign a value or score to: *I'll rate this movie based on its storyline and acting.*
2. To set or determine the price or charge: *The company decided to rate their services higher next year.*
3. To perceive or judge the quality or level of: *She rates her students' work on a scale of 1 to 10.*

### Adjective
1. Measured by a standard or scale: *Her performance was rated excellent.*
2. Characterized by a specific level or degree: *A five-star hotel offers a luxurious rate of service.*

## Phrases
- "Rate something" means to give a numerical value or assessment.
- "Go by rate" refers to being determined or calculated according to a set price.
- "Below average rate" indicates a lesser quality or performance.
