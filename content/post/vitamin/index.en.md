## Pronunciation
/vɪˈtaɪmɪn/

## Definitions
### Noun
A **vitamin** is a organic substance that is essential for maintaining good health and proper bodily function, but cannot be synthesized by the body in sufficient quantities and must be obtained through diet.

### Verb
To **take vitamins** or **consume vitamins** refers to the act of ingesting vitamin supplements or incorporating them into one's daily routine to maintain health.

### Adjective
"Vitamin-rich" describes food or products that are high in essential vitamins.

## Phrases
- "A daily multivitamin" is a supplement containing various vitamins.
- "Get your daily dose of vitamins" is a reminder to consume necessary vitamins.
- "Vitamin C is essential for a healthy immune system."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She takes a daily vitamin to support her overall well-being."
- **Verb**: "The doctor recommended he increase his vitamin D intake."
- **Adjective**: "The fruit salad is packed with vitamin C."

## Related Words
- **Vitamin A**: Aids in vision and skin health.
- **Vitamin B complex**: Includes several B vitamins involved in energy metabolism.
- **Vitamin D**: Helps with calcium absorption for strong bones.
- **Vitamin E**: An antioxidant protecting cells from damage.
- **Vitamin C**: Crucial for collagen production and immune function.
