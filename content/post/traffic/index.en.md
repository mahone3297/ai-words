## Pronunciation
/ˈtræfɪk/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **Traffic**: (1) The flow of people, vehicles, or goods along a road, street, or network, especially during rush hours. (2) A large amount of data transmitted over a network. (3) The movement of financial transactions in a market. (4) Air traffic, particularly airplanes flying through a particular area.

### Verb
- **To traffic**: (1) To transport goods or people between places, often illegally. (2) To facilitate or control the movement of goods or people, typically as part of a business operation.

## Phrases
- "Heavy traffic" indicates a congested state.
- "Traffic jam" refers to a blockage of vehicles.
- "Internet traffic" is the flow of data on the web.
- "Air traffic control" manages the movement of aircraft.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The morning commute was plagued by heavy traffic."
- **Verb**: "The smugglers were caught trafficking drugs across the border."
- **As a verb**: "The company optimizes internet traffic to improve website performance."
