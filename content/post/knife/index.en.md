## Pronunciation

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A sharp-edged hand tool with a handle, typically used for cutting or slicing. For example: "The chef expertly handled the carving knife to slice through the roast."
- **Verb (informal)**: To stab or cut with a knife. For example: "In self-defense, he knifed the attacker."

## Phrases
- "To have a/the knife out for someone": To have ill intentions towards someone; to be looking for an opportunity to harm or criticize them. Example: "Ever since the argument, she's had the knife out for him."
- "The knife edge": Refers to a situation that is precarious, uncertain, or finely balanced. Example: "The election result was on a knife edge until the last votes were counted."
- "A double-edged sword/knife": Something that has both advantages and disadvantages. Example: "His intelligence proved to be a double-edged knife; it helped him excel academically but also isolated him from his peers."
