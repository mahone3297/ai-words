# Outline

## Pronunciation
/ˈaʊt.laɪn/

## Definitions
### Noun (n)
- A general plan or sketch showing the main features or structure of something, often used to organize ideas or information. For example: "She presented an outline of her research project."
- The contour or shape of something, especially when visible against a lighter background. For example: "The outline of the mountains was clearly visible on the horizon."

### Verb (v)
- To draw or describe the outer edge or boundaries of something. For example: "He outlined the shapes with a pencil before filling them in with color."
- To summarize or present the main points or structure of something in brief form. For example: "The professor outlined the key concepts covered in the course."

## Phrases
- "Outline a plan/document" - to sketch out the main details or structure beforehand.
- "In outline" - broadly or generally speaking; giving only the main features.
- "To follow the outline" - to adhere to the planned structure or framework.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The book provides an outline of the history of art from medieval times to modern day."
- **Verb**: "Before starting the painting, she carefully outlined each element with a fine brush."
