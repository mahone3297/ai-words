## Pronunciation
/ˈʌndərˌlaɪn/

## Definitions
- **Verb**: To mark a word or part of a word in a text by drawing a line beneath it to indicate emphasis, definition, or to draw attention to it. Can also refer to the act of underlining as a form of notation.
- **Noun**: The line drawn beneath a word or part of a word, typically for emphasis or reference.

## Phrases
- "Underline a word" means to draw a line beneath a word to emphasize or highlight it.
- "Underlining key terms" is a study technique for note-taking.
- "Underlined text" indicates important information in a printed or digital document.

## Example Sentences
- **Verb**: "Please underline all new vocabulary words in the text."
- **Noun**: "The teacher circled the important points and underlined them with a marker."
- **Adverb**: "She underlined carefully to avoid smudging the ink."

## Synonyms
- Highlight
- Emphasize
- Mark (as important)
- Stress (in writing)
- Note (with a line)
