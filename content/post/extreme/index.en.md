## Word: Extreme

## Pronunciation
/ɪkˈstriːm/

## Definitions
- **Adjective (adj)**: Of a character or kind farthest removed from the ordinary or average; utmost or very great. For example: "The extreme heat made it difficult to work outdoors."
- **Noun (n)**: The furthest point or limit of something; the utmost degree. For example: "They were willing to go to extreme lengths to achieve their goal."
- **Noun (n)**: A person who holds an extreme or fanatical view. For example: "He was known as an extreme environmentalist."
  
## Phrases
- **To the extreme**: To an extremely high degree or extent. For example: "His dedication to fitness was taken to the extreme."
- **Extreme measures**: Drastic actions taken in an emergency or to achieve a desired outcome. For example: "In order to save the company, they had to resort to extreme measures."
- **Extreme sports**: Sports involving a high level of risk or danger, such as skydiving or rock climbing.

## Example Sentences
- **Adjective**: "She exhibited extreme caution while driving on the icy roads."
- **Noun**: "Living in poverty, they faced many hardships and extremes of weather."
- **Noun**: "The political extremist group gained significant media attention."

- Maximum
- Acute
- Intense
- Unrestrained
- Excessive
