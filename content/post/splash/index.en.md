## Pronunciation
/splæʃ/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. The action or sound made when something hits a surface with force, causing water, liquid, or other substances to be sent out. For example: "The rain created a big splash when it hit the puddle."
   2. A sudden or conspicuous display or manifestation, often of energy or enthusiasm. For example: "Her entrance was a splash of color in the otherwise dull event."
   3. A social media sensation or viral moment. For example: "The new app had a huge splash in the tech world."

- **Verb**: To make a splashing motion or noise by hitting or entering a liquid. For example: "He jumped into the pool, creating a big splash."
   2. To create a sudden impact or stir, often in a public context. For example: "Her book had a splash in the literary scene."

## Phrases
- "Make a splash" means to create a strong impression or generate excitement.
- "Splish-splash" is a onomatopoeic term for the sound of something splashing.
- "She made a big splash at her first charity event."

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "The kids were having fun splashing around in the pool."
- **Verb**: "The artist's painting was a splash of vibrant colors on the gallery wall."
- **Interjection**: "Oh no, my shoe just splashed with mud!"
