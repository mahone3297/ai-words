## Pronunciation
/telɪˈɡræf/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: 1. An early method of long-distance communication using Morse code or electrical signals, now mostly replaced by telephones and computers. 2. A system or device for sending messages over a distance, especially one using electromagnetic waves or cables. 3. (informal) The news or information transmitted through such a system.

- **Verb**: To send or transmit a message using a telegraph.

## Phrases
- "Send a telegram" or "telegraph a message" means to communicate via telegraph.
- "The telegraph operator" was a person who worked with a telegraph machine.
- "The birth of the telegraph" marks a significant moment in communication history.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "In the 19th century, people relied on the telegraph to stay connected with loved ones across long distances."
- **Verb**: "The journalist telegraphed the breaking news to the newspaper's headquarters."
- **Phrasal Verb**: "The message was telegraphed in Morse code."

## Synonyms
- Telegram (as a noun)
- Dispatch (for sending a message)
- Cable (informal, referring to a telegraph message)
- Signal (less formal, broader meaning)
- Relay (implying transmission of information)
