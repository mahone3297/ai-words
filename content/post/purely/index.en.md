## Pronunciation
/ˈpɜːrli/

## Definitions
- **Adverb**: In an entirely or completely unalloyed manner; without any mixture or qualification. For example: "She solved the problem purely by luck."
- **Adjective**: Free from any mixture or admixture; absolute or pure. For example: "The water was purely clean."

## Phrases
- "Purely by chance" indicates something happening unexpectedly.
- "His intentions were purely altruistic."
- "A purely scientific approach" emphasizes objectivity.

## Example Sentences
- **Adverb**: "She danced purely out of joy."
- **Adjective**: "The solution was purely theoretical, with no practical application yet."
- "He drank the tea purely for its health benefits."
