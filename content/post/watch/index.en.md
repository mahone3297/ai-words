## Pronunciation
/wɒtʃ/

## Definitions
### Noun
- **A clock or watch**: A device that measures and displays time, often worn on a wrist or carried in a pocket.
- **To observe**: The act of paying close attention to something, usually to monitor events or progress.
- **Television show**: A regularly scheduled program broadcast on television.

### Verb
- **To look at**: The act of directing one's gaze at something or someone to see or monitor.
- **To keep track of**: To follow the progress of (time) using a watch.
- **To anticipate**: To be prepared or expect something based on past experiences or information.

## Phrases
- "Keep an eye on" implies watching over or supervising something.
- "Wristwatch" refers to a watch worn on the wrist.
- "Pocket watch" describes a watch carried in a pocket.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "He checked his watch before leaving for the meeting."
- **Verb**: "She watched the sunset from the balcony."
- **Television Show**: "I usually watch 'Breaking Bad' every Sunday evening."
