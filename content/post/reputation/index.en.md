## Pronunciation
/rɪˈpjuːtəʃən/

## Definitions
- **Noun**: A general perception or estimation of someone's character, abilities, or qualities, often based on public opinion or previous actions. **Adjective**: Having a good or favorable reputation. **Verb**: To acquire or maintain a good standing or image in the opinion of others.

## Phrases
- "Reputation management" refers to the process of shaping and maintaining a positive online image.
- "Reputation precedes you" means that your past actions or behavior are known before you even meet someone.
- "Reputation is everything" highlights the importance of one's standing in a community or profession.

## Example Sentences
- **Noun**: "She worked hard to restore her reputation after a series of mistakes."
- **Verb**: "The company's reputation suffered due to the recent scandal."
- **Adjective**: "He has an impeccable reputation in the business world."

## Synonyms
- Reputation: Recognition, fame, credibility, standing, repute, esteem, goodwill
- Maintain: Protect, preserve, uphold, bolster, enhance
- Shape: Form, cultivate, build, establish, refine

## Antonyms
- Damaged/reputational downfall: Ruin, tarnish, disgrace, blemish, disgrace, defamation
